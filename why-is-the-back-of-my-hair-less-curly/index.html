Why Is The Back Of My Hair Less Curly

Written by Daniel Aug 03, 2023 · 4 min read

Curly hair can be a challenge to manage, especially when it comes to maintaining a consistent curl pattern throughout the entire head of hair. Many people with curly hair notice that their curls are less defined in the back of their head. In this article, we'll explore the reasons behind this common issue and provide tips on how to address it.

Why does this happen?

There are a few reasons why the back of your hair may be less curly than the rest of your hair. One of the most common reasons is that the hair in the back of your head is frequently exposed to friction from your pillowcase or hairbrush. This can cause the curls to become frizzy and less defined over time.

Another reason why the back of your hair may be less curly is due to the natural growth pattern of your hair. The hair in the back of your head tends to grow downwards, which can cause the curls to stretch out and become less defined.

How to address this issue

If you're struggling with less defined curls in the back of your head, there are a few things you can do to address the issue:

1. Change your pillowcase

One easy way to reduce friction on the back of your head is to switch to a silk or satin pillowcase. These materials are much smoother than cotton, which can help to preserve your curls and prevent frizz.

2. Use a wide-tooth comb

Using a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush can also help to reduce friction on your hair. Start at the ends of your hair and gently work your way up, being careful not to tug or pull on your curls.

3. Apply product evenly

Make sure you're applying your styling products evenly throughout your hair, including the back. This will help to ensure that your curls are evenly defined and reduce the risk of frizz.

4. Get regular trims

Regular trims can help to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can lead to frizz and less defined curls.

Tips for caring for curly hair

In addition to the tips mentioned above, there are a few general tips that can help to keep curly hair healthy and defined:

1. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your curls and lead to frizz. Instead, embrace your natural texture and use styling products to enhance your curls.

2. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can lead to dryness and frizz. Look for a sulfate-free shampoo that will gently cleanse your hair without stripping it of its natural moisture.

3. Deep condition regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help to nourish and moisturize your curls, making them more defined and less prone to frizz. Try using a deep conditioner once a week to keep your curls healthy and hydrated.

FAQ

Q: Can genetics play a role in the curl pattern of my hair?

A: Yes, genetics can play a role in the texture and curl pattern of your hair. If you have family members with curly hair, it's likely that you'll have a similar texture and curl pattern.

Q: Can coloring or bleaching my hair affect my curl pattern?

A: Yes, coloring or bleaching your hair can damage your curls and lead to a less defined curl pattern. If you're considering coloring or bleaching your hair, be sure to consult with a professional stylist who can help you maintain the health of your curls.

In conclusion

If you're struggling with less defined curls in the back of your head, there are a few things you can do to address the issue. By changing your pillowcase, using a wide-tooth comb, applying product evenly, and getting regular trims, you can help to keep your curls healthy and defined. Additionally, following general tips for caring for curly hair, like avoiding heat styling and deep conditioning regularly, can help to keep your curls looking their best.
