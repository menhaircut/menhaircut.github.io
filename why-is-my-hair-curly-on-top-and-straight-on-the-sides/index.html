Why Is My Hair Curly On Top And Straight On The Sides

Written by Bardi Nov 10, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Have you ever looked in the mirror and wondered why your hair seems to have a mind of its own? If you have curly hair on top and straight hair on the sides, you're not alone. In fact, many people experience this common hair type. In this article, we'll discuss why this happens and provide tips on how to care for and style your hair.

Description

Curly hair on top and straight hair on the sides is a hair type that is characterized by curly or wavy hair on the crown of the head and straight hair on the sides. It's a common hair type that can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, hormonal changes, and heat damage.

How to

1. Embrace Your Natural Hair Type

The first step to caring for your hair is to embrace your natural hair type. This means understanding that your hair will have different textures and patterns throughout your head, and that's okay. You can work with your hair type to create a unique and stylish look.

2. Use the Right Products

Using the right products can make all the difference in caring for your hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair. These products will contain ingredients that help to define and enhance your curls, while also providing moisture and protection.

3. Avoid Heat Damage

Heat damage can cause your hair to become dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. To avoid heat damage, try to limit your use of heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from damage.

4. Style Your Hair Carefully

When styling your hair, be gentle and avoid pulling or tugging at your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, and avoid using brushes, as they can cause breakage and frizz. Try to avoid tight hairstyles like ponytails and braids, as they can cause tension and damage to your hair.

Step by Step

1. Wash Your Hair

The first step in caring for your hair is to wash it with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, as these products will help to enhance your curls and provide moisture and protection.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help detangle and moisturize your hair. This will help to prevent breakage and frizz and make your hair easier to style.

3. Style Your Hair

Once your hair is moisturized and detangled, you can style your hair using your preferred method. Whether you prefer to air dry your hair or use a diffuser to enhance your curls, be gentle and avoid pulling or tugging at your hair.

Tips

1. Protect Your Hair While Sleeping

Protect your hair while sleeping by using a satin or silk pillowcase, or by tying your hair up with a silk scarf. This will help to prevent breakage and frizz while you sleep.

2. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every six to eight weeks to keep your hair looking its best.

3. Experiment with Different Styles

Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you. Whether you prefer to wear your hair up or down, curly or straight, there are endless possibilities for styling your hair.

FAQ

Q: Is curly hair on top and straight hair on the sides a common hair type?

A: Yes, curly hair on top and straight hair on the sides is a common hair type that can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, hormonal changes, and heat damage.

Q: Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on my curly hair?

A: While you can use regular shampoo and conditioner on your curly hair, it's best to use products that are specifically designed for curly hair. These products will contain ingredients that help to define and enhance your curls, while also providing moisture and protection.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: How often you should wash your hair depends on your hair type and personal preference. If you have curly hair, you may find that you don't need to wash your hair as often, as curly hair tends to be drier than other hair types. Aim to wash your hair every two to three days, or as needed.

Hair Care

Caring for your hair is an important part of maintaining healthy, beautiful hair. By embracing your natural hair type, using the right products, avoiding heat damage, and styling your hair carefully, you can create a unique and stylish look that works for you. Remember to protect your hair while sleeping, get regular trims, and experiment with different styles to keep your hair looking its best.
