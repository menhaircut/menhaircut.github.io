How To Style Medium Length Hair With Layers And Bangs

Written by Robby Jul 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have medium length hair with layers and bangs, you may be wondering how to style it to make the most of your haircut. With the right techniques and products, you can create a variety of looks that showcase your hair's natural texture and movement. In this article, we'll cover how to style medium length hair with layers and bangs, including step-by-step instructions, tips, and frequently asked questions.

Description

Medium length hair with layers and bangs is a popular haircut that adds depth and dimension to your hair. Layers can help to create movement and texture, while bangs add a framing effect to your face. This type of haircut is versatile and can be styled in a variety of ways, from sleek and straight to curly and voluminous.

How to Style Medium Length Hair with Layers and Bangs

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

The first step in styling your hair is to wash and condition it with products that are suitable for your hair type. If your hair is dry or damaged, use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to help restore its health. If your hair is oily, use a clarifying shampoo to remove excess oil and buildup. Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water to help seal the cuticle and promote shine.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing and conditioning your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your hair and protect it from heat damage. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product evenly through your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair using a round brush to create volume and texture. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends, using the brush to lift and separate your hair. Use a concentrator nozzle on your blow dryer to direct the heat and prevent frizz.

Step 4: Style Your Bangs

To style your bangs, use a small round brush and blow dry them in the direction you want them to lay. If you want a side-swept look, blow dry them to the side and use a bit of hairspray to hold them in place. If you want a straight across look, blow dry them straight down and use a flat iron to smooth them out.

Step 5: Add Texture and Volume

To add texture and volume to your hair, use a curling iron or flat iron to create waves or curls. Use a bit of texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add volume and hold. If you want a sleek and straight look, use a straightening iron to smooth out your hair and finish with a shine serum or spray.

Tips

- Use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from styling tools
- Experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair type
- Don't overuse styling products, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy
- Consider getting regular trims to maintain your haircut and prevent split ends
- Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized

FAQ

Q: Can I style my hair with layers and bangs if it's naturally curly?
A: Yes, you can style your hair with layers and bangs if it's naturally curly. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance your curls and use a curling iron to define them.

Q: What's the best way to style my hair if I'm in a rush?
A: If you're short on time, try a messy bun or ponytail. You can also try a dry shampoo to refresh your hair and add volume.

Hair Care

To keep your hair looking its best, it's important to take care of it on a regular basis. This includes washing and conditioning your hair with products that are suitable for your hair type, using a leave-in conditioner to protect your hair from heat damage, and getting regular trims to maintain your haircut. You can also use deep conditioning treatments and hair masks to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using harsh chemicals or styling tools that can damage your hair, and always use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools.
