What Was The Genre Of Music In The 60S

Written by William Oct 14, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you curious about the music that defined the 60s? This decade was a time of change and revolution, and the music of this era reflected this transformative period. In this article, we'll explore the various genres of music that emerged during the 60s, including rock, pop, folk, and soul.

Rock Music

Rock music became increasingly popular during the 60s, especially with the rise of bands like The Beatles, The Rolling Stones, and The Who. This genre of music was characterized by its use of electric guitars, drums, and bass, creating a high-energy sound that was perfect for dancing and singing along. Some of the most iconic rock songs of the 60s include "Hey Jude" by The Beatles, "Satisfaction" by The Rolling Stones, and "My Generation" by The Who.

Pop Music

Pop music also flourished during the 60s, with artists like Elvis Presley, The Beach Boys, and The Supremes dominating the airwaves. Pop music was characterized by its catchy melodies, upbeat rhythms, and simple lyrics that were easy to sing along to. Some of the most popular pop songs of the 60s include "Can't Help Falling in Love" by Elvis Presley, "Good Vibrations" by The Beach Boys, and "Baby Love" by The Supremes.

Folk Music

Folk music was another popular genre during the 60s, with artists like Bob Dylan and Joan Baez leading the way. Folk music was characterized by its acoustic sound, simple melodies, and socially conscious lyrics that often addressed issues like civil rights and war. Some of the most iconic folk songs of the 60s include "Blowin' in the Wind" by Bob Dylan, "We Shall Overcome" by Joan Baez, and "The Times They Are a-Changin'" by Bob Dylan.

Soul Music

Soul music emerged during the 60s, with artists like James Brown, Otis Redding, and Aretha Franklin becoming household names. Soul music was characterized by its use of gospel-inspired vocals, horn sections, and funky rhythms that were perfect for dancing. Some of the most popular soul songs of the 60s include "Respect" by Aretha Franklin, "I Got You (I Feel Good)" by James Brown, and "Try a Little Tenderness" by Otis Redding.

Conclusion

The 60s was a decade of change and revolution, and the music of this era reflected this transformative period. Rock, pop, folk, and soul were just a few of the genres that emerged during this time, each with their own unique sound and style. Whether you're a fan of The Beatles or Bob Dylan, there's no denying that the music of the 60s continues to have a lasting impact on the world of music today.
