How Do You Thin Thick Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Nov 21, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can be voluminous and full of body, it can also be difficult to manage and style. If you're looking to thin out your thick locks, you've come to the right place. In this post, we'll walk you through the steps on how to thin thick hair and provide some helpful tips along the way.

  2. How to Thin Your Hair at Home
  3. Tools You'll Need

  5. Tips for Maintaining Thinned Hair

Why You Should Thin Your Thick Hair

Thinning your hair can make it more manageable and easier to style. It can also help to reduce the amount of time it takes to blow dry or straighten your hair, which can be a time-consuming process. Additionally, thinning your hair can help to reduce the weight of your hair, which can be beneficial for those who suffer from headaches or neck pain.

How to Thin Your Hair at Home

Thinning your hair at home is a simple process that can be done using a few basic tools. However, if you're not comfortable doing it yourself, it's best to consult with a professional stylist.

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, you'll need a few tools:

  • Thinning shears
  • Regular scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to thin your hair at home:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Section your hair into manageable sections using hair clips.
  3. Starting with one section, comb your hair straight.
  4. Using the thinning shears, cut into your hair at a 45-degree angle, starting from the middle of your hair and working your way down to the ends. Be sure to only cut a small amount at a time.
  5. Repeat this process on all sections of your hair.
  6. Use regular scissors to trim any uneven ends.
  7. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Maintaining Thinned Hair

Once you've thinned your hair, it's important to maintain it properly. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body to your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products, as they can weigh down your hair.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on a daily basis.

FAQs

Can I thin my hair without using thinning shears?

While thinning shears are the most effective tool for thinning hair, you can also use regular scissors to remove some of the bulk. However, be sure to only cut a small amount at a time to avoid uneven results.

Can thinning my hair cause damage?

Thinning your hair using the proper techniques and tools should not cause damage. However, be sure to avoid cutting too much hair at once, as this can cause uneven results and damage to your hair.

How often should I thin my hair?

This will depend on your hair type and how quickly it grows. However, it's generally recommended to thin your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the desired thickness.

Final Thoughts

Thinning thick hair can be a great way to make it more manageable and easier to style. By following the steps outlined in this post and using the proper tools, you can achieve the desired results. Just be sure to maintain your thinned hair properly to keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
