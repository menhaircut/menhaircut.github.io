How To See Which Hairstyle Suits You A Complete Guide

Are you tired of the same old hairstyle every day? Do you want to switch things up but don't know where to start? The first step is figuring out which hairstyle best suits you. In this article, we'll guide you through how to see which hairstyle suits you and provide tips on how to maintain it.

How to Determine Which Hairstyle Suits You

Before you start browsing through magazines or Pinterest for hairstyle ideas, it's important to determine which hairstyle suits you best. Here are some factors to consider:

Face Shape

Your face shape is one of the most important factors when it comes to choosing the right hairstyle. There are six basic face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. Depending on your face shape, certain hairstyles will look more flattering than others.

For example, if you have a round face shape, a long layered haircut with soft waves can help elongate your face. On the other hand, if you have a square face shape, a bob or lob with side-swept bangs can soften your jawline.

Hair Texture

Your hair texture also plays a role in determining which hairstyle suits you. If you have fine hair, a textured bob or pixie cut can add volume and movement. If you have thick, curly hair, a long layered haircut can help define your curls.

Lifestyle

Your lifestyle is another important factor to consider. If you're constantly on the go and don't have time to style your hair, a low-maintenance haircut like a lob or a messy bun may be more suitable for you. If you have a more formal job or attend events frequently, a sleek updo or a classic blowout may be better suited for your lifestyle.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know what to consider when choosing a hairstyle, it's time to put it into practice. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to determine which hairstyle suits you best:

Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape

Use a measuring tape to measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Once you have your measurements, compare them to the following guidelines:

  • Oval: Forehead is slightly wider than the chin, and the length of the face is about one and a half times the width.
  • Round: Prominent cheeks and a rounded chin, with the length and width of the face roughly the same.
  • Square: Prominent jawline and forehead, with the length and width of the face roughly the same.
  • Heart: Forehead is wider than the cheeks and the chin is pointed.
  • Diamond: Prominent cheekbones and a narrow forehead and jawline.
  • Oblong: The face is longer than it is wide, with a prominent forehead and chin.

Step 2: Consider Your Hair Texture

Take a good look at your hair texture. Is it fine, medium, or thick? Is it straight, wavy, curly, or kinky? Knowing your hair texture will help you determine which hairstyles will work best for you.

Step 3: Assess Your Lifestyle

Think about your lifestyle and how much time you're willing to spend on your hair. Do you want a low-maintenance haircut that you can quickly style in the morning, or are you willing to spend more time on your hair for a more polished look?

Step 4: Browse Hairstyle Ideas

Now that you have a good idea of what you're looking for, it's time to browse hairstyle ideas. Look for hairstyles that are similar to your face shape and hair texture, and take note of any that catch your eye.

Step 5: Consult with Your Hair Stylist

Once you've narrowed down your options, it's time to consult with your hair stylist. They can provide expert advice on which hairstyles will work best for your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle.

Tips for Maintaining Your New Hairstyle

Congratulations, you've found your perfect hairstyle! Here are some tips on how to maintain it:

  • Invest in quality hair care products that are specifically designed for your hair type.
  • Visit your hair stylist regularly for trims and touch-ups.
  • Experiment with different styling techniques to find what works best for you.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors by wearing a hat or using a protective hair spray.

FAQs

Q: What if I can't decide on a hairstyle?

A: If you're having trouble deciding on a hairstyle, try experimenting with different hair accessories or temporary hair color. This can give you a new look without committing to a drastic haircut or color change.

Q: Can I still wear a certain hairstyle even if it doesn't suit my face shape?

A: Of course! At the end of the day, it's all about what makes you feel confident and comfortable. However, keep in mind that certain hairstyles may be more flattering than others depending on your face shape.

Q: Can I still get a certain hairstyle even if I don't have the same hair texture as the model?

A: Absolutely! Your hair stylist can work with your hair texture to create a similar look that suits you.

Hair Care Essentials

Here are some essential hair care products and tools to keep your new hairstyle looking its best:

  • Shampoo and conditioner
  • Hairbrush or comb
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Hair dryer
  • Flat iron or curling iron
  • Hair spray or other styling products

Remember, finding the right hairstyle takes time and patience. By following these steps and consulting with your hair stylist, you'll be sure to find a hairstyle that suits you and makes you feel confident and beautiful.
