How To Cut Yorkie Hair Short At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 19, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're a proud Yorkshire Terrier owner, you know that grooming your little pup is essential to keeping them healthy and happy. However, taking your Yorkie to the groomer every few weeks can add up quickly. One way to save money on grooming expenses is to learn how to cut Yorkie hair short at home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting your Yorkie's hair short, step-by-step.

How to Cut Yorkie Hair Short at Home

Before you start, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Brush
  • Comb
  • Towel
  • Treats (to keep your pup calm and happy)

Step 1: Brush Your Yorkie's Hair

Start by brushing your Yorkie's hair thoroughly. This will remove any tangles or mats and make the hair easier to cut. Use a slicker brush to get rid of any knots, and follow up with a comb to part the hair.

Step 2: Trim the Hair Around the Ears

Using your scissors, carefully trim the hair around your Yorkie's ears. Be sure to cut the hair evenly, and avoid cutting too close to the skin. This will help prevent any accidental cuts or nicks.

Step 3: Trim the Hair on the Body

Using your clippers, trim the hair on your Yorkie's body. Start at the neck and work your way down to the tail, using a comb to guide the clippers. Be sure to use a guard to avoid cutting the hair too short.

Step 4: Trim the Hair on the Legs

Use your scissors to trim the hair on your Yorkie's legs. Start at the top and work your way down to the paw, being careful not to cut the hair too short. Use your comb to hold the hair in place as you cut.

Step 5: Trim the Hair on the Feet

Using your scissors, trim the hair on your Yorkie's feet. Be sure to cut the hair evenly and avoid cutting too close to the skin. This will help prevent any accidental cuts or nicks.

Step 6: Clean Up Around the Anus

Using your scissors, carefully trim the hair around your Yorkie's anus. This will help keep your pup clean and prevent any matting or tangles.

Step 7: Give Your Yorkie a Bath

After you've finished trimming your Yorkie's hair, give them a bath to remove any loose hair or debris. Use a gentle dog shampoo and be sure to rinse thoroughly. Dry your pup with a towel and give them a treat for being such a good boy or girl!

Tips for Cutting Your Yorkie's Hair Short at Home

  • Start slow and be patient. Cutting your Yorkie's hair can take time, so don't rush the process.
  • Use treats to keep your pup calm and happy.
  • Be careful not to cut the hair too short, as this can cause skin irritation.
  • Invest in quality grooming tools to make the process easier.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're unsure about anything.

FAQ About Cutting Yorkie Hair Short at Home

Can I cut my Yorkie's hair short all over?

Yes, you can cut your Yorkie's hair short all over. Just be sure to use a guard on your clippers to avoid cutting the hair too short.

What if I accidentally cut my Yorkie's skin?

If you accidentally cut your Yorkie's skin, clean the area with a cotton ball and some hydrogen peroxide. Apply some antiseptic cream to the area and monitor it closely for signs of infection.

How often should I cut my Yorkie's hair?

You should aim to cut your Yorkie's hair every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking neat and tidy.

How to Care for Your Yorkie's Hair

After cutting your Yorkie's hair short, it's important to take good care of their coat. Brush their hair regularly to prevent tangles and mats, and give them a bath every 4-6 weeks to keep them clean. Use a gentle dog shampoo and be sure to rinse thoroughly. You can also use a leave-in conditioner to keep their hair soft and shiny.

Cutting your Yorkie's hair short at home can be a great way to save money on grooming expenses. Just be sure to take your time, use quality tools, and be patient with your pup. With a little practice, you'll be a pro at cutting your Yorkie's hair in no time!
