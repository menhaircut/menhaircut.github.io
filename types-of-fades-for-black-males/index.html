Types Of Fades For Black Males A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Oct 25, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're a black male looking to switch up your hairstyle, a fade may be just what you need. Fades are a popular haircut style that involve gradually cutting hair shorter and shorter, creating a seamless "fade" between different lengths of hair. There are several different types of fades for black males, each with its own unique look and technique. In this article, we'll explore some of the most popular types of fades for black males, how to achieve them, and how to care for your hair after the cut.

Types of Fades for Black Males

1. High Top Fade

The high top fade is a classic hairstyle that involves cutting the sides and back of the hair short, while leaving a longer layer of hair on top. This style is often referred to as a "box fade" because of its square shape. The high top fade was popularized in the 1980s and 1990s by hip hop artists like Kid 'n Play and Big Daddy Kane.

2. Low Fade

The low fade is a popular choice for black males because it is low maintenance and easy to style. This style involves cutting the hair short on the sides and back, gradually decreasing in length towards the neckline. The hair on top can be left longer or cut short for a more polished look.

3. Mid Fade

The mid fade is a versatile style that can be worn with a variety of hair lengths and textures. This style involves cutting the hair short on the sides and back, but not as short as a low fade. The fade gradually blends into the longer hair on top, creating a seamless transition.

4. Taper Fade

The taper fade is a popular choice for black males because it is a subtle and understated style. This style involves gradually tapering the hair shorter and shorter from the top of the head down to the neckline. The hair on top can be left longer or cut short.

5. Skin Fade

The skin fade is a bold and edgy style that involves cutting the hair extremely short on the sides and back, gradually fading into the skin. This style is often worn with longer hair on top for contrast.

How to Achieve a Fade Haircut

If you're interested in getting a fade haircut, it's best to visit a professional barber or stylist who has experience with this style. Here are the general steps involved in achieving a fade:

Step 1: Decide on the type of fade you want (high top, low fade, etc.) and communicate this to your barber.

Step 2: Your barber will start by cutting your hair on the sides and back to the desired length.

Step 3: Your barber will then begin to gradually fade the hair shorter and shorter, using clippers or scissors to create a seamless transition between lengths.

Step 4: Your barber will finish by trimming and styling the hair on top to your desired length and shape.

Tips for Maintaining Your Fade

Once you've achieved your desired fade, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its shape and style. Here are a few tips:

- Visit your barber regularly for touch-ups and trims.

- Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

- Use a styling product, such as pomade or hair wax, to keep your hair in place and add shine.

FAQ

1. Will a fade work with my hair texture?

Yes, fades can be achieved with a variety of hair textures, from straight to curly to kinky. Your barber can help you choose the best type of fade for your hair type.

2. How often should I get my fade touched up?

This will depend on how quickly your hair grows and how well you maintain your style. In general, it's recommended to visit your barber every 2-4 weeks for touch-ups.

3. Can I style my hair differently after getting a fade?

Yes, you can experiment with different styling products and techniques to achieve different looks with your fade.

Hair Care for Black Males

Regardless of the type of fade you choose, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are a few tips:

- Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for your hair type.

- Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, which can damage your hair.

- Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.

- Limit your use of hair products, as some can cause buildup and damage your hair over time.

By following these tips and choosing the right type of fade for your style and hair type, you can achieve a stylish and flattering haircut that suits your personality and lifestyle.
