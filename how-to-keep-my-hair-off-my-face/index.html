How To Keep Your Hair Off Your Face Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Aug 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have long hair, chances are you've struggled with keeping it off your face at some point. Whether you're working out, trying to focus on a task, or just want to look put-together, having your hair in your face can be frustrating. Fortunately, there are plenty of easy and stylish ways to keep your locks out of your eyes. Here are some tips and tricks for keeping your hair off your face.

How to Keep Your Hair Off Your Face

1. Use a Headband

Headbands are a great way to keep your hair off your face while adding a cute accessory to your outfit. You can choose from a variety of styles, including elastic, cloth, and metal, and wear them across your forehead or further back on your head.

2. Try a Top Knot

A top knot is another popular way to keep your hair off your face. Simply gather your hair at the top of your head and twist it into a bun. You can secure it with a hair tie or bobby pins.

3. Use Hair Clips

Hair clips are a quick and easy way to keep your hair out of your face. You can use them to pin back your bangs or clip your hair to the side. Choose from a variety of colors and styles to match your outfit.

4. Braid Your Hair

Braids are not only stylish but also practical for keeping your hair off your face. You can choose from a variety of braid styles, including French, fishtail, and Dutch braids. You can also braid your hair to the side or in a crown braid for a more formal look.

5. Try a Ponytail

A classic ponytail is always a reliable way to keep your hair off your face. You can wear it high or low, and add a hair tie or scrunchie for a pop of color.

Tips for Keeping Your Hair Off Your Face

1. Use Hair Products

Hair products like gel, mousse, and hairspray can help keep your hair in place. Apply a small amount of product to your hair before styling it to help it stay put.

2. Use Bobby Pins

Bobby pins are a great tool for keeping your hair in place. You can use them to secure a headband or clip your hair to the side. Make sure to choose bobby pins that match your hair color.

3. Choose the Right Hairstyle

Not all hairstyles are created equal when it comes to keeping your hair off your face. Experiment with different styles to see what works best for you.

4. Consider a Haircut

If you're having trouble keeping your hair off your face, it might be time for a haircut. A shorter hairstyle can be easier to manage and style.

FAQs About Keeping Your Hair Off Your Face

Q: Can I keep my hair off my face without using hair products?

A: Yes! There are plenty of ways to keep your hair off your face without using products. Try using a headband, hair clips, or a ponytail.

Q: Can I wear a hat to keep my hair off my face?

A: Yes, hats are a great way to keep your hair off your face while also protecting your skin from the sun. Choose from a variety of styles, including baseball caps, beanies, and fedoras.

Hair Care Tips

Keeping your hair off your face is important, but so is taking care of your hair. Here are some tips for keeping your locks healthy and strong:

1. Wash Your Hair Regularly

Washing your hair regularly can help keep it clean and healthy. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's right for your hair type.

2. Use Heat Protectant

If you use heat styling tools like a straightener or curling iron, make sure to use a heat protectant to prevent damage to your hair.

3. Don't Overbrush Your Hair

Brushing your hair too much can cause breakage and damage. Use a wide-tooth comb or brush gently to avoid pulling on your hair.

4. Get Regular Haircuts

Regular haircuts can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

5. Eat a Healthy Diet

What you eat can affect the health of your hair. Make sure to eat a diet rich in vitamins and minerals, including protein, iron, and biotin.

Keeping your hair off your face doesn't have to be a hassle. With these tips and tricks, you can keep your locks out of your eyes and look great doing it. Just remember to take care of your hair, too!
