How To Naturally Straighten Short Curly Hair

Written by Luffy Sep 16, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of using heat tools to straighten your short curly hair? It’s time to embrace your natural hair and learn how to naturally straighten it without causing damage. In this article, we’ll cover the basics of how to naturally straighten short curly hair, including step-by-step instructions, tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Naturally Straighten Short Curly Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Naturally Straightening Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care for Naturally Straight Hair

How to Naturally Straighten Short Curly Hair

The key to naturally straightening short curly hair is to use gentle techniques that won’t damage your hair. Here are some natural ways to straighten curly hair:

  • Blow-dry your hair on a low heat setting using a diffuser to stretch out your curls and create a straighter look.
  • Use a hair straightening brush or a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair while it’s still damp.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or a hair oil to help smooth out your hair and reduce frizz.
  • Wrap your hair in a silk scarf or sleep on a silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Try using natural hair masks made with ingredients like egg whites, coconut oil, or aloe vera to help straighten your hair over time.

Remember, these techniques may not give you perfectly straight hair, but they will help to reduce frizz and create a smoother, more manageable look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these simple steps to naturally straighten short curly hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel-dry your hair gently, then apply a leave-in conditioner or a hair oil to help smooth out your curls.
  3. Blow-dry your hair on a low heat setting using a diffuser attachment to stretch out your curls and create a straighter look.
  4. Use a hair straightening brush or a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair while it’s still damp.
  5. Wrap your hair in a silk scarf or sleep on a silk pillowcase overnight to prevent frizz and breakage.
  6. In the morning, use a hair oil or serum to smooth out any frizz or flyaways.

Repeat these steps as needed, but try not to over-wash or over-style your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.

Tips for Naturally Straightening Hair

Here are some additional tips for naturally straightening short curly hair:

  • Avoid using heat tools like flat irons or curling irons, as these can damage your hair over time.
  • Be gentle when detangling your hair, and always use a wide-tooth comb or hair straightening brush.
  • Use a silk scarf or pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.
  • Try using natural hair masks or oils to help nourish and straighten your hair over time.
  • Be patient! It may take some time for your hair to naturally straighten, but the results will be worth it.

FAQs

Here are some common questions about naturally straightening short curly hair:

Can I naturally straighten my hair without using any products?

Yes, you can use techniques like blow-drying with a diffuser or using a hair straightening brush without any products. However, using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil can help to reduce frizz and create a smoother look.

How often should I wash my hair?

It’s best to wash your hair no more than 2-3 times per week to avoid drying it out.

Can I still wear my hair curly while I’m trying to straighten it naturally?

Absolutely! You don’t have to straighten your hair every day, and it’s important to embrace your natural curls. Just be sure to use gentle styling techniques and avoid using heat tools too often.

Hair Care for Naturally Straight Hair

Once you’ve achieved your desired level of natural straightening, it’s important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair nourished and hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often, and always use a heat protectant spray if you do use them.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental stressors by wearing a hat or using a hair oil with SPF.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve natural, straight hair without causing damage or sacrificing your curls. Embrace your natural hair and enjoy the benefits of a healthier, more manageable look!
