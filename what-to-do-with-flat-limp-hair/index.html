What To Do With Flat Limp Hair Tips And Tricks For A Fuller Look

Written by Christine Sep 05, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Do you struggle with flat, limp hair that just won't seem to hold any volume? You're not alone. Many people have this same problem, but luckily there are plenty of solutions to help add some life and bounce to your hair. In this article, we'll give you some tips and tricks to try at home.

Table of Contents

  • Hair Care
  • How to Add Volume to Flat Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQ

Hair Care

The first step to achieving fuller, more voluminous hair is to make sure you're taking care of it properly. Here are some tips for healthy hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won't weigh your hair down.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers and straighteners.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet that includes plenty of protein and vitamins.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

How to Add Volume to Flat Hair

There are several ways to add volume to flat hair, depending on your hair type and personal preferences. Here are some popular methods:

Blow Drying

Blow drying your hair can help add volume, but it's important to do it properly. Use a round brush to lift your hair at the roots as you blow dry, and finish with a blast of cool air to set the style.

Teasing

Teasing, or backcombing, involves using a comb to create small tangles at the roots of your hair. This can help add volume, but be careful not to overdo it or you could damage your hair.

Using Products

There are many products on the market designed specifically to add volume to flat hair. Look for volumizing shampoos, mousses, and sprays that will give your hair the lift it needs.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to adding volume to flat hair:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a volumizing product, such as mousse or spray, to your roots.
  3. Blow dry your hair, using a round brush to lift your hair at the roots.
  4. Tease your hair at the crown for extra volume.
  5. Finish with a hairspray to set your style.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for adding volume to flat hair:

  • Use dry shampoo to absorb excess oil and add texture to your hair.
  • Sleep with your hair in a loose bun or braid to add natural volume overnight.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles, such as a messy bun or loose waves, to add volume and texture.
  • Consider getting a haircut with layers, which can help add depth and dimension to your hair.

FAQ

Q: Can I add volume to my hair without using products?

A: Yes, you can try blow drying your hair with a round brush or teasing your hair at the crown.

Q: Will backcombing damage my hair?

A: Backcombing can cause damage if done too frequently or aggressively. Be gentle and use a wide-tooth comb to minimize damage.

Q: How often should I wash my hair to maintain volume?

A: Everyone's hair is different, but generally washing your hair every 2-3 days can help maintain volume without stripping your hair of natural oils.

By following these tips and tricks, you can say goodbye to flat, limp hair and hello to a fuller, more voluminous look. Experiment with different methods to find what works best for you and your hair type.
