How To See If You Suit Short Hair

Written by Eveline Jun 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your long locks and considering a dramatic change? Short hair can be a fun and refreshing change, but how do you know if it will suit you? In this article, we will explore different ways to determine if short hair will be a good fit for you.

Table of Contents

  • Determining Your Face Shape
  • Considering Your Hair Type
  • Evaluating Your Lifestyle
  • Styling and Maintenance
  • Tips for Short Hair Success

Determining Your Face Shape

One of the most important factors to consider when deciding if short hair is right for you is your face shape. Different styles of short hair will flatter different face shapes. Here are the most common face shapes and which styles work best:

  • Round Face: Short hair with volume on top can help elongate the face.
  • Square Face: A short bob with side-swept bangs can soften angular features.
  • Heart-Shaped Face: A pixie cut or short bob with layers can balance out a wider forehead.
  • Oval Face: Lucky you - almost any short style works well with an oval face!

Still not sure what your face shape is? Here's a quick way to find out:

  1. Pull your hair back and take a photo of your face straight on.
  2. Trace the outline of your face in the photo with a marker or pencil.
  3. Compare your outline to the common face shapes above.

Considering Your Hair Type

Another important factor to consider is your hair type. Some hair types work better with short hair than others. Here's what to consider:

  • Straight Hair: Straight hair is easy to style and can be versatile with short haircuts.
  • Curly Hair: Curly hair can be more difficult to style and may require more maintenance with shorter haircuts.
  • Thick Hair: Thick hair can be difficult to manage with short haircuts, but can also look stunning with the right cut.
  • Thin Hair: Short haircuts can add volume to thin hair, but may require more styling to maintain.

Evaluating Your Lifestyle

Short hair can be a great option for people with busy lifestyles, but it's important to consider your daily routine before making the chop. Here are some things to think about:

  • Work: Does your job require a certain level of professionalism or dress code? Make sure your short hair fits with your workplace culture.
  • Hobbies: If you're an avid swimmer or athlete, short hair may be more practical and less time-consuming to maintain.
  • Personal Style: Do you prefer a low-maintenance or high-fashion look? Short hair can offer both, but make sure it aligns with your personal style.

Styling and Maintenance

Short hair can require more maintenance and styling than longer locks. Here are some things to consider:

  • Styling Time: Short haircuts may require more styling and product to maintain their shape.
  • Salon Visits: Short haircuts may require more frequent salon visits to maintain their shape and length.
  • Product Usage: Short haircuts may require different types of styling products than longer hair.

Tips for Short Hair Success

If you've decided that short hair is right for you, here are some tips for success:

  • Bring a Photo: Bring a photo of your desired haircut to your stylist to ensure you're both on the same page.
  • Experiment with Products: Try different styling products to find what works best for your new cut.
  • Be Patient: Short hair can take some getting used to - be patient with yourself as you adjust to your new look.

FAQs

How short is too short?

The right length for you will depend on your face shape, hair type, and personal style. It's best to consult with a stylist to determine the best length for you.

Will short hair make my face look rounder?

Not necessarily! The right short haircut can actually elongate the face and create a more balanced look.

Is short hair more high-maintenance than long hair?

Short hair can require more maintenance and styling, but it can also be more low-maintenance in terms of daily upkeep.

Hair Care for Short Hair

Short hair may require different hair care techniques than longer hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash Regularly: Short hair can get oily more quickly than longer hair, so it may require more frequent washing.
  • Use Lightweight Products: Short hair may require lighter-weight styling products to avoid weighing down the hair.
  • Protect from Heat: Use a heat protectant when styling short hair with hot tools.

Conclusion

Short hair can be a bold and exciting change, but it's important to consider your face shape, hair type, lifestyle, and maintenance before making the chop. If you've decided that short hair is right for you, follow our tips for success and enjoy your new look!
