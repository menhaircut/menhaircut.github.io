What To Do With Curly Hair After The Shower A Comprehensive Guide

Curly hair requires special attention and care. One of the most important steps in a curly hair routine is what you do with your hair after a shower. In this post, we'll discuss how to properly handle your curls after washing, including step-by-step instructions, useful tips, and answers to frequently asked questions. Read on to find out more!

How to Handle Curly Hair After the Shower

Most people with curly hair make the mistake of rubbing their hair vigorously with a towel after washing, which can lead to frizz and damage. Instead, you should gently squeeze out the excess water with a microfiber towel or a cotton t-shirt. Avoid using regular towels, as they can cause friction and damage your curls.

Next, you should apply a leave-in conditioner or a styling product to your damp hair. This will help lock in moisture and define your curls. You can use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, but be gentle and avoid pulling or tugging.

Once you've applied your product and detangled your hair, you have a few options for how to style your curls. You can air-dry, diffuse, or plop your hair, depending on your preferences and hair type. We'll discuss these methods in more detail in the following sections.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're new to the curly hair game, here's a step-by-step guide for how to handle your hair after the shower:

  1. Gently squeeze out the excess water with a microfiber towel or a cotton t-shirt
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling product to your damp hair
  3. Detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers
  4. Choose a styling method (air-dry, diffuse, or plop)
  5. If air-drying, scrunch your hair periodically to encourage curl formation
  6. If diffusing, use a low heat and low speed setting, and scrunch your hair with the diffuser
  7. If plopping, wrap your hair in a t-shirt or a microfiber towel for 20-30 minutes
  8. Once your hair is completely dry, you can fluff and adjust your curls as needed

Useful Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when handling your curly hair after the shower:

  • Use a microfiber towel or a cotton t-shirt to avoid frizz and damage
  • Apply your product in small sections to ensure even distribution
  • Detangle your hair gently, starting from the ends and working your way up
  • Experiment with different styling methods to find what works best for your hair
  • Avoid touching your hair while it's drying to minimize frizz
  • Consider using a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer for faster and more defined drying

FAQs

Q: Can I use a regular towel to dry my curly hair?

A: No, regular towels can cause friction and damage your curls. Use a microfiber towel or a cotton t-shirt instead.

Q: Should I detangle my hair when it's wet or dry?

A: It's best to detangle your hair when it's damp or wet, as this is when it's most pliable and less likely to break.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: This depends on your hair type and personal preferences. Some people wash their hair every day, while others wash it once a week. Experiment to find what works for you.

Curly Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy and beautiful curls. Here are some general tips to keep in mind:

  • Use sulfate-free and silicone-free hair products to avoid buildup and damage
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray or serum
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth
  • Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve gorgeous, defined curls that you'll love showing off. Remember to be patient and experiment with different products and methods until you find what works best for your hair. Happy styling!
