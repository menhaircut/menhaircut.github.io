How To Cut Your Own Hair In Steps At Home A Diy Guide

Written by Bardi Nov 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're tired of spending money on haircuts or simply want to try something new, cutting your own hair at home may be a good option for you. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a salon-worthy look without leaving your house. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of cutting your own hair in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language. Let's get started!

How to Cut Your Own Hair in Steps at Home

Before we delve into the steps, here are a few things you'll need to cut your hair at home:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror
  • Spray bottle with water

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air-dry or blow-drying it. It's easier to cut your hair when it's dry, but if you have curly hair, you may want to cut it when it's wet.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into four parts: the top, left, right, and back. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Cut the Back Section

Start with the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair straight across, making sure to cut only a little at a time. Repeat this process until you've cut all the hair in the back section.

Step 4: Cut the Left and Right Sections

Move on to the left and right sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the side you're working on and pull it straight up. Cut the hair at an angle or straight across, depending on the look you want to achieve. Repeat this process on the other side.

Step 5: Cut the Top Section

For the top section, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the back and working your way to the front. This will create layers and volume.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Using your comb, blend the layers by combing your hair from the roots to the ends. This will ensure that the layers blend seamlessly.

Step 7: Check Your Hair

Use your mirror to check your hair from all angles. Make any necessary adjustments by cutting more hair or blending the layers.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Once you're happy with your haircut, style your hair as desired. You can use a hair straightener, curling iron, or simply let it air-dry.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair at Home

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back once it's cut.
  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. They'll make the process easier and give you better results.
  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing through the process can lead to mistakes.
  • Watch tutorials online to get a better understanding of the process.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your hair if you have curly hair. However, it's best to cut your hair when it's wet to ensure that the curls are even.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always try to fix it by blending the layers or cutting more hair. If all else fails, you can always visit a professional stylist to fix the mistake.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After cutting your own hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and shine. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Use a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Visit a professional stylist every few months to trim your hair and keep it healthy.

With these tips and steps, you can cut your own hair at home like a pro. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun with it!
