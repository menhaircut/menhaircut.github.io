How To Start Dreads On Long Hair

Description

Starting dreadlocks on long hair can be a daunting task, but with the right guidance and care, it can be done successfully. Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that has been around for centuries and can be worn in a variety of styles. Whether you want to go for a bohemian look or a more polished look, dreads can be styled to suit your personality and style. In this article, we’ll give you a step-by-step guide on how to start dreads on long hair, as well as tips for maintaining them and answers to frequently asked questions.

How to Start Dreads on Long Hair

Before you start your dreadlocks, you need to decide on the method you want to use. There are several methods to choose from, including:

  • Backcombing
  • Twist and Rip
  • Crochet
  • Freeform

The backcombing method is the most popular and involves teasing the hair backwards to form dreadlocks. The twist and rip method involves twisting sections of hair and ripping them apart to create the dreadlocks. The crochet method involves using a crochet hook to pull hair through an existing dreadlock and knotting it. The freeform method involves letting your hair naturally dread over time without any manipulation.

Backcombing

The backcombing method is relatively easy and can be done on your own or with the help of a friend. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup.
  2. Section your hair into small sections using clips or hair ties.
  3. Take one section of hair and backcomb it from the tip to the root using a fine-toothed comb.
  4. Repeat this process for all sections of hair, making sure that each section is tightly backcombed.
  5. Roll each section of hair between your palms to form a cylindrical shape.
  6. Secure the ends of each dreadlock with a small rubber band.
  7. Leave the rubber bands in for a few weeks until the dreads have formed.
  8. After a few weeks, remove the rubber bands and palm roll the dreads regularly to maintain their shape.

Twist and Rip

The twist and rip method is similar to the backcombing method, but instead of teasing the hair, you twist and rip it. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup.
  2. Section your hair into small sections using clips or hair ties.
  3. Take one section of hair and twist it tightly.
  4. Rip the twisted hair apart from the tip to the root.
  5. Repeat this process for all sections of hair, making sure that each section is tightly twisted and ripped.
  6. Roll each section of hair between your palms to form a cylindrical shape.
  7. Secure the ends of each dreadlock with a small rubber band.
  8. Leave the rubber bands in for a few weeks until the dreads have formed.
  9. After a few weeks, remove the rubber bands and palm roll the dreads regularly to maintain their shape.

Crochet

The crochet method is a bit more complicated and is best done by a professional stylist. Here’s how it works:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup.
  2. Section your hair into small sections using clips or hair ties.
  3. Take one section of hair and wrap it around your finger to form a loop.
  4. Insert the crochet hook through an existing dreadlock and pull the loop of hair through.
  5. Twist the loop to form a knot at the base of the dreadlock.
  6. Repeat this process for all sections of hair, making sure that each section is tightly crocheted.
  7. After all sections are crocheted, palm roll each dreadlock to maintain its shape.

Freeform

If you want to go for a more natural look, you can let your hair naturally dread over time without any manipulation. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup.
  2. Avoid brushing or combing your hair.
  3. Allow your hair to naturally tangle and knot.
  4. After a few months, your hair should start to form dreadlocks.
  5. Once your hair has formed dreadlocks, you can palm roll them to maintain their shape.

Step by Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to start dreads on long hair:

  1. Decide on the method you want to use to create your dreadlocks.
  2. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup.
  3. Section your hair into small sections using clips or hair ties.
  4. Follow the instructions for your chosen method to create your dreadlocks.
  5. Secure the ends of each dreadlock with a small rubber band.
  6. Leave the rubber bands in for a few weeks until the dreads have formed.
  7. After a few weeks, remove the rubber bands and palm roll the dreads regularly to maintain their shape.
  8. Be patient – it can take several months for your dreadlocks to fully mature.

Tips for Starting Dreads on Long Hair

Here are some tips to help you start your dreadlocks on long hair:

  • Start with clean, detangled hair.
  • Choose a method that works best for your hair type and texture.
  • Be patient – it can take several months for your dreadlocks to fully mature.
  • Palm roll your dreads regularly to maintain their shape.
  • Wear a scarf or hat at night to prevent your dreads from unraveling.
  • Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using heavy products on your dreads.
  • Avoid using a hair dryer on your dreads, as it can cause them to unravel.

FAQs about Starting Dreads on Long Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreadlocks on long hair:

How long does it take for dreadlocks to form?

It can take several months for your dreadlocks to fully form, depending on your hair type and texture.

Can I wash my hair after starting dreadlocks?

Yes, you can wash your hair after starting dreadlocks. In fact, it’s important to keep your scalp clean to prevent buildup and odors.

Can I start my dreadlocks on my own?

Yes, you can start your dreadlocks on your own, but it’s recommended to seek the help of a professional stylist for best results.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks on Long Hair

Here are some tips for caring for your dreadlocks on long hair:

  • Use a residue-free shampoo to keep your dreads clean.
  • Palm roll your dreads regularly to maintain their shape.
  • Avoid using heavy products on your dreads, as they can cause buildup.
  • Wear a scarf or hat at night to prevent your dreads from unraveling.
  • Avoid using a hair dryer on your dreads, as it can cause them to unravel.
