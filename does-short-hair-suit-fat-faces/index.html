Does Short Hair Suit Fat Faces?

Table of Content:

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ

Description

Short hair has been a popular trend for years, but many people with fat faces wonder if short hair suits them. The answer is yes, short hair can suit fat faces, but it depends on the style and cut. You need to choose a style that flatters your face shape and enhances your best features.

How to

Before cutting your hair short, it is essential to consider your face shape. If you have a round face, avoid blunt cuts that end at your chin. Instead, opt for layered cuts that add volume and texture to your hair. If you have a square face, choose a short bob that ends at your jawline to soften your features. If you have an oval face, you can experiment with various short haircuts, as this face shape can carry off almost any style.

Step by step

1. Consult with your hairstylist to determine the best short hairstyle for your face shape.

2. Choose a style that flatters your features and enhances your best features.

3. Consider the texture and thickness of your hair before choosing a cut.

4. Maintain your short hair by getting regular trims every 6-8 weeks.

Tips

1. Experiment with different styles and lengths to find the perfect short hairstyle for you.

2. Use hair products to add volume and texture to your hair.

3. Consider getting highlights or lowlights to add dimension to your short hair.

4. Avoid heavy bangs that can make your face appear rounder.

FAQ

Q: Can I wear short hair if I have a fat face?

A: Yes, short hair can suit fat faces, but it depends on the style and cut. Consult with your hairstylist to determine the best short hairstyle for your face shape.

Q: Will short hair make my face look rounder?

A: It depends on the cut and style. Blunt cuts that end at your chin can make your face look rounder, while layered cuts that add volume and texture can slim your face.

Hair Care

Short hair requires regular maintenance to keep it looking fresh and stylish. Get regular trims every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape of your short hairstyle. Use hair products to add volume and texture to your hair, and consider getting highlights or lowlights to add dimension. Avoid heavy bangs that can make your face appear rounder.
