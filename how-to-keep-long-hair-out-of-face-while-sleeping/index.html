How to Keep Long Hair Out of Face While Sleeping | Tips and Tricks

Long hair is a blessing, but it can be a curse when it comes to sleeping. Waking up with tangled hair and a puffy face is not pleasant. Fortunately, there are ways to keep your hair out of your face while you sleep. In this article, we will provide you with tips and tricks on how to keep long hair out of face while sleeping.

  1. How to Keep Long Hair Out of Face While Sleeping
  2. Step by Step Guide
  3. Tips and Tricks
  4. FAQs

How to Keep Long Hair Out of Face While Sleeping

The first step to keeping your hair out of your face while sleeping is to tie it up. However, the type of hairstyle you choose matters. Here are some hairstyles to consider:

Braid

A braid is a classic hairstyle that will keep your hair in place. To create a braid, start at the top of your head and weave your hair into a three-strand plait. Secure the end of the braid with a hairband or a scrunchie. This hairstyle will help prevent tangles and keep your hair out of your face.

Topknot

A topknot is a simple hairstyle that is perfect for keeping long hair out of your face while sleeping. To create a topknot, gather your hair into a high ponytail and twist it into a bun. Secure the bun with hairpins or a scrunchie. This hairstyle will also help prevent tangles and keep your hair in place.

Loose Bun

A loose bun is a comfortable hairstyle that will keep your hair out of your face while sleeping. To create a loose bun, gather your hair into a low ponytail and twist it into a bun. Leave some hair out to create a messy look. Secure the bun with hairpins or a scrunchie. This hairstyle will help prevent tangles and keep your hair in place.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to keep long hair out of face while sleeping:

  1. Brush your hair to remove tangles.
  2. Choose a hairstyle that will keep your hair out of your face. (Refer to the hairstyles mentioned earlier)
  3. Tie your hair up with a hairband or a scrunchie.
  4. Adjust the hairstyle to your comfort level.
  5. Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and tangles.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to keep in mind:

  • Avoid using hair elastics that have metal parts as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair tie that is gentle on your hair, such as a scrunchie or a silk hairband.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or a hair oil to your hair before sleeping to prevent tangles.
  • Wrap your hair with a silk scarf to protect it from friction and tangles.

FAQs

Q: Will tying my hair up while sleeping cause hair breakage?

A: Tying your hair up too tightly or using harsh hair ties can cause hair breakage. However, using a gentle hair tie and a loose hairstyle will prevent hair breakage.

Q: Is it necessary to use a silk or satin pillowcase?

A: Using a silk or satin pillowcase will prevent friction and tangles. However, if you don't have one, you can wrap your hair with a silk scarf.

Hair Care

Keeping your hair healthy and well-maintained is important to prevent tangles and breakage. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your long hair out of your face while sleeping and wake up with silky, tangle-free hair.
