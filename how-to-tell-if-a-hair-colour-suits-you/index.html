How To Tell If A Hair Colour Suits You

Written by Bardi Aug 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you thinking of changing your hair colour but not sure if it will suit you? Choosing the right hair colour can be overwhelming, but don't worry, we've got you covered. In this post, we will discuss how to tell if a hair colour suits you, step by step. Let's dive in!

  • Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone
  • Step 2: Consider Your Eye Color
  • Step 3: Look at Your Natural Hair Color
  • Step 4: Try on Wigs or Temporary Hair Dye

Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone

The first step in determining if a hair colour suits you is to determine your skin tone. Your skin tone can be warm, cool, or neutral. You can determine your skin tone by looking at the veins on your wrist. If your veins appear green, you have a warm skin tone. If your veins appear blue, you have a cool skin tone. If you can't tell if your veins appear green or blue, you may have a neutral skin tone.

For warm skin tones, hair colours that have golden or reddish undertones work well. For cool skin tones, hair colours with blue or violet undertones work well. For neutral skin tones, you can try both warm and cool hair colours.

Step 2: Consider Your Eye Color

Another factor to consider when choosing a hair colour is your eye color. Some hair colours can make your eyes pop, while others can clash with your eye color. For example, if you have blue eyes, hair colours with warm undertones like golden blonde or copper can make your eyes stand out. If you have brown eyes, hair colours with cool undertones like ash brown or platinum blonde can make your eyes stand out.

Step 3: Look at Your Natural Hair Color

Your natural hair colour can also play a role in determining if a hair colour suits you. If you have dark hair, going too light can wash you out. If you have light hair, going too dark can be too harsh. It's best to choose a hair colour that's within two shades of your natural hair colour.

Step 4: Try on Wigs or Temporary Hair Dye

If you're still unsure if a hair colour suits you, try on wigs or temporary hair dye. This will give you a better idea of how a hair colour will look on you without committing to a permanent change. You can also try virtual hair colour apps to see how a hair colour will look on you.

Step 5: Consult with a Professional

If you're still unsure, it's best to consult with a professional hairstylist. They can give you advice on what hair colours will suit you best based on your skin tone, eye color, and natural hair colour. They can also recommend hair colours that will complement your overall look.

Tips for Choosing the Right Hair Colour

  • Consider the maintenance of the hair colour. Some hair colours require more upkeep than others.
  • Choose a hair colour that complements your wardrobe and overall style.
  • Don't choose a hair colour based solely on trends. What's popular now may not be popular in a few months.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I have multiple skin tones?

If you have multiple skin tones, choose a hair colour that complements the majority of your skin tones. For example, if you have warm undertones on your face but cool undertones on your neck, choose a hair colour that has both warm and cool undertones.

Can I dye my hair a different color if I have dyed it before?

Yes, you can dye your hair a different colour if you have dyed it before. However, you may need to strip the previous colour before applying a new one. It's best to consult with a professional before making any drastic changes to your hair colour.

Hair Care Tips for Colored Hair

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent fading.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often as it can strip the colour.

That's it! We hope this post has helped you determine how to tell if a hair colour suits you. Remember, choosing the right hair colour can be a fun and exciting change, so don't be afraid to experiment. Good luck!
