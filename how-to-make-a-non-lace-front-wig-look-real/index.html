How To Make A Non Lace Front Wig Look Real

Written by Christine Aug 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of wearing a wig that looks fake and unnatural? Whether you're wearing a wig for medical reasons or just for fun, you want it to look as natural as possible. Non-lace front wigs are great for those who don't want to spend a lot of money on a lace front wig, but they can be a bit tricky to style to look natural. In this article, we'll show you how to make a non-lace front wig look real.

Description

Wearing a wig can be a great way to switch up your look or to help you feel confident while going through hair loss. However, when it comes to non-lace front wigs, it can be a bit challenging to make them look natural. The good news is that with a few tips and tricks, you can style your non-lace front wig to look just as realistic as a lace front wig.

How to

Step 1: Choose the Right Wig

Choosing the right wig is the first step in making it look real. Look for a wig that has a similar texture and color to your natural hair. It's also important to choose a wig that fits your head properly, as an ill-fitting wig can look fake.

Step 2: Cut and Style the Wig

Once you've chosen the right wig, it's time to cut and style it. Take your wig to a professional hairstylist who can cut and style it to look like your natural hair. They can also add layers and texture to the wig to make it look more realistic.

Step 3: Use Products

Using products on your wig can help it look more natural. Use a hairspray or mousse to add volume and texture to the wig. You can also use a dry shampoo to help remove any shine from the wig, which can make it look fake.

Step 4: Wear a Wig Cap

Wearing a wig cap can help keep your wig in place and make it look more natural. Choose a wig cap that matches your skin tone to help it blend in with your natural hairline.

Tips

  • Use a wig stand to help style your wig.
  • Don't be afraid to add layers to your wig to make it look more natural.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your wig.
  • Wash your wig regularly to keep it looking fresh.

FAQ

Q: Can I wear a non-lace front wig in a ponytail?

Yes, you can wear a non-lace front wig in a ponytail, but it may not look as natural as a lace front wig. To make it look more natural, try using hair clips to secure the wig in place.

Q: How long does a non-lace front wig last?

A non-lace front wig can last up to six months with proper care and maintenance.

Hair Care

To keep your non-lace front wig looking its best, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your wig every 6-8 wears with a wig shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a wig brush or wide-tooth comb to detangle your wig.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your wig, as this can damage the fibers.
  • Store your wig on a wig stand to help maintain its shape.

By following these tips and tricks, you can make your non-lace front wig look just as natural as a lace front wig. With a little bit of effort, you can feel confident wearing your wig in any situation.
