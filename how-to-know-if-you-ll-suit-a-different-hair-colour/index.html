How To Know If You ll Suit A Different Hair Colour

Written by Aprilia Jul 21, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering changing your hair colour but unsure if it will suit you? Well, you're not alone! Many people struggle to decide on a new hair colour because they don't know if it will complement their skin tone, eye colour, and face shape. In this article, we'll provide you with some helpful tips on how to know if you'll suit a different hair colour. Let's get started!

How to

There are a few ways to determine if a different hair colour will suit you or not. Here are some tips:

1. Determine Your Skin Tone

The first step in deciding on a new hair colour is to determine your skin tone. There are three main categories: cool, warm, and neutral. If you have cool undertones, you may look better with hair colours like platinum blonde, ash brown, or black. If you have warm undertones, you may look better with hair colours like golden blonde, copper, or mahogany. If you have neutral undertones, you can choose from a wide range of hair colours.

2. Consider Your Eye Colour

Your eye colour can also play a role in determining which hair colour will suit you best. Those with blue eyes may look better with cooler hair colours, while those with brown or green eyes may look better with warmer hair colours.

3. Evaluate Your Face Shape

Finally, consider your face shape when choosing a new hair colour. Some hair colours may make your face appear wider or narrower, so it's essential to choose a colour that complements your face shape. For example, if you have a square face shape, you may look better with a hair colour that softens your features, such as a warm caramel or honey blonde.

Step by Step

Now that you know how to determine if a different hair colour will suit you let's go through the steps:

Step 1: Research Different Hair Colours

Start by researching different hair colours and shades that complement your skin tone, eye colour, and face shape. Look at pictures online or in magazines to get an idea of what you like.

Step 2: Consult with a Professional

It's always a good idea to consult with a professional hairstylist before making any drastic changes to your hair. They can provide you with advice on which hair colours will suit you best and help you achieve your desired look.

Step 3: Try on Wigs or Hair Extensions

If you're still unsure about which hair colour will suit you, try on wigs or hair extensions in different colours. This will give you a visual idea of how you will look with a different hair colour.

Step 4: Take the Plunge

Once you've done your research, consulted with a professional, and tried on different hair colours, it's time to take the plunge and try a new hair colour. Remember, it's just hair, and it will grow back if you don't like it.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to help you choose a hair colour that will suit you:

1. Consider Your Lifestyle

When choosing a new hair colour, consider your lifestyle. Are you willing to spend time and money maintaining your new hair colour? If not, choose a colour that requires minimal upkeep.

2. Take Your Personality into Account

Your hair colour can reflect your personality, so choose a colour that you feel comfortable with and that represents who you are.

3. Don't Be Afraid to Experiment

Finally, don't be afraid to experiment with different hair colours. You never know, you may find a colour that you love and that suits you better than your natural hair colour.

FAQ

Q: Can I dye my hair if I have sensitive skin?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair even if you have sensitive skin. However, it's important to use a hair dye that is specifically designed for sensitive skin and to do a patch test before applying the dye to your entire head.

Q: How often should I touch up my roots?

A: The frequency of root touch-ups depends on the speed of your hair growth and the colour of your hair. On average, most people need to touch up their roots every 4-6 weeks.

Q: Will dyeing my hair damage it?

A: Dyeing your hair can cause damage, especially if you use harsh chemicals or bleach. However, if you take good care of your hair and use quality products, you can minimize the damage.

Hair Care

After dyeing your hair, it's essential to take good care of it to maintain its health and colour. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Colour-Safe Shampoo and Conditioner

Using a colour-safe shampoo and conditioner can help maintain the vibrancy of your hair colour and prevent it from fading.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and cause your hair colour to fade. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun's UV rays can cause your hair colour to fade, so it's essential to protect your hair from the sun. Wear a hat or use a hair product with UV protection when spending time outdoors.

4. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can make your hair colour look dull.

Now that you know how to determine if a different hair colour will suit you, it's time to choose a new hair colour and take the plunge. Remember, it's just hair, and it will grow back if you don't like it. Have fun and experiment with different colours until you find one that you love!
