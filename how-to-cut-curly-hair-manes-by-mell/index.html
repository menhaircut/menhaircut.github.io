How To Cut Curly Hair Manes By Mell A Comprehensive Guide

How to Cut Curly Hair Manes by Mell

Before we get started, it's important to note that cutting curly hair manes is not like cutting straight hair. Curly hair has a tendency to bounce back up after it's cut, so it's important to be strategic with your cuts. Mell recommends cutting curly hair when it's dry, so you can see how the curls fall naturally.

Mell also recommends cutting curly hair in its natural state. That means no straightening or blow-drying before the cut. If you cut curly hair when it's straightened, you'll end up with a completely different style once the curls bounce back.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here are Mell's step-by-step instructions for cutting curly hair manes:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning the hair.
  2. Allow the hair to air dry completely.
  3. Section the hair into four parts: two on the top of the head and two on the bottom.
  4. Take one section at a time and cut the curls individually.
  5. Use a pair of hair cutting scissors to cut the curls, taking off no more than an inch at a time.
  6. Continue cutting each section until you've cut all of the curls.
  7. Check for any unevenness and make any necessary adjustments.

If you're cutting your own hair, Mell recommends using two mirrors to see the back of your head.

Tips

Here are some tips from Mell to help you get the best results when cutting curly hair manes:

  • Don't cut too much off at once. It's better to take off a little at a time and make adjustments as needed.
  • Use hair cutting scissors, not regular scissors. Regular scissors can damage the hair and cause split ends.
  • Cut the hair when it's dry so you can see how the curls fall naturally.
  • Cut the hair in its natural state, without straightening or blow-drying beforehand.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting curly hair manes can take longer than cutting straight hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting curly hair manes:

Q: How often should I cut my curly hair?

A: Mell recommends getting a trim every three to four months to keep your curls looking their best.

Q: Can I cut my curly hair when it's wet?

A: Mell recommends cutting curly hair when it's dry, so you can see how the curls fall naturally.

Q: Should I cut my own curly hair or go to a hairstylist?

A: If you're comfortable cutting your own hair, then go for it! Just make sure you have the right tools and take your time. If you're not comfortable cutting your own hair, then it's best to go to a hairstylist who specializes in cutting curly hair manes.

Hair Care Advice

Here are some tips from Mell to help you take care of your curly hair after you've cut it:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to help hydrate and define your curls.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying to help enhance your curls.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to help prevent frizz.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often, as they can damage your hair.

And there you have it! Now you know how to cut curly hair manes by Mell, including step-by-step instructions, tips, FAQs, and hair care advice. Happy cutting!
