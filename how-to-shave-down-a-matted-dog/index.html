How To Shave Down A Matted Dog A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Sep 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If your furry friend has a coat that easily gets tangled, you know how frustrating it can be to try and comb out the mats. Sometimes, the only solution is to shave down the matted fur. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to do it safely and effectively.

Description

Matted fur can be a serious problem for dogs. Not only does it look unsightly, but it can also cause discomfort and even pain for your pet. Mats can trap dirt, moisture, and even insects, leading to skin irritation and infection. If left untreated, mats can become so severe that they pull on the skin, causing sores and even permanent damage.

How to Shave Down a Matted Dog

Step 1: Prepare the Area

Before you start shaving, make sure you have a clean, well-lit area to work in. Place your dog on a non-slip surface, such as a rubber mat or towel. You'll also need a pair of sharp scissors, electric clippers, and a comb.

Step 2: Assess the Matting

Take a close look at the matted fur to determine how severe the problem is. If the mats are small and close to the skin, you may be able to comb them out. If the mats are large and tight, you'll need to shave them down.

Step 3: Cut the Mats

Using your scissors, carefully cut through the mats. Be sure to hold the fur away from the skin to avoid accidentally nicking it. Start at the base of the mat and work your way up. Cut small sections at a time until the mat is completely removed.

Step 4: Trim the Fur

Once the mats are removed, use your electric clippers to trim the fur to a length of about one inch. Be sure to use a clipper guard to prevent accidental cuts. Work in the direction of the fur growth, and use a comb to guide the clippers.

Step 5: Clean Up

After you're finished, give your dog a bath to remove any loose hair and debris. Be sure to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to soothe the skin. Dry your dog thoroughly with a towel or blow dryer, and give them a treat for being a good sport!

Tips

Here are some additional tips to make the process easier:

  • Use a slicker brush to help remove the mats before you start cutting.
  • Be patient and work slowly to avoid injuring your dog.
  • If your dog is particularly anxious or uncooperative, consider taking them to a professional groomer.
  • Use a cool setting on your blow dryer to avoid burning your pet's skin.
  • Remember to praise and reward your dog throughout the grooming process.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about shaving down a matted dog:

Is it safe to shave a matted dog?

Yes, it's safe to shave a matted dog as long as you do it carefully and properly. Avoid using dull scissors or clippers, as they can easily nick your pet's skin.

What if my dog won't sit still?

If your dog is uncooperative or anxious, consider taking them to a professional groomer. They have the tools and experience to handle even the most difficult dogs.

Will my dog's hair grow back after being shaved?

Yes, your dog's hair will grow back after being shaved. However, it may take several months for the coat to return to its original length and texture.

Hair Care

To prevent mats from forming in the future, it's important to maintain your dog's coat with regular brushing and grooming. Use a slicker brush to remove tangles and loose hair, and give your dog a bath every 6-8 weeks with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. If you notice mats forming, address them as soon as possible to avoid more serious problems down the road.
