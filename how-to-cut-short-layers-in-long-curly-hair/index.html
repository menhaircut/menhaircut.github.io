How To Cut Short Layers In Long Curly Hair

Written by Petter Aug 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of having long, unmanageable curly hair? Do you want a new look that will make your curls more manageable and add volume to your hair? If so, cutting short layers in your long curly hair might be the solution you're looking for. In this article, we will guide you on how to cut short layers in long curly hair, step by step.

Description

Short layers in long curly hair are a great way to add volume and shape to your hair. By cutting shorter layers, you will create a more defined shape and add movement to your curls. This style is perfect for those who want to refresh their look without losing their length. However, it's important to note that cutting curly hair can be tricky, so it's best to follow a few guidelines before attempting to cut your hair yourself.

How to Cut Short Layers in Long Curly Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, you will need the right tools. Make sure you have a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and hair clips to hold your hair in place. You may also want to invest in a spray bottle so you can dampen your hair as you cut.

Step 2: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Before you start cutting, wash and condition your hair to make it easier to work with. Make sure to detangle your curls gently and use a wide-tooth comb to remove any knots or tangles.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start cutting your hair by taking one section at a time. Comb through the section and decide how much hair you want to cut. Remember that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so be mindful of how much you cut. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the hair straight across. Repeat this step on each section of your hair.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you have cut all the sections, blend the layers together by combing through your hair. Make sure there are no uneven areas, and the layers blend seamlessly.

Tips

  • Start by cutting less hair than you think you need. You can always cut more if needed, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Be cautious when cutting the front sections of your hair. These sections are more visible, and mistakes are more noticeable.
  • Consider going to a professional hairstylist if you're not confident in cutting your hair yourself.

FAQ

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 8-12 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

Will cutting my hair damage my curls?

No, cutting your hair will not damage your curls. In fact, it can help your curls look more defined and healthy.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, make sure to take care of it properly. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair. Apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls hydrated and defined. You can also use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to avoid frizz and preserve your curls' shape. Lastly, avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and cause split ends.

By following these guidelines, you can achieve a beautiful, layered look for your long curly hair. Remember to take your time and be patient when cutting your hair, and don't be afraid to seek professional help if needed. Happy cutting!
