How To Cut Classic Bob With Long Asymmetrical Fringe

Written by William Oct 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a new and trendy hairstyle, why not try a classic bob with a long asymmetrical fringe? This style is perfect for those who want to switch up their look and add a touch of edge to their hair. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of cutting a classic bob with long asymmetrical fringe, step by step. We'll also provide some tips on how to maintain this hairstyle and answer some frequently asked questions. Let's get started!

  • Step by Step

How to Cut Classic Bob with Long Asymmetrical Fringe

The first step in cutting a classic bob with a long asymmetrical fringe is to gather your tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a spray bottle filled with water. Once you have your tools ready, follow these steps:

Step 1: Divide the Hair

Start by dividing the hair into four sections: the top, left side, right side, and back. Use hair clips to secure the sections you're not working on.

Step 2: Cut the Back

Start with the back section of the hair. Comb the hair straight down and trim it to the desired length. Make sure to cut in a straight line across the back.

Step 3: Cut the Sides

Next, move on to the left and right sides of the hair. Comb the hair straight down and cut it to the desired length. Make sure to keep the length of the hair on both sides even.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Finally, move on to the top section of the hair. Comb the hair straight up and cut it to the desired length. You can choose to leave some length on the top or cut it short, depending on your preference.

Step 5: Cut the Fringe

Now it's time to cut the fringe. Start by dividing the hair into three sections: the left, middle, and right. Comb the hair straight down and cut it to the desired length on each side. Make sure to cut the hair at an angle to create the asymmetrical effect.

Step by Step

Here's a summary of the steps to cutting a classic bob with a long asymmetrical fringe:

  1. Divide the hair into four sections: top, left side, right side, and back.
  2. Cut the back section to the desired length.
  3. Cut the left and right sides to the desired length.
  4. Cut the top section to the desired length.
  5. Divide the fringe into three sections: left, middle, and right.
  6. Cut the fringe to the desired length on each side, at an angle to create the asymmetrical effect.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting a classic bob with a long asymmetrical fringe:

  • Start with clean, dry hair.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Take your time and cut small sections of hair at a time.
  • When cutting the fringe, make sure to cut at an angle to create the asymmetrical effect.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're not confident in your cutting skills.

FAQ

How long does this hairstyle last?

This hairstyle can last anywhere from four to six weeks, depending on how fast your hair grows.

Can this hairstyle be done on all hair types?

Yes, this hairstyle can be done on all hair types, but it may look slightly different depending on the texture of your hair.

What face shapes does this hairstyle suit?

This hairstyle is versatile and can suit many face shapes, but it's especially flattering on those with oval or heart-shaped faces.

Hair Care

To maintain this hairstyle, make sure to trim the ends every four to six weeks. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair healthy and moisturized. You can also use a leave-in conditioner to add extra moisture to the hair. Avoid using heat styling tools too often and always use a heat protectant spray before styling.

Now that you know how to cut a classic bob with a long asymmetrical fringe, it's time to try it out for yourself! With a little practice, you'll be able to master this trendy hairstyle and make it your own.
