Why Does The Hair On The Back Of My Head Stick Up

Written by Daniel Jul 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever looked in the mirror and noticed that the hair on the back of your head seems to have a mind of its own? No matter what you do, it just won't lay flat like the rest of your hair. This can be a frustrating and embarrassing problem, but it's actually quite common. In this article, we'll explore the reasons why the hair on the back of your head sticks up and what you can do about it.

Description

The hair on the back of your head may stick up for a variety of reasons. It could be due to genetics, the shape of your head, or the way your hair grows. It may also be caused by the products you use on your hair, the way you style it, or even your sleeping habits. Whatever the cause, it's important to understand why your hair is sticking up so that you can find a solution that works for you.

How To

If you're tired of dealing with unruly hair on the back of your head, there are a few things you can try to smooth it down. Here are some tips:

Use a Stronger Pomade or Gel

If you're using a light or medium hold product, it may not be strong enough to keep your hair down. Try switching to a stronger pomade or gel that has more hold. Be careful not to use too much, though, as this can make your hair look greasy.

Blow Dry Your Hair in the Opposite Direction

If your hair is sticking up because it's growing in a certain direction, you can try blow drying it in the opposite direction. This can help to smooth it down and train it to grow in a different direction.

Get a Haircut

If all else fails, you may need to get a haircut. A shorter style can help to control unruly hair and make it easier to manage. Talk to your stylist about the best cut for your hair type and face shape.

Step by Step

If you want to try blow drying your hair in the opposite direction, here are the steps to follow:

  1. Wash your hair and towel dry it until it's damp.
  2. Apply a heat protectant to your hair to prevent damage from the blow dryer.
  3. Use a round brush to blow dry your hair in the opposite direction of where it naturally falls. You can also use your fingers to help guide the hair in the right direction.
  4. Once your hair is dry, apply a small amount of pomade or gel to keep it in place.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when dealing with hair that sticks up:

  • Avoid brushing your hair too much, as this can make it frizzy and more difficult to manage.
  • Try sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent hair breakage and frizz.
  • Don't apply too much product to your hair, as this can weigh it down and make it look greasy.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair that sticks up:

Q: Is there a way to permanently fix hair that sticks up?

A: If your hair is sticking up due to genetics or the shape of your head, there may not be a permanent solution. However, you can try different styling techniques and products to manage it.

Q: Can using a flat iron help to smooth down hair that sticks up?

A: Yes, a flat iron can be a useful tool for smoothing down unruly hair. Just be sure to use a heat protectant and don't apply too much heat, as this can damage your hair.

Q: Will changing my shampoo or conditioner help to manage hair that sticks up?

A: It's possible that using a different shampoo or conditioner could help to manage your hair, especially if it's dry or damaged. Look for products that are designed for your hair type and avoid those that contain harsh chemicals.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for managing hair that sticks up. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it.
  • Get regular haircuts to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

By following these tips and techniques, you can manage hair that sticks up and achieve a smooth, polished look that you can be proud of.
