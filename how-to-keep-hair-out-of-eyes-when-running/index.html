How To Keep Hair Out Of Eyes When Running Tips And Tricks

Written by William Jul 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're a regular runner, you know how annoying it can be when your hair keeps getting in your eyes. Not only is it uncomfortable, but it can also be dangerous if it obstructs your vision. Fortunately, there are several ways to keep your hair out of your face when you're hitting the pavement. In this article, we'll give you some tips and tricks to help you keep your hair under control while you run.

How to Keep Hair Out of Eyes When Running: Tips and Tricks

1. Tie Your Hair Back

The most obvious way to keep your hair out of your eyes while running is to tie it back. Make sure to use a hair tie that is tight enough to keep your hair in place but not so tight that it causes a headache. You can also use bobby pins to secure any loose strands.

2. Wear a Headband or a Sweatband

If your hair is still falling in your face even when it's tied back, try wearing a headband or a sweatband. These accessories will keep your hair in place and absorb sweat at the same time. Choose a headband or a sweatband that is comfortable and fits snugly on your head.

3. Use Hair Clips or Hairpins

If you have bangs or shorter layers, you can use hair clips or hairpins to keep them out of your eyes. Make sure to choose clips or pins that are lightweight and won't fall out while you run.

4. Try a Ponytail Wrap or a Bun Wrap

If you have long hair, you can try a ponytail wrap or a bun wrap. These wraps will keep your hair in place and prevent it from bouncing around while you run. Make sure to choose a wrap that is comfortable and doesn't slide around on your head.

5. Use Hair Gel or Hair Spray

If all else fails, you can use hair gel or hair spray to keep your hair in place. Make sure to choose a product that is lightweight and won't leave your hair feeling stiff or sticky.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Keep Hair Out of Eyes When Running

Now that you know some tips and tricks to keep your hair out of your face while running, let's go through the step-by-step process of how to do it:

Step 1: Gather Your Hair

Gather your hair into a ponytail or a bun, depending on your preference.

Step 2: Tie Your Hair Back

Secure your hair with a hair tie or a wrap. Make sure it's tight enough to keep your hair in place but not so tight that it causes discomfort.

Step 3: Use Hair Clips or Hairpins

If you have any loose strands, use hair clips or hairpins to secure them in place.

Step 4: Wear a Headband or a Sweatband

If your hair is still falling in your face, wear a headband or a sweatband to keep it in place.

Step 5: Use Hair Gel or Hair Spray

If all else fails, use hair gel or hair spray to keep your hair in place.

Tips for Hair Care When Running

Now that you know how to keep your hair out of your face while running, let's talk about some tips for hair care:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Running can dry out your hair, so make sure to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. This will keep your hair healthy and shiny.

2. Don't Wash Your Hair Too Often

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils. Try to wash your hair every other day or every three days.

3. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Using a leave-in conditioner before you run can help protect your hair from sweat and the elements.

4. Wear a Hat or a Cap

If you're running outside, wear a hat or a cap to protect your hair from the sun and wind.

FAQs

Q: Can I use a hair tie to tie my hair back?

A: Yes, a hair tie is a great way to keep your hair in place while running. Make sure to choose a hair tie that is tight enough to keep your hair in place but not so tight that it causes discomfort.

Q: What type of headband or sweatband should I wear?

A: Choose a headband or a sweatband that is comfortable and fits snugly on your head. Make sure it's made of a material that is breathable and doesn't cause irritation.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: Try to wash your hair every other day or every three days. Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils.

Conclusion

Keeping your hair out of your face while running is essential for a comfortable and safe run. Try out different methods to see what works best for you, and don't forget to take care of your hair with moisturizing shampoos, leave-in conditioners, and protective hats or caps. Happy running!
