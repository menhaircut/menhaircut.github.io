How To Cut My Little Boy s Hair With Clippers

Written by Daniel Oct 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for an easy way to give your little boy a haircut, you may want to consider using clippers. Not only is it quick and efficient, but it's also a great way to save money on haircuts. In this article, we'll take you through the steps of cutting your little boy's hair with clippers, as well as some tips and tricks to make the process as smooth as possible.

Description

Cutting your little boy's hair with clippers is a great way to give him a fresh, clean look without having to spend a lot of money at the barbershop. With the right tools and a little bit of know-how, you can easily give your little one a stylish haircut from the comfort of your own home.

How to

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step in cutting your little boy's hair with clippers is to gather all of the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of clippers, scissors, a comb, a spray bottle filled with water, and a towel or cape to protect your child's clothes.

Step 2: Choose the Right Clippers

When it comes to choosing the right clippers, it's important to consider the size and type of blade. For little boys, it's best to use clippers with a smaller blade, as they are easier to maneuver around the ears and neck.

Step 3: Wash and Dry Your Child's Hair

Before you begin cutting, it's important to wash and dry your child's hair. This will help to remove any dirt or debris and make it easier to cut.

Step 4: Choose the Right Length

When using clippers, it's important to choose the right length guard. This will depend on the style you're going for, as well as your child's hair type and texture. It's always a good idea to start with a longer guard and work your way down, as you can always go shorter if needed.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Once you've chosen the right guard, it's time to start cutting. Begin at the nape of the neck and work your way up, using the clippers to gradually trim the hair. Be sure to use the comb to lift the hair and cut it evenly.

Step 6: Trim the Sides and Front

Once you've finished the back, it's time to move on to the sides and front. Use the clippers to trim the hair around the ears and neck, and then use the scissors to trim any longer strands on top.

Step 7: Finish with Scissors

Once you've finished using the clippers, it's time to finish off the haircut with scissors. Use the scissors to trim any remaining longer strands, and to give the hair a clean, polished look.

Tips

  • Always start with a longer guard and work your way down
  • Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it evenly
  • Be sure to trim around the ears and neck carefully
  • Use scissors to trim any remaining longer strands
  • Take breaks if your child becomes fidgety or restless

FAQ

  • Can I cut my little boy's hair without clippers?

    • Yes, you can cut your little boy's hair without clippers, but it may be more difficult and time-consuming.

  • What if I make a mistake?

    • If you make a mistake, don't panic! It's just hair and it will grow back. You can always take your child to a professional if needed.

  • How often should I cut my little boy's hair?

    • This will depend on your child's hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's a good idea to trim the hair every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking neat and tidy.

Hair Care

After you've finished cutting your little boy's hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Be sure to wash and condition the hair regularly, and use a wide-tooth comb to detangle any knots or tangles. You may also want to consider using a leave-in conditioner or styling product to keep the hair looking healthy and shiny.
