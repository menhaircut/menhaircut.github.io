Best Sunglass Shape For Face Type A Guide To Looking Cool And Confident

Written by Bardi Nov 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

When it comes to choosing sunglasses, it's not just about the style or color that catches your eye. The shape of the sunglasses is just as important, as it can either complement or clash with your face shape. In this guide, we'll help you find the best sunglass shape for your face type based on your features, so you can look and feel your best all year round.

Table of Contents

  • How to Determine Your Face Shape
  • Tips for Choosing the Right Sunglass Shape
  • FAQs about Sunglass Shapes and Face Types
  • Hair Care Tips for Sunglass Lovers

How to Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in finding the best sunglass shape for your face type is to determine your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. Here's how to figure out which one you have:

  • Oval: Your face is longer than it is wide, with a rounded jawline and forehead.
  • Round: Your face is almost as wide as it is long, with a soft, curved jawline and cheekbones.
  • Square: Your face is roughly the same length and width, with a strong, angular jawline and forehead.
  • Heart: Your face is widest at the forehead and tapers down to a narrow, pointed chin.
  • Diamond: Your face is widest at the cheekbones, with a narrow forehead and jawline.
  • Oblong: Your face is longer than it is wide, with a straight jawline and forehead.

Once you know your face shape, you can start looking for sunglasses that will flatter your features and enhance your natural beauty.

Tips for Choosing the Right Sunglass Shape

Now that you know your face shape, it's time to start shopping for sunglasses that will suit you best. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Oval: Almost any sunglass shape will work for you, but try to avoid frames that are too large or too small for your face.
  • Round: Look for angular or geometric frames that will add definition to your features. Square or rectangular shapes work well.
  • Square: Rounded or oval frames will soften your features and balance out your strong jawline. Aviators and cat-eye shapes are also flattering.
  • Heart: Look for frames that are wider at the bottom than at the top, such as aviators or cat-eye styles. Rimless or light-colored frames can also work well.
  • Diamond: Frames with exaggerated browlines or cat-eye shapes will balance out your features and highlight your cheekbones. Avoid narrow frames that will make your face look even longer.
  • Oblong: Look for frames that are wider than your face, such as oversized or round styles. Aviators and square frames can also work well.

Remember that these are just guidelines, and ultimately you should choose sunglasses that make you feel confident and stylish.

FAQs about Sunglass Shapes and Face Types

Q: Can I wear any sunglass shape if I have a round face?

A: While angular or geometric frames are generally more flattering for round faces, you can still wear any shape you like as long as it fits well and complements your personal style.

Q: What if I have a combination of face shapes?

A: It's not uncommon to have features of multiple face shapes. In this case, look for sunglasses that balance out your features and flatter your most prominent ones.

Q: How do I know if a sunglasses frame is too big or too small for my face?

A: If the frame extends beyond the width of your face or sits too high or low on your nose, it may be too large. If the frame is smaller than the width of your face or sits too close to your eyes, it may be too small.

Hair Care Tips for Sunglass Lovers

Wearing sunglasses can be a great way to protect your eyes and elevate your style, but it can also cause damage to your hair. Here are some tips to keep your locks healthy and happy while wearing sunglasses:

  • Avoid wearing sunglasses on top of your head, as this can create tension and breakage in your hair.
  • Choose sunglasses with adjustable nose pads to prevent them from sliding down your nose and pulling on your hair.
  • Invest in a high-quality hair serum or oil to protect your strands from sun damage and keep them moisturized and shiny.
  • Opt for low-maintenance hairstyles like braids or buns when wearing sunglasses for extended periods of time.

By following these tips and choosing the right sunglass shape for your face type, you can rock any look with confidence and style. Happy shopping!
