How To Low Fade Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to change up your hairstyle, a low fade might be just the thing you need. This popular haircut is characterized by a gradual taper of hair at the back and sides of the head, which creates a clean and polished look. In this guide, we'll take you through the process of getting a low fade haircut, step by step.

Description

A low fade is a type of haircut that involves cutting hair on the sides and back of the head shorter than the hair on top. This results in a tapered effect, with hair gradually getting shorter as it approaches the neckline. The fade can be low, medium, or high, depending on how high up the head the taper begins.

How to Low Fade Hair

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting a low fade haircut:

Step 1: Choose the Right Barber

It's important to choose a barber who has experience with fades. Look for a barber who specializes in men's haircuts and has a portfolio of fade haircuts they have done in the past.

Step 2: Decide on the Length and Style

Before getting your haircut, decide on the length and style you want. Do you want a short, buzzed look or a longer, textured cut? Bring pictures or examples of the style you want to show your barber.

Step 3: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair before going to the barber. This will make it easier for your barber to work with and will ensure that your hair is clean and free of any product buildup.

Step 4: Start with the Sides

Your barber will start by cutting the hair on the sides of your head. They will use clippers to gradually taper the hair, starting with a longer guard and gradually moving to a shorter guard.

Step 5: Blend the Top

Next, your barber will blend the top of your hair into the fade. They will use scissors or clippers to cut the hair on top to the desired length, and then use a comb or brush to blend it into the fade.

Step 6: Clean Up the Neckline

Finally, your barber will clean up the neckline and around the ears, using clippers or scissors to create a clean and polished look.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when getting a low fade haircut:

  • Communicate clearly with your barber about the length and style you want
  • Bring pictures or examples of the style you want to show your barber
  • Wash your hair before going to the barber
  • Visit a barber who specializes in men's haircuts and has experience with fades
  • Maintain your haircut with regular trims to keep it looking sharp

FAQ

What is the difference between a low fade and a high fade?

The difference between a low fade and a high fade is where the taper starts. A low fade starts just above the ears and tapers down to the neckline, while a high fade starts at the temples and tapers up to the crown of the head.

How often should I get a low fade haircut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain the look. As a general rule, you should get a trim every 4-6 weeks to keep your haircut looking fresh.

Hair Care

To keep your low fade looking sharp, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized, and avoid using harsh products that can dry out your scalp.

Regular trims are also important to maintain the shape of your low fade. Visit your barber every 4-6 weeks for a trim, and communicate any changes you want to make to your haircut. With proper care and maintenance, your low fade can look sharp and stylish for weeks to come.
