How To Cut Your Own Bangs At Home For Straight Hair

Written by Robby Nov 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending money at the salon just to get your bangs trimmed? Or maybe you don't have the time to go to the salon and need a quick trim. Well, you're in luck because cutting your own bangs at home is easier than you think. In this article, we'll go over everything you need to know about cutting your own bangs for straight hair.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Before we dive into the actual process of cutting your own bangs, let's first talk about the benefits of doing it yourself. The obvious benefit is saving money and time by not having to go to the salon. However, cutting your own bangs also gives you complete control over the length and style of your bangs. Plus, it's a fun and empowering experience to do it yourself.

How to

The first step in cutting your own bangs is to gather the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. It's also a good idea to have a mirror and some clips to section off your hair.

Once you have your tools, it's time to start cutting. Begin by wetting your bangs with the spray bottle. This will make it easier to cut and will help you see the length more clearly.

Step by Step

  1. Start by combing your bangs straight down.
  2. Use your fingers to section off the amount of hair you want to cut.
  3. Hold the hair between your fingers and use the scissors to cut straight across.
  4. Repeat this process until you've achieved your desired length.
  5. Check your work in the mirror and make any necessary adjustments.

It's important to remember to cut your bangs a little bit longer than you actually want them. This will give you some wiggle room in case you make a mistake. You can always go back and cut more, but you can't add hair back once it's been cut.

Tips

Here are a few extra tips to keep in mind when cutting your own bangs:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to longer ones.
  • Use the comb and your fingers to keep your bangs straight while cutting.
  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to take your time and make small cuts.
  • If you have thick hair, it may be easier to thin out your bangs before cutting them.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own bangs:

Can I cut my bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can still cut your own bangs if you have curly hair. However, it may be a bit more difficult to get a straight cut. You may want to consider straightening your hair before cutting your bangs.

How often should I trim my bangs?

It's recommended to trim your bangs every 6-8 weeks to maintain the length and style.

Hair Care

After cutting your own bangs, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking fresh. Here are some tips:

  • Use a small amount of hair oil or serum to keep your bangs smooth and shiny.
  • Don't wash your hair too often, as this can cause your bangs to become greasy and lose their shape.
  • Use a dry shampoo in between washes to keep your hair looking fresh.

Now that you know how to cut your own bangs at home for straight hair, you can save time and money while also having complete control over your hair. Just remember to take it slow and steady, and don't be afraid to make small cuts. Happy cutting!
