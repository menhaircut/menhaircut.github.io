How To Make All My Hair Grey A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your natural hair color and looking for a bold new look? Perhaps you've been inspired by the trend of grey hair and want to give it a try. Whatever your reason, making all of your hair grey is a process that requires some preparation and know-how. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve this look and answer some common questions about maintaining grey hair.

Table of Contents

  1. Preparation
  2. Bleaching
  3. Toning
  4. Dyeing
  5. Hair Care
  6. FAQ

Preparation

Before you start the process of making your hair grey, there are a few things you'll need to do to prepare. First, make sure your hair is in good condition. If it's damaged or already colored, you may need to wait before attempting this process. Also, be sure to get the right supplies. You'll need:

  • Bleach powder
  • Developer
  • Toner
  • Semi-permanent grey hair dye
  • Latex gloves
  • Old towels or clothing to protect your skin and clothes

Once you have all your supplies, it's time to start the process.

Bleaching

The first step in making your hair grey is to lighten it with bleach. This is necessary because grey hair dye won't show up on darker hair. Here's how to do it:

  1. Put on your gloves and old clothing or towels to protect your skin and clothes.
  2. Mix the bleach powder and developer according to the instructions on the package.
  3. Apply the bleach mixture to your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. Be sure to saturate all of your hair.
  4. Leave the bleach on for the recommended amount of time, usually 30-45 minutes.
  5. Rinse out the bleach thoroughly with cool water.

After bleaching, your hair will likely be a brassy yellow color. This is where the toner comes in.

Toning

Toner is a product that helps to neutralize unwanted tones in your hair, like yellow or orange. To make your hair grey, you'll need to use a toner with a blue or purple base. Here's how to do it:

  1. Mix the toner according to the instructions on the package.
  2. Apply the toner to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Be sure to saturate all of your hair.
  3. Leave the toner on for the recommended amount of time, usually 10-20 minutes.
  4. Rinse out the toner thoroughly with cool water.

After toning, your hair should be a light, silvery color. Now it's time to dye it grey.

Dyeing

For the final step, you'll need to use a semi-permanent grey hair dye. Semi-permanent dye will fade over time, so you won't have to worry about roots showing up as your hair grows. Here's how to do it:

  1. Mix the dye according to the instructions on the package.
  2. Apply the dye to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Be sure to saturate all of your hair.
  3. Leave the dye on for the recommended amount of time, usually 20-30 minutes.
  4. Rinse out the dye thoroughly with cool water.

After dyeing, your hair should be a beautiful shade of grey. However, maintaining grey hair requires some special care.

Hair Care

Grey hair can be more fragile and prone to breakage than other hair colors. To keep your grey hair looking its best, follow these tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness.
  • Avoid heat styling as much as possible, as it can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Use a purple shampoo and conditioner once a week to prevent yellowing or brassiness.
  • Avoid chlorine and salt water, as they can cause your hair to turn green.

FAQ

Here are some common questions about making your hair grey:

Q: Can I make my hair grey without bleaching it?

A: Unfortunately, no. Grey hair dye won't show up on darker hair, so bleaching is necessary to lighten your hair enough for the dye to take.

Q: How often will I need to touch up my grey hair?

A: Semi-permanent dye will fade over time, so you'll need to touch up your roots every 4-6 weeks.

Q: Can I dye my hair grey at home?

A: Yes, it's possible to dye your hair grey at home. Just be sure to follow the instructions carefully and take the necessary precautions to protect your skin and clothing.

Now that you know how to make all of your hair grey, you're ready to rock this bold new look. Just be sure to take good care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
