How To Play Sports With Short Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Oct 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Sports and physical activities are an essential part of leading a healthy lifestyle. However, for those with short hair, it can be challenging to keep your hair out of your face while playing. Don't worry; we've got you covered with some handy tips and tricks to make playing sports with short hair much more manageable.

How to:

1. Tie Your Hair Back

If you have enough hair length, you can tie it back in a ponytail or bun. Make sure to use a hairband or scrunchie that won't slip or fall out during your activity. This is a simple and practical solution that will keep your hair out of your face.

2. Use Headbands or Hair Clips

If you have shorter hair, you can use headbands or hair clips to keep your hair out of your face. These accessories can be found in different colors and styles, so you can choose one that matches your outfit or style.

3. Wear a Hat

If you're playing outdoor sports and the sun is shining bright, you can wear a baseball cap or a visor. Not only will it keep your hair out of your face, but it will also protect your face and eyes from the sun's harmful rays.

4. Use Hair Gel

If your hair is too short to tie back or clip, you can use hair gel to keep it in place. Gel can help keep your hair in place while you're playing sports or doing physical activities.

5. Get a Shorter Haircut

If you're an avid sports player, you can consider getting a shorter haircut that's easier to manage during physical activities. A shorter haircut will not only keep your hair out of your face, but it will also make your hair easier to wash and style.

Step by Step:

Step 1: Brush or Comb Your Hair

Before tying your hair back or using any accessories, make sure to brush or comb your hair to avoid any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Tie Your Hair Back (if applicable)

If you have enough hair length, tie your hair back in a ponytail or bun. Use a hairband or scrunchie to secure it in place.

Step 3: Use Headbands or Hair Clips

If you have shorter hair, use headbands or hair clips to keep your hair out of your face. Place them strategically to keep your hair secure.

Step 4: Wear a Hat

If you're playing outdoor sports, wear a baseball cap or visor to keep your hair out of your face and protect your face from the sun.

Step 5: Use Hair Gel

If your hair is too short to tie back or clip, use hair gel to keep it in place. Apply a small amount to your hair and style it as desired.

Tips:

- Use hair accessories that match your outfit or style.
- Use hair gel that's specifically designed for sports and physical activities.
- Consider getting a shorter haircut if you're an avid sports player.
- Use a hairband or scrunchie that won't slip or fall out during your activity.
- Tie your hair back tightly to avoid it from falling out during your activity.

FAQ:

Q: Can I use hair gel for all hair types?

A: No, hair gel is specifically designed for certain hair types, so make sure to choose a product that's suitable for your hair type.

Q: Can I wear a hat during indoor sports?

A: It's not necessary to wear a hat during indoor sports, but if you prefer to wear one, make sure it's not against any rules or regulations.

Hair Care:

Playing sports can take a toll on your hair, so it's essential to take care of it properly. After playing, make sure to wash your hair thoroughly to remove any sweat or dirt buildup. Apply a conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and moisturized. Avoid using hair products that contain harsh chemicals or alcohol, as they can damage your hair over time.

Playing sports with short hair doesn't have to be difficult. With these tips and tricks, you can keep your hair out of your face and focus on the game. Remember to take care of your hair properly to keep it healthy and strong.
