How To Tease Hair 60S Style A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jun 06, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're looking to add some volume and retro appeal to your hair, teasing is the way to go. And what better style to tease your hair than the iconic 60s look? In this article, we'll show you how to tease your hair 60s style in relaxed English language. From the tools you'll need to the step-by-step process, we've got you covered. So let's get started!

Table of Contents

  1. Tools You'll Need
  2. Preparation
  3. Teasing
  4. Styling
  5. Tips for Teasing Hair
  6. FAQs
  7. Hair Care After Teasing

Tools You'll Need

Before we get started, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You'll need:

  • Teasing comb
  • Hairspray
  • Bobby pins or hair clips

Optional:

  • Dry shampoo
  • Hair oil or serum

Preparation

Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair tends to be oily, use dry shampoo to soak up any excess oil. Apply hair oil or serum to the ends of your hair to prevent breakage.

Next, part your hair down the middle and divide it into two sections. Clip away one section and start with the other.

Teasing

Take a small section of hair at the crown of your head and hold it straight up. Using your teasing comb, backcomb the hair towards your scalp. Repeat this process with small sections of hair, working your way towards the back of your head.

Once you've teased the back of your head, move on to the sides. Take small sections of hair and tease them towards the back of your head, following the same technique as before.

When you're done teasing, use your comb to smooth the top layer of your hair. This will give you a polished look while still maintaining the volume you've created.

Styling

Now it's time to style your hair. You can leave it down for a more casual look or create a bouffant for a more formal look.

To create a bouffant, take the top section of your hair and gently pull it back. Pin it in place with bobby pins or hair clips. Use your teasing comb to gently smooth the sides of your hair and create a rounded shape. Finish with a generous amount of hairspray.

Tips for Teasing Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when teasing your hair:

  • Start with a small section of hair and work your way up. This will prevent tangling and breakage.
  • Use hairspray to hold your tease in place.
  • Don't over-tease your hair. Too much teasing can lead to breakage and damage.

FAQs

Q: How long will my tease last?

A: Your tease should last until you wash your hair. Make sure to use a gentle shampoo to prevent further damage.

Q: Can I tease my hair if it's thin?

A: Yes, but be gentle. Thin hair is more prone to breakage, so use a light hand when teasing.

Hair Care After Teasing

After teasing your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to prevent damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to prevent further damage.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it healthy.

And there you have it - a step-by-step guide on how to tease your hair 60s style. Whether you're going for a retro look or just want some extra volume, teasing is a fun and easy way to achieve the look you want. So go ahead, try it out and let us know how it goes!
