Why Does Your Hair Turn Gray When We Get Older

Written by Daniel Jul 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As we age, our hair changes in many ways. One of the most noticeable changes is the development of gray hair. While gray hair is often associated with aging, it can actually start appearing at any age. So why does your hair turn gray when we get older?

What is Melanin?

Melanin is the pigment that gives our skin, hair, and eyes their color. It is produced by cells called melanocytes. The amount and type of melanin in our body is determined by our genetics. There are two types of melanin that determine hair color: eumelanin, which is responsible for brown and black hair, and pheomelanin, which is responsible for red and blonde hair.

What Causes Gray Hair?

Gray hair occurs when the melanocytes in our hair follicles stop producing melanin. As a result, new hair that grows in is no longer colored, but instead appears gray or white. While aging is the most common cause of gray hair, there are other factors that can contribute to its development.

Genetics and Gray Hair

Genetics play a big role in determining when we start to go gray. If your parents or grandparents went gray early, it's likely that you will too. The age at which we start going gray is largely determined by our genes.

Stress and Gray Hair

While stress is not a direct cause of gray hair, it can contribute to its development. Stress can cause the body to produce more free radicals, which can damage cells, including melanocytes. This can lead to premature graying.

Vitamins and Gray Hair

There is some evidence to suggest that certain vitamins may help prevent or slow down the development of gray hair. Vitamin B12, for example, is important for melanin production and may help delay the onset of gray hair. Other vitamins, such as vitamin D and vitamin E, may also be beneficial.

Treatment Options for Gray Hair

While there's no way to reverse gray hair, there are several treatment options available to help cover it up. The most common treatment is hair dye, which can be applied to the hair to restore its color. There are also temporary options, such as hair mascaras and sprays, that can be used to cover up gray hair.

Hair Care Tips for Gray Hair

Gray hair can be more fragile and prone to dryness than other types of hair. To keep your gray hair looking its best, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are designed for gray hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as curling irons and straighteners, which can damage hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep hair moisturized.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protective spray.
