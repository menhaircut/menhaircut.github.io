Is There A Way To Not Lose Your Hair During Chemo

Written by Bardi Jul 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Dealing with cancer can be a daunting task, and undergoing chemotherapy is one of the most challenging parts of the journey. One of the most common side effects of chemotherapy is hair loss. Losing your hair can be devastating, and it can take a toll on your emotional well-being. But is there a way to not lose your hair during chemo? In this blog post, we will explore some techniques that may help you keep your hair during chemotherapy.

Description

Hair loss is a common side effect of chemotherapy. Chemotherapy drugs target rapidly growing cells, including cancer cells, but also hair follicles. As a result, hair loss occurs. Hair loss can affect the scalp, eyebrows, eyelashes, and other body hair. The amount of hair loss depends on the type and dosage of chemotherapy drugs you receive. Some people may lose all of their hair, while others may only experience thinning.

How to

If you are wondering whether there is a way to not lose your hair during chemo, the answer is yes. Here are some techniques that may help:

Scalp Cooling

Scalp cooling is a technique that can help reduce the amount of chemotherapy drugs that reach the hair follicles. It involves wearing a cooling cap before, during, and after chemotherapy treatment. The cold temperature can cause the blood vessels in the scalp to constrict, which reduces the amount of chemotherapy drugs that reach the hair follicles.

Chemotherapy Timing

Some chemotherapy drugs can cause more hair loss than others. You can work with your medical team to determine the best time to undergo chemotherapy treatment. Depending on your cancer type and stage, your doctor may recommend a different approach to chemotherapy, which may result in less hair loss.

Low-Level Laser Therapy

Low-level laser therapy is a non-invasive technique that uses red light to stimulate hair growth. This technique may help reduce hair loss during chemotherapy. The light energy stimulates the hair follicles, which can result in thicker, healthier hair growth.

Step by Step

Here are some step-by-step instructions on how to use scalp cooling:

  1. Wash your hair with a mild shampoo before treatment.
  2. Apply the cooling cap to your head.
  3. Secure the cap with straps or adhesive tape.
  4. Start the cooling process before chemotherapy begins.
  5. Keep the cap on during chemotherapy treatment.
  6. Continue to wear the cap for several hours after chemotherapy treatment.
  7. Remove the cap and wash your hair again.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you prevent hair loss during chemotherapy:

  • Avoid harsh hair treatments, such as bleaching or coloring.
  • Use a mild shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as curling irons or straighteners.
  • Wear a hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun.
  • Consider using a wig or hairpiece.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair loss during chemotherapy:

Is hair loss permanent?

Hair loss during chemotherapy is usually temporary. Your hair will grow back after treatment ends, although it may be a different texture or color.

How long does it take for hair to grow back?

It can take several months for hair to grow back after chemotherapy treatment ends. The growth rate varies from person to person.

Can I dye my hair after chemotherapy?

You should wait until your hair has fully grown back before dyeing it. Your hair may be more fragile and delicate after chemotherapy, so it's best to wait until it's healthy.

Hair Care

If you do experience hair loss during chemotherapy, it's essential to take care of your scalp and any remaining hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a mild shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools.
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair.
  • Consider using a soft-bristled brush or wide-tooth comb.
  • Protect your scalp from the sun by wearing a hat or scarf.

While hair loss during chemotherapy is a challenging experience, there are techniques you can use to help prevent or reduce it. Talk to your medical team about your options and find the approach that works best for you.
