How To Fix Bangs Cut Too Short A Guide For Hair Care In 2023

Written by Christine Jun 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you dealing with bangs that were cut too short? Don't worry, it happens to the best of us. Whether it was a DIY haircut or a trip to the salon gone wrong, there are ways to fix your bangs and get them back to a length that you love. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to fix bangs cut too short, as well as some tips and hair care advice to keep your bangs looking great.

Table of Contents

  • How to Fix Bangs Cut Too Short
  • Step-by-Step Guide

  • FAQ

How to Fix Bangs Cut Too Short

If your bangs are too short, there are a few different ways to fix them depending on how short they are and what style you're going for. Here are some options:

  • Grow them out: This is the most obvious solution, but it can take some time. In the meantime, you can use bobby pins or headbands to keep your bangs out of your face.
  • Add layers: If your bangs are only slightly too short, adding some layers can help blend them in with the rest of your hair. This works especially well if you have wavy or curly hair.
  • Get a trim: If your bangs are really short and you don't want to wait for them to grow out, getting a trim can help. A hairstylist can blend your bangs in with the rest of your hair or give you a new style that works with your shorter bangs.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you decide to try fixing your bangs at home, here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Wash and dry your hair: Start with clean, dry hair so you can see exactly how short your bangs are.
  2. Section off your bangs: Use a comb to section off your bangs from the rest of your hair.
  3. Assess the length: Determine how much shorter your bangs are than you wanted them to be.
  4. Trim the ends: Using sharp scissors, trim the very ends of your bangs to even them out.
  5. Add layers: If your bangs are still too short, consider adding some layers to blend them in with the rest of your hair.
  6. Style your bangs: Once you're happy with the length, style your bangs as desired.

Tips for Fixing Short Bangs

Here are some tips to keep in mind when fixing short bangs:

  • Take it slow: Don't try to fix your bangs all at once. Take small steps and assess the length as you go.
  • Use sharp scissors: Dull scissors can make the problem worse by creating uneven ends.
  • Consider your face shape: Different bang styles work better with different face shapes, so consider what will flatter your features.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help: If you're not confident in your ability to fix your bangs, ask a friend or hairstylist for help.

FAQ

Can I fix my bangs if they're too short?

Yes, there are ways to fix bangs that are too short. You can try growing them out, adding layers, or getting a trim from a hairstylist.

How long does it take for bangs to grow out?

It can take several weeks to several months for bangs to grow out, depending on how short they are and how fast your hair grows.

What should I do if I'm not confident in my ability to fix my bangs?

If you're not confident in your ability to fix your bangs, it's best to ask a friend or hairstylist for help.

Hair Care Advice for Bangs

To keep your bangs looking great, try these hair care tips:

  • Wash your bangs frequently: Bangs can get oily quickly, so wash them every other day or as needed.
  • Blow dry your bangs: Use a round brush and blow dryer to dry your bangs in the direction you want them to go.
  • Use a heat protectant: If you use hot tools on your bangs, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.
  • Trim your bangs regularly: To keep your bangs looking their best, get them trimmed every 4-6 weeks.

With these tips and tricks, you can fix your short bangs and keep them looking great. Remember to take it slow, use sharp scissors, and consider your face shape to get the best possible results. Happy styling!
