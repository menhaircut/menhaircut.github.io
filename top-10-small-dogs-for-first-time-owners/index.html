Top 10 Small Dogs For First Time Owners

Written by Frank Oct 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Welcome to our guide on the top 10 small dogs for first time owners! If you're looking to add a furry friend to your family, a small dog can be a great option. They're often easier to take care of than larger breeds, and they're great for apartment living. In this guide, we'll introduce you to our top 10 small dog breeds for first time owners, and offer some tips for taking care of your new pup.

1. Chihuahua

The Chihuahua is one of the smallest dog breeds and is perfect for first time owners. They're loyal, affectionate, and easy to train. They're also great for apartment living and don't require a lot of exercise. However, they can be prone to dental problems, so make sure to take care of their teeth.

2. Pomeranian

The Pomeranian is a fluffy little dog that is full of personality. They're playful, intelligent, and love attention. They're also great for apartment living and don't require a lot of exercise. However, they can be prone to shedding, so make sure to brush them regularly.

3. Pug

The Pug is a small, stocky dog that is known for their wrinkly face and curly tail. They're easygoing, friendly, and love to snuggle. They're also great for apartment living and don't require a lot of exercise. However, they can be prone to obesity, so make sure to monitor their diet.

4. Shih Tzu

The Shih Tzu is a small, fluffy dog that is known for their long, luxurious coat. They're affectionate, loyal, and great with kids. They're also great for apartment living and don't require a lot of exercise. However, their coat requires regular grooming to prevent matting.

5. Dachshund

The Dachshund, or "wiener dog," is a small dog with a long body and short legs. They're intelligent, playful, and love to cuddle. They're also great for apartment living, but they do require regular exercise to prevent obesity.

6. Yorkshire Terrier

The Yorkshire Terrier, or "Yorkie," is a small dog with a big personality. They're loyal, affectionate, and easy to train. They're also great for apartment living, but their long coat requires regular grooming to prevent matting.

7. Bichon Frise

The Bichon Frise is a small, fluffy dog that is known for their cheerful disposition. They're playful, affectionate, and great with kids. They're also great for apartment living, but their coat requires regular grooming to prevent matting.

8. Maltese

The Maltese is a small, white dog that is known for their silky coat. They're affectionate, intelligent, and easy to train. They're also great for apartment living, but their coat requires regular grooming to prevent matting.

9. Cavalier King Charles Spaniel

The Cavalier King Charles Spaniel is a small dog with a big heart. They're affectionate, loyal, and great with kids. They're also great for apartment living, but they do require regular exercise to prevent obesity.

10. French Bulldog

The French Bulldog is a small, muscular dog that is known for their "bat ears" and wrinkly face. They're affectionate, playful, and easy to train. They're also great for apartment living, but they can be prone to respiratory problems due to their short snouts.

FAQ

What should I consider before getting a small dog?

Before getting a small dog, consider your lifestyle and living situation. Small dogs are great for apartment living, but they still need exercise and attention. Make sure you have the time and resources to take care of a dog before getting one.

Do small dogs require less exercise than larger dogs?

Yes, small dogs generally require less exercise than larger dogs, but they still need daily walks and playtime to stay healthy and happy.

What are some common health problems in small dogs?

Some common health problems in small dogs include dental problems, obesity, respiratory problems, and joint problems.

Hair Care

If you're getting a small dog with a long, fluffy coat, it's important to take care of their hair. Brush their hair regularly to prevent matting, and consider taking them to a professional groomer for regular haircuts. Use a dog-specific shampoo and conditioner to keep their coat healthy and smelling fresh.
