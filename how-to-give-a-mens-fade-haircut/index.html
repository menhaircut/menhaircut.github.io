How To Give A Men s Fade Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 27, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're looking to give a men's fade haircut, you're in the right place. Here, we'll break down the steps involved in giving a fade haircut, including what tools you'll need, how to choose the right length for your fade, and how to fade hair on the sides and back of the head.

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, you'll need a few tools:

  • Clippers
  • Guard attachments
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair gel or pomade

How to Choose the Right Length for Your Fade

The first step in giving a fade haircut is choosing the right length for the fade. This will depend on how short your client wants their hair on the sides and back.

Generally, you'll want to start with a longer guard attachment and work your way down to a shorter one. For example, you might start with a #4 guard attachment and work your way down to a #1 or #0.5.

How to Fade Hair on the Sides and Back of the Head

Now that you've chosen the right length for your fade, it's time to get to work. Here's how to fade hair on the sides and back of the head:

  1. Start with the longest guard attachment and trim the hair around the sides and back of the head.
  2. Switch to a shorter guard attachment and trim the hair from the top of the ear to the temple.
  3. Switch to an even shorter guard attachment and trim the hair from the temple to the hairline.
  4. Repeat steps 2 and 3 with an even shorter guard attachment until you reach the desired length.
  5. Blend the hair at the top of the fade with scissors or clippers, depending on the client's preference.
  6. Use a comb to blend the fade and the longer hair at the top of the head.
  7. Finish with hair gel or pomade to style the hair.

Tips for Giving a Men's Fade Haircut

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when giving a men's fade haircut:

  • Take your time and be patient.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress.
  • Blend the hair gradually to avoid any harsh lines.
  • Be mindful of your client's hair texture and adjust your technique accordingly.

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about giving a men's fade haircut:

How long does a fade haircut last?

A fade haircut typically lasts 2-3 weeks, depending on how fast the client's hair grows.

What's the difference between a fade and a taper?

A fade is a gradual change in hair length from the shortest point to the longest point, while a taper is a more abrupt change in hair length.

How often should I get a fade haircut?

It's up to you and your personal preference. Some people prefer to get a fade haircut every 2-3 weeks, while others might wait longer.

Hair Care Tips

After giving a fade haircut, it's important to take care of your client's hair. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair healthy.
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling the hair.
  • Use hair gel or pomade sparingly to avoid buildup.

With these tips and techniques, you can give a great men's fade haircut that your clients will love. Remember to take your time, be patient, and adjust your technique based on your client's hair texture and personal preferences. Happy cutting!
