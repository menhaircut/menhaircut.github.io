How To Cut Wavy Hair In Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 21, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Wavy hair can be tricky to cut, especially if you want to add layers. But with the right technique and tools, anyone can achieve a stunning layered haircut for their wavy locks. In this article, we'll show you how to cut wavy hair in layers, from start to finish.

  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Wavy Hair in Layers
  • FAQs

How to Cut Wavy Hair in Layers

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's go over the basic tools you'll need to cut wavy hair in layers:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Clips or hair ties

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Once you've rinsed out the conditioner, gently towel-dry your hair until it's damp. Do not rub your hair vigorously with a towel, as this can cause damage and frizz.

Next, let your hair air-dry until it's about 80% dry. You can also use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair without causing too much frizz.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Use your wide-tooth comb to divide your hair into four sections: two front sections and two back sections. Clip or tie each section to keep them separate.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start with one of the back sections. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your index and middle fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting at the bottom and working your way up. Be sure to cut at an angle to create a layered effect.

Repeat this process with each section of hair, working your way up to the crown of your head. Once you've finished the back sections, move on to the front sections.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all of your hair into layers, use your scissors to blend the layers together. Take small sections of hair and cut them at a slight angle to create a seamless transition between the different layers.

Step 5: Check Your Work

After you've finished cutting your hair, take a look in the mirror to make sure everything looks even. If you notice any uneven sections, go back and trim them until everything looks balanced.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide for cutting wavy hair in layers:

  1. Wash and towel-dry your hair until it's damp.
  2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles.
  3. Divide your hair into four sections: two front sections and two back sections.
  4. Clip or tie each section to keep them separate.
  5. Start with one of the back sections. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your index and middle fingers.
  6. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting at the bottom and working your way up.
  7. Repeat this process with each section of hair, working your way up to the crown of your head.
  8. Once you've finished the back sections, move on to the front sections.
  9. Blend the layers together by taking small sections of hair and cutting them at a slight angle.
  10. Check your work in the mirror to make sure everything looks even.

Tips for Cutting Wavy Hair in Layers

Here are some helpful tips to keep in mind when cutting wavy hair in layers:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use sharp scissors to make clean cuts.
  • Cut at an angle to create a layered effect.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can lead to uneven layers.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting hair in layers can be time-consuming, but the end result is worth it.

FAQs

How often should I cut my wavy hair?

It's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my own wavy hair in layers?

While it's possible to cut your own hair in layers, it can be difficult to achieve a professional-looking result. It's best to have a professional stylist cut your hair if you're looking for a more polished look.

What should I do if I accidentally cut too much hair?

If you accidentally cut too much hair, don't panic. You can always go back and trim the other sections to even everything out. If you're really unhappy with the results, you can always visit a professional stylist to fix the issue.

Hair Care Tips for Wavy Hair

Here are some tips to keep your wavy hair looking healthy and beautiful:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead.
  • Avoid using heat tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.

That's it! With this guide, you should be able to cut your wavy hair in layers like a pro. Remember to take your time, use sharp scissors, and be patient. Good luck!
