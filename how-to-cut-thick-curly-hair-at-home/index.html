How To Cut Thick Curly Hair At Home A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the salon to maintain your thick curly hair? Well, with the right tools and techniques, you can easily cut your own hair at home! In this comprehensive guide, we'll take you through everything you need to know to cut your thick curly hair at home. From tools to technique, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • Step-by-Step Guide

Tools You'll Need

Before we dive into the technique, let's talk about the tools you'll need to cut your thick curly hair at home:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Flat iron (optional)

How to Cut Thick Curly Hair at Home

Cutting thick curly hair at home may seem daunting, but with the right technique, it's actually quite simple. Here's what you need to do:

Step 1: Wash and Detangle Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, wash and detangle it thoroughly. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that your cut is even. Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair from the ends to the roots.


Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Next, divide your hair into sections using hair ties or clips. Start by dividing your hair in half, from ear to ear. Then, divide each half into two or three sections, depending on the thickness of your hair.


Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you've divided your hair into sections, you're ready to start cutting. Follow these steps:

Step 1: Start with the Bottom Section

Start with the bottom section of your hair. Take a small section of hair, about half an inch wide, and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and use your other hand to cut the ends off. Start with a small amount and work your way up, checking the length as you go.

Step 2: Move on to the Next Section

Once you've finished the bottom section, move on to the next section, working your way up your head. Use the bottom section as a guide for the length of the next section.

Step 3: Cut the Crown Section

The crown section is the top of your head. To cut this section, comb the hair straight up and cut the ends off, using the bottom sections as a guide for length.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the sections, use your scissors to blend the layers together. Take small sections of hair and cut into the ends at a slight angle, working your way around your head until the layers are blended.

Step 5: Trim the Bangs (Optional)

If you have bangs, trim them to your desired length. Comb your bangs straight down and cut them straight across, using the bridge of your nose as a guide for length.

Step 6: Use Clippers (Optional)

If you want an even shorter cut, you can use clippers. Use a guard attachment to achieve your desired length and run the clippers over your hair, starting at the nape of your neck and working your way up.


Tips for Cutting Thick Curly Hair

Cutting thick curly hair can be challenging, but these tips will help you achieve the perfect cut:

  • Always cut your hair when it's dry, as curly hair can shrink when it dries.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head as you cut.
  • Don't cut too much off at once. It's better to start with a little and work your way up.
  • Invest in a good pair of scissors. Sharp scissors will give you a clean cut and prevent split ends.

FAQs

Can I cut my curly hair when it's wet?

No, it's best to cut curly hair when it's dry. When curly hair dries, it can shrink up to an inch, so cutting it when it's wet can result in a shorter cut than you intended.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It's recommended to cut curly hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.

Do I need to use clippers to cut my thick curly hair?

No, clippers are optional. If you want a shorter cut, you can use clippers with a guard attachment to achieve your desired length.


Hair Care Tips for Thick Curly Hair

Now that you know how to cut your thick curly hair at home, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some hair care tips for thick curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.

Cutting your thick curly hair at home is a great way to save money and maintain your own personal style. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy cut in the comfort of your own home.
