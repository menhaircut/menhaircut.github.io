How To Make Your Fringe Look Nice Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Jul 25, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of your fringe looking messy and unruly? Don't worry, you're not alone. Fringes can be tricky to manage, but with the right techniques and products, you can get them looking sleek and stylish. In this article, we'll be sharing our top tips and tricks for making your fringe look nice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Make Your Fringe Look Nice
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Fringe
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Make Your Fringe Look Nice

The first step to making your fringe look nice is to have it cut properly by a professional hairdresser. A good haircut will ensure that your fringe is the right length and shape for your face. After that, you can follow these tips:

1. Blow-dry Your Fringe

Blow-drying your fringe is a great way to get it looking sleek and straight. Use a round brush to guide your hair as you blow-dry, and aim the nozzle of your hairdryer downwards to smooth out any kinks.

2. Use a Straightening Iron

If your fringe is particularly unruly, you can use a straightening iron to get it looking sleek. Make sure to use a heat protectant spray first to prevent damage to your hair.

3. Style Your Fringe to the Side

If you're struggling to get your fringe to sit flat, try styling it to the side instead. This can give you a more relaxed and natural look, and it's also a great way to hide any imperfections in your cut.

4. Don't Overdo It

Remember, less is more when it comes to styling your fringe. Avoid using too much product or heat, as this can damage your hair and make your fringe look greasy or limp.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting your fringe looking nice:

1. Shampoo and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. This will clean your hair and remove any product buildup.

2. Towel-Dry Your Hair

Gently towel-dry your hair, taking care not to rub it vigorously. This can cause damage and frizz.

3. Apply Heat Protectant

If you're planning to use a blow-dryer or straightening iron, apply a heat protectant spray to your hair. This will help to prevent damage and breakage.

4. Blow-Dry Your Fringe

Using a round brush, blow-dry your fringe, aiming the nozzle of your hairdryer downwards. This will smooth out any kinks and give you a sleek finish.

5. Straighten Your Fringe (Optional)

If your fringe is particularly unruly, you can use a straightening iron to get it looking sleek. Make sure to use a heat protectant spray first to prevent damage to your hair.

6. Style Your Fringe

Once your fringe is dry and straightened, you can style it to your liking. Whether you prefer it straight and sleek or swept to the side, make sure to use a light touch and avoid overdoing it.

Tips for Maintaining Your Fringe

Once you've got your fringe looking nice, you'll want to keep it that way. Here are some tips for maintaining your fringe:

1. Trim It Regularly

To keep your fringe looking neat and tidy, you'll need to trim it regularly. This will prevent it from growing too long and getting in your eyes.

2. Use a Dry Shampoo

If your fringe starts to look greasy between washes, try using a dry shampoo to refresh it. This will absorb excess oil and give you a clean, fresh look.

3. Don't Touch It Too Much

Avoid touching your fringe too much throughout the day, as this can transfer oils from your fingers onto your hair and make it look greasy.

FAQ

What if I have curly hair?

If you have curly hair, blow-drying and straightening your fringe may not be an option. Instead, try using a curl-defining product to enhance your natural curls and give your fringe some definition.

What if I have a cowlick?

If you have a cowlick in your fringe, try blow-drying it in the opposite direction to your natural hair growth. This can help to smooth out the cowlick and give you a more even finish.

Hair Care Tips

Finally, here are some general hair care tips to keep your hair looking healthy and strong:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo and Conditioner

Choose a shampoo and conditioner that are gentle on your hair and scalp. Look for products that are free from sulfates, parabens, and other harsh chemicals.

2. Avoid Heat Styling Too Often

Heat styling can be damaging to your hair, so try to avoid doing it too often. If you must use a blow-dryer or straightening iron, use a heat protectant spray first and keep the heat setting low.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet

Your hair needs nutrients to grow strong and healthy, so make sure to eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals.

4. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

UV rays from the sun can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle. Protect your hair by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant hair product.

By following these tips and tricks, you can get your fringe looking nice and keep it that way. Remember, the key is to be gentle and avoid overdoing it with heat and product.
