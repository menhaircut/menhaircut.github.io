How To Curl The Left Side Of Your Hair With A Curling Iron

Written by Oliver Jun 15, 2023 · 5 min read

Curling your hair can be a fun way to switch up your hairstyle and add some volume to your locks. However, it can be tricky to curl the left side of your hair with a curling iron if you're not used to it. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps of how to curl the left side of your hair with a curling iron, and provide some tips and hair care advice along the way.

Description

Curling the left side of your hair with a curling iron can be challenging, especially if you're right-handed. It can be difficult to maneuver the curling iron with your non-dominant hand, and it's easy to burn yourself or create uneven curls. However, with some practice and patience, you can achieve beautiful, bouncy curls on both sides of your head.

How to Curl the Left Side of Your Hair with a Curling Iron

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you start curling your hair, it's important to prep it properly. Start by washing your hair with a hydrating shampoo and conditioner, and towel-dry it gently. Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair to prevent damage from the curling iron. If you have thin or fine hair, you can also apply a volumizing mousse or spray to add some texture and lift to your locks.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, you'll need to section your hair to make it easier to curl. Divide your hair into two sections, with the left side separated from the right side. Use a hair clip or elastic band to secure the right side of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Heat Up Your Curling Iron

Turn on your curling iron and wait for it to heat up to the desired temperature. If you have fine or thin hair, use a lower heat setting (around 300°F). If you have thick or coarse hair, you can use a higher heat setting (around 400°F).

Step 4: Grab a Section of Hair

Take a small section of hair from the left side of your head, about one inch wide. Hold the curling iron vertically, with the barrel facing downwards. Place the section of hair between the barrel and the clamp, close to your scalp.

Step 5: Curl Your Hair

Slowly rotate the curling iron away from your face, wrapping the section of hair around the barrel. Hold the curling iron in place for about 10-15 seconds, depending on your hair type and the heat setting. Gently release the clamp and slide the curling iron downwards, allowing the curl to fall naturally.

Step 6: Repeat the Process

Continue curling sections of hair on the left side of your head, working your way from the front to the back. Make sure to curl sections of hair in the same direction, away from your face, for a more uniform look.

Step 7: Finish with Hairspray

Once you've curled all the sections on the left side of your head, release the right side and repeat the process. When you're done, mist your hair with a light hairspray to hold the curls in place.

Tips for Curling the Left Side of Your Hair with a Curling Iron

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when curling the left side of your hair with a curling iron:

  • Use a curling iron with a clamp, as it will help you control the sections of hair better than a wand.
  • Practice with your non-dominant hand on the right side of your head to get used to the motion.
  • Don't curl too much hair at once, as it can result in uneven curls and tangles.
  • Use a heat protectant spray or serum to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Let the curls cool down before touching them to avoid frizz and flyaways.

FAQ

Can you curl the left side of your hair with a wand instead of a curling iron?

Yes, you can use a wand instead of a curling iron to curl the left side of your hair. However, it can be more difficult to control the sections of hair and avoid burning yourself.

What should you do if you accidentally burn your hair while curling it?

If you accidentally burn your hair while curling it, immediately release the curling iron and run cold water over the burned section. Apply aloe vera gel or a cooling cream to soothe the burn.

Hair Care Tips

Curling your hair can be fun, but it's important to take care of your locks to prevent damage and breakage. Here are some hair care tips to follow:

  • Wash your hair with a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep it moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools every day, as it can damage your hair and cause split ends.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and keep it looking healthy.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and prevent dryness.

Now that you know how to curl the left side of your hair with a curling iron, you can experiment with different hairstyles and looks. Remember to be patient, practice, and take care of your hair to achieve the best results.
