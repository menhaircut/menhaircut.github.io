How To Make Hair Look Thicker At Scalp Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Aug 11, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of having flat, lifeless hair at the scalp? Do you want to give your hair a boost of volume and thickness? If so, you're in luck! In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to make hair look thicker at scalp. From hair care to styling techniques, we've got you covered. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Hair Care
  • How To Make Hair Look Thicker At Scalp
  • Step-By-Step Guide

Hair Care

Before we dive into the styling techniques, it's important to take care of your hair first. Healthy hair is the foundation for thick and voluminous hair at the scalp. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

1. Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner that contains ingredients like biotin, collagen, and keratin. These ingredients help to strengthen the hair and add volume.

2. Hair Masks

Once a week, use a hair mask that contains protein or keratin. This helps to strengthen the hair and prevent breakage.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage the hair and make it look flat. Try to avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands.

How To Make Hair Look Thicker At Scalp

Now that you've taken care of your hair, it's time to add some volume and thickness at the scalp. Here are some techniques to try:

1. Teasing

Teasing, also known as backcombing, involves using a comb to create volume at the roots. Simply take a small section of hair and comb it towards the scalp. Repeat this process until you've achieved the desired volume.

2. Blow Drying

Blow drying your hair upside down can add volume and lift at the roots. Use a round brush to lift the hair and direct the heat towards the roots.

3. Hair Extensions

Hair extensions can add instant volume and thickness to your hair. Choose extensions that match your hair color and texture for a seamless blend.

Step-By-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to make hair look thicker at scalp:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to give your hair a boost of volume.

Step 2: Towel Dry Your Hair

Gently towel dry your hair to remove excess water.

Step 3: Apply a Volumizing Mousse

Apply a volumizing mousse to your roots and massage it in.

Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Flip your head upside down and blow dry your hair using a round brush to lift the hair at the roots.

Step 5: Tease Your Hair

Take small sections of hair and tease them towards the scalp to create volume.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to make hair look thicker at scalp:

1. Use Dry Shampoo

Dry shampoo can add volume and texture to your hair. Spray it at the roots and massage it in with your fingers.

2. Try a Half-Up, Half-Down Hairstyle

A half-up, half-down hairstyle can create the illusion of thicker hair. Simply pull the top half of your hair back and secure it with a clip or hair tie.

3. Use Hair Accessories

Hair accessories like headbands and hair clips can add volume and texture to your hair. Choose accessories that match your personal style.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about how to make hair look thicker at scalp:

1. Can I make my hair look thicker at scalp without using heat styling tools?

Yes, you can use techniques like teasing, blow drying, and hair extensions to add volume and thickness to your hair without using heat styling tools.

2. How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people wash their hair every day, while others wash it once or twice a week. Use a shampoo and conditioner that works for your hair type.

3. Will using hair extensions damage my hair?

No, if you choose high-quality extensions and take care of them properly, they won't damage your hair. Be sure to follow the manufacturer's instructions for care and maintenance.

Now that you know how to make hair look thicker at scalp, it's time to try these techniques for yourself. Remember to take care of your hair first and have fun experimenting with different styles and techniques. Happy styling!
