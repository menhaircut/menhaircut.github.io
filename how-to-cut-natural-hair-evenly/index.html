How To Cut Natural Hair Evenly A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 17, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of uneven haircuts and spending too much money at the salon? Cutting your own natural hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home. In this step-by-step guide, we’ll walk you through the process of how to cut natural hair evenly, along with some tips and tricks to help you along the way.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You’ll Need
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair

Tools You’ll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, it’s important to have the right tools on hand. Here are the essentials:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties to section your hair
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Optional tools include a mirror, a cape or towel to protect your clothes, and hair cutting shears for more precise cuts.

Prepping Your Hair

Before you begin cutting your hair, it’s important to prep it properly. Start by washing and conditioning your hair as you normally would, then dry it completely. Comb through any tangles and knots, and make sure your hair is free of any styling products.

Next, decide on the length you want to cut your hair. It’s a good idea to start with a smaller cut and work your way up, so you don’t accidentally cut off too much. Use a ruler or tape measure to measure the length you want to cut, and mark it with a hair tie or clip.

Sectioning Your Hair

Now it’s time to section your hair. Divide your hair into four sections using clips or hair ties: two in the front and two in the back. The sections should be even and run from the top of your head to the nape of your neck.

Take one of the back sections and clip the other three sections out of the way. Comb through the section you’re going to cut, and make sure it’s smooth and even. Starting at the bottom, cut straight across the hair using your marked length as a guide. Continue cutting small sections until you’ve cut the entire section.

Repeat this process with the other back section, then move on to the front sections.

Cutting Your Hair

When cutting your hair, it’s important to remember a few key tips:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut
  • Cut small sections at a time to avoid cutting too much
  • Hold the hair taut while cutting to avoid uneven cuts
  • Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet, as it can appear longer than it actually is

Remember, it’s always better to cut less than to cut too much. You can always go back and trim more later if needed.

Tips for Cutting Natural Hair Evenly

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve an even cut:

  • Make sure you’re cutting in good lighting, so you can see any uneven areas
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head
  • Take breaks if you start to feel fatigued, as this can lead to uneven cuts
  • Consider getting a friend or family member to help with hard-to-reach areas

FAQs

Can I cut my natural hair when it’s wet?

No, it’s best to cut your hair when it’s dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.

What if I accidentally cut too much?

Don’t worry! Hair grows back. If you accidentally cut too much, you can always trim it again once it grows out.

Do I need to use hair cutting shears?

No, while hair cutting shears can provide a more precise cut, you can achieve an even cut with regular scissors.

Hair Care After Cutting

Once you’ve finished cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it properly. Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to moisturize your hair, and avoid using heat styling tools for at least a few days. Trim your hair regularly to maintain your new style, and enjoy your fresh, even cut!
