How To Figure Out My Hair Type Male A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Nov 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Knowing your hair type is crucial in determining the right hair care regimen. It helps you understand how to care for your hair, what products to use, and what styles suit you best. However, figuring out your hair type can be challenging, especially for men who are new to the hair game. In this article, we will discuss how to figure out your hair type as a male, including the steps, tips, and hair care practices.

Table of Contents

  1. Step 1: Determine Your Hair Texture
  2. Step 2: Identify Your Hair Density
  3. Step 3: Evaluate Your Hair Porosity

  5. Frequently Asked Questions
  6. Hair Care Practices for Different Hair Types

Step 1: Determine Your Hair Texture

The first step in figuring out your hair type is to determine your hair texture. Hair texture refers to the thickness or diameter of your individual hair strands. There are three main types of hair texture: fine, medium, and coarse.

To determine your hair texture, take a single strand of hair and hold it between your fingers. If you can barely feel the strand, you have fine hair. If you can feel the strand but it's not too thick or too thin, you have medium hair. If the strand feels thick and coarse, you have coarse hair.

Step 2: Identify Your Hair Density

The second step in figuring out your hair type is to identify your hair density. Hair density refers to the number of hair strands on your head per square inch. There are three main types of hair density: low, medium, and high.

To identify your hair density, divide your hair into sections and examine your scalp. If you can see your scalp clearly through your hair, you have low hair density. If you can see some scalp but not too much, you have medium hair density. If you can barely see any scalp, you have high hair density.

Step 3: Evaluate Your Hair Porosity

The third step in figuring out your hair type is to evaluate your hair porosity. Hair porosity refers to your hair's ability to absorb and retain moisture. There are three main types of hair porosity: low, medium, and high.

To evaluate your hair porosity, take a strand of hair and drop it into a glass of water. If the strand floats on top, you have low porosity hair. If the strand sinks to the bottom, you have high porosity hair. If the strand sinks slowly to the middle, you have medium porosity hair.

Tips for Figuring Out Your Hair Type

Here are some tips to help you figure out your hair type:

  • Observe how your hair behaves when wet and dry.
  • Take note of how your hair reacts to different products.
  • Ask a hair stylist for advice.
  • Research different hair types and compare them to your own.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the most common hair type for men?

The most common hair type for men is straight or wavy hair with medium thickness and density.

2. Can my hair type change over time?

Yes, your hair type can change due to hormonal changes, aging, and other factors.

3. Is it necessary to know my hair type?

Knowing your hair type can help you take better care of your hair and achieve the best results with your hairstyle.

Hair Care Practices for Different Hair Types

Here are some hair care practices for different hair types:

1. Fine Hair

Use lightweight products to avoid weighing down your hair. Avoid using heat styling tools too often as they can damage fine hair.

2. Medium Hair

Use products that provide moisture and shine. Experiment with different hairstyles as medium hair is versatile.

3. Coarse Hair

Use products that provide moisture and help tame frizz. Avoid using heat styling tools too often as they can damage coarse hair.

4. Low Porosity Hair

Use lightweight products that can penetrate your hair shaft. Use heat when deep conditioning to help open up your hair cuticles.

5. High Porosity Hair

Use products that provide moisture and help seal in moisture. Avoid using heat styling tools too often as they can further dry out high porosity hair.

Figuring out your hair type as a male can be challenging, but it's worth the effort. Knowing your hair type can help you take better care of your hair and achieve the best results with your hairstyle. Use the steps, tips, and hair care practices discussed above to figure out your hair type and start taking better care of your hair.
