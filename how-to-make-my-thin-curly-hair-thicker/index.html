How To Make My Thin Curly Hair Thicker Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Jul 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have thin curly hair, you know the struggle of trying to make it look thick and voluminous. But don't worry, there are ways to give your hair more body and bounce. In this article, we will share tips and tricks on how to make your thin curly hair thicker.

Description

Thin curly hair can be challenging to style, as it tends to fall flat and lack volume. But with the right techniques and products, you can give your hair more body and thickness. Whether you have natural curls or relaxed hair, these tips and tricks will help you achieve the look you want.

How to Make My Thin Curly Hair Thicker: Step by Step

Step 1: Use the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

The first step to making your thin curly hair thicker is to use the right shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are specifically designed for thin hair and contain ingredients like biotin and keratin, which can help strengthen and thicken your locks. Avoid heavy conditioners that can weigh your hair down.

Step 2: Add Volume with a Blow Dryer

To add volume to your hair, use a blow dryer and a round brush. Blow dry your hair upside down, and use the round brush to lift your roots and create volume. You can also use a diffuser attachment to enhance your natural curls and add more body.

Step 3: Try a Volumizing Mousse

A volumizing mousse can help give your thin curly hair more body and thickness. Apply the mousse to your damp hair, focusing on your roots, and then blow dry your hair as usual. The mousse will help hold your style in place and give your hair more volume.

Step 4: Use a Root Lifter

If you want to add even more volume to your roots, try using a root lifter. Apply the product to your roots before blow drying your hair, and then use a round brush to lift and style your hair. The root lifter will help give your hair more lift and body.

Tips for Making Your Thin Curly Hair Thicker

Here are some additional tips for making your thin curly hair thicker:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, as brushes can pull and break your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh your hair down. Instead, opt for lightweight products that won't leave your hair greasy or flat.
  • Try sleeping on a silk pillowcase to prevent breakage and frizz.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and keep your hair healthy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make my thin curly hair thicker without using heat?

Yes, you can make your thin curly hair thicker without using heat. Try using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner, and then air dry your hair to enhance your natural curls. You can also use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance your curls without using too much heat.

What styling products should I use for thin curly hair?

For thin curly hair, it's best to use lightweight styling products that won't weigh your hair down. Look for products like mousses, gels, and sprays that are specifically designed for thin hair. Avoid heavy creams and oils that can leave your hair greasy and flat.

How often should I wash my thin curly hair?

It's best to wash your thin curly hair every 2-3 days, as washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and make it dry and brittle. If you have an oily scalp, you can use a dry shampoo in between washes to keep your hair looking fresh.

Hair Care for Thin Curly Hair

To keep your thin curly hair healthy and strong, follow these hair care tips:

  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals, which can help promote hair growth and strength.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water, which can help keep your hair and scalp healthy.

By following these tips and tricks, you can make your thin curly hair thicker and more voluminous. With the right techniques and products, you can achieve the look you want and feel confident in your hair.
