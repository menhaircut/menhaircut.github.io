How To Roll Natural Hair With Sponge Rollers

Written by Frank Nov 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to create beautiful, bouncy curls without using heat, sponge rollers are a great option. Not only are they gentle on your hair, but they're also easy to use and can help you achieve a variety of styles. In this article, we'll show you how to roll natural hair with sponge rollers, step by step.

How to

Before you get started, make sure you have everything you need. You'll need a set of sponge rollers, a comb or brush, and some styling products like leave-in conditioner, curling cream, or mousse. Here's how to roll natural hair with sponge rollers:

Step 1: Start with clean, damp hair

Wash your hair and towel dry it until it's damp, but not dripping wet. Comb or brush it to remove any tangles, and apply a leave-in conditioner or curling cream to help define your curls.

Step 2: Section your hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. The size of the sections will depend on the size of the sponge rollers you're using and the type of curls you want to create.

Step 3: Roll your hair

Take a section of hair and wrap it around a sponge roller, starting at the ends and rolling it up towards your scalp. Secure the roller in place by bending the ends towards each other. Repeat this process for all of the sections of hair.

Step 4: Let your hair dry

Once all of your hair is rolled up, let it air dry or use a hooded dryer. The length of time it takes for your hair to dry will depend on your hair type and the size of the sponge rollers you're using.

Step 5: Remove the rollers

Once your hair is completely dry, remove the sponge rollers by gently bending them in the opposite direction of the way they were rolled. Use your fingers to separate the curls and create the desired style.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when rolling your natural hair with sponge rollers:

  • Use smaller rollers for tighter curls and larger rollers for looser curls
  • Apply a styling product like mousse or curling cream before rolling your hair to help define your curls
  • Don't roll your hair too tightly or you may end up with frizz or breakage
  • Let your hair air dry if possible, as using heat can damage your hair
  • Sleep with a satin bonnet or pillowcase to help preserve your curls

FAQ

Here are some commonly asked questions about rolling natural hair with sponge rollers:

Can I use sponge rollers on relaxed hair?

Yes, you can use sponge rollers on relaxed hair. Just make sure your hair is completely dry before removing the rollers to avoid frizz or breakage.

How long should I leave the rollers in my hair?

The length of time you should leave the rollers in your hair will depend on your hair type and the size of the rollers you're using. As a general rule, leave the rollers in until your hair is completely dry.

How often should I use sponge rollers on my hair?

You can use sponge rollers on your hair as often as you like, but be mindful of how tightly you're rolling your hair and how often you're using heat to dry your hair. Excessive heat and tension can damage your hair over time.

Hair Care

To keep your natural hair healthy and strong, it's important to follow a regular hair care routine. This includes washing and conditioning your hair regularly, using a deep conditioning treatment once a week, and avoiding excessive heat and tension. You should also trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to roll your natural hair with sponge rollers like a pro. Whether you're looking to create tight curls or loose waves, sponge rollers are a great way to achieve the look you want without using heat.
