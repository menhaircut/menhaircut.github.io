How To Cut Side Swept Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 23, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of your current hairstyle and looking for a change? Side swept bangs can be a great way to switch things up and add a touch of sophistication to your look. But if you're not sure how to cut them, don't worry – it's easier than you might think. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to cut side swept bangs, along with some tips and tricks to make the process as smooth as possible.

Description

Side swept bangs are a popular hairstyle that involves cutting a section of hair across your forehead at an angle, so that it falls to one side of your face. This style can work with a variety of hair lengths and textures, and can be customized to suit your face shape and personal preferences. Side swept bangs can also be a great way to highlight your eyes and cheekbones, and can help to frame your face in a flattering way.

How to Cut Side Swept Bangs

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a fine-toothed comb, and some hair clips to hold your hair in place. Once you have everything you need, follow these steps:

Step 1: Choose the Right Length

The first step in cutting side swept bangs is to decide how long you want them to be. You can choose to cut them just above your eyebrows for a more dramatic look, or a bit longer for a softer effect. Use your fingers or a comb to measure the length you want, and mark it with a clip or a piece of tape.

Step 2: Section Off Your Hair

Next, use your comb to section off the hair that you want to cut. Start at the top of your head and work your way down, separating the hair into a triangular shape that extends from the center of your forehead to the sides of your head. Use your hair clips to hold the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Cut Your Bangs

Using your scissors, cut your hair at an angle, starting from the center of your forehead and working your way outwards. Be sure to use sharp, clean scissors to avoid damaging your hair. Cut small sections at a time, and check your progress frequently in a mirror to make sure your bangs are even.

Step 4: Blend Your Bangs

Once you've cut your bangs to the desired length, use your scissors to blend the edges so that they look natural. You can do this by holding the scissors vertically and making small snips along the edges of your bangs.

Step 5: Style Your Bangs

Finally, use a blow dryer and a round brush to style your bangs. Brush them to one side of your face, and use the dryer to create volume and movement. You can also use a flat iron or curling iron to add texture and shape to your bangs.

Tips for Cutting Side Swept Bangs

Cutting your own bangs can be a bit intimidating, but with these tips, you can make the process easier and more successful:

  • Start with dry, clean hair to make it easier to see the length and texture of your hair.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Cut small sections at a time to avoid making big mistakes.
  • Check your progress frequently in the mirror to make sure your bangs are even.
  • Be patient – cutting your own hair can take some practice, so don't get discouraged if it doesn't turn out perfectly the first time.

FAQs

Can I cut my bangs when they're wet?

It's generally best to cut your hair when it's dry, since wet hair can shrink and make your bangs shorter than you intended. However, if you prefer to cut your hair when it's wet, be sure to cut it longer than you want it to be, to account for any shrinkage.

How often do I need to trim my bangs?

How often you trim your bangs depends on how quickly your hair grows and how neat you want your bangs to look. As a general rule, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh and tidy.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake while cutting your bangs, don't panic. You can always trim them a bit shorter or blend them more to hide any unevenness. If you're really unhappy with the results, you can always visit a professional stylist to fix the problem.

Hair Care

Once you've cut your side swept bangs, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep your bangs clean and fresh.
  • Blow dry your hair on a low heat setting to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Avoid using too much heat styling tools, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a small amount of hair oil or serum to keep your bangs smooth and frizz-free.
  • Visit a professional stylist for regular trims and shaping.

Now that you know how to cut side swept bangs, you can experiment with this versatile and stylish look. With a bit of practice and patience, you can create a new and flattering hairstyle that suits your face shape and personality.
