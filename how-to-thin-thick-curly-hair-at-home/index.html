How To Thin Thick Curly Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're blessed with thick, curly hair, you know how difficult it can be to tame. Styling can take forever, and the weight of your hair can be a burden. Luckily, there's a simple solution to this problem: thinning your hair. In this step-by-step guide, we'll show you how to thin thick curly hair at home, so you can enjoy lighter, more manageable locks.

How to Thin Thick Curly Hair at Home

Before we get started, it's important to note that thinning your hair should be done with caution. If done incorrectly, it can cause damage to your hair and scalp.

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

To thin your hair at home, you'll need a pair of thinning shears, a comb, and a mirror. Thinning shears are different from regular scissors because they have teeth that remove hair as they cut. You can find them at most beauty supply stores or online.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Thinning your hair when it's wet can cause it to look uneven, so it's best to do it when your hair is dry. Wash and dry your hair as you normally would, making sure it's completely dry before you start thinning.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use your comb to section your hair into four equal parts: two in the front and two in the back. Clip each section out of the way, so you can work on one section at a time.

Step 4: Start Thinning

Take a small section of hair from the first section you're working on and comb it straight. Hold your thinning shears at a 45-degree angle and cut into the hair, making sure the teeth of the shears are facing away from your scalp. Cut about Â¼ inch of hair at a time and be careful not to cut too much.

Continue thinning each section of hair, making sure to blend the cut pieces with the uncut pieces for a natural look.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After you've thinned all four sections of your hair, check your progress in the mirror. If you're happy with the results, you're done! If you want to thin your hair more, repeat the process until you achieve your desired look.

Tips for Thinning Thick Curly Hair

Thinning your hair can be a delicate process, so it's important to follow these tips to ensure the best results:

  • Don't thin your hair too much. It's better to start with a little and add more if needed.
  • Always use sharp thinning shears. Dull shears can cause damage to your hair.
  • Be careful not to thin your hair too close to your scalp. This can cause your hair to look uneven and choppy.
  • Take your time and be patient. Thinning your hair can be time-consuming, but the results are worth it.

FAQ:

Can I thin my hair with regular scissors?

No, regular scissors are not designed for thinning hair. You'll need thinning shears to achieve the best results.

How often should I thin my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, you should thin your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care After Thinning

After thinning your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for caring for your newly thinned hair:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, like flat irons and curling irons, too often.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up.
  • Trim your hair regularly to keep it looking healthy and prevent split ends.

Thinning your thick curly hair at home can be a great way to achieve a lighter, more manageable look. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow our tips for the best results.
