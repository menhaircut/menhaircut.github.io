Cutting Short Spiky Haircuts A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Nov 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a low-maintenance haircut that still looks stylish, a short spiky haircut might be the perfect option for you. Not only is it easy to style, but it also adds texture and dimension to your hair. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut short spiky haircuts in relaxed English language.

Description

A short spiky haircut involves cutting the hair short on the sides and back, while leaving the top longer to create spikes. This style is versatile and can be customized to suit different face shapes and hair types. Whether you have straight, wavy, or curly hair, you can achieve a spiky look with the right haircut and styling products.

How to Cut Short Spiky Haircuts

Cutting short spiky haircuts can be done at home or at a salon. If you're comfortable with using clippers and scissors, you can try cutting your own hair. However, if you're not confident in your skills, it's best to seek the help of a professional hair stylist.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's how to cut short spiky haircuts:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to cut and style.
  2. Section your hair by using clips or hair ties. This will help you focus on one section at a time.
  3. Use clippers to cut the sides and back of your hair. Start with a longer guard and gradually go shorter until you reach your desired length.
  4. Blend the sides and back with the top by using scissors or clippers with a guard. This will create a seamless transition between the two sections.
  5. Cut the top of your hair using scissors. Start by cutting a small section and gradually work your way to the front. Leave the hair longer in the front to create spikes.
  6. Use thinning shears to remove bulk and create texture. This will make it easier to style your hair later on.
  7. Style your hair by using a pomade or wax. Apply a small amount to your fingertips and work it through your hair, focusing on the tips to create spikes.
  8. Use a blow dryer to set the style in place. Use a diffuser attachment to avoid frizz and preserve the texture of your hair.
  9. Finish off with a hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips for Cutting Short Spiky Haircuts

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting short spiky haircuts:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually go shorter. You can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back.
  • Use a comb to guide your scissors when cutting the top of your hair. This will ensure that you're cutting evenly and not creating any choppy layers.
  • Invest in quality hair cutting tools. This will make the process easier and ensure that you're getting a clean cut.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. If you're not confident in your skills, it's better to seek the help of a professional hair stylist.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about short spiky haircuts:

Can short spiky haircuts be styled differently?

Yes, short spiky haircuts can be styled in different ways depending on your preference. You can create a messy look by using a texturizing product, or a sleek look by using a gel.

What face shapes suit short spiky haircuts?

Short spiky haircuts work well on oval, square, and heart-shaped faces. However, it's important to consider your hair texture and thickness when choosing a haircut that suits you.

What styling products are best for short spiky haircuts?

Pomades, waxes, and gels are great products for styling short spiky haircuts. They provide hold and texture without weighing down the hair.

Hair Care for Short Spiky Haircuts

To keep your short spiky haircut looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair every other day to prevent it from drying out.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to maintain the natural oils in your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape of your haircut.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to cut short spiky haircuts with ease. Remember to take your time and be patient, as cutting hair requires practice and skill. Good luck!
