How To Train Hair To Go Back

Are you tired of your hair constantly falling in your face? Do you want to achieve a sleek and polished look? Training your hair to go back is a simple and effective solution. In this article, we will provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to train your hair to go back.

How to Train Hair to Go Back

Training your hair to go back requires time and patience. The key is to create a consistent routine and stick to it. Here are the general steps to follow:

Step 1: Cleanse and Condition

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Use lukewarm water to prevent damage to your hair. Rinse your hair thoroughly to remove any residue.

Step 2: Apply Styling Products

Apply a styling product that will help hold your hair in place. Use a comb to evenly distribute the product and avoid clumps. Consider using a gel, pomade, or wax to achieve the desired hold.

Step 3: Blow Dry

Blow dry your hair in the direction you want it to go. Use a round brush to add volume to your hair. Be sure to apply heat protectant to prevent damage to your hair.

Step 4: Train Your Hair

Use your comb to train your hair to go back. Start by combing your hair in the direction you want it to go. Use your fingers to hold your hair in place for a few seconds. Repeat this process until your hair starts to stay in place on its own.

Step 5: Maintain Your Hairstyle

Maintain your hairstyle by regularly trimming your hair to prevent split ends. Use a quality hairbrush to keep your hair smooth and frizz-free. Consider using a dry shampoo to refresh your hair between washes.

Step-by-Step Instructions

For a more detailed breakdown of how to train your hair to go back, follow these step-by-step instructions:

Step 1: Cleanse and Condition

  1. Wet your hair with lukewarm water.
  2. Apply a gentle shampoo to your hair and massage your scalp.
  3. Rinse your hair thoroughly with lukewarm water.
  4. Apply a conditioner to your hair and leave it in for a few minutes.
  5. Rinse your hair thoroughly with lukewarm water.

Step 2: Apply Styling Products

  1. Towel dry your hair to remove excess water.
  2. Apply a styling product to your hair, such as gel, pomade, or wax.
  3. Use a comb to evenly distribute the product and avoid clumps.

Step 3: Blow Dry

  1. Use a round brush to blow dry your hair in the direction you want it to go.
  2. Apply heat protectant to prevent damage to your hair.

Step 4: Train Your Hair

  1. Use your comb to train your hair to go back.
  2. Start by combing your hair in the direction you want it to go.
  3. Use your fingers to hold your hair in place for a few seconds.
  4. Repeat this process until your hair starts to stay in place on its own.

Step 5: Maintain Your Hairstyle

  1. Regularly trim your hair to prevent split ends.
  2. Use a quality hairbrush to keep your hair smooth and frizz-free.
  3. Consider using a dry shampoo to refresh your hair between washes.

Tips for Training Hair to Go Back

Here are some tips to help you train your hair to go back:

  • Use a quality styling product that will hold your hair in place without making it look greasy or stiff.
  • Be patient and consistent with your routine. It may take several weeks for your hair to stay in place on its own.
  • Use a comb with fine teeth to help train your hair.
  • Consider using a hairband or headband to keep your hair in place while you train it.

FAQ

How long does it take to train your hair to go back?

It may take several weeks for your hair to stay in place on its own. Be patient and consistent with your routine.

What styling products should I use to train my hair to go back?

Consider using a gel, pomade, or wax to achieve the desired hold. Use a product that will hold your hair in place without making it look greasy or stiff.

How often should I trim my hair?

It is recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends.

Hair Care for Training Hair to Go Back

Here are some hair care tips to help you maintain your hairstyle:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, too often as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.
  • Eat a balanced diet to promote healthy hair growth.

Training your hair to go back is a simple and effective way to achieve a sleek and polished look. By following these steps and tips, you can train your hair to stay in place and maintain your desired hairstyle.
