How To Fix Thinning Shears A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 10, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Thinning shears are an essential tool for any hair stylist or barber. They are designed to remove bulk from hair without sacrificing length, creating a layered and textured look. However, over time, thinning shears can become dull or misaligned, making them less effective. In this guide, we will show you how to fix thinning shears so that you can continue to create beautiful hairstyles.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Fix Thinning Shears
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Thinning Shears

Description

Thinning shears are a type of scissors with teeth along the blade that remove some hair and leave some hair behind. They are used to create a textured look, remove bulk, and blend layers. Over time, the teeth on the blade can become dull, misaligned, or even break off, making the shears less effective. It is important to know how to fix thinning shears so that they can continue to create beautiful hairstyles.

How to Fix Thinning Shears

The first step in fixing thinning shears is to identify the problem. There are several issues that can arise with thinning shears:

  • Dull blades
  • Misaligned blades
  • Broken teeth

Once you have identified the problem, you can take steps to fix it. Here are the basic steps for fixing thinning shears:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Disassemble the shears by removing the screw that holds the blades together.
  2. Use a sharpening stone or file to sharpen the blades. Be careful not to remove too much material, as this can damage the shears.
  3. Align the blades by holding them up to the light and making sure they are perfectly parallel. If they are not, use pliers to gently bend them back into place.
  4. Replace any broken teeth by removing the screw that holds the tooth in place and replacing it with a new one.
  5. Reassemble the shears by screwing the blades back together.

Tips for Maintaining Thinning Shears

The best way to prevent thinning shears from becoming dull or misaligned is to take good care of them. Here are some tips for maintaining your thinning shears:

  • Keep them clean and dry
  • Oil the blades regularly
  • Store them in a dry place
  • Use them only on clean, dry hair
  • Have them professionally sharpened once or twice a year

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I fix my thinning shears myself?
A: Yes, with the right tools and knowledge, you can fix your thinning shears yourself.

Q: How often should I sharpen my thinning shears?
A: You should have your thinning shears professionally sharpened once or twice a year.

Q: Can I use thinning shears on wet hair?
A: No, you should only use thinning shears on clean, dry hair.

Hair Care Tips

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy hair. Here are some tips for keeping your hair in good condition:

  • Wash your hair regularly
  • Use a conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Trim your hair regularly
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals

By following these tips, you can keep your hair looking great and your thinning shears in top condition.
