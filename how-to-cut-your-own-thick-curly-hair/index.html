How To Cut Your Own Thick Curly Hair

Written by Oliver Oct 04, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're tired of spending countless hours and money at the hair salon, why not try cutting your own hair? As someone with thick curly hair, it can be daunting to take on the task of cutting your own hair. But fear not, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve salon-worthy results from the comfort of your own home. In this blog post, we'll walk you through how to cut your own thick curly hair step by step.

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clip or hair tie
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Large mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have your tools ready, let's get started on cutting your own thick curly hair.

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair as you normally would. This will help detangle your hair and make it easier to cut. After you've rinsed out the conditioner, gently towel dry your hair so that it's damp but not soaking wet.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into four parts – two in the front and two in the back. Clip or tie each section so that it's out of the way. This will make it easier for you to cut each section one at a time.

Step 3: Cut the Back Section

Start with the back section of your hair. Using your comb, separate a small section of hair at the nape of your neck. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut off the desired length. Repeat this process with each section until you've cut all of the back section of your hair.

Step 4: Cut the Front Section

Next, move on to the front section of your hair. Start by combing out a small section of hair in front of your ear. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut off the desired length. Repeat this process with each section of your hair until you've cut all of the front section of your hair.

Step 5: Check Your Layers

Once you've cut all of your hair, remove the clips or ties and shake out your hair. Check to make sure that your layers are even. If they're not, go back and trim any sections that need it.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Now that you've cut your hair, it's time to style it. Use your fingers or a diffuser to scrunch your curls and create volume. You can also use a curling iron or straightener to create a different look.

Tips for Cutting Thick Curly Hair

Cutting your own thick curly hair can be tricky, but with these tips, you'll be able to achieve great results:

  • Start with small sections of hair to ensure that you don't cut off too much at once.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your hair, start with a small trim and work your way up to a bigger cut.
  • Take breaks if you need to – cutting your own hair can be tiring!

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair if it's extremely thick?

Yes, you can cut your own hair if it's extremely thick. Just be sure to start with smaller sections of hair to ensure that you don't cut off too much at once.

How often should I cut my thick curly hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Curly Hair

Now that you've cut your own hair, it's important to take care of it to ensure that it stays healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips for taking care of thick curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently.
  • Sleep with a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

Now that you know how to cut your own thick curly hair, give it a try! With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve salon-worthy results from the comfort of your own home.
