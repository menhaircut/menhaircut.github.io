How To Do Forties Hairstyles A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Oct 04, 2023 Â· 5 min read

The forties were a time of glamour and elegance, and the hairstyles of the era reflected that. From victory rolls to pin curls, forties hairstyles were all about creating a polished look that was both sophisticated and feminine. In this article, we will show you how to recreate some of the most iconic forties hairstyles.

Victory Rolls

Victory rolls are one of the most iconic forties hairstyles. They are a classic look that is perfect for special occasions or just to add a touch of glamour to your everyday style. Here's how to do victory rolls:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section off the front of your hair on both sides of your head, from your temples to your ears.
  3. Take one section of hair and brush it out.
  4. Roll the hair up and away from your face, creating a loop. Secure the loop with bobby pins.
  5. Repeat on the other side.
  6. Spray with hairspray to keep the style in place.

Tip: For a more dramatic look, try making your victory rolls bigger by using more hair.

Pin Curls

Pin curls are another classic forties hairstyle that is still popular today. They are perfect for creating soft, romantic waves that are perfect for special occasions. Here's how to do pin curls:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Section off your hair into small sections, no larger than 1 inch in diameter.
  3. Using a comb, wrap the hair around your finger, starting at the ends and rolling up towards your scalp.
  4. Pin the curl in place with a bobby pin.
  5. Repeat on the rest of your hair.
  6. Allow your hair to dry completely, then remove the pins.
  7. Brush out your hair to create soft waves.
  8. Spray with hairspray to keep the style in place.

Tip: For tighter curls, use smaller sections of hair.

S Wave

The S wave is a subtle, yet elegant forties hairstyle that is perfect for adding a touch of glamour to any look. Here's how to do an S wave:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section off your hair into small sections, about 1 inch in diameter.
  3. Using a curling iron, curl each section of hair towards your face.
  4. Once the curls have cooled, brush them out.
  5. Using your fingers, create an S shape in the hair by pushing the hair up and then down.
  6. Spray with hairspray to keep the style in place.

Tip: For a more defined S wave, use a smaller curling iron.

Faux Bob

A faux bob is a fun and easy way to switch up your look without committing to a drastic haircut. Here's how to do a faux bob:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section off the bottom half of your hair and pin it up.
  3. Tuck the top section of your hair under, creating the illusion of a bob.
  4. Secure the hair in place with bobby pins.
  5. Release the bottom section of your hair and curl it with a curling iron.
  6. Brush out the curls to create soft waves.
  7. Spray with hairspray to keep the style in place.

Tip: For a more authentic look, add finger waves to the top section of your hair before tucking it under.

Faux Hawk

A faux hawk is a fun and edgy forties hairstyle that is perfect for making a statement. Here's how to do a faux hawk:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section off the top half of your hair and pin it up.
  3. Curl the bottom section of your hair with a curling iron.
  4. Brush out the curls to create soft waves.
  5. Release the top section of your hair and tease it at the roots.
  6. Smooth the top layer of hair over the teased section, creating a faux hawk effect.
  7. Spray with hairspray to keep the style in place.

Tip: For a more dramatic look, add a hair accessory like a headband or hair clip to the top section of your hair.

Faux Gibson Tuck

A faux Gibson tuck is a sophisticated and elegant forties hairstyle that is perfect for formal occasions. Here's how to do a faux Gibson tuck:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section off the top half of your hair and pin it up.
  3. Take the bottom section of your hair and twist it into a loose bun at the nape of your neck.
  4. Secure the bun with bobby pins.
  5. Release the top section of your hair and tease it at the roots.
  6. Smooth the top layer of hair over the teased section and tuck it into the bun, creating a faux Gibson tuck effect.
  7. Spray with hairspray to keep the style in place.

Tip: For a more glamorous look, add a hair accessory like a sparkly barrette or hair comb to the side of the bun.

Hair Care Tips

To achieve the perfect forties hairstyle, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and moisturized.
  • Limit your use of heat styling tools to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to give your hair an extra boost of moisture.

FAQ

What hair length is best for forties hairstyles?

Forties hairstyles were typically worn on medium to long hair lengths. However, many of the styles can be adapted for shorter hair lengths with the use of hair extensions or by modifying the technique slightly.

Can forties hairstyles be worn for everyday?

Absolutely! Many of the forties hairstyles are versatile enough to be worn for everyday, especially if you opt for a softer, more natural look. Just be sure to use hairspray to keep the style in place.

What type of hair works best for forties hairstyles?

Forties hairstyles can work for all hair types, but some styles may require a bit of tweaking depending on your hair's texture and thickness. For example, pin curls work best on fine to medium hair, while victory rolls are better suited for thicker hair types.

With these tips and techniques, you can easily recreate the glamour and elegance of forties hairstyles. Whether you're going for a classic look or something more edgy and modern, there's a forties hairstyle to suit your style and personality.
