How To Know If Auburn Hair Suits You

Written by Petter Nov 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you thinking of switching up your hair color? Auburn hair may be a beautiful option to consider. However, before taking the plunge, it's important to know if this hair color will suit you. In this blog post, we'll cover how to determine if auburn hair will complement your skin tone, eye color, and overall appearance. Here's what you need to know:

Table of Contents

  1. Determining Your Skin Tone
  2. Considering Your Eye Color
  3. Assessing Your Overall Appearance
  4. How to Choose the Right Auburn Hair Color
  5. Tips for Maintaining Auburn Hair
  6. FAQs

Determining Your Skin Tone

The first step in determining if auburn hair will suit you is to assess your skin tone. There are three main categories of skin tone: warm, cool, and neutral. Warm skin tones have yellow, gold, or peach undertones, while cool skin tones have pink, red, or blue undertones. Neutral skin tones have a balance of both warm and cool undertones.

To determine your skin tone, look at the veins on your wrist. If they appear greenish, you likely have warm undertones. If they appear bluish, you likely have cool undertones. If you can't tell if they're green or blue, you may have a neutral skin tone.

If you have warm undertones, auburn hair with golden or copper hues may complement your complexion. If you have cool undertones, auburn hair with more red or burgundy tones may be a better fit. Neutral skin tones have a bit more flexibility and can pull off a range of auburn shades.

Considering Your Eye Color

Your eye color can also play a role in determining if auburn hair will suit you. In general, auburn hair tends to complement green, hazel, or brown eyes. If you have blue eyes, auburn hair can still work, but you may want to opt for a lighter shade to avoid clashing with your eye color.

Assessing Your Overall Appearance

When deciding if auburn hair will suit you, it's important to consider your overall appearance. This includes your natural hair color, your style preferences, and your personality. If you're someone who likes to stand out and make a statement, a bold auburn shade may be perfect for you. If you prefer a more subtle change, a softer auburn shade may be a better fit.

How to Choose the Right Auburn Hair Color

Once you've determined that auburn hair will suit you, it's time to choose the right shade. Here are some tips:

  • Consider your skin tone and eye color to determine the best shade of auburn for you.
  • Look at pictures of people with auburn hair to get an idea of what shades you like.
  • Ask your stylist for their professional opinion on what shade will suit you best.

Tips for Maintaining Auburn Hair

Now that you've got your new auburn hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to prevent fading.
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can strip the color.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to too much sunlight or chlorine, as these can also cause fading.

FAQs

Can I dye my hair auburn if I have dark hair?

Yes, but you may need to bleach your hair first to lighten it enough to achieve the desired auburn shade.

How often do I need to touch up my auburn hair?

This will depend on how quickly your hair grows and how quickly the color fades. In general, you may need to touch up your roots every 4-6 weeks.

Can I dye my hair auburn at home, or should I go to a salon?

While you can dye your hair auburn at home, it's generally recommended to go to a salon for the best results. A professional stylist can help you choose the right shade and ensure that the color is applied evenly.

In conclusion, auburn hair can be a beautiful and flattering color for many people. By determining your skin tone, eye color, and overall appearance, you can determine if this hair color will suit you. If you do decide to go for it, remember to take care of your new hair color with the right products and maintenance.
