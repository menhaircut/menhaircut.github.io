How To Cut Your Bangs Shorter A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your bangs getting in your eyes or just want a new look? Cutting your bangs shorter can be a great way to change up your hairstyle. However, if you're not careful, it can also be a disaster. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of how to cut your bangs shorter without ruining your hair.

Description

Before we get started, it's important to note that cutting your own hair can be risky. If you're not confident in your abilities, it's best to leave it to a professional. However, if you're feeling brave, cutting your bangs shorter can be a fun DIY project.

How to

Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • A spray bottle filled with water

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is wet, it will be longer and harder to cut accurately.

Step 2: Use the comb to separate your bangs from the rest of your hair. Clip the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Decide how short you want your bangs to be. It's always better to start longer and cut more if needed. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can't put hair back.

Step 4: Using the comb, comb your bangs straight down over your forehead. This will give you a clear view of the hair you need to cut.

Step 5: Take your hair cutting scissors and make small, vertical cuts into your bangs. Don't cut straight across, as this can create a harsh, blunt look. Instead, make small snips to create texture and movement.

Step 6: After each snip, take a step back and look in the mirror to make sure you're happy with the length and shape. It's easier to cut more than it is to fix a mistake.

Step 7: Continue cutting until you've achieved your desired length and shape.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind while cutting your bangs shorter:

  • Start with small snips and work your way up. It's always easier to cut more than it is to fix a mistake.
  • Don't cut straight across. Instead, make small, vertical snips to create texture and movement.
  • Take breaks to step back and look in the mirror. It's important to make sure you're happy with the length and shape.
  • Be patient. Cutting your hair can take time, especially if you're doing it for the first time.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting bangs shorter:

Q: How often should I cut my bangs?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to change up your look. Some people cut their bangs every few weeks, while others go months without trimming them.

Q: Can I cut my bangs while they're wet?

A: It's not recommended, as wet hair is longer and harder to cut accurately. It's best to cut your bangs when they're dry.

Q: What if I make a mistake?

A: If you make a mistake, don't panic! You can always go to a professional stylist to fix it. Alternatively, you can try styling your hair in a way that hides the mistake.

Hair Care

After cutting your bangs shorter, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.

Cutting your bangs shorter can be a fun way to switch up your hairstyle. With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to do it with confidence and ease. Remember, if you're not comfortable cutting your own hair, it's always best to leave it to the professionals.
