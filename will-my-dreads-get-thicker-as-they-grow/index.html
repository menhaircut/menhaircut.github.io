Will My Dreads Get Thicker As They Grow

Written by Frank Sep 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have recently started your dreadlock journey, you may be wondering if your dreads will get thicker as they grow. The short answer is yes, but there are a few factors that come into play. In this article, we will discuss how to thicken your dreads as they grow, how to care for them, and answer some frequently asked questions about dreadlocks.

Description

Dreadlocks, also known as "locs," are a hairstyle that involves matting and twisting sections of hair until they become tightly coiled. As your hair grows and your dreads mature, they will naturally become thicker. However, there are some things you can do to encourage thicker, fuller dreads.

How To Thicken Your Dreads As They Grow

One of the best ways to thicken your dreads as they grow is to keep them healthy. This means washing your hair regularly, using a residue-free shampoo, and avoiding products that can cause buildup or damage. You may also want to consider using a dreadlock-specific shampoo or a natural clarifying rinse like apple cider vinegar to keep your scalp and hair clean.

In addition to keeping your hair clean, you can also encourage thicker dreads by backcombing. Backcombing involves teasing the hair backwards towards the scalp to create tangles and knots. This technique can help create more volume and make your dreads appear thicker.

Step By Step Guide To Thicken Your Dreads

1. Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo or a dreadlock-specific shampoo.
2. Avoid using products that can cause buildup or damage, such as heavy conditioners or styling products.
3. Use a natural clarifying rinse like apple cider vinegar to keep your scalp and hair clean.
4. Consider backcombing your dreads to create more volume and thickness.

Tips For Thicker Dreads

Here are a few additional tips for thicker, fuller dreads:

- Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can cause damage and breakage
- Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent breakage and protect your hair while you sleep
- Avoid tight hairstyles or styles that pull on your dreads, as this can cause traction alopecia
- Consider using aloe vera or other natural hair growth treatments to promote healthy hair growth
- Be patient! It takes time for dreads to mature and thicken, so don't get discouraged if your hair doesn't look exactly how you want it to right away.

FAQ

Q: Will my dreads get thinner as they grow?

A: No, your dreads should not get thinner as they grow. In fact, they should become thicker as new hair grows in and the existing hair mats and tightens.

Q: Can I use regular shampoo on my dreads?

A: It's best to use a residue-free shampoo or a dreadlock-specific shampoo to avoid buildup and keep your hair and scalp clean. Regular shampoos may contain ingredients that can cause residue or buildup in your dreads.

Q: Can I dye my dreads?

A: Yes, you can dye your dreads, but it's important to use a dye that is safe for your hair type and to avoid over-processing your hair. It's also a good idea to consult with a professional stylist before attempting to dye your dreads.

Hair Care Tips For Healthy Dreads

Here are a few additional tips for caring for your dreads:

- Avoid using harsh chemicals or treatments on your hair
- Massage your scalp regularly to promote healthy hair growth
- Use a light oil or serum to keep your hair hydrated and prevent breakage
- Don't over-wash your hair, as this can strip away natural oils and cause dryness
- Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a scarf or hat

In conclusion, your dreads will naturally become thicker as they grow, but there are things you can do to encourage healthy, full dreads. By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can achieve the thick, voluminous dreads you've always wanted.
