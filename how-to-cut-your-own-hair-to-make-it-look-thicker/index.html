How To Cut Your Own Hair To Make It Look Thicker A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jun 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're one of the many people out there with fine or thin hair, you know how frustrating it can be to find a style that works for you. Luckily, there's a simple solution: cutting your hair to make it look thicker. With a few simple tips and tricks, you can give your hair the appearance of more volume and body. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your own hair to make it look thicker, step by step.

Description

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, but with the right technique, it's actually quite simple. By layering your hair and adding texture, you can create the illusion of thicker, fuller locks. In this article, we'll provide you with everything you need to know to get started, from choosing the right tools to executing the perfect cut.

How to

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you get started, you'll need to gather a few key tools. You'll need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to help section your hair. If you're feeling adventurous, you can also invest in a pair of thinning shears or a razor comb to add even more texture to your hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will help you get a more accurate cut and prevent any unwanted surprises.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

To make the cutting process easier, you'll want to section your hair into manageable pieces. Start by dividing your hair into two sections, from ear to ear. Clip the top section out of the way and focus on the bottom section first.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Layers

Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at a slight angle, starting about an inch from the bottom. Repeat this process, working your way around your head, until you've cut all the bottom layers.

Step 5: Layer the Top Sections

Once you've finished cutting the bottom layer, unclip the top section of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the top layer and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at a slight angle, starting about an inch from the bottom. Repeat this process, working your way around your head, until you've cut all the top layers.

Step 6: Add Texture

If you want to add even more texture to your hair, you can use thinning shears or a razor comb to create layers and remove bulk. Be careful not to overdo it, though, as this can lead to frizzy, damaged hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind as you cut your hair to make it look thicker:

  • Start with small sections of hair, and take your time to ensure a precise cut
  • Use a comb to help guide your scissors and keep your cuts straight
  • Cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can stretch and result in uneven cuts
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different cutting techniques and styles

FAQ

Can I cut my hair with regular scissors?

Yes, you can use regular scissors to cut your hair, but it's important to make sure they're sharp and clean. Dull or dirty scissors can lead to jagged, uneven cuts and damage your hair.

How often should I cut my hair to make it look thicker?

This will depend on your individual hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, it's a good idea to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.

Hair Care

Cutting your hair to make it look thicker is just one part of a healthy hair care routine. To keep your locks looking their best, be sure to:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too often
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals

With these tips and tricks, you'll be well on your way to a fuller, more voluminous head of hair. Happy cutting!
