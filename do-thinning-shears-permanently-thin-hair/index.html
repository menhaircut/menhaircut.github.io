Do Thinning Shears Permanently Thin Hair A Guide To Thinning Shears

Written by Frank Oct 25, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Thinning shears are a staple tool in many hairstylists' kits. They are used to remove bulk and add texture to hair, creating a more natural and effortless look. However, there is a common misconception that thinning shears permanently thin hair. In this article, we will explore this myth and provide you with a guide to thinning shears.

What are Thinning Shears?

Thinning shears, also known as texturizing shears, are scissors with notched blades that remove hair in a graduated way. They are used to thin out thick hair, blend layers, and add texture to hair. Thinning shears are especially useful for creating a more natural and effortless look.

Do Thinning Shears Permanently Thin Hair?

No, thinning shears do not permanently thin hair. Thinning shears only remove a portion of the hair, leaving the rest of the hair intact. The hair will grow back, and the thickness will return over time. Thinning shears are a temporary solution for thick hair, and the results will eventually grow out.

How to Use Thinning Shears

Thinning shears should only be used by trained professionals. If used incorrectly, they can damage hair and create an uneven cut. Here is a step-by-step guide to using thinning shears:

Step 1: Section the Hair

Section the hair into small sections, and secure them with clips. This will make it easier to work with the hair and ensure that you don't miss any spots.

Step 2: Choose the Right Shears

Choose the right thinning shears for the job. Thinning shears come in different sizes and textures. The right shears will depend on the thickness and texture of the hair you are working with.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Hold the thinning shears at a 45-degree angle to the hair, and cut into the hair in a zigzag motion. This will remove a portion of the hair, leaving the rest of the hair intact. Repeat this process throughout the hair until you achieve the desired texture.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears

Here are some tips for using thinning shears:

  • Only use thinning shears on dry hair.
  • Do not thin the hair too much. Over-thinning can create an uneven and choppy look.
  • Thinning shears should only be used on the ends of the hair, not the roots.
  • Always start with a small amount of hair and work your way up.
  • Regularly clean and sharpen your thinning shears for the best results.

FAQs

Can thinning shears be used on all hair types?

Thinning shears can be used on all hair types, but the type of shears used will depend on the thickness and texture of the hair.

Can thinning shears damage hair?

Thinning shears can damage hair if used incorrectly. It is important to only use thinning shears on dry hair and to not over-thin the hair.

Are thinning shears better than regular scissors?

Thinning shears are not better than regular scissors. They are simply a different tool used for a specific purpose. Thinning shears are used to remove bulk and add texture to hair, while regular scissors are used for cutting hair.

Hair Care

Thinning shears are a useful tool for creating a more natural and effortless look. However, it is important to use them correctly to avoid damaging hair. Regularly cleaning and sharpening your thinning shears will ensure the best results.

Remember, thinning shears do not permanently thin hair. The hair will grow back, and the thickness will return over time.
