How To Spike Short Black Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Nov 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to add some edge to your short black hair, spiking it up is a great option. Not only is it a cool and edgy style, but it's also relatively easy to achieve. In this article, we'll walk you through how to spike short black hair, including tips for styling and hair care.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Spike Short Black Hair
  2. Tips for Styling Short Spiky Hair
  3. FAQs
  4. Hair Care for Short Spiky Hair

How to Spike Short Black Hair

Before you begin styling your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. Here's how to spike short black hair:

  1. Start with a small amount of hair gel or pomade. You don't want to use too much, as this can make your hair look greasy and weighed down.
  2. Apply the gel or pomade evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the roots and tips.
  3. Using your fingers or a comb, work the gel or pomade into your hair, lifting and separating the strands as you go.
  4. Once you've achieved your desired level of spikiness, use a hair spray to set the style in place. This will help your spikes stay put throughout the day.

Remember, less is more when it comes to hair gel and pomade. Start with a small amount and add more as needed. You can always add more product, but it's harder to take it away once it's in your hair.

Alternative Methods

If you don't want to use hair gel or pomade, there are other methods you can try to spike your short black hair. Here are a few options:

  • Hairspray: Apply hairspray to your hair and then use your fingers to create spikes. This method works best if your hair is already somewhat spiky.
  • Blow-drying: After washing your hair, blow-dry it while using your fingers to create spikes. This method works best if you have naturally curly or wavy hair.
  • Teasing: Use a comb to tease your hair, then apply hairspray to hold the spikes in place.

Experiment with different methods to find the one that works best for your hair type and desired style.

Tips for Styling Short Spiky Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when styling your short spiky hair:

  • Don't overdo it: As mentioned earlier, less is more when it comes to hair gel and pomade. Don't use too much, or your hair will look greasy and weighed down.
  • Use the right products: Look for hair gel or pomade that's specifically designed for spiky hairstyles. These products will give you the hold and texture you need to create spikes.
  • Get regular haircuts: Short spiky hair looks best when it's freshly cut. Visit your hairstylist every 4-6 weeks to keep your hair looking sharp.

FAQs

How long does spiky hair last?

With the right products and techniques, spiky hair can last all day. However, it's important to use a hairspray to set the style and keep the spikes in place.

Can I spike my hair if it's not black?

Yes, you can spike your hair regardless of its color. However, keep in mind that some hair colors may require more product to achieve the desired level of spikiness.

Can I spike my hair without using products?

Yes, there are alternative methods for spiking your hair that don't involve products. Try blow-drying your hair while using your fingers to create spikes, or teasing your hair with a comb and then using hairspray to hold the spikes in place.

Hair Care for Short Spiky Hair

Short spiky hair requires regular maintenance to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips for caring for your spiky hairstyle:

  • Wash your hair regularly: Spiky hair can trap sweat and oil, so it's important to wash your hair regularly to prevent buildup.
  • Use a moisturizing shampoo: Look for a shampoo that's designed for dry or damaged hair, as spiky hair can be prone to dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and make it harder to achieve the spiky look you want. Stick to air-drying your hair or blow-drying it with your fingers.

By following these tips, you can keep your short spiky hair looking sharp and stylish.

Conclusion

In conclusion, spiking short black hair is an edgy and stylish look that's easy to achieve with the right products and techniques. Experiment with different methods to find the one that works best for you, and don't forget to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
