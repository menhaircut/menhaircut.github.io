Best Hair Colour For Older Brunettes A Complete Guide

Written by Eveline Nov 22, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you an older brunette looking to change up your hair colour? With so many options out there, it can be overwhelming to choose the best hair colour for you. In this article, we’ll go over everything you need to know about the best hair colour for older brunettes, including how to choose a colour, how to dye your hair at home, and how to take care of your newly coloured locks.

Table of Contents

  • How to Choose the Best Hair Colour for Older Brunettes
  • At-Home Hair Dyeing for Older Brunettes
  • Tips for Maintaining Your New Hair Colour
  • FAQ About the Best Hair Colour for Older Brunettes
  • Hair Care for Older Brunettes

How to Choose the Best Hair Colour for Older Brunettes

When it comes to choosing a hair colour, there are a few things to keep in mind. First and foremost, you want to choose a colour that complements your skin tone. For older brunettes, warm colours tend to work best. Think shades of honey, caramel, and golden brown.

Another thing to consider is your natural hair colour. If you’re looking for a low-maintenance option, you may want to choose a colour that’s close to your natural shade. This way, your roots won’t be as noticeable as your hair grows out.

Lastly, think about your lifestyle. If you’re someone who spends a lot of time outdoors, you may want to choose a colour that won’t fade as quickly in the sun.

At-Home Hair Dyeing for Older Brunettes

While it’s always best to have a professional dye your hair, it’s not always feasible. If you’re looking to dye your hair at home, there are a few things to keep in mind.

First, make sure you choose a dye that’s specifically formulated for older hair. As we age, our hair becomes more fragile and delicate, so you want to make sure you’re using a product that won’t damage your locks.

Next, follow the instructions carefully. It’s always best to do a patch test before applying the dye to your entire head. This will help you determine if you have any allergies or sensitivities to the product.

Finally, make sure you have all the necessary tools on hand. This includes gloves, a timer, and a hair dyeing brush.

Tips for Maintaining Your New Hair Colour

Once you’ve dyed your hair, you want to make sure it lasts as long as possible. Here are a few tips for maintaining your new hair colour:

  • Use a colour-safe shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently
  • Avoid heat styling as much as possible
  • Wear a hat or scarf when spending time in the sun

FAQ About the Best Hair Colour for Older Brunettes

Here are a few frequently asked questions about the best hair colour for older brunettes:

What are the best hair colours for older brunettes?

The best hair colours for older brunettes are warm shades of honey, caramel, and golden brown.

Can older brunettes dye their hair at home?

Yes, older brunettes can dye their hair at home. Just make sure you choose a dye that’s specifically formulated for older hair, and follow the instructions carefully.

Hair Care for Older Brunettes

Whether you’ve dyed your hair or not, it’s important to take good care of your locks. Here are a few tips for keeping your hair healthy and strong:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Eat a balanced diet that’s rich in vitamins and minerals

By following these tips, you can keep your hair looking and feeling its best, no matter your age or hair colour.
