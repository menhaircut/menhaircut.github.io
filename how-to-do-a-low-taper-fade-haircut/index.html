How To Do A Low Taper Fade Haircut

Written by Eveline Jul 16, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you curious about how to do a low taper fade haircut? This haircut has become increasingly popular in recent years, and it’s easy to see why. The low taper fade is a versatile haircut that can work for a variety of hair types and styles. In this article, we’ll be discussing how to do a low taper fade haircut step by step, including tips and frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  • How to Do a Low Taper Fade Haircut
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Step by Step

The low taper fade is a haircut that starts with longer hair on top and gradually gets shorter as it goes towards the neck. Here’s how to do a low taper fade in six easy steps:

Step 1: Preparation

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Comb your hair so that it’s smooth and easy to work with.

Step 2: Determine the Length

Determine how long you want your hair to be on top. Use clippers or scissors to cut your hair to the desired length.

Step 3: Create the Fade

Use clippers to create a fade by gradually decreasing the length of your hair as you move towards the back and sides. Start with a longer clipper guard and work your way down to a shorter guard. Blend the hair so that there are no harsh lines.

Step 4: Work on the Sides and Back

Use clippers or scissors to cut the hair on the sides and back of your head. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard. Blend the hair so that there are no harsh lines.

Step 5: Clean Up the Neckline

Use clippers to clean up the neckline. Make sure the hairline is straight and even.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired using a comb or your fingers.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when doing a low taper fade haircut:

  • Start with a longer clipper guard and work your way down to a shorter guard to create a gradual fade.
  • Use scissors to blend the hair if you don’t have clippers.
  • Take your time and be patient. A good haircut takes time and precision.
  • Use a comb to help guide your clippers or scissors.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about the low taper fade haircut:

Q: Can I do a low taper fade on my own?

A: While it’s possible to do a low taper fade on your own, it’s recommended that you have someone else do it for you. A professional barber or stylist can ensure that the haircut is done correctly and evenly.

Q: What hair types work best for a low taper fade?

A: A low taper fade can work for a variety of hair types, including straight, wavy, and curly hair.

Q: How often should I get a low taper fade?

A: The frequency of haircuts depends on how quickly your hair grows and how well you maintain your haircut. Generally, it’s recommended that you get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain your low taper fade.

Hair Care

After getting a low taper fade haircut, it’s important to take care of your hair to ensure that it stays healthy and looks great. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers and straighteners.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your haircut and prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to do a low taper fade haircut, you can try it out for yourself or ask your barber or stylist for the perfect haircut. With these tips and tricks, you’ll be able to achieve a stylish and versatile look that works for any occasion.
