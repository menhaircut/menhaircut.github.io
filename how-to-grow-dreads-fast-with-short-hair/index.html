How To Grow Dreads Fast With Short Hair Tips Steps And Hair Care

If you have short hair and want to grow dreads fast, you've come to the right place. In this blog post, we'll give you tips and steps on how to grow dreads fast with short hair, along with some hair care advice to keep your dreads healthy and looking great.

Tip 1: Start with clean hair

Before you start growing your dreads, make sure your hair is clean and free of any product buildup. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any dirt, oil, or buildup from your hair. This will help your hair lock up faster and make it easier to maintain your dreads.

Tip 2: Use a good dread wax or gel

Using a good quality dread wax or gel can help your hair lock up faster and make it easier to maintain your dreads. Look for a product that is specifically designed for dreadlocks and that contains natural ingredients like beeswax or shea butter.

Tip 3: Twisting and palm rolling

Twisting and palm rolling your hair can help your dreads lock up faster. To twist your hair, separate a small section of hair and twist it in a clockwise direction. To palm roll your hair, roll a section of hair between your palms in a back and forth motion. Repeat these steps on all sections of your hair.

Tip 4: Protective styling

Protective styling can help your dreads grow faster and prevent damage to your hair. Consider wearing a hat, scarf or bandana to protect your hair from the elements. You can also try braiding your hair to protect your dreads.

Tip 5: Be patient

Growing dreads takes time and patience. It can take anywhere from 6 months to a year for your dreads to fully mature. Don't get discouraged if your dreads don't look perfect right away. With time and proper care, they will eventually lock up and look great.

Step 1: Wash and condition your hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any dirt, oil, or buildup. After washing, condition your hair with a moisturizing conditioner to keep it soft and healthy.

Step 2: Divide your hair into sections

Divide your hair into small sections using hair ties or clips. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and how thick you want your dreads to be.

Step 3: Apply dread wax or gel

Apply a small amount of dread wax or gel to each section of your hair. Use your fingers to work the product into your hair, making sure to coat each strand evenly.

Step 4: Twist and palm roll

Twist each section of hair in a clockwise direction, using your fingers or a comb to help guide the hair. Once you've twisted a section, palm roll it between your hands to help it lock up.

Step 5: Repeat the process

Repeat the process on all sections of your hair, making sure to twist and palm roll each section thoroughly. Once you've finished, cover your hair with a scarf or bandana to protect it while it locks up.

FAQs

How often should I wash my dreads?

You should wash your dreads once a week to keep them clean and free of buildup. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove dirt and oil, and a moisturizing conditioner to keep your hair soft and healthy.

Can I grow dreads with relaxed hair?

Yes, you can grow dreads with relaxed hair. However, it may take longer for your hair to lock up, and you may need to use more product to help your hair hold its shape.

How long does it take to grow dreads with short hair?

It can take anywhere from 6 months to a year for your dreads to fully mature, depending on the thickness of your hair and how well you take care of it.

Hair Care for Dreads

Proper hair care is essential for keeping your dreads healthy and looking great. Here are some tips to help you take care of your hair:

  • Wash your hair once a week with a clarifying shampoo to remove dirt and buildup.
  • Use a moisturizing conditioner to keep your hair soft and healthy.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or greasy products, as they can cause buildup and make your hair look greasy.
  • Protect your hair from the elements by wearing a hat, scarf, or bandana.
  • Avoid using heat tools like flat irons or curling irons, as they can damage your hair and cause breakage.

By following these tips and steps, you can grow dreads fast with short hair and keep them looking great for years to come.
