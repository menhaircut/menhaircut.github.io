How Long Does Dreads Grow In A Month A Complete Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Dreadlocks, or simply dreads, are a popular and trendy hairstyle that has been around for centuries. It is a unique and versatile hairstyle that can be rocked by anyone, regardless of hair type and length. One of the most common questions asked by individuals who are new to the dreadlock world is how long does dreads grow in a month? In this guide, we will discuss everything you need to know about dreadlock growth, including tips on how to promote healthy hair growth and maintain your dreads.

How Much Does Hair Grow in a Month?

Before diving into the specifics of dreadlock growth, it's essential to understand how much hair grows in a month. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month. However, this varies depending on various factors such as age, genetics, diet, and overall health. Some people may experience faster hair growth, while others may have slower hair growth.

How Long Does It Take to Grow Dreads?

The time it takes to grow dreads varies from person to person. It can take up to six months to a year for your hair to lock fully, depending on your hair's texture, length, and thickness. However, with proper hair care and maintenance, you can speed up the process and achieve healthy, beautiful dreads in no time.

How Long Do Dreads Grow in a Month?

On average, dreadlocks grow about 1/4 to 1/2 inch per month. However, this also depends on various factors such as hair type, length, and thickness. It's essential to note that while dreads may grow at a slower pace, they tend to be more resilient and less prone to breakage compared to loose hair.

How to Promote Healthy Dreadlock Growth

If you're looking to promote healthy dreadlock growth, here are some tips you should follow:

1. Keep Your Scalp Clean

A clean scalp promotes healthy hair growth. Make sure to wash your hair regularly using a residue-free shampoo. You can also use an apple cider vinegar rinse to help remove any buildup on your scalp.

2. Moisturize Your Hair

Dreads tend to be drier than loose hair, so it's crucial to moisturize your hair regularly. Use a light oil or leave-in conditioner to keep your dreads hydrated.

3. Avoid Tight Styles

Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair, causing breakage and damage to your locks. Opt for looser styles that won't put too much tension on your hair.

4. Be Gentle When Styling

When styling your hair, be gentle to avoid pulling or damaging your locks. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, and avoid using heat styling tools.

5. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims helps keep your dreads healthy and promotes growth. Trimming your hair every 2-3 months helps prevent split ends and breakage.

FAQs About Dreadlock Growth

1. Can I Speed Up Dreadlock Growth?

You can speed up dreadlock growth by promoting healthy hair growth. Follow the tips mentioned above, and be patient. Remember, healthy hair growth takes time.

2. How Often Should I Wash My Hair?

You should wash your hair every 1-2 weeks using a residue-free shampoo. However, if you have an oily scalp, you may need to wash your hair more frequently.

3. Can I Use Hair Growth Products on My Dreads?

Yes, you can use hair growth products on your dreads. However, make sure to choose products that won't cause buildup on your scalp or hair.

Conclusion

So, how long does dreads grow in a month? On average, dreads grow about 1/4 to 1/2 inch per month. However, this varies depending on various factors such as hair type, length, and thickness. To promote healthy dreadlock growth, make sure to keep your scalp clean, moisturize your hair, avoid tight styles, be gentle when styling, and get regular trims. With proper hair care and maintenance, you can achieve healthy, beautiful dreads in no time.
