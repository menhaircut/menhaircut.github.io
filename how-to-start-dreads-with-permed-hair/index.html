How To Start Dreads With Permed Hair A Step By Step Guide

Are you looking to start dreadlocks but have permed hair? It can be a tricky process, but with the right steps and hair care, it's possible! In this post, we will guide you through the process of starting dreads with permed hair, including tips and answers to frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  • How to Start Dreads with Permed Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Starting Dreads with Permed Hair

Description

Permed hair has been chemically treated to achieve a curly, wavy, or kinky texture. This can make starting dreadlocks a bit more challenging, but it is still possible. Starting dreads with permed hair requires patience and proper hair care to avoid damage and breakage.

How to Start Dreads with Permed Hair

Before starting the dreadlocking process, it's important to note that permed hair is fragile and may break easily. It's recommended that you wait at least six months after getting a perm before starting dreadlocks. This allows your hair to recover from the chemical treatment and grow out a bit, making it more resilient.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here are the steps to follow when starting dreads with permed hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Shampoo and condition your hair, then let it air dry completely.
  2. Divide your hair into sections. Using a comb, create sections of hair that are about an inch wide. Secure each section with a hair tie or clip.
  3. Backcomb each section. Starting at the roots, use a fine-toothed comb to backcomb each section of hair. This creates knots and tangles that will eventually form into dreadlocks.
  4. Twist each section. After backcombing, twist each section of hair in the same direction until it tightens into a coil.
  5. Maintain your dreads. As your hair grows, continue to twist and maintain your dreadlocks. Use a residue-free locking gel or wax to help them form and hold their shape.

Tips for Starting Dreads with Permed Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when starting dreadlocks with permed hair:

  • Be patient. It can take several months for your dreads to fully form and mature.
  • Use a residue-free locking gel or wax. This will help your dreadlocks form and stay in place without leaving behind buildup or residue.
  • Avoid over-twisting. Over-twisting your dreads can cause damage and breakage, so be gentle when maintaining them.
  • Protect your hair at night. Wear a silk or satin bonnet or scarf to prevent your dreads from rubbing against cotton pillowcases, which can cause frizz and breakage.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreads with permed hair:

  • Can I start dreads with freshly permed hair? It's not recommended to start dreadlocks immediately after getting a perm. Wait at least six months to allow your hair to recover and grow out a bit.
  • Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on my dreadlocks? It's best to use a shampoo and conditioner that is specifically formulated for dreadlocks to avoid buildup and residue.
  • Can I color my hair while I have dreadlocks? Yes, but it's recommended that you wait until your dreads have fully matured before coloring your hair to avoid damage and breakage.

Hair Care for Dreads with Permed Hair

Maintaining healthy dreadlocks with permed hair requires proper hair care. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your dreads regularly. Use a residue-free shampoo and conditioner specifically formulated for dreadlocks.
  • Avoid using heavy oils and products. Heavy products can cause buildup and make it difficult for your dreads to lock and mature.
  • Protect your hair from heat. Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and blow dryers, as they can cause damage and breakage.
  • Get regular trims. Trimming your hair every few months can help prevent split ends and breakage.

Starting dreadlocks with permed hair is possible with patience and proper hair care. Follow these steps and tips to achieve healthy, beautiful dreadlocks that you can be proud of!
