How To Make Dreads With Curly Hair Step By Step Guide

  • How to Make Dreads with Curly Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Dreads with Curly Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Dreads with Curly Hair

Introduction

If you have curly hair and want to make dreads, you're in luck! Dreads can look amazing on curly hair, and you can achieve this look with a bit of patience and effort. In this step-by-step guide, we'll take you through the process of making dreads with curly hair, as well as provide tips for maintaining them and caring for your hair.

How to Make Dreads with Curly Hair

Before you start making dreads, it's important to note that curly hair tends to dread faster than straight hair. This is because curly hair is naturally more textured and has a tendency to tangle. Here's how to make dreads with curly hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step is to wash your hair thoroughly with a clarifying shampoo. This will remove any product buildup or oils that may prevent your hair from dreading properly. Rinse your hair well and towel dry it.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into small squares using a rat-tail comb. The size of the sections will depend on how thick you want your dreads to be. Take one section of hair and twist it tightly in one direction. Repeat this process with each section of hair until your entire head is twisted.

Step 3: Backcomb Your Hair

Starting at the roots, use a metal comb to backcomb each twisted section of hair. Hold the section of hair taut with one hand and use the comb to backcomb the hair in the opposite direction. This will create a tangled, matted effect that will eventually form into a dreadlock.

Step 4: Palm Roll Your Hair

Once you've backcombed your hair, use your palms to roll the hair tightly in one direction. This will help to smooth out any bumps or tangles and create a more even-looking dread. Repeat this process with each section of hair.

Step 5: Secure Your Dreads

Finally, use a small rubber band or hair tie to secure the end of each dread. You can also use a crochet hook to pull loose hairs into the dread, which will help to tighten and secure it even further.

Tips for Maintaining Dreads with Curly Hair

Maintaining dreads with curly hair requires a bit of extra effort, but the results are well worth it. Here are some tips for keeping your dreads looking their best:

  • Protect your dreads while sleeping by wearing a satin or silk bonnet or wrapping your hair in a scarf.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products on your dreads, as these can weigh them down and cause buildup.
  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo to keep them clean and free of buildup.
  • Use a crochet hook to pull any loose hairs into your dreads and tighten them up.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your dreads, as this can damage the hair and cause it to break.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about making dreads with curly hair:

Can I make dreads with curly hair?

Absolutely! Curly hair is actually easier to dread than straight hair, as it tends to tangle and knot more easily.

How long does it take to make dreads with curly hair?

The time it takes to make dreads with curly hair will depend on how thick your hair is and how many dreads you want. On average, it can take anywhere from 6 to 12 hours.

Do I need to cut my hair to make dreads?

No, you don't need to cut your hair to make dreads. You can make dreads with any length of hair.

Hair Care for Dreads with Curly Hair

Caring for dreads with curly hair requires a bit of extra effort, but it's important to keep your hair healthy and strong. Here are some tips for caring for your hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo to prevent buildup.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products on your hair, as these can weigh it down and cause buildup.
  • Use a crochet hook to pull any loose hairs into your dreads and tighten them up.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair, as this can damage the hair and cause it to break.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Congratulations! You now know how to make dreads with curly hair. With a bit of patience and effort, you can achieve a unique and stylish look that's all your own.
