How To Tell If You Would Look Good With Dark Hair

Written by Petter Jun 24, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're considering making a big change to your hair color, it's important to make sure that the new color will complement your skin tone and facial features. Dark hair can be a dramatic and bold choice, but it may not be the right fit for everyone. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks to help you determine if dark hair would look good on you.

Table of Contents

  • Considering Your Eye Color
  • Analyzing Your Face Shape
  • Assessing Your Hair Type
  • Trying On Wigs or Hair Extensions

Determining Your Skin Tone

Your skin tone is one of the most important factors to consider when deciding if dark hair would suit you. If you have a warm skin tone, with golden or yellow undertones, then warm shades of dark hair such as chocolate brown or chestnut may work well for you. If you have a cool skin tone, with pink or blue undertones, then cooler shades of dark hair such as jet black or cool dark brown may be a better option. You can determine your skin tone by looking at the veins on the inside of your wrist - if they appear green, you likely have a warm skin tone, while blue veins indicate a cool skin tone.

Considering Your Eye Color

Your eye color can also play a role in whether or not dark hair will suit you. If you have light-colored eyes such as blue or green, then dark hair can create a striking contrast that looks beautiful. If you have dark eyes such as brown or black, then dark hair can intensify your eye color and create a sultry and mysterious look. However, if you have hazel eyes, you may want to be cautious with dark hair, as the color can clash with the green or gold flecks in your eyes.

Analyzing Your Face Shape

Your face shape can also impact how well dark hair will suit you. If you have a round face, then dark hair can create a slimming effect and make your face appear more angular. If you have a long or oval-shaped face, then dark hair can add depth and dimension to your features. If you have a square face, then a softer, more layered haircut can balance out the sharp angles of your face.

Assessing Your Hair Type

Your hair type is another important consideration when deciding if dark hair is right for you. If you have fine or thin hair, then dark hair can make your hair appear fuller and thicker. If you have thick or coarse hair, then dark hair can add shine and depth to your hair. However, if you have damaged or brittle hair, then dark hair can emphasize those flaws and make your hair look even more damaged.

Trying On Wigs or Hair Extensions

If you're still unsure whether or not dark hair would look good on you, you can always try on wigs or hair extensions to get a better idea. Many wig stores or beauty supply shops offer a variety of wig colors and styles to try on, and some even have virtual try-on tools that allow you to see what you would look like with different hair colors. Trying on wigs or hair extensions can give you a better idea of what you would look like with dark hair without committing to a permanent change.

FAQ

What if I have a lot of gray hair?

Dark hair can be a great choice for covering up gray hair, but it's important to choose a shade that complements your skin tone and looks natural. Consider consulting with a hairstylist to determine the best shade of dark hair for your gray hair.

What if I have a bold or unique style?

Dark hair can complement a bold or unique style, but it's important to choose a shade that works well with your overall look. If you have a colorful or eclectic wardrobe, then a cooler shade of dark hair such as jet black or dark blue may be a good fit. If you have a more classic or sophisticated style, then a warmer shade of dark hair such as chocolate brown may be a better option.

Hair Care Tips for Dark Hair

Once you've decided to go for dark hair, it's important to take care of your new color to keep it looking vibrant and healthy. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for color-treated hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can strip the color and cause fading.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as flat irons and curling irons too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.

By following these tips and tricks, you can determine whether or not dark hair would look good on you and take care of your new color once you've made the change. Good luck!
