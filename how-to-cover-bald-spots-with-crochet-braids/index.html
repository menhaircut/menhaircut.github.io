How To Cover Bald Spots With Crochet Braids

Written by William Aug 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you struggling with bald spots or thinning hair? Do you want to hide those bald spots and still look stylish? Crochet braids might just be the solution you’ve been looking for. In this article, we’ll show you how to cover bald spots with crochet braids, step by step.

Description

Crochet braids are a protective hairstyle that involves attaching hair extensions onto your natural hair using a crochet needle. This technique is popular because it’s quick, easy, and affordable. It’s also a great way to cover bald spots or thinning hair.

How to Cover Bald Spots with Crochet Braids

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to cover bald spots with crochet braids:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start, make sure your hair is clean and moisturized. Detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb or brush. If you have any knots or tangles, use a detangling spray or conditioner to make it easier to comb through.

Step 2: Braid Your Hair

Next, you’ll need to braid your hair. You can either do this yourself or have a professional do it for you. The type of braids you choose will depend on the style you want to achieve. For example, if you want a curly or wavy style, you can do small or medium-sized braids. If you want a straight style, you can do larger braids.

Step 3: Install the Crochet Braids

Once your hair is braided, it’s time to install the crochet braids. Take a crochet needle and thread the hair extensions onto it. Insert the needle under the braid and pull the hair through. Tie a knot to secure the hair in place. Continue this process until all the bald spots are covered.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Once all the crochet braids are installed, you can style your hair however you like. You can leave it down or put it up in a ponytail or bun. You can also add accessories like beads or hair jewelry for a more stylish look.

Tips

Here are some tips to make the process easier:

  • Choose hair extensions that match your natural hair color and texture.
  • Make sure your braids are tight enough to hold the hair extensions in place.
  • Use a crochet needle with a hook that’s small enough to fit under the braid.
  • Don’t pull the hair extensions too tight, as this can cause tension on your scalp and lead to more hair loss.

FAQ

Are crochet braids good for covering bald spots?

Yes, crochet braids are a great way to cover bald spots or thinning hair. The hair extensions add volume and thickness to your hair, making it look fuller and more voluminous.

How long do crochet braids last?

Crochet braids can last anywhere from 4 to 8 weeks, depending on how well you take care of them. To make them last longer, avoid using heavy products or pulling on the hair extensions too much.

Can I wash my hair with crochet braids?

Yes, you can wash your hair with crochet braids. However, you should be gentle and use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid tangling or damaging the hair extensions.

Hair Care

To maintain healthy hair while wearing crochet braids, make sure to:

  • Moisturize your hair daily with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil.
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a satin or silk bonnet or pillowcase.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and the hair extensions.
  • Take breaks between hairstyles to allow your hair to rest and recover.

Conclusion

Crochet braids are a great way to cover bald spots or thinning hair. They’re easy to install, affordable, and versatile. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a stylish and natural-looking hairstyle that will boost your confidence and make you feel beautiful.
