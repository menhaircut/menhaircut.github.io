Why Do Dreadlocks Get Thicker

Written by Aprilia Nov 26, 2023 ¬∑ 4 min read

Dreadlocks have been around for centuries and have become a popular hairstyle for people of all ages, genders, and cultures. One thing that people frequently notice about their dreadlocks is that they tend to get thicker over time. If you're wondering why this happens, keep reading to learn more.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Start Dreadlocks
  • Step-by-Step Guide on How to Maintain Dreadlocks
  • Tips on How to Keep Your Dreadlocks Healthy
  • FAQs about Dreadlocks and Hair Care

How to Start Dreadlocks

If you're considering getting dreadlocks, you may be wondering how to start them. There are several methods to choose from, including:

  • Backcombing
  • Crochet Hooking
  • Twist and Rip
  • Freeform

No matter which method you choose, the key to starting dreadlocks is to have patience. It can take several months for your hair to fully lock up, so be prepared for a long journey.

Step-by-Step Guide on How to Maintain Dreadlocks

Once you've started your dreadlocks, it's important to maintain them properly to prevent them from becoming too thick or tangled. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to maintain your dreadlocks:

  1. Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo
  2. Avoid using heavy conditioners or oils on your scalp
  3. Separate your dreadlocks regularly to prevent them from merging together
  4. Retwist or palm roll your dreadlocks to keep them tight and neat
  5. Avoid using rubber bands or hair ties on your dreadlocks, as they can cause breakage

Following these steps will help keep your dreadlocks healthy and prevent them from getting too thick.

Tips on How to Keep Your Dreadlocks Healthy

In addition to the steps listed above, here are some additional tips on how to keep your dreadlocks healthy:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your scalp
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth
  • Stay hydrated to keep your hair and scalp moisturized

Following these tips will not only help keep your dreadlocks healthy, but it can also prevent them from getting too thick.

FAQs about Dreadlocks and Hair Care

1. Why do dreadlocks get thicker?

Dreadlocks get thicker because the hair strands become matted and compacted over time. This is due to the natural process of the hair locking together, as well as the maintenance techniques used to keep them tight and neat.

2. Can I thin out my dreadlocks?

Yes, you can thin out your dreadlocks by carefully cutting away some of the excess hair. However, it's important to be cautious when doing this to avoid damaging your dreadlocks or creating uneven sections.

3. How often should I wash my dreadlocks?

It's recommended to wash your dreadlocks at least once a week to keep them clean and healthy. However, some people may need to wash their hair more or less frequently depending on their hair type and lifestyle.

4. Can I use regular shampoo on my dreadlocks?

No, it's best to use a residue-free shampoo specifically designed for dreadlocks. Regular shampoos can leave behind a buildup that can cause your dreadlocks to become heavy, greasy, and difficult to maintain.

5. How long does it take for dreadlocks to form?

It can take several months to a year for dreadlocks to fully form, depending on your hair type, length, and maintenance routine. However, some people may see progress within a few weeks or months.

6. Can I color my dreadlocks?

Yes, you can color your dreadlocks, but it's important to use a dye that is safe for your hair type and won't cause damage. It's also recommended to seek the advice of a professional stylist to avoid any potential mistakes.

In conclusion, dreadlocks get thicker over time due to the natural process of the hair locking together and the maintenance techniques used to keep them neat and tight. However, following a proper hair care routine and being patient can help prevent your dreadlocks from becoming too thick.
