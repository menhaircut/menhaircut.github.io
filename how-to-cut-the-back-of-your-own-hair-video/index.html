How To Cut The Back Of Your Own Hair Video A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to save some money and cut your own hair at home, you may be wondering how to tackle the tricky task of cutting the back of your hair. Luckily, there are plenty of tutorials available online that can guide you through the process. In this article, we'll provide a step-by-step guide to cutting the back of your own hair, along with some tips and FAQs to help you achieve a salon-worthy look.

Description

Learning how to cut the back of your own hair can be intimidating at first, but with the right tools and guidance, it's definitely doable. Whether you're looking to trim your ends or go for a more dramatic cut, there are plenty of techniques you can use to achieve your desired look.

How-To

To get started, you'll need a few key tools: a pair of sharp hair-cutting scissors, a comb, and a handheld mirror. You'll also want to make sure you're working in a well-lit area with a clean workspace.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you begin cutting, it's important to wash and dry your hair. This will help you achieve a more even cut and make it easier to see what you're doing.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, you'll want to section your hair into manageable pieces. Start by parting your hair down the middle, then create two more sections on either side of your head. Clip each section out of the way using hair clips.

Step 3: Begin Cutting

Now it's time to start cutting. Begin with the bottom section of your hair, using your comb to separate a small section of hair at the back of your head. Hold the hair between your fingers and use your scissors to trim off any split ends or damaged hair.

Continue working your way up your head, taking small sections of hair and trimming them as needed. Be sure to check your progress in the handheld mirror to make sure you're cutting evenly on both sides.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've finished cutting the back of your hair, you'll want to blend the layers to create a more natural look. Use your scissors to feather the ends of your hair, taking small snips to create a soft, blended effect.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Finally, style your hair as desired. Whether you're going for a sleek, straight look or something more voluminous and curly, take the time to style your hair properly to show off your new cut.

Tips

  • Start with small snips and work your way up gradually to avoid any mistakes or mishaps.
  • Use a handheld mirror to check your progress as you cut.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help from a friend or family member if you're struggling to reach the back of your head.
  • Invest in a quality pair of hair-cutting scissors to ensure a clean, even cut.

FAQ

Can I cut the back of my hair without a handheld mirror?

While it's certainly possible to cut the back of your hair without a handheld mirror, it can be more challenging. Consider enlisting the help of a friend or family member to hold a larger mirror for you.

What if I make a mistake while cutting?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic! It's always better to take things slow and steady, and remember that hair grows back. If you're really unhappy with the result, you can always book an appointment with a professional stylist to fix any mistakes.

Hair Care

Remember to take good care of your hair after cutting it. This means regularly washing and conditioning your hair, and using a heat protectant spray when using hot tools like flat irons or curling wands. Additionally, consider investing in a quality hair mask or deep conditioner to keep your locks looking healthy and shiny.
