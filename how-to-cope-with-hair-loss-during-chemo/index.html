How To Cope With Hair Loss During Chemo

Written by Bardi Jul 25, 2023 · 4 min read

Dealing with hair loss during chemotherapy can be a difficult experience. However, it's important to remember that hair loss is a temporary side effect of treatment and there are ways to cope with it. Here are some tips and advice to help you deal with hair loss during chemo.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Cope with Hair Loss During Chemo
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips to Help You Cope
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips During Chemo

Step by Step Guide:

1. Prepare Yourself

Before starting chemo, it's important to prepare yourself for the possibility of hair loss. Talk to your doctor about what to expect and ask for recommendations for coping strategies.

2. Consider a Haircut

Some people find it helpful to cut their hair short before starting chemo. This can make the hair loss less noticeable and easier to manage.

3. Look into Wigs, Scarves, and Hats

There are many options available for covering your head during chemo. Wigs, scarves, and hats can all be good choices, depending on your personal style and preferences.

4. Take Care of Your Scalp

During chemo, it's important to keep your scalp clean and moisturized. Use gentle, fragrance-free products and avoid harsh chemicals that could irritate your skin.

5. Be Kind to Yourself

Dealing with hair loss can be emotional and challenging. Remember to be kind to yourself and take time for self-care activities that make you feel good.

Tips to Help You Cope:

1. Talk to Others

Connecting with others who have gone through hair loss during chemo can be helpful. Consider joining a support group or talking to friends and family members who have been through similar experiences.

2. Focus on Other Features

Remember that you have many other features that make you unique and beautiful. Focus on highlighting your eyes, lips, or other features that you love.

3. Experiment with Scarves and Hats

Scarves and hats can be fun accessories that allow you to express your personal style. Try experimenting with different colors and styles to find the ones that work best for you.

4. Consider a Wig

If you're interested in wearing a wig, consider talking to a hair stylist who specializes in wigs. They can help you find the right style and fit for your needs.

5. Stay Positive

Remember that hair loss is a temporary side effect of chemo. Try to stay positive and focus on the positive aspects of your treatment and recovery.

Frequently Asked Questions:

1. Will My Hair Grow Back?

Yes, in most cases your hair will grow back after chemo. However, it may take several months for your hair to return to its normal thickness and texture.

2. Will My Hair Grow Back Differently?

Sometimes hair may grow back with a different texture or color. However, in most cases it will eventually return to its normal state.

3. Can I Dye My Hair During Chemo?

It's generally not recommended to dye your hair during chemo, as the chemicals in hair dye could irritate your scalp. However, you can talk to your doctor about specific recommendations for your treatment.

Hair Care Tips During Chemo:

1. Use Gentle Products

During chemo, it's important to use gentle, fragrance-free products on your hair and scalp. Look for products that are specifically designed for sensitive skin.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like blow dryers and straighteners can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Avoid using these tools during chemo.

3. Be Gentle When Brushing

When brushing your hair during chemo, be gentle to avoid pulling out more hair. Use a soft-bristled brush and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair.

4. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

During chemo, your scalp may be more sensitive to the sun. Wear a hat or use a scarf to protect your head when you're outside.

Remember that hair loss during chemo is a temporary side effect of treatment. With the right support and coping strategies, you can manage this challenging experience and emerge stronger on the other side.
