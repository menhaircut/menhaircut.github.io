How To Remove Bulk From Thick Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Aug 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing, but it can also be a curse. If you're tired of dealing with the bulk and weight of your thick hair, you're not alone. Many people struggle with this issue, but there are ways to remove bulk from your hair and make it more manageable. In this article, we'll show you how to remove bulk from thick hair in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

  2. How to Remove Bulk from Thick Hair

Description

If you have thick hair, you know that it can be difficult to manage. Not only does it take longer to dry and style, but it can also be heavy and bulky. Removing bulk from thick hair can help make it more manageable and easier to style.

There are several ways to remove bulk from your hair, including thinning shears, razor cutting, and texturizing. We'll go over each of these methods in more detail below.

How to Remove Bulk from Thick Hair

Before you start removing bulk from your hair, it's important to understand that not all methods will work for everyone. The best method for you will depend on your hair type, texture, and the look you're trying to achieve.

That being said, here are three common methods for removing bulk from thick hair:

1. Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are scissors that have teeth on one blade and a straight edge on the other. They're designed to remove bulk from hair without changing the length. Thinning shears can be used on wet or dry hair, but it's best to use them on dry hair so you can see exactly how much hair you're removing.

To use thinning shears, take a small section of hair and hold it straight up. Open the thinning shears and place them about halfway down the section of hair. Close the shears and gently slide them down the hair, removing some of the bulk. Repeat this process on the rest of your hair until you've achieved the desired look.

2. Razor Cutting

Razor cutting is another method for removing bulk from thick hair. It involves using a razor blade to cut the hair at an angle, which creates texture and removes bulk. Razor cutting is best done by a professional, as it can be difficult to do correctly on your own.

To razor cut your hair, your stylist will take small sections of hair and use a razor blade to cut them at an angle. This creates texture and removes bulk, making your hair lighter and easier to manage.

3. Texturizing

Texturizing is a method for removing bulk from thick hair by cutting into the hair to create different lengths. This creates texture and movement in the hair, making it easier to style. Texturizing can be done with scissors or a razor blade.

To texturize your hair, your stylist will take small sections of hair and cut into them at different lengths. This creates texture and movement in the hair, making it lighter and more manageable.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for removing bulk from thick hair using thinning shears:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Section your hair into manageable sections.
  3. Take a small section of hair and hold it straight up.
  4. Open the thinning shears and place them about halfway down the section of hair.
  5. Close the shears and gently slide them down the hair, removing some of the bulk.
  6. Repeat this process on the rest of your hair until you've achieved the desired look.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hair

Now that you've removed bulk from your hair, it's important to maintain it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips for maintaining your hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent your hair from drying out.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair too often, as this can cause damage.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy.

FAQs

1. Will removing bulk from my hair make it look thinner?

Yes, removing bulk from your hair can make it look thinner. However, it can also make it look more textured and voluminous, depending on the method you use.

2. Can I remove bulk from my hair myself?

While it's possible to remove bulk from your hair yourself, it's best to have it done by a professional stylist. This will ensure that the cut is even and that you achieve the desired look.

3. How often should I remove bulk from my hair?

This will depend on how quickly your hair grows and how quickly it starts to feel heavy and bulky again. In general, you should aim to remove bulk from your hair every 6-12 months.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Thick hair requires a little extra care to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips for caring for your thick hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Try not to wash your hair every day, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

With these tips and methods for removing bulk from your hair, you'll be able to achieve a lighter, more manageable look that you'll love. Remember to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
