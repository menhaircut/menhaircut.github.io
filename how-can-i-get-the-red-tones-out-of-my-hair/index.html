How Can I Get The Red Tones Out Of My Hair

Written by Bardi Oct 21, 2023 · 4 min read

If you've recently dyed your hair and ended up with a red tone that's just not working for you, don't worry! There are several ways to get those red tones out of your hair and achieve your desired color. In this article, we'll go over some tips, tricks, and FAQs to help you get the perfect hair color.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get the Red Tones Out of Your Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Achieving Your Desired Color
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Color Treatment

How to Get the Red Tones Out of Your Hair

Before we get started, it's important to understand that removing red tones from your hair can be a challenging process. It's always a good idea to seek help from a professional stylist who can guide you through the process and help you achieve the desired color. However, if you want to try it on your own, here are some options:

Bleach

Bleaching your hair is the most effective way to remove red tones, but it's also the most damaging. Bleaching your hair can cause dryness, breakage, and even hair loss. If you choose to bleach your hair, it's essential to follow the instructions carefully and use a good quality bleach. Start with a small section of hair to avoid damaging your entire head.

Color Remover

Color removers are specifically designed to remove artificial hair color. They are less damaging than bleach but may not be as effective. You can find color removers at your local beauty supply store. Follow the instructions carefully and use gloves to avoid skin irritation.

Toning Shampoo

Toning shampoos are designed to neutralize unwanted tones in your hair. They won't remove red tones completely, but they can help to tone them down. Use a toning shampoo that's specifically designed for your hair color and follow the instructions carefully.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to get the red tones out of your hair:

  1. Choose the method that works best for you.
  2. Read the instructions carefully before starting.
  3. Wear gloves to avoid skin irritation.
  4. Start with a small section of hair to avoid damaging your entire head.
  5. Apply the product evenly to your hair and work it through from roots to ends.
  6. Leave the product on for the recommended time.
  7. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  8. Apply a deep conditioner to restore moisture to your hair.
  9. Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water.
  10. Repeat the process if necessary.

Tips for Achieving Your Desired Color

Here are some tips to help you achieve your desired color:

  • Choose a color that complements your skin tone.
  • Use a good quality hair dye.
  • Follow the instructions carefully.
  • Do a strand test before applying the dye to your entire head.
  • Use a deep conditioner to restore moisture to your hair after dyeing.

FAQs

Can I dye my hair after removing the red tones?

Yes, you can dye your hair after removing the red tones. However, it's important to wait at least a week before dyeing your hair to avoid damaging it further.

Will removing the red tones damage my hair?

Removing the red tones from your hair can be damaging, especially if you use bleach. It's important to follow the instructions carefully and use a good quality product to minimize damage.

Can I remove the red tones from my hair naturally?

There are no natural ways to remove artificial hair color. However, you can use natural ingredients such as coconut oil or avocado to moisturize your hair after color treatment.

Hair Care After Color Treatment

After removing the red tones from your hair, it's important to take good care of it to avoid further damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping the color from your hair.
  • Apply a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions.

With these tips and tricks, you can get the red tones out of your hair and achieve your desired color. Remember to take good care of your hair and seek professional help if necessary. Happy hair coloring!
