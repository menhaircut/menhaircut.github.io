Can Your Hair Fall Out After Having A Baby

Written by Frank Jun 18, 2023 · 4 min read

Welcome to our blog post about one of the most common concerns of new mothers - hair loss after childbirth. In this article, we will discuss the reasons behind postpartum hair loss, how to deal with it, and most importantly, how to prevent it. So, let's get started with the table of content:

  3. Step by Step Guide to Prevent Hair Loss
  4. Tips for Taking Care of Your Hair After Pregnancy

Description

Postpartum hair loss, also known as telogen effluvium, is a common condition that affects up to 50% of women after giving birth. During pregnancy, your hormones change, which can lead to thicker, fuller hair. However, after delivery, your hormones return to their normal levels, causing the hair to fall out at a faster rate than usual. This process usually begins around three months after childbirth and can last up to a year. While it can be distressing, it is a normal part of the postpartum experience.

How to Deal with Postpartum Hair Loss

The good news is that postpartum hair loss is usually temporary and your hair will grow back. Here are some tips to help you deal with it:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like straighteners and curling irons
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet
  • Take a multivitamin that includes biotin and iron
  • Reduce stress through meditation or yoga

Step by Step Guide to Prevent Hair Loss

If you want to take a more proactive approach to preventing postpartum hair loss, here is a step by step guide:

  1. Start taking a prenatal vitamin before you get pregnant. This will ensure that your body has all the nutrients it needs for healthy hair growth.
  2. Eat a diet rich in protein, iron, and vitamins A, C, and E. Foods like eggs, spinach, sweet potatoes, and almonds are all great for hair health.
  3. Avoid over-washing or over-brushing your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  4. Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  5. Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or relaxers.

Tips for Taking Care of Your Hair After Pregnancy

Now that you know how to prevent postpartum hair loss, here are some tips for taking care of your hair after pregnancy:

  • Be gentle when washing or brushing your hair
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle wet hair
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and curling irons
  • Consider getting a shorter haircut that is easier to manage
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about postpartum hair loss:

When does postpartum hair loss start?

Postpartum hair loss usually starts around three months after giving birth.

How long does postpartum hair loss last?

Postpartum hair loss can last up to a year, but most women see their hair return to its normal thickness within six to nine months.

Is postpartum hair loss preventable?

While you can't completely prevent postpartum hair loss, you can take steps to minimize it by following a healthy diet, taking care of your hair, and avoiding harsh chemicals and heat styling tools.

Hair Care Products for Postpartum Hair Loss

If you're looking for hair care products to help prevent postpartum hair loss, look for ones that contain biotin, iron, and other vitamins and minerals that promote hair health. Some popular options include:

  • Monat Hair Care System
  • Viviscal Hair Growth Supplements
  • Nioxin Cleanser Shampoo
  • Keranique Hair Regrowth Treatment

Final Thoughts: Remember that postpartum hair loss is a normal part of the postpartum experience, and it will eventually grow back. By following a healthy diet, taking care of your hair, and avoiding harsh chemicals and heat styling tools, you can minimize the amount of hair loss you experience. If you're concerned about the amount of hair you're losing, talk to your doctor or a dermatologist for advice.
