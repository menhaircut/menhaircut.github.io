How To Stop Your Hair From Falling Out After Bleaching

Written by Frank Sep 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you worried about your hair falling out after bleaching? You are not alone. Bleaching can cause damage to your hair, and if not done correctly, it can lead to hair breakage and hair loss. In this article, we will discuss how to stop your hair from falling out after bleaching.

  • How to Stop Your Hair from Falling Out After Bleaching

Description

Bleaching is a chemical process that removes the natural color from your hair strands. The process involves using a mixture of peroxide and bleach powder. This mixture can be damaging to your hair if not done correctly. It can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause it to become brittle and prone to breakage.

If you have already bleached your hair and are now experiencing hair fall, don't panic. There are several things you can do to stop your hair from falling out and promote healthy hair growth.

How to Stop Your Hair from Falling Out After Bleaching

Below are some tips on how to stop your hair from falling out after bleaching:

1. Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments can help to strengthen your hair and prevent breakage. Look for a protein treatment that contains hydrolyzed keratin or collagen. These ingredients can help to repair the damage caused by bleaching and restore your hair's elasticity.

2. Moisturize Your Hair

Bleaching can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and prone to breakage. To prevent this, you need to moisturize your hair regularly. Look for a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner that contains natural oils such as argan oil, coconut oil, or jojoba oil. These oils can help to hydrate your hair and prevent breakage.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons can further damage your hair. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from the heat. Also, avoid using these tools on wet hair as this can cause your hair to become more brittle and prone to breakage.

4. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help to prevent split ends and breakage. If you notice that your hair is breaking or falling out, it may be time to trim your hair. A trim can help to remove damaged ends and promote healthy hair growth.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to stop your hair from falling out after bleaching:

Step 1: Use a Protein Treatment

Apply a protein treatment to your hair once a week. Leave it on for the recommended time and then rinse it out.

Step 2: Moisturize Your Hair

Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner every time you wash your hair. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to keep it hydrated throughout the day.

Step 3: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to protect your hair.

Step 4: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you stop your hair from falling out after bleaching:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair to prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using hair elastics that can pull on your hair and cause it to break.
  • Wear a hat or scarf when you are outside to protect your hair from the sun and wind.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair such as hair dyes and relaxers.

FAQs

Q: Can I color my hair after bleaching?

A: It is not recommended to color your hair right after bleaching as this can further damage your hair. Wait at least 2-3 weeks before coloring your hair.

Q: Can I bleach my hair again if it falls out?

A: It is not recommended to bleach your hair again if it is falling out. This can cause further damage to your hair and lead to more breakage.

Q: How long does it take for hair to grow back after falling out?

A: It can take several months for hair to grow back after falling out. Be patient and take good care of your hair to promote healthy hair growth.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential to prevent breakage and hair loss. Follow the tips above to stop your hair from falling out after bleaching. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine.

If you are experiencing severe hair loss or breakage, it is best to consult a professional hairstylist or a trichologist for advice.
