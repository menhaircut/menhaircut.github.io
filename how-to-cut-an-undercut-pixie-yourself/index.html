How To Cut An Undercut Pixie Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you ready to take the plunge and try out an edgy undercut pixie? Whether you're looking to save money on salon visits or just prefer the DIY approach, cutting your own hair can seem daunting at first. But with the right tools, technique, and a little patience, you can achieve a stylish and low-maintenance look that's perfect for summer. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to cut an undercut pixie yourself, as well as provide some tips and hair care advice to keep your new 'do looking fresh.

How to

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, make sure you have the following tools on hand:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Clippers with guard attachments
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or elastics
  • Mirror

It's also a good idea to have a towel or cape to drape over your shoulders to catch any hair clippings.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into two parts: the top section and the undercut section. Use a comb to part your hair horizontally from ear to ear, clipping the top section out of the way. Next, use clippers with a guard attachment to buzz the hair on the undercut section of your head. The length of the buzz cut is up to you, but a 2-3 guard is a good starting point.

Step 3: Cut the Top Section

Once you've buzzed the undercut section, unclip the top section and comb it straight up. Use your hair cutting scissors to cut the hair to your desired length, starting from the back and working your way forward. It's important to take your time and cut small sections at a time, checking your progress in the mirror as you go.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut the top section to your desired length, use your scissors to blend the layers between the top and undercut sections. To do this, take small sections of hair and use your scissors to cut at an angle, creating a gradual blend between the two sections. Be careful not to cut too much or create any choppy layers.

Step 5: Clean Up the Edges

Finally, use your clippers or scissors to clean up the edges around your ears and nape of your neck. You can also use a razor or trimmer to create a more textured look, if desired.

Tips

  • Start with a longer length and work your way shorter, as it's easier to take off more hair than add it back on.
  • Use a spray bottle to mist your hair with water before cutting, as wet hair is easier to manage and cut.
  • Don't be afraid to ask a friend or family member for help, especially when it comes to the back of your head.
  • When in doubt, consult a professional hairstylist for guidance and advice.

FAQ

How long does an undercut pixie last?

An undercut pixie can last anywhere from 4-8 weeks, depending on how fast your hair grows and how often you trim it.

Can I style an undercut pixie in different ways?

Absolutely! An undercut pixie is versatile and can be styled in a variety of ways, from sleek and polished to messy and textured. Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for you.

How do I maintain my undercut pixie?

Regular trims every 4-6 weeks, along with using a quality shampoo and conditioner, can help maintain your undercut pixie and keep it looking fresh.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and appearance. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioning treatment to keep your hair hydrated and nourished, and avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage. Consider investing in a quality hair oil or serum to keep your hair smooth and shiny, and don't forget to protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protective spray.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your own undercut pixie and rock a bold and stylish look. Happy cutting!
