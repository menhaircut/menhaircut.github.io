How To Cut Your Own Hair Straight Shoulder Length A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Sep 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the hair salon every time you need a trim? Or maybe you just want to try something new and cut your own hair straight shoulder length? Whatever the reason, cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you've never done it before. But with a little bit of patience and practice, you can achieve a beautiful, straight, shoulder-length haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll guide you through the process step by step, from preparation to finishing touches.

Table of Contents

  1. Preparation
  2. Sectioning Your Hair
  3. Cutting Your Hair
  4. Finishing Touches
  5. Tips for a Successful Haircut
  6. FAQs

Preparation

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather a few supplies:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • Hair clips or elastics to section your hair
  • A mirror
  • A towel or cape to catch any loose hair

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before you start. Wet hair is harder to cut and can cause uneven results.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is an important part of the cutting process. It helps you to stay organized and ensures that you cut each section evenly. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start by parting your hair down the middle, from your forehead to the nape of your neck.
  2. Clip one side of your hair up and out of the way.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the unclipped side and comb it straight down. Make sure the section is even and free of tangles.
  4. Hold the section of hair between your index and middle fingers, then slide your fingers down to the length you want to cut. For a shoulder-length cut, aim for the middle of your shoulder blades.
  5. Use your scissors to cut straight across the section of hair. Take your time and make sure the scissors are sharp and clean.
  6. Repeat this process, working your way around your head until you've cut all of the sections on one side.
  7. Unclip the other side of your hair and repeat the process.

Cutting Your Hair

Now that you've sectioned your hair, it's time to start cutting. Here are some tips to help you get the best results:

  • Take your time and cut small sections of hair at a time. It's better to cut too little than too much.
  • Use the comb to keep your sections even and to guide your scissors.
  • Start at the back of your head and work your way towards the front. This will help you to keep your sections even and avoid choppy layers.
  • Be careful not to cut too much off the ends of your hair. Remember, you can always go back and trim more if needed.

Finishing Touches

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's time to add some finishing touches. Here's what to do:

  • Check your hair in the mirror to make sure it's even and straight. If you notice any uneven sections, go back and trim them.
  • Use the scissors to soften any harsh lines or angles.
  • Add some layers or texture if desired, using the same sectioning and cutting techniques.

Tips for a Successful Haircut

Cutting your own hair can be tricky, but these tips will help you to achieve a beautiful shoulder-length cut:

  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. Using regular scissors or dull blades can result in uneven and damaged hair.
  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections. This will help you to avoid cutting too much off at once.
  • Be patient and take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and time-consuming.

FAQs

Is it easy to cut your own hair?

It can be challenging to cut your own hair, especially if you've never done it before. However, with a little bit of practice and patience, you can achieve a beautiful haircut from home.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic! You can always go back and trim the section again. If you're really unsure, it's always best to start with a small trim and work your way up to a larger cut.

Hair Care After the Cut

Once you've cut your hair, it's important to take good care of it to maintain its health and shine. Here are some tips:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat tools like curling irons or straighteners, as they can damage your hair.
  • Visit a professional stylist for regular trims to keep your hair looking its best.

Cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience, but it's important to take your time and be patient. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful, straight shoulder-length cut that you'll love. Good luck!
