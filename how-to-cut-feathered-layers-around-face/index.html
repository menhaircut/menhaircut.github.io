How To Cut Feathered Layers Around Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your dull and lifeless hair? Do you want to add some volume and movement to your locks? Well, you're in luck because feathered layers around the face can do just that! In this article, we'll guide you through the process of cutting feathered layers around the face, step-by-step. So, let's get started!

Description

Feathered layers around the face are a popular hairstyle that adds volume and movement to your hair. These layers are cut in a way that they frame your face and give your hair a soft and feathered look. The layers are usually longer towards the front and shorter towards the back, creating a natural and effortless look.

How to Cut Feathered Layers Around Face

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting. Wet hair can stretch and lead to uneven cuts.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair by parting it down the middle and then from ear to ear. This will create four sections of hair, two in the front and two in the back.

Step 3: Cut the First Section

Begin with one of the front sections. Hold the section of hair between your index and middle fingers and point-cut the ends to create a soft and feathered look. Continue cutting the section until you reach your desired length.

Step 4: Cut the Second Section

Repeat step 3 with the other front section of hair.

Step 5: Cut the Third Section

Now, move on to the back sections of hair. Take one section at a time and point-cut the ends to create feathered layers. Start at the top of the section and work your way down to the ends.

Step 6: Cut the Fourth Section

Repeat step 5 with the other back section of hair.

Step 7: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all four sections, it's time to blend the layers. Comb your hair and check for any unevenness. If you find any, use your scissors to feather the ends and blend the layers together.

Step 8: Trim the Bangs

If you have bangs, trim them to your desired length. Remember to point-cut the ends to create a soft and feathered look.

Step 9: Style Your Hair

Finally, style your hair as desired. You can use a curling iron or straightener to add some texture and movement to your locks.

Tips

  • Always use sharp scissors to ensure a clean and precise cut.
  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • When cutting the back sections, use a mirror to ensure symmetry.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're unsure about cutting your own hair.
  • Take breaks if needed to avoid hand fatigue.

FAQ

Can I cut feathered layers around my face on my own?

Yes, you can cut feathered layers around your face on your own. However, it's always best to seek professional help if you're unsure about cutting your own hair.

What type of scissors should I use to cut feathered layers?

Use sharp hair-cutting scissors to ensure a clean and precise cut. Don't use regular scissors as they can lead to split ends and uneven cuts.

How often should I trim my feathered layers?

It's recommended to trim your feathered layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain their shape and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

To keep your feathered layers looking their best, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner to keep your locks hydrated and healthy. Avoid using heat styling tools too often and always use a heat protectant spray before styling. Finally, protect your hair from the sun and pollution by wearing a hat or scarf when outdoors.

Now that you know how to cut feathered layers around your face, go ahead and give it a try! With a little practice, you'll be able to create a beautiful and effortless hairstyle that's sure to turn heads.
