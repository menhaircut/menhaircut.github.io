How To Thin Out Thick Hair With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Oct 08, 2023 · 4 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can make your hair look voluminous and full, it can also make it difficult to manage and style. If you're tired of dealing with heavy, bulky hair, then it's time to learn how to thin out your locks with clippers. In this step-by-step guide, we'll show you how to thin out thick hair with clippers. Let's get started!

Description

Thick hair can be a challenge to manage, especially if you're looking for a sleek, polished look. Clipping your hair is a great way to thin it out and create a more manageable style. With a few simple tools and techniques, you can thin out your hair and create a more streamlined look.

How to Thin Out Thick Hair with Clippers

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you get started, you'll need to gather all the tools you'll need. You'll need a pair of hair clippers, a comb, and a pair of scissors. Make sure your clippers are sharp and well-maintained so that they'll work effectively.

Step 2: Wash Your Hair

Before you start clipping, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will help you get a more accurate cut and avoid any tangles or snags that can occur when working with damp hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using a comb. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward. This will help you keep track of which sections you've already clipped and ensure that you get an even cut.

Step 4: Clip the Ends

Start at the bottom of each section and work your way up. Use the clippers to cut off the ends of your hair. Make sure to use short, quick strokes and avoid cutting too much at once. You can always go back and trim more if needed.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've clipped the ends of each section, it's time to blend the layers. Use your scissors to trim any longer pieces and create a more even look. Take your time and work carefully to avoid any mistakes.

Step 6: Check Your Progress

After you've finished clipping and blending, take a step back and check your progress. Make any final adjustments as needed to ensure that your hair looks even and well-thinned.

Tips for Thinning Out Thick Hair with Clippers

1. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one as needed.

2. Don't cut too much at once. It's better to take your time and work gradually to avoid any mistakes.

3. Work in even sections to ensure that you get an even cut.

4. Use a comb to help guide your clippers and create a more uniform look.

5. Be patient and take your time. Thinning out thick hair can be a time-consuming process, but it's worth it in the end.

FAQs

Q: Can I thin out my hair with scissors instead of clippers?

A: Yes, you can thin out your hair with scissors, but clippers are generally more effective and efficient.

Q: How often should I thin out my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, you should aim to thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care for Thinned Out Hair

After you've thinned out your hair, it's important to take good care of it to maintain its health and appearance. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and nourished. Avoid using too much heat when styling, and consider using a heat protectant spray to minimize damage. Additionally, trim your hair regularly to avoid split ends and promote healthy growth.

Thinning out thick hair with clippers can be a great way to achieve a more manageable, polished look. By following these simple steps and tips, you can create a well-thinned hairstyle that you'll love. Happy clipping!
