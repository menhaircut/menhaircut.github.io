Best Bangs For Thick Straight Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Aug 25, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have thick straight hair, you know the struggle of finding the perfect hairstyle that complements your hair type. One of the best ways to switch up your look and add some edge to your hair is by getting bangs. However, not all bangs work well with thick straight hair. In this article, we’ll guide you through the best bangs for thick straight hair, how to style them, and how to take care of them.

Table of Contents

  • How to Choose the Best Bangs for Thick Straight Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide on How to Style Your Bangs
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Bangs
  • FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions About Bangs for Thick Straight Hair
  • Hair Care Tips for Thick Straight Hair with Bangs

How to Choose the Best Bangs for Thick Straight Hair

When it comes to choosing the best bangs for your thick straight hair, you need to consider your face shape and hair texture. Here are some bang styles that work well with thick straight hair:

Straight Bangs

Straight bangs are the most common and classic type of bangs. They work well with thick straight hair because they don’t require too much maintenance. Straight bangs are also versatile and can be styled in different ways.

Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs are another great option for thick straight hair. They add some dimension to your hair and frame your face nicely. Side-swept bangs can also be styled in different ways, such as pinning them back or curling them.

Blunt Bangs

Blunt bangs are a bold and edgy option for those who want to make a statement. They work well with thick straight hair because they don’t require too much styling. Blunt bangs also add some structure to your face.

Step-by-Step Guide on How to Style Your Bangs

Styling your bangs can be a bit tricky, especially if you’re new to having bangs. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to style your bangs:

Step 1: Wash and Blow Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and blow-drying it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before styling your bangs.

Step 2: Section Your Bangs

Section your bangs from the rest of your hair using a comb or your fingers. This will make it easier to style your bangs without interfering with the rest of your hair.

Step 3: Use a Straightener or Curling Iron

Depending on the type of bangs you have, use a straightener or curling iron to style your bangs. For straight bangs, use a straightener to keep them sleek and straight. For side-swept bangs, use a curling iron to add some texture.

Step 4: Apply Hairspray

Once you’re happy with your bangs, apply some hairspray to keep them in place. Avoid using too much hairspray, as this can make your hair look stiff and unnatural.

Tips for Maintaining Your Bangs

Keeping your bangs looking fresh and stylish requires some maintenance. Here are some tips for maintaining your bangs:

Trim Your Bangs Regularly

To keep your bangs looking neat and tidy, trim them regularly. You can either do this yourself or visit a hairstylist.

Avoid Touching Your Bangs Too Much

Touching your bangs too much can make them look greasy and messy. Try to avoid touching your bangs as much as possible.

Use Dry Shampoo

Dry shampoo can be a lifesaver for those days when you don’t have time to wash your hair. It can also help absorb excess oil and keep your bangs looking fresh.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions About Bangs for Thick Straight Hair

Q: Can I still wear bangs if I have a round face?

A: Yes, you can still wear bangs if you have a round face. Opt for side-swept or angled bangs that elongate your face.

Q: How do I know if straight bangs will suit me?

A: Straight bangs suit most face shapes, but if you’re unsure, try using a hair app to see how you would look with straight bangs.

Q: How do I style my bangs if I have oily hair?

A: Use dry shampoo to absorb excess oil and keep your bangs looking fresh.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Straight Hair with Bangs

Having thick straight hair with bangs requires some extra care to keep your hair looking healthy and voluminous. Here are some hair care tips for thick straight hair with bangs:

Use a Hydrating Shampoo and Conditioner

Thick straight hair can be prone to dryness, so use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

Avoid Using Too Much Heat

Using too much heat can damage your hair and make it look dull and lifeless. Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.

Brush Your Hair Regularly

Brushing your hair regularly can help distribute natural oils and keep your hair looking shiny and healthy.

Conclusion

Choosing the best bangs for thick straight hair requires some consideration of your face shape and hair texture. Straight, side-swept, and blunt bangs are all great options for thick straight hair. Styling and maintaining your bangs requires some extra care, but with the right tips and tools, you can keep your bangs looking fresh and stylish.
