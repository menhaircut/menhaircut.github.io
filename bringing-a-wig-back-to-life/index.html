Bringing A Wig Back To Life Tips And Tricks

Written by Daniel Sep 12, 2023 · 4 min read

Wigs can be a lifesaver when you want to change your look without committing to a haircut or color change. But after a while, even the best wigs can start to look a little lifeless. If you're wondering how to bring a wig back to life, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks for reviving your wig and making it look as good as new.

How to Revive a Wig: Step by Step

Step 1: Detangle the Wig

Before you can start reviving your wig, you need to detangle it. Use a wide-tooth comb or wig brush to gently work out any knots or tangles. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots to avoid damaging the hair fibers.

Step 2: Wash the Wig

Next, it's time to wash the wig. Fill a sink or basin with cool water and add a small amount of wig shampoo. Gently swish the wig around in the water, being careful not to rub or scrub the fibers. Rinse the wig thoroughly with cool water to remove all the shampoo.

Step 3: Condition the Wig

After washing, it's important to condition the wig to help restore its softness and shine. Apply a small amount of wig conditioner to the hair and work it through with your fingers. Let the conditioner sit on the hair for a few minutes before rinsing thoroughly with cool water.

Step 4: Air Dry the Wig

Once the wig is clean and conditioned, it's time to dry it. Avoid using heat tools like blow dryers or curling irons, as these can damage the fibers. Instead, gently blot the wig with a towel to remove excess water, and then lay it flat on a towel to air dry. Make sure to avoid direct sunlight or heat sources, as this can cause the wig to lose its shape.

Tips for Maintaining Your Wig

If you want to keep your wig looking its best, there are a few things you can do to maintain it:

  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to help it keep its shape
  • Avoid using heat tools or styling products that contain alcohol, as these can damage the fibers
  • Don't wash your wig too often–once every 8-10 wears is usually enough
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your wig, as this can cause the fibers to become frizzy
  • Use a wig cap to protect your natural hair and keep the wig in place

FAQ: Bringing a Wig Back to Life

Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on my wig?

No, it's important to use products specifically designed for wigs, as regular shampoo and conditioner can damage the fibers.

How often should I wash my wig?

Generally, you should wash your wig once every 8-10 wears, but this can vary depending on how often you wear it and how much styling product you use.

Can I use heat tools on my wig?

It's best to avoid using heat tools on your wig, as these can damage the fibers. If you need to style your wig, use a low heat setting and a heat protectant spray.

Hair Care Tips for All Types of Hair

Whether you have natural hair or wear wigs, taking care of your hair is important. Here are a few tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it soft and hydrated
  • Avoid over-styling your hair with heat tools or tight hairstyles that can cause breakage
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and promote healthy growth
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to support healthy hair growth

With these tips and tricks, you can bring your wig back to life and keep it looking great for longer. Whether you're new to wigs or a seasoned pro, taking care of your hair is key to looking and feeling your best.
