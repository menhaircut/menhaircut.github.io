How To Stop Hair Going Frizzy In Winter

Written by Bardi Aug 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Winter weather can be harsh on your hair, leaving it dry, brittle, and prone to frizz. But don't worry, there are simple steps you can take to keep your hair looking smooth and sleek all winter long. In this article, we'll share our top tips for preventing frizz and maintaining healthy hair during the colder months.

How to Prevent Frizzy Hair in Winter

Frizzy hair is caused by a lack of moisture in the hair, which can be exacerbated by cold, dry winter weather. Here are some tips to keep your hair hydrated and healthy:

1. Use a Hydrating Shampoo and Conditioner

Look for products that contain moisturizing ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, or argan oil. These will help to restore moisture to your hair and prevent frizz.

2. Don't Over-Wash Your Hair

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and prone to frizz. Try to wash your hair no more than twice a week, and use dry shampoo in between washes to keep your hair looking fresh.

3. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Once a week, use a deep conditioning treatment to give your hair an extra boost of moisture. Apply the treatment to your hair and leave it on for at least 10-15 minutes before rinsing it out.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can further dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz. Try to avoid using these tools as much as possible, and opt for air-drying or using a diffuser instead.

Step-by-Step Guide to Winter Hair Care

Follow these simple steps to keep your hair looking healthy and frizz-free all winter long:

1. Wash Your Hair with Lukewarm Water

Hot water can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and prone to frizz. Use lukewarm water instead, and try to rinse your hair with cool water at the end to seal the cuticle and prevent frizz.

2. Pat Your Hair Dry with a Microfiber Towel

Don't rub your hair vigorously with a towel, as this can cause damage and frizz. Instead, gently pat your hair dry with a microfiber towel to absorb the excess moisture.

3. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help keep it hydrated and smooth. Focus on the ends of your hair, where it tends to be driest.

4. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb to Detangle Your Hair

Don't use a brush on wet hair, as this can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair.

5. Air-Dry Your Hair or Use a Diffuser

Air-drying your hair is the best way to prevent frizz, but if you need to use a hair dryer, use a diffuser attachment to help distribute the heat evenly and prevent frizz.

Tips for Frizz-Free Hair

Here are some additional tips to help keep your hair looking smooth and frizz-free:

1. Wear a Hat

A hat can help protect your hair from the cold and wind, and prevent it from getting too dry and frizzy.

2. Use a Humidifier

Indoor heating can be very drying on your hair, so consider using a humidifier to add moisture back into the air.

3. Avoid Touching Your Hair Too Much

Touching your hair too much can cause frizz, so try to avoid playing with it throughout the day.

FAQs About Winter Hair Care

1. Can I still get a haircut in the winter?

Yes, you can still get a haircut in the winter! In fact, getting regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can lead to frizz.

2. Should I use a heavier conditioner in the winter?

Yes, using a heavier conditioner in the winter can help keep your hair hydrated and prevent frizz. Look for products that contain ingredients like shea butter or coconut oil.
