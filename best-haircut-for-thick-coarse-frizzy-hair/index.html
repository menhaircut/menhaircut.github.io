Best Haircut For Thick Coarse Frizzy Hair

Written by Petter Oct 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're someone with thick, coarse, and frizzy hair, you know the daily struggle of managing your locks. Finding the right haircut that suits your hair type can be a daunting task. Luckily, we've got you covered with the best haircut for thick coarse frizzy hair. In this article, we'll guide you on how to choose the right haircut, how to maintain it, and some tips to make your hair look healthier and more manageable.

How to Choose the Right Haircut

Choosing the right haircut can make a huge difference in how your hair looks and feels. Here are some tips to help you choose the best haircut for your thick coarse frizzy hair.

1. Layers

Adding layers to your hair can help to remove some of the bulk and weight. This will make your hair feel lighter and more manageable. It's best to start with longer layers and gradually work your way up to shorter layers to avoid the risk of excessive frizz.

2. Long Bob

The long bob, also known as the lob, is a great haircut for thick coarse frizzy hair. It's a versatile cut that can be styled in many ways and it's long enough to weigh down the frizz. The lob is also a low-maintenance cut that requires minimal styling.

3. Blunt Cut

A blunt cut is a simple yet effective way to manage thick coarse frizzy hair. This cut involves cutting straight across the hair, without any layers. This cut will make your hair look thicker and more voluminous.

Step by Step Guide

Now that you know what haircut to choose, here's a step-by-step guide on how to achieve the perfect look.

Step 1: Wash and Condition

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products specifically designed for thick, coarse, and frizzy hair. This will help to hydrate and soften your hair, making it easier to manage.

Step 2: Towel Dry

Gently towel dry your hair to remove excess water. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously with the towel as this can cause frizz.

Step 3: Apply Styling Product

Apply a styling product to your hair to help tame the frizz and keep your hair in place. Choose a product that suits your hair type, such as a serum or cream.

Step 4: Blow Dry

Using a round brush, blow dry your hair. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends. Use the brush to smooth out any kinks and tangles.

Step 5: Style

Once your hair is dry, style it according to your preference. You can use a straightener or curling iron to achieve the desired look.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you manage your thick coarse frizzy hair:

1. Use Heat Protectant

Before using any heat styling tools, apply a heat protectant to your hair. This will help to prevent damage and breakage.

2. Avoid Over-washing

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, causing it to become dry and frizzy. Try to limit washing to 2-3 times a week.

3. Use a Wide-tooth Comb

Avoid using a brush on your hair as it can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

FAQ

1. Can I still get a short haircut if I have thick coarse frizzy hair?

Yes, you can still get a short haircut if you have thick coarse frizzy hair. However, it's important to choose a haircut that suits your hair type and face shape.

2. What styling products should I use for my thick coarse frizzy hair?

You should use styling products that are specifically designed for thick coarse frizzy hair. This includes serums, creams, and oils.

3. How often should I trim my hair?

It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential to maintaining healthy and manageable locks. Here are some hair care tips for thick coarse frizzy hair:

1. Deep Condition

Once a week, use a deep conditioning treatment to help hydrate and nourish your hair.

2. Protect Your Hair

When going out in the sun or swimming, protect your hair with a hat or swim cap. This will prevent damage from UV rays and chlorine.

3. Use a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help to reduce frizz and prevent breakage.

In conclusion, the best haircut for thick coarse frizzy hair is one that suits your hair type and face shape. Adding layers, choosing a lob, or going for a blunt cut are all great options. Remember to take care of your hair by using the right products and styling techniques. With these tips, you can say goodbye to frizzy hair and hello to healthy and manageable locks.
