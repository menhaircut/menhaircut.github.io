Do Fringes Suit Round Faces

Description

For those with round faces, finding the right hairstyle can be a bit of a challenge. One question that comes up quite often is whether or not fringes suit round faces. The answer is yes, fringes can work on round faces, but it all comes down to the type of fringe and how it's styled.

How To

The first step to determining whether or not fringes will suit your round face is to understand the shape of your face. Round faces typically have a wider forehead, fuller cheeks, and a rounded chin. The goal when choosing a fringe is to create balance and soften the angles of the face.

Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape

Use a measuring tape to measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. If all three measurements are similar, you likely have a round face. Another way to determine your face shape is to stand in front of a mirror and use a lipstick or washable marker to trace the outline of your face. Step back and look at the shape that you've traced.

Step 2: Choose the Right Type of Fringe

When it comes to fringes for round faces, there are a few different styles that work well:

  • Side-swept fringes: These fringes are angled and swept to one side, which helps to create the illusion of length and balance on a round face.
  • Long, layered fringes: These fringes work well on round faces because they create the illusion of height and length, which helps to balance out the fullness of the cheeks.
  • Wispy fringes: These fringes are light and airy, which helps to soften the angles of a round face.

Step 3: Get Your Fringe Styled

Once you've chosen the right type of fringe, it's important to get it styled in a way that flatters your face shape. Here are a few tips:

  • Side-swept fringes: Ask your stylist to angle the fringe so that it's longer on one side, which will help to balance out the fullness of your face.
  • Long, layered fringes: Ask your stylist to add layers to your fringe to create movement and texture. This will help to create the illusion of height and length.
  • Wispy fringes: Ask your stylist to cut the fringe so that it's longer in the middle and shorter on the sides. This will help to create a soft, wispy effect.

Tips

  • When choosing a fringe, avoid heavy, blunt fringes that can make a round face look even rounder.
  • If you have a short forehead, avoid short fringes that will make your forehead look even shorter.
  • If you have a long forehead, consider a straight, blunt fringe that will help to shorten the length of your forehead.
  • If you have curly hair, consider a curly fringe that will blend in with the rest of your curls.

FAQ

Q: Can I wear a fringe if I have a double chin?

A: Yes, you can wear a fringe if you have a double chin. However, it's important to choose the right type of fringe. Side-swept fringes and long, layered fringes can help to create balance and take the focus off of the chin area.

Q: Can I wear a blunt fringe if I have a round face?

A: It's best to avoid heavy, blunt fringes if you have a round face. These types of fringes can make your face look even rounder. Instead, opt for a side-swept fringe, a long, layered fringe, or a wispy fringe.

Hair Care

Once you've got your fringe styled, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your fringe regularly to keep it looking fresh.
  • Blow-dry your fringe with a round brush to create volume and movement.
  • Use a small amount of styling product, such as a light-hold mousse or a texturizing spray, to add texture and hold to your fringe.
  • Visit your stylist every 4-6 weeks for a trim to keep your fringe looking sharp.

So, do fringes suit round faces? Yes, they can work well, as long as you choose the right type of fringe and get it styled in a way that flatters your face shape. Follow these tips and you'll have a fringe that complements your round face perfectly!
