Should I Use Thinning Shears On My Thick Hair

Written by Petter Dec 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning shears are a popular tool for hair stylists to create texture and volume in hair. They are especially useful for people with thick hair who want to remove some of the bulk and weight. However, before you decide to use thinning shears on your thick hair, there are a few things you should consider.

What Are Thinning Shears?

Thinning shears, also known as texturizing shears, are scissors with teeth on one or both blades. When used on hair, they remove some of the strands, creating a more textured and layered look. They are often used to thin out thick hair, but can also be used to add texture to fine hair.

How to Use Thinning Shears on Thick Hair

If you decide to use thinning shears on your thick hair, it's important to do it correctly to avoid damaging your hair. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start using the thinning shears.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to section your hair into smaller sections. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you thin out your hair evenly.

Step 3: Start at the Bottom

Start at the bottom of your hair and work your way up. Take a small section of hair and hold it at a 45-degree angle. Use the thinning shears to cut into the hair, starting about halfway down the strand and working your way towards the ends. Repeat this process on all sections of your hair.

Step 4: Don't Overdo It

When using thinning shears, it's important not to overdo it. You don't want to thin out your hair too much, as this can lead to split ends and breakage. Use the shears sparingly and only on areas where you want to remove some of the bulk.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Thick Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when using thinning shears on your thick hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up
  • Use the shears sparingly
  • Don't cut too close to the scalp
  • Use a comb to guide the shears and ensure even cutting
  • Be gentle and take your time

FAQ About Using Thinning Shears on Thick Hair

Can Thinning Shears Damage My Hair?

If used incorrectly, thinning shears can damage your hair. It's important to use them sparingly and avoid cutting too close to the scalp.

Can Thinning Shears Make My Hair Thinner?

Yes, thinning shears can make your hair thinner by removing some of the bulk. However, it's important not to overdo it and thin out your hair too much.

Can I Use Thinning Shears on Relaxed Hair?

Yes, you can use thinning shears on relaxed hair. However, it's important to be gentle and avoid cutting too close to the scalp to avoid damaging your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Hair

If you have thick hair, here are a few tips to keep it healthy and manageable:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers and flat irons
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

Conclusion

Thinning shears can be a useful tool for people with thick hair who want to remove some of the bulk and create a more textured look. However, it's important to use them correctly and avoid overdoing it to prevent damage to your hair. If you're unsure about using thinning shears, consult with a professional hair stylist for guidance.
