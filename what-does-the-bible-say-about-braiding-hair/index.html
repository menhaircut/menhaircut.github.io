What Does The Bible Say About Braiding Hair

Written by William Oct 09, 2023 Â· 5 min read

When it comes to hair styling, many people find themselves turning to the Bible for guidance. With so many different hair care practices and styles out there, it can be difficult to know what is acceptable in the eyes of God. One question that often comes up is whether or not it is okay to braid your hair. In this post, we will take a closer look at what the Bible has to say about braiding hair.

What Does the Bible Say About Braiding Hair?

While the Bible doesn't specifically mention braiding hair, it does have a few things to say about hair and beauty. In 1 Peter 3:3-4, the Bible says:

Your beauty should not come from outward adornment, such as elaborate hairstyles and the wearing of gold jewelry or fine clothes. Rather, it should be that of your inner self, the unfading beauty of a gentle and quiet spirit, which is of great worth in God's sight.

This passage suggests that we should not place too much emphasis on our outward appearance, but instead focus on cultivating inner beauty. However, it does not necessarily mean that braiding your hair is off-limits. In fact, there are many examples of braided hair in the Bible.

Braided Hair in the Bible

One of the most famous examples of braided hair in the Bible is the story of Samson and Delilah. Samson's strength was tied to his long hair, which he wore in seven braids. Delilah famously tricked Samson into revealing the secret of his strength, and then cut off his hair while he slept, rendering him powerless.

Another example of braided hair can be found in the book of Esther. In Esther 2:17, it is said that Esther "won the favor of everyone who saw her" when she was presented to King Xerxes wearing a crown and a beautiful gown. While the Bible does not specifically mention Esther's hair, it is likely that she had it styled in some way for this important occasion.

How to Braid Hair

If you are interested in braiding your hair, there are many different styles to choose from. Here is a simple tutorial for a basic three-strand braid:

  1. Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles.
  2. Divide your hair into three equal sections.
  3. Cross the right section over the middle section.
  4. Cross the left section over the middle section.
  5. Repeat steps 3 and 4 until you reach the end of your hair.
  6. Tie off the end of the braid with a hair elastic.

There are many variations on this basic braid, including French braids, Dutch braids, and fishtail braids. You can also experiment with different partings and hair accessories to create a unique look.

Tips for Braiding Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect braid:

  • Start with clean, dry hair.
  • Use a detangling spray or conditioner to make your hair easier to work with.
  • Practice, practice, practice! The more you braid, the easier it will become.
  • Experiment with different styles and techniques to find what works best for you.

FAQ About Braiding Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about braiding hair:

Is braiding your hair good for your hair?

Yes, braiding your hair can be good for your hair. It can help to protect your hair from damage and breakage, especially if you have curly or textured hair. However, it is important to avoid braiding your hair too tightly, as this can cause tension and damage to your scalp and hair follicles.

Can you braid wet hair?

It is best to avoid braiding wet hair, as it can be more prone to breakage and damage. If you must braid your hair while it is wet, be sure to use a gentle detangling spray or conditioner to make it easier to work with.

How long does a braid last?

A braid can last anywhere from a few hours to several days, depending on the style and your hair type. It is important to take care when removing your braid, as pulling or tugging can cause damage to your hair.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some general hair care tips to help keep your hair healthy and strong:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but not too often.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools.
  • Take care when brushing or combing your hair, especially if it is wet.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet to support hair growth and strength.

Conclusion

So, what does the Bible say about braiding hair? While the Bible does not specifically prohibit braiding your hair, it does suggest that we should focus more on inner beauty than outward appearance. However, there are many examples of braided hair in the Bible, and braiding your hair can be a fun and creative way to express yourself.

If you do decide to braid your hair, be sure to take care to avoid damaging your hair or scalp. Experiment with different styles and techniques to find what works best for you, and don't be afraid to try something new!
