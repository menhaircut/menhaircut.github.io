What Not To Do When Wearing Braces A Guide For Proper Care

Written by Daniel Jul 14, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Braces are an effective way to straighten teeth and improve your smile. However, maintaining proper care of your braces is essential to ensure they work effectively and prevent any damage. In this article, we will discuss what not to do when wearing braces and provide tips on how to care for them.

Table of Contents:

  • Do NOT Tighten Your Braces
  • Do NOT Eat Hard Foods
  • Do NOT Neglect Flossing
  • Do NOT Skip Appointments
  • What You SHOULD Do
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Do NOT Tighten Your Braces

One of the most important things to remember when wearing braces is to never try to adjust them yourself. Tightening or adjusting your braces can cause serious damage to your teeth and gums. Only your orthodontist should make adjustments to your braces. If you feel that your braces are too loose or too tight, contact your orthodontist immediately to schedule an appointment.

Do NOT Eat Hard Foods

Eating hard foods such as popcorn, nuts, and hard candy can cause damage to your braces. The wires and brackets may break, and the bands may come loose. Avoiding hard foods is essential to maintain the effectiveness of your braces. You should also avoid sticky foods such as gum and taffy, as they can get stuck in your braces and be difficult to remove.

Do NOT Neglect Flossing

Flossing is an essential part of oral care, especially when wearing braces. Food particles can easily get stuck in your braces and cause decay if not removed properly. Neglecting flossing can lead to serious dental problems. Use a floss threader to clean between the wires and brackets and floss at least once a day to maintain healthy teeth and gums.

Do NOT Skip Appointments

Regular appointments with your orthodontist are important to ensure that your braces are working effectively. Skipping appointments can delay the progress of your treatment and may require additional appointments in the future. It's also important to follow your orthodontist's instructions for care and maintenance of your braces.

What You SHOULD Do

Proper care and maintenance of your braces are essential to ensure that they work effectively. Here are a few things you should do:

  • Brush your teeth at least twice a day with a soft-bristled toothbrush.
  • Use fluoride toothpaste to prevent decay.
  • Clean your braces with a special brush designed for braces.
  • Wear a mouthguard when playing sports to protect your braces.

FAQs

How long do I need to wear braces?

The length of time you need to wear braces depends on your individual needs. Your orthodontist will recommend a treatment plan based on the severity of your condition.

Do braces hurt?

Braces may cause discomfort or soreness, especially after adjustments. Over-the-counter pain relievers can help relieve any pain or discomfort.

Hair Care Tips

Wearing braces can be challenging, especially when it comes to hair care. Here are a few tips to help you maintain healthy hair while wearing braces:

  • Avoid using hair elastics that can get caught in your braces.
  • Use a soft-bristled brush to avoid damaging your braces.
  • Regularly wash and condition your hair to prevent tangles.
  • Consider tying your hair up in a ponytail or bun to keep it away from your face and braces.

In conclusion, proper care and maintenance of your braces are essential to ensure they work effectively. Avoiding hard foods, flossing regularly, and not trying to adjust your braces yourself are crucial to preventing damage. Follow your orthodontist's instructions and attend regular appointments to ensure the best possible results.
