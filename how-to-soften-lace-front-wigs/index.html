How To Soften Lace Front Wigs

Written by Robby Oct 01, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’re new to wearing wigs, you may find that some lace front wigs can be a bit stiff and uncomfortable to wear. But don’t worry – there are a few simple steps you can take to soften lace front wigs and make them more comfortable to wear.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Soften Lace Front Wigs
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Softening Lace Front Wigs
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care for Your Lace Front Wig

How to Soften Lace Front Wigs

Before we begin, it’s important to note that not all lace front wigs are the same. Some wigs may be made of synthetic materials, while others may be made of human hair. The method you use to soften your wig may vary depending on the material it’s made of, so be sure to check the manufacturer’s instructions before getting started.

Here are some general steps you can take to soften your lace front wig:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. First, wash your wig with a wig shampoo and conditioner. This will remove any dirt or debris that may be causing your wig to feel stiff.

  2. Next, gently comb through your wig with a wide-tooth comb or wig brush. Be sure to start at the ends of your wig and work your way up to avoid damaging the lace.

  3. Fill a bowl with warm water and add a few drops of fabric softener. Swirl the water around to mix the fabric softener in.

  4. Submerge your wig in the water and let it soak for about 10-15 minutes.

  5. Remove the wig from the water and gently squeeze out any excess water. Do not twist or wring your wig, as this can damage the lace.

  6. Place your wig on a towel and gently pat it dry with another towel. Do not rub the wig, as this can cause tangles and damage the hair.

  7. Once your wig is mostly dry, you can apply a wig conditioner or leave-in conditioner to the hair. This will help to soften the hair and make it more manageable.

  8. Finally, style your wig as desired and allow it to air dry completely.

Tips for Softening Lace Front Wigs

Here are a few additional tips to help you soften your lace front wig:

  • Be gentle when combing or brushing your wig to avoid damaging the lace.

  • Avoid using heat styling tools like curling irons or straighteners on your wig, as this can damage the hair and make it more difficult to soften.

  • If your wig is made of synthetic materials, be sure to use a wig conditioner specifically designed for synthetic wigs.

  • If you’re unsure about how to care for your wig, consult the manufacturer’s instructions or speak to a professional wig stylist.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about softening lace front wigs:

  • Can I use regular fabric softener on my wig? It’s best to use a fabric softener specifically designed for wigs, as regular fabric softener may contain ingredients that can damage the hair.

  • How often should I soften my wig? This will depend on how often you wear your wig and how frequently you wash it. In general, you may need to soften your wig every few weeks or months to keep it feeling comfortable.

  • Can I use a hair dryer to dry my wig? It’s best to let your wig air dry naturally to avoid damaging the hair. If you must use a hair dryer, use a low heat setting and be sure to keep the dryer at least a foot away from the wig.

Hair Care for Your Lace Front Wig

Proper hair care is essential for keeping your lace front wig looking and feeling its best. Here are a few tips to help you care for your wig:

  • Use a wig shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for your wig’s material.

  • Be gentle when combing or brushing your wig to avoid damaging the lace.

  • Avoid using heat styling tools like curling irons or straighteners on your wig, as this can damage the hair.

  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head when not in use to help maintain its shape.

With these tips and tricks, you can soften your lace front wig and enjoy a comfortable, natural-looking hairstyle. Happy styling!
