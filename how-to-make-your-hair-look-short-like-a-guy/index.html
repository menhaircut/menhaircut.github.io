Step 1: Choose Your Cut

The first step in making your hair look short like a guy is choosing the right cut. Some popular options include the buzz cut, crew cut, and undercut. Talk to your stylist about which cut might work best for your face shape and hair type. Keep in mind that shorter cuts may require more frequent salon visits to maintain the shape.

After washing your hair, gently towel dry it to remove excess water. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously with the towel, as this can cause damage and frizz. Next, use a blow dryer on a low heat setting to dry your hair. If you have curly hair, you may want to use a diffuser attachment to help define your curls.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Now comes the fun part â styling your new short hair. Depending on the cut you chose, you may not need to do much styling at all. For a more polished look, consider using a styling product like gel, pomade, or wax. Start with a small amount of product and work it through your hair, using your fingers to style it into place.

Tips for Maintaining Short Hair

  • Invest in a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair looking healthy.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • If you have curly hair, consider using a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define your curls.
  • Be prepared to visit the salon more frequently to maintain the shape of your cut.

Can I still wear my hair in a ponytail with a short cut?

Depending on the length of your hair, you may still be able to wear it in a small ponytail or braid. However, keep in mind that shorter cuts may not allow for as much versatility in styling.
