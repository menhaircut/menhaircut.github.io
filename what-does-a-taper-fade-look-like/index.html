What Does A Taper Fade Look Like A Guide To This Classic Haircut

Written by Luffy Jun 28, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking for a stylish and versatile haircut, a taper fade might be the perfect choice for you. This classic haircut is popular among men of all ages and styles, and it involves gradually fading the hair from the top to the sides and back of the head. In this article, we'll explain what a taper fade looks like, how to get it, and how to take care of your hair after the cut.

Description: What Does a Taper Fade Look Like?

A taper fade is a type of haircut where the hair is cut shorter and shorter as it goes down the head, creating a gradient effect. The hair is typically left longer on top, and gradually fades to a shorter length on the sides and back of the head. The result is a clean, stylish look that can be worn in a variety of ways.

One of the defining features of a taper fade is the sharp line that separates the longer hair on top from the shorter hair on the sides and back. This line can be straight or curved, depending on the style you're going for. The hair on top can be styled in a variety of ways, from a slicked-back look to a messy fringe.

How to Get a Taper Fade

If you're interested in getting a taper fade, the first step is to find a skilled barber who has experience with this type of haircut. Make sure to bring a photo or description of the style you're looking for, so your barber can create the look you want.

The barber will start by cutting the hair on top to the desired length. Then, they'll use clippers to gradually fade the hair down to a shorter length on the sides and back. The length of the fade can vary depending on your preference, but it's typically longer on top and shorter on the sides and back.

Once the barber has created the desired gradient effect, they'll use scissors or clippers to blend the hair and create a smooth transition between the different lengths. They may also use a razor to create a sharp line between the longer hair on top and the shorter hair on the sides and back.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Do a Taper Fade Haircut

If you're feeling adventurous, you can try doing a taper fade haircut at home. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you achieve the look:

  1. Start by washing your hair and drying it thoroughly.
  2. Use clippers to cut the hair on the sides and back of your head to the desired length.
  3. Use a longer clipper guard to fade the hair up to the desired length on the sides and back.
  4. Blend the different lengths of hair using scissors or clippers.
  5. Create a sharp line between the longer hair on top and the shorter hair on the sides and back using a razor.
  6. Style the hair on top using your preferred product.

Tips for Maintaining a Taper Fade

Once you've got your taper fade haircut, it's important to maintain it to keep it looking sharp and stylish. Here are a few tips to help you take care of your hair:

  • Get regular trims to keep the fade looking fresh.
  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Style your hair with a product that works for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair too often, as this can damage the hair and make it harder to style.

FAQs

How long does a taper fade last?

A taper fade can last anywhere from a few weeks to a few months, depending on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain the style.

Can I style my hair differently with a taper fade?

Absolutely! A taper fade is a versatile haircut that can be styled in a variety of ways. You can slick it back, comb it over, or leave it messy and tousled.

Is a taper fade suitable for all hair types?

Yes! A taper fade can work for all hair types, from straight to curly to wavy. Your barber can adjust the length and taper to suit your hair type and texture.

Hair Care Tips for Taper Fade Haircuts

When it comes to taking care of your hair after a taper fade haircut, there are a few things to keep in mind. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot water on your hair, as this can strip away natural oils and dry out your scalp.
  • Avoid using harsh styling products that can damage your hair and scalp.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and style of your taper fade.

Overall, a taper fade is a classic and stylish haircut that can work for anyone. Whether you're looking for a sharp, professional look or a more casual, laid-back style, a taper fade might be just what you need. With the right barber, styling products, and hair care routine, you can keep your taper fade looking fresh and stylish for weeks to come.
