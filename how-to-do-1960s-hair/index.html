How To Do 1960S Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Sep 10, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking to channel your inner retro diva with a classic 1960s hairstyle? Look no further! In this blog post, we'll show you how to do 1960s hair in a few simple steps. From the famous beehive to the chic pixie cut, we've got you covered with all the tips and tricks you need to achieve a stunning 1960s look.

How to Do a 1960s Beehive

The beehive was one of the most iconic hairstyles of the 1960s. This classic 'do is characterized by its voluminous shape, which resembles a beehive (hence the name). Here's how to do a 1960s beehive:

Step 1: Tease Your Hair

Start by parting your hair down the middle and teasing the crown section with a fine-tooth comb. This will create the height and volume needed for the beehive.

Step 2: Create a Ponytail

Gather the teased section of your hair into a ponytail at the crown of your head. Secure it with an elastic band.

Step 3: Backcomb Your Ponytail

Backcomb the ponytail with a fine-tooth comb to create even more volume. Be sure to smooth out the top layer of hair with a brush or comb.

Step 4: Pin the Hair

Wrap the ponytail around the elastic band to create a bun shape. Pin the hair in place with bobby pins, making sure to hide the elastic band.

Step 5: Finish with Hairspray

Finish the beehive by spritzing it with hairspray to hold the style in place. Use a comb to smooth down any flyaways or frizz.

And there you have it—a classic 1960s beehive!

How to Do a 1960s Pixie Cut

The pixie cut was another popular hairstyle of the 1960s. This short and sassy 'do is perfect for anyone looking to make a bold statement. Here's how to do a 1960s pixie cut:

Step 1: Choose the Right Length

The key to a great pixie cut is choosing the right length for your face shape. Consult with a hairstylist to determine the best length for you.

Step 2: Cut the Hair

Cut the hair to the desired length, making sure to create layers and texture for added dimension. Use a comb and scissors to trim the hair evenly.

Step 3: Style the Hair

Apply a small amount of texturizing product to the hair to create a messy, tousled look. Use your fingers to style the hair and add volume where needed.

Step 4: Finish with Hairspray

Finish the pixie cut by spritzing it with hairspray to hold the style in place. Use a comb to smooth down any flyaways or frizz.

And there you have it—a chic and stylish 1960s pixie cut!

Tips for Maintaining 1960s Hair

Now that you know how to do 1960s hair, it's important to maintain the look. Here are a few tips to keep your retro 'do looking its best:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body and texture to the hair.
  • Avoid using too much heat on the hair, as this can cause damage and frizz.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and length of your hairstyle.
  • Invest in quality hair products, such as hairspray and texturizing paste, to keep your hairstyle in place.

FAQs

Q: Can I do 1960s hair on short hair?

A: Yes! The beehive may be more difficult to achieve on short hair, but the pixie cut is a great option for anyone with shorter locks.

Q: How do I create a 1960s updo?

A: To create a 1960s updo, start by teasing the crown section of your hair to create volume. Pull the hair back into a ponytail or bun and pin in place with bobby pins. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Q: What hair accessories were popular in the 1960s?

A: Hair accessories such as headbands, barrettes, and scarves were popular in the 1960s. Incorporate these accessories into your hairstyle for an authentic retro look.

Conclusion

Now that you know how to do 1960s hair, you can channel your inner vintage vixen with confidence! Whether you opt for a beehive or a pixie cut, these retro hairstyles are sure to make a statement. Remember to maintain your hairstyle with regular trims and quality hair products to keep your 'do looking its best.
