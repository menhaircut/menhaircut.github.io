How To Cut Thick Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to switch up your hairstyle with some thick bangs? Cutting bangs can be intimidating, especially if you have thick hair. But fear not, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a perfect set of thick bangs. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting thick bangs step by step.

How to Cut Thick Bangs

Before we dive into the actual process, let's talk about the tools you will need to cut your thick bangs. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp haircutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips to hold hair in place
  • A mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have your tools ready, let's get started with cutting your thick bangs:

  1. Wash your hair and blow-dry it straight. This will help you see the natural fall of your hair and make it easier to cut.
  2. Section off the hair you want to cut into a triangle shape. Use the arch of your eyebrows as a guide for the width of the triangle.
  3. Clip the rest of your hair away from your face to avoid accidentally cutting it.
  4. Take the section of hair you want to cut and comb it straight down. Make sure it is taut and even.
  5. Using your haircutting scissors, start trimming the ends of your hair in a straight line. Don't cut too much at once; it's better to take small snips to avoid any mistakes.
  6. Continue cutting your hair in a straight line until you reach your desired length. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can't add hair back.
  7. Take a step back and look at your bangs in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments to the length or shape.
  8. Once you're happy with the length and shape of your bangs, use your scissors to add texture by cutting into the ends. This will give your bangs a softer, more natural look.
  9. Repeat the process on the other side, making sure both sides are even.
  10. Style your bangs as desired and enjoy your new look!

Tips for Cutting Thick Bangs

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting your thick bangs:

  • Always cut your hair when it is dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process. It's better to take small snips than to cut off too much at once.
  • Use a comb to make sure your hair is even and taut before cutting.
  • If you're unsure about the length or shape of your bangs, start by cutting a little bit at a time.
  • When cutting into the ends for texture, make sure you're cutting at a slight angle to avoid any harsh lines.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting thick bangs:

Can I cut my own bangs?

Yes, you can cut your own bangs, but it's important to be patient and take your time. If you're unsure about the process, it's always best to visit a professional hairdresser.

How do I know what length to cut my bangs?

It's best to start with a longer length and work your way up to your desired length. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can't add hair back.

How often should I trim my bangs?

It's recommended to trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh and healthy.

Hair Care for Thick Bangs

Now that you have your thick bangs, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Use a heat protectant spray when styling your bangs with hot tools.
  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid over-washing and stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or products on your bangs, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Regularly trim your bangs to avoid split ends and maintain their shape.

Cutting thick bangs can seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it's a simple process. Follow our step-by-step guide and keep our tips in mind for a perfect set of thick bangs.
