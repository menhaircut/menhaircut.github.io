How To Do 50S Hair Curls

Written by Eveline Jun 09, 2023 Â· 3 min read

The 1950s was a decade of glamour, and one of the most iconic hairstyles of this era was the voluminous curls. These curls were popularized by Hollywood stars like Marilyn Monroe and Elizabeth Taylor and are still in style today. So, if you want to achieve that classic look, read on to learn how to do 50s hair curls.

Description

50s hair curls are a vintage hairstyle that involves creating bouncy, large curls that frame the face. This style is perfect for special occasions or just to add some glamour to your everyday look.

How to

What You Will Need:

  • Heat protectant spray
  • Curling iron
  • Sectioning clips
  • Hairspray

Step by Step:

Step 1: Start by washing your hair and applying a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from the curling iron.

Step 2: Divide your hair into sections, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Use sectioning clips to keep the hair in place.

Step 3: Take a one-inch section of hair and wrap it around the curling iron, starting from the mid-shaft and curling towards the roots. Hold for 10-15 seconds before releasing the curl.

Step 4: Continue curling each section of hair, alternating the direction of the curls. This will create a more natural and voluminous look.

Step 5: Once you have curled all of your hair, gently brush through the curls with a soft-bristled brush or wide-tooth comb to blend them together.

Step 6: Finish off the style with a light mist of hairspray to hold the curls in place.

Tips

  • For a more dramatic look, tease the hair at the crown of your head before curling to create extra volume.
  • Use a smaller curling iron for tighter curls or a larger one for looser curls.
  • Don't overdo the hairspray, as this can make your hair look stiff and unnatural.

FAQ

Can I do 50s hair curls on short hair?

Yes, you can still achieve 50s hair curls on short hair. Use a smaller curling iron and focus on curling the ends of your hair for a more vintage look.

How long will 50s hair curls last?

With proper care and a good hairspray, 50s hair curls can last all day or even into the next day.

Hair Care

To keep your 50s hair curls looking their best, use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and shiny. Avoid using too much heat on your hair and always use a heat protectant spray before using a curling iron.

Now that you know how to do 50s hair curls, it's time to channel your inner Marilyn Monroe and rock this classic style!
