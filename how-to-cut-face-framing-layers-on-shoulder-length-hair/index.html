How To Cut Face Framing Layers On Shoulder Length Hair

Written by Christine Nov 02, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking for a way to freshen up your shoulder length hair, adding face framing layers is a great option. Not only do they add dimension and movement to your hair, but they also accentuate your facial features. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to cut face framing layers on shoulder length hair.

Description

Face framing layers are cut to frame your face and draw attention to your features. They are shorter than your overall hair length and can be cut at different angles and lengths to create different looks. Face framing layers are a great option for shoulder length hair because they add movement and dimension without sacrificing overall length.

How to Cut Face Framing Layers on Shoulder Length Hair

Cutting face framing layers on shoulder length hair can be done at home or at a salon. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to see the natural fall of your hair and where to cut the layers.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: two on the top and two on the bottom. Clip the top sections away from the bottom sections.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Starting with the bottom sections, take a small piece of hair and pull it straight out from your head. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from just below your chin and going outward. Repeat this process for each section until you've cut all the layers you want.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the layers, blend them together with a comb or your fingers. This will create a seamless transition between the layers and the rest of your hair.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide on how to cut face framing layers on shoulder length hair:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the tools you need. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to see the natural fall of your hair and where to cut the layers.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: two on the top and two on the bottom. Clip the top sections away from the bottom sections.

Step 4: Decide on the Length and Angle of Your Layers

Decide on the length and angle of your layers. Face framing layers are typically cut at an angle, starting from just below your chin and going outward. The length of the layers will depend on your preference and face shape.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Starting with the bottom sections, take a small piece of hair and pull it straight out from your head. Cut the hair at an angle, following the line you've decided on. Repeat this process for each section until you've cut all the layers you want.

Step 6: Check for Balance

Check for balance by comparing the length of the layers on each side. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure they are even.

Step 7: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the layers, blend them together with a comb or your fingers. This will create a seamless transition between the layers and the rest of your hair.

Tips for Cutting Face Framing Layers on Shoulder Length Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting face framing layers on shoulder length hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair to ensure you don't cut too much off at once.
  • Use a sharp pair of scissors to ensure a clean, even cut.
  • Don't cut too close to your face or you may end up with choppy layers.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and it's important to take breaks to avoid mistakes.

FAQ

Can I cut face framing layers on my own?

Yes, you can cut face framing layers on your own. However, if you're not confident in your cutting abilities, it's best to go to a salon to avoid any mistakes.

How often should I cut my face framing layers?

How often you cut your face framing layers will depend on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain the look. On average, you should cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape.

Hair Care Tips for Face Framing Layers on Shoulder Length Hair

Here are some hair care tips to keep your face framing layers looking their best:

  • Use a heat protectant when styling your hair with heat tools.
  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

By following these tips and steps, you can easily cut face framing layers on your shoulder length hair at home. Remember to take your time and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. With a little practice, you'll be a pro at cutting your own hair in no time!
