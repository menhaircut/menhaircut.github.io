How To Keep A Lace Front Wig In Place Tips And Tricks

Written by Petter Nov 22, 2023 · 5 min read

If you are new to wearing a lace front wig or have struggled with keeping it in place, you are not alone. The good news is that there are simple tips and tricks that can help you keep your wig secure and looking great. In this article, we’ll cover everything you need to know about how to keep a lace front wig in place.

Table of Contents

  • How to Keep a Lace Front Wig in Place
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Keep a Lace Front Wig in Place

Before we dive into the tips and tricks, let’s start with the basics of how to properly put on a lace front wig:

  1. First, prepare your hair by braiding it or putting it in a flat style against your head.
  2. Next, put on a wig cap to help keep your natural hair in place and to create a smooth surface for the wig to sit on.
  3. Secure the wig cap with hairpins, making sure it is snug against your head.
  4. Align the wig with your natural hairline, making sure the ear tabs are even on both sides.
  5. Use the adjustable straps inside the wig to tighten or loosen it as needed, so it fits securely on your head.
  6. Finally, trim any excess lace from the front of the wig, being careful not to cut into the hairline.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know how to properly put on a lace front wig, let’s go through the steps you can take to keep it in place:

  1. Use wig glue or tape: One of the easiest and most effective ways to keep a lace front wig in place is to use wig glue or tape. Apply a thin layer of glue or tape along the hairline, making sure to avoid getting any on your natural hair. Press the wig firmly into place and hold for a few seconds to allow the glue or tape to set.
  2. Try a wig grip or band: Another option is to use a wig grip or band, which is a soft, stretchy headband that helps keep wigs in place without the need for glue or tape. Simply place the wig grip or band over your head, then put the wig on top of it and adjust as needed.
  3. Use bobby pins: If you prefer not to use glue or tape, you can also use bobby pins to secure the wig in place. Simply slide the pins through the wig cap and into your natural hair, being careful not to pull too tightly or cause any discomfort.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks that can help you keep your lace front wig in place:

  • Use a wig cap that matches your skin tone to create a more natural look.
  • When applying glue or tape, make sure to follow the manufacturer’s instructions and avoid using too much, as this can cause the wig to shift or slip.
  • If you experience any discomfort or itching while wearing a lace front wig, try using a wig liner or scalp protector to create a barrier between the wig and your skin.
  • Consider using a wig stand or mannequin head to store your wig when not in use, as this can help maintain its shape and prolong its lifespan.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about keeping a lace front wig in place:

  • How long can I wear a lace front wig?
  • It is generally recommended to wear a lace front wig for no more than 6-8 hours at a time, as prolonged wear can cause irritation or damage to your natural hair.

  • Can I swim or exercise while wearing a lace front wig?
  • While it is possible to wear a lace front wig while swimming or exercising, it is not recommended, as the water or sweat can cause the wig to shift or slip.

  • How often should I wash my lace front wig?
  • It is recommended to wash your lace front wig every 7-10 wears, or as needed depending on how much product or sweat has built up on the hair.

Hair Care Tips

Finally, it is important to take proper care of your lace front wig to ensure it stays in good condition:

  • Avoid using hot tools on the wig, such as flat irons or curling irons, as this can damage the hair fibers.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or wig brush to gently detangle the hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  • Store the wig in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight or heat sources, such as radiators or hair dryers.

With these tips and tricks, you should be able to keep your lace front wig in place and looking great. Remember to take good care of your wig to ensure it lasts as long as possible, and don’t be afraid to experiment with different methods to find what works best for you.
