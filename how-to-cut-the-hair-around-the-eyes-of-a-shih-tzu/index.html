How To Cut The Hair Around The Eyes Of A Shih Tzu

Written by Petter Sep 05, 2023 · 4 min read

If you are a shih tzu owner, you know how important it is to keep your furry friend’s hair trimmed and neat. One of the most challenging areas to cut is around the eyes. It’s vital to trim the hair around your shih tzu's eyes to prevent eye irritation and infections. In this guide, we will show you how to cut the hair around the eyes of a shih tzu safely and comfortably.

Step by Step Guide

Before you start cutting your shih tzu's hair, make sure you have the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and some treats to keep your furry friend calm during the process. Follow these simple steps to cut the hair around your shih tzu's eyes:

  1. Start by combing the hair around your shih tzu's eyes to remove any tangles or mats. This will make it easier to cut the hair evenly.
  2. Hold your shih tzu's head still with one hand and use the scissors to trim the hair on the top of the nose. Cut straight across, following the natural line of the nose.
  3. Use the scissors to trim the hair between the eyes. Be careful not to cut too close to the skin, as this can cause irritation or injury.
  4. Cut the hair on the sides of the eyes, making sure to keep the hair length even on both sides.
  5. Trim the hair above the eyes, but be cautious not to cut too close to the eyelashes. The eyelashes protect your shih tzu's eyes and keep dirt and debris out.

Remember to take breaks and give your shih tzu some treats to keep them calm and relaxed. If you are unsure about cutting your shih tzu's hair, consider taking them to a professional groomer.

Tips for Cutting Shih Tzu's Hair

Cutting a shih tzu's hair can be a daunting task, especially around the eyes. Here are some tips to make the process easier:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid pulling or tugging on your shih tzu's hair.
  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Ensure your shih tzu is comfortable and relaxed by giving them treats and taking breaks during the grooming process.
  • Consider using a muzzle if your shih tzu is prone to biting or getting nervous during grooming.
  • Be careful not to get any hair in your shih tzu's eyes, as this can cause irritation and discomfort.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I cut my shih tzu's hair?

It's recommended to trim your shih tzu's hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain their coat and prevent matting.

Why is it essential to cut the hair around my shih tzu's eyes?

Trimming the hair around your shih tzu's eyes is crucial to prevent irritation and infections. Long hair can rub against the eyes and cause redness, swelling, and discharge.

Can I use clippers instead of scissors to cut my shih tzu's hair?

It's best to use scissors when cutting your shih tzu's hair, especially around the eyes. Clippers can be too harsh and loud, causing your shih tzu to become nervous and agitated.

Hair Care for Shih Tzu's

Proper hair care is essential for shih tzu's to keep their coat healthy and shiny. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Brush your shih tzu's hair every day to prevent matting and tangles.
  • Trim the hair around the ears, paws, and anus to prevent dirt and debris from getting trapped.
  • Bathe your shih tzu once a month with a mild shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a detangling spray to make brushing easier and less painful for your shih tzu.
  • Consider taking your shih tzu to a professional groomer for regular haircuts and maintenance.

Cutting the hair around your shih tzu's eyes may seem challenging, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done safely and comfortably. Remember to take your time, be patient, and reward your furry friend with treats and cuddles. Happy grooming!
