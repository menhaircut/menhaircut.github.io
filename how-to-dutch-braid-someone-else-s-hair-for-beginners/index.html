How To Dutch Braid Someone Else s Hair For Beginners A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Oct 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Dutch braids are a popular hairstyle that is perfect for any occasion. Whether you're going to a party, a wedding or just a casual day out, a Dutch braid is a great way to add some style to your look. But what if you don't know how to do a Dutch braid on someone else's hair? Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we will give you a step-by-step guide on how to Dutch braid someone else's hair for beginners.

Description

A Dutch braid is a type of braid that is created by weaving three strands of hair in reverse order. This means that the outer strands are crossed under the middle strand instead of over it, which creates a braid that sits on top of the hair rather than underneath it. Dutch braids are also known as "reverse French braids" and can be done on any length of hair.

How to Dutch Braid Someone Else's Hair for Beginners

Step 1: Prepare the Hair

Before you start braiding, make sure that the hair is clean, detangled and dry. You can also apply some hair oil or serum to add some shine and make it easier to work with.

Step 2: Divide the Hair

Divide the hair into three sections, just like you would for a regular braid. Take a small section of hair from the top of the head and divide it into three equal parts.

Step 3: Start Braiding

Start the braid by crossing the right section of hair under the middle section, then cross the left section under the middle section. This is the opposite of a regular braid where you cross the sections over the middle.

Step 4: Add Hair

As you continue braiding, add hair to each section before you cross it under the middle section. Take a small section of hair from the side of the head and add it to the right section, then cross it under the middle section. Repeat the same process on the left side.

Step 5: Continue Braiding

Continue braiding by adding hair to each section and crossing it under the middle section until you reach the nape of the neck. At this point, you should have added all of the hair to the braid, and you can continue braiding without adding any more hair.

Step 6: Finish the Braid

Once you reach the end of the hair, continue braiding without adding any more hair until you reach the end. Secure the braid with a hair tie or ribbon.

Tips

  • Practice makes perfect. Don't be discouraged if your first attempt doesn't look perfect. Keep practicing and you'll get the hang of it.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress. This will help you see if you're adding hair evenly and if the braid is sitting straight.
  • Don't pull the hair too tight. You want the braid to sit comfortably on the head, so avoid pulling the hair too tightly.
  • Try different variations. Once you've mastered the basic Dutch braid, you can experiment with different styles and variations.

FAQ

Can I Dutch braid short hair?

Yes, you can Dutch braid short hair. You may need to use smaller sections of hair and create a tighter braid, but it is possible.

How long does a Dutch braid last?

A Dutch braid can last for a few days if you take care of it properly. You can wrap the braid in a silk scarf or sleep on a silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, it's important to take care of it properly. This means washing it regularly, conditioning it, and avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible. You should also use a wide-toothed comb or a detangling brush to prevent breakage and damage to the hair.

Conclusion

Dutch braids are a fun and versatile hairstyle that anyone can do. With a little practice, you can master the art of Dutch braiding someone else's hair and create a variety of different looks. Just remember to be patient, take your time, and have fun with it!
