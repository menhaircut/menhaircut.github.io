How To Cut Long Curly Boy Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Do you have a long-haired curly boy at home and want to give him a haircut? With a few tools and some patience, you can do it yourself at home. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut long curly boy hair at home. We will also give you some tips and answer frequently asked questions about hair care. So, let's get started!

How to Cut Long Curly Boy Hair at Home

Cutting long curly boy hair at home can be challenging, but it's not impossible. Here are some things you will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A mirror

Before you start cutting, make sure your child's hair is clean and dry. Wet hair is harder to cut, and you may end up cutting more than you intended.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut long curly boy hair at home:

  1. Start by parting your child's hair in the middle. Use the comb to create a straight line from the forehead to the nape of the neck.
  2. Brush the hair down on both sides of the parting, so it's smooth and straight.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the front and comb it straight. Hold it between your fingers, and cut it to the desired length. Do the same for the other side.
  4. Continue cutting small sections of hair on both sides, making sure they are the same length. Cut the hair at an angle to create layers.
  5. When you reach the back of the head, work in small sections to ensure you don't miss any hair.
  6. Stand behind your child to cut the back of the hair. Comb it straight and cut it at an angle to create layers.
  7. Use the scissors to trim any uneven bits.
  8. Brush the hair to remove any loose hair.
  9. Style the hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you cut curly hair:

  • Don't cut the hair too short as curly hair tends to look shorter when dry.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid splitting the hair ends.
  • Don't cut the hair when it's wet as it will look shorter when dry.
  • Don't pull the hair too tight when cutting as it will bounce back when released.
  • Take your time and be patient.

FAQs

1. How often should I cut my child's hair?

It depends on how quickly the hair grows. On average, children's hair grows about half an inch per month. So, it's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks.

2. Should I cut my child's hair when it's wet or dry?

It's better to cut curly hair when it's dry as it will look shorter when dry. Wet hair is harder to cut, and you may end up cutting more than you intended.

3. Can I use regular scissors to cut hair?

No, it's recommended to use sharp scissors specifically designed for cutting hair. Regular scissors can cause split ends and damage the hair.

Hair Care

Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Wash the hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and moisturized.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle the hair when wet. Don't use a brush as it can cause breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as they can damage the hair. If you have to use them, use a heat protectant spray.
  • Trim the hair regularly to get rid of split ends and keep it healthy.
  • Avoid pulling the hair too tight as it can cause breakage.

Now that you know how to cut long curly boy hair at home, you can save money and time by doing it yourself. Just remember to be patient and take your time. Good luck!
