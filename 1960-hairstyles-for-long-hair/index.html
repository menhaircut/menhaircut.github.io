1960 Hairstyles For Long Hair A Guide To Retro Glamour

Description

If you're looking to channel your inner vintage goddess, consider trying out 1960 hairstyles for long hair. This era was all about big, voluminous hair with plenty of curls and waves. Think Brigitte Bardot and Audrey Hepburn – these icons of the 60s were known for their glamorous hairstyles that still inspire us today.

Whether you have naturally long hair or you're willing to invest in some hair extensions, you can achieve a variety of 1960s hairstyles that will make you feel like a true bombshell. From the beehive to the bouffant, the 1960s had plenty of iconic hairstyles that are worth trying out today.

How to Achieve 1960 Hairstyles for Long Hair

The key to achieving 1960 hairstyles for long hair is to invest in some styling tools and products that will help you create volume and hold. Here are some essentials to consider:

  • Hot rollers
  • Curling iron
  • Hairspray
  • Teasing comb
  • Bobby pins

With these tools in hand, you can start experimenting with different 1960s hairstyles that will make you feel like a retro queen.

Beehive

This iconic hairstyle is all about creating a voluminous updo that sits high on your head. To achieve the beehive, you'll need to tease your hair at the crown and use bobby pins to secure it in place. You can also add a bouffant or some curls to the ends of your hair for added glamour.

Bouffant

The bouffant is another classic 1960s hairstyle that involves teasing your hair at the crown and creating a bump of volume. You can then smooth your hair back and secure it in a ponytail or updo. This hairstyle is perfect for formal events or just adding a touch of vintage flair to your everyday look.

Big Curls

If you want to keep your long hair down, consider adding some big curls to create a classic 1960s look. You can use a curling iron or hot rollers to achieve this style, and be sure to finish it off with some hairspray to hold the curls in place.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to achieving a beehive hairstyle:

  1. Start by sectioning off the top of your hair with a clip.
  2. Tease the hair at the crown with a teasing comb, creating a bump of volume.
  3. Smooth the top layer of hair over the teased section and secure it with bobby pins.
  4. Take the rest of your hair and pull it into a high ponytail, leaving some hair out at the front.
  5. Tease the hair in the ponytail to create more volume, then twist it into a bun and secure it with bobby pins.
  6. Use a curling iron or hot rollers to curl the front sections of your hair.
  7. Finish off the look with some hairspray to hold everything in place.

Tips for Maintaining Your 1960 Hairstyle

Once you've achieved your desired 1960 hairstyle, there are a few tips to keep in mind to maintain it throughout the day:

  • Use hairspray to hold your hairstyle in place.
  • Carry bobby pins and a teasing comb with you for touch-ups throughout the day.
  • Avoid touching or brushing your hair too much, as this can cause your hairstyle to fall apart.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about 1960 hairstyles for long hair:

Can I achieve 1960 hairstyles with short hair?

While some 1960s hairstyles were specifically designed for longer hair, there are still plenty of options for those with shorter hair. Consider trying out a pixie cut or a sleek bob for a retro look.

Do I need to use hot rollers to achieve 1960 hairstyles?

While hot rollers can be helpful for achieving certain 1960s hairstyles, they're not always necessary. You can also use a curling iron or other heat styling tools to create the look you want.

How often should I wash my hair to maintain my 1960s style?

It's generally recommended to wash your hair every two to three days to maintain your 1960s hairstyle. This will help preserve the volume and hold of your hair, while also keeping it healthy and clean.

Hair Care Tips for Long Hair

Finally, if you want to maintain healthy and beautiful long hair, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors by wearing a hat or using a protective spray.

With these tips in mind, you can achieve a beautiful and authentic 1960s hairstyle that will make you feel like a true vintage queen.
