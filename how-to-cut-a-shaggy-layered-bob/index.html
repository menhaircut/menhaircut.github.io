How To Cut A Shaggy Layered Bob A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking for a new haircut that's both trendy and easy to maintain, a shaggy layered bob might be the perfect choice for you. This style is versatile, flattering, and works well for different hair types and face shapes. In this post, we'll show you how to cut a shaggy layered bob at home, step by step. We'll also provide some tips on how to style and care for your new haircut. Let's get started!

Description

A shaggy layered bob is a haircut that combines the classic bob shape with choppy layers and textured ends. This style is great for adding volume and movement to fine or thin hair, as well as softening the angles of a square or angular face. A shaggy layered bob can be cut to different lengths, from chin-length to shoulder-length, and can be customized with different fringe styles and layering techniques. This haircut is low-maintenance and can be styled in different ways, from messy and tousled to sleek and polished.

How to Cut a Shaggy Layered Bob

Cutting a shaggy layered bob requires some basic hair cutting skills, but it's not too difficult to do at home with the right tools and techniques. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Sectioning clips or rubber bands
  • A comb or brush
  • A spray bottle with water
  • A mirror or someone to assist you

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut a shaggy layered bob:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair that's been brushed or combed to remove tangles and knots.
  2. Section your hair into four parts: the top, left side, right side, and back. Use sectioning clips or rubber bands to keep each section separate.
  3. Starting at the back, take a small section of hair (about 1 inch wide) and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it using the point-cutting technique: point the scissors upward and make small snips into the hair, creating a textured, choppy effect. Repeat this process on the rest of the back section, working your way up to the crown.
  4. Move to the left side section and repeat the point-cutting technique on the bottom layer of hair. Then, take a diagonal section of hair from the left side to the crown and cut it at a 45-degree angle using the same technique. This will create a diagonal layer that blends into the back section.
  5. Repeat step 4 on the right side section, making sure to match the angle and length of the left side layer.
  6. Finally, move to the top section and cut it using the same point-cutting technique, but leaving the length slightly longer than the sides and back. This will create a layered effect and add volume to the crown.
  7. Trim any stray or uneven hairs, especially around the neckline and ears.
  8. If you want bangs, you can cut them now using either the point-cutting or blunt-cutting technique. For a shaggy look, you can cut them at an angle or with a razor.
  9. Style your hair as desired, using a texturizing spray or pomade to enhance the choppy, shaggy effect.
  10. Admire your new shaggy layered bob!

Tips for Cutting a Shaggy Layered Bob

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting a shaggy layered bob:

  • Start with a conservative length, and gradually cut more if you want a shorter or more dramatic look.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as it's easier to trim more than to add length back.
  • Use the point-cutting technique sparingly on the top layer, as too much texture can create a frizzy or messy look.
  • Keep your scissors sharp and clean, and sanitize them before and after use.
  • If you're not confident in cutting your hair yourself, consider getting a professional haircut or practicing on a wig or mannequin head first.

FAQs

Can I cut a shaggy layered bob on curly or wavy hair?

Absolutely! In fact, a shaggy layered bob can enhance the natural texture and volume of curly or wavy hair. Just make sure to cut the layers in a way that complements your curl pattern, and use a diffuser or air-dry your hair to avoid frizz.

How often do I need to trim a shaggy layered bob?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain your haircut. As a general rule, you should trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep the layers and shape intact. However, if you notice split ends, breakage, or unevenness, you may need to trim more frequently.

Can I color my shaggy layered bob?

Yes, you can! A shaggy layered bob can be a great canvas for different hair colors and highlights. Just make sure to use a gentle, ammonia-free hair dye and to follow the instructions carefully. You may also want to consult a professional colorist if you're new to hair coloring.

Hair Care Tips for a Shaggy Layered Bob

To keep your shaggy layered bob looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and avoid over-washing or using hot water.
  • Air-dry your hair or use a diffuser to avoid heat damage, and minimize the use of hot tools like flat irons or curling wands.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and smooth.
  • Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and pollution by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your haircut.

Cutting a shaggy layered bob can be a fun and rewarding experience, especially if you're looking for a low-maintenance yet stylish haircut. With the right tools, techniques, and care, you can rock this look with confidence and ease. We hope you found this guide helpful and informative. Happy cutting!
