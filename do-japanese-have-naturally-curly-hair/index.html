Do Japanese Have Naturally Curly Hair

Written by Robby Sep 12, 2023 · 4 min read

Welcome to our blog about all things hair! Today, we will be discussing a topic that we often get asked about: do Japanese people have naturally curly hair? In this article, we will explore the answer to this question, how to care for curly hair, and provide some tips for those who want to embrace their natural curls.

Table of Contents

  • Do Japanese Have Curly Hair?
  • Types of Curly Hair
  • How to Care for Curly Hair
  • Tips for Embracing Your Natural Curls
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Final Thoughts

Do Japanese Have Curly Hair?

The answer is yes, some Japanese people do have naturally curly hair. Although straight hair is often associated with Japanese people, there are actually many different hair types and textures within the Japanese population.

However, it is important to note that the majority of Japanese people have straight or slightly wavy hair. This is due to genetic factors, as well as cultural beauty standards that often prioritize straight hair.

Types of Curly Hair

Curly hair can come in many different types and textures. The most common classification system for curly hair is the Andre Walker Hair Typing System, which categorizes hair into four types:

  • Type 1: Straight hair
  • Type 2: Wavy hair
  • Type 3: Curly hair
  • Type 4: Coily hair

Within the Type 3 and Type 4 categories, there are also subcategories based on the size and shape of the curls.

How to Care for Curly Hair

Caring for curly hair can be a bit more challenging than caring for straight hair, as curls tend to be more prone to dryness, frizz, and tangles. Here are some tips for keeping curly hair healthy and looking its best:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for curly hair
  • Avoid brushing or combing hair when wet, as this can cause breakage and frizz
  • Instead, use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle hair while it is damp
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define and moisturize curls
  • Avoid heat styling tools, as these can cause damage and dryness
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent tangles and frizz

Tips for Embracing Your Natural Curls

If you have naturally curly hair, it can be tempting to straighten it or try to make it conform to cultural beauty standards. However, embracing your natural curls can be a liberating and empowering experience. Here are some tips for embracing your curls:

  • Experiment with different haircuts and styles that work well with your curls
  • Use products that enhance and define your curls, such as curl creams and gels
  • Avoid comparing your curls to others', as everyone's hair is unique and beautiful in its own way
  • Remember that curly hair is not a trend or a fad, but a natural and beautiful hair type

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Japanese people have afro-textured hair?

While afro-textured hair is not common among Japanese people, it is possible for someone of Japanese descent to have afro-textured hair if they have African or Afro-Caribbean ancestry.

Can Japanese people get perms?

Yes, Japanese people can get perms if they want to add curl or volume to their hair. However, it is important to find a stylist who is experienced in working with curly hair and to use high-quality products to prevent damage.

What hair products are best for curly hair?

There are many hair products on the market that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as curl creams, gels, and leave-in conditioners. It is best to look for products that are sulfate-free and contain ingredients that moisturize and define curls, such as shea butter, coconut oil, and aloe vera.

Final Thoughts

So, do Japanese people have naturally curly hair? The answer is yes, some do! However, straight hair is still the most common hair type among Japanese people. If you have naturally curly hair, we hope that this article has provided you with some useful tips for caring for and embracing your curls. Remember, curly hair is beautiful and unique, so rock those curls with pride!
