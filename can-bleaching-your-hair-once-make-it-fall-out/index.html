Can Bleaching Your Hair Once Make It Fall Out

Description

Bleaching your hair can be a fun way to change up your look. However, many people are hesitant to bleach their hair because they fear it will fall out. While it is true that bleaching your hair can cause damage, it is not necessarily true that it will make your hair fall out.

There are a few factors that determine whether or not your hair will fall out after bleaching:

  • The health of your hair before bleaching
  • The strength of the bleach used
  • The length of time the bleach is left on your hair
  • Whether or not you follow proper hair care after bleaching

How To Bleach Your Hair Safely

If you want to bleach your hair without causing damage, there are a few things you can do:

  • Choose a high-quality bleach
  • Use a lower volume developer
  • Do not leave the bleach on your hair for too long
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment after bleaching

Choose a High-Quality Bleach

The quality of the bleach you use is important. Cheaper bleaches often contain harsh chemicals that can damage your hair. Look for a high-quality bleach that is specifically formulated for at-home use.

Use a Lower Volume Developer

The developer is the ingredient that activates the bleach. The higher the volume of developer, the more damage the bleach can cause. If you have fine or fragile hair, use a lower volume developer.

Do Not Leave the Bleach on Your Hair for Too Long

The longer you leave the bleach on your hair, the more damage it can cause. Follow the instructions on the packaging and do not leave the bleach on your hair for longer than recommended.

Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment After Bleaching

Bleaching your hair can cause it to become dry and brittle. After bleaching, use a deep conditioning treatment to restore moisture and strength to your hair.

Step By Step Guide

If you are new to bleaching your hair, here is a step-by-step guide:

  1. Choose a high-quality bleach and developer
  2. Read the instructions carefully
  3. Put on gloves to protect your hands
  4. Mix the bleach and developer in a plastic bowl
  5. Divide your hair into sections and apply the bleach to each section
  6. Leave the bleach on your hair for the recommended amount of time
  7. Rinse your hair thoroughly with lukewarm water
  8. Apply a deep conditioning treatment
  9. Style your hair as desired

Tips For Bleaching Your Hair

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Do not bleach your hair if it is already damaged
  • Take a break between bleachings to allow your hair to recover
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair after bleaching
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid exposing your hair to chlorine and saltwater

FAQs About Bleaching Your Hair

Can bleaching your hair once make it fall out?

Bleaching your hair once is not necessarily going to make it fall out. However, it can cause damage and breakage if not done properly.

How long should I wait between bleachings?

It is recommended that you wait at least four weeks between bleachings to allow your hair to recover.

Can I bleach my hair if I have previously relaxed it?

If you have relaxed your hair, it is best to avoid bleaching it. Relaxed hair is already fragile and prone to damage, and bleaching can make it even more so.

Hair Care After Bleaching

Proper hair care after bleaching is essential to keep your hair healthy and prevent further damage. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible
  • Avoid exposing your hair to chlorine and saltwater
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Trim your hair regularly to remove split ends

In conclusion, bleaching your hair once is not necessarily going to make it fall out. However, it can cause damage and breakage if not done properly. Follow the tips and guidelines outlined in this article to safely bleach your hair and keep it healthy.
