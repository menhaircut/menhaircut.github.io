How To Stop Hair Growth In Pcos Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Sep 17, 2023 · 4 min read

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder that affects many women. One of the most common symptoms of PCOS is unwanted hair growth, known as hirsutism. If you are struggling with PCOS-related hair growth, you are not alone. In this article, we will discuss how to stop hair growth in PCOS, including tips and tricks to manage this condition.

Table of Contents

  • How to Stop Hair Growth in PCOS
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Managing PCOS Hair Growth
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Stop Hair Growth in PCOS

There are several ways to stop hair growth in PCOS. These include:

  • Medications
  • Hormonal therapies
  • Topical treatments
  • Hair removal techniques

It is important to note that not all of these treatments will work for everyone. You should always consult with your healthcare provider to determine the best course of treatment for your individual needs.

Medications

There are several medications that can help reduce hair growth in PCOS. These include:

  • Spironolactone
  • Flutamide
  • Finasteride

These medications work by blocking the effects of androgens, which are hormones that can cause hair growth in women with PCOS.

Hormonal Therapies

Hormonal therapies can also be effective in reducing hair growth in PCOS. These include:

  • Birth control pills
  • GnRH agonists
  • Metformin

Birth control pills can regulate hormone levels and reduce hair growth. GnRH agonists work by reducing the production of androgens. Metformin is typically used to treat diabetes, but it can also help regulate hormone levels in women with PCOS.

Topical Treatments

Topical treatments can also be effective in reducing hair growth in PCOS. These include:

  • Eflornithine cream
  • Retinoids

Eflornithine cream works by slowing down the growth of hair follicles. Retinoids can help reduce inflammation and improve skin texture, which can help reduce the appearance of hair growth.

Hair Removal Techniques

There are several hair removal techniques that can help manage hair growth in PCOS. These include:

  • Shaving
  • Waxing
  • Threading
  • Laser hair removal
  • Electrolysis

It is important to note that these techniques only provide temporary relief and may need to be repeated regularly.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you are struggling with hair growth in PCOS, here is a step-by-step guide to help you manage this condition:

  1. Consult with your healthcare provider to determine the best course of treatment.
  2. Select a hair removal technique that works best for you.
  3. Follow a regular hair removal schedule.
  4. Consider using topical treatments to help reduce hair growth.
  5. Practice good hair care habits to maintain healthy hair.

Tips for Managing PCOS Hair Growth

Here are some tips to help manage hair growth in PCOS:

  • Avoid plucking or threading, as these techniques can stimulate hair growth.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your skin and hair.
  • Consider using a depilatory cream instead of shaving.
  • Exfoliate regularly to prevent ingrown hairs.
  • Use a moisturizer to prevent dry skin.

FAQ

Can PCOS hair growth be permanent?

PCOS hair growth can be permanent if left untreated. However, with proper treatment and management, it is possible to reduce hair growth and prevent it from becoming permanent.

Is laser hair removal safe for women with PCOS?

Yes, laser hair removal is safe for women with PCOS. However, it may not be effective for everyone and may need to be repeated regularly.

Are there any natural remedies for PCOS hair growth?

While there are no natural remedies that can cure PCOS hair growth, some women have found that spearmint tea can help reduce androgen levels and reduce hair growth.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips to help maintain healthy hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid tying your hair too tightly.

In conclusion, if you are struggling with PCOS-related hair growth, there are several ways to manage this condition. Consult with your healthcare provider to determine the best course of treatment for your individual needs. With proper treatment and management, it is possible to reduce hair growth and maintain healthy hair.
