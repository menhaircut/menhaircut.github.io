How To Cut Child s Long Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Nov 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a parent or guardian, you know that keeping a child's hair tidy and neat can be a challenge. Regular visits to the salon or barber can be expensive, and not always practical. In this article, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut a child's long hair at home. We'll cover everything from preparation to post-haircut care, so you can confidently give your child a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home.

How to Prepare for a Child's Haircut at Home

Before you begin cutting your child's hair, there are a few things you will need to do to prepare:

Gather Your Supplies

You will need a few basic supplies to cut your child's hair at home. These include:

  • Scissors
  • Clippers (if you plan to use them)
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or elastics
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Cape or towel to protect clothing

Choose the Right Time

Choose a time when your child is well-rested and not hungry. It's best to schedule the haircut for a time when you have plenty of time, and won't be interrupted. You may also want to choose a time when your child is in a good mood and feeling relaxed.

Find the Right Location

Choose a location where you can comfortably cut your child's hair. It's best to choose a place with good lighting and a flat surface for your child to sit on. You may also want to cover the floor with a towel or sheet to catch any hair that falls.

How to Cut a Child's Long Hair at Home

Now that you're prepared, it's time to start cutting your child's hair. Here are some step-by-step instructions:

Step 1: Wet the Hair

Use a spray bottle filled with water to wet your child's hair. This will make it easier to cut and prevent the hair from flying around.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Use a comb to section the hair. Divide it into four sections: two on the top and two on the bottom. Use hair clips or elastics to hold each section in place.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start cutting the hair in the back. Use scissors or clippers to trim the hair to the desired length. Work your way up to the top of the head, cutting small sections at a time. Be careful not to cut too much off at once.

Step 4: Trim the Bangs

If your child has bangs, trim them carefully. Use scissors to cut the bangs straight across, or at an angle. Be sure to cut them slightly longer than the desired length, as they will spring up slightly when dry.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

If your child's hair is layered, use scissors to blend the layers together. This will create a natural, flowing look.

Step 6: Check for Symmetry

Check for symmetry by comparing the length of the hair on each side of the head. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure that the hair is even.

Step 7: Clean Up

Use a comb and scissors to clean up any stray hairs around the ears and neck. Use a blow dryer to dry the hair, or allow it to air dry.

Tips for Cutting a Child's Long Hair at Home

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your child's hair at home:

  • Start with small snips, and work your way up to the desired length.
  • Be patient and take breaks if your child becomes restless.
  • Use a mirror to check your work and make sure the haircut is even.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help from a friend or family member if you need it.

FAQ About Cutting a Child's Long Hair at Home

What if I Make a Mistake?

If you make a mistake while cutting your child's hair, don't panic. You can always trim the hair a bit shorter, or take your child to a professional stylist to have it fixed.

How Often Should I Cut My Child's Hair?

How often you cut your child's hair will depend on how quickly it grows, and the desired length. As a general rule, most children need a haircut every 6-8 weeks.

Can I Use Clippers to Cut My Child's Hair?

Yes, you can use clippers to cut your child's hair. Just be sure to use the appropriate guard size, and go slowly to avoid cutting too much off at once.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Child's Long Hair at Home

After cutting your child's hair, be sure to wash it thoroughly to remove any loose hairs. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair healthy and shiny. You may also want to use a detangling spray or leave-in conditioner to make combing and styling easier.

Cutting your child's hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right preparation, tools, and technique, you can give your child a great haircut without leaving home. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
