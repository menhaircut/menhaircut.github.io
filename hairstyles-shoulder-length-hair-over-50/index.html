Hairstyles For Shoulder Length Hair Over 50

Written by Frank Jul 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're over 50 and looking for a stylish and practical hairstyle, shoulder-length hair is a great option. It's versatile, easy to maintain, and can be styled in many different ways to suit your personality and lifestyle. In this article, we'll show you some of the best hairstyles for shoulder length hair over 50, and provide you with tips on how to care for and style your locks.

Description

Shoulder-length hair is a popular choice for women over 50 because it's easy to manage, and is neither too short nor too long. It's a versatile hairstyle that can be worn straight, wavy, or curly, and can be styled in a variety of ways, including updos, braids, and ponytails. Shoulder-length hair is also a great option for those with thinning hair, as it can add volume and fullness to your locks.

How to

The first step in achieving a great hairstyle for shoulder-length hair is to choose the right cut. If you have thin or fine hair, a blunt cut with layers can add volume and texture to your locks. If you have thick hair, a choppy cut with layers can help to thin out your hair and make it more manageable. Once you have your cut, you can start experimenting with different styles.

Step by Step

Here are some step-by-step instructions for achieving some of the most popular shoulder-length hairstyles:

1. The Bob

The bob is a classic hairstyle that is perfect for shoulder-length hair. To achieve this style, simply cut your hair to chin-length or just above the shoulders, and then style it with a side part or a blunt fringe. You can also add texture by using a curling iron or flat iron to create soft waves or curls.

2. The Lob

The lob is a longer version of the bob, and is a great option for those who want to keep their hair off their face but still have some length. To achieve this style, cut your hair to shoulder-length, and style it with loose waves or curls. You can also add volume and texture by using a texturizing spray or mousse.

3. The Shag

The shag is a layered haircut that is great for those with fine or thinning hair. To achieve this style, cut your hair to shoulder-length, and then add layers throughout your hair. You can also add volume and texture by using a texturizing spray or mousse, and then style your hair with a messy, tousled look.

Tips

Here are some tips for caring for and styling your shoulder-length hair:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage your locks and make them look dull and lifeless.
  • Invest in a good quality hairbrush or comb, and use it to detangle your locks gently.
  • Try to avoid pulling your hair back tightly, as this can put stress on your hair and scalp and lead to breakage.
  • If you have thinning hair, consider using a volumizing spray or mousse to add fullness and texture to your locks.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about hairstyles for shoulder-length hair over 50:

1. Is shoulder-length hair suitable for all face shapes?

Yes, shoulder-length hair is a versatile hairstyle that can be adapted to suit all face shapes, including round, square, oval, and heart-shaped faces.

2. Can shoulder-length hair be styled up or down?

Yes, shoulder-length hair can be styled in many different ways, including updos, braids, and ponytails. It can also be worn down with loose waves or curls.

3. How often should I trim my shoulder-length hair?

It's recommended that you trim your hair every six to eight weeks to keep it healthy and looking its best.

Hair Care

Finally, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and shiny. This means using high-quality hair products, avoiding too much heat, and getting regular trims. With the right care and styling, shoulder-length hair can be a great choice for women over 50.

So go ahead and try out some of these hairstyles for shoulder length hair over 50, and enjoy the confidence and style that comes with a great haircut!
