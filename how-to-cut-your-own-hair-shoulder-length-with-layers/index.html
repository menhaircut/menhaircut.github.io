How To Cut Your Own Hair Shoulder Length With Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can seem like a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a simple and cost-effective way to maintain your style. In this guide, we will walk you through the process of cutting your own hair shoulder length with layers. Whether you're looking to save money or just want to try something new, this guide will give you the confidence to create a beautiful layered cut at home.

Tools You'll Need

Before you begin, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror
  • Spray bottle filled with water

How to Cut Your Own Hair Shoulder Length with Layers

Follow these step-by-step instructions to cut your own hair shoulder length with layers:

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Wash and dry your hair before you begin cutting. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting. Wet hair can appear longer than it actually is, which can lead to uneven cuts.

Step 2: Part your hair down the middle

Using a comb, part your hair down the middle from front to back. This will help you create even layers on both sides of your head.

Step 3: Section your hair

Section your hair into four parts: two on each side and two in the back. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

Step 4: Cut the back sections

Start with the back sections of your hair. Take one section at a time and comb it straight down. Use your fingers to hold the hair at the length you want to cut it. Use sharp scissors to cut the hair straight across. Repeat this process for the other back section.

Step 5: Cut the side sections

Move on to the side sections of your hair. Take one section at a time and comb it straight down. Use your fingers to hold the hair at the length you want to cut it. Cut the hair straight across, starting at the back and working your way to the front. Repeat this process for the other side section.

Step 6: Blend the layers

Remove the hair clips from the back sections and let your hair fall naturally. Use your comb to separate the layers and blend them together. Take small sections at a time and use your scissors to cut into the ends of the hair, creating a soft, layered look.

Step 7: Check your work

Use your mirror to check your work. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure that your hair is even and the layers are blended.

Tips for a Professional-Looking Cut

  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can lead to uneven cuts.
  • Take your time. Cutting your hair is not a race. Take breaks if you need to.
  • Start with small cuts. You can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back once it's cut.
  • Don't cut too much. It's better to cut a little bit at a time than to cut too much and regret it later.
  • Use a mirror to check your work from all angles.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Cutting curly hair can be more challenging than cutting straight hair. If you have curly hair, consider cutting it when it's dry so you can see the natural curl pattern. Cut small sections at a time and be patient.

Can I cut my own hair if it's thick?

Cutting thick hair can be a bit more time-consuming than cutting thin hair, but it's still possible to do at home. Use sharp scissors and take your time. Consider cutting your hair in sections to make it more manageable.

Is it better to cut my hair wet or dry?

It's generally better to cut your hair when it's dry. Wet hair can appear longer than it actually is, which can lead to uneven cuts. Dry hair also provides more control when cutting.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After cutting your own hair, it's important to take good care of it to maintain your new style. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to prevent split ends and breakage. Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to keep it looking fresh.

Cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can create a beautiful layered cut at home. Follow these step-by-step instructions and you'll be on your way to a new, stylish look.
