How To Cut Your Own Hair Short With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Oct 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to save time and money on haircuts, cutting your own hair short with clippers can be a great solution. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a professional-looking cut in the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting your own hair short with clippers, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparation
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Cleaning Up
  • Tips for a Great Cut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Your Cut

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to make sure you have the right tools. Here's what you should have on hand:

  • Clippers with guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Mirror
  • Towel or cape to catch hair
  • Clip or hair tie to section hair

Make sure your clippers are fully charged or plugged in before you begin.

Preparation

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. Comb it out to remove any tangles and make sure it's completely dry. Wear an old shirt or towel around your neck to protect your clothes from hair clippings.

Next, decide on the length you want your hair to be. Choose the appropriate guard for your clippers to achieve the desired length. If you're not sure which guard to use, start with a longer one and work your way down.

Section your hair using a clip or hair tie. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward, making sure to cut even sections.

Cutting Your Hair

Begin by using the clippers on the sides and back of your head. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using an upward motion. Use the comb to lift the hair and make sure it's even. Take your time and go slowly to avoid mistakes.

Move to the top of your head, using the same technique. Use the comb to lift the hair and clip it evenly. Be careful not to cut too much at once.

Once you've finished using the clippers, use scissors to trim any areas that need it. This is especially important around the ears and neckline.

Cleaning Up

Use a mirror to check for any missed spots or uneven areas. Use the clippers or scissors to fix any mistakes. Once you're satisfied with the cut, remove the guard from the clippers and use them to clean up the edges around your ears and neckline.

Take a shower to wash away any loose hair. Use a towel to dry your hair and style it as desired.

Tips for a Great Cut

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down if you're not sure what length you want.
  • Go slowly and take your time to avoid mistakes.
  • Use the comb to lift the hair and make sure it's even.
  • Trim any areas that need it with scissors.
  • Use a mirror to check for missed spots or uneven areas.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair with kitchen scissors?

No, you should use hair cutting scissors to ensure a clean cut and avoid damaging your hair.

Can I use clippers without guards?

No, using clippers without guards can result in uneven and choppy cuts. Always use guards to achieve a clean and even cut.

How often should I cut my hair?

This depends on your hair type and desired length. On average, most people need to get their hair cut every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care After Your Cut

After your cut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Trimming your hair regularly can also help maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

With this guide, you should now be able to confidently cut your own hair short with clippers. Remember to take your time and follow the steps carefully to achieve a professional-looking cut. Happy cutting!
