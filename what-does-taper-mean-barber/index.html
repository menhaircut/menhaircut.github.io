What Does Taper Mean Barber

Written by Bardi Aug 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you planning to visit a barber anytime soon? Do you want to get a stylish haircut but don't know the right terms to communicate with your barber? If so, you're in the right place. In this article, we will discuss the meaning of taper in barber terms, and everything else you need to know to get a perfect haircut.

Definition of Taper

Taper is a term used in the barber industry to describe the gradual change in hair length from the top of the head down to the neckline. It involves trimming the hair at the sides and back of the head shorter than the hair on top. Tapering is a popular technique used by barbers to blend different lengths of hair seamlessly.

How to Get a Taper Haircut

If you want to get a taper haircut, you should first find a reputable barber who is experienced in this technique. Once you have found your barber, follow these steps to get a perfect taper haircut:

Step 1: Choose Your Style

Before you visit the barber, you should have an idea of the style you want. You can choose from a low, medium or high taper, depending on how much hair you want to be trimmed. You can also decide whether you want a skin fade or a shadow fade.

Step 2: Communicate with Your Barber

When you arrive at the barber shop, communicate your desired style to your barber clearly. Show them pictures of the style you want, and explain how you want the taper to look. A good barber will listen to your needs and offer suggestions based on your hair type and face shape.

Step 3: Trim the Hair

Once your barber has understood your needs, they will start trimming your hair. They will use clippers and scissors to cut the hair at the sides and back of your head. They will gradually change the length of the hair as they move down towards the neckline.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

After trimming the hair, the barber will use a comb and scissors to blend the different lengths of hair seamlessly. They will make sure that there are no harsh lines or uneven lengths.

Step 5: Style the Hair

Finally, the barber will style your hair using a hairdryer, comb, and hair products. They will make sure that your hair looks exactly how you want it, and that it is easy to maintain.

Tips for Maintaining a Taper Haircut

Once you have got a taper haircut, you should take care of it to keep it looking sharp. Here are some tips for maintaining your taper haircut:

  • Visit your barber regularly to keep your hair in shape.
  • Use high-quality hair products to style your hair.
  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can damage it.
  • Use a comb or brush to style your hair gently.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a taper and a fade?

A taper involves gradually changing the length of the hair, while a fade involves cutting the hair very short and gradually blending it into the skin. Tapering is a more subtle technique than fading.

Can I get a taper haircut with long hair?

Yes, you can get a taper haircut with long hair. Your barber will trim the sides and back of your head shorter than the hair on top, creating a tapered effect.

How often should I get a taper haircut?

You should get a taper haircut every 2-4 weeks, depending on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it loses its shape.

Conclusion

Now that you know what taper means barber, you can confidently communicate with your barber and get the perfect haircut. Just remember to choose a reputable barber, communicate your desired style clearly, and take care of your hair to maintain your taper haircut.
