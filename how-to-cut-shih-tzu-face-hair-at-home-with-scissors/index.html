How To Cut Shih Tzu Face Hair At Home With Scissors

Are you tired of paying a professional groomer to trim your Shih Tzu's face hair? With a little patience and practice, you can learn how to cut your Shih Tzu's face hair at home with scissors. In this blog post, we will provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to do it yourself, along with tips and frequently asked questions.

How to Cut Shih Tzu Face Hair at Home with Scissors

Before you start cutting your Shih Tzu's face hair, you will need the following items:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb or brush
  • Treats (optional)

It's important to use sharp scissors to avoid pulling or tearing your dog's hair. If your scissors are dull, it can cause discomfort and uneven cuts. You can use any type of scissors, but we recommend using grooming scissors made specifically for dogs.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Follow these step-by-step instructions to cut your Shih Tzu's face hair:

  1. Brush or comb your Shih Tzu's face hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  2. Hold your Shih Tzu's head still with one hand while using the other hand to trim the hair around the eyes. Use the tips of the scissors to trim the hair, and be careful not to poke your dog's eyes.
  3. Trim the hair on the top of your Shih Tzu's head by holding the hair between your fingers and using the tips of the scissors to trim the hair to the desired length.
  4. Trim the hair on the sides of your Shih Tzu's face by holding the hair between your fingers and cutting it to the desired length.
  5. Trim the hair on your Shih Tzu's chin by holding the hair between your fingers and cutting it to the desired length.
  6. Trim the hair on your Shih Tzu's cheeks by holding the hair between your fingers and cutting it to the desired length.
  7. Step back and check your work. If there are any uneven spots, use the scissors to even them out.
  8. Give your Shih Tzu a treat and praise for being a good dog!

Tips for Cutting Your Shih Tzu's Face Hair

Here are some tips to help you cut your Shih Tzu's face hair:

  • Start slowly and work in small sections. It's better to cut too little than too much.
  • Use treats to reward your Shih Tzu for staying still and calm during the grooming process.
  • Be careful not to cut the skin or poke your dog's eyes with the scissors.
  • Always use sharp scissors to avoid pulling or tearing your dog's hair.
  • If your Shih Tzu has a lot of hair, it may be easier to trim the hair in several sessions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use any type of scissors to cut my Shih Tzu's face hair?

You can use any type of scissors, but we recommend using grooming scissors made specifically for dogs. These scissors are designed to cut through thick, matted hair without pulling or tearing.

How often should I cut my Shih Tzu's face hair?

It depends on how quickly your dog's hair grows. Some Shih Tzus need to be groomed every 4-6 weeks, while others can go up to 3 months between grooming sessions. It's important to keep your Shih Tzu's hair trimmed to prevent matting and tangles.

What should I do if I accidentally cut my Shih Tzu's skin?

If you accidentally cut your dog's skin, apply pressure to the wound with a clean cloth or tissue. If the bleeding doesn't stop after a few minutes, contact your veterinarian.

Hair Care for Your Shih Tzu

Regular grooming is essential for your Shih Tzu's health and well-being. In addition to trimming their face hair, you should also brush their coat regularly to prevent matting and tangles. You can also bathe your Shih Tzu once a month to keep their coat clean and shiny.

By following these tips and step-by-step instructions, you can learn how to cut your Shih Tzu's face hair at home with scissors. With a little practice and patience, you can save money and bond with your furry friend at the same time. Happy grooming!
