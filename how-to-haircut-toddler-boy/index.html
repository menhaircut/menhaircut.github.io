How To Haircut Toddler Boy A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Sep 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a parent struggling to give your toddler boy a haircut? Do you dread taking them to the salon or barber shop? Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut your toddler boy's hair at home, as well as tips and FAQs to help you along the way. Let's get started!

How to

Before you begin, gather all necessary tools, such as scissors, clippers, combs, and spray bottles. Make sure your toddler is calm and comfortable, and distract them with toys or a favorite movie if necessary. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to haircut toddler boy:

Step 1: Wash and Brush Hair

Start by washing your toddler's hair and brushing it thoroughly. Wet hair is much easier to cut and detangle, so make sure it's damp but not dripping.

Step 2: Trim the Bangs

Use scissors to trim the bangs first. Comb them down straight and cut across in a straight line. Be careful not to cut too much or too close to the scalp.

Step 3: Use Clippers

Next, use clippers with a guard to trim the sides and back of the hair. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one. Use a comb to lift sections of hair and cut in a straight line.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

Use scissors to blend the hair on top with the shorter sides and back. Cut small sections at a time and blend the hair using a comb.

Step 5: Clean Up

Use scissors to clean up any stray hairs or uneven edges. Check the haircut from all angles and make any necessary adjustments.

Step 6: Style

Finally, style the hair as desired using a comb or brush. Apply a small amount of hair gel or pomade if necessary.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help make the haircut process easier:

  • Use distractions such as toys or videos to keep your toddler calm and still.
  • Start with longer guards and work your way down to shorter ones to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use a spray bottle to dampen the hair if it starts to dry out during the haircut.
  • Take breaks if necessary to give your toddler a chance to move around or take a snack break.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about how to haircut toddler boy:

How often should I cut my toddler's hair?

This depends on how fast their hair grows and their hairstyle. Generally, a haircut every 4-6 weeks is sufficient for most toddlers.

Is it okay to use clippers on a toddler's hair?

Yes, it's safe to use clippers on a toddler's hair as long as you use a guard and are careful not to cut too close to the scalp.

What should I do if my toddler won't sit still during the haircut?

Try using distractions such as toys or videos, or take a break and try again later. If your toddler is really resisting, consider taking them to a professional salon or barber shop.

Hair Care

After the haircut, make sure to wash your toddler's hair thoroughly to remove any loose hairs. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep their hair soft and healthy. Avoid using styling products or heat styling tools on their hair as much as possible.

Cutting your toddler boy's hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience for both of you. With the right tools and techniques, you can give them a stylish and comfortable haircut without leaving home. Good luck!
