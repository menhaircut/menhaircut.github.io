How To Cut Your Own Relaxed Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to save some money and give yourself a fresh new look, cutting your own relaxed hair can be a great option. While it can be intimidating to take scissors to your own hair, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy cut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to safely and successfully cut your own relaxed hair, as well as offer some tips for maintaining your new look.

Description

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a rewarding experience. In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know to give yourself a fresh new look, from preparing your hair and tools to making the first cut. We'll also offer some tips for maintaining your new cut and keeping your hair healthy.

How to Cut Your Own Relaxed Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, you'll need to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. It's also a good idea to have a mirror or two set up so you can see your hair from all angles.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. This will help you see any split ends or damaged areas that need to be trimmed. Once your hair is dry, comb it out so it's smooth and tangle-free.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into small sections that are easy to work with. Clip each section up and out of the way, starting at the back and working your way forward.

Step 4: Make the First Cut

Starting with the back sections, take a small piece of hair between your fingers and make a small snip. Be conservative with your first cut and take off only a little bit of hair at a time. You can always go back and take off more if you need to.

Step 5: Work Your Way Around Your Head

Continue working your way around your head, taking small sections and making small snips. Be sure to keep your cuts even and symmetrical on both sides of your head.

Step 6: Check Your Progress

As you go, check your progress in the mirrors you've set up. Make sure your cuts are even and that you're happy with the length and shape of your hair.

Step 7: Trim Any Stray Hairs

Once you've finished cutting all the sections, go back and trim any stray hairs or uneven areas. Use your comb to lift up sections of hair and trim any areas that need it.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Once you're happy with your cut, style your hair as you normally would. You may want to blow dry and flat iron your hair to get a sleek, polished look.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Relaxed Hair

  • Start with a conservative cut and take off only a little bit of hair at a time.
  • Use hair clips to section your hair and make it easier to work with.
  • Be sure to keep your cuts even and symmetrical on both sides of your head.
  • Check your progress in multiple mirrors to ensure evenness and symmetry.
  • Trim any stray hairs or uneven areas after you've finished cutting all the sections.
  • Consider cutting your hair in stages, starting with a small trim and gradually taking off more as you get more comfortable.

FAQ

Can I cut my own relaxed hair?

Yes, you can cut your own relaxed hair. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy cut from the comfort of your own home.

What tools do I need to cut my own relaxed hair?

You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. It's also a good idea to have a mirror or two set up so you can see your hair from all angles.

How do I avoid cutting too much hair?

How do I maintain my new cut?

To maintain your new cut, be sure to trim any stray hairs or uneven areas regularly. Use a nourishing hair oil or serum to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized, and avoid over-styling with heat tools. Use a hair oil or serum to add shine and prevent split ends, and trim your hair regularly to maintain your new cut.
