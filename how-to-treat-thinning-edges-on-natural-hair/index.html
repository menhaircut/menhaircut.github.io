How To Treat Thinning Edges On Natural Hair

Written by Petter Nov 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have natural hair, then you are probably aware that thinning edges can be a common problem. Thinning edges can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, improper styling, and harsh chemical treatments. However, you don't have to live with thinning edges forever. In this article, we will be discussing how to treat thinning edges on natural hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Treat Thinning Edges on Natural Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips to Prevent Thinning Edges
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Thinning Edges

How to Treat Thinning Edges on Natural Hair

The first step in treating thinning edges is to identify the cause. Once you know the cause, you can then take steps to remedy the problem. Here are some common causes of thinning edges:

1. Genetics

If thinning edges run in your family, then there may not be much you can do to prevent it. However, you can take steps to slow down the process.

2. Improper Styling

Wearing tight hairstyles, such as braids and weaves, can cause stress on the hairline, leading to thinning edges. If possible, try to avoid wearing these styles for long periods of time.

3. Harsh Chemical Treatments

Chemical treatments, such as relaxers and perms, can cause damage to the hairline, leading to thinning edges. If you must use these treatments, be sure to follow the instructions carefully and avoid leaving the product on for too long.

Step by Step Guide

Now that you know the common causes of thinning edges, here are some steps you can take to treat the problem:

1. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help to improve blood circulation, which can promote hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp for a few minutes each day.

2. Use Natural Oils

Natural oils, such as coconut oil and castor oil, can help to nourish the hairline and promote hair growth. Apply the oil to your hairline each night before bed.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid wearing tight hairstyles, such as braids and weaves, for long periods of time. If you must wear these styles, be sure to take breaks in between and give your hairline a chance to rest.

4. Use a Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help to prevent breakage and damage to your hairline. Satin is a smooth fabric that won't snag or pull on your hair.

Tips to Prevent Thinning Edges

Prevention is key when it comes to thinning edges. Here are some tips to help you prevent the problem:

1. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet rich in vitamins and nutrients can help to promote healthy hair growth. Be sure to eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, and lean protein.

2. Avoid Chemical Treatments

Avoid using harsh chemical treatments, such as relaxers and perms. If you must use these treatments, be sure to follow the instructions carefully and avoid leaving the product on for too long.

3. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid pulling or tugging on your hairline, and be gentle when brushing or combing your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with soft bristles.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can thinning edges be reversed?

Yes, thinning edges can be reversed with proper treatment and care.

2. How long does it take to see results?

It can take several weeks or even months to see results, so be patient and consistent with your treatment.

3. Are there any natural remedies for thinning edges?

Yes, natural oils such as coconut oil and castor oil can help to nourish the hairline and promote hair growth.

Hair Care for Thinning Edges

Once you have treated your thinning edges, it's important to take steps to prevent the problem from occurring again. Here are some tips to help you care for your hair:

1. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid wearing tight hairstyles, such as braids and weaves, for long periods of time. If you must wear these styles, be sure to take breaks in between and give your hairline a chance to rest.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and be gentle when brushing or combing your hair.

3. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, on a regular basis. If you must use these tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

In conclusion, thinning edges can be a common problem for those with natural hair. However, with proper care and treatment, you can reverse the problem and prevent it from occurring again. Remember to be patient and consistent with your treatment, and always be gentle with your hair.
