How To Give A Low Taper Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Giving a low taper fade is a popular hairstyle for men that involves gradually cutting the hair shorter as it goes down the head. This style is great for those who want a clean and polished look, and it's a versatile style that can be worn in a variety of ways. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to give a low taper fade, including tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Give a Low Taper Fade
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Give a Low Taper Fade

To give a low taper fade, you'll need a pair of clippers, scissors, a comb, and some hair product. It's important to start with clean, dry hair that's been washed and conditioned.

Step 1: Start with a Longer Guard

Begin by using clippers with a longer guard to trim the hair on the top of the head. You can start with a #4 or #5 guard and work your way down.

Step 2: Blend the Sides

Use the clippers with a shorter guard to blend the hair on the sides of the head. Start with a #2 or #3 guard and work your way down. Blend the hair in a circular motion, working from the bottom up.

Step 3: Taper the Neckline

Use the clippers with a shorter guard to taper the hair on the neckline. Start with a #1 or #2 guard and work your way down. Taper the hair in an upward motion, blending it into the hair on the sides and top of the head.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Use scissors to cut the hair on the top of the head to the desired length. Use a comb to hold the hair in place and cut it in a straight line.

Step 5: Blend the Top

Use the clippers with a longer guard to blend the hair on the top of the head into the hair on the sides. Blend the hair in a circular motion, working from the top down.

Step 6: Finish with Product

Apply a small amount of hair product to the hair to give it a polished look. Use a comb to style the hair as desired.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to give a low taper fade:

  1. Start with a longer guard
  2. Blend the sides
  3. Taper the neckline
  4. Cut the top
  5. Blend the top
  6. Finish with product

Tips for a Great Low Taper Fade

Here are some tips for giving a great low taper fade:

  • Take your time and work slowly
  • Use a mirror to check your progress
  • Use a comb to hold the hair in place while you cut
  • Use the clippers in a circular motion for a more natural look
  • Blend the hair gradually for a seamless transition

FAQs

How often should I get a low taper fade?

It's recommended to get a low taper fade every 2-4 weeks to maintain the style.

Can I give myself a low taper fade?

It's possible to give yourself a low taper fade, but it's recommended to have a professional do it for the best results.

What type of hair works best for a low taper fade?

A low taper fade works best on straight or slightly wavy hair.

Hair Care for a Low Taper Fade

To maintain a low taper fade, it's important to keep your hair clean and moisturized. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner, and apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Avoid using too much heat on your hair, and use a heat protectant if you do use hot tools. Trim your hair regularly to maintain the style, and use a hair product that works well for your hair type and desired look.

With these tips and techniques, you can give a great low taper fade that looks polished and professional. Whether you're a barber or just want to learn how to give a fade at home, this style is a classic look that's sure to impress.
