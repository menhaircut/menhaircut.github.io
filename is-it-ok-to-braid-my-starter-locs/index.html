Is It Ok To Braid My Starter Locs

Written by Daniel Aug 14, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you considering starting your loc journey but not sure if it’s ok to braid your starter locs? Or maybe you’ve already started your journey and are wondering if you can braid your hair without damaging your locs? Whatever your situation, we understand the importance of taking care of your hair during the loc journey. That’s why we’ve put together this guide to help you answer the question, “Is it ok to braid my starter locs?”

Description

Before we dive into whether or not it’s ok to braid your starter locs, let’s first define what starter locs are. Starter locs are the beginning stages of the loc journey. This is when your hair is starting to form into individual locs but they are not fully matured yet. This stage can last anywhere from a few months to a year or more, depending on your hair type and how you care for your hair.

How to

If you’ve decided to braid your starter locs, there are a few things to keep in mind to ensure that your locs remain healthy and strong:

1. Choose the right type of braids

Not all braids are created equal, and some can be more damaging to your hair than others. When it comes to braiding your locs, it’s best to stick with simple braiding styles that won’t put too much tension on your hair. Avoid braids that are too tight or that pull on your hairline, as this can cause breakage and damage to your locs.

2. Don’t braid your hair too often

While it’s ok to braid your starter locs occasionally, it’s important not to do it too often. Braiding your hair too often can cause tension and stress on your locs, which can lead to breakage and damage. Aim to braid your hair no more than once a month, and give your hair a break in between braiding sessions.

3. Moisturize your hair regularly

Keeping your hair moisturized is essential to maintaining healthy locs. When you braid your hair, it can be more difficult for moisture to penetrate your hair shaft. To combat this, make sure to moisturize your hair regularly with a good leave-in conditioner or oil. This will help to keep your hair soft and supple, which can prevent breakage and damage.

Step by step

Here’s a step-by-step guide to braiding your starter locs:

Step 1:

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will help to remove any buildup or dirt from your hair, which can make it easier to braid.

Step 2:

Detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers. This will help to prevent any tangles or knots from forming when you braid your hair.

Step 3:

Part your hair into sections using a comb. The size of the sections will depend on how thick your hair is and how many locs you have.

Step 4:

Take one section of hair and divide it into three equal parts.

Step 5:

Begin braiding your hair, starting at the root and working your way down to the ends. Make sure to braid loosely and avoid pulling on your hair.

Step 6:

Secure the end of your braid with an elastic band or hair tie.

Step 7:

Repeat steps 4-6 for each section of hair.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when braiding your starter locs:

1. Protect your hair at night

When you braid your hair, it’s important to protect your hair at night to prevent breakage and damage. Consider wrapping your hair in a silk or satin scarf or sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase.

2. Avoid using heat

If you plan on using heat styling tools on your hair, make sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage. However, it’s best to avoid using heat styling tools altogether, as they can be damaging to your hair.

3. Be patient

Remember that the loc journey is a marathon, not a sprint. It takes time and patience to grow healthy, strong locs. Be gentle with your hair and give it the time and care it needs to grow and mature.

FAQ

Q: Can I braid my starter locs if they are thin?

A: Yes, you can braid your starter locs even if they are thin. However, it’s important to be gentle and avoid pulling on your hair, as this can cause breakage and damage.

Q: How often should I wash my hair if I braid my starter locs?

A: It’s best to wash your hair once a week if you have braids. This will help to remove any buildup or dirt from your hair, which can prevent breakage and damage.

Q: How long should I wait before braiding my starter locs?

A: It’s best to wait until your starter locs are fully formed before braiding your hair. This can take anywhere from a few months to a year or more, depending on your hair type and how you care for your hair.

Hair Care

Overall, it’s ok to braid your starter locs as long as you do it in a way that is gentle and doesn’t cause damage to your hair. Remember to be patient with your hair, take the time to care for it properly, and avoid any styling techniques that can be damaging. With the right care and attention, you can grow healthy, strong locs that will last a lifetime.
