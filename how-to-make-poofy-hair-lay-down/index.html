How To Make Poofy Hair Lay Down A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 27, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have poofy or frizzy hair, you know how frustrating it can be to get it to lay down. Whether you're getting ready for a big event or just want to look put-together for everyday life, taming your hair can feel like an uphill battle. But don't worry – with a few simple tips and tricks, you can get your hair to lay down and stay that way. In this article, we'll walk you through the process step by step.

Description

Poofy hair can be caused by a number of factors, including humidity, dryness, and genetics. Whatever the cause, it can make styling your hair a challenge. In this article, we'll focus on how to make poofy hair lay down using a variety of techniques, including hair care tips, styling products, and heat styling tools.

How to Make Poofy Hair Lay Down: Step by Step

Step 1: Start with Clean, Conditioned Hair

The first step to getting your poofy hair to lay down is to start with clean, conditioned hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type, whether that's curly, wavy, or straight. Make sure to rinse your hair thoroughly to avoid any product buildup that could weigh your hair down.

Step 2: Apply a Smoothing Product

After washing and conditioning your hair, apply a smoothing product to help control frizz and add shine. Look for products that contain ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, or shea butter, which can help hydrate and smooth your hair. Apply the product evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Next, it's time to blow dry your hair. Use a round brush to help smooth your hair as you dry it. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends, using the brush to pull your hair straight as you go. Use the cool setting on your blow dryer to set your style and help reduce frizz.

Step 4: Use a Flat Iron

If your hair is still poofy after blow drying, you can use a flat iron to help smooth it out. Make sure to use a heat protectant product to avoid damaging your hair. Section your hair and run the flat iron over each section, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Be careful not to hold the flat iron in one spot for too long, as this can cause heat damage.

Step 5: Finish with a Holding Product

To help your hair stay in place, finish your styling routine with a holding product like hairspray or gel. Choose a product that provides a strong hold without making your hair feel crunchy or stiff. Apply the product evenly throughout your hair, using your fingers or a comb to distribute it evenly.

Tips for Making Poofy Hair Lay Down

Here are a few additional tips to help you get your poofy hair to lay down:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage and make your hair more prone to frizz.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.
  • Consider getting a haircut with layers to help reduce volume and make your hair easier to style.

FAQ

Why is my hair so poofy?

Poofy hair can be caused by a number of factors, including humidity, dryness, and genetics. If you're not sure what's causing your poofy hair, talk to your hairstylist or a dermatologist for advice.

Can I make my hair lay down without heat styling?

Yes! While heat styling can be helpful for making poofy hair lay down, there are other methods you can try. For example, you can try using a silk or satin pillowcase to help reduce frizz, or you can braid your hair at night to help it lay down in the morning.

Hair Care for Poofy Hair

If you have poofy hair, it's important to take good care of it to help reduce frizz and make it easier to style. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help hydrate your hair
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and flat irons too often, as this can damage your hair
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf

By following these tips and using the techniques we've outlined in this article, you can make your poofy hair lay down and look sleek and polished. Whether you're getting ready for a big event or just want to look your best for everyday life, you can achieve the style you want with a little effort and the right products and tools.
