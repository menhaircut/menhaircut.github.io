How To Blow Dry Hair For Less Volume A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Blow drying your hair is a quick and easy way to style your hair, but sometimes it can leave your hair looking voluminous and frizzy. If you're looking for a way to blow dry your hair for less volume, then you've come to the right place. In this article, we will cover everything you need to know about how to blow dry your hair for less volume. From the tools you need to the techniques you should use, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You Need
  • Washing Your Hair
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Blow Drying Techniques
  • Cool Shot

  • FAQs

Tools You Need

The first step to blow drying your hair for less volume is to make sure you have the right tools. Here are the tools you need:

  • Blow dryer with a concentrator nozzle
  • Paddle brush or round brush
  • Hair clips
  • Hair serum or oil

Make sure you have all these tools before you start blow drying your hair.

Washing Your Hair

The next step is to wash your hair. Make sure you use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner if you want to reduce the volume of your hair. After washing, gently towel dry your hair until it is slightly damp.

Prepping Your Hair

Before you start blow drying your hair, you need to prep your hair. Apply a small amount of hair serum or oil to your hair to protect it from heat damage. Then, divide your hair into sections using hair clips.

Blow Drying Techniques

Now it's time to start blow drying your hair. Here are the techniques you can use to reduce the volume:

1. Use a Concentrator Nozzle

Attach the concentrator nozzle to your blow dryer to focus the air flow on a specific section of your hair. This will help you to control the direction of your hair and reduce volume.

2. Use a Paddle Brush or Round Brush

Using a paddle brush or round brush, start blow drying your hair by pulling your hair downwards. This will help to straighten your hair and reduce the volume. Make sure you keep the brush close to your hair to avoid frizz.

3. Use a Low Heat Setting

Using a low heat setting on your blow dryer can help to reduce the volume of your hair. High heat can cause your hair to become frizzy and voluminous.

Cool Shot

Once you have blow dried your hair, use the cool shot button on your blow dryer to cool down your hair. This will help to set your hair and reduce any remaining volume.

Finishing Touches

Once your hair is completely dry, you can add some finishing touches to reduce the volume. Apply a small amount of hair serum or oil to your hair to smooth out any frizz. You can also use a hairspray to keep your hair in place.

Tips for Success

Here are some tips to help you blow dry your hair for less volume:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to reduce the volume of your hair
  • Divide your hair into sections to make it easier to blow dry
  • Use a low heat setting on your blow dryer to avoid frizz
  • Use a cool shot button to set your hair and reduce volume
  • Apply hair serum or oil to protect your hair from heat damage
  • Use a hairspray to keep your hair in place

FAQs

1. How often should I blow dry my hair?

It is recommended that you blow dry your hair no more than twice a week to avoid heat damage. You can air dry your hair on the other days.

2. Can I blow dry my hair without a concentrator nozzle?

Yes, you can blow dry your hair without a concentrator nozzle, but it may be harder to control the direction of your hair and reduce volume.

Hair Care Tips

Blow drying your hair can cause heat damage, so it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before blow drying your hair
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Avoid using hot tools on a daily basis

Now that you know how to blow dry your hair for less volume, you can enjoy smooth and sleek hair every day. Remember to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and shiny.
