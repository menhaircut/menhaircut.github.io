How To Cut Hair So It Feathers Back Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 11, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you looking for an easy way to add some texture and movement to your hair? Feathering is a popular technique that can give your hair a light, airy look. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut hair so it feathers back in a few simple steps.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Hair so it Feathers Back
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Feathering Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Feathered Hair

How to Cut Hair so it Feathers Back

Feathering is a technique where the ends of the hair are thinned out and tapered to create a light, airy look. It's a great way to add texture and movement to your hair without sacrificing length. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow dry it. You want your hair to be clean and free of any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, divide your hair into sections. Use hair clips to separate the top section of your hair from the bottom section.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start with the bottom section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use scissors to cut the hair at a 45-degree angle, starting from the middle of the hair shaft and moving towards the ends. This will create a feathered effect.

Repeat this process on all sections of your hair, working your way up to the top section.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've finished cutting all sections of your hair, use a comb to blend the layers together. This will create a seamless, feathered look.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Finally, style your hair as desired. Feathered hair looks great when it's styled with loose waves or curls.

Tips for Feathering Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect feathered look:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Work with small sections of hair to ensure even feathering.
  • Start with a small amount of hair and cut more if needed. You can always go back and cut more later.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once to avoid any mistakes.

FAQs

Can you feather short hair?

Yes, you can feather short hair. Just make sure to work with smaller sections of hair and cut at a 45-degree angle to create a feathered effect.

How often should you get your hair feathered?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the feathered effect grows out. Generally, getting your hair feathered every 6-8 weeks is a good rule of thumb.

Can you feather curly hair?

Yes, you can feather curly hair. Just be careful not to thin out the curls too much, as this can cause frizz.

Hair Care for Feathered Hair

Feathered hair requires some extra care to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair to prevent damage.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the feathered effect.

With these tips and techniques, you can easily cut your hair so it feathers back. Whether you have long or short hair, feathering is a great way to add texture and movement to your hair. Give it a try and see how it can transform your look!
