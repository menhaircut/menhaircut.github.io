How To Cut Short Curly Hair With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 02, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have short curly hair, you know the struggle of finding the perfect haircut. With its unique texture and shape, it can be challenging to find a style that works for you. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we’ll show you how to cut short curly hair with clippers, step-by-step.

Description

Many people with short curly hair prefer to use clippers rather than scissors. Clippers allow you to achieve a more uniform cut, which can be challenging with curly hair. Additionally, they are faster and more efficient, making them a great option for those who want to save time.

How to Cut Short Curly Hair with Clippers

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need:

  • A set of clippers with various attachments
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • Scissors (optional)

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before you start cutting. Wet hair can be more challenging to cut, and it can also change the way your hair looks once it dries.

Step 3: Choose Your Clipper Attachment

The attachment you choose will depend on the length you want your hair to be. For shorter hair, start with a shorter attachment and work your way up. It’s always better to start with a longer attachment and work your way down if needed.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start at the back of your head and work your way up. Use the clipper to cut small sections of hair, moving the clipper against the direction of hair growth. Continue until you have cut all the hair on the back of your head. Make sure to blend the hair on the sides and back for a more uniform look.

Step 5: Move to the Sides

Use the same technique to cut the hair on the sides of your head. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using the clipper to cut small sections of hair. Make sure to blend the hair on the sides with the hair on the back for a more natural look.

Step 6: Trim the Top

Once you have cut the sides and back, use scissors to trim the hair on the top of your head. You can either cut the hair straight across or use a texturizing technique to achieve a more layered look.

Step 7: Check the Length

Check the length of your hair in the mirror to make sure it’s even. If you notice any areas that are longer or shorter than others, go back and trim them.

Step 8: Clean Up

Once you’re done cutting, use a comb to remove any loose hairs from your head and neck. You can also use a brush to style your hair if desired.

Tips

  • Start with a longer attachment and work your way down if needed
  • Use scissors to trim the hair on the top of your head
  • Blend the hair on the sides and back for a more natural look
  • Check the length of your hair in the mirror to make sure it’s even

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair with clippers?

Yes, you can cut your own hair with clippers. However, it may be easier to have someone else do it for you, especially if you’re not comfortable using clippers.

What’s the best way to clean my clippers?

To clean your clippers, remove the blades and use a small brush to remove any hair or debris. You can also use a disinfectant spray or wipe to sanitize the blades.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it’s essential to take good care of it to maintain its shape and texture. Use a shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for curly hair to keep it moisturized and healthy. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or styling product to enhance your curls.

Cutting short curly hair with clippers may seem intimidating at first, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut from the comfort of your own home. Follow these steps, tips, and hair care advice to achieve a look you’ll love.
