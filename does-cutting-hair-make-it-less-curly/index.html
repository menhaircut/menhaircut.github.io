Does Cutting Hair Make It Less Curly

Written by Aprilia Jul 21, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have curly hair, you may have heard that cutting it can make it less curly. But is this really true? In this article, we’ll explore this question and provide you with all the information you need to know about cutting curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

Description

Curly hair tends to be more fragile than straight hair, and it can be difficult to manage. Many people with curly hair believe that cutting it will make it less curly, but this is not entirely true. While cutting your hair can change its shape and appearance, it won't change its texture or curl pattern.

If you have curly hair, it's important to understand that the way you cut it can make a big difference in how it looks and feels. A good haircut can enhance your curls and make them more defined, while a bad haircut can leave you with frizz and undefined curls.

How to Cut Curly Hair

Cutting curly hair is different from cutting straight hair. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair before cutting it.
  • Cut your hair when it's dry, not wet. This will help you see the shape of your curls better.
  • Avoid using thinning shears, as they can damage your hair and make it look frizzy.
  • Consider getting a layered haircut, as this can help enhance your curls and give your hair more volume.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to cutting curly hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as you normally would.
  2. Detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb.
  3. Divide your hair into sections using hair clips.
  4. Cut your hair section by section, starting from the bottom and working your way up.
  5. Use sharp scissors to cut your hair, making sure to cut at an angle to create layers.
  6. Once you've finished cutting your hair, rinse it with cold water to help seal the cuticles and prevent frizz.
  7. Style your hair as you normally would.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting curly hair:

  • Don't cut your hair too short, as this can make it harder to manage and style.
  • Consider getting a curly hair specialist to cut your hair, as they will have experience working with curly hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair, as this can damage your curls and make them look frizzy.

FAQs

Q: Will cutting my hair make it less curly?

A: Cutting your hair won't change its texture or curl pattern. However, a good haircut can enhance your curls and make them more defined.

Q: Should I cut my hair when it's wet or dry?

A: It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry, as this will help you see the shape of your curls better.

Q: Can I use thinning shears on my curly hair?

A: It's best to avoid using thinning shears on curly hair, as they can damage your hair and make it look frizzy.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

If you have curly hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a conditioner specifically designed for curly hair to help keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to help enhance your curls.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair, as this can damage your curls.

In conclusion, cutting your hair won't make it less curly, but it can change its shape and appearance. If you have curly hair, it's important to find a good stylist who understands how to cut and style curly hair. With the right care and maintenance, you can keep your curly hair healthy, bouncy, and beautiful.
