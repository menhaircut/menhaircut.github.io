How To Use Thinning Scissors Fringe A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Dec 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to add some texture to your fringe without sacrificing length, thinning scissors are the perfect tool. Thinning scissors are designed to remove bulk from the hair without creating a blunt cut. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to use thinning scissors fringe in a few simple steps.

Description

Thinning scissors, also known as thinning shears, are a type of hair cutting tool that have teeth on one or both blades. These teeth help to remove bulk from the hair without creating a blunt cut. Thinning scissors are commonly used on curly, thick, or unruly hair to help create a more textured look.

How to Use Thinning Scissors Fringe: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the tools you need. You'll need a pair of thinning scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off the hair.

Step 2: Section Off Your Hair

Start by sectioning off your fringe. Use hair clips to secure the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Comb Your Fringe

Comb your fringe straight down over your forehead. Make sure it's smooth and free of tangles.

Step 4: Use the Thinning Scissors

Starting at the outer edge of your fringe, use the thinning scissors to cut into the hair at a diagonal angle. Take small sections of hair at a time and work your way across your fringe. Make sure to use the thinning scissors on both sides of your fringe to create an even look.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

As you cut, periodically stop and check your progress in the mirror. Make sure the texture looks even and that you're not removing too much hair.

Step 6: Blend Your Fringe

Once you've finished cutting your fringe with the thinning scissors, use your regular scissors to blend any harsh lines. Take your time and make sure your fringe looks natural and even.

Tips for Using Thinning Scissors Fringe

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when using thinning scissors on your fringe:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up. It's easier to remove more hair than to try to add it back.
  • Take breaks and periodically check your progress in the mirror. This will help you avoid removing too much hair.
  • Be gentle with the thinning scissors. They're designed to remove hair without creating a blunt cut, but they can still damage your hair if you're too rough.
  • Practice makes perfect. If you're new to using thinning scissors, start by practicing on a small section of hair before moving on to your entire fringe.

FAQs About Thinning Scissors Fringe

How often should I use thinning scissors on my fringe?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it starts to look bulky. As a general rule, you should use thinning scissors on your fringe every 6-8 weeks.

Can I use thinning scissors on wet hair?

It's best to use thinning scissors on dry hair. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut and may not give you the desired texture.

Are thinning scissors better than regular scissors for cutting fringe?

It depends on what you're trying to achieve. Thinning scissors are great for creating a textured look, but they may not be the best option if you're looking for a blunt cut. Regular scissors are better for creating a clean, even line.

Hair Care Tips for Fringe

Here are a few hair care tips to keep your fringe looking its best:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep your fringe clean and free of oil.
  • Blow dry your fringe straight to help it lay flat.
  • Avoid using too much product on your fringe, as this can weigh it down and make it look greasy.
  • If you have bangs that tend to get oily, consider using a dry shampoo to absorb excess oil.

Using thinning scissors on your fringe can be a great way to add texture and dimension to your hair. By following these simple steps and tips, you'll be able to create a beautiful, natural-looking fringe that complements your face shape and personal style.
