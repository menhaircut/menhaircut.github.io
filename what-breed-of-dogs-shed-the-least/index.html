What Breed Of Dogs Shed The Least A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Sep 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're someone who loves dogs but hates the incessant shedding that comes with them, you're not alone. Shedding can be a real nuisance, causing allergies, messy homes and endless cleaning. But don't worry, there are breeds of dogs that shed the least. In this article, we'll guide you through the dog breeds that are known for their minimal shedding and how to take care of their fur.

What Breed of Dogs Shed the Least?

Before we dive into the list of breeds that shed the least, it's important to note that all dogs shed to some extent. However, some breeds have a lot less hair than others, which means less shedding. Here are some dog breeds that are known for shedding the least:

  • Poodle
  • Bichon Frise
  • Maltese
  • Shih Tzu
  • Italian Greyhound
  • Whippet
  • Basenji

Poodle

One of the most popular breeds for people with allergies, poodles don't shed much at all. They have curly, hypoallergenic coats that require regular grooming to prevent matting. Poodles come in different sizes, including standard, miniature and toy.

Bichon Frise

The Bichon Frise has a soft, curly coat that doesn't shed much. They require regular grooming to keep their coat in good condition. Bichon Frises are known for being playful, affectionate and great with children.

Maltese

Maltese dogs have a long, silky coat that doesn't shed much. They require regular grooming to keep their coat looking good. Maltese dogs are known for being friendly, gentle and great with children.

Shih Tzu

Shih Tzus have a long, straight coat that doesn't shed much. They require regular grooming to prevent matting. Shih Tzus are known for being loyal, affectionate and great with families.

Italian Greyhound

Italian Greyhounds have a short, smooth coat that doesn't shed much. They don't require much grooming, but their coat needs to be protected in cold weather. Italian Greyhounds are known for being affectionate, intelligent and good with children.

Whippet

The Whippet has a short, smooth coat that doesn't shed much. They don't require much grooming, but their coat does need to be protected in cold weather. Whippets are known for being gentle, affectionate and great with children.

Basenji

The Basenji has a short, fine coat that doesn't shed much. They don't require much grooming, but their coat should be brushed weekly. Basenjis are known for being independent, intelligent and great with families.

How to Take Care of Low-Shedding Dog Breeds

Low-shedding dogs require regular grooming to keep their coats in good condition. Here are some tips:

Brushing

Regular brushing is important to prevent matting and to remove loose hair. Depending on the breed, you may need to brush your dog daily or weekly.

Bathing

Low-shedding dogs don't need to be bathed often, but when they do, it's important to use a gentle dog shampoo. Bathing too often can strip their coat of its natural oils.

Trimming

Some low-shedding breeds, like the Poodle, require regular trimming to keep their coats in good condition. You can do this at home with clippers or take your dog to a professional groomer.

Protecting Their Coat

Low-shedding dogs are more sensitive to cold weather, so it's important to protect their coat with a coat or sweater when it's chilly outside.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do low-shedding dogs cause allergies?

While low-shedding dogs are less likely to cause allergies, they can still trigger allergies in some people. It's important to spend time with a dog before adopting to see if you're allergic.

Do low-shedding dogs need less exercise?

No, low-shedding dogs still need regular exercise to stay healthy and happy.

Hair Care for Low-Shedding Dogs

Here are some products that can help keep your low-shedding dog's coat in good condition:

  • Brushes
  • Combs
  • Clippers
  • Gentle dog shampoo
  • Conditioner
  • Coats and sweaters for cold weather

Now that you know which dog breeds shed the least and how to take care of their coats, you can make an informed decision when choosing a dog that's right for you. Remember, no dog is completely hypoallergenic, but low-shedding breeds are a great option for people with allergies or those who want a cleaner home.
