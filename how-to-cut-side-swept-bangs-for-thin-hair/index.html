How To Cut Side Swept Bangs For Thin Hair

Written by Luffy Oct 15, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Do you have thin hair and want to try out side swept bangs? This style can add a touch of glamour and sophistication to any hairstyle, but it can be tricky to achieve the perfect cut. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of cutting side swept bangs for thin hair. From preparation to finishing touches, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • Preparation
  • Sectioning the Hair
  • Cutting the Bangs
  • Blending and Styling
  • Tips for Cutting Side Swept Bangs for Thin Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Thin Hair with Bangs

Preparation

Before you start cutting your bangs, you need to have the right tools and products. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips or hair ties
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Hair styling products (optional)

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before you start cutting. It's also a good idea to style your hair the way you normally wear it, so you can see how the bangs will look with your everyday hairstyle.

Sectioning the Hair

To start cutting your side swept bangs, you need to section off the hair you want to cut. Here's how:

  1. Start by dividing your hair into three sections: left, right, and center.
  2. Take the center section and comb it straight down in front of your face.
  3. Decide how wide you want your bangs to be and use your comb to make a horizontal parting at that point.
  4. Clip or tie back the rest of your hair to keep it out of the way.

Make sure the parting is straight and even, as this will affect the final look of your bangs.

Cutting the Bangs

Now it's time to cut the bangs. Take the following steps:

  1. Hold the hair between your fingers at the point where you want to start cutting.
  2. Using your scissors, make a small cut into the hair. This will be your guide for the rest of the cut.
  3. Using the guide, cut the hair in a diagonal line towards the opposite side of your face. Keep the scissors at an angle to create the side swept effect.
  4. As you cut, make small snips rather than one big cut. This will give you more control and prevent you from accidentally cutting too much hair.
  5. Repeat steps 1-4 on the other side of your face, making sure the length and angle of the bangs match.

Remember that it's better to cut too little hair than too much. You can always go back and trim more if needed.

Blending and Styling

Once you've cut the bangs, it's time to blend them in with the rest of your hair. Here's how:

  1. Remove the clips or ties from the rest of your hair.
  2. Take a section of hair from the top of your head and backcomb it slightly. This will create volume and help the bangs blend in with the rest of your hair.
  3. If you want to add more volume, use a volumizing product and blow dry your hair with a round brush.
  4. Style your bangs as desired. You can wear them straight, curled, or swept to the side.

Make sure to blend the bangs in with your hairstyle, rather than having them look like a separate section of hair.

Tips for Cutting Side Swept Bangs for Thin Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting side swept bangs for thin hair:

  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to make small snips and check your progress frequently.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Consider getting your bangs cut by a professional if you're not confident in your skills.
  • Experiment with different styles and lengths to find what works best for you.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting side swept bangs for thin hair:

  • Will side swept bangs make my hair look thinner? Side swept bangs can actually make your hair look thicker, especially if you add volume and texture.
  • How often should I trim my bangs? It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs start to look overgrown. As a general rule, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks.
  • Can I still wear my hair in a ponytail with side swept bangs? Yes, you can still wear your hair in a ponytail with side swept bangs. Simply pull back the rest of your hair and secure it with a hair tie, leaving the bangs to frame your face.

Hair Care for Thin Hair with Bangs

If you have thin hair with bangs, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body to your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Blow dry your hair upside down to add volume at the roots.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut side swept bangs for thin hair and rock this stylish look. Happy cutting!
