How To Cut My Own Hair High And Tight Step By Step Guide

If you're looking to cut your own hair high and tight, you've come to the right place. This style has been popular for decades and is a great option for those who want a low-maintenance, military-inspired look. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of cutting your own hair high and tight, step-by-step. We'll also provide some tips and answer some frequently asked questions.

How to Cut My Own Hair High and Tight

Before we get started, it's important to note that cutting your own hair can be difficult and may take some practice. If you're not confident in your abilities, it may be best to visit a professional stylist. However, if you're up for the challenge, here's how to cut your own hair high and tight.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair. Use clippers with a #1 guard to buzz the hair on the sides and back of your head. Start from the bottom and work your way up, using short, upward strokes. Be sure to blend the hair on the sides and back with the hair on top.

Step 2: Switch to a #2 guard and clip the hair on the top of your head. Again, start from the back and work your way forward, using short, upward strokes. Be sure to blend the hair on top with the hair on the sides and back.

Step 3: Use scissors to trim the hair on top of your head. Pull small sections of hair straight up and trim the ends with the scissors. Be sure to cut the hair evenly and avoid cutting too much off.

Step 4: Use the clippers to clean up the hairline around your ears and neck. Use a #0 guard for a closer cut.

Step 5: Use a comb to check for any uneven spots and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair High and Tight

Tip 1: Invest in a good pair of clippers. Cheap clippers can be difficult to use and may not cut evenly.

Tip 2: Take your time. Cutting your own hair can be stressful, but rushing can lead to mistakes.

Tip 3: Use a mirror. It can be difficult to see the back of your head, so use a mirror to check for any spots you may have missed.

Tip 4: Practice makes perfect. Don't be discouraged if your first attempt doesn't turn out perfectly. Keep practicing and you'll get better.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut my own hair high and tight with scissors?

A: While it's possible to cut your own hair high and tight with scissors, it's much easier to use clippers. Clippers allow for a closer, more even cut.

Q: Can I use any guard size?

A: For a high and tight haircut, it's best to use a #1 or #2 guard on the sides and back. Use a #0 guard for a closer cut around the hairline.

Hair Care for High and Tight Haircuts

Now that you've successfully cut your own hair high and tight, it's important to take care of your new haircut. Here are some tips:

Tip 1: Wash your hair regularly. High and tight haircuts can show dirt and oil easily.

Tip 2: Use a styling product, such as pomade or wax, to keep your hair in place.

Tip 3: Get regular trims. Even though high and tight haircuts are low-maintenance, they still require upkeep.

With this step-by-step guide, tips, and FAQs, you should be able to cut your own hair high and tight with confidence. Remember to take your time, invest in good clippers, and practice regularly. Good luck!
