What Medications Can Cause Hair Loss And Thinning

Written by Christine Nov 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you noticing more hair in your brush or on your pillow? Losing hair can be a distressing experience, but it can be even more concerning when it's a side effect of a medication you're taking. In this article, we'll take a closer look at what medications can cause hair loss and thinning, and what you can do about it.

Table of Contents

  • Medications That Can Cause Hair Loss and Thinning
  • How to Prevent Hair Loss While Taking Medications
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Hair Care While Taking Medications

Medications That Can Cause Hair Loss and Thinning

There are many different medications that can cause hair loss or thinning, including:

  • Antidepressants
  • Blood thinners
  • Chemotherapy drugs
  • Hormonal contraceptives
  • Immunosuppressants
  • Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs)
  • Statins
  • Thyroid medications

If you're experiencing hair loss or thinning, it's important to talk to your doctor to determine if your medication is the cause. In some cases, your doctor may be able to switch you to a different medication that does not have this side effect.

How to Prevent Hair Loss While Taking Medications

If you're taking a medication that can cause hair loss or thinning, there are steps you can take to minimize the impact:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair
  • Take a biotin supplement
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet

It's important to note that these steps may not completely prevent hair loss, but they can help to minimize it.

Step-by-Step Guide to Hair Care While Taking Medications

If you're experiencing hair loss or thinning due to medication, you may need to adjust your hair care routine to help protect your hair. Here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Start with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  3. Avoid using heat styling tools, or use them on the lowest heat setting.
  4. Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, like ponytails or braids.
  5. If you must use heat styling tools or tight hairstyles, use a heat protectant or hair serum to help protect your hair.
  6. Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to help nourish your hair.
  7. Take a biotin supplement to help support healthy hair growth.
  8. Eat a healthy, balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals.
  9. If your hair loss is severe, consider talking to your doctor about prescription hair loss treatments, like minoxidil.

Tips for Managing Hair Loss and Thinning

Here are a few additional tips to help you manage hair loss and thinning:

  • Use volumizing products, like a volumizing shampoo or mousse, to help create the appearance of fuller hair.
  • Consider getting a shorter haircut to help reduce the appearance of thinning hair.
  • Wear a hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun.
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair to avoid pulling out more hair.

FAQs about Medications and Hair Loss

Can hair loss from medication be permanent?

In most cases, hair loss from medication is temporary and will grow back once you stop taking the medication or switch to a different one. However, in rare cases, hair loss can be permanent.

Can hair loss from medication be prevented?

While it may not be possible to completely prevent hair loss from medication, there are steps you can take to minimize the impact, such as using gentle hair care products and avoiding heat styling tools.

What should I do if I'm experiencing hair loss from medication?

If you're experiencing hair loss or thinning, it's important to talk to your doctor to determine if your medication is the cause. Your doctor may be able to switch you to a different medication that does not have this side effect, or recommend hair loss treatments like minoxidil.

Remember, hair loss can be a distressing experience, but there are steps you can take to help manage it. By using gentle hair care products, eating a healthy diet, and taking a biotin supplement, you can help support healthy hair growth and minimize the impact of medication-related hair loss.
