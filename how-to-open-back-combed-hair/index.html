How To Open Back Combed Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have ever back combed your hair, you know how difficult it can be to undo the tangles and knots. Backcombing, also known as teasing, involves combing hair backwards towards the scalp to create volume and texture. While it may look great, undoing backcombed hair can be a nightmare. However, with the right techniques and tools, you can easily open up your backcombed hair without damaging it. In this post, we will show you how to open back combed hair in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

Description

Backcombing is a popular hairstyling technique used to create voluminous and textured hairstyles. It involves teasing sections of hair backwards towards the scalp using a fine-toothed comb. This process creates knots and tangles in the hair, which gives it more volume and texture. While backcombing can be a great way to add some oomph to your hair, it can also cause damage if not done properly. Furthermore, opening up backcombed hair can be a real challenge, especially if you don't know the right techniques.

How to Open Back Combed Hair

The key to opening up backcombed hair is patience and the right tools. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to do it:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

The first thing you need to do is prep your hair for the process. Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup. Then, apply a generous amount of conditioner to your hair and leave it on for a few minutes. This will help soften your hair and make it easier to detangle.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Once your hair is fully conditioned, use a wide-toothed comb to detangle it. Start from the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots. Be gentle and patient, as you don't want to cause any more tangles or knots.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into manageable parts. Use hair clips to secure each section and avoid tangling and knotting them again.

Step 4: Comb Your Hair

Using a fine-toothed comb, gently comb each section of your hair in the opposite direction of the backcombing. Start from the ends and work your way up to the roots. Be patient and take your time, as this process can take a while.

Step 5: Use Your Fingers

If you encounter any stubborn knots or tangles, use your fingers to gently loosen them. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause damage.

Step 6: Rinse Your Hair

Once you have combed through each section, rinse your hair with cool water to remove any remaining conditioner. Be sure to use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair once more.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you open up backcombed hair:

  • Use a detangling spray or serum to make it easier to comb through your hair.
  • Be patient and gentle when combing out tangles and knots.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as blow dryers and flat irons until your hair is fully detangled.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner to help prevent future tangles and knots.

FAQ

Can I use a regular comb to open up backcombed hair?

No, it's best to use a fine-toothed comb to avoid causing more tangles and knots. A regular comb can make the process more difficult.

Will opening up backcombed hair damage my hair?

No, as long as you are patient and gentle, opening up backcombed hair should not cause any damage.

How often can I backcomb my hair?

It's best to limit backcombing to once or twice a week to avoid damaging your hair.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential to maintaining healthy and beautiful hair. Here are some tips to keep your hair in top condition:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and health.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a protective spray.

With these tips and techniques, you can easily open up backcombed hair without damaging it. Remember to be patient and gentle, and your hair will thank you for it!
