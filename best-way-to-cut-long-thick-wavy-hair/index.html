Best Way To Cut Long Thick Wavy Hair A Complete Guide

Cutting long thick wavy hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're not a professional hairdresser. But with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a stunning haircut that suits your face shape and brings out your natural waves. In this blog post, we'll show you the best way to cut long thick wavy hair, step by step, with tips and FAQ for beginners. So, let's get started!

Description

If you have long thick wavy hair, you know how challenging it can be to keep it looking healthy and stylish. The key to a great haircut is to work with your natural waves and texture, rather than against it. Cutting long thick wavy hair requires patience, precision, and the right tools, such as sharp scissors, sectioning clips, and a comb.

How to Cut Long Thick Wavy Hair

The best way to cut long thick wavy hair is to start with clean, dry hair and work in small sections. Use a comb to separate each section and clip the rest of the hair out of the way. Cut each section to the desired length, taking care not to cut too much at once. Use the comb to blend the cut sections together for a seamless look.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step by step guide on how to cut long thick wavy hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Brush your hair to remove any tangles.
  3. Section your hair into manageable sections using sectioning clips.
  4. Start cutting the hair at the back of your head, moving forward.
  5. Cut small sections of hair at a time, starting with the bottom layer.
  6. Use your fingers to hold the hair in place and cut the hair to the desired length.
  7. Repeat the process with each section of hair, working your way up to the top layer.
  8. Check the length of your hair in the mirror and make any necessary adjustments.
  9. Blend the sections of hair together using a comb for a seamless finish.
  10. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Long Thick Wavy Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting long thick wavy hair:

  • Invest in a good pair of scissors that are sharp and easy to handle.
  • Work in a well-lit area with a large mirror to see what you're doing.
  • Use sectioning clips to keep the rest of your hair out of the way.
  • Cut small sections of hair at a time to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Take breaks if your arms get tired or you need to rest.
  • Don't be afraid to seek professional help if you're unsure about cutting your own hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting long thick wavy hair:

Can I cut my own long thick wavy hair?

Yes, you can cut your own long thick wavy hair, but it requires patience and practice. Start with small sections and work your way up to the top layer.

Do I need to use special scissors to cut long thick wavy hair?

Yes, it's best to use sharp, high-quality scissors that are designed for cutting hair. Avoid using regular household scissors, as they can damage your hair and create split ends.

How often should I cut my long thick wavy hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Hair Care Tips for Long Thick Wavy Hair

Here are some hair care tips to keep your long thick wavy hair healthy and shiny:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as it can cause breakage and damage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to your hair to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and make it prone to breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Cutting long thick wavy hair can be a fun and rewarding experience, as long as you take your time and follow the right techniques. With these tips and tricks, you can achieve a stunning haircut that makes the most of your natural waves and texture. Good luck!
