How To Hide Thinning Hair Back Of Head

Written by Aprilia Jun 09, 2023 · 4 min read

Thinning hair can be a source of insecurity for many people. If you're experiencing hair loss at the back of your head, it can be particularly challenging to hide. But don't worry, there are several ways to conceal thinning hair and boost your confidence.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Hide Thinning Hair Back of Head
  2. Step by Step Guide
  3. Tips to Conceal Thinning Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care Tips to Prevent Thinning Hair

How to Hide Thinning Hair Back of Head

Here are some ways to hide thinning hair at the back of your head:

1. Use Hair Fibers

Hair fibers are a quick and easy way to conceal thinning hair. They are made of natural keratin fibers that blend with your hair and fill in the gaps, making your hair appear fuller. Simply sprinkle the fibers onto your hair and pat gently to distribute them evenly.

2. Try Hair Extensions

Hair extensions are a popular way to add volume and length to your hair. You can choose from clip-in, tape-in, or sew-in extensions, depending on your preference. Clip-in extensions are the easiest to use and can be attached to your hair in minutes.

3. Get a Hairpiece

A hairpiece, also known as a toupee, is a custom-made wig that covers the thinning area at the back of your head. Hairpieces are available in a variety of colors, styles, and materials, so you can choose one that matches your hair and looks natural.

4. Wear a Hat or Scarf

A hat or scarf can be a stylish way to conceal thinning hair. Choose a hat or scarf that complements your outfit and covers the thinning area at the back of your head. This is a great option for a casual day out or a bad hair day.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to hiding thinning hair at the back of your head:

Step 1: Choose Your Method

Decide which method you want to use to conceal your thinning hair. Hair fibers, extensions, hairpieces, hats, and scarves are all great options.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before applying any products. This will ensure that the products adhere to your hair properly.

Step 3: Apply the Product

Apply the hair fibers, extensions, or hairpiece according to the manufacturer's instructions. If you're wearing a hat or scarf, position it properly over the thinning area at the back of your head.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired. You can use a comb or brush to blend the fibers or extensions with your natural hair. If you're wearing a hairpiece, style it to match your natural hair.

Tips to Conceal Thinning Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you conceal thinning hair:

1. Use a Thickening Shampoo

A thickening shampoo can help add volume to your hair and make it appear fuller. Look for shampoos that contain ingredients like biotin, keratin, and collagen.

2. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles like ponytails, braids, and buns can pull on your hair and make the thinning area more noticeable. Opt for looser hairstyles that don't put too much pressure on your hair.

3. Get a Professional Haircut

A professional haircut can help camouflage thinning hair. Ask your hairstylist for a cut that adds volume and texture to your hair.

FAQs

1. Can I use hair fibers if I have very little hair at the back of my head?

Yes, hair fibers can be used even if you have very little hair. They will cling to the existing hairs and create the illusion of fuller hair.

2. Will hair extensions damage my hair?

Clip-in extensions are safe to use and won't damage your hair if you follow the manufacturer's instructions. However, more permanent extensions like tape-in or sew-in extensions can damage your hair if they're not installed properly or if you leave them in for too long.

Hair Care Tips to Prevent Thinning Hair

Here are some tips to prevent thinning hair:

1. Eat a Balanced Diet

A balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Excessive heat styling can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Try to limit your use of blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons.

3. Use Gentle Hair Products

Avoid using harsh chemicals and sulfates in your hair products. Look for gentle, natural products that won't strip your hair of its natural oils.

By following these tips and using the methods outlined above, you can hide thinning hair at the back of your head and boost your confidence.
