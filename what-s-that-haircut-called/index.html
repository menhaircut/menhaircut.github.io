What s That Haircut Called

Written by Frank Nov 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who struggles with finding the right words to describe the haircut you want? Do you find yourself saying things like, "I want it shorter in the back and longer in the front," or "I want layers, but not too many"? If so, don't worry - you're not alone. In this post, we'll go over some common hairstyles and their names, so you can finally answer the question, "What's that haircut called?"

Description

Before we get into specific haircuts, it's important to understand some basic terminology. Here are a few terms you should know:

  • Layers: This refers to the different lengths of hair in a haircut. Layers can add texture and movement to hair.
  • Bangs: These are the pieces of hair that fall over the forehead. Bangs can be blunt or textured, and can be worn in a variety of lengths.
  • Pixie cut: This is a very short haircut, usually with layers and/or bangs.
  • Bob: A bob is a short haircut that is usually chin-length or shorter. It can be straight or layered.
  • Lob: A lob is a longer version of a bob, usually falling between the chin and shoulders.
  • Shag: A shag is a layered haircut that is often choppy and textured.

Now that you know some basic terminology, let's get into specific haircuts.

How to

When you're getting a haircut, it's important to communicate clearly with your stylist. Here are some tips for describing the haircut you want:

  • Bring pictures: If you have a specific haircut in mind, bring in pictures to show your stylist. This can help ensure you're on the same page.
  • Use descriptive words: Instead of saying "shorter in the back," say "graduated" or "stacked."
  • Be specific about length: If you want your hair a specific length, use a measuring tape to show your stylist exactly where you want it.

Step by Step

Here are some popular haircuts and how to describe them:

Pixie Cut

A pixie cut is a very short haircut, usually with layers and/or bangs. To describe a pixie cut, say you want your hair cut very short all over, with layers and/or bangs.

Bob

A bob is a short haircut that is usually chin-length or shorter. To describe a bob, say you want your hair cut to chin-length or shorter, with or without layers.

Lob

A lob is a longer version of a bob, usually falling between the chin and shoulders. To describe a lob, say you want your hair cut to shoulder-length or slightly above, with or without layers.

Shag

A shag is a layered haircut that is often choppy and textured. To describe a shag, say you want your hair cut with lots of layers and texture, with pieces cut at varying lengths.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Consider your face shape: Certain haircuts look better on certain face shapes. Ask your stylist for their opinion on what would work best for you.
  • Think about maintenance: Some haircuts require more upkeep than others. Make sure you're willing to put in the time and effort to maintain your new haircut.
  • Be open to suggestions: Your stylist may have ideas for tweaks to the haircut you want that could make it even better.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about haircuts:

How often should I get a haircut?

This depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. As a general rule, getting a trim every 6-8 weeks can help keep your hair healthy.

Can I pull off a short haircut?

Short haircuts can be flattering on a variety of face shapes and hair types. Talk to your stylist about what would work best for you.

Will my hair grow back if I don't like my haircut?

Yes, hair grows back! If you're not happy with your new haircut, talk to your stylist about what can be done to fix it. In the meantime, try out some new styling techniques to make the most of your new 'do.

Hair Care

No matter what haircut you get, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible, or use a heat protectant spray beforehand.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote healthy hair growth.

Now that you know some common haircuts and how to describe them, you can confidently ask for the haircut you want. Happy styling!
