How To Look After Short Curly Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Sep 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Short curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it's easy to style and looks great, it can also be difficult to manage and prone to frizziness. But with the right care and attention, you can keep your short curly hair looking its best. In this article, we'll take you through everything you need to know about how to look after short curly hair, from washing and conditioning to styling and maintenance.

How to Look After Short Curly Hair

The key to looking after short curly hair is to keep it moisturized, as curly hair tends to be drier than straight hair. This means washing and conditioning your hair regularly, using a deep conditioning treatment once a week, and avoiding heat styling as much as possible. Here are some more tips:

Washing and Conditioning

When washing your hair, use a sulfate-free shampoo that's specifically designed for curly hair. Sulfates can strip the hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and frizzy. Be sure to massage the shampoo into your scalp and rinse thoroughly. Follow up with a conditioner that's also designed for curly hair, and leave it in for a few minutes to help detangle your hair.

Drying

Avoid using a towel to dry your hair, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze out excess water. Don't rub your hair vigorously, as this can cause damage. Let your hair air dry if possible, or use a diffuser if you need to speed up the drying process.

Styling

When it comes to styling short curly hair, less is more. Avoid using too many products, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy. Instead, try using a lightweight curl defining cream or gel to enhance your curls. Use your fingers to scrunch your hair as it dries, and avoid touching it too much once it's dry.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to looking after short curly hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo designed for curly hair.
  2. Apply a conditioner designed for curly hair, and leave it in for a few minutes.
  3. Gently squeeze out excess water with a microfiber towel or old t-shirt.
  4. Apply a lightweight curl defining cream or gel to your hair.
  5. Scrunch your hair with your fingers as it dries.
  6. Avoid touching your hair too much once it's dry.
  7. Avoid heat styling as much as possible.
  8. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
  9. Trim your hair regularly to keep it healthy.
  10. Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some more tips and tricks for looking after short curly hair:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair before styling.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizziness.
  • Avoid using hair elastics and clips that can cause breakage.
  • Protect your hair with a hat or scarf when it's windy or cold.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about looking after short curly hair:

How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and lifestyle, but most people with curly hair can get away with washing it once or twice a week. If your hair gets particularly oily or sweaty, you may need to wash it more often.

How do I prevent frizz?

Frizz is caused by dryness and damage, so the best way to prevent it is to keep your hair moisturized and avoid heat styling as much as possible. You can also use a leave-in conditioner and a lightweight curl defining cream or gel to help keep your curls in place.

Should I use a diffuser?

A diffuser can help to enhance your curls and speed up the drying process, but you don't need to use one if you prefer to air dry your hair. If you do use a diffuser, use it on a low heat setting and avoid touching your hair too much as it dries.
