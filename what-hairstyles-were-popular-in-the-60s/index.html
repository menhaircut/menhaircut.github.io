What Hairstyles Were Popular In The 60S

Written by Christine Oct 25, 2023 · 4 min read

The 60s was a decade of change and experimentation in fashion and hairstyles. From the iconic bouffant to the sleek bob, there were plenty of hairstyles that defined the era. In this article, we’ll take a look at the most popular hairstyles from the 60s and how you can recreate them today.

Popular Hairstyles in the 60s

The 60s was a decade of rebellion, and hairstyles were no exception. Here are some of the most popular hairstyles from the era:

The Bouffant

The bouffant was one of the most iconic hairstyles of the 60s, characterized by a large, puffy crown that was achieved by backcombing the hair. It was often worn by celebrities like Jackie Kennedy and Brigitte Bardot, and it remains a popular style today.

The Beehive

The beehive was another popular hairstyle in the 60s. It was similar to the bouffant, but with a taller crown and less volume on the sides. The style was often achieved by teasing the hair and pinning it in place.

The Pixie Cut

The pixie cut, made popular by actress Audrey Hepburn, was a short and chic hairstyle that was perfect for the mod look of the 60s. It was characterized by short, layered hair that was styled to look messy and tousled.

The Bob

The bob was a popular hairstyle in the 60s that remains a classic today. It was a short, blunt cut that was often worn straight or with a slight wave. The style was made famous by fashion icon Twiggy.

How to Recreate 60s Hairstyles

If you want to recreate a 60s hairstyle, here are some tips:

For the Bouffant

Start by backcombing the hair at the crown to create volume. Then, smooth the top layer of hair over the backcombed section and pin it in place. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

For the Beehive

Tease the hair at the crown and smooth it back, creating a cone shape. Pin the hair in place and use hairspray to hold the style in place.

For the Pixie Cut

To achieve a pixie cut, have your hair cut into short, layered layers. Style with a texturizing product to create a messy, tousled look.

For the Bob

To create a bob, have your hair cut to the desired length and style with a straightening iron or curling iron to create a slight wave. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips and FAQs

Here are some tips for maintaining your 60s hairstyle:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to help create volume and hold in the hair.
  • Try using a round brush when blow-drying your hair to create volume and lift at the roots.
  • Use a hairspray that provides hold without making the hair stiff or sticky.

Here are some frequently asked questions about 60s hairstyles:

Q: Can I recreate a 60s hairstyle on short hair?

A: Yes! Many 60s hairstyles, like the pixie cut and the bob, were short hairstyles. You can still recreate the iconic looks with shorter hair.

Q: What hair type works best for 60s hairstyles?

A: 60s hairstyles work best on hair that is thick and has some natural texture. However, with the right products and styling tools, anyone can achieve a 60s hairstyle.

Hair Care for 60s Hairstyles

To keep your 60s hairstyle looking its best, it’s important to take care of your hair. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage. Finally, be gentle when styling your hair, as rough handling can cause breakage and frizz.

With these tips and tricks, you can recreate the most iconic hairstyles from the 60s and make them your own. Whether you’re going for a mod look or a classic bob, there’s a 60s hairstyle for everyone.
