How To Look After Curly Hair Uk A Guide For Healthy Bouncy Locks

Written by Luffy Jul 09, 2023 · 4 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing or a curse, depending on how well you take care of it. In the UK, where the weather can be unpredictable, curly hair needs extra TLC to stay healthy and bouncy. In this guide, we will go over the best tips and tricks for how to look after curly hair UK. Whether you have tight curls or loose waves, this guide is for you.

Table of Contents

  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to

The first step to looking after your curly hair is to identify your hair type. This will help you choose the right products and techniques for your hair. The three main types of curly hair are:

  • Type 2: Wavy hair
  • Type 3: Curly hair
  • Type 4: Coily hair

Once you know your hair type, you can start taking steps to look after your curls. This includes:

Step by Step

  1. Start with a gentle shampoo. Look for a sulfate-free shampoo that won’t strip your hair of its natural oils.
  2. Condition well. Use a conditioner that is specifically designed for curly hair. Apply it generously and leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing.
  3. Detangle your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair while it’s still wet. Start from the ends and work your way up.
  4. Apply a styling product. This can be a curl cream, gel or mousse. Apply it to wet hair and scrunch your curls upwards to encourage definition.
  5. Air dry or diffuse. Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls gently.
  6. Refresh your curls. Use a spray bottle filled with water and a little bit of conditioner to refresh your curls on non-wash days.
  7. Sleep on silk. Silk or satin pillowcases and hair wraps can help prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.
  8. Trim regularly. Regular trims will keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for looking after your curly hair:

  • Avoid heat styling. Heat can damage curly hair, so try to avoid using hot tools as much as possible.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week. This will help keep your curls moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry. This can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use your fingers or a wide-tooth comb to detangle.
  • Protect your hair from the sun. Wear a hat or use a UV protectant spray to prevent sun damage.
  • Experiment with different styling products. What works for one person’s curls may not work for yours, so don’t be afraid to try different products and techniques.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about how to look after curly hair:

  • Can I brush my curly hair?
    It’s best to avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair while it’s wet.
  • How often should I wash my curly hair?
    This will depend on your hair type and personal preference. Some people with curly hair prefer to wash their hair every day, while others only wash it once a week. Experiment to find what works best for you.
  • What should I do if my curls are flat?
    Try using a volumizing mousse or applying your styling product to damp hair instead of wet hair. You can also try diffusing your hair upside down to encourage volume.
  • Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on my curly hair?
    It’s best to use a shampoo and conditioner that is specifically designed for curly hair. Regular shampoos can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can lead to dryness and frizz.

Hair Care

Looking after your curly hair doesn’t have to be complicated. By following these tips and using the right products, you can keep your curls healthy and bouncy. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and don’t be afraid to experiment to find what works best for you. Happy styling!
