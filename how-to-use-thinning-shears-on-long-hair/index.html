How To Use Thinning Shears On Long Hair

Written by Frank Oct 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning shears are a great tool to use on long hair. They can help to reduce hair volume, create texture, and remove bulk. If you're interested in using thinning shears on your long hair, then you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to use thinning shears on long hair, along with some useful tips and frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Thinning shears are scissors with teeth on one blade and a smooth edge on the other. They are used to create a layered effect on long hair, which helps to reduce bulk and add texture. Thinning shears are especially useful for people with thick hair who want to lighten the load without losing length.

How to

Before you start using thinning shears on your hair, it's important to make sure that your hair is clean and dry. You should also have a clear idea of the style you want to achieve. Here are the steps you can follow:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair. You can use hair clips to hold each section in place. This will make it easier for you to work on one section at a time.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Comb the section of hair you want to thin. Make sure that it's free of tangles and easy to work with.

Step 3: Hold the Thinning Shears

Hold the thinning shears in your dominant hand. Use your other hand to hold the hair you want to work on.

Step 4: Start Thinning

Start thinning your hair by opening and closing the thinning shears while moving them down the length of your hair. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once. It's better to thin gradually than to cut too much at once.

Step 5: Repeat

Repeat the process on each section of hair you want to thin. Remember to work slowly and carefully.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you use thinning shears on your long hair:

  • Start by thinning a small amount of hair. You can always thin more later if needed.
  • Hold the thinning shears at an angle to create a natural-looking effect.
  • Only use thinning shears on the ends of your hair. Avoid using them too close to the roots.
  • Work on one section of hair at a time to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Be careful not to cut yourself when using thinning shears. The teeth can be sharp.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about using thinning shears on long hair:

Can thinning shears be used on all types of hair?

Yes, thinning shears can be used on all types of hair. However, they are especially useful for people with thick hair, as they can help to reduce bulk and add texture.

How often should I use thinning shears on my hair?

This depends on your hair type and the style you want to achieve. It's best to consult with a hairstylist to determine how often you should use thinning shears on your hair.

Can thinning shears damage my hair?

Thinning shears can damage your hair if not used properly. It's important to use them carefully and slowly, and to avoid cutting too much hair at once.

Hair Care

After using thinning shears on your hair, it's important to take care of it. Here are some tips to help you care for your hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to keep it looking neat and healthy.
  • Use a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to nourish your hair.

Now that you know how to use thinning shears on long hair, you can give it a try yourself. Remember to take it slowly and carefully, and to consult with a hairstylist if you have any questions or concerns.
