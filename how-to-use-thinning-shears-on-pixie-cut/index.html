How To Use Thinning Shears On Pixie Cut A Step By Step Guide

If you have a pixie cut and want to add some texture or remove some bulk, thinning shears can be a great tool. However, using them can be tricky, especially if you're not a professional hairstylist. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to use thinning shears on pixie cut, as well as some tips and FAQs.

How to Use Thinning Shears on Pixie Cut

Thinning shears are used to remove some of the hair's bulk and add texture. They have one serrated blade and one straight blade, and they cut some of the hair while leaving some behind. This creates a softer, more gradual transition between different lengths and textures.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to use thinning shears on pixie cut:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is wet, it may appear longer than it actually is when it dries, and you may end up cutting more than you intended.
  2. Decide where you want to use the thinning shears. You can use them all over your head, or just in certain areas where you want to remove some bulk, such as the sides or the back.
  3. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight up. Hold it with your non-dominant hand.
  4. Use the thinning shears to cut the section of hair. Hold the shears vertically and make small snips along the section, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Don't cut too much at once, and be careful not to cut too close to the scalp.
  5. Release the section of hair and check the results. If you're happy with the texture and length, move on to the next section. If not, trim a little more until you achieve the desired effect.
  6. Repeat steps 3 to 5 on the rest of your hair, working in small sections and checking the results as you go.
  7. When you're done, comb your hair and style it as usual. You should notice a softer, more textured look.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Pixie Cut

Here are some tips to help you use thinning shears on pixie cut:

  • Start with a little hair and cut more if needed. You can always go back and cut more, but you can't put the hair back once it's cut.
  • Use the thinning shears sparingly. If you use them too much, your hair may end up looking frizzy or thinned out.
  • Don't cut too close to the scalp. This can create an uneven look and make your hair look choppy.
  • Use good-quality thinning shears. Cheap or dull shears may damage your hair or create an uneven look.
  • Consult a professional hairstylist if you're not sure how to use thinning shears or if you want a more drastic change to your hairstyle.

FAQs

How often should I use thinning shears on my pixie cut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it becomes too bulky. As a general rule, you can use thinning shears every 6 to 8 weeks to maintain your hairstyle.

Can I use thinning shears on wet hair?

No, it's best to use thinning shears on dry hair. Wet hair can appear longer than it actually is when it dries, and you may end up cutting more than you intended.

Can I use thinning shears on curly hair?

Yes, you can use thinning shears on curly hair to add texture and remove bulk. However, be careful not to cut too much, as curly hair tends to shrink when cut.

Hair Care After Using Thinning Shears on Pixie Cut

After using thinning shears on your pixie cut, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can dry out your hair and make it more prone to breakage.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Visit your hairstylist regularly for trims and touch-ups.

Using thinning shears on your pixie cut can be a great way to add texture and remove bulk. By following our step-by-step guide and tips, you can achieve a softer, more natural look. Just remember to use good-quality shears and consult a professional hairstylist if you're not sure how to use them.
