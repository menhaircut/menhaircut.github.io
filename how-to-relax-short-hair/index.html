How To Relax Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have short hair and want to relax it, you may be wondering where to start. Relaxing your hair can be a great way to achieve a smoother, straighter look. However, it's important to do it correctly to avoid damaging your hair. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps to relax short hair, as well as tips, FAQs, and hair care advice to help you get the most out of your new look.

Table of Contents

  • How to Relax Short Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Relaxing Short Hair
  • FAQs

How to Relax Short Hair

Relaxing short hair can be done at home or in a salon. Before you start, make sure your hair is clean and free of any products. You'll also need to gather the right tools, including a relaxer kit, gloves, a comb, and a timer.

If you're not sure which relaxer kit to use, look for one that's specifically designed for short hair. Follow the instructions carefully, as using too much or leaving the relaxer on for too long can damage your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to relaxing short hair:

  1. Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties.
  2. Put on gloves to protect your hands from the relaxer.
  3. Mix the relaxer according to the instructions.
  4. Apply the relaxer to each section, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  5. Use a comb to distribute the relaxer evenly.
  6. Set a timer for the recommended time, usually around 10-15 minutes.
  7. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  8. Apply the neutralizing shampoo that comes with the kit to stop the relaxing process.
  9. Rinse your hair again with warm water.
  10. Apply a deep conditioner to restore moisture to your hair.
  11. Rinse your hair once more and style as desired.

Tips for Relaxing Short Hair

Here are some tips to help you relax short hair:

  • Don't use a relaxer on damaged or over-processed hair.
  • Protect your hairline and ears with petroleum jelly to avoid irritation from the relaxer.
  • Avoid washing your hair for at least 48 hours after relaxing to give your hair time to adjust.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently after relaxing.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools for at least a week after relaxing to avoid damaging your hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about relaxing short hair:

Is it safe to relax short hair at home?

Yes, but it's important to follow the instructions carefully to avoid damaging your hair. If you're not sure how to relax your hair at home, consider going to a salon instead.

How often should I relax my short hair?

It's best to wait at least 8-12 weeks between relaxers to avoid damaging your hair.

Can I color my hair after relaxing it?

It's best to wait at least a week after relaxing your hair before coloring it to avoid damaging your hair.

Hair Care After Relaxing

After relaxing your short hair, it's important to take care of it properly to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to keep split ends at bay.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.

Relaxing short hair can be a great way to achieve a sleek, straight look. By following these steps and taking care of your hair properly, you can enjoy your new style without worrying about damage or breakage.
