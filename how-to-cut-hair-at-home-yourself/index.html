How To Cut Hair At Home Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Oct 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Welcome to our step-by-step guide on how to cut hair at home yourself. With the ongoing pandemic, many people are hesitant to visit salons and barbershops. Cutting your own hair at home can save you money and reduce your risk of exposure to the virus. In this article, we will teach you how to cut your hair at home, provide tips and answer common questions. So, let's get started!

How to Cut Hair at Home Yourself

Before we dive into the steps, it's essential to gather the necessary tools. Here are the things you'll need:

  • Scissors (hair-cutting shears are recommended)
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Cape or towel to cover your clothes
  • Mirror

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair before cutting it. Wet hair is longer and heavier than dry hair, which can lead to uneven cuts. It's best to work with clean, dry hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into manageable parts using hair ties or clips. Start by dividing your hair into three sections: the top, middle, and bottom. Clip each section separately.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Section

Start with the bottom section. Comb it out and trim the ends. Be cautious and cut small increments at a time. It's better to cut less than more.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Section

Move onto the middle section. Comb it out, and hold it between your fingers. Cut the ends with scissors or clippers. Remember to cut small increments.

Step 5: Cut the Top Section

Finally, work on the top section. Comb it out and hold it between your fingers. Cut the ends at an angle to create layers. If you're not comfortable with cutting layers, skip this step and leave your hair with a blunt cut.

Step 6: Trim the Bangs (Optional)

If you have bangs, trim them carefully. Comb them forward and cut them at an angle. Remember to cut small increments.

Step 7: Check for Unevenness

Check your hair for unevenness by running your fingers through it. If you notice any unevenness, trim it further. Be cautious and cut small increments.

Step 8: Clean Up

Once you're done cutting your hair, clean up the area. Remove any hair from the floor and sink. Shake out your clothes and wash your hands.

Step 9: Style Your Hair

You've done it! Style your hair as you desire. Blow-dry or air-dry it, and add any hair products you'd like.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair at Home

Here are some tips to help you cut your hair at home:

  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can lead to split ends and uneven cuts.
  • Cut your hair in a well-lit area.
  • Be cautious and cut small increments. It's better to cut less than more.
  • Watch tutorials online. There are plenty of YouTube videos and blog posts that can help you with hair-cutting techniques.
  • Don't be too hard on yourself. Remember, hair grows back! If you make a mistake, it's not the end of the world.

FAQ: Cutting Hair at Home

Can I cut my hair with regular scissors?

It's not recommended. Regular scissors aren't sharp enough and can lead to split ends and uneven cuts. Hair-cutting shears are recommended.

Should I cut my hair wet or dry?

Dry hair is preferred as wet hair is longer and heavier than dry hair, which can lead to uneven cuts.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. On average, it's recommended to cut your hair every six to eight weeks.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

Once you've cut your hair, it's essential to take care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Limit heat styling to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your haircut.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can cause breakage.
  • Eat a healthy diet with plenty of nutrients to promote hair health.

With these tips and steps, you can cut your hair at home yourself. It may take some practice, but with time, you'll become a pro. Stay safe and happy cutting!
