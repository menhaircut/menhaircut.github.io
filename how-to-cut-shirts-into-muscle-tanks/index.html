How To Cut Shirts Into Muscle Tanks A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 23, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to add a little edge to your wardrobe, cutting your shirts into muscle tanks might just be the way to go. Not only are they perfect for summer, but they're also a great way to show off your gains. In this article, we'll show you how to cut shirts into muscle tanks in a few simple steps.

How to Cut Shirts into Muscle Tanks

Before we get started, you'll need a few things:

  • A shirt you're willing to cut
  • A pair of scissors
  • A fabric marker or chalk (optional)

Step 1: Choose Your Shirt

Choose a shirt that you're okay with potentially ruining. It's also a good idea to choose a shirt that's a little loose-fitting, as muscle tanks tend to be a bit more relaxed than regular tanks.

Step 2: Mark Your Cut Lines

If you want a more precise cut, you can use a fabric marker or chalk to mark where you want to cut. Otherwise, you can just eyeball it.

Step 3: Cut Off the Sleeves

Using your scissors, cut off the sleeves of the shirt. You can cut them off right at the seam or a little bit further down, depending on how much of your arm you want to show.

Step 4: Cut the Neckline

Next, cut off the neckline of the shirt. You can make it as wide or narrow as you like. Just be sure to cut slowly and carefully so you don't accidentally make it too big.

Step 5: Cut the Sides

If you want a more relaxed fit, you can cut the sides of the shirt a little bit. This will give you a bit more room to move around in. Just be sure not to cut too much off, or you might end up with a crop top instead of a muscle tank.

Step 6: Try It On

Once you've made all your cuts, try the shirt on to make sure you're happy with the fit. If there are any areas you want to adjust, you can always go back and make more cuts.

Tips for Cutting Shirts into Muscle Tanks

Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Start with a shirt that's a little bit bigger than what you normally wear. This will give you more room to work with.
  • Use a fabric marker or chalk to mark where you want to cut if you want a more precise cut.
  • Don't cut too much off at once. It's better to cut a little bit and then try the shirt on, rather than cutting too much and ruining the shirt.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting shirts into muscle tanks can be a bit tricky, so don't rush it.

FAQs

Can I cut any shirt into a muscle tank?

Technically, yes. However, some shirts might not be the best candidates for cutting. For example, shirts with thick seams or patterns might not look as good once they've been cut. It's best to stick with plain, simple shirts.

Can I wash my muscle tank after I've cut it?

Yes, you can wash your muscle tank just like you would any other shirt. Just be sure to follow the care instructions on the label.

Hair Care Tips for Muscle Tank Wearers

Since muscle tanks tend to show off a bit more skin than regular shirts, you might want to pay extra attention to your hair. Here are a few tips:

  • If you have long hair, consider tying it back to keep it out of your face and off your neck.
  • Use a bit of hairspray or gel to keep your hair in place.
  • Consider trying a new hairstyle, like a messy bun or braid, that looks good with a muscle tank.

By following these tips and tricks, you'll be able to cut shirts into muscle tanks like a pro. Not only will you have a new addition to your wardrobe, but you'll also be showing off your hard-earned gains.
