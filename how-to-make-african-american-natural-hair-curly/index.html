How To Make African American Natural Hair Curly A Comprehensive Guide

Are you tired of your straight hair and want to switch things up with some curls? If you have African American natural hair, achieving curls can be a bit tricky. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we will guide you through the steps to make your natural hair curly. Let's dive in!

How to Make African American Natural Hair Curly

The first step to making your natural hair curly is to assess your hair type. African American natural hair can range from type 3 to type 4c curls. Depending on your hair type, the process of making your hair curly may differ. Here are some ways you can make your hair curly:

1. Twist Outs

Twist outs are a popular method to make African American natural hair curly. To achieve this look, follow these steps:

  1. Wash and condition your hair
  2. Detangle your hair
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner and a styling product
  4. Section your hair into small sections and twist each section
  5. Allow the twists to dry completely
  6. Untwist the twists and fluff your hair to achieve the desired volume

Twist outs can last up to a week if you sleep with a satin bonnet or pillowcase.

2. Bantu Knots

Bantu knots are another way to make African American natural hair curly. Here's how:

  1. Wash and condition your hair
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner and a styling product
  3. Section your hair into small sections and twist each section
  4. Wrap each twist around itself to create a knot
  5. Allow the knots to dry completely
  6. Untwist the knots and fluff your hair to achieve the desired volume

Bantu knots can last up to a week if you sleep with a satin bonnet or pillowcase.

3. Flexi Rods

Flexi rods are a great option to make African American natural hair curly. Here's how:

  1. Wash and condition your hair
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner and a styling product
  3. Section your hair into small sections
  4. Wrap each section around a flexi rod and secure the rod in place
  5. Allow the rods to dry completely
  6. Remove the rods and fluff your hair to achieve the desired volume

Flexi rods can last up to a week if you sleep with a satin bonnet or pillowcase.

Step by Step Guide

Now that you know the different methods to make your African American natural hair curly, here's a step-by-step guide to follow:

  1. Start with clean and conditioned hair
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner and a styling product to your hair
  3. Section your hair into small sections
  4. Choose a method (twist outs, bantu knots, or flexi rods) and follow the steps listed above
  5. Allow your hair to dry completely before removing the twists, knots, or rods
  6. Fluff your hair to achieve your desired volume

Tips for Making African American Natural Hair Curly

Here are some tips to make your African American natural hair curly:

  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
  • Apply a styling product that works for your hair type
  • Don't use too much product as it can weigh down your hair
  • Allow your hair to dry completely before removing the twists, knots, or rods
  • Sleep with a satin bonnet or pillowcase to maintain the curls

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I make my African American natural hair curly without heat?

A: Yes, you can use the methods listed above (twist outs, bantu knots, or flexi rods) to make your hair curly without heat.

Q: How long will my curls last?

A: Your curls can last up to a week if you sleep with a satin bonnet or pillowcase. You can also refresh your curls with a water and conditioner spray.

Q: Can I use heat to make my African American natural hair curly?

A: Yes, you can use a curling iron or a wand to make your hair curly. However, heat can damage your hair if used too often, so it's best to use heat sparingly.

Hair Care for Curly African American Natural Hair

Here are some tips to take care of your curly African American natural hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils
  • Deep condition your hair once a week
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using heat too often

With these tips and methods, you can make your African American natural hair curly and rock a new look. Have fun experimenting with different styles and find what works best for you!
