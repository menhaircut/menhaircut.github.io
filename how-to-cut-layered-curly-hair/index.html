How To Cut Layered Curly Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by William Sep 14, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that maintaining your curls can be a challenge. One way to enhance your curls is by getting a layered haircut. Layered haircuts add dimension and movement to your hair, making your curls look more defined and bouncy. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting layered curly hair.

Description

A layered haircut involves cutting different lengths of hair to create layers. The shorter layers add volume and texture to your hair, while the longer layers provide length and weight. Layered haircuts work well for all types of curly hair, from loose waves to tight coils.

How to Cut Layered Curly Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to have the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a wide-tooth comb, and hair clips to section your hair.

Step 1: Wash and Detangle Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair while it's wet. Gently comb from the ends to the roots to avoid breakage. Rinse your hair thoroughly and towel dry.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use hair clips to section your hair into four equal parts: two at the front and two at the back. Start by securing the top sections of your hair and work your way down.

Step 3: Cut the First Layer

Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer and hold it between your fingers. Cut the ends of the hair at an angle, starting from the middle of the hair shaft and working your way down to the ends. This will create a soft, layered effect. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you have completed the first layer.

Step 4: Cut the Second Layer

Release the next section of hair and repeat the same process. Cut the ends of the hair at an angle, starting from the middle of the hair shaft and working your way down to the ends. Make sure the second layer is shorter than the first layer to create a gradual transition.

Step 5: Cut the Third Layer

Release the next section of hair and repeat the same process. Cut the ends of the hair at an angle, starting from the middle of the hair shaft and working your way down to the ends. Make sure the third layer is shorter than the second layer to create a gradual transition.

Step 6: Cut the Fourth Layer

Release the final section of hair and repeat the same process. Cut the ends of the hair at an angle, starting from the middle of the hair shaft and working your way down to the ends. Make sure the fourth layer is shorter than the third layer to create a gradual transition.

Step 7: Blend the Layers

Once you have completed all the layers, use your scissors to blend the layers together. Take small sections of hair and use a sliding motion to remove any harsh lines or unevenness.

Tips for Cutting Layered Curly Hair

  • Use a pair of sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Start with small sections of hair and cut slowly to avoid cutting too much.
  • Cut your hair when it's dry to see how the curls will fall.
  • Don't cut your hair too often to avoid losing length.
  • Consult a professional hairstylist if you're not confident in cutting your hair.

FAQs

How often should I cut my layered curly hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your curls lose their shape. As a general rule, you should get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain your layered haircut.

Can I cut my curly hair myself?

If you're confident in your hair cutting skills, you can cut your curly hair yourself. However, if you're not sure, it's best to consult a professional hairstylist.

Will a layered haircut work for all types of curly hair?

Yes, a layered haircut can work for all types of curly hair. However, the length and shape of the layers may vary depending on the texture and density of your hair.

Hair Care after Cutting Layered Curly Hair

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of your curls to maintain their shape and health. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Avoid using a brush or fine-tooth comb on your curls to prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your curls to prevent damage.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and frizz.

With these tips and techniques, you can easily cut your layered curly hair at home and maintain your curls' health and beauty. Remember to take your time, start with small sections of hair, and consult a professional if you're not confident in your hair cutting skills. Happy cutting!
