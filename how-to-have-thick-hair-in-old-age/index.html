How To Have Thick Hair In Old Age Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Sep 04, 2023 Â· 5 min read

As we age, our hair tends to thin out, making it harder to achieve the thick, voluminous locks we once had. But there are ways to combat this natural process and maintain a full head of hair well into your golden years. In this article, we'll be discussing how to have thick hair in old age, including helpful tips, step-by-step instructions, and answers to frequently asked questions.

Hair Care Tips

The first step to having thick, healthy hair is taking good care of it. This means washing it regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner, using a wide-toothed comb to detangle it, and avoiding harsh chemicals and heat styling tools. Here are some additional hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, such as braids and ponytails
  • Don't brush your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

By following these simple hair care tips, you can help ensure that your hair stays healthy and strong as you age.


Dietary Changes

Believe it or not, what you eat can actually have an impact on the health of your hair. To promote thick hair growth, it's important to eat a diet rich in vitamins and minerals that support hair health. Here are some dietary changes you can make:

  • Include plenty of protein in your diet, as hair is made up of protein
  • Eat foods high in vitamin C, which helps your body produce collagen
  • Get enough iron, which is essential for healthy hair growth
  • Eat foods high in omega-3 fatty acids, such as salmon and walnuts, which can help reduce inflammation and promote healthy hair follicles

By making these dietary changes, you can help ensure that your body has the nutrients it needs to maintain thick, healthy hair.


Supplements for Hair Growth

In addition to making dietary changes, you may also want to consider taking supplements to promote hair growth. Here are some supplements that have been shown to support healthy hair:

  • Biotin: A B-vitamin that helps strengthen hair and nails
  • Zinc: Helps maintain healthy hair follicles
  • Vitamin D: Helps with hair growth and thickness
  • Folic acid: Helps with the production of red blood cells, which are necessary for healthy hair growth

Before taking any supplements, be sure to talk to your doctor to make sure they're right for you.


Choosing the Right Haircut

The right haircut can do wonders for making your hair look thicker and fuller. Here are some tips for choosing the right haircut:

  • Opt for a layered haircut, which can add volume and texture to your hair
  • Avoid blunt cuts, which can make your hair look thinner
  • Consider getting bangs, which can help conceal a receding hairline
  • Try a shorter haircut, which can make your hair look fuller and thicker

By choosing the right haircut, you can make the most of the hair you have and create the illusion of thicker locks.


Styling Tips

The way you style your hair can also have an impact on how thick and voluminous it looks. Here are some styling tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid using too much product, which can weigh your hair down and make it look thin
  • Blow dry your hair upside down, which can add volume at the roots
  • Use a round brush when blow drying your hair to create volume and bounce
  • Try a volumizing mousse or spray to add thickness and texture to your hair

By following these styling tips, you can create the illusion of thicker, fuller hair.


Best Products for Thick Hair

If you're looking for products to help make your hair look thicker and fuller, here are some options to consider:

  • Volumizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Root lifting spray or mousse
  • Hair thickening serum
  • Volumizing hair powder

Experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair type and styling needs.


Myths About Hair Growth

There are many myths out there about what does and doesn't promote hair growth. Here are some common myths to be aware of:

  • Cutting your hair will make it grow faster: While regular trims can help prevent split ends, they won't make your hair grow faster
  • Brushing your hair 100 times a day will make it healthier: Over-brushing your hair can actually cause damage and breakage
  • Washing your hair every day will make it fall out: Washing your hair regularly is important for maintaining a healthy scalp and hair

By separating fact from fiction, you can make informed decisions about how to care for your hair.


FAQs

Q: Can hair thinning be reversed?

A: While hair thinning can't be reversed completely, there are steps you can take to prevent further thinning and promote healthy hair growth.

Q: Is there a best time of day to take hair growth supplements?

A: There's no one-size-fits-all answer to this question, as it depends on the specific supplement you're taking. Be sure to read the label and follow the directions carefully.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: This can vary depending on your hair type and lifestyle, but most people should aim to wash their hair every 2-3 days.

By following these tips and tricks, you can maintain thick, healthy hair well into your old age. Remember to take good care of your hair, make dietary changes as needed, and experiment with different styling products and techniques to find what works best for you. And if you have any specific concerns or questions about your hair, don't hesitate to talk to your doctor or a hair care professional.
