Description

Layering your hair is a great way to add volume and movement to your style. By cutting different lengths into your hair, you can create a layered look that is both chic and modern. And the best part is that you can do it yourself at home!

How To

The first step in layering your shoulder length hair is to gather the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and some hair clips. Itâs important to use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.

Step by Step

Follow these steps to layer your own shoulder length hair:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair.
  2. Using the comb, separate your hair into sections. The sections should be about 1 inch thick.
  3. Clip the top sections of your hair away from your face.
  4. Starting at the bottom section, comb through your hair to remove any tangles.
  5. Hold the section of hair straight up and use the scissors to cut the ends at a slight angle.
  6. Repeat this process with each section of hair, working your way up to the top.
  7. Once you have cut all of the sections, remove the clips and comb through your hair to blend the layers.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you layer your own shoulder length hair:

  • Start with a little bit of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Take your time and be patient. Itâs better to cut a little bit at a time than to take off too much.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress.
  • If you have curly hair, cut the layers when your hair is dry so you can see how the curls will fall.

  • Can I layer my hair if itâs thin?
  • Yes, layering your hair can actually make it appear thicker and fuller.

  • Get regular trims to maintain your layers.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
