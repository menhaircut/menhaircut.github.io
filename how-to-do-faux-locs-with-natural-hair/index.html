How To Do Faux Locs With Natural Hair

Written by Luffy Aug 04, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Faux locs are one of the most popular protective styles for natural hair. They are a great way to achieve the look of dreadlocks without the commitment or maintenance. Faux locs are also a great protective style because they help to protect your natural hair from damage and breakage. In this blog post, we will discuss how to do faux locs with natural hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to

The first step in doing faux locs with natural hair is to gather all of the necessary supplies. You will need:

  • Crochet hook
  • Marley hair
  • Rubber bands
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Mousse or gel

Once you have all of your supplies, you can begin the process of installing your faux locs.

Step by Step

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Before starting the installation process, make sure that your hair is clean and conditioned. This will help to ensure that your hair is healthy and well-moisturized.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small to medium-sized sections. The size of the sections will depend on your preference, but smaller sections will result in a more natural-looking style.

Step 3: Braid Your Hair

Braid each section of hair from the root to the tip. Make sure that the braid is tight and secure.

Step 4: Crochet the Hair

Using a crochet hook, pull the Marley hair through the base of the braid. Continue to crochet the hair until you reach the end of the braid.

Step 5: Seal the Ends

Once you reach the end of the braid, use a rubber band to seal the end of the hair.

Step 6: Repeat

Repeat steps 3-5 until all of your hair is braided and crocheted with the Marley hair.

Step 7: Style

Once all of your hair is braided and crocheted, you can style your faux locs as desired. You can leave them long, or you can cut them to your desired length.

Tips

  • Make sure that your hair is well-moisturized before starting the installation process.
  • Use a mousse or gel to help keep your hair in place while braiding and crocheting.
  • Take breaks as needed to avoid fatigue and ensure that you are installing your faux locs correctly.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner to help keep your hair moisturized while wearing your faux locs.

FAQ

How long do faux locs last?

Faux locs can last anywhere from 4-8 weeks, depending on how well they are maintained.

Can you wash faux locs?

Yes, you can wash your faux locs. However, it is best to avoid getting the base of the braids wet to prevent frizzing.

Can you swim with faux locs?

Yes, you can swim with faux locs. However, it is best to wear a swim cap to protect them from getting wet.

Hair Care

While wearing your faux locs, it is important to take care of your natural hair.

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or oil.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Avoid sleeping with wet hair to prevent frizzing.
  • Avoid pulling on your faux locs, as this can cause breakage.

By following these tips and taking care of your natural hair, you can enjoy your faux locs while keeping your hair healthy and strong.

Now that you know how to do faux locs with natural hair, you can enjoy this stylish and protective hairstyle. With the right supplies and a little bit of patience, you can achieve the look of dreadlocks without the commitment.
