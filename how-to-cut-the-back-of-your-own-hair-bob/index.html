How To Cut The Back Of Your Own Hair Bob A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 31, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially when it comes to the back of your head. But with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and professional-looking bob haircut without leaving your home. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting the back of your own hair bob.

Description

A bob haircut is a classic and versatile style that never goes out of fashion. It is a short to medium-length haircut that can be customized to suit your face shape and hair type. While going to a salon to get a bob cut is ideal, it can be costly and time-consuming. Cutting your own hair bob at home can save you money and give you control over the final outcome.

How to Cut the Back of Your Own Hair Bob: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, you will need the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips
  • Mirror
  • Spray bottle

Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean. You can use regular household scissors or invest in professional haircutting scissors for better results.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash your hair and dry it thoroughly before cutting. Wet hair is longer and heavier than dry hair, so cutting it when wet can result in a shorter and uneven cut. Use a spray bottle to dampen your hair if necessary.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: top, left, right, and back. Use clips to secure each section in place.

Step 4: Cut the Back

Start with the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the bottom and hold it between your fingers. Use the comb to hold the hair straight and cut the ends with scissors. Repeat this process with the rest of the back section, making sure to cut evenly and not to cut too much hair at once.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

Use a mirror to check your progress and make sure the back is even. If necessary, make small adjustments and continue cutting until you are satisfied with the length and shape.

Step 6: Cut the Sides

Release the side sections of your hair and cut them to match the length of the back. Use the same technique of taking small sections of hair and cutting them with scissors. Make sure to blend the back and sides together for a seamless look.

Step 7: Cut the Top

Release the top section of your hair and cut it to the desired length. You can use the back section as a guide to ensure that the top is even. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the back and moving towards the front, for a layered look.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Once you have finished cutting your hair, style it as desired. You can use a blow dryer, flat iron, or curling iron to achieve different looks.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair Bob

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut a little at a time to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't cut your hair when it is wet as it can result in an uneven cut.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress and make adjustments as necessary.
  • Be patient and take your time to achieve the desired result.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair bob without any experience?

While it is always best to have some experience or training before cutting your own hair, you can still achieve a decent haircut by following the steps above and being patient.

What is the best way to cut the back of my own hair bob?

Start with small sections of hair and cut a little at a time. Use a mirror to check your progress and make adjustments as necessary. Take your time and be patient.

Hair Care after Cutting

After cutting your hair, it is important to take care of it to maintain its health and beauty. Use a good-quality shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair type. Avoid using too much heat on your hair and protect it from the sun and other environmental factors. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain its shape.
