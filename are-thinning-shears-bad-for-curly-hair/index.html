Are Thinning Shears Bad For Curly Hair The Truth Revealed

Written by Frank Aug 30, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have curly hair, you may be wondering if thinning shears are a good idea. Thinning shears are often used by hair stylists to remove bulk and create texture in hair, but are they safe for curly hair? In this article, we’ll explore the pros and cons of using thinning shears on curly hair and provide some tips for maintaining healthy, beautiful curls.

Table of Contents

  • How to Use Thinning Shears on Curly Hair
  • Pros and Cons of Using Thinning Shears on Curly Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Healthy Curly Hair After Using Thinning Shears
  • Frequently Asked Questions About Thinning Shears and Curly Hair
  • Conclusion

How to Use Thinning Shears on Curly Hair

Thinning shears are a great tool for removing bulk from curly hair, but they should be used with care. Here’s how to use thinning shears on curly hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Curly hair should be washed and conditioned with products that are gentle and moisturizing.
  2. Section the hair. Divide the hair into sections using clips or hair ties.
  3. Choose the right shears. Thinning shears should have teeth that are spaced far apart to avoid damaging the curls.
  4. Start at the bottom. Begin thinning the hair at the bottom of each section, working your way up to the top.
  5. Use light pressure. Thinning shears should be used with light pressure to avoid damaging the curls.
  6. Blend the hair. Once you have thinned each section, use regular shears to blend the hair and create a natural look.

Pros and Cons of Using Thinning Shears on Curly Hair

There are both pros and cons to using thinning shears on curly hair. Here are a few to consider:

Pros

  • Thinning shears can remove bulk and create texture in curly hair.
  • They can help reduce frizz and make curls more defined.
  • Thinning shears can make styling curly hair easier and faster.

Cons

  • Thinning shears can damage the hair if not used properly.
  • They can create uneven texture and make curls look choppy.
  • If overused, thinning shears can cause the hair to become too thin and lose its volume.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Curly Hair After Using Thinning Shears

If you’ve used thinning shears on your curly hair, there are a few things you can do to maintain healthy, beautiful curls:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, as they can dry out curly hair.
  • Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Use a diffuser when blow drying your hair to help define your curls.

Frequently Asked Questions About Thinning Shears and Curly Hair

Can thinning shears cause split ends?

Thinning shears can cause split ends if they are not used properly. It’s important to use the right type of shears and to use light pressure when thinning the hair.

Are thinning shears bad for all curly hair types?

Thinning shears can be used on all curly hair types, but they should be used with care. It’s important to choose the right type of shears and to use light pressure when thinning the hair.

How often should I use thinning shears on my curly hair?

It’s best to use thinning shears on curly hair no more than once every six to eight weeks. Overuse can cause the hair to become too thin and lose its volume.

Conclusion

Thinning shears can be a great tool for creating texture and reducing bulk in curly hair, but they should be used with care. By following the tips in this article and maintaining a healthy hair care routine, you can achieve beautiful, healthy curls with the help of thinning shears.
