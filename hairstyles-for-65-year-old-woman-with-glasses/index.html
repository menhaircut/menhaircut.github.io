Hairstyles for 65 Year Old Woman with Glasses

As we age, our hair and face shape change too. Choosing a hairstyle that flatters our face shape and works well with our glasses can be tricky. In this article, we'll explore the best hairstyles for 65 year old woman with glasses.

Description

Hairstyles for 65 year old woman with glasses should be easy to manage and complement your glasses. They should also be age-appropriate and enhance your natural features.

How to Choose the Right Hairstyle

When choosing a hairstyle, consider your face shape and glasses. If you have a round face, opt for hairstyles that add height and length. If you have a square face, go for soft layers and wispy bangs. If you have an oval face, lucky you! You can pull off almost any hairstyle.

For glasses, consider the frame shape and size. If you have large glasses, opt for hairstyles that add volume to the crown of your head. If you have small glasses, go for hairstyles that frame your face and don't cover your glasses.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step by step guide on how to achieve some of the best hairstyles for 65 year old woman with glasses:

1. Bob

Start with clean, dry hair. Part your hair to the side and use a straightening iron to create a sleek, straight look. Finish with hairspray.

2. Pixie Cut

Start with clean, dry hair. Use a styling cream to create a messy, textured look. Use your fingers to style your hair and finish with hairspray.

3. Shoulder-Length Layers

Start with clean, dry hair. Use a curling iron to create loose waves. Finish with hairspray.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when choosing a hairstyle:

  • Choose a hairstyle that complements your glasses
  • Choose a hairstyle that flatters your face shape
  • Choose a hairstyle that is easy to manage
  • Choose a hairstyle that enhances your natural features

FAQ

Q: Can I still have long hair at 65?

A: Yes, you can! However, long hair can be harder to manage and may not be the most flattering for your face shape and glasses.

Q: Can I color my hair at 65?

A: Yes, you can! However, choose a hair color that complements your skin tone and doesn't require high maintenance.

Hair Care

As we age, our hair may become thinner and drier. To keep your hair healthy, use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using too much heat on your hair and trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
