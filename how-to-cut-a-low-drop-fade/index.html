How To Cut A Low Drop Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Nov 23, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking to give your hairstyle a fresh new look? A low drop fade might be just what you need. This haircut is characterized by a gradual fade from the crown of the head down to the nape of the neck, creating a seamless transition from longer to shorter hair. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieving a low drop fade, as well as provide some tips and tricks to perfecting this stylish look. Let's get started!

How to Cut a Low Drop Fade

Before we begin, make sure you have the necessary tools on hand. You'll need:

  • Clippers with various guard sizes
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Water spray bottle
  • Clipper oil

Step 1: Wash and Condition Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to wash and condition your hair to ensure it's clean and easy to work with. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type and rinse thoroughly.

Step 2: Determine the Length of the Fade

Decide how long you want the fade to be. A low drop fade typically starts at the crown of the head and gradually fades down to the nape of the neck. You can choose how short you want the fade to be by selecting a guard size for your clippers. Generally, a size 2 or 3 guard works well for a low drop fade.

Step 3: Start with the Crown

Begin by using your clippers with the chosen guard size to trim the hair at the crown of the head. Use a smooth, consistent motion, starting at the center of the crown and moving outward towards the sides. Take your time and make sure the hair is evenly trimmed.

Step 4: Blend the Sides

Next, use a shorter guard size (such as a 1 or 2) to blend the hair on the sides of the head. Start at the top of the ears and work your way down towards the nape of the neck. Use a smooth, steady motion to ensure a seamless transition from longer to shorter hair.

Step 5: Fade the Hairline

Use a zero guard (or no guard) to create a fade on the hairline. This will create a gradual transition from the shorter hair on the sides to the longer hair on top. Start at the nape of the neck and work your way up towards the crown of the head, using a smooth, consistent motion.

Step 6: Blend the Fade

Using a guard size that's in between the two you used for the crown and sides (such as a 2 or 3), blend the fade between the hairline and the sides of the head. Use a smooth, consistent motion to create a seamless transition.

Step 7: Trim the Top

Use scissors to trim the hair on top of the head to your desired length. Be sure to blend the top with the fade for a cohesive look.

Step 8: Clean Up

Use your clippers and scissors to clean up any stray hairs or uneven lines. Take your time and make sure the haircut is neat and tidy.

Step 9: Style as Desired

Once your haircut is complete, style your hair as desired. You can use products like gel or pomade to add texture and hold.

Tips for Cutting a Low Drop Fade

  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting hair is a skill that takes practice to perfect.
  • Use a mirror to check your work from different angles.
  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep hair damp and easier to work with.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help or advice from a professional stylist.

FAQ

How long does a low drop fade last?

A low drop fade typically lasts 2-3 weeks before it needs to be trimmed again.

Is a low drop fade suitable for all hair types?

A low drop fade can work well for most hair types, but it may not be suitable for extremely curly or thick hair. If you're unsure, consult with a professional stylist.

Can I cut a low drop fade at home?

Yes, with the proper tools and guidance, you can cut a low drop fade at home. However, it's important to take your time and be patient to ensure a neat and tidy haircut.

Hair Care Tips

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and clean.
  • Avoid using excessive heat on your hair (such as blow dryers or flat irons) as this can cause damage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and prevent breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair product with SPF.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to achieve a low drop fade that looks sharp and stylish. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Happy cutting!
