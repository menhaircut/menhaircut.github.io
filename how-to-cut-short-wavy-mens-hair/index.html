How To Cut Short Wavy Men s Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Oct 14, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're a man with short and wavy hair, you know how challenging it is to keep it looking neat and tidy. Regular haircuts are a must to keep your hair looking sharp and stylish. But, going to the barber every time can be expensive and time-consuming. That's why learning how to cut your own hair is a valuable skill that can save you money and time.

Description

This blog post is a step-by-step guide on how to cut short wavy men's hair. It includes tips and tricks on how to achieve the perfect cut, as well as how to care for your hair after the haircut. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced haircutter, this guide will help you achieve a professional-looking haircut at home.

How to Cut Short Wavy Men's Hair

Step 1: Gather the Necessary Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of hair clippers, scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. If you don't have hair clippers, you can use scissors to trim your hair, but it will take longer.

Step 2: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with shampoo and conditioner to get rid of any dirt and oil. Towel dry your hair and comb it to remove any tangles.

Step 3: Choose the Right Clipper Guard

If you're using hair clippers, choose the right clipper guard. The guard you choose will determine how short your hair will be. For short wavy hair, a #3 or #4 guard is ideal. If you want a shorter cut, use a #2 guard, but be careful not to cut your hair too short.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start cutting your hair from the back of your head, using the clippers or scissors. Work your way up to the crown of your head, following the natural curve of your head. Be sure to cut in small sections and use the comb to guide the clippers or scissors.

Step 5: Cut the Sides and Top

Next, cut the sides and top of your hair. Use the same technique as before, cutting in small sections and following the natural curve of your head. Use the comb to guide the clippers or scissors.

Step 6: Blend the Edges

Once you've finished cutting your hair, use the scissors to blend the edges. This will give your hair a more natural look and prevent any harsh lines.

Step 7: Check for Any Uneven Spots

Check your hair for any uneven spots and trim them with scissors. Be sure to check the back of your head, as it's easy to miss spots there.

Step 8: Clean Up

Clean up any hair clippings on your clothes and the floor. Take a shower to remove any remaining hair clippings.

Tips

- Start with a longer guard and work your way down if you want a shorter cut.

- Don't cut your hair too short, as it will be difficult to fix any mistakes.

- Use the scissors to cut any stray hairs that the clippers missed.

- Cut your hair in a well-lit area so you can see what you're doing.

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It's recommended to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and style.

Q: Can I use hair clippers on wet hair?

A: It's best to use hair clippers on dry hair, as wet hair can clog the blades and make it difficult to cut.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and style. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized. Avoid using hot tools such as flat irons and blow dryers, as they can damage your hair. Use a hair gel or pomade to style your hair, and avoid touching it too much to prevent oil buildup.

Cutting your own hair can be a bit intimidating at first, but with practice, it can become a valuable skill. Follow these steps and tips to achieve a professional-looking haircut at home. Happy cutting!
