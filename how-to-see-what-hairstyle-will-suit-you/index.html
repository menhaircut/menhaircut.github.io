How To See What Hairstyle Will Suit You A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Sep 16, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of your current hairstyle and looking to switch things up? Or maybe you’re just not sure what kind of hairstyle would work best for your face shape and hair texture. Whatever the reason may be, figuring out what hairstyle will suit you can be a daunting task. But fear not, because we’ve got you covered with this comprehensive guide on how to see what hairstyle will suit you.

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in figuring out what hairstyle will suit you is determining your face shape. Your face shape can be oval, round, square, heart-shaped, or diamond-shaped. To determine your face shape, pull your hair back and look in the mirror. Use a measuring tape to measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Then, measure the length of your face from your hairline to your chin. Based on these measurements, you can determine your face shape.

Once you’ve determined your face shape, you can research hairstyles that work best for that shape. For example, if you have a round face shape, you may want to opt for a long, layered haircut or a short bob with volume at the crown to elongate your face.

Assessing Your Hair Texture

Another important factor to consider when choosing a hairstyle is your hair texture. Is your hair straight, wavy, curly, or kinky? This will determine what kind of cuts and styles will work best for you. For example, if you have curly hair, you may want to avoid a blunt bob and opt for a layered cut that will help to define your curls.

Consider Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle can also play a role in what hairstyle will suit you. If you’re someone who is always on the go and doesn’t have a lot of time to style your hair, you may want to opt for a low-maintenance hairstyle that doesn’t require a lot of upkeep. On the other hand, if you have more time to devote to your hair, you may want to consider a more intricate style that requires more styling.

Understanding Your Hair Type

In addition to your hair texture, you’ll also want to consider your hair type. Is your hair fine or thick? Is it oily or dry? These factors can impact what kind of products and styling techniques will work best for you.

Haircut Options for Different Face Shapes

Now that you’ve determined your face shape, it’s time to research haircut options that will work best for you. Here are some examples:

  • Oval face shape: Anything goes! Oval faces can pull off just about any hairstyle.
  • Round face shape: Long, layered cuts or short bobs with volume at the crown can help to elongate the face.
  • Square face shape: Soft, layered cuts that fall below the chin can help to soften the jawline.
  • Heart-shaped face: Long, side-swept bangs and chin-length cuts can balance out the forehead and chin.
  • Diamond-shaped face: Short, layered cuts can help to balance out the forehead and chin.

Choosing the Right Hair Color

In addition to the cut, you’ll also want to consider what hair color will work best for you. If you’re looking to make a big change, you may want to consult with a professional colorist to determine what shades will complement your skin tone and eye color.

Investing in the Right Styling Tools

To maintain your new hairstyle, you’ll need to invest in the right styling tools. This may include a quality hair dryer, flat iron, curling iron, and brushes and combs designed for your hair type.

Tips for Maintaining Your New Hairstyle

Once you have your new hairstyle, it’s important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. This may include using the right products for your hair type, avoiding heat damage, and getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQs

How do I know if a certain hairstyle will work for my face shape?

Research different hairstyles that work best for your face shape, and consider consulting with a hairstylist for their professional opinion.

What kind of haircut should I get if I have fine hair?

A layered cut can help to add volume and dimension to fine hair.

How often should I get a trim?

It’s recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Conclusion

Choosing a new hairstyle can be a fun and exciting process, but it’s important to consider factors such as your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle to determine what will work best for you. By following these tips and doing your research, you’ll be on your way to finding the perfect hairstyle that will make you look and feel your best.
