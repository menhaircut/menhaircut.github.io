How Long Does It Take For Dreads To Grow

Written by Frank Jul 13, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking to start your dreadlock journey but wondering how long it will take for them to fully mature? Well, the answer is not that simple as it varies from person to person depending on different factors such as hair texture, length, and maintenance. In this article, we’ll explore the different aspects of growing dreads, including how long it takes, how to do it, and how to care for them.

Table of Contents

How to Grow Dreads

Growing dreads is a process that requires patience and dedication. There are different methods to start your dreadlock journey, including the backcombing method, twist and rip, and freeform or neglect method. Each method has its own pros and cons, and you should choose the one that suits your hair type and lifestyle.

Backcombing Method

The backcombing method involves sectioning your hair and backcombing each section using a comb until it forms a knot. This method requires more maintenance and can be painful if not done correctly. However, it provides an instant dreadlock look and can work on any hair type.

Twist and Rip Method

The twist and rip method involve sectioning your hair and twisting each section until it forms a knot. This method is less painful than backcombing and provides a more natural-looking dreadlock. However, it takes longer to mature and requires more maintenance.

Freeform or Neglect Method

The freeform or neglect method involves letting your hair naturally mat and knot over time without any interference. This method requires the least maintenance and provides the most natural-looking dreadlock. However, it takes the longest to mature, and the results may not be uniform.

Step-by-Step Guide to Growing Dreads

Here’s a step-by-step guide to growing dreads using the backcombing method:

Step 1: Shampoo your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue.

Step 2: Section your hair using clips or bands. The size of the sections will determine the size of your dreadlocks.

Step 3: Take one section and backcomb it using a comb until it forms a knot. Repeat for all sections.

Step 4: Use a dread wax or gel to hold the knots together and prevent them from unraveling.

Step 5: Palm roll each dread to smooth out any bumps and encourage them to lock together.

Step 6: Repeat the process every few weeks until your dreads are fully mature.

Tips for Growing Healthy Dreads

Here are some tips to ensure healthy dread growth:

  • Keep your hair clean and free from buildup or residue.
  • Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using conditioner.
  • Avoid using a hairdryer or any heat styling tools as it can damage your hair and slow down the locking process.
  • Be patient and avoid over-manipulating your dreads as it can cause breakage and slow down the maturing process.
  • Maintain a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote healthy hair growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take for dreads to grow?

The time it takes for dreads to mature varies from person to person and can take anywhere from six months to a year or more.

Can I start dreads with short hair?

Yes, you can start dreads with short hair, but it may take longer to mature. It’s best to wait until your hair is at least three inches long to start your dread journey.

Can I wash my hair with dreads?

Yes, you can wash your hair with dreads, but it’s essential to use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using conditioner as it can cause buildup and slow down the locking process.

Dreadlock Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips for maintaining healthy dreads:

  • Wash your hair every 7-10 days using a residue-free shampoo.
  • Use a dreadlock-specific shampoo to prevent buildup and promote healthy hair growth.
  • Avoid using oil-based products as it can cause buildup and attract dirt.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to avoid lint buildup and reduce friction.
  • Maintain a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote healthy hair growth.

In conclusion, growing dreads is a unique journey that requires patience and dedication. The time it takes for your dreads to mature depends on various factors, including your hair texture, length, and maintenance. By following the tips and steps outlined in this article, you can ensure healthy dread growth and enjoy your new hairstyle.
