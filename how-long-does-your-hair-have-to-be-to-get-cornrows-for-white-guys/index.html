How Long Does Your Hair Have To Be To Get Cornrows For White Guys

Written by Aprilia Jul 08, 2023 · 4 min read

Cornrows are a popular hairstyle among men and women of all races. They are a great way to keep your hair neat and tidy, especially during the summer months. If you're a white guy considering getting cornrows, you may be wondering how long your hair needs to be for the style to work. In this article, we'll explore the answer to that question and provide some tips for taking care of your cornrows once you've got them.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Cornrows for White Guys
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Getting Cornrows
  • Tips for Maintaining Cornrows
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Cornrows

How to Get Cornrows for White Guys

Before we get into the specifics of how long your hair needs to be for cornrows, let's talk about how to get the style in general. Cornrows are created by weaving small sections of hair from the scalp into a tight, narrow braid. The braids are then styled into a variety of patterns, depending on the desired look.

If you're new to cornrows, it's best to have a professional stylist create the style for you. They will be able to ensure that the braids are even and tight, and that your scalp is not damaged in the process. You can also ask for advice on which patterns will work best with your hair type and face shape.

Step-by-Step Guide to Getting Cornrows

If you're feeling adventurous and want to try creating cornrows yourself, here's a basic step-by-step guide:

  1. Start with clean, detangled hair. Use a wide-tooth comb to remove any knots or tangles.
  2. Part your hair into small sections using a rat-tail comb. The sections should be no wider than half an inch.
  3. Apply a little bit of hair gel or pomade to each section to help it stay in place.
  4. Starting at the front of your head, use your rat-tail comb to create a small section of hair to braid.
  5. Divide the section into three equal parts.
  6. Begin braiding by crossing the right strand over the middle strand, then crossing the left strand over the new middle strand. Continue braiding, alternating sides, until you reach the end of the section.
  7. Secure the end of the braid with a small hair elastic or a clip.
  8. Repeat steps 4-7 with each section of hair until your entire head is braided.

Tips for Maintaining Cornrows

Once you have your cornrows, it's important to take good care of them to ensure they last as long as possible. Here are some tips:

  • Wear a satin or silk scarf or bonnet to bed to help protect your cornrows from frizzing and tangling.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your cornrows, as this can cause them to loosen or break.
  • Moisturize your scalp regularly to prevent dryness and itching.
  • Wash your cornrows gently with a mild shampoo and conditioner. Be sure to rinse thoroughly to prevent buildup.
  • Don't keep your cornrows in for too long. Depending on your hair type and the quality of your braids, you may need to take them out and redo them after a few weeks.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions people have about getting cornrows as a white guy:

How long does my hair need to be for cornrows?

For cornrows to work, your hair needs to be at least two inches long. This will ensure that the braids are tight and secure.

Can white guys get cornrows?

Yes! Cornrows are a versatile hairstyle that can be worn by anyone, regardless of race or gender.

Will cornrows damage my hair?

If done correctly, cornrows should not damage your hair. However, if the braids are too tight or if you keep them in for too long, you may experience hair loss or breakage.

Hair Care for Cornrows

Cornrows require a little bit of extra care to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair while wearing cornrows:

  • Moisturize your scalp regularly with a light oil, such as coconut or jojoba oil.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products, as these can weigh down your braids and cause buildup.
  • Don't scratch your scalp or pick at your braids, as this can cause damage and frizzing.
  • When it's time to take out your cornrows, be sure to do so gently. Use a pair of scissors to snip the elastics or clips holding the braids in place, then carefully unravel each braid.

In conclusion, cornrows are a great hairstyle for white guys who want a low-maintenance, stylish look. Just be sure that your hair is at least two inches long before you attempt the style, and take good care of your cornrows once you've got them. With a little bit of effort, you can enjoy healthy, beautiful hair all summer long!
