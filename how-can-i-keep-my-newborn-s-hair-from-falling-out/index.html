How Can I Keep My Newborn s Hair From Falling Out

Written by Frank Jun 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As a new parent, you may be concerned about your newborn's hair falling out. It is normal for babies to lose some of their hair in the first few months of life, but there are steps you can take to keep their hair healthy and prevent excessive shedding. In this post, we will discuss how to care for your baby's hair and keep it from falling out.

Table of Contents

  • How to Care for Your Baby's Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Prevent Hair Loss
  • Tips for Keeping Your Baby's Hair Healthy
  • FAQs about Newborn Hair Loss
  • Baby Hair Care Products

How to Care for Your Baby's Hair

It is important to care for your baby's hair just as you would your own. Here are some tips for keeping your baby's hair healthy:

  • Wash your baby's hair with a gentle shampoo. Use a small amount and avoid getting it in their eyes.
  • Use a soft brush or comb to gently detangle your baby's hair.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your baby's hair.
  • Keep your baby's scalp clean and dry to prevent irritation.

Following these basic steps can help to promote healthy hair growth and prevent excessive shedding.

Step-by-Step Guide to Prevent Hair Loss

If you are concerned about your baby's hair falling out, here are some additional steps you can take:

  • Massage your baby's scalp. Gently massaging your baby's scalp with your fingertips can help to increase blood flow and stimulate hair growth.
  • Use a soft baby hat. Wearing a soft hat can protect your baby's hair from rubbing against surfaces and prevent hair loss.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles. Pulling your baby's hair back tightly can cause damage and hair loss. Stick to loose hairstyles or leave their hair down.
  • Choose the right hair accessories. Avoid using tight headbands or clips that can pull on your baby's hair. Opt for soft, flexible hair ties or barrettes.

By following these steps, you can help to prevent excessive hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.

Tips for Keeping Your Baby's Hair Healthy

In addition to the steps above, here are some tips for keeping your baby's hair healthy:

  • Use a moisturizing conditioner. If your baby's hair is dry, using a moisturizing conditioner can help to keep it healthy.
  • Avoid over-washing. Washing your baby's hair too frequently can strip away natural oils and dry out their scalp.
  • Protect their hair from the sun. If your baby will be spending time in the sun, protect their hair and scalp with a hat or sunscreen.
  • Be gentle when drying. After washing your baby's hair, pat it dry gently with a soft towel. Avoid rubbing their hair vigorously, as this can cause damage.

Following these tips can help to keep your baby's hair healthy and prevent excessive shedding.

FAQs about Newborn Hair Loss

Why do newborns lose their hair?

Newborns often lose some of their hair in the first few months of life. This is due to hormonal changes and is completely normal.

When will my baby's hair grow back?

Most babies will start to grow new hair within the first few months of life. However, it may take up to a year for their hair to fully grow back.

Can I use adult hair care products on my baby?

No, it is not recommended to use adult hair care products on your baby. These products may contain harsh chemicals that can be harmful to your baby's delicate skin and scalp.

Baby Hair Care Products

When it comes to caring for your baby's hair, it is important to choose the right products. Look for gentle, natural products that are specifically formulated for babies. Here are some popular baby hair care products:

  • Johnson's Baby Shampoo
  • Aveeno Baby Gentle Wash & Shampoo
  • California Baby Calming Shampoo & Body Wash
  • Burt's Bees Baby Bee Shampoo & Wash

By using gentle, baby-specific hair care products and following the steps above, you can help to keep your baby's hair healthy and prevent excessive shedding.

Remember, some hair loss is normal in newborns and is nothing to worry about. However, if you are concerned about your baby's hair loss, speak to your pediatrician for advice.
