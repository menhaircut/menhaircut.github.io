Do Dreadlocks Get Thicker Over Time

Written by Bardi Jun 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

As a dreadlock wearer, one of the most common questions you may get asked is, "Do dreadlocks get thicker over time?" It's a valid question, and the answer is yes, they do. In this article, we will delve deeper into this topic and provide you with answers to some of the most frequently asked questions about dreadlocks.

Description

Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that involves twisting and matting hair into rope-like strands. They can be achieved naturally or with the help of a professional hairdresser. Once you have dreadlocks, you may notice that they start to get thicker over time. This is because the hair strands continue to mat and compress, resulting in thicker locks.

How To

Getting thick dreadlocks is not something that can be achieved overnight. It requires patience, dedication, and proper hair care. Here are some tips to help you achieve thicker dreadlocks:

1. Allow Your Hair to Grow

The longer your hair is, the thicker your dreadlocks will be. So, if you want thicker locks, the first thing you need to do is let your hair grow. This will give you more hair to work with and create thicker dreadlocks.

2. Twist and Tangle Your Hair

The process of creating dreadlocks involves twisting and tangling your hair to create knots. The more you twist and tangle your hair, the thicker your dreadlocks will become. Be careful not to overdo it, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

3. Use Products to Help Your Hair Knot

There are many products on the market that can help your hair knot and create thicker dreadlocks. These products include dread wax, locking gel, and sea salt spray. However, be careful with the products you use as some can cause buildup and damage to your hair.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to get thicker dreadlocks:

Step 1: Let Your Hair Grow

The first step to getting thicker dreadlocks is to let your hair grow. This will give you more hair to work with and create thicker locks.

Step 2: Twist and Tangle Your Hair

The next step is to twist and tangle your hair to create knots. This can be done by hand, or you can use a comb or brush to help you create the knots.

Step 3: Use Products to Help Your Hair Knot

Once you have twisted and tangled your hair, you can use products such as dread wax or locking gel to help your hair knot and create thicker dreadlocks.

Step 4: Maintain Your Dreadlocks

Maintaining your dreadlocks is crucial to ensuring they continue to get thicker over time. This involves regular washing, conditioning, and retwisting. Using a silk or satin pillowcase can also help prevent breakage and damage to your hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve thicker dreadlocks:

1. Be Patient

Getting thick dreadlocks takes time. Be patient and don't be discouraged if your hair doesn't grow or thicken as quickly as you would like.

2. Avoid Using Heat

Avoid using heat on your hair as this can cause damage and breakage. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a hair dryer on a low heat setting.

3. Protect Your Hair

Protect your hair from the elements, such as the sun, wind, and cold weather. Wearing a hat or scarf can help protect your hair and prevent damage.

FAQ

Q: Can I get thick dreadlocks with short hair?

A: It is possible to get thick dreadlocks with short hair, but it may take longer. It's best to let your hair grow out as much as possible before starting the dreadlock process.

Q: Will my dreadlocks get thinner over time?

A: No, your dreadlocks will not get thinner over time. They may loosen or unravel slightly, but they should not get thinner unless you purposely cut or trim them.

Q: Can I dye my hair with dreadlocks?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair with dreadlocks, but be careful not to use too much bleach or harsh chemicals as this can damage your hair.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential to ensuring your dreadlocks continue to get thicker over time. Here are some hair care tips for dreadlock wearers:

1. Wash Your Hair Regularly

Washing your hair regularly is crucial to preventing buildup and keeping your hair and scalp healthy. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using conditioner on your dreadlocks.

2. Retwist Your Hair

Retwisting your hair every few weeks is essential to maintaining the shape and thickness of your dreadlocks.

3. Use Natural Products

Avoid using products that contain harsh chemicals or artificial fragrances. Instead, opt for natural products such as essential oils, coconut oil, and aloe vera gel.

In conclusion, dreadlocks do get thicker over time. With the right hair care routine and patience, you can achieve thicker, healthier dreadlocks that will last for years to come.
