How To Use Thinning Shears On Extensions A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning shears are a great tool for creating texture and layers in your hair. But, did you know that you can also use them on extensions? Thinning shears can help to blend your extensions seamlessly into your natural hair, giving you a more natural and polished look. In this guide, we will show you how to use thinning shears on extensions, step-by-step.

Description

Thinning shears are a type of scissors that have teeth on one or both blades. These teeth help to remove some of the hair while leaving some behind, creating a textured and layered look. When used on extensions, thinning shears can help to blend the extensions with your natural hair, making them look more natural and seamless.

How to Use Thinning Shears on Extensions: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you begin using thinning shears on your extensions, you need to prepare your hair. Start by washing and conditioning your hair and extensions. Once your hair is clean, blow-dry it or let it air dry. Brush your hair and extensions thoroughly to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, you need to section your hair. Divide your hair into two sections, one on top and one at the bottom. Clip the top section of your hair away from your face.

Step 3: Attach Your Extensions

Attach your extensions to the bottom section of your hair. Be sure to position them as close to your scalp as possible. Use hair clips to secure the extensions in place.

Step 4: Use Thinning Shears

Now, it's time to use your thinning shears. Start by holding the shears vertically and cutting into the extensions. Make sure to cut only a small amount of hair at a time. Use the shears to blend the extensions with your natural hair. Repeat this process until you have blended all of the extensions with your natural hair.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once you have blended your extensions with your natural hair, you can style your hair as desired. Use a curling iron or straightener to create waves or straighten your hair. Finish with a styling product to hold your look in place.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Extensions

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning shears on extensions:

  • Use thinning shears sparingly. Cutting too much hair with thinning shears can make your hair look thin and stringy.
  • Cut only a small amount of hair at a time. This will help you to achieve a more natural-looking blend.
  • Be patient. It may take some time to get the hang of using thinning shears on extensions. Practice on a small section of hair first before moving on to the rest of your hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about using thinning shears on extensions:

Can you use thinning shears on all types of extensions?

Thinning shears can be used on most types of extensions, including clip-ins, tape-ins, and sew-ins. However, be cautious when using them on synthetic extensions, as they may not be able to handle the heat from the shears.

Will using thinning shears damage my extensions?

When used correctly, thinning shears should not damage your extensions. However, cutting too much hair at once or using them too often can cause damage.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some tips to keep your hair and extensions looking their best:

  • Wash your hair and extensions regularly to keep them clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair and extensions, as this can cause damage.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools on your hair.
  • Brush your hair and extensions gently to avoid tangles and breakage.
  • Avoid sleeping with wet hair or extensions, as this can cause tangling and matting.

Using thinning shears on extensions can help to create a more natural-looking blend with your natural hair. Follow these steps and tips to achieve a seamless and polished look. Remember to take good care of your hair and extensions to keep them looking their best.
