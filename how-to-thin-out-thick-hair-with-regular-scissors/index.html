How To Thin Out Thick Hair With Regular Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Sep 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can give you a voluminous and luscious look, it can also be challenging to manage and style. Thinning out your hair can make it more manageable and easier to style. However, going to a professional stylist can be expensive. Don't worry, we have got you covered. In this article, we will guide you on how to thin out your thick hair with regular scissors.

Description

Thinning out thick hair can be done using different techniques. One of the most common ways is by using thinning shears, which are specialized scissors designed to remove bulk from the hair. However, not everyone has access to these specialized scissors, or they may not want to spend money on them. Regular scissors can be used as a substitute to thin out hair.

How to Thin Out Thick Hair with Regular Scissors

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to thin out thick hair with regular scissors:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, wash and dry it thoroughly. This will make it easier to see the hair's natural texture and thickness, making it easier to determine where to cut.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into four sections using hair clips. Start by parting your hair down the middle of your head, then from ear to ear. Clip each section out of the way, leaving one section loose.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Take a small section of hair from the loose section and hold it up at a 45-degree angle away from your head. Use your regular scissors to make small cuts vertically into the hair, about halfway down the shaft. Repeat this process on different sections until you have thinned out your hair to your desired thickness.

Step 4: Check Your Progress

Release each section one at a time to check how much hair you have thinned out. Be careful not to overdo it on one section, as this can create an uneven look.

Step 5: Blend Your Hair

Once you have thinned out your hair, use your regular scissors to blend the cut sections with the uncut sections. This will ensure a smooth and even look.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out your hair with regular scissors:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to larger ones.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can create an uneven look.
  • Use a comb to separate the hair and ensure you are only cutting the desired sections.
  • Be patient and take your time.

FAQ

1. Can I thin out my hair with regular household scissors?

Yes, you can use regular household scissors to thin out your hair. However, it is recommended to use hairdressing scissors as they are sharper and more precise.

2. How often should I thin out my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, it is recommended to thin out your hair every six to eight weeks.

3. Will thinning out my hair damage it?

Thinning out your hair should not damage it if done correctly. However, if you overdo it, it can create an uneven look and damage your hair.

Hair Care

Thinning out your hair can make it easier to manage and style. However, it is essential to take care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Avoid using hot tools such as curling irons and straighteners, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools or blow-drying your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish and moisturize your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals such as hair dyes and relaxers, as they can damage your hair.

Thinning out your thick hair can be done at home using regular scissors. Follow the steps and tips in this article to achieve a smooth and even look. Remember to take care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage.
