How To Prepare Your Toddler For A Haircut

Written by Bardi Aug 26, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have a toddler, you know how important it is to keep them looking neat and tidy. One of the ways to do this is by giving them a haircut. However, some toddlers can become anxious or scared at the thought of getting a haircut. That’s why it’s essential to prepare them for the experience beforehand. In this post, we’ll go over some tips on how to prepare your toddler for a haircut.

Table of Contents

  • How to Prepare Your Toddler for a Haircut
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for a Successful Haircut
  • FAQ

How to Prepare Your Toddler for a Haircut

Here are some steps you can take to prepare your toddler for a haircut:

1. Talk to Your Toddler About the Haircut

Explain to your toddler what will happen during the haircut. Use simple language and be positive about the experience. Tell them that haircuts are a way to keep them looking neat and tidy.

2. Show Them Pictures

Show your toddler pictures of other children getting haircuts. This will help them see that it’s a normal experience. You can also show them pictures of different hairstyles so they can choose what they like.

3. Role Play

You can role play with your toddler to help them feel more comfortable. Use a toy scissors and pretend to cut their hair. Let them play with the scissors too, so they can see that they’re not scary.

4. Bring a Comfort Item

Bring a comfort item, such as a favorite toy or blanket, to the haircut appointment. This will help your toddler feel more secure and calm.

5. Choose the Right Time

Choose a time when your toddler is well-rested and fed. This will help them feel more relaxed and less likely to be cranky or fussy.

Tips for a Successful Haircut

Here are some tips to help make the haircut experience a success:

1. Choose the Right Salon

Choose a salon that is child-friendly and has stylists who are experienced in cutting children’s hair.

2. Bring Snacks and Drinks

Bring snacks and drinks to keep your toddler occupied and happy during the haircut.

3. Bring a Favorite Toy or Book

Bring a favorite toy or book to keep your toddler entertained and distracted.

4. Be Patient

Be patient with your toddler during the haircut. It may take longer than usual, but it’s important to take breaks if your toddler is feeling overwhelmed.

5. Praise Your Toddler

Praise your toddler during and after the haircut. Let them know how proud you are of them for being brave.

FAQ

1. What if my toddler is still scared?

If your toddler is still scared, consider rescheduling the appointment or trying again at a later time.

2. How often should my toddler get a haircut?

It depends on your toddler’s hair type and growth rate. Generally, toddlers should get a haircut every 6-8 weeks.

3. What if my toddler won’t sit still?

If your toddler won’t sit still, try distracting them with a toy or book. You can also ask the stylist for help.

Hair Care After the Haircut

After the haircut, it’s important to take care of your toddler’s hair. Here are some hair care tips:

1. Use Mild Shampoo

Use a mild shampoo designed for children’s hair to avoid irritation. Avoid using adult shampoos as they can be too harsh for your toddler’s delicate scalp.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools on your toddler’s hair, as they can cause damage. Instead, opt for gentle styling techniques such as braids or ponytails.

3. Trim Regularly

Trim your toddler’s hair regularly to keep it looking neat and tidy. This will also help to prevent split ends.

4. Brush Gently

Use a soft brush to gently brush your toddler’s hair. Be careful not to pull or tug on the hair, as it can be painful.

By following these tips, you can help prepare your toddler for a haircut and make the experience a positive one. Remember to be patient and praise your toddler for being brave!
