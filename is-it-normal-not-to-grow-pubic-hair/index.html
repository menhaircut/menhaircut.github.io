Is It Normal Not To Grow Pubic Hair

Written by Petter Nov 16, 2023 · 3 min read

If you’re someone who has noticed that you don’t have pubic hair, you might be wondering if it’s normal. The short answer is yes, it can be normal not to grow pubic hair. In this article, we’ll discuss why some people don’t grow pubic hair, when to be concerned about it, and how to take care of the hair you do have.

Why Don’t Some People Grow Pubic Hair?

Pubic hair typically starts to grow during puberty, which is when your body goes through a lot of changes. However, not everyone will experience the same changes at the same time. Some people may start growing pubic hair earlier or later than others, while some people may not grow it at all. This can be due to a variety of factors, including genetics, hormones, and underlying medical conditions.

When Should You Be Concerned?

If you’re someone who has never grown pubic hair, it’s important to talk to your doctor. While it can be normal not to grow pubic hair, it can also be a sign of an underlying medical condition. Some conditions that can cause a lack of pubic hair include hypopituitarism, Turner syndrome, and certain types of cancer.

How to Take Care of Pubic Hair

If you do have pubic hair, it’s important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

1. Keep it clean.

Make sure to wash your pubic hair regularly with a mild soap and water. This will help prevent infections and keep your hair looking healthy.

2. Trim it.

If you prefer to keep your pubic hair short, you can use scissors or clippers to trim it. Just be careful not to cut yourself.

3. Avoid harsh chemicals.

Avoid using harsh chemicals like hair dyes or bleach on your pubic hair. These can cause irritation and damage to the skin.

FAQ

Q: Is it normal not to have pubic hair at 14?

A: Yes, it can be normal not to have pubic hair at 14. Pubic hair can start to grow at different times for different people.

Q: Can stress cause a lack of pubic hair?

A: While stress can affect your body in many ways, it’s unlikely to cause a lack of pubic hair.

Q: Can shaving or waxing cause pubic hair not to grow back?

A: No, shaving or waxing won’t cause pubic hair not to grow back. However, it may take some time for the hair to grow back after shaving or waxing.

Hair Care

Overall, whether you have pubic hair or not, it’s important to take care of your hair. This can help prevent infections, irritation, and other issues. If you’re concerned about the lack of pubic hair, talk to your doctor. They can help determine if there’s an underlying medical condition and provide treatment if necessary.
