How To Cut Long Hair But Keep The Length

Written by Aprilia Nov 08, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you tired of having long hair that looks dull and lifeless? Do you want to switch up your style without sacrificing your length? Cutting long hair can be a daunting task, but with the right technique, you can trim your ends and keep your length. In this article, we’ll show you how to cut long hair but keep the length in a few simple steps.

How to

The key to cutting long hair while keeping the length is to focus on the ends. This means avoiding cutting too much hair off the top layers and instead, trimming just the tips. Here are the steps you can follow:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will give you a better idea of how your hair falls naturally and where you need to trim.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into four sections, using clips to secure each section. This will make it easier to work with smaller sections and ensure that you don’t miss any spots.

Step 3: Trim the Ends

Starting with one section, take a small amount of hair between your fingers and twist it. Use a pair of sharp hairdressing scissors to trim the ends, cutting at an angle to create a soft, feathered effect.

Repeat this process on each section, making sure to trim only the tips of the hair.

Step 4: Check Your Work

Once you’ve finished trimming, let down all the sections and check your work in the mirror. Make any final adjustments to ensure that your hair is even and balanced.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting long hair:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can’t add hair back once it’s gone.
  • Invest in a good pair of hairdressing scissors. Using dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Don’t rush the process. Take your time and be patient to avoid making any mistakes.
  • Consider getting a friend to help you. It can be difficult to cut the back of your hair on your own.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting long hair while keeping the length:

Can I cut my own long hair?

Yes, but it can be tricky. It’s important to have the right technique and tools to avoid damaging your hair.

How often should I trim my long hair?

It’s recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Will cutting my long hair make it grow faster?

No, cutting your hair doesn’t affect its growth rate. However, trimming the ends can make your hair appear healthier and fuller.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and length. Here are some tips:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and prevent breakage.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends from traveling up the hair shaft.

By following these tips, you can keep your long hair looking healthy and beautiful while still enjoying a fresh new style.
