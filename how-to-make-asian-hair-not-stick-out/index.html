How To Make Asian Hair Not Stick Out A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 10, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of your Asian hair sticking out no matter how much you try to style it? If so, you're not alone. Many people with Asian hair struggle with this issue, but the good news is that there are steps you can take to make your hair lie flat and stay in place. In this post, we'll walk you through how to make Asian hair not stick out, step by step.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Make Asian Hair Not Stick Out
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Managing Asian Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Asian Hair

How to Make Asian Hair Not Stick Out

There are several ways you can make your Asian hair not stick out. Here are some of the most effective:

1. Use the Right Products

The products you use on your hair can make a big difference in how it behaves. Look for products that are designed specifically for Asian hair, as they will be formulated to work with the unique texture of your hair. Avoid products that are too heavy, as they can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.

2. Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow drying your hair can help to smooth it down and make it lie flat. Use a round brush to pull your hair straight as you blow dry, and aim the dryer nozzle down to help smooth the cuticle. Don't forget to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

3. Straighten Your Hair

If blow drying alone isn't enough to tame your hair, consider using a straightening iron. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray and work in small sections to ensure that you get all of your hair straightened evenly.

4. Try a Keratin Treatment

A keratin treatment can be a great way to temporarily smooth out frizzy or unruly hair. This treatment involves applying a keratin solution to your hair and then sealing it in with heat. The result is smoother, shinier hair that is easier to manage.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to making your Asian hair not stick out:

1. Start with Clean Hair

Wash your hair with a shampoo and conditioner designed for Asian hair. Rinse thoroughly to ensure that all product is removed from your hair.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help detangle and soften it. This will make it easier to style and help prevent breakage.

3. Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair using a round brush to smooth it down. Focus the dryer nozzle down to help smooth the cuticle and prevent frizz.

4. Straighten Your Hair

If necessary, use a straightening iron to further smooth out your hair. Work in small sections to ensure that you get all of your hair straightened evenly.

5. Finish with a Serum

Apply a small amount of serum to your hair to add shine and prevent frizz. Be careful not to use too much, as this can make your hair look greasy.

Tips for Managing Asian Hair

Here are some tips for managing Asian hair:

1. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it more prone to frizz and breakage. Aim to wash your hair every other day or every three days.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Using a wide-tooth comb can help to prevent breakage and tangles. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots.

3. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Using heat tools like blow dryers and straightening irons can damage your hair over time. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray before using these tools, and try to limit your use of them as much as possible.

FAQs

1. Can I Use a Keratin Treatment on Asian Hair?

Yes, a keratin treatment can be a great option for Asian hair. Just be sure to choose a treatment that is formulated for your hair type.

2. How Often Should I Wash My Hair?

It's best to wash your hair every other day or every three days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.

Hair Care for Asian Hair

Here are some additional tips for caring for your Asian hair:

1. Use a Satin Pillowcase

Using a satin pillowcase can help to prevent breakage and frizz while you sleep.

2. Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help to keep your hair soft and healthy. Aim to deep condition your hair once a week or once every other week.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

UV rays can damage your hair just like they can damage your skin. Be sure to wear a hat or use a UV protectant spray when you're out in the sun for extended periods of time.

Conclusion

With the right products and techniques, you can make your Asian hair lie flat and stay in place. Try out some of the tips we've outlined here and see what works best for you. And don't forget to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong!
