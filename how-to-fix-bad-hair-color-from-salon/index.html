How To Fix Bad Hair Color From Salon A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jun 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Have you ever walked out of a salon with a hair color that you absolutely hate? Whether it's too brassy, too dark, or just not what you were expecting, a bad hair color can really ruin your day. Luckily, there are steps you can take to fix a bad hair color from a salon. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to fix your hair color and get the look you want.

Description: What is Bad Hair Color from Salon?

Bad hair color from a salon can happen for a number of reasons. Sometimes the colorist doesn't understand what you're looking for, or they might not have the skills to achieve the look you want. Other times, the color might turn out differently than expected due to the condition of your hair, the products used, or even the lighting in the salon.

No matter what the cause, a bad hair color can be frustrating and disappointing. But don't worry - there are ways to fix it!

How to: Fixing Bad Hair Color from Salon

Step 1: Don't Panic

The first step to fixing a bad hair color is to stay calm. It's easy to get upset or angry when you don't like the way your hair looks, but getting emotional won't help the situation. Take a deep breath and remind yourself that this is a fixable problem.

Step 2: Contact Your Salon

If you don't like your hair color, the first thing you should do is contact your salon. Explain the situation to them and ask if they can fix it. Most salons will be happy to correct a color mistake, especially if it's something that can be easily fixed.

If you're not comfortable going back to the same salon, you can always go to a different one for a color correction. Just make sure you do your research and find a salon that has experience with color corrections.

Step 3: Assess the Damage

Before you can fix your hair color, you need to assess the damage. Take a good look at your hair in natural light and decide what it is that you don't like about the color. Is it too dark? Too light? Too brassy? Once you know what the problem is, you can start to work on a solution.

Step 4: Decide on the Corrective Color

Based on the damage assessment, you need to decide on the corrective color. If your hair is too dark, you might need to lighten it. If it's too light, you might need to darken it. If it's too brassy, you might need to tone it. Consult a professional colorist if you're unsure what color to use.

Step 5: Prep Your Hair

Before you start applying the corrective color, you need to prep your hair. This means washing it with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup and oils that might interfere with the color. You should also deep condition your hair to help restore moisture and prevent damage.

Step 6: Apply the Corrective Color

Once your hair is prepped, it's time to apply the corrective color. Follow the instructions on the box or consult a professional colorist if you're not sure how to apply the color. Make sure to apply the color evenly and thoroughly, and be sure to cover all of your hair.

Step 7: Rinse and Condition

After you've applied the corrective color, rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water. Use a color-safe conditioner to help lock in the color and prevent fading. Be sure to rinse your hair well, as leftover color can cause damage and staining.

Step 8: Monitor Your Hair

Once you've fixed your hair color, it's important to monitor it for any further damage or fading. Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using hot tools or exposing your hair to direct sunlight. You might also want to schedule regular appointments with a professional colorist to maintain your new color.

Tips for Fixing Bad Hair Color from Salon

  • Don't try to fix bad hair color on your own - consult a professional colorist.
  • Be patient - fixing bad hair color can take time.
  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to help maintain your new color.
  • Avoid using hot tools or exposing your hair to direct sunlight, as this can cause damage and fading.

FAQs About Fixing Bad Hair Color from Salon

Q: Can I fix bad hair color on my own?

A: It's not recommended to try to fix bad hair color on your own, as this can cause further damage and make the problem worse. Consult a professional colorist for help.

Q: How long does it take to fix bad hair color?

A: Fixing bad hair color can take time, depending on the severity of the damage and the corrective measures needed. Be patient and follow the steps outlined in this article.

Q: How can I prevent bad hair color in the future?

A: To prevent bad hair color in the future, be clear and specific with your colorist about what you want. Bring in pictures or examples if possible. Also, make sure to go to a reputable salon with experienced colorists.

Hair Care: Maintaining Your New Color

Once you've fixed your bad hair color, it's important to maintain your new color with proper hair care. Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using hot tools or exposing your hair to direct sunlight. You might also want to schedule regular appointments with a professional colorist to maintain your new color.

Fixing bad hair color from a salon can be frustrating, but it's not the end of the world. By following the steps outlined in this article and consulting a professional colorist, you can get the hair color you want and deserve. Good luck!
