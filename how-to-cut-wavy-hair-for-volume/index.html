How To Cut Wavy Hair For Volume A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Nov 23, 2023 · 4 min read

Wavy hair can be a blessing and a curse all at once. While it has natural volume, it can be difficult to style and maintain. That's where a good haircut comes in. By cutting your wavy hair in the right way, you can enhance its natural volume and create a low-maintenance style that looks great all the time. In this blog post, we'll take you through a step-by-step guide on how to cut wavy hair for volume.

Description

Wavy hair can be tricky to manage, but it's also one of the most versatile hair types out there. With the right cut, you can create a low-maintenance style that looks great all the time. In this step-by-step guide, we'll show you how to cut wavy hair for volume.

How to Cut Wavy Hair for Volume: Step by Step

Step 1: Choose the Right Length

The first step in cutting wavy hair for volume is to choose the right length. For wavy hair, a longer length is usually better since it allows the waves to show off their natural texture. However, you don't want to go too long or it can become weighed down and lose volume.

Step 2: Layer Your Hair

The next step is to layer your hair. This will help create volume and movement throughout your hair. Ask your stylist to cut your hair in long, loose layers that start at your chin and go down. This will create a natural-looking shape that enhances your waves.

Step 3: Add Texture

To further enhance your waves, you'll want to add texture to your hair. This can be done through point cutting, which involves cutting the ends of your hair at an angle. This will create a softer, more textured look that enhances your waves.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Once your hair is cut, it's time to style it. To create volume, use a volumizing mousse or spray on damp hair. Then, blow dry your hair using a diffuser attachment. This will help enhance your waves and create natural-looking volume.

Tips for Cutting Wavy Hair for Volume

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting wavy hair for volume:

  • Choose a stylist who has experience working with wavy hair.
  • Start with a longer length and gradually work your way shorter.
  • Ask your stylist to cut your hair in long, loose layers.
  • Add texture to your hair through point cutting.
  • Use a volumizing mousse or spray to enhance your waves.
  • Blow dry your hair with a diffuser attachment to create natural-looking volume.

FAQ

Can I cut my wavy hair myself?

We do not recommend cutting your wavy hair yourself. It's best to see a professional stylist who has experience working with wavy hair. They can help you choose the right length and style for your hair type and create a cut that enhances your natural waves.

How often should I cut my wavy hair?

It's recommended to get a haircut every 8-12 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

Hair Care for Wavy Hair

Wavy hair requires some special care to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

Conclusion

Cutting wavy hair for volume can be a great way to enhance your natural waves and create a low-maintenance style that looks great all the time. By following the steps outlined in this guide and taking good care of your hair, you can have healthy, voluminous waves that turn heads wherever you go.
