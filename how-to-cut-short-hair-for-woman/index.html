How To Cut Short Hair For Women A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Nov 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your hair short can be a liberating experience. Not only is it a stylish and trendy look, but it also saves you time and money on hair products and styling. However, cutting short hair can be tricky, especially if you're doing it yourself. In this article, we'll guide you on how to cut short hair for women, step-by-step.

Description

Before we get started, let's define what short hair means. Short hair refers to any haircut that falls above the shoulders. It's a versatile and low-maintenance style that can suit any face shape and hair type. Whether you're looking for a pixie cut, bob, or lob, cutting your hair short requires some basic knowledge and tools.

How To

Step One: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. If you're using clippers, make sure they're fully charged and have different guard sizes for length options.

Step Two: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair before cutting it. Wet hair is easier to cut, and it gives you a better idea of how your hair will look when it's dry. If you have curly hair, consider cutting it when it's dry to avoid shrinkage.

Step Three: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: the top, left, right, and back. Use hair clips to secure each section. Start with the back section and work your way to the front.

Step Four: Cut the Back Section

Start with the back section and cut it to your desired length. Use a comb to hold your hair in place and cut it straight across. If you're using clippers, start with the longest guard size and work your way down to a shorter length. Blend the sides and the back by using a blending shear or scissors.

Step Five: Cut the Side Sections

Once you've cut the back section, move on to the side sections. Use the length of the back section as a guide and cut the sides accordingly. Hold your hair at a 45-degree angle and cut it diagonally for a softer look.

Step Six: Cut the Top Section

Finally, move on to the top section. Use your fingers to hold your hair up and cut it to your desired length. You can cut it straight across for a blunt look or use scissors to create layers for a textured look.

Tips

  • Start with a longer length and work your way down if you're not sure how short you want to go.
  • Invest in a good pair of scissors and clippers.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head.
  • When in doubt, consult a professional hairstylist.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut short hair with regular scissors?
A: While you can technically use regular scissors to cut your hair, it's not recommended. Regular scissors can damage your hair and create split ends. It's best to use sharp hair-cutting scissors.

Q: How often should I cut my short hair?
A: It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. On average, you should cut your short hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and style.

Hair Care

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and use a heat protectant spray when you do.

Cutting short hair for women can be a fun and empowering experience. With the right tools and knowledge, you can achieve a stylish and low-maintenance look. Just remember to take care of your hair and consult a professional hairstylist if you're not sure how to cut it.
