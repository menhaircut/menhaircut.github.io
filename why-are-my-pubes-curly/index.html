Why Are My Pubes Curly Explained In Simple Terms

Written by Petter Nov 08, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you one of those people who have curly pubic hair? You're not alone. Many people have curly pubes, and it is completely normal. In this blog post, we'll explain why your pubic hair is curly and provide tips for hair care.

What Causes Pubic Hair to be Curly?

Pubic hair is curly because of the shape of the hair follicle. The hair follicles in the pubic area are oval-shaped, which causes the hair that grows out of them to be curly. The shape of the hair follicle is determined by genetics, which means that if your parents have curly pubic hair, you're more likely to have it too.

Is Curly Pubic Hair Normal?

Yes, curly pubic hair is completely normal. In fact, most people have curly pubic hair to some extent. It's just another variation of human hair, like straight or wavy hair.

How to Take Care of Curly Pubic Hair

Curly pubic hair requires a little extra care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

1. Keep it Clean

It's important to keep your pubic hair clean to prevent bacteria from building up. Use a gentle cleanser and warm water to wash your pubic hair. Avoid using harsh soaps or shampoos, as they can dry out the hair and cause it to become brittle.

2. Condition Regularly

Conditioning your pubic hair can help keep it moisturized and prevent it from becoming dry or brittle. Use a conditioner specifically designed for curly hair, and leave it in for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools, such as blow dryers or flat irons, can damage curly hair and cause it to become frizzy. Avoid using these tools on your pubic hair, and let it air dry instead.

4. Trim It Regularly

Regular trims can help keep your pubic hair looking neat and prevent split ends. Use scissors specifically designed for trimming pubic hair, and be careful not to cut yourself.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is Curly Pubic Hair More Prone to Ingrown Hairs?

A: Yes, curly pubic hair is more prone to ingrown hairs because of the way it grows out of the follicle. To prevent ingrown hairs, use a gentle exfoliating scrub on your pubic area, and avoid tight clothing that can rub against the skin.

Q: Can I Straighten My Pubic Hair?

A: It is not recommended to straighten your pubic hair, as the heat can damage the hair and cause it to become brittle. If you want to change the appearance of your pubic hair, consider trimming it or shaping it instead.

Q: Is It Normal to Have Different Hair Types on My Body?

A: Yes, it is completely normal to have different hair types on different parts of your body. For example, you may have curly pubic hair but straight hair on your head. This is just another example of the natural variation in human hair.

The Bottom Line

Curly pubic hair is completely normal and is determined by genetics. If you have curly pubic hair, take care of it by keeping it clean, conditioning it regularly, avoiding heat styling, and getting regular trims. If you have any concerns about your pubic hair, talk to your doctor or a dermatologist.
