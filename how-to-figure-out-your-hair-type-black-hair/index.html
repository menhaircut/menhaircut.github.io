How To Figure Out Your Hair Type Black Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Petter Nov 22, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you struggling to figure out your hair type? If you have black hair, it can be especially difficult to determine which hair type you have since there are so many variations. However, understanding your hair type is crucial for proper hair care and styling. In this article, we’ll cover everything you need to know about how to figure out your hair type black hair.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Determine Your Hair Type
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Identifying Your Hair Type
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care Based on Your Hair Type

Description

Before we dive into how to figure out your hair type black hair, let’s first talk about what hair type actually means. Hair type refers to the shape and pattern of your hair strands. There are four main hair types: straight, wavy, curly, and coily. Each hair type has its own unique characteristics and requires different care and styling techniques.

If you have black hair, you may fall into one of these four categories or somewhere in between. Black hair can range from fine and straight to thick and coily, which can make it challenging to determine your hair type. However, with the right techniques and knowledge, you can figure out your hair type and create a hair care routine that works best for you.

How to Determine Your Hair Type

The best way to determine your hair type is by examining your hair strands when they are completely dry and free of any styling products. Here are a few things to look for:

1. Straight Hair

Straight hair is characterized by hair strands that are completely flat and have no curl or wave pattern. If your hair falls straight down from your scalp without any curl or wave, you likely have straight hair.

2. Wavy Hair

Wavy hair is characterized by hair strands that have a slight S-shaped wave pattern. If your hair has a gentle wave pattern, especially towards the ends of your hair, you likely have wavy hair.

3. Curly Hair

Curly hair is characterized by hair strands that have a distinct curl pattern. If your hair has well-defined curls that range from loose to tight, you likely have curly hair.

4. Coily Hair

Coily hair is characterized by hair strands that have a tight curl pattern or coils that range from tight to super tight. If your hair has a zigzag pattern or coils tightly around itself, you likely have coily hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

To determine your hair type, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Make sure to rinse your hair thoroughly and gently pat it dry with a towel.

Step 2: Let Your Hair Air Dry

Allow your hair to air dry completely without any styling products. Don’t use a blow dryer or any other heat styling tools.

Step 3: Examine Your Hair

Examine your hair strands to see if they fall into any of the categories we mentioned earlier: straight, wavy, curly, or coily. Pay attention to the shape and pattern of your hair strands.

Step 4: Determine Your Hair Type

Based on your observations, determine which hair type you have. Keep in mind that you may have a combination of two or more hair types.

Tips for Identifying Your Hair Type

Here are a few additional tips to help you identify your hair type:

1. Look at Old Photos

Take a look at old photos of yourself to see how your hair has changed over time. This can help you determine whether your hair has always been a certain type or if it has changed over time.

2. Ask for Help

If you’re still unsure about your hair type, ask a stylist or hair care professional for help. They can examine your hair and provide you with advice on how to care for it.

3. Use a Hair Typing System

There are several hair typing systems available online that can help you determine your hair type. These systems typically use a number or letter system to categorize different hair types based on their characteristics.

FAQs

Q: Can my hair type change over time?

A: Yes, your hair type can change over time due to factors such as hormonal changes, medication, and age.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people with coily hair may only need to wash their hair once a week, while others with straight hair may need to wash their hair every other day.

Q: Do I need to use different hair care products based on my hair type?

A: Yes, using hair care products that are specifically designed for your hair type can help keep your hair healthy and looking its best.

Hair Care Based on Your Hair Type

Once you’ve determined your hair type, it’s important to use hair care products and styling techniques that are best suited for your hair. Here are a few tips:

1. Straight Hair

Use lightweight hair care products and avoid heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair. Consider using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body to your hair.

2. Wavy Hair

Use hair care products that enhance your natural wave pattern and add definition to your hair. Consider using a curl defining cream or mousse.

3. Curly Hair

Use hair care products that are moisturizing and help define your curls. Consider using a leave-in conditioner and a curl enhancing gel.

4. Coily Hair

Use hair care products that are rich and moisturizing to help prevent breakage and maintain your hair’s natural oils. Consider using a deep conditioning treatment and a heavy styling cream or butter.

By understanding your hair type and using the right hair care products and techniques, you can keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.
