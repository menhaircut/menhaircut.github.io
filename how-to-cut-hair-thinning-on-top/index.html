How To Cut Hair Thinning On Top A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 10, 2023 · 5 min read

Thinning hair on top can be a distressing experience for many people. Whether you are dealing with male or female pattern baldness, or just a general thinning of the hair, it’s important to know how to cut your hair in a way that will flatter your face and make you feel confident. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps of how to cut hair thinning on top, along with some tips and FAQs to help you get the best results.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Hair Thinning on Top
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Hair Thinning on Top
  • FAQs about Cutting Hair Thinning on Top
  • Hair Care for Thinning Hair

How to Cut Hair Thinning on Top

Cutting hair thinning on top requires a bit of finesse, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a look that will make you feel confident and stylish. Here are some key steps to keep in mind.

Choose the Right Cut

The first step in cutting hair thinning on top is to choose the right cut. A shorter cut can help to disguise thinning hair, while a longer cut can make hair look more voluminous. Talk to your stylist about what cut would work best for your hair type and face shape.

Use the Right Tools

When cutting hair thinning on top, it’s important to use the right tools. A sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and a mirror are all essential. You may also want to invest in clippers or a razor for more precise cuts.

Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Before you begin cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to cut and will give you a better idea of how the final style will look. If you have curly hair, consider straightening it before you begin.

Take it Slow

When cutting hair thinning on top, it’s important to take it slow. Work in small sections and trim a little bit at a time. This will help you avoid cutting too much and will give you more control over the final result.

Blend the Edges

To create a natural-looking cut, it’s important to blend the edges. Use a comb to blend the hair around the edges of the cut, creating a seamless transition between the shorter and longer sections.

Check Your Progress

As you cut, check your progress in the mirror. This will help you make sure you are cutting evenly and will give you a better idea of how the final style will look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the key steps for cutting hair thinning on top, here is a step-by-step guide to help you achieve the look you want.

Step 1: Choose the Right Cut

Start by talking to your stylist about what cut will work best for your hair type and face shape. A shorter cut can help to disguise thinning hair, while a longer cut can make hair look more voluminous.

Step 2: Gather Your Tools

Make sure you have a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and a mirror. You may also want to invest in clippers or a razor for more precise cuts.

Step 3: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you begin cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. If you have curly hair, consider straightening it before you begin.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Use clips to section your hair, starting with the top section. Work in small sections and trim a little bit at a time.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Begin cutting the top section of your hair, working from the front to the back. Take it slow and work in small sections, trimming a little bit at a time.

Step 6: Blend the Edges

Use a comb to blend the hair around the edges of the cut, creating a seamless transition between the shorter and longer sections.

Step 7: Check Your Progress

As you cut, check your progress in the mirror. This will help you make sure you are cutting evenly and will give you a better idea of how the final style will look.

Tips for Cutting Hair Thinning on Top

Here are some additional tips to help you get the best results when cutting hair thinning on top:

  • Consider a shorter cut to help disguise thinning hair.
  • Use clippers or a razor for more precise cuts.
  • Work in small sections and trim a little bit at a time.
  • Blend the edges to create a natural-looking cut.
  • Check your progress in the mirror to make sure you are cutting evenly.

FAQs about Cutting Hair Thinning on Top

Q: Can I cut my own hair if it’s thinning on top?

A: Yes, you can cut your own hair if it’s thinning on top. Just make sure you have the right tools and take it slow.

Q: What cut works best for thinning hair?

A: A shorter cut can help to disguise thinning hair, while a longer cut can make hair look more voluminous.

Q: How often should I cut my hair if it’s thinning on top?

A: It’s a good idea to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks if it’s thinning on top. This will help to keep it looking tidy and well-maintained.

Hair Care for Thinning Hair

In addition to cutting your hair, there are a few things you can do to care for thinning hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo to add body to your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools, as they can damage hair and make it look thinner.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

Conclusion

Cutting hair thinning on top may seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a look that will make you feel confident and stylish. Remember to choose the right cut, use the right tools, and take it slow. And don’t forget to care for your hair with a healthy diet and gentle hair care products.
