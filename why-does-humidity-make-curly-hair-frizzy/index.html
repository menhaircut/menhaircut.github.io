Why Does Humidity Make Curly Hair Frizzy

Written by Petter Nov 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse at the same time. While it looks amazing on good hair days, it can turn into a frizzy mess on humid days. If you are someone who struggles with frizzy hair, you might be wondering why does humidity make curly hair frizzy? In this blog post, we will explain the science behind frizzy hair and share some tips on how to manage it.

Table of Content

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by step
  4. Tips

Description

Humidity is the amount of moisture present in the air. When the air is humid, it can penetrate the hair shaft, causing the hair to absorb moisture. Curly hair is naturally more porous than straight hair, which means it absorbs moisture more easily. When curly hair absorbs moisture from the air, the hair cuticles swell and lift, causing the hair to become frizzy and unmanageable. This is why humidity makes curly hair frizzy.

How to

If you have curly hair, you might be wondering how to manage frizz on humid days. Here are some tips:

Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner

Sulfates can strip the hair of its natural oils, which can make curly hair more prone to frizz. Look for a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that can help hydrate and nourish your hair.

Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can damage the hair and make it more prone to frizz. Instead, embrace your natural curls and use styling products that can help define and enhance them.

Use a leave-in conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help hydrate and nourish your hair, which can make it less prone to frizz. Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner to damp hair and distribute it evenly throughout your hair.

Step by step

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to manage frizz on humid days:

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel dry your hair gently.
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner to damp hair.
  4. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  5. Apply a curl-enhancing product to define your curls.
  6. Avoid touching your hair while it dries.
  7. Once your hair is dry, use a serum or oil to smooth any frizz or flyaways.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to manage frizz on humid days:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry. This can cause the hair to become frizzy.
  • Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel. This can help reduce frizz.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as it can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles, such as tight ponytails or braids. This can cause tension on the hair, which can lead to breakage and frizz.

FAQ

Q: Can I use a hair straightener to manage frizz on humid days?

A: While a hair straightener can help smooth out frizz, it can also damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. It's best to embrace your natural curls and use styling products that can help define and enhance them.

Q: Can I use a hair mask to manage frizz on humid days?

A: Yes, a hair mask can help hydrate and nourish your hair, which can make it less prone to frizz. Apply a hair mask once a week to help keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for managing frizz on humid days. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your hair hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as hair dryers and curling irons, as they can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Trim your hair regularly to help prevent split ends and breakage.

By following these tips and taking proper care of your hair, you can manage frizz on humid days and enjoy beautiful, defined curls.
