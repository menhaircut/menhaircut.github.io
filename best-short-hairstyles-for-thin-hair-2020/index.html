Best Short Hairstyles For Thin Hair 2020

Written by Luffy Jun 15, 2023 · 6 min read

If you have thin hair, you might think that your hairstyle options are limited. However, that couldn't be further from the truth. Short hairstyles are a great way to add body and volume to thin hair, and there are plenty of options to choose from. In this article, we'll go over the best short hairstyles for thin hair in 2020.

How to Choose the Best Short Hairstyle for Thin Hair

When it comes to choosing the best short hairstyle for thin hair, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, you want to choose a style that adds volume and body to your hair. Second, you want to choose a style that complements your face shape. And finally, you want to choose a style that you feel comfortable and confident in. Here are some tips for choosing the best short hairstyle for thin hair:

  • Look for styles with layers, as they can add volume and movement to thin hair.
  • Avoid styles that are too blunt, as they can make thin hair look even thinner.
  • Consider adding bangs to your hairstyle, as they can frame your face and add volume to the top of your head.
  • Don't be afraid to play with color, as highlights and lowlights can add depth and dimension to thin hair.

The Pixie Cut

The pixie cut is a classic short hairstyle that can work well for those with thin hair. This style involves cutting your hair short on the sides and back, while leaving the top longer. The longer top can be styled in a variety of ways, such as slicked back or tousled, to add volume and body to thin hair.

How to Style a Pixie Cut for Thin Hair:

  1. Apply a volumizing mousse to your damp hair.
  2. Blow dry your hair with a round brush, lifting at the roots to add volume.
  3. Use a styling cream or wax to add texture and definition to your hair.

The Bob

The bob is another classic short hairstyle that can work well for those with thin hair. This style involves cutting your hair short to medium length and adding layers to create volume and movement.

How to Style a Bob for Thin Hair:

  1. Apply a volumizing spray to your damp hair.
  2. Blow dry your hair with a round brush, focusing on the roots to add volume.
  3. Use a curling iron or flat iron to add texture and movement to your hair.

The Lob

The lob, or long bob, is a great option for those who want a slightly longer hairstyle. This style involves cutting your hair to shoulder-length or just above, and adding layers to create volume and movement.

How to Style a Lob for Thin Hair:

  1. Apply a volumizing mousse to your damp hair.
  2. Blow dry your hair with a round brush, lifting at the roots to add volume.
  3. Use a curling iron or flat iron to add texture and movement to your hair.

The Shag

The shag is a fun and edgy hairstyle that can work well for those with thin hair. This style involves cutting your hair into layers of varying lengths, with the shortest layers framing your face.

How to Style a Shag for Thin Hair:

  1. Apply a volumizing spray to your damp hair.
  2. Blow dry your hair with a round brush, focusing on the roots to add volume.
  3. Use a styling cream or wax to add texture and definition to your hair.

The Faux Hawk

The faux hawk is a trendy and bold hairstyle that can work well for those with thin hair. This style involves cutting your hair short on the sides and back, while leaving the top longer and styled upwards.

How to Style a Faux Hawk for Thin Hair:

  1. Apply a styling gel or pomade to your damp hair.
  2. Blow dry your hair with your fingers, lifting at the roots to add volume.
  3. Use a flat iron to add texture and definition to your hair.

Bangs

Bangs are a great way to add volume and frame your face when you have thin hair. There are a variety of bang styles to choose from, including blunt, side-swept, and wispy.

How to Style Bangs for Thin Hair:

  1. Apply a volumizing spray to your damp hair.
  2. Blow dry your hair with a round brush, focusing on your bangs to add volume.
  3. Use a flat iron to add texture and definition to your bangs.

Layers

Adding layers to your short hairstyle can help create volume and movement. When you have thin hair, it's important to choose layers that are subtle and blend well with the rest of your hair.

How to Style Layers for Thin Hair:

  1. Apply a volumizing mousse to your damp hair.
  2. Blow dry your hair with a round brush, lifting at the roots to add volume.
  3. Use a curling iron or flat iron to add texture and movement to your hair.

Color

Playing with color can be a great way to add depth and dimension to your thin hair. Highlights and lowlights can create the illusion of thicker hair, while bold colors can add a fun and edgy touch to your hairstyle.

How to Choose the Right Color for Thin Hair:

  • Choose a color that complements your skin tone.
  • Avoid colors that are too harsh or brassy, as they can make thin hair look even thinner.
  • Consider adding highlights or lowlights to create depth and dimension.

Tips for Styling Thin Hair

When it comes to styling thin hair, there are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body to your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Blow dry your hair upside down to add volume at the roots.
  • Use a round brush to lift your hair at the roots while blow drying.
  • Consider using a dry shampoo to add texture and volume to your hair.

FAQs

Q: Can I still have bangs with thin hair?

A: Yes, bangs can be a great way to add volume and frame your face when you have thin hair. Just be sure to choose a style that complements your face shape and hair texture.

Q: Will adding layers make my thin hair look thinner?

A: It's possible that adding too many layers or layers that are too blunt can make thin hair look thinner. However, subtle layers that blend well with the rest of your hair can help create volume and movement.
