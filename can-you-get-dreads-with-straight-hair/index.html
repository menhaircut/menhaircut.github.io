Can You Get Dreads With Straight Hair

Written by Luffy Jul 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have straight hair and you've been contemplating getting dreadlocks, you might be wondering if it's even possible. The answer is yes, you can get dreads with straight hair! In this blog post, we'll go over everything you need to know about getting dreads with straight hair, including how to do it, step-by-step instructions, tips for maintaining your dreads, and more.

Description

Dreadlocks, also known as dreads or locs, are a hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are created by twisting or matting the hair into long, rope-like coils. While dreadlocks are often associated with people of African descent, people of all races and ethnicities can wear them. However, if you have naturally straight hair, it may take a little more work to get your hair to lock up.

How to Get Dreads with Straight Hair

The process of getting dreads with straight hair is called "dreading." It involves sectioning the hair, backcombing or twisting the sections, and allowing the hair to naturally lock up over time. There are several methods for dreading straight hair, including:

Backcombing

Backcombing involves teasing the hair backwards to create knots and tangles. This method requires a lot of patience and time, but it can be very effective for straight hair. Once the hair has been backcombed, it can be palm rolled to help the knots tighten and lock up.

Twisting

Twisting involves dividing the hair into sections and twisting each section until it locks up. This method is less harsh on the hair than backcombing, but it can take longer to achieve the desired look. Twisting can be done with or without the use of hair products.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here are the basic steps for getting dreads with straight hair:

  1. Wash your hair thoroughly and let it air dry.
  2. Section your hair into small, even sections using hair ties or clips.
  3. Backcomb or twist each section of hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  4. Use a dread wax or locking gel to help the knots tighten and lock up.
  5. Continue to backcomb or twist your hair regularly, at least once a week, until your dreads are fully locked up.
  6. Once your dreads are fully locked up, you can trim any loose or frizzy ends to keep them looking neat.

Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads

Here are some tips for maintaining your dreads:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but be gentle to avoid loosening the knots.
  • Use a residue-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent buildup.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your dreads.
  • If your dreads start to feel too tight or uncomfortable, you can gently loosen them with your fingers or a crochet hook.
  • Be patient - it can take several months or even years for your dreads to fully mature.

FAQ

Can you get dreads with short hair?

Yes, you can get dreads with short hair. However, it may take longer for your hair to fully lock up, and you may need to start with smaller sections of hair.

How long does it take to get dreads with straight hair?

The time it takes to get dreads with straight hair can vary depending on your hair type, the method you use, and how well you maintain your dreads. It can take anywhere from several months to several years for your dreads to fully mature.

Do you need to use products to get dreads with straight hair?

No, you don't need to use products to get dreads with straight hair. However, using a dread wax or locking gel can help the knots tighten and lock up more quickly.

Hair Care for Dreads

Once you have dreads, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips for hair care for dreads:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but be gentle to avoid loosening the knots.
  • Use a residue-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent buildup.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your dreads.
  • Use a crochet hook to gently remove any loose hairs or buildup from your dreads.
  • Protect your dreads from sun damage by wearing a hat or scarf.

With these tips and a little patience, you can successfully get dreads with straight hair and enjoy this unique and stylish hairstyle.
