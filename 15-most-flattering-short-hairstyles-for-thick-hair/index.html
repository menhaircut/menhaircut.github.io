15 Most Flattering Short Hairstyles For Thick Hair

1. Pixie Cut

The pixie cut is a classic short hairstyle that works well with thick hair. This cut is characterized by short layers all around the head, with longer pieces on top that can be styled in different ways. It’s a low-maintenance hairstyle that looks great on anyone.

How to:

To achieve the pixie cut, your stylist will cut your hair short all around the head, leaving longer pieces on top. You can style the longer pieces with a side part or by slicking them back with some hair gel.

Tips:

  • If you have a round face, ask your stylist to create some height on top to elongate your face.
  • If you have a square face, ask for a softer, more textured cut to balance out your features.
  • Use a small amount of hair product to style your hair, as too much can make it look greasy.

FAQ:

  • Will a pixie cut work with curly hair?

    • Yes, a pixie cut can work well with curly hair. Just make sure to ask your stylist for a cut that will work with your natural curl pattern.

  • How often do I need to get a trim?

    • You should get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your pixie cut looking fresh.

Hair Care:

To keep your pixie cut looking its best, use a shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for short hair. You can also use a hair oil to add shine and prevent frizz.

2. Bob Cut

The bob cut is a classic short hairstyle that works well with thick hair. This cut is characterized by a blunt cut at the bottom, with layers throughout to add movement and texture. It’s a versatile hairstyle that can be styled in different ways.

How to:

To achieve the bob cut, your stylist will cut your hair to a length that falls just above your shoulders. They will then add layers throughout to add movement and texture. You can style your bob with a middle or side part, or with some loose waves.

Tips:

  • If you have a round face, ask your stylist for a longer bob to elongate your face.
  • If you have a square face, ask for a textured bob to balance out your features.
  • Use a texturizing spray to add volume and movement to your bob.

FAQ:

Hair Care:
