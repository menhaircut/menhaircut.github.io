How To Use Thinning Shears On Naturally Curly Hair

Written by Luffy Sep 13, 2023 · 3 min read

Thinning shears are a great tool for those with naturally curly hair. They can help to remove bulk and add texture to your curls, giving you a more polished look. However, it's important to use them correctly to avoid damaging your hair. In this post, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to use thinning shears on naturally curly hair.

Description

Thinning shears are scissors that have teeth or notches on one blade. They are used to remove bulk from hair without affecting the length. Thinning shears can be used on any hair type, but they are particularly useful for those with naturally curly hair. Curly hair tends to be thicker and more voluminous, and thinning shears can help to reduce this volume and add texture to your curls.

How to Use Thinning Shears on Naturally Curly Hair

Follow these steps to use thinning shears on your naturally curly hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that are suitable for your hair type. After washing, gently towel-dry your hair.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. This will help to ensure that your curls are evenly distributed and that there are no knots or tangles.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don't miss any areas.

Step 4: Start Thinning

Starting from the bottom section of your hair, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use the thinning shears to cut the section of hair. Be sure to only cut about 1/4 to 1/2 inch of hair at a time.

Step 5: Move Upwards

Continue to work your way upwards, taking small sections of hair and using the thinning shears to cut them. Be sure to keep the shears at an angle to avoid creating blunt lines.

Step 6: Check Your Progress

After you've finished thinning your hair, check your progress. Make sure that your hair is evenly thinned and that there are no blunt lines or areas that are too thin.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired. You should notice that your curls have more texture and are less bulky.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning shears:

  • Use good quality thinning shears to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Be sure to only cut a small amount of hair at a time to avoid creating blunt lines.
  • Use the thinning shears sparingly to avoid thinning your hair too much.
  • Be careful when using thinning shears near your scalp to avoid cutting your skin.

FAQ

Can thinning shears be used on all hair types?

Yes, thinning shears can be used on all hair types. However, they are particularly useful for those with naturally curly hair.

Can thinning shears damage my hair?

Thinning shears can damage your hair if they are not used correctly. Be sure to only cut a small amount of hair at a time and use good quality shears.

Hair Care

Using thinning shears on your naturally curly hair can be a great way to add texture and reduce bulk. However, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy. Be sure to use a good quality shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. You should also deep condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized.
