How To Cut Layers To Make Hair Look Thicker A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 11, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you tired of your limp, lifeless hair? Do you wish you had more volume and thickness? If so, then cutting layers into your hair may be the solution you’ve been searching for. In this article, we’ll provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut layers to make hair look thicker. We’ll also provide tips, answer frequently asked questions, and offer hair care advice to ensure your new haircut stays healthy and full of volume.

  2. Step-by-Step Guide

How to Cut Layers to Make Hair Look Thicker

The key to making hair look thicker is to add layers. Layers create movement and volume in the hair, making it appear fuller and more voluminous. The process of cutting layers into your hair is relatively simple and can be done at home or by a professional stylist. Here are the basic steps:

1. Choose Your Length and Layers

Before you start cutting, decide how long you want your hair to be and how many layers you want. The longer your hair, the more layers you can add without it looking too choppy. If you have short hair, focus on adding layers towards the top of your head to create volume.

2. Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or ties. Start by separating the top section of your hair from the bottom. Then, divide the top section into two or three smaller sections, depending on how much hair you have. This will make it easier to cut each layer evenly.

3. Begin Cutting

Start by cutting the bottom layer. Hold a small section of hair between your fingers and cut it straight across. This will be your guide for the rest of the layers. Work your way up, cutting each layer slightly shorter than the one before it. Be sure to cut in a straight line and avoid cutting too much at once.

4. Blend the Layers

Once you’ve cut all the layers, blend them together by combing your hair and checking for any uneven spots. Use scissors to trim any sections that look too choppy or uneven.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you’re still unsure about how to cut layers to make hair look thicker, here’s a more detailed step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and towel-drying it. Wet hair is easier to cut and style, so make sure your hair is damp before you start cutting.

Step 2: Comb and Section Your Hair

Comb your hair to remove any tangles, then use clips or ties to section your hair. Divide your hair into two or three sections, depending on how thick it is.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Hold a small section of hair between your fingers and cut it straight across. This will be your guide for the rest of the layers. Work your way up, cutting each layer slightly shorter than the one before it. Be sure to cut in a straight line and avoid cutting too much at once.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you’ve cut all the layers, blend them together by combing your hair and checking for any uneven spots. Use scissors to trim any sections that look too choppy or uneven.

Tips for Cutting Layers to Make Hair Look Thicker

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting layers to make hair look thicker:

1. Use Sharp Scissors

Sharp scissors will make it easier to cut through your hair and will produce a cleaner, more precise cut. Dull scissors can result in split ends and uneven layers.

2. Cut Small Sections at a Time

Cutting too much at once can result in a choppy, uneven cut. Instead, cut small sections of hair at a time and work your way up.

3. Cut at an Angle

Cutting layers at an angle can create a more natural, textured look. To do this, hold the scissors at a 45-degree angle and cut into the hair.

4. Consider Face Shape

The type of layers you choose should complement your face shape. If you have a round face, focus on adding layers towards the top of your head to create height. If you have a square face, focus on adding layers around your jawline to soften your features.

FAQs

How often should I cut my hair to maintain thickness?

You should aim to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain thickness and prevent split ends.

Can I cut layers into curly hair?

Yes, you can cut layers into curly hair. However, be sure to cut the hair when it’s dry to ensure an even cut.

Should I use thinning shears to make my hair look thicker?

No, using thinning shears can actually make your hair look thinner. Instead, focus on adding layers to create volume.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Thick Hair

Once you’ve cut layers into your hair to make it look thicker, it’s important to take care of it to ensure it stays healthy and full of volume. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Thickening Shampoo and Conditioner

Look for products specifically designed to add volume and thickness to your hair.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools can damage your hair and make it look thinner. Try to air dry your hair whenever possible, or use a low-heat setting on your blow dryer.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

A wide-tooth comb is gentler on your hair and can help prevent breakage and split ends.

4. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun’s UV rays can damage your hair and make it look thinner. Wear a hat or use a UV-protectant spray to keep your hair healthy and full of volume.

By following these tips and using the steps outlined in this article, you can cut layers into your hair to make it look thicker and more voluminous. Remember to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and full of life.
