Is It Normal To Lose Hair When Growing It Out

Written by William Nov 08, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you currently growing out your hair but noticing more strands falling out than usual? It can be alarming to see more hair in your brush or on your shower floor, but is it normal to lose hair when growing it out? In this blog post, we will explore this question and provide you with some tips to care for your hair during the growing-out process.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Grow Out Your Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Growing Out Your Hair

Description

It is normal to lose up to 100 hairs per day, as our hair goes through a natural growth and shedding cycle. However, when growing out your hair, you may notice more hair loss than usual. This is because your hair is in a more fragile state and may be more prone to breakage. Additionally, if you are transitioning from a short hairstyle to a longer one, you may not have been aware of the amount of hair you were losing before.

How to Grow Out Your Hair

If you are looking to grow out your hair, there are a few steps you can take to encourage healthy growth:

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

Your hair needs nutrients to grow, so make sure you are eating a well-balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals.

2. Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water to keep your hair and scalp hydrated. Dehydration can lead to dry, brittle hair that is more prone to breakage.

3. Get Regular Trims

While it may seem counterintuitive, getting regular trims can actually help your hair grow longer. Trimming the ends of your hair can prevent split ends from traveling up the hair shaft and causing breakage.

4. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid harsh brushing, tight hairstyles, and excessive heat styling. These can all cause damage to your hair and lead to breakage.

Step-by-Step Guide to Growing Out Your Hair

If you are looking for a more detailed guide to growing out your hair, here are some steps you can follow:

1. Set Realistic Expectations

Growing out your hair takes time, so be patient and set realistic expectations for your hair growth. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month.

2. Choose a Hairstyle

Decide on the length and style you are aiming for so that you can plan your hair care routine accordingly.

3. Start with a Clean Slate

If you have damaged or over-processed hair, consider cutting it short and starting fresh. This will give your hair a chance to grow back healthy and strong.

4. Be Consistent with Your Hair Care Routine

Stick to a regular hair care routine that includes shampooing, conditioning, and moisturizing. Use products that are specifically designed for your hair type and avoid over-washing or over-styling your hair.

Tips for Growing Out Your Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you grow out your hair:

1. Protect Your Hair While You Sleep

Consider sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent breakage and tangles.

2. Experiment with Protective Hairstyles

Braids, twists, and buns can all help protect your hair from damage while you are growing it out.

3. Be Careful with Chemical Treatments

Avoid chemical treatments like perms, relaxers, and color treatments while you are growing out your hair, as these can all cause damage and breakage.

FAQs about Hair Loss and Growing Your Hair Out

1. Can stress cause hair loss?

Yes, stress can cause hair loss. When we are under stress, our body produces a hormone called cortisol, which can disrupt the normal growth cycle of our hair.

2. Will taking biotin help my hair grow?

Biotin is a B vitamin that is often marketed as a hair growth supplement. While some studies have shown that it may be beneficial for hair growth, there is not enough evidence to support its effectiveness for everyone.

3. Should I brush my hair when it is wet?

No, it is best to avoid brushing your hair when it is wet, as this can cause breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Growing Out Your Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips to help you grow out your hair:

1. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Once a week, use a deep conditioning treatment to nourish and hydrate your hair.

2. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can increase blood flow to your hair follicles and promote healthy hair growth.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles like ponytails and braids, as these can pull on your hair and cause damage.

In conclusion, it is normal to lose hair when growing it out, but there are steps you can take to minimize breakage and encourage healthy growth. By following a healthy hair care routine and being gentle with your hair, you can achieve the long, healthy locks you've been dreaming of.
