How To Style Edges On Natural Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Christine Jul 30, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have natural hair, you know that edges can be the most challenging part of your hair to style. Edges are the small baby hairs around your hairline that can add the finishing touch to your hairstyle. In this guide, we will show you how to style edges on natural hair, step by step.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Style Edges on Natural Hair
  2. Step by Step Guide
  3. Tips for Styling Edges on Natural Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care for Healthy Edges

How to Style Edges on Natural Hair

Styling edges on natural hair can seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve sleek, polished edges that complete any hairstyle. Here are some steps to follow:

1. Start with clean, moisturized hair

Before styling your edges, ensure that your hair is clean and moisturized. This will make it easier to manipulate your hair and create the style you want.

2. Apply edge control product

Choose an edge control product that works for your hair type. Apply the product to your edges using a small brush or your fingertips. Be sure to apply enough product to hold your edges in place without weighing them down.

3. Use a toothbrush or edge brush to smooth edges

Take a toothbrush or edge brush and use it to smooth your edges into the style you want. Start at the back of your hairline and work your way forward, smoothing the product into your hair as you go.

4. Secure edges with a scarf or headband

To keep your edges in place, wrap a scarf or headband around your head. This will help the product set and hold your style in place.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step by step guide on how to style edges on natural hair:

Step 1:

Start with clean, moisturized hair.

Step 2:

Choose an edge control product that works for your hair type.

Step 3:

Apply the edge control product to your edges using a small brush or your fingertips.

Step 4:

Use a toothbrush or edge brush to smooth your edges into the style you want.

Step 5:

Secure your edges with a scarf or headband and let the product set.

Step 6:

Remove the scarf or headband and style your hair as desired.

Tips for Styling Edges on Natural Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when styling your edges:

1. Use a small brush or your fingertips

When applying edge control product, use a small brush or your fingertips to ensure that you apply the product evenly and precisely.

2. Don’t use too much product

Using too much product can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy. Apply just enough product to hold your edges in place without weighing them down.

3. Use a scarf or headband

Wrapping a scarf or headband around your head can help the product set and hold your style in place.

4. Experiment with different products

Not all edge control products work for all hair types. Experiment with different products to find the one that works best for your hair.

FAQs

Q: Can I use gel to style my edges?

A: Yes, you can use gel to style your edges. However, gel may not work for all hair types and can leave your hair feeling stiff and crunchy.

Q: How often should I style my edges?

A: It’s best to style your edges only when necessary to avoid putting too much stress on your hairline. Try to limit styling to once or twice a week.

Q: Can I style my edges without using product?

A: Yes, you can style your edges without using product. However, using product can help hold your style in place and make it last longer.

Hair Care for Healthy Edges

Healthy edges start with a healthy hair care routine. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy edges:

1. Moisturize regularly

Dry hair is more prone to breakage, so it’s essential to moisturize your hair regularly. Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated.

2. Avoid tight hairstyles

Tight hairstyles like braids, weaves, and ponytails can put stress on your hairline, causing breakage and thinning. Avoid tight hairstyles as much as possible.

3. Protect your hair at night

Sleeping with a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase can help protect your hair from breakage and keep it hydrated.

4. Trim your ends regularly

Split ends can travel up the hair shaft and cause breakage, so it’s essential to trim your ends regularly to keep your hair healthy.

By following these tips and using the right edge control product, you can achieve sleek, polished edges that complete any natural hairstyle. Remember to take care of your hair by moisturizing regularly, avoiding tight hairstyles, protecting your hair at night, and trimming your ends regularly.
