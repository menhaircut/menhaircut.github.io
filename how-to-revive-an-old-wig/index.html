Reviving An Old Wig How To Bring Your Hairpiece Back To Life

Written by Petter Aug 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Wigs are a great way to change up your look without committing to a drastic haircut or dye job. However, with time and wear, even the best wigs can lose their lustre and become dull and lifeless. If you have an old wig that you love but it's looking a little worse for wear, don't worry - there are ways to revive it and bring it back to life. In this article, we'll go through step-by-step instructions on how to revive an old wig and give it a new lease on life.

How to Revive an Old Wig: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Detangle the Wig

The first thing you need to do is detangle the wig. Use a wide-tooth comb or wig brush to gently comb through the wig, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. Be gentle and patient, as wigs can easily become tangled and knotted.

Step 2: Wash the Wig

Next, you'll need to wash the wig to remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup. Fill a sink or basin with cool water and add a tablespoon of wig shampoo. Gently swish the wig around in the water, being careful not to rub or scrub it too hard. Rinse the wig thoroughly with cool water.

Step 3: Condition the Wig

After washing the wig, apply a small amount of wig conditioner to the hair, being careful to avoid the roots. Use your fingers to distribute the conditioner evenly throughout the hair. Let the conditioner sit for a few minutes, then rinse it out with cool water.

Step 4: Dry the Wig

Once you've washed and conditioned the wig, it's time to dry it. Gently blot the excess water from the wig with a towel, then place it on a wig stand or mannequin head to air dry. Avoid using a blow dryer, as the heat can damage the wig.

Step 5: Style the Wig

Once the wig is dry, it's time to style it. Use a wig brush or comb to gently style the hair, being careful not to tug or pull too hard. You can also use heat styling tools to create curls or waves, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

Tips for Reviving an Old Wig

Here are a few additional tips to help you revive an old wig:

  • Store your wig properly when you're not wearing it, either on a wig stand or in a box or bag.
  • Avoid using harsh or abrasive products on your wig, as they can damage the hair.
  • Don't sleep in your wig or wear it in the shower or pool, as this can cause tangling and damage.
  • If your wig has lost its shape, consider taking it to a professional wig stylist for reshaping.

FAQs

Can you revive a synthetic wig?

Yes, you can revive a synthetic wig using the same steps outlined above. However, be sure to use wig-specific products, as regular hair products can damage synthetic hair.

How often should you revive your wig?

How often you revive your wig depends on how often you wear it and how much product you use on it. Generally, you should aim to wash and condition your wig every 6-8 wears, or when it starts to look dull or dirty.

Can you revive a wig that's been heat damaged?

If your wig has been heat damaged, it may be difficult to revive it completely. However, you can try using a deep conditioning treatment to help restore some moisture and shine to the hair.

Hair Care for Your Revived Wig

Now that you've successfully revived your old wig, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are a few hair care tips for your wig:

  • Avoid exposing your wig to direct sunlight or heat for extended periods of time, as this can cause fading and damage.
  • Use a wig-specific brush or comb to style your wig, as regular brushes and combs can cause damage and tangling.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools on your wig too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Store your wig in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat.

Reviving an old wig may take some time and effort, but the end result is well worth it. With a little TLC, you can bring your old wig back to life and enjoy it for years to come.
