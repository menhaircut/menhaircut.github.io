Description

When it comes to haircuts, there are many different measurements used to determine the length of the cut. One of the most common measurements is the number system, where the length is determined by the guard or blade used on the clippers. A 4 haircut is a popular choice for men and women alike, but many people wonder how long it actually is in millimeters (mm).

How to

To determine how long a 4 haircut is in mm, you will need to know the blade or guard size being used. A 4 guard is typically about 1/2 inch or 12.7 mm in length. This means that a 4 haircut will leave the hair at a length of 12.7 mm all over the head.

Step by step

If you are considering getting a 4 haircut, here are the steps you can expect:

  1. The stylist will start by selecting a 4 guard for their clippers.
  2. They will then run the clippers over your entire head, cutting the hair to a length of 12.7 mm.
  3. If you have any longer sections of hair, the stylist may trim them with scissors to blend them in with the rest of the cut.
  4. Finally, they will use a comb and scissors to trim any areas that need extra attention, such as around the ears or at the neckline.

Tips

If you are getting a 4 haircut, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Be clear with your stylist about exactly what you want. A 4 haircut may mean different things to different people, so it's important to communicate your desired length and style.
  • Bring a picture of the style you want to help your stylist visualize your desired look.
  • Ask your stylist for advice on how to maintain your new haircut at home, including recommended products and styling techniques.

FAQ

What other guard or blade sizes are commonly used?

The most common guard or blade sizes used for haircuts are:

  • 0 (1/16 inch or 1.5 mm)
  • 1 (1/8 inch or 3 mm)
  • 2 (1/4 inch or 6 mm)
  • 3 (3/8 inch or 9.5 mm)
  • 4 (1/2 inch or 12.7 mm)
  • 5 (5/8 inch or 16 mm)
  • 6 (3/4 inch or 19 mm)
  • 7 (7/8 inch or 22 mm)
  • 8 (1 inch or 25 mm)

Is a 4 haircut suitable for all hair types?

While a 4 haircut can work for many different hair types, it may not be the best choice for everyone. If you have very thick or curly hair, a shorter length may be more appropriate to prevent the hair from looking too bulky or unruly.

Hair Care

Once you have your 4 haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers and flat irons, as they can damage the hair and cause split ends.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the length and prevent split ends.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and prevent frizz.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can enjoy your 4 haircut and keep it looking great for weeks to come.
