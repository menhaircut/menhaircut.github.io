How To Figure Out Hair Type For Curly Girls

Written by Bardi Oct 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Knowing your hair type is important for proper hair care, especially for curly girls. Understanding your hair type can help you choose the right products and styling techniques to enhance your natural curls. In this article, we'll show you how to figure out your hair type and provide tips for hair care.

Table of Contents

  • How to Figure Out Your Hair Type
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Hair Care
  • FAQs

How to Figure Out Your Hair Type

There are four main hair types: straight, wavy, curly, and coily. Within each hair type, there are subcategories based on the thickness and texture of your hair. Here are the general characteristics of each hair type:

Straight Hair

Straight hair is typically smooth and shiny. It tends to be oily and lacks volume. Straight hair is the easiest to manage and style.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair is characterized by loose curls or waves. It tends to be frizzy and can have a combination of both straight and curly textures. Wavy hair is versatile and can be styled in many ways.

Curly Hair

Curly hair is defined by tight, spiral curls. It tends to be dry and prone to frizz. Curly hair can be challenging to manage but can also be very beautiful.

Coily Hair

Coily hair is the tightest curl pattern, also known as kinky or afro-textured hair. It tends to be very dry and requires a lot of moisture. Coily hair is fragile and requires gentle handling.

Now that you know the general characteristics of each hair type, you can determine which category your hair falls under.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here are the steps to determine your hair type:

  1. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Allow your hair to air dry or use a t-shirt to gently dry your hair.
  3. Do not brush or comb your hair after washing.
  4. Observe the natural shape of your hair:

Straight Hair

If your hair dries straight with no visible curl pattern, you have straight hair.

Wavy Hair

If your hair dries with a slight "S" shape or loose waves, you have wavy hair.

Curly Hair

If your hair dries with a defined curl pattern, you have curly hair. The tighter the curl, the curlier your hair is.

Coily Hair

If your hair dries with a very tight curl pattern or zigzag shape, you have coily hair.

Remember that hair types can change over time due to hormonal changes, aging, and hair damage. It's important to re-evaluate your hair type periodically and adjust your hair care routine accordingly.

Tips for Hair Care

Once you know your hair type, you can choose the right hair care products and styling techniques to enhance your natural curls. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to add moisture to your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to deeply nourish your hair.
  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to avoid frizz and breakage.

FAQs

What if my hair falls under multiple hair types?

It's common for hair to have more than one texture, especially if you have wavy or curly hair. In this case, you can choose products and styling techniques that work best for the majority of your hair.

Can I change my hair type?

Hair type is determined by genetics, so you cannot change your hair type. However, you can change the appearance and health of your hair through proper hair care.

What should I do if I'm still unsure of my hair type?

If you're still unsure of your hair type, consult with a hair care professional or a curly hair specialist. They can help you determine your hair type and provide personalized hair care advice.

Now that you know how to figure out your hair type, you can start taking care of your curls with confidence. Remember to embrace your natural curls and enjoy the journey of discovering what works best for your hair.
