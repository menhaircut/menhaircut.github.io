Why Does Vsg Cause Hair Loss

Written by William Jul 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering undergoing vertical sleeve gastrectomy (VSG) but worried about the possibility of hair loss? It's a common concern among people who are planning to undergo this weight loss surgery. The good news is that hair loss after VSG is temporary and can be managed with proper hair care. In this article, we'll explore the reasons behind why VSG causes hair loss and what you can do to minimize its effects.

What is VSG?

VSG is a surgical procedure that involves removing a large portion of the stomach to reduce its size by up to 80%. The remaining stomach is shaped like a sleeve or tube, which limits the amount of food you can eat and makes you feel full faster. This results in significant weight loss over time.

Why Does VSG Cause Hair Loss?

VSG causes hair loss because it is a major surgery that puts stress on the body. During the surgery, the body is put under anesthesia, and the stomach is removed, which can cause trauma to the body. The body then redirects its resources to heal the trauma, which can result in hair loss. Additionally, after VSG, your body is in a state of rapid weight loss, which can also contribute to hair loss.

How to Minimize Hair Loss After VSG?

There are several ways to minimize hair loss after VSG:

1. Take Vitamins and Supplements

After VSG, your body may not be able to absorb all the nutrients it needs from food alone. Taking vitamins and supplements, such as biotin, zinc, and iron, can help ensure that your body has the nutrients it needs to keep your hair healthy and strong.

2. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet that is rich in protein, fruits, and vegetables can also help reduce hair loss after VSG. Protein is especially important for hair growth, so make sure to include lean meats, eggs, and beans in your diet.

3. Avoid Harsh Chemical Treatments

Avoid using harsh chemical treatments on your hair, such as bleach and hair dyes. These treatments can weaken your hair and make it more prone to breakage.

4. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Be gentle with your hair when washing and styling it. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner and avoid pulling your hair too tightly when styling it. Also, avoid using hot styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair.

FAQ About Hair Loss After VSG

1. Is hair loss after VSG permanent?

No, hair loss after VSG is usually temporary and will improve over time.

2. How long does hair loss after VSG last?

Hair loss after VSG usually peaks around 3-6 months after surgery and can last up to a year. After that, hair growth should return to normal.

3. Can hair loss after VSG be prevented?

While hair loss after VSG cannot be completely prevented, taking vitamins and supplements, eating a balanced diet, and being gentle with your hair can help minimize its effects.

Hair Care After VSG

After VSG, it's important to take care of your hair to minimize hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. Here are some tips:

1. Wash Your Hair Regularly

Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo and conditioner to keep your scalp and hair clean and healthy.

2. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help stimulate hair growth by increasing blood flow to the hair follicles. Use your fingertips to gently massage your scalp for a few minutes each day.

3. Use a Wide-Toothed Comb

Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush. This will help prevent hair breakage and damage.

4. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid wearing tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, as they can pull on your hair and cause damage.

5. Be Patient

Finally, be patient. Hair loss after VSG can be frustrating, but it's a normal part of the recovery process. With proper hair care and time, your hair will grow back.

Overall, hair loss after VSG is a common but temporary side effect of the surgery. By taking care of your hair and following the tips outlined in this article, you can minimize its effects and promote healthy hair growth.
