How To Cut The Back Of Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Sep 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Learning how to cut the back of hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're doing it yourself. But with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy cut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process step by step, so you can achieve the perfect cut every time.

How to Cut the Back of Hair: A Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin cutting your hair, you'll need to gather the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A wide-tooth comb
  • A handheld mirror
  • A hair clip or elastic band

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into two parts, top and bottom. Use a hair clip or elastic band to secure the top section of hair on the top of your head. This will allow you to focus on the back of your hair without getting in the way of the front.

Step 3: Comb Out Your Hair

Using a wide-tooth comb, comb out the back section of your hair. This will help you see any tangles or knots that may be present, which can affect the outcome of your cut.

Step 4: Determine Your Desired Length

Before you start cutting, decide on the length you want to achieve. It's best to start with a longer length and work your way down, as it's easier to cut more hair off than to add it back on.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Using your hair cutting scissors, start cutting the back of your hair. Begin at the bottom of your hairline and work your way up, cutting small sections at a time. Use the comb to hold the hair straight and to guide your scissors as you cut.

Step 6: Check Your Progress

Periodically check your progress by using a handheld mirror to view the back of your hair. This will help you see if you're cutting evenly and if you're achieving the desired length.

Step 7: Blend the Hair

Once you've cut the desired length, use your scissors to blend the hair by cutting at a slight angle. This will help create a more natural look and prevent any harsh lines.

Step 8: Repeat on the Other Side

Repeat the process on the other side of your head, making sure to keep your sections even and your cuts consistent.

Step 9: Check for Any Unevenness

After you've finished cutting the back of your hair, check for any unevenness or spots that need touching up. Use your scissors to make any necessary adjustments.

Step 10: Finish With Hair Care

Finish off your cut with some hair care. Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help smooth and nourish your hair. Style your hair as desired and enjoy your fresh new cut!

Tips for Cutting the Back of Your Hair

Here are a few additional tips to help you achieve the perfect cut:

  • Take your time and cut slowly, especially if you're a beginner.
  • Use the comb to hold the hair straight and to guide your scissors as you cut.
  • Check your progress frequently to ensure you're achieving the desired length.
  • Don't hesitate to ask for help if you're struggling to reach certain areas.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I Cut the Back of My Hair Myself?

Yes, you can cut the back of your hair yourself. Just be sure to follow the steps outlined in this guide and take your time to ensure a smooth and even cut.

How Often Should I Cut the Back of My Hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair growth and prevent split ends.

Is It Better to Cut Wet or Dry Hair?

It's generally easier to cut hair when it's wet as it's more pliable and easier to work with. However, if you have curly hair, it's best to cut it dry to prevent any shrinkage.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and shine. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage and dryness.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help nourish and protect your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

Now that you know how to cut the back of your hair, you can achieve a salon-worthy cut from the comfort of your own home. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow the steps outlined in this guide. Happy cutting!
