How To Style Bangs With Thick Hair A Guide For Effortless Styling

Written by Luffy Jul 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're blessed with thick hair, you know that it can be both a blessing and a curse. While you enjoy the volume and fullness that thick hair provides, it can also be quite challenging to style, especially when it comes to bangs. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll share tips and tricks on how to style bangs with thick hair for a chic and effortless look.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Style Bangs with Thick Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Styling Bangs with Thick Hair

How to Style Bangs with Thick Hair

Before we get into the nitty-gritty of styling your bangs, it's important to understand the different types of bangs and which one will suit your face shape and hair texture. Here are some popular types of bangs:

  • Straight bangs
  • Side-swept bangs
  • Layered bangs
  • Curtain bangs

Once you've decided on the type of bangs you want, it's time to style them. Here are some tips to get you started:

1. Blow-dry Your Bangs

Blow-drying your bangs is the first step to styling them. Use a round brush to blow-dry your bangs in the direction you want them to fall. If you have side-swept bangs, blow-dry them to the opposite side to add volume.

2. Use a Flat Iron

If you want a sleek look, use a flat iron to straighten your bangs. Make sure to use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools to prevent damage to your hair.

3. Add Texture

If you want a more textured look, use a texturizing spray or dry shampoo on your bangs. This will add volume and texture to your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to style bangs with thick hair:

Step 1:

Wash your hair and blow-dry it until it's about 80% dry.

Step 2:

Section your hair and clip it up, leaving only your bangs down.

Step 3:

Blow-dry your bangs using a round brush, directing the hair away from your face.

Step 4:

Use a flat iron to straighten your bangs if you want a sleek look.

Step 5:

Add texture to your bangs using a texturizing spray or dry shampoo.

Tips for Styling Bangs with Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when styling your bangs:

  • Don't overdo it with the heat styling tools. Overuse of heat can damage your hair.
  • Use a dry shampoo to extend the life of your blowout.
  • Trim your bangs regularly to keep them looking fresh and neat.
  • If you have curly hair, consider getting bangs that are longer in the middle and shorter on the sides.

FAQs

Can you style bangs with thick hair?

Yes, you can definitely style bangs with thick hair. However, it's important to choose the right type of bangs and use the right styling tools to achieve the desired look.

How do you keep your bangs from getting greasy?

Use a dry shampoo on your bangs to absorb excess oil and keep them looking fresh.

How often should you trim your bangs?

It's recommended to trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking neat and tidy.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Hair

Here are some hair care tips for thick hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent your hair from drying out.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair to prevent damage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush.

There you have it, a complete guide on how to style bangs with thick hair. With these tips and tricks, you'll be rocking your bangs in no time!
