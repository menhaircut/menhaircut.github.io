Who Suits Platinum Hair A Guide To Rocking The Trendiest Hair Color

Written by Oliver Aug 06, 2023 · 5 min read

Platinum hair is a trend that has been around for years, and it continues to be a popular choice for those who want to make a statement. While it may seem like a bold choice, platinum hair can actually be versatile and flattering on many different skin tones and hair types. In this article, we'll explore who suits platinum hair, how to achieve the look, tips for maintaining it, and more.

Who Suits Platinum Hair?

Platinum hair can actually be a flattering choice for many different skin tones and hair types. However, some people may be more suited to the look than others. Generally speaking, those with cool or neutral undertones in their skin tend to look best with platinum hair. This includes people with fair to medium skin tones, as well as those with pink, red, or blue undertones.

The key to making platinum hair work for you is to choose the right shade. There are many different shades of platinum, from icy white to warm blonde. If you have cool undertones, you may want to opt for an icy or silver platinum. If you have warm undertones, a warmer, golden platinum may be more flattering.

Platinum hair can also work for those with different hair types. While it may be more challenging to achieve on darker hair, it can still be done with the right techniques. Those with lighter hair may find it easier to achieve the desired shade.

How to Achieve Platinum Hair

Achieving platinum hair can be a challenging process, especially if you have dark hair or have never colored your hair before. It's important to understand that it may take several sessions to achieve the desired shade, and it's crucial to take good care of your hair throughout the process.

Step-by-Step Guide to Coloring Your Hair Platinum

Before you begin, it's essential to consult with a professional stylist to determine whether platinum hair is a good choice for you and to develop a plan for achieving the desired shade. Here is a basic step-by-step guide to coloring your hair platinum:

  1. Prepare your hair by washing it with a clarifying shampoo. This will remove any buildup or residue that may interfere with the coloring process.
  2. Apply a protective treatment to your hair, such as Olaplex, to minimize damage.
  3. Mix your bleach according to the instructions and apply it to your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  4. Cover your hair with a plastic cap and let the bleach process for the recommended amount of time. This may take several hours, depending on your hair type and the desired shade.
  5. Rinse your hair thoroughly and apply a toner to achieve the desired shade of platinum.
  6. Rinse your hair again and condition it with a deep conditioner to help repair any damage from the coloring process.

Tips for Maintaining Platinum Hair

Once you've achieved your desired shade of platinum hair, it's important to take good care of it to maintain the color and prevent damage. Here are some tips for maintaining platinum hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent fading and damage.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible, as they can cause further damage to your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray if you must use heat styling tools.
  • Consider using a purple shampoo to help maintain the platinum tone and prevent brassy tones from developing.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Common FAQs About Platinum Hair

Here are some common questions and answers about platinum hair:

Is platinum hair damaging to your hair?

Yes, platinum hair can be damaging to your hair, especially if you have dark hair or have never colored your hair before. It's important to take good care of your hair throughout the coloring process and to use protective treatments to minimize damage.

How often should I touch up my platinum hair?

This will depend on your hair type and how quickly your hair grows. Generally speaking, you may need to touch up your roots every 4-6 weeks to maintain the color.

Hair Care for Platinum Hair

Taking good care of your hair is essential when you have platinum hair. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy and hydrated:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent fading and damage.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible, as they can cause further damage to your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray if you must use heat styling tools.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.

Conclusion

Platinum hair can be a stunning and bold choice for those who want to make a statement. With the right shade and proper care, platinum hair can be flattering on many different skin tones and hair types. If you're considering platinum hair, be sure to consult with a professional stylist to develop a plan for achieving the desired shade and maintaining the health of your hair.
