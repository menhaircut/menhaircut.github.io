How To Prevent Hair Falling Out After Bleaching A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Aug 28, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Bleaching hair has become a popular trend in the beauty industry. It can give you a new look and feel, but it can also damage your hair. The process of bleaching hair removes the natural pigment, making it lighter. However, this procedure can leave your hair dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. In this article, we'll provide you with some tips on how to prevent hair falling out after bleaching.

Description

When you bleach your hair, the process can damage the cuticle, which is the outer protective layer of your hair. This can make your hair prone to breakage and falling out. To prevent hair falling out after bleaching, you need to take some precautions and follow a hair care routine that will help to nourish and protect your hair.

How to Prevent Hair Falling Out After Bleaching

Here are some tips to prevent hair falling out after bleaching:

1. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Deep conditioning treatments can help to restore moisture to your hair and prevent breakage. You should use a deep conditioning treatment once a week, or more often if your hair is particularly damaged. Look for products that contain ingredients like keratin, argan oil, or avocado oil.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can further damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. If possible, avoid using heat styling tools like curling irons or straighteners. If you must use heat styling tools, use them on the lowest heat setting and use a heat protectant spray.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trimming can help to prevent split ends and breakage. You should aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks, or more often if your hair is particularly damaged.

4. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Using a wide-tooth comb can help to prevent breakage and damage to your hair. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up, gently detangling any knots or tangles.

5. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Leave-in conditioners can help to nourish and protect your hair throughout the day. Look for products that contain ingredients like coconut oil or shea butter. Apply a small amount to your hair after washing, and leave it in for added protection.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to prevent hair falling out after bleaching:

Step 1: Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair once a week or more often if your hair is particularly damaged.

Step 2: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like curling irons or straighteners. If you must use them, use them on the lowest heat setting and use a heat protectant spray.

Step 3: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks, or more often if your hair is particularly damaged.

Step 4: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.

Step 5: Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing to nourish and protect your hair throughout the day.

Tips for Preventing Hair Loss After Bleaching

Here are some additional tips for preventing hair loss after bleaching:

1. Don't Overprocess Your Hair

Overprocessing your hair can cause serious damage and make it more prone to breakage. Follow the instructions on the bleaching kit carefully and don't leave the product on for longer than recommended.

2. Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments can help to strengthen your hair and prevent breakage. Look for products that contain ingredients like hydrolyzed keratin or silk protein.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles like ponytails or braids can put stress on your hair and cause breakage. Try to avoid these hairstyles or wear them loosely.

4. Eat a Balanced Diet

A balanced diet can help to nourish your hair from the inside out. Make sure you're getting enough protein, vitamins, and minerals in your diet.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can bleaching cause hair loss?

A: Yes, bleaching can cause hair loss if it damages the cuticle of your hair and makes it more prone to breakage.

Q: How long does it take for hair to recover after bleaching?

A: It can take several months for hair to recover after bleaching. You should follow a hair care routine and be patient while waiting for your hair to grow and recover.

Hair Care After Bleaching

After bleaching your hair, it's important to follow a hair care routine that will help to nourish and protect your hair. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils. Look for products that are sulfate-free and contain nourishing ingredients like argan oil or coconut oil.

2. Apply a Hair Mask

Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair. Look for products that contain ingredients like shea butter or jojoba oil.

3. Avoid Chlorine

Chlorine can further damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Avoid swimming in chlorinated pools or wear a swim cap to protect your hair.

4. Protect Your Hair From the Sun

The sun can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Wear a hat or use a hair product with SPF to protect your hair from the sun's harmful rays.

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can prevent hair falling out after bleaching and keep your hair healthy and beautiful.
