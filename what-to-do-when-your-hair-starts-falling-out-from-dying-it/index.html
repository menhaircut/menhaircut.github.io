What To Do When Your Hair Starts Falling Out From Dying It

Written by Luffy Jul 27, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you've been dyeing your hair for a while, you may have noticed that your hair is starting to fall out. This can be a scary and frustrating experience, but the good news is that there are things you can do to prevent further damage and promote hair growth. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks for what to do when your hair starts falling out from dyeing it.

Table of Contents

  • How to Prevent Hair Loss from Dyeing
  • Step by Step Guide to Caring for Hair

Description

Hair loss from dyeing can be caused by a number of factors, including damage to the hair shaft, irritation to the scalp, and chemical exposure. When hair is damaged, it can become brittle and prone to breaking, which can lead to hair loss. Additionally, chemical exposure from hair dye can cause scalp irritation, which can also contribute to hair loss.

How to Prevent Hair Loss from Dyeing

The best way to prevent hair loss from dyeing is to take steps to protect your hair from damage. This includes using high-quality hair dye, avoiding over-processing your hair, and using hair care products that are designed to nourish and strengthen your hair.

When you're dyeing your hair, it's important to follow the instructions on the package carefully. This means not leaving the dye on for longer than recommended and avoiding overlapping areas that have already been dyed. You should also avoid using heat styling tools like straighteners and curling irons, as these can further damage your hair.

Step by Step Guide to Caring for Hair

1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type.

2. Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.

4. Avoid using heat styling tools like straighteners and curling irons, as these can further damage your hair.

5. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish and strengthen your hair.

Tips for Healthy Hair

1. Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals, as this can help promote hair growth.

2. Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach and peroxide.

3. Use a satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent breakage while you sleep.

4. Massage your scalp regularly to promote blood flow and stimulate hair growth.

FAQ

Q: How long does it take for hair to grow back after falling out from dyeing?

A: It can take several months for hair to grow back after falling out from dyeing. In some cases, it may take up to a year for hair to fully regrow.

Q: Can hair dye cause permanent hair loss?

A: In rare cases, hair dye can cause permanent hair loss. This is usually the result of an allergic reaction to the chemicals in the dye.

Hair Care

When it comes to hair care, prevention is key. By taking steps to protect your hair from damage and nourishing it with the right products, you can help prevent hair loss from dyeing. If you're experiencing hair loss, it's important to speak with a dermatologist or trichologist to determine the underlying cause and develop a treatment plan.
