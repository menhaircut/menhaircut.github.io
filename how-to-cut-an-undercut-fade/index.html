How To Cut An Undercut Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jun 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a trendy and stylish haircut, the undercut fade is a great option. This popular hairstyle features short hair on the sides and back, with longer hair on the top. The undercut fade is versatile and can be styled in many different ways, making it a great choice for men of all ages and styles. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut an undercut fade step by step.

How to Cut an Undercut Fade

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you begin cutting hair, you'll need to gather your supplies. You'll need a good pair of clippers, scissors, a comb, and hair clips. You may also want to have a cape or towel to protect clothing.

Step 2: Prepare the Hair

Start by washing and drying the hair. You'll want to make sure the hair is clean and free of any tangles. Use a comb to part the hair into sections, and use hair clips to keep the sections separate.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Begin cutting the hair on the sides and back with the clippers. Use a shorter guard to create a fade, gradually increasing the length as you move up the head. Make sure to blend the hair so that it looks natural.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Once you've finished cutting the sides and back, move on to the top of the head. Use scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. You can leave the hair longer on top or cut it short for a more dramatic look.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Use scissors to blend the hair on top with the hair on the sides and back. This will create a seamless transition between the two lengths of hair. Make sure to blend the hair evenly so that it looks natural.

Step 6: Style the Hair

Once you've finished cutting the hair, style it to your liking. You can use hair products to create volume or texture, or leave it natural for a more relaxed look.

Tips for Cutting an Undercut Fade

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting an undercut fade:

  • Start with a longer guard on the clippers and gradually work your way down to a shorter guard as you move up the head.
  • Use a comb to create a straight line between the longer hair on top and the shorter hair on the sides and back.
  • Take your time and make small cuts to ensure that the hair looks even.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're not confident in your cutting skills.

FAQs

How long does an undercut fade last?

An undercut fade typically lasts about 2-4 weeks, depending on how quickly your hair grows.

Can an undercut fade be styled in different ways?

Yes, an undercut fade can be styled in many different ways. You can slick it back, comb it over, or leave it messy and textured.

Is an undercut fade suitable for all hair types?

An undercut fade can work for most hair types, but it may not be suitable for very thin or fine hair.

Hair Care for an Undercut Fade

To keep your undercut fade looking its best, you'll need to take good care of your hair. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair clean and healthy. You may also want to use a hair oil or serum to keep the hair moisturized and prevent damage. Regular trims will also help to keep the hair looking neat and tidy.

Now that you know how to cut an undercut fade, you can try this trendy hairstyle for yourself. With a little practice and patience, you can create a stylish and versatile look that's perfect for any occasion.
