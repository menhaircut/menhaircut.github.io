How To Get Thick Dreads With Straight Hair

Written by Oliver Sep 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

In recent years, dreadlocks have become increasingly popular, and for good reason. They are a unique and stylish way to express oneself. However, many people with straight hair are under the impression that they cannot achieve thick dreads. That's not true! With the right techniques and a little patience, anyone can get thick, beautiful dreads, even with straight hair. In this article, we'll show you how to get thick dreads with straight hair.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Get Thick Dreads with Straight Hair
  2. Step by Step Guide
  3. Tips for Getting Thick Dreads
  4. Frequently Asked Questions
  5. Hair Care Tips for Dreads

Step by Step Guide

Here are the steps you should follow to get thick dreads with straight hair:

Step 1: Let Your Hair Grow

The first step to getting thick dreads is to let your hair grow. You need at least 6 inches of hair to start the process, and the longer your hair is, the thicker your dreads will be. So be patient and let your hair grow.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

The next step is to section your hair. Use a comb to create even sections of hair. The size of the sections will depend on how thick you want your dreads to be.

Step 3: Backcomb Your Hair

Once you have your sections, it's time to backcomb your hair. Take one section at a time and use a comb to backcomb it. This will create knots in your hair that will eventually turn into dreads. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 4: Twist Your Hair

After backcombing, it's time to twist your hair. Take one section at a time and twist it. This will help the knots to form into dreads. Make sure to twist your hair tightly and evenly.

Step 5: Use Dread Wax

Once you have twisted your hair, it's time to use dread wax. Dread wax helps to hold the dreads together and also helps them to form more quickly. Apply a small amount of dread wax to each dread and twist it again to distribute the wax evenly.

Step 6: Maintain Your Dreads

Now that you have your dreads, it's important to maintain them. This means washing them regularly and keeping them clean. You can also palm roll your dreads to help them stay tight and neat.

Tips for Getting Thick Dreads

Here are some tips to help you get thick dreads:

  • Be patient. Getting thick dreads takes time, so don't rush the process.
  • Use a good quality dread wax. This will help your dreads to form more quickly and also help them to stay together.
  • Wash your dreads regularly. This will help to keep them clean and prevent them from smelling.
  • Use a residue-free shampoo. This will help to prevent buildup in your dreads.
  • Don't use a hair dryer on your dreads. Let them air dry instead.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting thick dreads with straight hair:

Can I get thick dreads with short hair?

No. You need at least 6 inches of hair to start the process of getting dreads.

Can I get thick dreads without using dread wax?

Yes, you can. However, dread wax helps the dreads to form more quickly and also helps them to stay together.

How often should I wash my dreads?

You should wash your dreads once a week.

Hair Care Tips for Dreads

Here are some hair care tips for maintaining your dreads:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Use a light oil to keep your scalp moisturized.
  • Avoid using heavy conditioners on your dreads.
  • Don't use a hair dryer on your dreads. Let them air dry instead.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent your dreads from getting tangled.

By following these tips and steps, you can get thick, beautiful dreads with straight hair. Remember to be patient and take good care of your dreads, and they will look great for years to come.
