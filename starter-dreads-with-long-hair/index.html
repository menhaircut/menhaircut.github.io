Starter Dreads With Long Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 15, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you've been considering getting starter dreads with long hair, this is the perfect guide for you. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about the process, from how to start your dreads to hair care tips and frequently asked questions. Let's dive in!

What Are Starter Dreads?

Starter dreads are the initial stages of dreadlocks. This is the point where the hair is sectioned and twisted to create the dreadlock formation. Starter dreads can be achieved on any hair length, texture or type, including long hair.

How to Start Your Dreads

Here are the steps to follow if you want to start your dreads:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

It's important to start with clean hair. Use a residue-free shampoo to remove any product buildup or oils from your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into the desired size of your dreadlocks. Use rubber bands or hair ties to separate the sections and keep them in place.

Step 3: Twist Your Hair

Twist each section of hair in a clockwise direction until it starts to coil on itself. You can use a small amount of locking gel or beeswax to help the hair hold its shape.

Step 4: Maintain Your Dreads

Once you've started your dreads, you'll need to maintain them to keep them looking neat and tidy. This involves washing them regularly and palm rolling them to keep them tight.

Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads

Here are some tips to help you maintain your starter dreads:

  • Wash your dreads with a residue-free shampoo at least once a week.
  • Use a crochet hook to pull loose hairs back into the dreadlock.
  • Palm roll your dreads regularly to keep them tight.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can cause buildup in your dreads.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about starter dreads:

Can I Start Dreads on Relaxed Hair?

Yes, you can start dreads on relaxed hair. However, it's important to note that relaxed hair is more fragile than natural hair, so you'll need to take extra care to avoid breakage.

Can I Wash My Dreads?

Yes, you can wash your dreads. In fact, it's important to keep them clean to prevent buildup and odors. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using heavy conditioners or oils that can cause buildup.

How Long Does It Take to Start Dreads?

The time it takes to start dreads depends on your hair type and texture. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months to start seeing progress.

Final Thoughts

Starting your dreads with long hair can be a fun and exciting process. However, it's important to take the time to properly maintain your dreads to avoid breakage and keep them looking neat and tidy. Follow the tips and steps outlined in this guide, and you'll be well on your way to achieving the perfect set of starter dreads.
