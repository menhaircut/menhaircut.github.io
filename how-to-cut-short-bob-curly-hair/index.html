How To Cut Short Bob Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jun 30, 2023 · 4 min read
If you have curly hair, you know how challenging it can be to maintain it. However, with the right haircut, it can be a breeze to manage. A short bob is a versatile and trendy hairstyle that looks great on curly hair. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of cutting short bob curly hair, step-by-step.

Description

A short bob is a classic haircut that has been popular for decades. It is a versatile hairstyle that can be worn by women of all ages and hair types. When it comes to curly hair, a short bob is a great way to show off your natural texture and volume. It is also a low-maintenance haircut that doesn't require a lot of styling.

How to Cut Short Bob Curly Hair

Cutting curly hair requires a different approach than cutting straight hair. Curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so it's essential to cut it when it's wet. Here's how you can cut short bob curly hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a moisturizing conditioner to keep your curls hydrated. Rinse your hair thoroughly and gently pat it dry with a towel.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently. Start from the ends and work your way up to the roots. Be gentle to avoid causing breakage or damage to your curls.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four equal parts using clips. Divide your hair down the middle from the forehead to the nape of your neck, then from ear to ear.

Step 4: Cut the Back

Start by cutting the back of your hair. Take one section down and comb it straight down. Hold it between your index and middle fingers and cut it at the desired length. Repeat this process for the remaining sections.

Step 5: Cut the Sides

Next, cut the sides of your hair. Take one section down and comb it straight down. Hold it between your index and middle fingers and cut it at the desired length. Repeat this process for the remaining sections.

Step 6: Define Your Curls

Once you've cut your hair, define your curls using a curl-defining product. Apply the product to your hair, scrunch it up, and let it air dry.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting short bob curly hair: - Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair. - Cut your hair when it's wet to avoid shrinkage. - Don't cut too much hair at once to avoid making mistakes. - Cut your hair in small sections to ensure evenness.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my curly hair myself? A: Yes, you can cut your curly hair yourself. However, it's essential to be careful and take your time to avoid making mistakes. Q: How often should I cut my curly hair? A: It's recommended to cut your curly hair every six to eight weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

Maintaining healthy curly hair requires proper care. Here are some tips to keep your curls looking their best: - Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a moisturizing conditioner. - Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons. - Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently. - Apply a curl-defining product to your hair to enhance your curls' definition.

Conclusion

Cutting short bob curly hair requires a different approach than cutting straight hair. However, with the right technique and tools, it can be a breeze to manage. Follow our step-by-step guide to achieve a stylish and low-maintenance haircut that shows off your natural curls. Remember to take care of your curls to keep them looking their best.
