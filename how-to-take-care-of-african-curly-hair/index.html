How To Take Care Of African Curly Hair

Written by Bardi Aug 15, 2023 · 5 min read

As someone with African curly hair, you know how important it is to take care of your locks. But with so many different products and techniques out there, it can be hard to know where to start. In this article, we’ll cover everything you need to know about taking care of African curly hair, from how to wash and style it to tips for preventing breakage and promoting healthy growth.

Table of Contents

  • How to Wash African Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Styling African Curly Hair
  • Tips for Preventing Breakage and Promoting Healthy Growth

How to Wash African Curly Hair

Washing your hair may seem like a simple task, but when you have African curly hair, it’s important to use the right techniques and products to avoid damage and breakage. Here’s a step-by-step guide to washing your African curly hair:

Step 1: Pre-Poo

Before you wash your hair, it’s important to pre-poo. This involves applying a conditioning treatment to your hair before shampooing to prevent it from becoming too dry. You can use a store-bought pre-poo product or make your own using ingredients like coconut oil, honey, and avocado.

Step 2: Shampoo

Next, it’s time to shampoo your hair. Choose a sulfate-free shampoo designed for curly hair to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Be sure to massage the shampoo into your scalp and gently work it through your hair. Rinse thoroughly.

Step 3: Condition

After shampooing, it’s important to condition your hair to restore moisture and prevent tangles. Use a moisturizing conditioner designed for curly hair and comb it through your hair with a wide-tooth comb. Leave the conditioner on for at least 5-10 minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 4: Deep Condition

Once a week, it’s a good idea to do a deep conditioning treatment to keep your hair healthy and strong. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair, cover it with a plastic cap, and sit under a hooded dryer or wrap your head with a warm towel. Leave the conditioner on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it out.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling African Curly Hair

Styling African curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve beautiful, healthy curls. Here’s a step-by-step guide to styling your African curly hair:

Step 1: Apply Leave-In Conditioner

Start by applying a leave-in conditioner to your hair to add moisture and prevent frizz. Use a quarter-sized amount and work it through your hair with your fingers.

Step 2: Apply Curl Enhancing Cream

Next, apply a curl enhancing cream to define your curls and add shine. Use a dime-sized amount and work it through your hair, focusing on the ends.

Step 3: Scrunch Your Hair

Scrunch your hair gently to encourage your curls to form. Use a microfiber towel or T-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel to prevent frizz.

Step 4: Air Dry or Diffuse

Finally, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls. Avoid touching your hair too much while it dries to prevent frizz.

Tips for Preventing Breakage and Promoting Healthy Growth

When it comes to African curly hair, preventing breakage and promoting healthy growth is key. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy and strong:

  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent breakage and frizz while you sleep.
  • Avoid using heat tools like flat irons and curling irons, which can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using tight hair ties or rubber bands, which can pull on your hair and cause breakage.
  • Drink plenty of water and eat a balanced diet to promote healthy hair growth from the inside out.

FAQs

  • Q: How often should I wash my African curly hair?
    A: It’s best to wash your hair once a week or every other week to avoid drying it out.
  • Q: What products are good for African curly hair?
    A: Look for sulfate-free shampoos, moisturizing conditioners, leave-in conditioners, and curl enhancing creams designed for curly hair.
  • Q: How can I prevent frizz in my African curly hair?
    A: Use a leave-in conditioner, avoid touching your hair too much while it dries, and use a microfiber towel or T-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel.
