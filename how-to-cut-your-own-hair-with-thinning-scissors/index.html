How To Cut Your Own Hair With Thinning Scissors

Written by Daniel Jun 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a way to freshen up your hairdo without breaking the bank, learning how to cut your own hair with thinning scissors can be an excellent solution. Not only can you save money on salon visits, but you can also customize your haircut to your preferences. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting your own hair with thinning scissors, step-by-step.

How to

Before we dive in, there are a few things you'll need to cut your hair with thinning scissors. Here's what you'll need:

  • Thinning scissors
  • Regular scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Water spray bottle
  • Mirror

Once you have all the necessary tools, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash your hair

The first step is to wash your hair thoroughly and let it air dry. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that your hair is clean and free of any styling products.

Step 2: Section your hair

Next, use the comb to section your hair into manageable parts. Use hair clips to secure each section in place.

Step 3: Start cutting

Starting with the bottom section, use the regular scissors to cut your hair to the desired length. Then, use the thinning scissors to create texture and remove bulk. Repeat this process for each section of your hair, working your way up from the bottom.

Step 4: Check your progress

Periodically check your progress by looking in the mirror. If you notice any uneven spots, use the regular scissors to trim them down. Be careful not to cut too much off at once.

Step 5: Finish up

Once you're happy with your new haircut, use the water spray bottle to dampen your hair slightly. This will help you see any stray hairs that need to be trimmed. Use the regular scissors to trim any remaining flyaways.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own hair with thinning scissors:

  • Never cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair is more difficult to cut and can lead to uneven results.
  • Invest in a good pair of thinning scissors. Cheap ones can damage your hair and make it look thin and stringy.
  • Take your time. Cutting your own hair can be nerve-wracking, but rushing through the process can lead to mistakes.
  • Start with small cuts. You can always trim more later, but you can't put hair back once it's cut off.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair with thinning scissors:

Can I use thinning scissors on curly hair?

Yes, you can use thinning scissors on curly hair. However, be careful not to remove too much bulk, as this can cause curly hair to lose its shape.

How often should I cut my own hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it starts to look unruly. As a general rule, aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Can I use regular scissors instead of thinning scissors?

Yes, you can use regular scissors to cut your hair. However, thinning scissors are designed to remove bulk and create texture, so you may not get the same results.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's essential to take care of it properly to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Learning how to cut your own hair with thinning scissors can be a fun and rewarding experience. With a little practice and patience, you can achieve salon-worthy results from the comfort of your own home.
