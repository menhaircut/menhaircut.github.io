Can Thick Coarse Hair Be Cut Short Everything You Need To Know

Written by Christine Aug 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thick and coarse hair can be challenging to manage, especially if you prefer a shorter hairstyle. However, with the right approach and techniques, it is possible to cut thick, coarse hair short without causing damage or losing its natural texture. In this blog post, we will answer the question, "can thick coarse hair be cut short?" and provide tips and tricks for a successful cut.

Description

Thick, coarse hair is characterized by a large hair shaft diameter and a rough texture. This type of hair tends to be curly or wavy, making it prone to frizz and tangling. Cutting thick, coarse hair short can be a daunting task, as it requires the right tools, techniques, and expertise to achieve the desired result.

How to Cut Thick Coarse Hair Short

Before cutting your thick, coarse hair short, it's important to prepare it properly. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Begin by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help to soften your hair and make it easier to cut. Rinse thoroughly and towel dry your hair.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Using a wide-toothed comb or brush, gently detangle your hair to remove any knots or tangles. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Separate your hair into sections using hair clips or elastic bands. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly and avoid any uneven sections.

Step 4: Choose Your Cutting Tool

For thick, coarse hair, it's best to use sharp scissors or clippers. Scissors are ideal for creating textured or layered cuts, while clippers are suitable for short, uniform cuts.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Begin cutting your hair in small sections, starting at the back of your head and working your way to the front. Cut your hair to the desired length, using your fingers as a guide to create an even cut. Be sure to cut your hair in small increments to avoid cutting too much at once.

Step 6: Blend Your Hair

Once you've cut your hair to the desired length, blend the different sections using scissors or clippers. This will create a seamless transition between the different sections of your hair.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Finally, style your hair as desired using a styling product, such as a mousse or gel. This will help to define your hair's natural texture and keep it in place throughout the day.

Tips for Cutting Thick Coarse Hair Short

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting thick, coarse hair short:

  • Use sharp scissors or clippers to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Cut your hair in small sections to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Work slowly and carefully to ensure an even cut.
  • Use a styling product to define your hair's natural texture and keep it in place.

FAQs

Can thick coarse hair be cut short without losing its natural texture?

Yes, it is possible to cut thick, coarse hair short without losing its natural texture. However, it's important to use the right techniques and tools to achieve the desired result.

What is the best way to cut thick coarse hair?

The best way to cut thick, coarse hair is to use sharp scissors or clippers and cut your hair in small sections. Be sure to work slowly and carefully to avoid cutting too much at once.

How often should I cut my thick, coarse hair?

It's recommended to cut thick, coarse hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Hair Care for Thick Coarse Hair

To keep your thick, coarse hair healthy and manageable, it's important to follow a regular hair care routine. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep it soft and hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or brush to detangle your hair gently.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals or treatments, such as bleach or relaxers, that can damage your hair.

In conclusion, cutting thick, coarse hair short is possible with the right approach and techniques. Follow our tips and tricks, and you'll be able to achieve the perfect short hairstyle without losing your hair's natural texture. Remember to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and manageable, and you'll have a fabulous head of hair that you can be proud of.
