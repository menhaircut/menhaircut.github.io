How To Fade A Pixie Cut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 29, 2023 · 4 min read
If you have a pixie cut and want to change up your hairstyle, fading it out can be a great option. Fading a pixie cut involves gradually blending the short hair on top with the longer hair on the sides and back. In this blog post, we’ll give you a step-by-step guide on how to fade a pixie cut, along with some tips and FAQs to help you achieve your desired look.

Table of Contents

  • How to Fade a Pixie Cut
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Fading a Pixie Cut
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for a Pixie Cut

How to Fade a Pixie Cut

Fading a pixie cut involves gradually blending the short hair on top with the longer hair on the sides and back. This can be done in a variety of ways, depending on your preferences and the length of your hair. Some common techniques for fading a pixie cut include using clippers, scissors, and razors to create a gradual transition between the short and long sections of hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to fade a pixie cut: 1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will ensure that your hair is clean and easy to work with. 2. Section off the top portion of your hair using clips or hair ties. This will allow you to focus on the sides and back of your hair first. 3. Use clippers with a guard to trim the sides and back of your hair to your desired length. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard if necessary. 4. Blend the clipped sections of hair with the longer hair on top using scissors or a razor. Use vertical cutting motions to create a gradual transition between the short and long sections of hair. 5. Unclip the top portion of your hair and blend it with the faded sections using scissors or a razor. Again, use vertical cutting motions to create a natural-looking fade. 6. Clean up any stray hairs and use styling products as desired.

Tips for Fading a Pixie Cut

Here are some tips to keep in mind when fading a pixie cut: - Start with a longer guard on your clippers and work your way down to a shorter guard if necessary. It’s easier to take off more hair than to add it back on! - Use vertical cutting motions with scissors or a razor to create a natural-looking fade. - Take breaks and step back from the mirror to assess your progress. It’s easy to get caught up in the details and lose sight of the bigger picture. - Consider going to a professional hairstylist if you’re unsure about fading your pixie cut yourself. They can provide expert advice and ensure that you achieve the look you want.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about fading a pixie cut: Q: How long does it take to fade a pixie cut?
A: The time it takes to fade a pixie cut depends on the length of your hair and the technique you use. It can take anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour or more. Q: Can I fade my pixie cut myself?
A: Yes, you can fade your pixie cut yourself if you have the right tools and technique. However, if you’re unsure about how to do it or want a more professional look, consider going to a hairstylist. Q: How often should I fade my pixie cut?
A: The frequency of fading your pixie cut depends on how quickly your hair grows and how much you want to maintain the faded look. It’s typically recommended to get a trim every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care for a Pixie Cut

Once you’ve faded your pixie cut, it’s important to take care of it properly to maintain the look. Here are some tips for hair care: - Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils. - Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, as this can damage your hair. - Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized. - Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape and prevent split ends. Fading a pixie cut can be a fun and stylish way to change up your look. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a natural-looking fade that complements your features. Remember to take breaks, step back from the mirror, and seek professional advice if necessary. Happy fading!
