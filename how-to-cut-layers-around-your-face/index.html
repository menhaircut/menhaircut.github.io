How To Cut Layers Around Your Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Aug 06, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of the same old hairstyle and looking for a change? One way to switch things up is by cutting layers around your face. Layered hair can add texture, volume and dimension to your hair. In this article, we'll cover the steps to cut layers around your face, tips for a successful cut, and how to care for your hair afterwards.

How to Cut Layers Around Your Face

Cutting layers around your face can be done by a professional stylist or at home with the proper tools and technique. If you're comfortable with cutting your own hair, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean and dry hair. This will allow you to see the natural fall of your hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to part your hair down the middle, creating two even sections. Then, take one section and twist it away from your face, securing it with a hair clip. This will be the first section you cut.

Step 3: Determine the Length of Your Layers

Decide how long you want your layers to be. For a subtle layer, start with a length that falls just below your chin. For a more dramatic look, go shorter.

Step 4: Cut the First Section

Take the first section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your pointer and middle finger, and cut at a slight angle. Start cutting from the mid-length of your hair, working your way down to the ends. Repeat this process on the other side.

Step 5: Check for Symmetry

Release your hair from the clip and check for symmetry. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure the layers match on both sides.

Step 6: Cut the Back Layers

To cut layers in the back, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at a slight angle. Work your way around your head, cutting small sections at a time.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a detailed step-by-step guide to cutting layers around your face:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean and dry hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Part your hair down the middle and twist one section away from your face, securing it with a hair clip.

Step 4: Determine Your Desired Length

Decide how long you want your layers to be.

Step 5: Cut the First Section

Take the first section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at a slight angle. Repeat on the other side.

Step 6: Release Your Hair and Check for Symmetry

Release your hair from the clip and check for symmetry. Make any necessary adjustments.

Step 7: Cut the Back Layers

Take small sections of hair and cut at a slight angle. Work your way around your head.

Step 8: Blend the Layers

Blend the layers by combing through your hair and making small adjustments as necessary.

Step 9: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired.

Tips for a Successful Cut

Follow these tips for a successful layered haircut:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Start with a small section of hair and work your way up to larger sections as you gain confidence.
  • Be patient and take your time to avoid mistakes.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it can lead to uneven layers.
  • Use a mirror to check for symmetry.

FAQs

Can I cut layers in my curly hair?

Yes, you can cut layers in curly hair. However, it's important to cut each individual curl to avoid creating uneven layers.

Do I need to use any products after cutting my layers?

Yes, you can use a light styling product to enhance your layers and add texture.

How often should I cut my layers?

It's recommended to cut your layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and volume.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers

After cutting your layers, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips:

  • Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair strong and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to avoid split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions.

Cutting layers around your face can be a fun and easy way to switch up your hairstyle. With the proper tools and technique, you can achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home. Follow these steps, tips and hair care advice, and you'll have beautiful, layered hair in no time!
