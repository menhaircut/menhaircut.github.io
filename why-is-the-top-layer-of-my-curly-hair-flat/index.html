Why Is The Top Layer Of My Curly Hair Flat

Written by Christine Sep 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you have curly hair that looks great at the bottom but flat at the top? You're not alone. Many curly-haired people struggle with this issue. In this article, we'll explore why the top layer of your curly hair might be flat and what you can do about it.

Description

Curly hair can be beautiful and striking, but it can also be challenging to style. One of the most common problems that people with curly hair face is flatness at the top. This means that the curls on the bottom layers of your hair are full and bouncy, but the curls on the top layer are limp and lack volume.

How to

There are several reasons why the top layer of your curly hair might be flat. One of the most common is that the hair is too heavy. When curly hair is long, the weight of the hair can drag down the curls at the top, making them look flat.

Another reason why the top layer of your curly hair might be flat is that you're not using the right products. Curly hair needs moisture to look its best, and if you're not using products that hydrate your hair, the curls at the top might be limp and lifeless.

Step by Step

If you're struggling with flatness at the top of your curly hair, here are some steps you can take to fix the problem:

  1. Cut your hair: If your hair is too long, it might be dragging down your curls. Consider getting a haircut that will lighten the load at the top of your head.
  2. Use the right products: Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair. These products will help hydrate your hair and give your curls the bounce they need.
  3. Style your hair differently: If you always wear your hair parted in the middle, try parting it on the side. This can help give your hair more volume at the top.
  4. Blow-dry your hair upside down: This might sound strange, but it works! By blow-drying your hair upside down, you can help lift the curls at the top of your head.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you deal with flatness at the top of your curly hair:

  • Avoid brushing your hair: Brushing curly hair can cause frizz and damage. Instead, use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid heat styling: Curling irons and straighteners can cause damage to your hair and make it look flat. Try to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Protect your hair while sleeping: Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction while you sleep. You can also wrap your hair in a silk scarf to protect it.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about flatness at the top of curly hair:

Q. Can genetics cause flatness at the top of curly hair?

A. Yes, genetics can play a role in the texture and thickness of your hair. If other members of your family have flatness at the top of their curly hair, you might be more likely to experience the same problem.

Q. Can using too much product cause flatness at the top of curly hair?

A. Yes, using too much product can weigh down your hair and make it look flat. Be sure to use only the recommended amount of product, and avoid using too many styling products at once.

Q. Can washing your hair too often cause flatness at the top of curly hair?

A. Yes, washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can make it look flat. Try to wash your hair no more than two or three times per week.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for keeping curly hair healthy and full of volume. Here are some tips for taking care of your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo: Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can make it look flat. Look for a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo that won't dry out your hair.
  • Deep condition regularly: Curly hair needs moisture to look its best. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals: Chemical treatments like perms and relaxers can damage your hair and make it look flat. Try to avoid using these treatments as much as possible.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can get rid of flatness at the top of your curly hair and enjoy bouncy, voluminous curls.
