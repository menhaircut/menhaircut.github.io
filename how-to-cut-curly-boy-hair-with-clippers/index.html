How To Cut Curly Boy Hair With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jul 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a parent or caregiver of a curly-haired boy, you know that cutting their hair can be a challenge. Using clippers can make the process easier and quicker, but it's important to know how to do it properly to avoid any mishaps. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps of how to cut curly boy hair with clippers.

How to Cut Curly Boy Hair with Clippers

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary supplies. You'll need a set of clippers with guards, scissors, a comb, a spray bottle of water, and a towel.

Step 2: Prep the Hair

Start by washing and drying the hair. Then, use the spray bottle to dampen the hair. This will help the clippers glide smoothly through the hair.

Step 3: Choose the Right Guard

The guard you choose will determine the length of the hair. For a shorter cut, use a smaller guard, and for a longer cut, use a larger guard.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Begin by using the clippers at the nape of the neck, moving upwards towards the crown. Use the comb to lift the hair and clip it using the guard. Continue around the sides and top of the head, being careful to follow the natural curve of the head.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Use scissors to blend the hair on top of the head with the hair on the sides. You can do this by cutting the hair at an angle, starting at the top and working your way down.

Step 6: Check for Any Uneven Spots

Take a step back and look at the haircut from all angles. If you notice any uneven spots, go back and trim them with the clippers or scissors.

Step 7: Clean Up

Use the towel to wipe away any excess hair. Then, wash and dry the hair again to remove any loose hairs.

Tips for Cutting Curly Boy Hair with Clippers

- Use a spray bottle to keep the hair damp while cutting.

- Start with a larger guard and work your way down to a smaller guard for a more gradual fade.

- Take breaks to step back and look at the haircut from all angles.

- Don't rush the process. Take your time to avoid any mistakes.

FAQ

How often should I cut my curly boy's hair?

This depends on how fast their hair grows and how long you want it to be. If you want a shorter cut, you may need to cut it every 4-6 weeks. If you want a longer cut, you may be able to wait 6-8 weeks.

Can I cut curly boy hair with scissors instead of clippers?

Yes, you can cut curly boy hair with scissors, but it may take longer and be more difficult to get an even cut. Clippers are faster and easier to use, especially for shorter cuts.

Hair Care for Curly Boys

To keep curly hair healthy and manageable, it's important to use the right products and techniques. Here are some tips:

- Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out the hair.

- Detangle the hair gently with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.

- Avoid brushing curly hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz.

- Use a leave-in conditioner or styling cream to define the curls and control frizz.

Cutting curly boy hair with clippers may seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a breeze. Follow these steps and tips for a successful haircut every time.
