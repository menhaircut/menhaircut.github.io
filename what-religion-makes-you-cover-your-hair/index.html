What Religion Makes You Cover Your Hair

Written by William Jul 22, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you have ever wondered why some women cover their hair, it is because of their religious beliefs. In many religions, covering the hair is seen as a symbol of modesty and piety. In this article, we will explore which religions require women to cover their hair and why.

Table of Contents

  • Islam
  • Judaism
  • Christianity
  • Sikhism
  • Hinduism
  • Buddhism

Islam

In Islam, covering the hair is considered a religious obligation for women who have reached puberty. It is seen as a way to maintain modesty and protect the woman's beauty from the gaze of men who are not related to her. The head covering is called a hijab, which can be a scarf, shawl, or veil that covers the hair, neck, and sometimes the face.

There are different types of head coverings in Islam, which vary by region and culture. For example, some women wear a burqa or niqab, which covers the entire face except for the eyes. Others wear a chador or abaya, which is a long cloak that covers the entire body. The type of hijab a woman wears also depends on personal preference and cultural norms.

How to Wear a Hijab

Wearing a hijab is a personal choice, but if you want to wear it, here are some steps to follow:

  1. Start by choosing a hijab that suits your style and comfort level.
  2. Put your hair in a bun or ponytail at the back of your head.
  3. Place the hijab over your head, with the longer end on one side.
  4. Wrap the longer end around your head, covering your neck and chin.
  5. Bring the longer end over your head and tuck it behind your opposite ear.
  6. Adjust the hijab to cover your hair and neck, and make sure it is comfortable.

Remember that wearing a hijab is a personal choice, and you should only wear it if you feel comfortable and confident.

Tips for Wearing a Hijab

If you are new to wearing a hijab, here are some tips to make it easier:

  • Start by wearing a simple hijab that is easy to wrap and adjust.
  • Choose a hijab that matches your outfit and style.
  • Experiment with different styles and fabrics to find what works best for you.
  • Use pins or clips to secure the hijab in place.
  • Practice wrapping the hijab at home before wearing it in public.

Hair Care with a Hijab

Wearing a hijab can affect your hair, especially if you wear it for long periods. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy while wearing a hijab:

  • Wash your hair regularly to prevent oil buildup and dandruff.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can damage your hair and scalp.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Massage your scalp to improve blood circulation and prevent hair loss.
  • Avoid wearing a hijab while your hair is wet to prevent mold and odor.

Judaism

In Judaism, covering the hair is a tradition for married women. It is seen as a sign of modesty and a way to show that the woman is taken. The head covering is called a sheitel, which is a wig or hair covering that covers the woman's hair completely.

The sheitel can be made of human or synthetic hair and is styled to match the woman's natural hair. It is worn in public and sometimes even at home, depending on the woman's level of observance.

How to Wear a Sheitel

If you want to wear a sheitel, here are some steps to follow:

  1. Choose a sheitel that matches your hair color and style.
  2. Put your hair in a bun or ponytail at the back of your head.
  3. Place the sheitel over your head, with the front edge just above your hairline.
  4. Secure the sheitel with bobby pins or clips.
  5. Style the sheitel to match your natural hair.

Remember that wearing a sheitel is a personal choice, and you should only wear it if you feel comfortable and confident.

Tips for Wearing a Sheitel

If you are new to wearing a sheitel, here are some tips to make it easier:

  • Choose a sheitel that is lightweight and comfortable to wear.
  • Use bobby pins or clips to secure the sheitel in place.
  • Style the sheitel to match your natural hair.
  • Wash the sheitel regularly to keep it clean and fresh.
  • Store the sheitel on a wig stand to maintain its shape.

Hair Care with a Sheitel

Wearing a sheitel can affect your hair, especially if you wear it for long periods. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy while wearing a sheitel:

  • Wash your hair regularly to prevent oil buildup and dandruff.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can damage your hair and scalp.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Massage your scalp to improve blood circulation and prevent hair loss.
  • Avoid wearing a sheitel while your hair is wet to prevent mold and odor.

Christianity

In Christianity, covering the hair is not a requirement, but some Christian denominations encourage women to cover their heads during worship. It is seen as a sign of respect and submission to God, as well as a way to differentiate between men and women.

The head covering can be a hat, scarf, veil, or other type of head covering that covers the hair and sometimes the face. The style and type of head covering varies by denomination and culture.

How to Wear a Head Covering

If you want to wear a head covering in church, here are some steps to follow:

  1. Choose a head covering that is appropriate for your denomination and culture.
  2. Place the head covering over your head, covering your hair and sometimes your face.
  3. Secure the head covering with pins or clips.
  4. Adjust the head covering to make sure it is comfortable and secure.

Remember that wearing a head covering is a personal choice, and you should only wear it if you feel comfortable and confident.

Tips for Wearing a Head Covering

If you are new to wearing a head covering, here are some tips to make it easier:

  • Choose a head covering that is comfortable and easy to wear.
  • Practice wearing the head covering at home before wearing it in church.
  • Choose a head covering that matches your outfit and style.
  • Use pins or clips to secure the head covering in place.
  • Experiment with different types of head coverings to find what works best for you.

Hair Care with a Head Covering

Wearing a head covering can affect your hair, especially if you wear it for long periods. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy while wearing a head covering:

  • Wash your hair regularly to prevent oil buildup and dandruff.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can damage your hair and scalp.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Massage your scalp to improve blood circulation and prevent hair loss.
  • Avoid wearing a head covering while your hair is wet to prevent mold and odor.
