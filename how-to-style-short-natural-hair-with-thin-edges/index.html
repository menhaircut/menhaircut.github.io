How To Style Short Natural Hair With Thin Edges

If you have short natural hair with thin edges, you may be wondering how to style it without causing further damage. Fortunately, there are many stylish and protective hairstyles that you can try. In this post, we'll provide tips on how to style your hair, step-by-step instructions, and hair care advice.

How to Style Short Natural Hair with Thin Edges

When styling short natural hair with thin edges, it's important to choose hairstyles that are gentle and won't cause further damage. Here are some styles to consider:

Braids or Twists

Braids and twists are a great option for short natural hair with thin edges. They can be done with or without extensions, and they provide protection for your hair while also allowing it to rest and grow. To ensure that your edges are protected, make sure that your braids or twists are not too tight.

Tapered Cut

A tapered cut is a short haircut that is longer on top and shorter on the sides and back. This style can be achieved with clippers or scissors, and it's a great way to showcase your natural hair texture while also giving your hair a new shape. This style is also very low maintenance and easy to maintain.

Finger Coils

Finger coils are a great way to define your natural curl pattern and add texture to your short hair. To create finger coils, you'll need a styling cream or gel and your fingers. Simply apply the product to your hair and twirl small sections of hair around your fingers. This style is low maintenance and can last for several days.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here are step-by-step instructions for creating finger coils:

  1. Wash and condition your hair.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair.
  3. Apply a styling cream or gel to your hair.
  4. Take a small section of hair and twirl it around your finger until it coils.
  5. Repeat until all of your hair is coiled.
  6. Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair.

Tips for Styling Short Natural Hair with Thin Edges

When styling short natural hair with thin edges, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid styles that are too tight, as they can cause further damage to your edges.
  • Protect your edges by using a satin scarf or bonnet at night.
  • Moisturize your hair regularly to prevent breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using heat on your hair too frequently.

FAQs

What causes thin edges?

Thin edges can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, tight hairstyles, chemical damage, and poor hair care practices.

Can natural hair grow back edges?

Yes, natural hair can grow back edges with proper hair care and healthy hair practices.

How can I protect my edges while sleeping?

You can protect your edges while sleeping by using a satin scarf or bonnet to prevent friction and breakage.

Hair Care for Short Natural Hair with Thin Edges

When caring for short natural hair with thin edges, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly to prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using heat on your hair too frequently.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can cause further damage to your edges.
  • Protect your edges by using a satin scarf or bonnet at night.

By following these tips and trying out some of the hairstyles we suggested, you can style your short natural hair with thin edges in a way that is both stylish and protective. Remember to care for your hair regularly to promote healthy growth and prevent further damage.
