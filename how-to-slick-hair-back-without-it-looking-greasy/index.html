How To Slick Hair Back Without It Looking Greasy

Written by Oliver Jul 22, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of your hair looking greasy and weighed down when you try to slick it back? Well, you’re not alone. Many people struggle with finding the right products and techniques to achieve a sleek and polished look without the unwanted grease. In this article, we’ll show you how to slick your hair back without it looking greasy, so you can rock that stylish look with confidence.

Description

Slicking your hair back is a classic, sophisticated look that can be dressed up or down depending on the occasion. However, getting it right can be tricky. If you’re using the wrong products or techniques, your hair can end up looking greasy and heavy, which is not the look you want to achieve. This article will guide you through the steps to achieving a sleek and polished look without the greasy residue.

How to Slick Hair Back Without It Looking Greasy

Step 1: Start with clean hair

The first step to achieving a sleek and polished look when slicking your hair back is to start with clean hair. This will ensure that there is no excess oil or product buildup on your scalp, which can contribute to a greasy look. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup and follow up with a lightweight conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.

Step 2: Apply a lightweight styling product

Once your hair is clean and dry, apply a lightweight styling product that is designed to provide hold without weighing your hair down. Look for products that are labeled as “matte” or “low shine” to avoid the greasy look. A small amount of product goes a long way, so start with a dime-sized amount and work it through your hair from root to tip.

Step 3: Use a fine-tooth comb

Using a fine-tooth comb, comb your hair straight back from your forehead. Be sure to comb all of your hair back, including the hair at the sides and the crown of your head. This will ensure that your hair stays in place and doesn’t fall out of the slicked-back style.

Step 4: Smooth out any bumps

If you notice any bumps or lumps in your hair, use your comb to smooth them out. This will help to create a polished look and prevent any unevenness in your hairstyle.

Step 5: Finish with hairspray

Once your hair is slicked back and styled to your liking, finish with a light mist of hairspray to hold the style in place. Be sure to choose a hairspray that is lightweight and won’t leave your hair looking stiff or crunchy.

Tips

- Use a blow dryer to dry your hair after applying the styling product for added hold and volume.

- Avoid using too much product, as this can lead to a greasy look.

- If you have fine or thin hair, use a volumizing product before applying the styling product to give your hair more body and texture.

FAQ

Q: Can I slick my hair back if I have curly hair?

A: Yes, you can slick back curly hair. However, you may need to use a stronger hold product to keep your curls in place.

Q: How often should I wash my hair if I want to slick it back?

A: It’s best to wash your hair every other day to avoid excess oil buildup on your scalp.

Hair Care

It’s important to take care of your hair to maintain a healthy scalp and promote hair growth. Be sure to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type. Avoid using hot tools such as hair straighteners and curling irons too often, as this can cause damage to your hair. Incorporate a weekly deep conditioning treatment to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.
