Can You Start Dreads With Long Hair

Written by William Jul 31, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you have long hair and are considering starting dreadlocks, you may be wondering if it's possible. The answer is yes, you can start dreads with long hair! In fact, having long hair can make the process easier and produce longer, thicker dreads. In this blog post, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to start dreads with long hair, as well as tips for maintaining and caring for your new hairstyle.

How to Start Dreads with Long Hair

Starting dreadlocks with long hair is a process that requires patience and dedication. Here are the steps you can follow:

Step 1: Decide on the Method

There are several methods you can use to start your dreadlocks, including the backcombing method, twist and rip method, and the neglect method. Research each method and choose the one that best suits your hair type and personal preferences.

Step 2: Wash Your Hair

Before starting the dreadlocking process, make sure your hair is clean and free of any products or build-up. Use a clarifying shampoo to thoroughly cleanse your hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, section your hair into the size of dreads you desire. Keep in mind that the smaller the sections, the more dreads you will have. Use hair ties or clips to secure each section.

Step 4: Begin the Method

Using your chosen method, start dreadlocking each section of hair. Follow the instructions carefully and take your time to ensure each dread is formed properly.

Step 5: Allow Time for Your Dreads to Form

Once you have completed the dreadlocking process, give your hair time to naturally form and tighten the dreads. This can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months.

Tips for Maintaining and Caring for Your Dreads

Now that you have started your dreadlocks, it's important to properly care for them to ensure they stay healthy and continue to look great. Here are some tips:

Tip 1: Wash Your Hair Regularly

Contrary to popular belief, dreadlocks need to be washed regularly to keep them clean and healthy. Use a residue-free shampoo and make sure to thoroughly rinse your hair to avoid build-up.

Tip 2: Avoid Using Conditioner

Conditioner can loosen your dreads and undo the progress you have made. Avoid using conditioner, or use a very small amount if necessary.

Tip 3: Be Gentle When Styling Your Hair

Avoid tight hairstyles or pulling your dreads too tightly. Be gentle when styling your hair to prevent breakage and damage.

Tip 4: Use a Satin or Silk Pillowcase

Cotton pillowcases can cause friction and damage to your dreads. Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent this.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I Start Dreads with Relaxed Hair?

Yes, you can start dreads with relaxed hair. Just keep in mind that relaxed hair is more fragile and may require extra care.

Can I Wash My Dreads in Chlorine Water?

It's best to avoid washing your dreads in chlorine water, as it can damage your hair and cause it to become brittle.

How Often Should I Retwist My Dreads?

Retwisting your dreads too often can cause them to become thin and weak. It's best to retwist them every 4-6 weeks.

Conclusion

Starting dreadlocks with long hair is possible and can produce beautiful, thick dreads. Follow the steps and tips outlined in this blog post to ensure a successful dreadlocking process and healthy-looking dreads. Remember to be patient and enjoy the journey!
