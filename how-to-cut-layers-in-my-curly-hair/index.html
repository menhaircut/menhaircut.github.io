How To Cut Layers In My Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 29, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of your curly hair looking flat and lifeless? Adding layers can give your curls more volume and bounce. But how do you cut layers in curly hair without making a mess? In this guide, we will show you how to cut layers in your curly hair at home.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Layers in My Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Layers in Curly Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Description

Layering is a technique where hair is cut at different lengths, creating dimension and movement. Layers can add volume to curly hair and prevent it from falling flat. However, cutting curly hair can be more challenging than cutting straight hair. Curly hair tends to shrink when dry, making it difficult to predict the final length of the cut. Additionally, curly hair can be prone to frizz, and cutting it incorrectly can make the frizz worse.

How to Cut Layers in My Curly Hair

If you’re feeling brave, you can try cutting your curly hair at home. However, we recommend going to a professional stylist if you’re unsure about cutting your hair yourself. A stylist will be able to assess your hair type and give you personalized advice.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with freshly washed and detangled hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls.
  2. Divide your hair into sections. Use clips or hair ties to separate your hair into manageable sections. The number of sections you create depends on the thickness and length of your hair.
  3. Decide on the length of the layers. Use a ruler or measuring tape to measure the length of the shortest layer you want. Keep in mind that curly hair will shrink when dry, so it’s better to cut less than you think you need.
  4. Start with the bottom layer. Take a small section of hair from the back of your head and comb it straight. Hold the section between your fingers at the length you want to cut. Cut the hair straight across using sharp scissors.
  5. Move on to the next layer. Take a section of hair from above the previously cut layer and repeat the same process. Make sure the length of the new layer is longer than the previous layer.
  6. Continue cutting the remaining layers. Work your way up to the top of your head, cutting each layer at a slightly longer length than the previous layer.
  7. Trim the ends. Once you’ve cut all the layers, check the overall shape of your hair. Use scissors to trim any uneven ends or stray hairs.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Curly Hair

  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Don’t cut too much at once. It’s better to cut less than you think you need and go back for more if necessary.
  • Be patient. Cutting curly hair takes time and precision. Don’t rush the process.
  • Consider getting a curly haircut. A professional stylist who specializes in curly hair can give you a personalized cut that enhances your curls.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut my curly hair when it’s wet or dry?

A: It’s best to cut curly hair when it’s dry. This allows you to see how much your hair will shrink when it dries and prevents you from cutting too much.

Q: How often should I cut my curly hair?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it becomes damaged. Generally, getting a trim every 6-8 weeks can help prevent split ends and breakage.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Now that you know how to cut layers in your curly hair, it’s important to take care of your curls to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy, bouncy curls:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling wands, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to damp hair to define your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Sleep on a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent your hair from tangling and breaking.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.

Cutting layers in curly hair can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a fresh, bouncy look. Remember to take care of your curls by using gentle products and avoiding heat styling. Happy cutting!
