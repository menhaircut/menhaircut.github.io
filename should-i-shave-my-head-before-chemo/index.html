Should I Shave My Head Before Chemo

Written by Oliver Jul 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Welcome to our blog post about whether or not you should shave your head before starting chemotherapy. Many patients who are about to undergo chemotherapy wonder whether or not they should shave their heads. In this article, we will provide you with information and tips to help you make the best decision for yourself.

Table of Content

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Chemotherapy is a treatment that uses powerful drugs to kill cancer cells. Unfortunately, it can also damage healthy cells, including hair follicles. As a result, many people who undergo chemotherapy experience hair loss.

Some people choose to shave their heads before starting chemotherapy, while others prefer to wait and see how much hair they lose before making a decision. There is no right or wrong answer, and it ultimately comes down to personal preference.

How to

If you do decide to shave your head before starting chemotherapy, here are some tips to help you do it safely and effectively:

1. Use Clippers

Using clippers is the safest and most effective way to shave your head. Begin by using a longer guard and gradually work your way down to a shorter one. This will help prevent cuts and nicks.

2. Get Help

Shaving your head can be difficult, especially if you have never done it before. Consider asking a friend or family member to help you. They can assist with hard-to-reach areas and provide support.

3. Moisturize

After shaving your head, it is important to moisturize your scalp. Use a gentle, fragrance-free moisturizer to prevent dryness and irritation.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide to help you shave your head before starting chemotherapy:

1. Gather Supplies

You will need clippers, a mirror, a towel, and moisturizer.

2. Cut Hair Short

Begin by cutting your hair as short as possible with the clippers.

3. Wet Hair

Wet your hair with warm water and wrap a towel around your shoulders to catch any loose hair.

4. Shave

Using the clippers, shave your head in the direction of hair growth. Start with a longer guard and gradually work your way down to a shorter one.

5. Moisturize

After shaving, apply a gentle, fragrance-free moisturizer to your scalp.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you prepare for hair loss during chemotherapy:

1. Cut Hair Short

Cutting your hair short before starting chemotherapy can make the hair loss process less traumatic.

2. Consider a Wig

If you are uncomfortable with the idea of going bald, consider purchasing a wig before starting chemotherapy.

3. Protect Your Scalp

After hair loss, your scalp may be more sensitive to the sun. Be sure to wear a hat or scarf to protect your skin.

FAQ

1. Will I Definitely Lose My Hair During Chemotherapy?

Not all chemotherapy drugs cause hair loss, and the severity of hair loss can vary depending on the drug and dosage used.

2. How Long Will It Take for My Hair to Grow Back?

It can take several months for hair to grow back after chemotherapy. The rate and thickness of regrowth can also vary.

3. Is Shaving My Head Necessary?

No, shaving your head is not necessary. Many people choose to wait and see how much hair they lose before making a decision.

Hair Care

If you decide not to shave your head before chemotherapy, here are some tips to help you care for your hair during treatment:

1. Be Gentle

Avoid harsh chemicals and treatments that can further damage your hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools.

2. Protect Your Hair

Wear a scarf or hat to protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather.

3. Consider a Haircut

Cutting your hair short can make it easier to manage during treatment.

In conclusion, whether or not to shave your head before chemotherapy is a personal decision. If you do decide to shave, be sure to follow the tips and steps outlined in this article. And if you choose to keep your hair, remember to be gentle and take care of it during treatment.
