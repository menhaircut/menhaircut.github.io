How To Stop Natural Hair From Frizzing After Straightening

Written by Bardi Aug 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Straightening natural hair can give a sleek and polished look, but it can also lead to frizz if not done properly. If you're tired of dealing with frizzy hair after straightening, don't worry, we've got you covered. In this blog post, we'll show you how to stop natural hair from frizzing after straightening with our step-by-step guide, tips, and FAQs.

Description

Frizz is a common problem that many naturalistas face after straightening their hair. It's caused by the hair cuticles lifting and exposing the hair shaft to moisture, which leads to frizz. But don't worry, with the right techniques and products, you can prevent frizz and keep your straightened hair looking flawless.

How to

Here are our top tips on how to stop natural hair from frizzing after straightening:

1. Start with clean hair

Before straightening your hair, make sure it's clean and free of any product buildup. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any excess oil or product from your hair.

2. Use a heat protectant

Apply a heat protectant to your hair before straightening to prevent heat damage and frizz. Make sure to evenly distribute the product throughout your hair.

3. Use a high-quality flat iron

Invest in a high-quality flat iron with ceramic plates that distribute heat evenly and prevent hot spots. Don't skimp on the quality of your flat iron as it can make a huge difference in preventing frizz.

4. Straighten small sections of hair

Don't try to straighten large sections of hair at once. Instead, work in small sections and use a fine-tooth comb to detangle your hair before straightening. This will prevent tangles and frizz.

5. Don't overheat your hair

Use the lowest heat setting possible to straighten your hair. Don't go over the same section of hair multiple times as this can lead to heat damage and frizz.

6. Finish with a hair oil

After straightening your hair, apply a hair oil to seal in moisture and prevent frizz. Use a small amount of oil and evenly distribute it throughout your hair.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to stop natural hair from frizzing after straightening:

Step 1: Start with clean hair

Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup from your hair.

Step 2: Apply a heat protectant

Spray a heat protectant throughout your hair to prevent heat damage and frizz.

Step 3: Work in small sections

Divide your hair into small sections and use a fine-tooth comb to detangle before straightening.

Step 4: Straighten your hair

Use a high-quality flat iron and straighten your hair in small sections, using the lowest heat setting possible.

Step 5: Apply hair oil

After straightening, apply a hair oil to seal in moisture and prevent frizz.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help prevent frizz after straightening:

1. Don't straighten wet hair

Wait until your hair is completely dry before straightening to prevent frizz.

2. Use a silk scarf or bonnet

Wrap your hair in a silk scarf or bonnet at night to prevent frizz while you sleep.

3. Don't touch your hair

Avoid touching your hair throughout the day as this can lead to frizz.

FAQ

Q: Can I straighten my hair without a heat protectant?

A: No, using a heat protectant is essential to prevent heat damage and frizz.

Q: Can I use a hair serum instead of a hair oil?

A: Yes, you can use a hair serum instead of a hair oil to prevent frizz after straightening.

Q: How often should I straighten my hair?

A: It's best to limit heat styling to once a week to prevent damage and frizz.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential to prevent frizz after straightening. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

1. Deep condition regularly

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish and moisturize your hair.

2. Protect your hair

Wear a hat or scarf when out in the sun to protect your hair from UV damage.

3. Use sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, leading to dryness and frizz. Use a sulfate-free shampoo to maintain healthy hair.

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can prevent frizz after straightening and keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.
