How To Trim The Back Of Your Short Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Sep 30, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have short hair, you know how important it is to keep it trimmed and looking its best. However, going to the hairdresser every few weeks can be expensive, time-consuming, and not always necessary. If you're looking to save some money and take care of your hair yourself, learning how to trim the back of your short hair is a great place to start. In this article, we'll show you how to do just that, with step-by-step instructions and helpful tips to make the process as easy and stress-free as possible.

How to Trim the Back of Your Short Hair: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step is to gather all the tools you'll need. You'll need a good quality pair of hairdressing scissors, a comb, and a handheld mirror. Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean, as dull or dirty scissors can damage your hair.

Related Keywords: hairdressing scissors, comb, handheld mirror, sharp, clean, damage

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start trimming, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to see where you need to cut and will ensure a more even finish.

Related Keywords: wash, dry, clean, even finish

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, create a horizontal parting across the back of your head, separating the top section of your hair from the bottom section. Clip the top section out of the way so you can focus on the bottom section.

Related Keywords: section, horizontal parting, clip, focus

Step 4: Start Trimming

Starting at the center of the back of your head, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and snip off the ends with your scissors. Be sure to cut only a small amount at a time, as it's easier to cut more if needed than to fix a mistake if you've cut too much.

Related Keywords: center, small section, comb, straight down, snip, ends, small amount, mistake

Step 5: Move Outward

Continue working outward from the center of your head, taking small sections of hair and trimming them to the desired length. Make sure to keep your cuts even and to check your progress in the handheld mirror to ensure symmetry.

Related Keywords: outward, small sections, desired length, even cuts, handheld mirror, symmetry

Step 6: Repeat on the Top Section

Once you've finished the bottom section, unclip the top section and repeat the process, starting at the center and working outward. Be sure to blend the top and bottom sections together by cutting any longer pieces so they match the shorter pieces.

Related Keywords: unclip, blend, longer pieces, match, shorter pieces

Step 7: Check Your Work

Once you've finished trimming, check the back of your head in the handheld mirror to make sure everything looks even and symmetrical. If you notice any areas that need touching up, repeat the process until you're happy with the results.

Related Keywords: check, even, symmetrical, touching up, results

Tips for Trimming the Back of Your Short Hair

Now that you know how to trim the back of your short hair yourself, here are a few tips to help you get the best results:

  • Start with small sections and work outward, cutting only a little at a time.
  • Use a handheld mirror to check your progress and ensure symmetry.
  • Don't be afraid to ask a friend or family member for help if you need it.
  • Invest in a good quality pair of hairdressing scissors to ensure clean cuts.

FAQs About Trimming the Back of Your Short Hair

Here are some common questions people have about trimming the back of their short hair:

Do I need to wet my hair before trimming?

No, you don't need to wet your hair before trimming. However, it's a good idea to wash and dry your hair beforehand to ensure an even finish.

How often should I trim the back of my short hair?

How often you should trim your hair depends on how quickly it grows and how quickly you want to maintain your desired length. As a general rule, it's a good idea to trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking its best.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake while trimming your hair, don't panic! Take a deep breath and try to fix the mistake as best you can. If the mistake is too severe, you can always go to a professional hairdresser to have it fixed.

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

Now that you've learned how to trim the back of your short hair yourself, here are some additional tips to help you take care of your hair:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically formulated for short hair.
  • Avoid using too many styling products, as this can weigh your hair down.
  • Incorporate regular hair treatments into your routine to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.

By following these tips and learning how to trim the back of your short hair yourself, you'll be able to keep your hair looking its best without breaking the bank. With a little practice and patience, you'll soon be a pro at trimming your own hair!
