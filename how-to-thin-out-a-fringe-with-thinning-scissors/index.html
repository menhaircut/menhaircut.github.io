How To Thin Out A Fringe With Thinning Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Nov 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have a thick and heavy fringe that you want to thin out, you don't have to go to a salon and spend a lot of money. You can do it yourself at home using thinning scissors. Here, we will guide you through the process of thinning out your fringe with thinning scissors.

Description

Thinning scissors are a type of scissors that have teeth on one blade and a smooth edge on the other. These scissors are used to remove bulk from hair without changing the overall length. They are commonly used for thinning out thick hair, especially around the edges and the fringe.

How to Thin Out a Fringe with Thinning Scissors

Before you start thinning out your fringe, make sure that your hair is clean and dry. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair. Use a comb to separate your fringe from the rest of your hair. Clip the rest of your hair away so that you can focus on thinning out your fringe.

Step 2: Comb Your Fringe

Use a comb to comb your fringe. Make sure that the hair is smooth and tangle-free. This will make it easier to cut.

Step 3: Hold Your Scissors Correctly

Hold the thinning scissors with the teeth pointing up. Use your thumb and your middle finger to open and close the scissors. Keep your index finger on the other blade for support.

Step 4: Start Cutting Your Fringe

Start cutting your fringe with the thinning scissors. Hold the scissors vertically and make small snips along the length of your fringe. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once. You can always go back and thin out more hair if needed.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

Check your progress as you go. Use a mirror to see how your fringe looks from different angles. If you see any areas that need more thinning, repeat the process until you are satisfied with the results.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out your fringe:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and thin out more if needed.
  • Always cut vertically, not horizontally.
  • Don't cut too close to the roots. Leave at least half an inch of hair.
  • Take breaks if your hand gets tired.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning out a fringe with thinning scissors:

Can I use regular scissors instead of thinning scissors?

You can, but regular scissors will remove more hair and change the overall length of your fringe. Thinning scissors are designed to remove bulk without changing the length.

Can I thin out my fringe if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can. Just be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as curly hair tends to bounce back and look shorter when dry.

How often should I thin out my fringe?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it gets thick. Generally, you can thin out your fringe every 2-3 months.

Hair Care

Thinning out your fringe can make it look lighter and more manageable. However, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage. Here are some tips for healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to protect your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Thinning out a fringe with thinning scissors is a simple and affordable way to change up your look. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a light and airy fringe that complements your face shape and style.
