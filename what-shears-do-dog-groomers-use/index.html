What Shears Do Dog Groomers Use

Written by Frank Aug 02, 2023 · 4 min read

As a dog groomer, using the right tools is crucial to providing top-notch grooming services. One of the essential tools that every dog groomer needs is a pair of shears. But what shears do dog groomers use? In this article, we’ll explore the different types of shears that dog groomers use and how to choose the right one for your grooming needs.

Description

Shears are scissors that are specifically designed for grooming dogs. They come in different sizes, shapes, and styles to suit different grooming needs. The blades of shears are usually made of high-quality stainless steel or titanium, which makes them durable and long-lasting. The handles are also designed to provide a comfortable grip, which reduces hand fatigue and prevents accidents.

How to Choose the Right Shears

Choosing the right shears can be overwhelming, especially for novice dog groomers. Here are some factors to consider when selecting shears:

Size

The size of the shears depends on the size of the dog and the area you want to groom. For instance, a small dog requires a small pair of shears, while a large dog needs a large pair. Similarly, if you want to groom small areas like the ears, you need a small pair of shears.

Blade Type

There are two types of blades: straight and curved. Straight blades are used for cutting straight lines, while curved blades are used for trimming around the ears and paws. Some shears also come with serrated blades, which are ideal for cutting through matted hair.

Blade Length

The blade length determines the amount of hair you can cut at once. Longer blades are ideal for trimming large areas, while shorter blades are suitable for small areas like the feet and face.

Step by Step Guide on Using Shears

Using shears can be tricky, especially if you’re new to dog grooming. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to use shears:

Step 1: Prepare the Dog

Make sure the dog is clean and dry before you start grooming. Brush the hair to remove tangles and mats.

Step 2: Choose the Right Shears

Choose the right shears depending on the area you want to groom. For instance, if you want to trim the ears, use curved shears.

Step 3: Hold the Shears Properly

Hold the shears in your dominant hand and place your thumb in the thumb hole and your finger in the finger hole. Make sure your hand is relaxed and your wrist is straight.

Step 4: Trim the Hair

Start trimming the hair by holding the shears at a 45-degree angle and cutting small sections at a time. Use your other hand to hold the hair in place.

Step 5: Check for Unevenness

Check for unevenness and make sure the hair is trimmed evenly. If you notice any unevenness, use the shears to trim the hair until it’s even.

Step 6: Finish Up

Once you’re done trimming the hair, brush the dog to remove any loose hair and give them a treat to reward them for being patient.

Tips for Using Shears

Here are some tips to help you use shears effectively:

  • Keep the shears sharp to prevent pulling the hair
  • Use a comb to guide the shears when trimming the hair
  • Trim the hair in the direction of growth
  • Use a detangler spray to prevent matting
  • Start with small sections and work your way up

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about shears:

Q: Can I use regular scissors for grooming my dog?

A: No, regular scissors are not suitable for grooming dogs. They can cause injuries and discomfort to the dog.

Q: How often should I sharpen my shears?

A: You should sharpen your shears after every 5-6 grooming sessions to maintain their sharpness.

Q: What should I do if my shears get rusty?

A: If your shears get rusty, clean them with a rust remover and dry them thoroughly before using them again.

Hair Care

Grooming your dog regularly can help keep their coat healthy and shiny. Use high-quality grooming products like shampoos and conditioners to keep their hair clean and moisturized. Brush their hair regularly to prevent matting and tangles. If you notice any skin or hair problems, consult a veterinarian immediately.

In conclusion, shears are essential tools for every dog groomer. By choosing the right shears and using them correctly, you can provide top-notch grooming services to your furry clients.
