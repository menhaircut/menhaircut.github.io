Description

Point cutting is a hair cutting technique that is commonly used by hairdressers to add texture and movement to the hair. It involves cutting the hair at an angle, which creates a softer, more natural-looking effect than straight cutting. However, for those with fine hair, point cutting can be a tricky technique to master. This is because fine hair is delicate and prone to breakage, which can be exacerbated by point cutting if not done correctly.

How to Point Cut Fine Hair

The first step to point cutting fine hair is to ensure that your hair is clean and dry. You should also have a sharp pair of scissors and a comb to help you section your hair. Once you have everything you need, follow these steps:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, create a middle parting in your hair from front to back. Then, create two more partings on either side of your head, so that you have three equal sections of hair.

Step 2: Start Point Cutting

Take a small section of hair from the middle section and hold it between your fingers. Using your scissors, point cut into the hair at an angle, moving your fingers downwards as you cut. This will create a softer, more textured effect in the hair.

Step 3: Repeat on the Other Sections

Continue point cutting small sections of hair throughout the middle and side sections, taking care not to cut too much hair at once. Remember to always point cut at an angle and to move your fingers downwards as you cut.

Step by Step Guide

If you're still unsure about how to point cut fine hair, here's a step by step guide:

  1. Wash and dry your hair
  2. Section your hair into three equal parts
  3. Take a small section of hair from the middle section
  4. Hold the hair between your fingers
  5. Point cut into the hair at an angle, moving your fingers downwards as you cut
  6. Repeat on the other sections of hair, taking care not to cut too much at once
  7. Check the length and texture of your hair as you go
  8. Trim any uneven strands and blend the layers together
  9. Style your hair as desired

Tips for Point Cutting Fine Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when point cutting fine hair:

  • Use a sharp pair of scissors to avoid damaging the hair
  • Point cut small sections of hair at a time to avoid cutting too much at once
  • Use a comb to help you section your hair evenly
  • Point cut at an angle to create a softer, more natural-looking effect
  • Move your fingers downwards as you cut to avoid creating choppy layers
  • Check the length and texture of your hair as you go to ensure an even cut

Frequently Asked Questions

Is point cutting bad for fine hair?

Point cutting is not necessarily bad for fine hair, but it can be if not done correctly. Fine hair is delicate and prone to breakage, so it's important to use a light touch when point cutting and to avoid cutting too much at once.

How often should I point cut my fine hair?

The frequency of point cutting will depend on your hair type and personal preference. Generally, it's recommended to point cut fine hair every six to eight weeks to maintain its shape and texture.

Can I point cut my own fine hair?

It is possible to point cut your own fine hair, but it can be tricky. It's important to have a sharp pair of scissors and to take your time when cutting. If you're unsure, it's best to visit a professional hairdresser.

Hair Care After Point Cutting Fine Hair

After point cutting your fine hair, it's important to take good care of it to prevent breakage and damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is designed for fine hair
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair gently
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair

By following these tips, you can ensure that your fine hair looks and feels its best after point cutting.
