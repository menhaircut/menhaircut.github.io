How To Make Your Hair Not Frizzy After Straightening

Written by Petter Nov 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending hours straightening your hair only to have it turn into a frizzy mess as soon as you step outside? Don't worry, you're not alone. Frizzy hair is a common problem, especially for those with curly or wavy hair. But the good news is that there are ways to prevent frizz and keep your hair looking smooth and sleek. In this article, we will discuss how to make your hair not frizzy after straightening.

Description

Frizz occurs when the hair cuticle becomes raised, allowing moisture to enter and cause the hair to swell. This can happen for a number of reasons, including humidity, heat damage, and using the wrong hair products. When you straighten your hair, you are essentially flattening the hair cuticle, which can help to prevent frizz. However, if you don't take the proper steps to maintain your straightened hair, it can quickly become frizzy again.

How to

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

The first step to preventing frizz is to start with clean hair. Make sure to use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup from hair products, which can weigh down your hair and make it more prone to frizz. Follow up with a moisturizing conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.

Step 2: Use a Heat Protectant

Before straightening your hair, make sure to apply a heat protectant to your hair. This will help to prevent heat damage and keep your hair looking shiny and healthy. Apply the heat protectant evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

Step 3: Straighten Your Hair in Sections

When straightening your hair, it's important to work in small sections. This will ensure that each section is straightened evenly and will help to prevent frizz. Use a straightening iron with ceramic plates, which will distribute heat evenly and help to prevent hot spots.

Step 4: Use a Serum or Oil

After straightening your hair, apply a serum or oil to your hair to keep it looking smooth and shiny. Use a small amount and apply it evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to prevent frizz:

  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.
  • Avoid using heavy hair products, which can weigh down your hair and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too often, as this can cause heat damage and make your hair more prone to frizz.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase, which can help to prevent frizz while you sleep.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about preventing frizz:

What causes frizz?

Frizz can be caused by a number of factors, including humidity, heat damage, and using the wrong hair products.

Can you prevent frizz?

Yes, you can prevent frizz by using the proper hair products, avoiding touching your hair too much, and using hot styling tools in moderation.

How do you keep straightened hair from frizzing?

To keep straightened hair from frizzing, use a heat protectant, straighten your hair in sections, and use a serum or oil to keep your hair looking smooth and shiny.

Hair Care

Preventing frizz is just one part of maintaining healthy hair. Here are some additional tips for keeping your hair looking its best:

  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too often, as this can cause heat damage.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals, which can help to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

Conclusion

Frizzy hair can be frustrating, especially after spending time straightening your hair. But with the proper hair care routine, you can prevent frizz and keep your hair looking smooth and sleek. Remember to start with clean hair, use a heat protectant, straighten your hair in sections, and use a serum or oil to keep your hair looking shiny and healthy. With these tips, you'll be able to enjoy smooth, frizz-free hair all day long.
