Can I Change My Face Shape With Surgery

Written by William Oct 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you are unhappy with the shape of your face, you may be wondering if it's possible to change it with surgery. This article will explore the different types of facial surgery that can alter your face shape and answer some common questions about the process.

Description

There are several types of facial surgery that can change the shape of your face. These include:

  • Rhinoplasty (nose job)
  • Facelift
  • Brow lift
  • Cheek implants
  • Jaw surgery

Each of these procedures targets a different area of the face and can have a significant impact on your overall face shape.

How to

The first step in changing your face shape with surgery is to schedule a consultation with a plastic surgeon. During this consultation, the surgeon will examine your face and discuss your goals for the procedure. They will also explain the risks and benefits of each type of surgery and help you choose the best option for your needs.

Once you have chosen a procedure, you will need to prepare for the surgery. This may involve quitting smoking, avoiding certain medications, and arranging for time off work or school. The surgery itself will be performed under anesthesia and may take several hours, depending on the complexity of the procedure.

After the surgery, you will need to follow your surgeon's instructions for recovery. This may include taking pain medication, avoiding strenuous activities, and keeping your head elevated. It's important to follow these instructions carefully to ensure a smooth recovery and optimal results.

Step by Step

The process of changing your face shape with surgery typically involves the following steps:

  1. Consultation with a plastic surgeon
  2. Selection of a procedure
  3. Preparation for surgery
  4. Surgery under anesthesia
  5. Recovery and follow-up care

Tips

If you are considering changing your face shape with surgery, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Choose a reputable plastic surgeon with experience in the specific procedure you are considering
  • Be realistic about your expectations and understand that surgery may not produce "perfect" results
  • Follow your surgeon's instructions for preparation, surgery, and recovery
  • Take good care of your skin and overall health to maintain the results of the surgery

FAQ

1. Is it safe to change my face shape with surgery?

Facial surgery is generally safe when performed by a qualified and experienced plastic surgeon. However, as with any surgery, there are risks involved, such as infection, bleeding, and adverse reactions to anesthesia. Your surgeon will explain these risks to you and help you make an informed decision about whether or not to proceed with the procedure.

2. How long does it take to recover from facial surgery?

The recovery time for facial surgery varies depending on the type of procedure, your overall health, and other factors. In general, you can expect to take a week or more off work or school and avoid strenuous activities for several weeks. Your surgeon will provide specific instructions for your recovery and follow-up care.

3. Will I look completely different after facial surgery?

Facial surgery can produce significant changes in your appearance, but it's important to have realistic expectations. Your surgeon will work with you to achieve a natural-looking result that enhances your features and complements your overall look.

Hair Care

After facial surgery, it's important to take good care of your skin and hair to promote healing and maintain the results. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid heat styling tools and harsh chemicals for several weeks after surgery
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid irritating the surgical area
  • Keep your hair clean and well-maintained to prevent infection
  • Avoid wearing tight hair accessories that could put pressure on the surgical site

With proper care and attention, you can achieve the face shape you desire through surgery and enjoy the results for years to come.
