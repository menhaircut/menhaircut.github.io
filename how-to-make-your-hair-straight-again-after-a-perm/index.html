How To Make Your Hair Straight Again After A Perm

Perming your hair can be a fun way to add some curls and volume to your locks. But what if you've had enough of the curls and want to go back to straight hair? Don't worry, there are ways to make your hair straight again after a perm. In this article, we'll show you how to do it step by step.

How to Make Your Hair Straight Again After a Perm

The first step to making your hair straight again after a perm is to wait for your perm to fully grow out. This can take anywhere from 3 to 6 months, depending on how fast your hair grows. Once your perm has grown out, you can start the process of straightening your hair.

Chemical Straightening

One option for straightening your hair is to use a chemical straightening treatment. These treatments use chemicals to break the bonds in your hair and make it straight. However, chemical straightening can be damaging to your hair, so it's important to do your research and find a reputable salon that uses quality products.

Flat Iron

Another option for straightening your hair is to use a flat iron. A flat iron uses heat to straighten your hair, but it's important to use a heat protectant spray to avoid damaging your hair. You should also use a high-quality flat iron with ceramic plates to avoid hot spots and ensure even heat distribution.

Blow Dryer

You can also use a blow dryer to straighten your hair. This method involves using a round brush to pull your hair straight as you blow dry it. It's important to use a heat protectant spray and a high-quality blow dryer with a concentrator nozzle to direct the airflow and avoid frizz.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step by step guide to help you make your hair straight again after a perm:

  1. Wait for your perm to fully grow out.
  2. Choose a straightening method that works best for you (chemical straightening, flat iron, or blow dryer).
  3. If you're using chemical straightening, find a reputable salon and schedule an appointment.
  4. If you're using a flat iron or blow dryer, wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue.
  5. Towel dry your hair and apply a heat protectant spray.
  6. If you're using a flat iron, divide your hair into sections and run the flat iron through each section, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  7. If you're using a blow dryer, divide your hair into sections and use a round brush to pull your hair straight as you blow dry it.
  8. Once you've straightened your hair, apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to add shine and combat frizz.
  9. Style your hair as desired.

Tips to Keep in Mind

Here are some tips to keep in mind when trying to make your hair straight again after a perm:

  • Be patient and wait for your perm to fully grow out before trying to straighten your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Invest in a high-quality flat iron or blow dryer with ceramic plates or a concentrator nozzle.
  • Apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to add shine and combat frizz.
  • Avoid heat styling your hair too often to prevent damage.

FAQs

Can I straighten my hair right after getting a perm?

No, it's not recommended to straighten your hair right after getting a perm. You should wait for your perm to fully grow out before trying to straighten your hair.

Is chemical straightening safe?

Chemical straightening can be damaging to your hair if not done properly. It's important to do your research and find a reputable salon that uses quality products.

Hair Care After Straightening

After straightening your hair, it's important to take care of it to prevent damage and maintain the straightness. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid heat styling your hair too often to prevent damage.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish and moisturize your hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet to prevent breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends.

By following these tips, you can keep your hair healthy and straight after a perm. Remember to be patient and take your time when straightening your hair to avoid damage.
