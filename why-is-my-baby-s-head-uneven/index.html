Why Is My Baby s Head Uneven

Written by Petter Jun 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As a new parent, it can be alarming to notice that your baby's head is uneven. However, it's actually quite common for babies to have uneven heads, and there are several reasons why this might happen. In this article, we'll explore the causes of uneven head shape in babies, how to prevent it, and what you can do if you're concerned about your baby's head shape.

Causes of uneven head shape in babies

One of the most common causes of uneven head shape in babies is positional plagiocephaly, also known as flat head syndrome. This occurs when a baby spends too much time in one position, causing the head to become flattened on one side. This can happen in the womb or after birth, and is often associated with the use of car seats, swings, and other infant gear that keep the baby in one position for extended periods of time.

Another cause of uneven head shape in babies is torticollis, a condition that results in the baby holding their head in an awkward position. This can lead to flattening on one side of the head and can also cause neck muscle stiffness and limited range of motion.

How to prevent uneven head shape in babies

The best way to prevent uneven head shape in babies is to ensure that they spend plenty of time in different positions throughout the day. This can be achieved by holding your baby or placing them in a variety of positions, such as tummy time or laying on their side. It's also important to limit the amount of time your baby spends in infant gear, such as car seats and swings, and to make sure that they have plenty of opportunities for movement and play.

What to do if you're concerned about your baby's head shape

If you're concerned about your baby's head shape, it's important to talk to your pediatrician. They can examine your baby and determine whether there is a medical issue that needs to be addressed. In some cases, physical therapy or other interventions may be recommended.

Tips for promoting healthy head shape in babies

Here are some tips for promoting healthy head shape in babies:

  • Give your baby plenty of tummy time
  • Limit the amount of time your baby spends in infant gear
  • Switch up your baby's sleeping position
  • Encourage your baby to turn their head in both directions
  • Use a firm mattress for your baby's bed

FAQ about uneven head shape in babies

Q: Is it normal for a baby's head to be uneven?

A: Yes, it's quite common for babies to have uneven heads. This is often due to positional plagiocephaly, which can be caused by spending too much time in one position.

Q: Will my baby's head shape improve on its own?

A: In many cases, yes. As your baby grows and becomes more mobile, their head shape may naturally improve. However, it's important to talk to your pediatrician if you're concerned about your baby's head shape.

Q: Can a helmet help correct uneven head shape?

A: In some cases, a helmet or other device may be recommended to help correct an uneven head shape. However, this is typically only recommended in severe cases and should always be done under the guidance of a medical professional.

Hair care for babies with uneven head shapes

If your baby has an uneven head shape, you may notice that their hair grows unevenly as well. Here are some tips for caring for your baby's hair:

  • Use a soft-bristled brush to gently groom your baby's hair
  • Avoid using tight hair accessories that could irritate your baby's scalp
  • Consider using a baby-safe shampoo and conditioner to help keep your baby's hair healthy

With these tips and an understanding of the causes of uneven head shape in babies, you can help promote a healthy head shape for your little one.
