What Breed Of Dog Always Stay Small A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Sep 01, 2023 · 5 min read

If you're looking for a furry companion that won't take up too much space, then you might be wondering what breed of dog always stays small. Fortunately, there are several breeds that fit into this category, and we're here to give you all the information you need to make an informed decision.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Choose the Right Small Breed Dog
  3. Step-by-Step Guide to Bringing Home Your Small Breed Dog
  4. Tips for Raising a Small Breed Dog
  5. Frequently Asked Questions
  6. Hair Care for Small Breed Dogs

Description

Small breed dogs are typically defined as dogs that weigh under 20 pounds and stand less than 16 inches tall. These dogs are often preferred by apartment dwellers or people who live in small homes because they take up less space and require less exercise than larger breeds.

There are several breeds that always stay small, including:

  • Chihuahua
  • Pomeranian
  • Pekingese
  • Shih Tzu
  • Yorkshire Terrier

Each of these breeds has its own unique personality and characteristics, so it's important to do your research before choosing the right one for you.

How to Choose the Right Small Breed Dog

When choosing a small breed dog, there are several factors to consider:

  • Temperament: Different breeds have different temperaments, so it's important to choose one that matches your personality and lifestyle.
  • Exercise Needs: While small breed dogs require less exercise than larger breeds, they still need daily walks and playtime.
  • Training: Some small breed dogs can be difficult to train, so consider your experience level before choosing a breed.
  • Grooming: Small breed dogs often require more frequent grooming than larger breeds, so make sure you're prepared to handle this responsibility.

Temperament

Chihuahuas are known for being feisty and loyal, while Pomeranians are playful and energetic. Pekingese are known for being stubborn and independent, while Shih Tzus are affectionate and friendly. Yorkshire Terriers are known for being intelligent and confident.

Exercise Needs

While small breed dogs don't require as much exercise as larger breeds, they still need daily walks and playtime. Chihuahuas and Pomeranians are both active breeds that enjoy playtime and walks, while Pekingese and Shih Tzus are more laid back and prefer short walks. Yorkshire Terriers are also active and enjoy daily walks and playtime.

Training

Chihuahuas can be difficult to train and may be prone to barking, while Pomeranians are intelligent and eager to please. Pekingese can be stubborn and difficult to train, while Shih Tzus are known for being easy to train. Yorkshire Terriers are intelligent and confident, but may be prone to stubbornness.

Grooming

Chihuahuas and Pomeranians both require frequent grooming to keep their long coats in good condition. Pekingese and Shih Tzus also require frequent grooming, while Yorkshire Terriers have a shorter coat that requires less maintenance.

Step-by-Step Guide to Bringing Home Your Small Breed Dog

Bringing home a new dog can be exciting, but it's important to make sure you're prepared to care for your new furry friend. Here's a step-by-step guide to bringing home your small breed dog:

  1. Prepare your home: Make sure your home is safe for your new dog by removing any hazards and providing a comfortable sleeping area.
  2. Choose the right food: Small breed dogs require a balanced diet that's appropriate for their size and activity level.
  3. Provide plenty of exercise: Even though small breed dogs require less exercise than larger breeds, they still need daily walks and playtime.
  4. Train your dog: Start training your dog as soon as possible to establish good behavior and prevent bad habits.
  5. Groom your dog: Small breed dogs often require more frequent grooming than larger breeds, so be prepared to brush and bathe your dog regularly.

Tips for Raising a Small Breed Dog

Here are some tips for raising a small breed dog:

  • Be patient: Small breed dogs can be stubborn and difficult to train, so be patient and persistent.
  • Provide plenty of socialization: Small breed dogs can be prone to anxiety and aggression if they're not properly socialized.
  • Watch for health problems: Small breed dogs are prone to certain health problems, so make sure you're aware of the signs and symptoms to watch for.
  • Be consistent: Establish a routine and stick to it to help your dog feel secure and comfortable.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the smallest breed of dog?

The Chihuahua is often considered the smallest breed of dog, weighing in at less than 6 pounds.

Do small breed dogs live longer?

Small breed dogs often have longer lifespans than larger breeds, with some living up to 15 years or more.

Are small breed dogs good with children?

Small breed dogs can be good with children, but it's important to supervise interactions and teach children how to properly handle and interact with the dog.

Hair Care for Small Breed Dogs

Small breed dogs often require more frequent grooming than larger breeds, so it's important to establish a hair care routine for your furry friend. Here are some tips for keeping your small breed dog's coat in good condition:

  • Brush your dog's coat regularly to prevent matting and tangling.
  • Bathe your dog as needed to keep their coat clean and healthy.
  • Trim your dog's nails regularly to prevent them from getting too long.
  • Clean your dog's ears regularly to prevent infection.

By following these tips and establishing a good hair care routine, you can help keep your small breed dog looking and feeling their best.

Overall, small breed dogs can make wonderful companions for people who live in small spaces or who prefer a dog that requires less exercise. By doing your research and choosing the right breed for your personality and lifestyle, you can enjoy many happy years with your furry friend.
