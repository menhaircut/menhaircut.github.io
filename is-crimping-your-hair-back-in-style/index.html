Is Crimping Your Hair Back In Style

Written by Aprilia Nov 29, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’re looking for a fresh hairstyle to try out this year, you might want to consider crimping your hair. Yes, you read that right – crimping, the trend that was popular in the 80s and 90s, is making a comeback. And with the right styling techniques and products, you can rock this look effortlessly.

Description

Crimping is a hairstyling technique that involves creating small, zigzag-like patterns in your hair. This can be achieved using a crimping iron or by braiding your hair and leaving it overnight. The result is a textured, voluminous look that can add a playful and edgy vibe to any outfit.

How to

Ready to try crimping your hair? Here’s how to do it:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair to ensure it’s clean and soft.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage from the crimping iron.
  3. Divide your hair into small sections and clamp the crimping iron onto each section, holding it for a few seconds before releasing it.
  4. Repeat this process until you’ve crimped your entire head of hair.
  5. Finish off with a hairspray or texturizing spray to hold the crimps in place.

Step by Step

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to crimp your hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Use a hydrating conditioner to keep your hair soft and smooth.

Step 2: Apply a Heat Protectant Spray

Before you start crimping your hair, apply a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from the crimping iron. This will also help your hair hold the crimps for longer.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair into Small Sections

Using a comb, divide your hair into small sections. This will make it easier to crimp your hair and ensure that you don’t miss any sections.

Step 4: Clamp the Crimping Iron onto Each Section

Take one section of hair and clamp the crimping iron onto it. Hold the iron in place for a few seconds before releasing it.

Step 5: Repeat the Process

Repeat the crimping process on each section of hair until you’ve crimped your entire head of hair.

Step 6: Finish with Hairspray or Texturizing Spray

Finish off your crimped hair with a light mist of hairspray or texturizing spray. This will help hold the crimps in place and add extra volume to your hair.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when crimping your hair:

  • Use a crimping iron with adjustable temperature settings to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Apply a hair serum or oil to your hair before crimping to keep it hydrated.
  • Don’t overdo it – crimping your hair too much can make it look frizzy and dry.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles, such as a half-up, half-down style or a messy bun.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about crimping your hair:

Is crimping your hair damaging?

Crimping your hair can be damaging if you use a crimping iron that’s too hot or if you crimp your hair too often. Make sure to use a heat protectant spray and to crimp your hair in moderation.

How long does crimped hair last?

Crimped hair can last for several days if you use the right styling products and techniques. Use a texturizing spray to help hold the crimps in place and avoid washing your hair too often.

Can you crimp short hair?

Yes, you can crimp short hair. Use a smaller crimping iron and focus on the top layers of your hair for a subtle, textured look.

Hair Care

Make sure to take care of your hair before and after crimping it. Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair soft and nourished, and apply a hair mask once a week to keep it healthy. Avoid using too much heat on your hair and let it air dry whenever possible.

So, is crimping your hair back in style? We say yes! Give this fun and playful hairstyle a try and see how it can amp up your look.
