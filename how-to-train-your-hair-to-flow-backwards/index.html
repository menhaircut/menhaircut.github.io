How To Train Your Hair To Flow Backwards

Written by Bardi Jun 15, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're tired of the same old hairstyle and want to switch things up, training your hair to flow backwards is a great option. This style is sleek, sophisticated, and can be achieved with just a few simple steps. In this article, we'll go over how to train your hair to flow backwards, as well as tips and tricks for maintaining this style. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Train Your Hair to Flow Backwards
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Backwards Flowing Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Your New Style

How to Train Your Hair to Flow Backwards

To train your hair to flow backwards, you'll need to start by growing it out to a length that can be easily styled. This typically means having at least 3-4 inches of hair on the top of your head. Once you've got the length, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Before styling your hair, it's important to wash it thoroughly with a high-quality shampoo and conditioner. This will help to remove any buildup or debris that could interfere with your styling process.

Step 2: Apply Styling Product

Once your hair is clean and damp, apply a styling product such as a pomade or wax. This will help to give your hair hold and control as you style it.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair Backwards

Using a wide-tooth comb, comb your hair backwards from your forehead. Start at the crown of your head and work your way down to the nape of your neck.

Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a blow dryer on low heat, blow dry your hair backwards. Use your hands to shape your hair as you go, and be sure to dry it completely.

Step 5: Finish with Hairspray

To ensure your hair stays in place all day, finish with a light hold hairspray. This will help to keep your hair looking sleek and polished.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide to training your hair to flow backwards:

  1. Grow your hair out to at least 3-4 inches on top
  2. Wash your hair with a high-quality shampoo and conditioner
  3. Towel dry your hair until it's damp
  4. Apply a styling product such as pomade or wax
  5. Comb your hair backwards using a wide-tooth comb
  6. Blow dry your hair backwards on low heat
  7. Shape your hair with your hands as you dry it
  8. Finish with a light hold hairspray

Tips for Maintaining Your Backwards Flowing Hair

Now that you've trained your hair to flow backwards, it's important to maintain this style. Here are some tips to help you do just that:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and strong
  • Reapply styling product throughout the day as needed
  • Visit your stylist regularly for trims and touch-ups
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz and mess up your style
  • Invest in a good quality blow dryer and use it on low heat to avoid damage to your hair

FAQs

Q: How long will it take to train my hair to flow backwards?

A: This will depend on the length and texture of your hair, as well as how often you style it. Generally, it can take a few weeks to a few months to train your hair to flow backwards.

Q: Can I still wear a hat with backwards flowing hair?

A: Yes! You can still wear hats and other accessories with this style. Just be sure to style your hair as usual once you take the hat off.

Q: Will backwards flowing hair work on all hair types?

A: Yes, this style can work on all hair types. However, if you have very curly or kinky hair, you may need to straighten it first before training it to flow backwards.

Hair Care Tips for Your New Style

Finally, here are some hair care tips to keep your backwards flowing hair looking its best:

  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons too often, as this can damage your hair
  • Invest in a silk pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz while you sleep
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors by wearing a hat or using a UV protection spray

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to rock the backwards flowing hair look with confidence. Remember to be patient as you train your hair, and don't be afraid to experiment with different styling products and techniques until you find what works best for you. Good luck!
