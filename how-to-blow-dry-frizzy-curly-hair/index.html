How To Blow Dry Frizzy Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Do you struggle with frizzy curly hair? Are you tired of trying to tame your unruly locks every morning? Blow drying your hair can make a big difference in managing and styling your curls. In this article, we'll guide you step-by-step on how to blow dry frizzy curly hair. We'll also provide some tips and tricks to help you achieve the best results. Let's get started!

How to Blow Dry Frizzy Curly Hair

Blow drying frizzy curly hair may seem like a daunting task, but with the right technique and tools, you can achieve beautiful, defined curls. Here's what you'll need:

  • Blow dryer with diffuser attachment
  • Wide-tooth comb or detangling brush
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Curl defining cream or gel

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to blow dry your frizzy curly hair:

  1. Start with freshly washed and conditioned hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to gently remove any tangles.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair. This will help protect your hair from heat damage caused by the blow dryer.
  3. Apply a curl defining cream or gel to your hair. This will help define your curls and reduce frizz. Use a quarter-sized amount and distribute evenly throughout your hair.
  4. Attach the diffuser attachment to your blow dryer. The diffuser helps to distribute heat evenly and reduce frizz.
  5. Flip your head upside down and gently place your hair into the diffuser. Turn on the blow dryer on a low heat and low speed setting.
  6. Move the diffuser in a circular motion around your head, focusing on the roots. Continue to dry your hair until it's about 80% dry.
  7. Flip your head back up and use the diffuser to dry the ends of your hair. Use a higher heat and speed setting, but be careful not to over-dry your hair.
  8. Once your hair is completely dry, use your fingers to fluff your curls and create volume.
  9. Finish with a light hold hairspray to help set your curls.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you achieve the best results:

  • Use a microfiber towel or cotton t-shirt to dry your hair before blow drying. Avoid using a regular towel as it can cause frizz.
  • Don't touch your hair too much while blow drying. This can cause frizz and disrupt your curls.
  • Experiment with different heat and speed settings to find what works best for your hair. Avoid using high heat as it can damage your hair.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to help reduce frizz while you sleep.
  • Avoid brushing your hair after blow drying as it can cause frizz. Instead, use your fingers to gently separate your curls.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about blow drying frizzy curly hair:

Can I blow dry my hair every day?

Blow drying your hair every day can cause damage and dryness. It's best to limit blow drying to 2-3 times per week and use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

What type of diffuser attachment should I use?

Look for a diffuser attachment with wide prongs that can accommodate your curls. Some diffusers also have adjustable settings for heat and speed.

Can I use a regular blow dryer without a diffuser attachment?

A regular blow dryer without a diffuser attachment can cause frizz and disrupt your curls. It's best to use a diffuser attachment to distribute heat evenly and reduce frizz.

Hair Care for Frizzy Curly Hair

Blow drying your hair is just one step in managing frizzy curly hair. Here are some additional hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often as they can cause damage and breakage. Instead, try air-drying your hair or using heat-free styling methods.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or using a hair sunscreen.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to blow dry your frizzy curly hair like a pro. Remember to be gentle with your hair, use the right tools and products, and experiment to find what works best for you. Happy styling!
