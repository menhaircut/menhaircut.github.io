How To Counteract Brassy Tones In Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 20, 2023 · 4 min read

As much as we love blonde and silver hair, dealing with brassy tones can be a nightmare. Brassy tones occur when the color starts to fade, and warm, yellow, or orange undertones start to show up. However, there’s no need to worry. With the right knowledge and products, you can easily counteract brassy tones and restore your hair to its former glory.

Table of Contents

  • How to Identify Brassy Tones
  • How to Counteract Brassy Tones in Hair
  • Step by Step Guide to Counteract Brassy Tones

How to Identify Brassy Tones

Before we dive into counteracting brassy tones in hair, it’s essential to know how to identify them. Brassy tones typically appear as yellow or orange hues in hair, and they usually occur after you’ve dyed your hair blonde or silver. Brassy tones can also be caused by exposure to hard water, chlorine, or excessive sun exposure.

How to Counteract Brassy Tones in Hair

There are several methods you can use to counteract brassy tones in hair. Here are some of the most effective ones:

Use a Purple Shampoo

Purple shampoo is an excellent product for counteracting brassy tones in blonde or silver hair. Purple is opposite yellow on the color wheel, so when you apply purple shampoo to your hair, it neutralizes the warm tones and restores your hair’s cool, ashy color. Use purple shampoo once a week or as needed to maintain your hair’s color.

Use a Toning Mask

Toning masks are another great way to counteract brassy tones in hair. They’re usually used once a week after shampooing and can be left on for 5-10 minutes. Toning masks work by depositing cool tones into your hair, which cancels out the warm tones and restores your hair’s color.

Opt for a Toning Treatment

If your hair is severely brassy, a toning treatment might be necessary. Toning treatments are usually done at a salon and can help restore your hair’s color in just one session. The process involves using a toner to neutralize the brassy tones and restore your hair’s natural color.

Step by Step Guide to Counteract Brassy Tones

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to counteract brassy tones in hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue.

Step 2: Apply Purple Shampoo

Apply purple shampoo to your hair, focusing on the areas with the most brassiness. Leave the shampoo on for 3-5 minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 3: Apply a Toning Mask

Apply a toning mask to your hair, focusing on the areas with the most brassiness. Leave the mask on for 5-10 minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 4: Rinse Your Hair

Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water to seal the cuticle and help lock in the color.

Tips for Preventing Brassy Tones

Here are some tips for preventing brassy tones in your hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo
  • Avoid washing your hair too often
  • Protect your hair from the sun
  • Use a leave-in conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I use purple shampoo?

You should use purple shampoo once a week or as needed to maintain your hair’s color.

Can I use purple shampoo on brown hair?

Purple shampoo is formulated for blonde and silver hair, and while it won’t harm brown hair, it won’t have the same effect.

Is it better to use a toning mask or a toning treatment?

Toning masks are great for maintaining your hair’s color, while toning treatments are more effective for severe brassiness.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Hair

Here are some hair care tips to help you maintain your hair’s health and color:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends

With these tips and tricks, you can easily counteract brassy tones in your hair and keep your hair looking healthy and vibrant. Remember to take care of your hair, and it will reward you with beautiful, shiny locks.
