Get Curly Hair Permanently A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Nov 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of using heated styling tools and chemical treatments to get curly hair? Well, you're not alone. Many people are looking for ways to get curly hair permanently without damaging their hair. In this article, we'll discuss how to get curly hair permanently, step-by-step, and answer some frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Get Curly Hair Permanently
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

Description

Getting curly hair permanently can be achieved through a chemical process called a perm. A perm involves applying a chemical solution to the hair to break and reform the bonds that give hair its shape. The result is permanent curly or wavy hair.

Perms have evolved significantly since the 1980s and now offer a much more natural-looking curl. However, it's essential to understand that getting a perm is still a chemical process that can damage your hair if not done correctly.

How to Get Curly Hair Permanently

Getting curly hair permanently is a process that involves several steps, including consultation, preparation, perming, and aftercare. Here are the basic steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Consultation

The first step in getting curly hair permanently is to schedule a consultation with a professional hairstylist who specializes in perming. During the consultation, you'll discuss the perm process, including the type of perm you want and the expected results.

Step 2: Preparation

Before getting a perm, it's essential to prepare your hair properly. This includes avoiding washing your hair for at least 24 hours before the perm, avoiding any heat styling tools or chemical treatments, and ensuring your hair is in good condition.

Step 3: Perming

The perming process involves applying a chemical solution to your hair, which breaks the bonds that give your hair its shape, and then reforming the bonds to create a curly or wavy pattern. The process can take several hours, depending on the length and thickness of your hair.

Step 4: Aftercare

After getting a perm, it's essential to follow a strict aftercare routine to maintain your curly hair. This includes avoiding washing your hair for at least 48 hours after the perm, using a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and avoiding any heat styling tools that can damage your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair

Maintaining curly hair can be challenging, but it's essential to keep your curls looking their best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to enhance your curls.
  • Avoid brushing your hair, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a microfiber towel or t-shirt to dry your hair, as regular towels can cause frizz.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause your curls to lose their shape.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is getting a perm damaging to my hair?

Perming is a chemical process that can damage your hair if not done correctly. However, a professional hairstylist can minimize the damage by using the right products and techniques.

How often do I need to get a perm?

A perm is permanent, so you don't need to get one regularly. However, as your hair grows, the curly pattern will become less defined, and you may need to get a touch-up in the future.

Can I color my hair after getting a perm?

It's best to wait at least two weeks after getting a perm before coloring your hair. Coloring your hair too soon after a perm can cause damage and breakage.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires specific care to maintain its shape and prevent damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid heat styling tools, as they can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your curls moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Getting curly hair permanently can be a great way to switch up your look and embrace your natural texture. However, it's essential to understand that perming is a chemical process that can damage your hair if not done correctly. If you're considering getting a perm, make sure to consult with a professional hairstylist and follow a strict aftercare routine to keep your curls looking their best.
