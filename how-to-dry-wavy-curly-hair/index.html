How To Dry Wavy Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Sep 06, 2023 · 5 min read

Welcome to our ultimate guide on how to dry wavy curly hair! If you have naturally wavy or curly hair, you know that the drying process can be a real challenge. But fear not, we’ve got you covered. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the best techniques and tools to help you achieve the perfect, frizz-free style. From diffusing to air-drying, we’ve got all the tips and tricks you need to know. So let’s get started!

Table of Contents

  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Perfectly Drying Wavy Curly Hair
  • FAQs

How to Dry Wavy Curly Hair

Drying wavy curly hair can be tricky, as it’s prone to frizz and can take a long time to dry. However, with the right technique, you can achieve a beautiful, defined style that lasts all day. Here are some tips on how to dry wavy curly hair:

1. Diffuse Your Hair

A diffuser is a great tool for drying wavy curly hair, as it helps to distribute heat evenly and reduce frizz. To use a diffuser, attach it to your blow dryer and set it to low heat. Flip your head upside down and gently scrunch your hair with the diffuser, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Continue until your hair is completely dry.

2. Air-Dry Your Hair

If you prefer not to use a blow dryer, air-drying is a great option for wavy curly hair. After washing your hair, gently squeeze out any excess water with a microfiber towel. Then, apply a leave-in conditioner or styling product to your hair and let it air-dry naturally. This method can take some time, so be patient!

3. Use a T-Shirt

If you’re looking for a gentler alternative to a towel, try using a t-shirt to dry your hair. Simply flip your head upside down and scrunch your hair with the t-shirt, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. This method helps to reduce frizz and prevent breakage.

Step-by-Step Guide

Ready to get started? Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to dry wavy curly hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo. Rinse thoroughly and apply a moisturizing conditioner, focusing on the ends of your hair. Rinse again and gently squeeze out any excess water with a microfiber towel.

Step 2: Apply Your Products

Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling product to your hair, focusing on the mid-lengths and ends. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Diffuse or Air-Dry

If you’re using a diffuser, attach it to your blow dryer and set it to low heat. Flip your head upside down and gently scrunch your hair with the diffuser, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. If you’re air-drying, simply let your hair dry naturally.

Step 4: Scrunch Out the Crunch

Once your hair is dry, it may have a slightly crunchy texture from the styling product. To soften your curls and create a more natural look, gently scrunch your hair with your hands or a t-shirt.

Tips for Perfectly Drying Wavy Curly Hair

Drying wavy curly hair can be a challenge, but with these tips, you’ll be able to achieve a beautiful, defined style:

1. Use a Microfiber Towel

Avoid using a regular towel to dry your hair, as it can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, opt for a microfiber towel, which is gentler on your hair and helps to reduce frizz.

2. Don’t Touch Your Hair Too Much

While it can be tempting to constantly touch and play with your curls, this can actually cause frizz and disrupt your curl pattern. Try to leave your hair alone as much as possible while it’s drying.

3. Use a Heat Protectant

If you’re using a blow dryer, be sure to apply a heat protectant to your hair first. This will help to prevent damage and keep your curls looking healthy.

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my wavy curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and personal preferences. Some people with wavy curly hair prefer to wash their hair every day, while others only wash once or twice a week. Experiment to find what works best for you.

Q: What’s the best way to prevent frizz in wavy curly hair?

A: The key to preventing frizz in wavy curly hair is to keep it moisturized. Use a leave-in conditioner or styling product to help lock in moisture and reduce frizz.

Hair Care Tips for Wavy Curly Hair

Drying your wavy curly hair is just one part of your hair care routine. Here are some additional tips for keeping your curls healthy and beautiful:

1. Use a Sulfate-Free Shampoo

Sulfates can be harsh on wavy curly hair, so opt for a sulfate-free shampoo instead. This will help to keep your curls moisturized and prevent damage.

2. Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help to moisturize and strengthen your curls. Try using a deep conditioner once a week or as needed.

3. Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Silk pillowcases are gentler on your hair than cotton, which can help to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.

With these tips and tricks, you’ll be able to achieve the perfect, frizz-free style for your wavy curly hair. Remember to be patient and experiment with different techniques to find what works best for you. Happy styling!
