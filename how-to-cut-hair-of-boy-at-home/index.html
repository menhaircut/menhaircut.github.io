How To Cut Hair Of Boy At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Nov 12, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you in need of a haircut for your little boy but don't want to go to a salon just yet? You might be surprised to know that it's possible to cut your son's hair at home with the right tools and techniques. Follow this step-by-step guide on how to cut hair of boy at home, and your little one will have a fresh new look in no time.

Description

Cutting hair at home can be a daunting task, especially if you're not a professional hairdresser. However, with the right tools, techniques, and patience, you can give your son a haircut that looks just as good as if he had been to a salon. By following this guide, you'll learn how to cut hair of boy at home and save yourself some money in the process.

How to Cut Hair of Boy at Home: Step by Step

Step 1: Choose the Right Tools

Before you start cutting your son's hair, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of hair cutting scissors, an electric clipper, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. Make sure your scissors are sharp and your clippers are oiled and ready to go.

Step 2: Wash and Dry the Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your son's hair is clean and dry. Use the spray bottle to dampen his hair, then comb it to remove any tangles. If his hair is very long, consider trimming it with scissors before using the clippers.

Step 3: Start with the Clippers

Using the clippers, start at the nape of your son's neck and work your way up. Use a longer guard for the sides and back, and a shorter guard for the top. Make sure to go slowly and carefully, checking your work as you go.

Step 4: Use Scissors for the Top

Once you've finished with the clippers, use the scissors to trim the top of your son's hair. Comb it straight up and use the scissors to snip away any longer strands. Blend the sides and back into the top using the scissors, taking care not to cut too much off.

Step 5: Trim the Bangs

If your son has bangs, use the scissors to trim them to the desired length. Comb them straight down and snip away any longer strands. Use the scissors to create a natural-looking curve, taking care not to cut too much off.

Step 6: Clean Up the Neckline

Using the clippers, clean up the hairline at the nape of your son's neck. Use a shorter guard to create a clean, straight line.

Step 7: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting, comb through your son's hair and check for any missed spots or uneven areas. Use the scissors to make any necessary adjustments.

Step 8: Clean Up

Once you're satisfied with your work, use a soft brush to remove any loose hair from your son's head and shoulders. Wash his hair to remove any remaining hair and style it as desired.

Tips for Cutting Hair of Boy at Home

  • Start with longer guards and work your way down to shorter ones to avoid cutting too much off at once.
  • Use a spray bottle filled with water to dampen the hair and keep it in place while cutting.
  • Take breaks if your son gets antsy or fidgety.
  • Watch tutorial videos online to get a better idea of the cutting techniques.
  • Be patient and take your time. It's better to take longer and get it right than to rush and make mistakes.

FAQs

Q: How often should I cut my son's hair?

A: It depends on how fast his hair grows and how long you want it to be. As a general rule, you should trim it every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking neat and tidy.

Q: What if I make a mistake?

A: Don't panic! Mistakes happen, and hair grows back. If you accidentally cut too much off, try to blend it in with the rest of the hair. If it's really bad, you can always take your son to a professional to have it fixed.

Q: Can I use regular scissors to cut hair?

A: No, you should always use hair cutting scissors. Regular scissors can damage the hair and make it look uneven.

Hair Care

After you've finished cutting your son's hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and moisturized. Avoid using hot tools like hair dryers and flat irons, as they can damage the hair. If your son has longer hair, consider using a leave-in conditioner or detangler to make it easier to manage.

Cutting your son's hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience for both of you. By following these tips and techniques, you can give your little one a fresh new look without ever leaving the house. Happy cutting!
