What Kind Of Dogs Have Curly Hair A Guide To Curly Haired Breeds

Written by Eveline Sep 20, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're a dog lover, you know that not all dogs have the same kind of hair. Some have long, silky hair, while others have short, wiry hair. But did you know that some breeds of dogs have curly hair? In this guide, we'll take a look at the different breeds of dogs that have curly hair and learn how to properly care for their locks.

What Breeds of Dogs Have Curly Hair?

Curly hair in dogs is caused by the presence of a particular gene. There are several breeds of dogs that carry this gene, and as a result, have curly hair. Here are some of the most popular breeds of curly haired dogs:

Poodle

Poodles are one of the most well-known breeds of dogs with curly hair. They are a non-shedding breed, which makes them a popular choice for people with allergies. Poodles have a dense, curly coat that comes in a variety of colors.

Bichon Frise

The Bichon Frise is another breed of dog with curly hair. They have a soft, curly coat that is hypoallergenic, making them a great choice for people with allergies. Bichon Frises are known for their friendly, affectionate personalities.

Labradoodle

The Labradoodle is a cross between a Labrador Retriever and a Poodle. They have a wavy, curly coat that is hypoallergenic, making them a popular choice for people with allergies. Labradoodles are known for their friendly, playful personalities.

Goldendoodle

The Goldendoodle is a cross between a Golden Retriever and a Poodle. They have a wavy, curly coat that is hypoallergenic, making them a great choice for people with allergies. Goldendoodles are known for their friendly, affectionate personalities.

How to Care for Curly Haired Dogs

Caring for curly haired dogs requires a bit of extra effort to keep their coats healthy and looking their best. Here are some tips for caring for your curly haired dog:

Brushing

Brush your dog's coat regularly to prevent matting and tangling. Use a slicker brush or a comb to gently remove any tangles or mats.

Bathing

Bathe your dog regularly to keep their coat clean and healthy. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is specifically formulated for curly haired dogs.

Grooming

Take your dog to a professional groomer every 6-8 weeks to keep their coat trimmed and looking its best.

Diet

Feed your dog a healthy diet that is rich in nutrients to promote healthy hair growth and a shiny coat.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do all curly haired dogs shed?

No, not all curly haired dogs shed. Some breeds, like the Poodle and Bichon Frise, are non-shedding breeds.

Can I groom my curly haired dog myself?

Yes, you can groom your curly haired dog yourself. However, it's important to use the right tools and techniques to avoid damaging their coat.

Why do some curly haired dogs need to be trimmed?

Curly haired dogs can develop mats and tangles if their coats are not regularly groomed. Trimming their coat can help prevent these issues and keep their coat healthy and looking great.

Final Thoughts

Curly haired dogs are a unique and adorable breed. Understanding their specific grooming needs is important to keep them healthy and looking their best. By following the tips in this guide, you can help your curly haired dog maintain a healthy, shiny coat.
