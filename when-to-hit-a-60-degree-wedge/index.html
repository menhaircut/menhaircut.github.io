When To Hit A 60 Degree Wedge A Guide For Golfers

Written by Aprilia Nov 03, 2023 · 4 min read

As a golfer, having the right wedge in your bag is essential. One of the most versatile wedges is the 60 degree wedge. It can be used for a variety of shots, from full swings to delicate chips and pitches around the green. But when is the best time to use a 60 degree wedge? In this guide, we’ll go over everything you need to know about when to hit a 60 degree wedge.

Description

A 60 degree wedge is a high-lofted club that is designed to help golfers get the ball up in the air quickly and land it softly on the green. It has a shorter shaft than a typical iron or driver, which allows for greater control and precision. The 60 degree wedge is often used for shots that require a high trajectory and a short distance to the hole.

How to Hit a 60 Degree Wedge

There are a few things to keep in mind when hitting a 60 degree wedge:

  • Position the ball slightly forward in your stance.
  • Open the face of the club slightly to increase the loft and spin on the ball.
  • Take a slightly shallower divot than you would with a lower lofted club.
  • Use a smooth, controlled swing to avoid hitting the ball too hard.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide to hitting a 60 degree wedge:

  1. Choose the right club for the shot based on the distance to the hole and the height of the obstacle you need to clear.
  2. Position the ball slightly forward in your stance.
  3. Open the face of the club slightly to increase the loft and spin on the ball.
  4. Take a practice swing to get a feel for the swing and the club.
  5. Take your stance and grip the club.
  6. Take a smooth, controlled swing, keeping your wrist firm and your arms straight.
  7. Make contact with the ball, striking it cleanly and with the center of the clubface.
  8. Follow through with your swing, keeping your eyes on the ball until it lands.
  9. Assess the shot and adjust your technique as needed for future shots.

Tips for Using a 60 Degree Wedge

Here are some tips for using a 60 degree wedge:

  • Practice hitting the club on the range to get a feel for the club and your swing.
  • Experiment with different ball positions and swing speeds to find what works best for you.
  • Use a light grip pressure to avoid hitting the ball too hard.
  • Take your time and focus on hitting the ball cleanly and with the center of the clubface.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about using a 60 degree wedge:

Q: When should I use a 60 degree wedge?

A: You should use a 60 degree wedge when you need to hit a high, soft shot with a short distance to the hole. It’s also useful for getting out of bunkers and hitting flop shots around the green.

Q: What is the loft of a 60 degree wedge?

A: The loft of a 60 degree wedge is, unsurprisingly, 60 degrees.

Q: How far should I be able to hit a 60 degree wedge?

A: The distance you can hit a 60 degree wedge depends on a variety of factors, including the loft of the club, your swing speed, and the conditions of the course. On average, most golfers can hit a 60 degree wedge between 50 and 75 yards.
