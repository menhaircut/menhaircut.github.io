How To Manage Coarse Thick Natural Hair

Written by Aprilia Sep 23, 2023 · 4 min read

Coarse thick natural hair can be a challenge to manage, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve healthy and manageable hair. In this article, we will provide you with step-by-step instructions, tips, and frequently asked questions to help you manage your coarse thick natural hair.

Table of Content

  • How to Manage Coarse Thick Natural Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips to Manage Coarse Thick Natural Hair

How to Manage Coarse Thick Natural Hair

Managing coarse thick natural hair requires patience and dedication. Here are some steps to help you achieve manageable hair:

1. Detangle Your Hair

Start by detangling your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to gently remove any knots or tangles. Begin at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots.

2. Apply a Deep Conditioner

After detangling, apply a deep conditioner to your hair. Use a product that is specifically designed for coarse thick hair. Leave the conditioner on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it out.

3. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing it. This will help to moisturize and soften your hair, making it more manageable.

4. Use a Styling Cream

Apply a styling cream to your hair to help define your curls and reduce frizz. Use a product that is specifically designed for coarse thick hair.

5. Use a Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help to reduce breakage and keep your hair moisturized. Satin pillowcases are gentle on your hair and won’t cause friction like cotton pillowcases.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to help you manage your coarse thick natural hair:

Step 1: Shampoo Your Hair

Start by shampooing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo. Massage the shampoo into your scalp and work it through the length of your hair. Rinse thoroughly.

Step 2: Condition Your Hair

Apply a deep conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Cover your hair with a shower cap and leave the conditioner on for at least 30 minutes. Rinse thoroughly.

Step 3: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 4: Apply a Styling Cream

Apply a styling cream to your hair, focusing on the ends. Use your fingers to define your curls and reduce frizz.

Step 5: Dry Your Hair

Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer. Avoid using a regular hairdryer without a diffuser, as this can cause frizz and breakage.

Step 6: Sleep on a Satin Pillowcase

Before going to bed, make sure to sleep on a satin pillowcase. This will help to reduce breakage and keep your hair moisturized.

Tips to Manage Coarse Thick Natural Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you manage your coarse thick natural hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to detangle your hair.
  • Apply a deep conditioner to your hair at least once a week.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair sunscreen.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best way to detangle coarse thick natural hair?

The best way to detangle coarse thick natural hair is to use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots.

How often should I deep condition my hair?

You should deep condition your hair at least once a week. Use a product that is specifically designed for coarse thick hair.

Can I use a regular hairdryer on my hair?

Avoid using a regular hairdryer on your hair, as this can cause frizz and breakage. Use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer instead.

Hair Care for Coarse Thick Natural Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips for coarse thick natural hair:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo.
  • Use a deep conditioner at least once a week.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing it.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair.
  • Sleep on a satin pillowcase to reduce breakage.

By following these tips and techniques, you can manage your coarse thick natural hair and achieve healthy and beautiful hair.
