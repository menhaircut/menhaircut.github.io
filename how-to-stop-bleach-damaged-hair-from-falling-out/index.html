How To Stop Bleach Damaged Hair From Falling Out

Written by Robby Jul 25, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you've recently bleached your hair, you may have noticed that your hair is falling out. This is a common problem, as bleach can damage your hair and cause it to break. Fortunately, there are steps you can take to stop your hair from falling out.

Table of Contents

  • How to Stop Bleach Damaged Hair from Falling Out
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips to Prevent Hair Damage
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Bleached Hair

How to Stop Bleach Damaged Hair from Falling Out

The first step to stopping hair loss due to bleach damage is to stop using bleach. If you continue to bleach your hair, you will only make the problem worse. Instead, switch to a gentler hair coloring method, such as a semi-permanent or demi-permanent hair color.

Next, you should start using hair care products that are specifically designed for damaged hair. Look for products that contain ingredients such as keratin, biotin, and vitamin E. These ingredients will help to strengthen your hair and prevent further breakage.

You should also avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as these can further damage your hair. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray or serum to help protect your hair.

Step by Step Guide

1. Stop using bleach and switch to a gentler hair coloring method.

2. Use hair care products that are specifically designed for damaged hair.

3. Avoid using heat styling tools, or use a heat protectant spray or serum.

4. Trim your hair regularly to remove split ends and prevent further breakage.

Tips to Prevent Hair Damage

1. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush.

2. Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip the natural oils from your hair.

3. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help restore moisture to your hair.

4. Wear a hat or use a hair scarf to protect your hair from the sun.

FAQs

Q: Can bleach damage my hair permanently?

A: Yes, bleach can cause permanent damage to your hair if it is used improperly or too frequently.

Q: How long does it take for hair to recover from bleach damage?

A: It can take several months for your hair to fully recover from bleach damage. However, with proper care, you can prevent further damage and promote healthy hair growth.

Hair Care for Bleached Hair

If you have bleached hair, it's important to take extra care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage. Here are some tips for caring for bleached hair:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to help prevent further damage.

2. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help restore moisture to your hair.

3. Trim your hair regularly to remove split ends and prevent further breakage.

4. Avoid using heat styling tools, or use a heat protectant spray or serum.

By following these tips and taking proper care of your hair, you can stop bleach damaged hair from falling out and promote healthy hair growth.
