Can You Lose Hair After Covid 19

Written by Oliver Aug 20, 2023 · 3 min read
Can You Lose Hair after COVID-19? | Hair Care Tips 2023

COVID-19 has been a global pandemic that has affected millions of people worldwide. Although its main symptoms are fever, cough, and difficulty breathing, there have been reports of hair loss after COVID-19.

What is COVID-19?

COVID-19 is a highly contagious respiratory illness caused by the novel coronavirus. It first appeared in Wuhan, China, in December 2019, and has since spread to every corner of the world. The virus spreads through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs or sneezes.

Can You Lose Hair after COVID-19?

Yes, some people have reported hair loss after recovering from COVID-19. Hair loss is a common symptom of many illnesses, including viral infections. However, the severity and duration of hair loss may vary from person to person.

Why Does Hair Loss Happen?

Hair loss can occur for several reasons, such as stress, hormonal changes, genetics, and vitamin deficiencies. In the case of COVID-19, hair loss can happen due to the following reasons:

  • Physical stress caused by the illness
  • Emotional stress caused by the pandemic
  • Malnutrition due to loss of appetite or difficulty eating
  • Medications used to treat COVID-19

How to Prevent Hair Loss after COVID-19?

Preventing hair loss after COVID-19 involves taking care of your overall health and well-being. Here are some tips to prevent hair loss:

  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet
  • Get enough sleep and rest
  • Exercise regularly
  • Manage stress through meditation, yoga, or other relaxation techniques
  • Take prescribed medications as directed by your doctor

How to Treat Hair Loss after COVID-19?

If you are experiencing hair loss after COVID-19, there are several treatments available to help promote hair growth. Here are some of them:

  • Topical medications such as minoxidil
  • Oral medications such as finasteride
  • Hair transplant surgery
  • Low-level laser therapy

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips to maintain healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Condition your hair after every wash
  • Avoid using hot styling tools frequently
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair
  • Eat a diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth

FAQ

Can COVID-19 cause permanent hair loss?

In most cases, hair loss after COVID-19 is temporary and resolves on its own within a few months. However, in rare cases, it may lead to permanent hair loss, especially if it is due to scarring alopecia.

Can wearing a mask cause hair loss?

Wearing a mask does not cause hair loss, but it may cause some hair breakage or damage around the ears due to friction or tension.

Can stress cause hair loss?

Yes, stress is one of the most common causes of hair loss. It can cause a condition called telogen effluvium, in which hair follicles prematurely enter the resting phase, leading to hair shedding.

Conclusion

COVID-19 can cause hair loss as a result of physical and emotional stress, malnutrition, and medications. However, hair loss is usually temporary and resolves on its own within a few months. Taking care of your overall health and well-being can help prevent hair loss, and several treatments are available to promote hair growth.
