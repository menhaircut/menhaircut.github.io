Is It Better To Cut Wavy Hair Wet Or Dry

Written by Robby Nov 02, 2023 · 4 min read

When it comes to cutting wavy hair, one of the most common questions is whether it’s better to do it wet or dry. The answer, as with many things in the world of hair care, is that it depends. In this post, we’ll explore the pros and cons of both methods, as well as some tips for achieving the best results.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Wavy Hair Wet
  • How to Cut Wavy Hair Dry
  • Tips for Cutting Wavy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Wavy Hair

How to Cut Wavy Hair Wet

Cutting wavy hair when it’s wet is a popular choice, particularly among professional hairstylists. The wetness of the hair helps to weigh it down and make it easier to work with, as well as allowing for more precise cuts. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Wash and condition the hair as normal.
  2. Comb through the hair to remove any tangles.
  3. Starting at the nape of the neck, section the hair into manageable portions using clips.
  4. Take one section at a time and comb it straight down.
  5. Cut the hair to the desired length, using scissors or clippers.
  6. Repeat with each section until all of the hair has been cut.

While cutting wavy hair when it’s wet can be an effective method, it’s worth noting that the hair will look different once it dries. This means that it’s important to take into account any changes in texture, length or shape that may occur.

How to Cut Wavy Hair Dry

Cutting wavy hair when it’s dry is less common, but can be a good option for those who want to see the final results before making any major changes. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning the hair as normal.
  2. Allow the hair to air dry completely.
  3. Once the hair is dry, comb through it to remove any tangles.
  4. Take one section at a time and comb it straight down.
  5. Cut the hair to the desired length, using scissors or clippers.
  6. Repeat with each section until all of the hair has been cut.

One of the benefits of cutting wavy hair when it’s dry is that you can see how the hair will look once it’s styled. This can be particularly useful if you’re trying out a new haircut or style.

Tips for Cutting Wavy Hair

Whether you choose to cut wavy hair wet or dry, there are some tips that can help you achieve the best results:

  • Use sharp scissors or clippers to ensure clean cuts.
  • Take your time and work in small sections to avoid making mistakes.
  • Consider your natural hair texture when choosing a haircut or style.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help from a professional hairstylist if you’re unsure.

FAQs

Q: Will cutting my wavy hair make it curlier?

A: No, cutting wavy hair won’t make it curlier. However, it can help to enhance the natural wave pattern.

Q: How often should I cut my wavy hair?

A: It’s recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I cut my own wavy hair?

A: It’s possible to cut your own wavy hair, but it’s recommended to seek the help of a professional hairstylist for best results.

Hair Care for Wavy Hair

Cutting wavy hair is just one part of maintaining healthy, beautiful locks. Here are some tips for caring for your wavy hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat tools on a daily basis to prevent damage to the hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to enhance the natural wave pattern.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

By following these tips, you can keep your wavy hair looking and feeling its best.
