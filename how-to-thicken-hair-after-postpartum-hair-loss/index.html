How To Thicken Hair After Postpartum Hair Loss

Written by Frank Nov 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you are a new mom, you may have experienced postpartum hair loss. This is a common occurrence that happens after giving birth. During pregnancy, your hair may have been thick and full, but once you have given birth, you may notice that your hair is thinning and falling out. Don't worry, this is completely normal and can be temporary. In this article, we will discuss how to thicken hair after postpartum hair loss.

How to Thicken Hair After Postpartum Hair Loss

There are several ways to thicken your hair after postpartum hair loss:

1. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet is important for overall health, but it can also help with hair growth. Make sure you are getting enough vitamins and minerals, especially biotin, vitamin D, and iron. These nutrients can help promote hair growth and thickness.

2. Use Hair Growth Products

There are many hair growth products on the market that can help thicken your hair. Look for products that contain ingredients such as biotin, keratin, and collagen. These ingredients can help strengthen and thicken your hair.

3. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help stimulate hair growth. Use your fingertips to gently massage your scalp for a few minutes each day. This can help increase blood flow to your scalp, which can promote hair growth.

4. Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools can damage your hair and make it look thinner. Try to avoid using them as much as possible. If you must use heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray.

5. Cut Your Hair Short

Cutting your hair short can make it look thicker. Shorter hair is less likely to weigh down and look flat. Consider getting a short haircut to help make your hair look thicker.

Tips to Help Thicken Your Hair

Here are some additional tips to help thicken your hair after postpartum hair loss:

1. Take a Biotin Supplement

Taking a biotin supplement can help promote hair growth and thickness.

2. Use a Thickening Shampoo and Conditioner

Using a thickening shampoo and conditioner can help make your hair look thicker.

3. Get Regular Haircuts

Getting regular haircuts can help get rid of split ends and make your hair look thicker.

4. Don't Over-Wash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and make it look thinner. Try to wash your hair every other day.

5. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, especially when it is wet. Wet hair is more prone to breakage.

FAQ

Q: How long does postpartum hair loss last?

A: Postpartum hair loss can last up to a year after giving birth.

Q: Will my hair grow back after postpartum hair loss?

A: Yes, your hair should grow back after postpartum hair loss. It may take some time, but it should eventually grow back.

Hair Care for Postpartum Hair Loss

Here are some additional hair care tips for postpartum hair loss:

1. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, especially when it is wet. Wet hair is more prone to breakage.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, especially when it is wet. This can help prevent breakage.

3. Don't Wear Tight Hairstyles

Wearing tight hairstyles such as braids or ponytails can pull on your hair and cause breakage. Try to wear looser hairstyles.

4. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair product that contains SPF.

In conclusion, postpartum hair loss is a common occurrence that can be temporary. By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can help thicken your hair after postpartum hair loss.
