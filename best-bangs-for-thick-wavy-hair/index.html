Best Bangs For Thick Wavy Hair A Guide For 2023

If you have thick wavy hair, you know how challenging it can be to find the right hairstyle. Bangs can be a great option to add some dimension and texture to your hair. However, not all types of bangs work well with thick wavy hair. In this article, we will guide you through the best bangs for thick wavy hair in 2023. We will cover everything from how to choose the right bangs for your face shape to hair care tips to keep your bangs looking their best.

How to Choose the Right Bangs for Your Face Shape

When choosing bangs for your thick wavy hair, it is essential to consider your face shape. Different face shapes require different types of bangs to flatter your features. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Heart-shaped faces: Side-swept bangs work well to soften a pointed chin.
  • Oval faces: You can rock almost any type of bangs, but consider going for a longer, feathered look.
  • Square faces: Soft, wispy bangs that fall around the cheekbones can help soften a strong jawline.
  • Round faces: Longer, side-swept bangs that fall past the eyebrows can help elongate the face.

Remember that these are just general guidelines. Ultimately, you should choose a style that makes you feel confident and beautiful.

Types of Bangs That Work Best with Thick Wavy Hair

Not all types of bangs work well with thick wavy hair. Here are some of the best options to consider:

Straight Across Bangs

Straight across bangs can work well with thick wavy hair if they are cut at the right length. Aim for a length that falls just below the eyebrow to prevent them from puffing up or looking too heavy.

Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs are a great option for thick wavy hair. They are versatile and can be cut at various lengths to flatter different face shapes. Side-swept bangs can be styled to blend in with the rest of your hair or stand out as a statement piece.

Wispy Bangs

Wispy bangs are a popular choice for those with thick wavy hair. They are cut in a way that creates a feathered, textured look that complements waves and curls. Wispy bangs can be cut at various lengths to suit your face shape and hair texture.

How to Cut Bangs for Thick Wavy Hair

If you are cutting your own bangs, it is essential to use the right technique to prevent them from looking uneven or choppy. Here's how to cut bangs for thick wavy hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair as you normally would.
  2. Section off the hair you want to cut into a triangle shape.
  3. Use sharp scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting from the center and working your way outwards.
  4. Check the length of your bangs and trim any uneven pieces.
  5. Style your bangs as desired.

If you are not confident in cutting your own bangs, it is best to leave it to a professional stylist.

How to Style Bangs for Thick Wavy Hair

Styling your bangs can make a significant difference in the overall look of your hair. Here are some tips for styling bangs for thick wavy hair:

  • Blow-dry your bangs with a round brush to smooth them out and prevent frizz.
  • Apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to your bangs to add shine and reduce flyaways.
  • Use a curling iron or straightener to style your bangs, depending on the look you want to achieve.

Remember to use heat protectant products to prevent damage to your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Bangs for Thick Wavy Hair

Maintaining bangs can be challenging, especially if you have thick wavy hair. Here are some tips to keep your bangs looking great:

  • Wash your bangs frequently to prevent oil buildup.
  • Use dry shampoo or baby powder to absorb excess oil between washes.
  • Trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to prevent them from growing too long.
  • Avoid touching your bangs throughout the day to prevent them from becoming greasy.

FAQs About Bangs for Thick Wavy Hair

Can I wear bangs if I have naturally curly hair?

Absolutely! Bangs can look great with naturally curly hair. Just make sure to choose a length and style that complements your curls.

Can I wear bangs if I have a cowlick?

Cowlicks can make it challenging to wear bangs, but it is still possible to pull off the look. Consider going for a side-swept style to work with your cowlick.

Can I wear bangs if I have a short forehead?

Yes! Just make sure to go for a longer, side-swept style that falls past your eyebrows.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Wavy Hair with Bangs

Thick wavy hair can be challenging to manage, but with the right care and styling techniques, you can keep your hair healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent your hair from becoming too dry.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Apply a weekly hair mask to nourish your hair and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is wet to prevent breakage.

By following these tips and choosing the right bangs for your face shape and hair texture, you can achieve a beautiful, stylish look that complements your natural features.
