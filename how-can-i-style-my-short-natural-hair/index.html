How Can I Style My Short Natural Hair

Written by Robby Jun 16, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Short natural hair can be a challenge to style, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve a variety of looks that are both stylish and easy to maintain. In this article, we'll show you how to style your short natural hair, step by step. Whether you're looking for a sleek and sophisticated look or a fun and funky style, we've got you covered.

Description

Short natural hair is hair that has not been chemically processed or altered in any way. It is often characterized by its texture, which can range from curly to kinky. Styling short natural hair can be challenging, but it's also a great opportunity to experiment with different looks and textures.

How to Style Your Short Natural Hair

There are many different ways to style short natural hair, depending on your hair type and texture. Here are some tips to help you achieve the look you want:

1. Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. This will help to remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup and leave your hair soft and manageable.

2. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help detangle and moisturize your strands. This will also help to protect your hair from damage and breakage.

3. Apply a Styling Product

Choose a styling product that works best for your hair type and texture. For example, if you have fine hair, you may want to use a lightweight mousse or gel. If you have thick or coarse hair, you may need a heavier product like a cream or butter.

4. Define Your Curls

If you have curly or kinky hair, you can define your curls by using a curl-defining product or technique. Try using a twist-out, braid-out, or flexi-rod set to create defined curls and waves.

5. Create a Protective Style

If you want to protect your hair from damage and breakage, you can create a protective style like braids, twists, or a low bun. This will also help to reduce the amount of manipulation your hair undergoes, which can lead to breakage and damage.

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo. Rinse thoroughly and apply a moisturizing conditioner. Leave the conditioner on for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and distribute the product evenly.

Step 3: Apply a Styling Product

Apply a styling product to your hair, focusing on the roots and the ends. Use your fingers to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 4: Define Your Curls

If you have curly or kinky hair, you can define your curls by using a curl-defining product or technique. Try using a twist-out, braid-out, or flexi-rod set to create defined curls and waves.

Step 5: Create a Protective Style

If you want to protect your hair from damage and breakage, you can create a protective style like braids, twists, or a low bun. This will also help to reduce the amount of manipulation your hair undergoes, which can lead to breakage and damage.

Tips for Styling Short Natural Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when styling your short natural hair:

  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, as they can cause damage to your hair.
  • Try using a diffuser when drying your hair to help enhance your natural curl pattern.
  • Experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for your hair type and texture.

FAQ

Q: Can I straighten my short natural hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten your short natural hair, but it's important to use a heat protectant and avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can cause damage to your hair.

Q: How often should I wash my short natural hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and texture, but generally, you should aim to wash your hair once a week or every two weeks.

Q: Can I color my short natural hair?

A: Yes, you can color your short natural hair, but it's important to use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner and to avoid using harsh chemicals that can damage your hair.

Hair Care for Short Natural Hair

To keep your short natural hair healthy and looking its best, it's important to follow a regular hair care routine. This includes:

  • Washing and conditioning your hair regularly
  • Using a leave-in conditioner to moisturize and detangle your hair
  • Using a styling product that works best for your hair type and texture
  • Avoiding heat styling tools and harsh chemicals
  • Getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends

Styling short natural hair can be a challenge, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve a variety of looks that are both stylish and easy to maintain. Whether you're looking for a sleek and sophisticated look or a fun and funky style, there's a style out there for you. So go ahead and experiment with different styles and products, and have fun with your short natural hair!
