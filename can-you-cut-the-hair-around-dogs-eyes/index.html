Can You Cut The Hair Around Dogs Eyes

Written by Petter Jul 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As a dog owner, you might be wondering whether you can cut the hair around your dog's eyes. The answer is yes, you can! However, it's important to do it carefully and safely to avoid hurting your furry friend. In this article, we'll discuss how to cut your dog's eye hair, step by step, and provide you with some tips and FAQs to help make the process easier.

Description

Cutting the hair around your dog's eyes is important for several reasons. First, it can help prevent eye irritation and infections by keeping hair from poking into your dog's eyes. Second, it can improve your dog's vision and overall comfort. Finally, it can help your dog look neat and tidy, which is always a plus!

How to Cut Your Dog's Eye Hair

Cutting your dog's eye hair can be tricky, but it's not impossible. Here's how to do it safely:

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

You'll need a pair of scissors specifically designed for dog grooming, a comb, and some treats to keep your dog calm and happy.

Step 2: Brush Your Dog's Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your dog's hair is clean and brushed. This will make it easier to see the hair you need to trim.

Step 3: Identify the Hair to Trim

Use the comb to separate the hair around your dog's eyes. Identify the hair that's poking into your dog's eyes or obstructing their vision. This is the hair you'll need to trim.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Using the dog grooming scissors, carefully trim the hair you identified in step 3. Start by trimming a little at a time, and check your progress often to make sure you're not cutting too much. Hold your dog's head still with one hand and cut with the other, being careful not to poke your dog's eyes with the scissors.

Step 5: Reward Your Dog

Once you're finished, give your dog a treat and lots of praise for being a good boy or girl.

Tips for Cutting Your Dog's Eye Hair

Here are some tips to help make the process of cutting your dog's eye hair easier:

  • Start slowly and trim a little at a time.
  • Use treats to keep your dog calm and happy.
  • Be patient and take breaks if your dog gets restless.
  • Don't rush, as this can lead to mistakes and potential injuries.
  • Consider enlisting the help of a professional groomer if you're unsure about cutting your dog's hair yourself.

FAQs About Cutting Your Dog's Eye Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your dog's eye hair:

Q: Can cutting my dog's eye hair harm their eyes?

A: If done incorrectly, cutting your dog's eye hair can lead to eye injuries. However, if done carefully and with the right tools, it should not harm your dog's eyes.

Q: How often should I cut my dog's eye hair?

A: The frequency of cutting your dog's eye hair depends on how fast it grows. Some dogs may need to have their hair trimmed every few weeks, while others may only need it done every few months.

Q: Can I use regular scissors to cut my dog's eye hair?

A: No, regular scissors are not recommended for cutting your dog's eye hair. You should use scissors specifically designed for dog grooming.

Hair Care for Dogs

Cutting your dog's eye hair is just one part of proper dog grooming. Here are some other tips for keeping your dog looking and feeling their best:

  • Regularly brush your dog's coat to prevent matting and tangles.
  • Bathe your dog as needed, using a dog-specific shampoo.
  • Trim your dog's nails to prevent them from getting too long and causing discomfort.
  • Regularly clean your dog's ears to prevent infections.

Conclusion

Now that you know how to cut your dog's eye hair, you can help keep them comfortable and healthy. Just remember to be patient, take your time, and use the right tools. And, if you're unsure about cutting your dog's hair yourself, don't hesitate to seek the help of a professional groomer.
