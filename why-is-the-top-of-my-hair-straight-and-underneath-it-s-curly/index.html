Why Is The Top Of My Hair Straight And Underneath It s Curly

Written by Robby Aug 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have curly hair, you may have noticed that the top of your hair tends to be straight while the underneath is curly. This can be frustrating, especially if you want your curls to be uniform. In this article, we'll explore why this happens and what you can do to get more consistent curls.

Description

The reason why the top of your hair is straight while the underneath is curly has to do with the way your hair grows. Hair growth patterns are different for everyone, and genetics play a big role in determining how your hair grows. Some people have hair that grows outwards, while others have hair that grows downwards. The direction of hair growth can also change throughout your life, so what worked for you in the past may not work for you now.

How to

If you're struggling with uneven curls, there are some things you can do to encourage more consistent growth patterns. Here are a few tips:

1. Get regular trims

Getting regular trims can help promote more consistent hair growth. When you trim your hair, you remove split ends and damaged hair, which can prevent healthy hair from growing. Trimming your hair every 6-8 weeks can help you maintain healthy hair and promote even growth patterns.

2. Use a diffuser

Using a diffuser can help you achieve more uniform curls. A diffuser is an attachment for your hair dryer that disperses air evenly over your curls, helping them to dry evenly. This can help prevent the top of your hair from being straight while the underneath is curly.

3. Experiment with styling products

Styling products can also help you achieve more consistent curls. Look for products that are designed for curly hair and experiment with different formulations to find what works best for you. Some products can help enhance your curls, while others can help smooth out any frizz.

Step by Step

Here are some steps you can take to promote more consistent hair growth:

1. Trim your hair regularly

Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to remove split ends and damaged hair.

2. Use a diffuser

Use a diffuser attachment for your hair dryer to dry your curls evenly.

3. Experiment with styling products

Try out different styling products to find what works best for your hair.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to help promote healthy hair growth:

1. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and prevent healthy growth. Try to avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons on a daily basis.

2. Eat a healthy diet

Eating a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth.

3. Be gentle with your hair

Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, and be gentle when brushing or combing it.

FAQ

Q: Is it normal for the top of my hair to be straight?

A: Yes, it's normal for the top of your hair to be straight while the underneath is curly. This is due to the way your hair grows and is determined by genetics.

Q: Can I change the way my hair grows?

A: You can't change the way your hair grows, but you can promote healthy growth patterns by getting regular trims, using a diffuser, and experimenting with styling products.

Q: What styling products should I use for curly hair?

A: Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as curl enhancing creams, mousses, and gels. Experiment with different formulations to find what works best for your hair.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important in maintaining healthy growth patterns. Here are a few tips to help you care for your curls:

1. Wash your hair regularly

Wash your hair every 2-3 days to keep it clean and healthy.

2. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Look for a sulfate-free shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils.

3. Deep condition regularly

Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.

4. Protect your hair from heat

Avoid using hot tools on your hair, or use a heat protectant spray if you must use them.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can promote healthy growth patterns and achieve more consistent curls.
