How Long To Keep Natural Hair In Braids

Written by Christine Jun 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Keeping your natural hair in braids can be a great way to protect it from damage and promote growth. However, it's essential to know how long to keep your hair in braids to avoid any unforeseen damages. In this article, we will discuss how long to keep natural hair in braids and the best practices to maintain healthy hair.

  • Step-by-Step Guide to Braiding Natural Hair
  • Tips for Keeping Natural Hair in Braids

How to Braid Natural Hair

Braiding natural hair can be done in many ways, but most people use the traditional three-strand braid or cornrows. To braid your natural hair, follow these simple steps:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  2. Part your hair into sections using a comb
  3. Take one section and divide it into three equal parts
  4. Start braiding the hair by crossing the right strand over the middle strand, then the left strand over the new middle strand
  5. Continue braiding until you reach the end of the hair
  6. Secure the end of the braid with a hair tie or rubber band
  7. Repeat the process until you have braided all sections of your hair

Remember to be gentle when braiding your natural hair to avoid any breakage or damage.

Step-by-Step Guide to Braiding Natural Hair

If you're new to braiding natural hair, a step-by-step guide can be helpful. Here's how to braid natural hair in detail:

  1. Wash and condition your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  2. Divide your hair into sections using a comb
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to each section to make it easier to braid
  4. Take one section and divide it into three equal parts
  5. Start braiding the hair by crossing the right strand over the middle strand, then the left strand over the new middle strand
  6. After the first few crosses, add a small section of hair from the root of the hair to the right and left strands before crossing them over the middle strand
  7. Continue braiding until you reach the end of the hair
  8. Secure the end of the braid with a hair tie or rubber band
  9. Repeat the process until you have braided all sections of your hair
  10. When you're done, cover your hair with a satin or silk scarf or bonnet to prevent breakage and frizz

Tips for Keeping Natural Hair in Braids

Here are some tips to keep your natural hair healthy while it's in braids:

  • Don't keep your hair in braids for more than six weeks to avoid damage and breakage
  • Moisturize your hair regularly to prevent dryness and breakage
  • Use a light oil or leave-in conditioner to keep your hair soft and supple
  • Avoid tight braids that can cause tension and damage to your hairline
  • Don't use heavy products that can weigh down your hair and cause buildup
  • Avoid wearing braids that are too heavy for your hair type or length

Frequently Asked Questions

How long should you keep natural hair in braids?

You should keep your natural hair in braids for no more than six weeks to avoid damage and breakage.

Can you wash your hair while it's in braids?

Yes, you can wash your hair while it's in braids, but be gentle to avoid any tangling or damage to your hair.

How do you prevent breakage when taking out braids?

To prevent breakage when taking out your braids, use a detangler or oil to make it easier to remove the braids. Be gentle and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Braided Natural Hair

Here are some tips to take care of your hair after taking out your braids:

  • Wash and condition your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Deep condition your hair to restore moisture and promote growth
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair soft and manageable
  • Avoid using heat styling tools that can cause further damage to your hair
  • Trim your ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage

By following these tips, you can keep your natural hair healthy and strong while it's in braids and after taking them out.
