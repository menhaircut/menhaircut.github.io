How To Cut Soft Layers In Your Own Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your hair looking flat and lifeless? Adding soft layers to your hair can give it more movement and volume. But going to a salon can be expensive, so why not learn how to cut soft layers in your own hair? In this article, we'll give you step-by-step instructions on how to achieve this look at home. Let's get started!

How to Cut Soft Layers in Your Own Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can create split ends. You can purchase hair cutting scissors at most drugstores or beauty supply stores.

Step 2: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

It's important to start with clean, dry hair so you can see exactly how it falls. Shampoo and condition your hair as usual, and then blow dry it straight. This will make it easier to see where to cut.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into four sections: two on the sides and two in the back. Clip each section up and out of the way.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting with one of the back sections, take a small section of hair (about 1 inch in width) and comb it straight down. Hold the section of hair between your fingers where you want to cut it, and then cut into the hair at an angle. This will create a soft, layered effect.

Continue cutting small sections of hair in this manner until you reach the ends of your hair. Then move on to the next section, and repeat the process.

Step 5: Cut the Sides

Once you've finished the back sections, move on to the sides. Take a small section of hair from the front of one side, and hold it between your fingers where you want to cut it. Cut into the hair at an angle, just as you did with the back sections. Continue cutting small sections of hair in this manner until you've finished both sides.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the sections, it's time to blend the layers. Comb through your hair to make sure there are no uneven areas. If you notice any areas that need more layering, take small sections of hair and cut into them at an angle until they blend in with the rest of your hair.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Once you're happy with your layers, style your hair as usual. You can use a curling iron or flat iron to add more volume and movement to your hair.

Tips for Cutting Soft Layers in Your Own Hair

  • Start with small sections of hair, as it's easier to add more layers than to take them away.
  • Keep your scissors at an angle to create a soft, blended effect.
  • Don't cut too much at once, as this can lead to uneven layers.
  • Take breaks if your arm gets tired, as cutting your own hair can be a lengthy process.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair wet?

It's best to cut your hair dry, as wet hair can be misleading. When hair is wet, it appears longer than it actually is. This can lead to cutting off more than you intended.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care After Cutting Soft Layers

To keep your hair looking healthy and vibrant after cutting soft layers, it's important to take good care of it. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. You can also use a hair mask once a week to help nourish your hair and keep it looking soft and shiny.

Cutting soft layers in your own hair can seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's a doable task. Follow the steps we've outlined in this article, and you'll have a beautiful, layered look in no time!
