How To Bleach Asian Hair Blonde Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 29, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you an Asian looking to achieve the perfect blonde hair? Bleaching Asian hair blonde can be a tricky process, but with the right preparation and technique, it can be done successfully. In this article, we’ll guide you through the steps to bleach Asian hair blonde and share some tips on how to maintain your new look.

Table of Contents

  • How to Bleach Asian Hair Blonde
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Bleaching Asian Hair Blonde
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care after Bleaching Asian Hair

How to Bleach Asian Hair Blonde

Bleaching Asian hair blonde requires a lot of patience and care. It’s important to understand that everyone’s hair is different, and the process may take longer for some people. Here are some things you’ll need to get started:

  • Bleach powder
  • Developer
  • Gloves
  • Brush
  • Bowl
  • Plastic wrap
  • Shampoo and conditioner

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Protect your Skin and Clothing

Wear gloves and an old shirt that you don’t mind getting bleach on. You can also apply petroleum jelly around your hairline to prevent the bleach from touching your skin.

Step 2: Prepare the Bleach Mixture

Follow the instructions on the bleach powder and developer to create a mixture. Generally, you’ll need to mix two parts developer to one part bleach powder. Mix the ingredients in a bowl until you have a smooth consistency.

Step 3: Apply the Bleach Mixture

Apply the bleach mixture to your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. Use a brush to apply the mixture evenly throughout your hair. Once your hair is coated, cover it with plastic wrap to help the bleach develop.

Step 4: Check the Color

Check your hair color every 10-15 minutes. You can do this by wiping away a small section of the bleach mixture with a cotton ball. If your hair has reached the desired level of blonde, rinse it off immediately. If not, continue to check every 5-10 minutes until you reach the desired level of blonde.

Step 5: Rinse and Shampoo

Once you’ve achieved the desired level of blonde, rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water. Shampoo your hair to remove any remaining bleach residue.

Tips for Bleaching Asian Hair Blonde

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect blonde and maintain your hair’s health:

  • Use a lower volume developer if you’re new to bleaching or have fine hair.
  • Apply coconut oil to your hair a few hours before bleaching to help protect your hair from damage.
  • Don’t wash your hair for a few days before bleaching to help protect your scalp from irritation.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly after bleaching to help maintain its health and prevent breakage.

FAQs

How long will it take to bleach my Asian hair?

The time it takes to bleach Asian hair depends on the starting color of your hair and the level of blonde you want to achieve. It can take anywhere from 30 minutes to a few hours.

Can I bleach my hair if I’ve recently colored it?

It’s best to wait a few weeks after coloring your hair before bleaching it. Bleaching too soon after coloring can cause damage and breakage.

Will bleaching my hair damage it?

Bleaching hair can cause damage and breakage, especially if not done properly. It’s important to deep condition your hair regularly after bleaching to help maintain its health.

Hair Care after Bleaching Asian Hair

After bleaching Asian hair blonde, it’s important to take care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to help maintain your hair’s moisture.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair as much as possible.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to help maintain its health and prevent breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends.

With the right preparation and aftercare, bleaching Asian hair blonde can be a success. Follow these steps and tips to achieve the perfect blonde and maintain your hair’s health.
