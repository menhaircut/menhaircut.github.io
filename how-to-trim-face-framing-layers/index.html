How To Trim Face Framing Layers The Ultimate Guide

Written by Bardi Nov 08, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’re looking to switch up your hairstyle and add some dimension to your locks, face framing layers may be just what you need. These layers help to accentuate your features and add shape to your face, giving you a fresh and youthful look. But how do you trim face framing layers at home without making a mess of your hair? In this article, we’ll walk you through the process step by step, and provide you with some expert tips and advice to help you achieve salon-quality results.

Description

Face framing layers are a popular hairstyle trend that involves cutting shorter layers around the face to create a flattering and dynamic look. These layers can be styled in a variety of ways, from straight and sleek to voluminous and curly, making them a versatile option for any hair type or texture. Whether you’re looking to add some depth to your hair or simply want to switch up your style, face framing layers are a great option.

How to Trim Face Framing Layers

Trimming your face framing layers at home can be a bit intimidating, especially if you’re not used to cutting your own hair. But with the right tools and techniques, it’s actually quite easy to achieve great results. Here’s how:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you get started, you’ll need to gather some basic tools to help you trim your hair. You’ll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to help section off your hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It’s best to trim your hair when it’s clean and dry, so start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. This will help you to see the natural shape of your hair and make it easier to trim your layers evenly.

Step 3: Section Off Your Hair

Using your comb, section off the hair around your face that you want to trim. This will typically be the hair that falls in front of your ears and frames your face. Use hair clips to hold the rest of your hair back.

Step 4: Trim Your Layers

Starting with one side of your face, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your hair cutting scissors to trim the hair to your desired length, working from the bottom up. Repeat this process on the other side of your face, making sure to keep your cuts even and symmetrical.

Step 5: Blend Your Layers

After you’ve trimmed your face framing layers to the desired length, use your scissors to blend the layers into the rest of your hair. This will help to create a seamless transition and prevent any harsh lines.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once you’ve finished trimming your face framing layers, it’s time to style your hair. You can use a curling iron or straightener to create the look you want, or simply let your hair air dry for a more natural look.

Tips for Trimming Face Framing Layers

Trimming your own hair can be tricky, but with these expert tips, you’ll be able to achieve salon-quality results:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up, rather than cutting off too much hair at once.
  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Make sure your hair is dry and tangle-free before you start trimming.
  • Take your time and be patient – rushing can lead to mistakes.
  • If you’re not sure how much hair to trim, start with a small amount and work your way up.

FAQs

1. How often should I trim my face framing layers?

It’s recommended that you trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

2. Can I trim my face framing layers with regular scissors?

No, it’s important to use sharp hair cutting scissors to ensure clean and even cuts.

3. Can I trim my face framing layers if I have curly hair?

Absolutely! Face framing layers can be styled in a variety of ways, including curly and wavy styles.

Hair Care Tips

Now that you know how to trim your face framing layers, it’s important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and nourished.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and nutrients to promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips and techniques, you’ll be able to trim your face framing layers like a pro, and keep your hair looking beautiful and healthy for years to come.
