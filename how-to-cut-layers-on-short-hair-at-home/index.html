How To Cut Layers On Short Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your short hair looking flat and lifeless? Adding layers can bring dimension and movement to your hairstyle. But going to a salon for a haircut can be expensive, especially if you need to maintain it frequently. Fortunately, cutting layers on short hair at home is not as difficult as you might think. In this guide, we will provide you with everything you need to know to achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home.

Description

Layered haircuts have been popular for decades, and for good reason. They add volume, texture, and shape to any haircut. Short hair, in particular, can benefit from layers as they can make your hair look fuller and more dynamic. However, cutting layers on short hair can be intimidating, especially if you are not a professional hairstylist. This guide will provide you with easy-to-follow steps, tips, and tricks to help you cut layers on short hair at home.

How to Cut Layers on Short Hair at Home

Before you start cutting your hair, you will need to have the right tools. Here are the essential items you will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Once you have gathered your tools, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure it is completely dry before you start cutting. Wet hair can shrink, making it harder to achieve the desired length.

Step 2: Section your hair

Using your comb, divide your hair into sections. Use hair clips to secure the top section of your hair, leaving the bottom section loose.

Step 3: Start cutting

Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the hair at an angle. This will create a soft, feathery effect. Repeat this process for the rest of the bottom layer.

Once you have finished cutting the bottom layer, release the top section of your hair and repeat the same process.

Step 4: Blend the layers

Using your scissors, blend the layers together by cutting into the ends of the hair. This will create a seamless transition between the layers.

Step 5: Check your work

Once you have finished cutting your hair, comb it out and check for any uneven sections. Make any necessary adjustments until you are happy with the result.

Tips for Cutting Layers on Short Hair at Home

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best result:

  • Start with a small section of hair and work your way up. It is easier to cut more hair than to fix a mistake.
  • Don't cut your hair too short. Remember that hair shrinks when it dries, so leave some extra length to compensate.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't cut your hair when it is wet. Wet hair can be misleading, and you might end up cutting more than you intended.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your hair can be tiring, so rest your hands and eyes regularly.

FAQ

Q: How do I know if layers will suit me?

A: Layers can flatter any face shape and hair type. However, if you have very fine hair, too many layers can make your hair look thin. Consult with a professional hairstylist if you are unsure.

Q: Can I cut my hair using regular scissors?

A: While you can cut your hair using regular scissors, it is not recommended. Regular scissors are not as sharp as hair-cutting scissors, and they can damage your hair.

Q: Can I cut layers on curly hair?

A: Yes, you can cut layers on curly hair. However, keep in mind that curly hair shrinks when it dries, so make sure you cut your hair longer than you intend it to be.

Hair Care after Cutting Layers on Short Hair

Now that you have successfully cut layers on your short hair, it is important to take care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible. If you need to use them, make sure to use a heat protectant spray first.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your haircut.
  • Use hair masks and oils to nourish and hydrate your hair.

With these tips, you can enjoy your new layered haircut for a long time.
