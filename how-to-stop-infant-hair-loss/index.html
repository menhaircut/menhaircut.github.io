How To Stop Infant Hair Loss Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Jun 14, 2023 · 5 min read

If you’re a new parent, you might be worried about your baby’s hair loss. It’s completely normal for infants to lose hair during their first few months of life. However, there are steps you can take to prevent or reduce hair loss in infants. In this article, we’ll explore how to stop infant hair loss and provide tips and tricks on hair care for your little one.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Stop Infant Hair Loss
  2. Hair Care for Infants
  3. FAQ

How to Stop Infant Hair Loss

1. Be Gentle with Your Baby’s Hair

Infant hair is delicate, so it’s important to be gentle when washing and brushing your baby’s hair. Use a soft-bristled brush or a wide-toothed comb to avoid tugging at the hair. Avoid using hair accessories like clips, ties, or headbands, as they can pull on the hair and cause damage.

2. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles like braids, ponytails, or buns can cause hair loss in infants. The tension from the hairstyle can pull on the hair, causing it to fall out. Instead, opt for loose hairstyles like a simple part or a gentle comb-over.

3. Don’t Over-wash Your Baby’s Hair

Washing your baby’s hair too often can strip the scalp of its natural oils, leading to dryness and hair loss. Aim to wash your baby’s hair no more than once or twice a week, using a mild, tear-free shampoo.

4. Keep Your Baby’s Scalp Healthy

A healthy scalp means healthy hair. To keep your baby’s scalp healthy, make sure to keep it clean and moisturized. Use a gentle moisturizer or oil to massage the scalp, which can promote hair growth and reduce hair loss.

5. Choose the Right Products

When it comes to hair products for infants, less is more. Avoid using adult hair products on your baby’s hair, as they can be too harsh. Instead, opt for products specifically designed for infants, like gentle shampoos and conditioners.

Hair Care for Infants

1. Use a Soft-bristled Brush

Use a soft-bristled brush to gently comb your baby’s hair. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends, being careful not to tug on the hair. Brushing your baby’s hair can help distribute natural oils and stimulate hair growth.

2. Massage the Scalp

Massaging your baby’s scalp can promote hair growth and reduce hair loss. Use a gentle moisturizer or oil to massage the scalp, using circular motions with your fingertips.

3. Protect Your Baby’s Hair from the Sun

Just like adults, infants can get sunburned. To protect your baby’s hair and scalp from the sun, use a hat or a lightweight scarf to cover their head when outdoors.

4. Don’t Use Heat Styling Tools

Avoid using heat styling tools like hair dryers, curling irons, or straighteners on your baby’s hair. The heat can damage the hair and cause it to fall out. Instead, let your baby’s hair air dry naturally.

FAQ

1. Is it normal for infants to lose hair?

Yes, it’s completely normal for infants to lose hair during their first few months of life. This is called “telogen effluvium” and is caused by hormonal changes in the body.

2. When will my baby’s hair grow back?

Most infants’ hair will grow back by the time they are 6 months old. However, every baby is different, and it can take up to a year for some babies’ hair to grow back.

3. Should I be worried if my baby is losing a lot of hair?

If your baby is losing a lot of hair, it’s always a good idea to talk to your pediatrician. While hair loss is normal in infants, excessive hair loss can sometimes be a sign of an underlying health issue.

4. Can I use adult hair products on my baby’s hair?

No, it’s not recommended to use adult hair products on your baby’s hair. Adult hair products can be too harsh for infants’ delicate hair and scalp. Instead, opt for products specifically designed for infants.

5. How often should I wash my baby’s hair?

You should aim to wash your baby’s hair no more than once or twice a week. Washing your baby’s hair too often can strip the scalp of its natural oils, leading to dryness and hair loss.

6. Can tight hairstyles cause hair loss in infants?

Yes, tight hairstyles like braids, ponytails, or buns can cause hair loss in infants. The tension from the hairstyle can pull on the hair, causing it to fall out. Instead, opt for loose hairstyles like a simple part or a gentle comb-over.

Conclusion

While hair loss in infants is normal, there are steps you can take to prevent or reduce it. By being gentle with your baby’s hair, avoiding tight hairstyles, and using the right products, you can help promote healthy hair growth and reduce hair loss. Remember to be patient – your baby’s hair will grow back in no time!
