How To Cut Long Layers In Medium Length Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to add some dimension and movement to your medium-length hair, cutting long layers is a great way to do so. Not only can this style give your hair more body, but it can also help to frame your face and create a softer, more feminine look. In this step-by-step guide, we'll show you how to cut long layers in medium length hair from the comfort of your own home.

Description

Long layers are a versatile haircut that can flatter a variety of face shapes and hair types. This style involves cutting hair at different lengths, which adds depth and texture to your hair. Long layers can be particularly flattering for medium length hair, as they can help to create the illusion of length and volume.

How to

Before you begin cutting, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to section off your hair.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow drying it. This will help you see the natural texture of your hair, which can make it easier to cut the layers.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use your comb to create a center part in your hair, then separate your hair into two sections. Clip each section to keep it out of the way.

Step 3: Cut Your First Layer

Starting with one side of your hair, take a small section of hair from the top of your head and hold it straight up. Use your scissors to cut the hair at a slight angle, about an inch or two from the ends. This will create your first layer.

Step 4: Continue Cutting Layers

Continue cutting layers in small sections, working your way down your head. For each layer, take a small section of hair and cut it at a slight angle, making sure to keep the length of the layers consistent.

Step 5: Repeat on the Other Side

Once you've finished one side of your hair, repeat the process on the other side.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting all the layers, use your comb to check for any unevenness. Make any necessary adjustments by trimming any sections that are longer than the rest.

Tips

- Start with small sections of hair to ensure evenness and consistency.

- Use a mirror to check the back of your head while cutting.

- If you have curly hair, consider cutting your hair while it's dry to avoid any surprises once your hair dries.

FAQ

How often should I cut my hair if I have long layers?

It's recommended to get a trim every 8-12 weeks to maintain the length and health of your hair.

Can I cut long layers in my own hair?

Yes, you can cut long layers in your own hair. However, it's important to be patient and take your time to ensure evenness and consistency.

Hair Care

To keep your long layers looking their best, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Consider using a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.

Cutting long layers in medium length hair is a great way to add some dimension and movement to your hair. By following these steps and taking care of your hair, you can enjoy this flattering and versatile style for months to come.
