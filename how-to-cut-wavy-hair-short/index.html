How To Cut Wavy Hair Short A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 26, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of your long, wavy locks and ready for a fresh, short haircut? Cutting wavy hair can be tricky, but with the right technique and tools, you can achieve a stylish and low-maintenance look. In this article, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut wavy hair short.

Description

Wavy hair can be a challenge to cut, as the natural texture and movement can make it difficult to achieve a clean and sharp look. However, with the right approach, cutting wavy hair short can give you a chic and effortless style that is easy to maintain. Whether you're looking for a bob, a pixie cut, or a shaggy style, there are plenty of options for wavy hair.

How to

Before you begin cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and clips to keep your hair in place. It's also a good idea to have a mirror and a spray bottle of water to dampen your hair as you cut. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will remove any dirt or product buildup and allow you to see your hair's natural texture. If your hair is particularly unruly, you may want to use a smoothing serum or cream to tame it.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use your comb to divide your hair into sections. Start at the back and work your way forward, clipping each section out of the way as you go. This will make it easier to cut each section evenly.

Step 3: Determine the Length

Decide on the length you want for your hair. Keep in mind that wavy hair tends to shrink when it dries, so you may want to leave it a little longer than you initially planned.

Step 4: Cut the Back

Start at the back of your head and work your way up. Take small sections of hair and hold them between your fingers, pulling them straight down. Use your scissors to cut the hair to the desired length, being careful not to take off too much at once.

Step 5: Cut the Sides

Release the sections you clipped at the sides of your head. Take small sections of hair and pull them straight out from your head, cutting them to the desired length.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

To create a more natural look, you may want to blend the layers of your hair. Comb each section of hair straight up and use your scissors to cut into the ends, creating a textured effect.

Step 7: Check the Length

Once you've finished cutting, check the length of your hair in the mirror. If you notice any uneven spots, go back and trim them.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Once you're happy with the length, style your hair as desired. You can use a blow dryer, curling iron, or straightener to create different looks.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect short, wavy haircut:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up, taking care not to cut off too much at once.
  • Use a comb to create a smooth, even surface for cutting.
  • Take breaks as needed to rest your hands and avoid fatigue.
  • Experiment with different styles and lengths to find what works best for you.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting wavy hair short:

Can I cut my wavy hair myself?

Yes, you can cut your wavy hair yourself if you have the right tools and technique. However, it's always a good idea to consult with a professional stylist for guidance.

What is the best length for wavy hair?

The best length for wavy hair depends on your personal style and preferences. However, shorter styles tend to be easier to manage and can show off your natural texture.

How often should I cut my wavy hair?

It's recommended to cut your wavy hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

To keep your short, wavy hair looking its best, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner formulated for wavy hair to keep it hydrated and frizz-free.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized.

Cutting wavy hair short can be a fun and exciting change, but it's important to take your time and be patient. With the right technique and care, you can achieve a stylish and low-maintenance look that shows off your natural texture.
