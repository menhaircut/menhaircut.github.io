Why Is One Side Of My Hair More Curly

Written by Eveline Aug 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have naturally curly hair, you may have noticed that one side of your hair is more curly than the other. This can be frustrating and make styling your hair more difficult. In this blog post, we will explore why one side of your hair is more curly, how to manage it, and some tips for taking care of your curly hair.

Description

Natural curly hair is caused by genetics, and it is common for people to have different curl patterns on different parts of their head. This is because the shape of the hair follicles on each side of your head can vary, resulting in different curl patterns.

Additionally, environmental factors, such as humidity and heat, can also affect the texture and curl pattern of your hair. If you sleep on one side of your head more than the other, this can also cause one side of your hair to be more curly.

How to Manage It

If you want to manage the difference in curl pattern between the two sides of your head, there are a few things you can do. One option is to get a haircut that is specifically designed for curly hair. A curly hair specialist will be able to create a cut that will help balance out the curl pattern on both sides of your head.

Another option is to use styling products that will help define your curls and create a more uniform look. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as curl creams or gels. Apply the product evenly to both sides of your head to help create a more balanced look.

Step by Step

1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are designed for curly hair.

2. Gently towel dry your hair, being careful not to rub your curls too vigorously.

3. Apply a curl cream or gel to your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly on both sides of your head.

4. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair, scrunching your curls as you go.

5. Once your hair is dry, use a hair oil or serum to add shine and reduce frizz.

Tips

- Sleep with your hair in a loose bun or braid to help prevent one side of your hair from becoming more curly than the other.

- Don't over-wash your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz.

- Invest in a silk pillowcase to help reduce friction and prevent frizz while you sleep.

FAQ

Q: Can I train my hair to be more curly on one side?

A: Unfortunately, you cannot train your hair to be more curly on one side. The curl pattern of your hair is determined by genetics and cannot be changed.

Q: Can I straighten my hair to create a more uniform look?

A: Yes, you can straighten your hair to create a more uniform look. However, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, as this can damage your hair.

Hair Care

Taking care of curly hair requires a bit of extra effort, but it is worth it to keep your curls looking their best. Be sure to use products that are specifically designed for curly hair, and avoid using products that contain sulfates or alcohol, as these can dry out your hair.

Additionally, be gentle when handling your hair, and avoid brushing it when it is dry. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it is wet, and use your fingers to gently separate your curls.

Finally, be sure to get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. With a little bit of extra care, you can keep your curly hair looking fabulous!
