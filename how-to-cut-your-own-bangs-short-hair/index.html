How To Cut Your Own Bangs For Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Dec 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to give yourself a new look or just want to trim your bangs, cutting your own bangs can be a great option. However, if you're new to cutting your own hair, it can be intimidating. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your own bangs for short hair in a few easy steps. We'll also provide you with some tips and tricks to make the process easier and answer some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair.

  • How to Cut Your Own Bangs for Short Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Cut Your Own Bangs for Short Hair

Before we get started, it's important to note that cutting your own bangs requires some skill and patience. It's also important to use the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips to section off your hair
  • Large mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own bangs for short hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair. It's easier to cut your hair when it's clean and dry.
  2. Comb your hair forward and separate your bangs from the rest of your hair. Use clips to hold the rest of your hair back.
  3. Decide on the length of your bangs. Use a ruler or measuring tape to ensure that your bangs are even.
  4. Hold your bangs between two fingers and cut the ends with sharp hair cutting scissors. Use small snips and cut slowly to avoid cutting too much at once.
  5. Check your work in the mirror. If your bangs are too long, trim them a little more. If they're too short, you'll have to wait for them to grow out.
  6. When you're happy with the length, use the scissors to add some texture to your bangs. Cut into the ends to create a choppy, textured look.
  7. Style your bangs as desired.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to make cutting your own bangs easier:

  • Use a sharp pair of hair cutting scissors. Dull scissors can cause split ends and make it difficult to cut your hair evenly.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair can shrink up to an inch when it dries, which can make your bangs shorter than you intended.
  • Use a mirror to check your work. Make sure that your bangs are even and at the right length.
  • Start with small snips. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Consider trimming your bangs when they're dry. This will give you a better idea of how they'll look when styled.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own bangs:

Can I cut my own bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own bangs if you have curly hair. However, it's important to cut your hair when it's dry to ensure that you're cutting it at the right length.

How often should I trim my bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how often you want to maintain your bangs. As a general rule, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Bangs

After you've cut your own bangs, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and styled. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair soft and smooth.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair nourished and hydrated.
  • Consider using a dry shampoo to extend the life of your bangs between washings.

Cutting your own bangs can be a great way to change up your look or maintain your hairstyle between salon visits. With the right tools and some patience, you can give yourself a stylish new 'do in no time.
