Can Hypothyroidism Cause Bald Spots

Written by Robby Sep 22, 2023 · 4 min read

When it comes to hair loss, there are many factors that can contribute to the problem. One of them is hypothyroidism, a condition where the thyroid gland doesn’t produce enough hormones. In this article, we’ll explore the link between hypothyroidism and bald spots, and what you can do to prevent or treat this condition.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Recognize Hypothyroidism-Related Bald Spots
  • Step-by-Step: How to Treat Hypothyroidism-Related Bald Spots

Description

Hypothyroidism is a condition that affects the thyroid gland, a small gland located in the neck. The thyroid gland produces hormones that regulate the body’s metabolism, or the way the body uses energy. When the thyroid gland doesn’t produce enough hormones, the body’s metabolism slows down, which can lead to a variety of symptoms, including hair loss.

Hair loss due to hypothyroidism can occur in different areas of the scalp, including the front, sides, and back. In some cases, the hair loss can be patchy, resulting in bald spots. If you have hypothyroidism and notice bald spots on your scalp, it’s important to seek medical attention to determine the underlying cause of your hair loss.

How to Recognize Hypothyroidism-Related Bald Spots

Bald spots caused by hypothyroidism can be difficult to distinguish from other types of hair loss. However, there are a few signs that can help you identify hypothyroidism-related bald spots:

  • Slow hair growth: If your hair is growing slowly or not at all, it could be a sign of hypothyroidism.
  • Dry, brittle hair: Hypothyroidism can cause your hair to become dry and brittle, which can lead to breakage and hair loss.
  • Thinning hair: If you notice that your hair is getting thinner over time, it could be a sign of hypothyroidism.
  • Bald spots: If you have small, circular bald spots on your scalp, it could be a sign of hypothyroidism-related hair loss.

Step-by-Step: How to Treat Hypothyroidism-Related Bald Spots

If your hair loss is caused by hypothyroidism, the best way to treat it is to address the underlying condition. Here are the steps you can take:

  1. Get a diagnosis: If you suspect that you have hypothyroidism, see a doctor for a diagnosis. Your doctor may perform a blood test to measure your thyroid hormone levels.
  2. Take medication: If you are diagnosed with hypothyroidism, your doctor may prescribe medication to help regulate your thyroid hormone levels.
  3. Eat a healthy diet: Eating a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, fruits, and vegetables can help support healthy hair growth.
  4. Manage stress: Stress can worsen hair loss, so finding healthy ways to manage stress, such as exercise or meditation, can help.

Tips for Managing Hypothyroidism-Related Hair Loss

While treating the underlying condition is the best way to manage hypothyroidism-related hair loss, there are some additional steps you can take to promote healthy hair growth:

  • Use gentle hair care products: Harsh chemicals in hair care products can damage your hair and worsen hair loss. Choose gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling tools like flat irons and hair dryers can damage your hair and worsen hair loss. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat to a minimum.
  • Protect your hair from the sun: UV rays from the sun can damage your hair and scalp. Wear a hat or use a hair care product with SPF to protect your hair.

FAQs About Hypothyroidism and Bald Spots

Q: Can hypothyroidism cause bald spots in women?

A: Yes, hypothyroidism can cause bald spots in both men and women.

Q: Will my hair grow back after hypothyroidism treatment?

A: In most cases, hair loss caused by hypothyroidism is reversible with treatment. However, it may take several months for your hair to grow back fully.

Q: Can hypothyroidism cause permanent hair loss?

A: In rare cases, hypothyroidism can cause permanent hair loss. However, this is not common and can usually be prevented with prompt treatment.

Hair Care for Hypothyroidism Patients

If you have hypothyroidism, taking good care of your hair can help prevent further hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly: Keeping your scalp clean can help prevent hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.
  • Massage your scalp: Massaging your scalp can improve blood flow to your hair follicles, which can promote healthy hair growth.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles: Tight hairstyles like braids and ponytails can pull on your hair and worsen hair loss. Opt for looser styles instead.

Overall, hypothyroidism can cause bald spots and other types of hair loss. If you have hypothyroidism and notice hair loss, seek medical attention to determine the underlying cause and get prompt treatment. With proper care and treatment, you can manage hypothyroidism-related hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.
