Is It Normal For Baby To Have Bald Spot On Back Of Head

Written by Bardi Jun 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As a new parent, it can be alarming to notice a bald spot on the back of your baby's head. However, it's important to know that this is a common occurrence in many babies and is often nothing to worry about. In this article, we'll answer the question, "is it normal for baby to have bald spot on back of head?" and provide tips on how to care for your baby's hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Determine if Bald Spot is Normal
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Caring for Your Baby's Hair
  • Tips for Preventing Bald Spots

Description

A bald spot on the back of your baby's head is typically a result of laying your baby on their back for extended periods of time. This can cause pressure on the baby's head and lead to hair loss in that area. While it's not harmful to your baby's health, it can be concerning for parents.

How to Determine if Bald Spot is Normal

As mentioned, a bald spot on the back of your baby's head is typically not a cause for concern. However, there are a few things to look for to determine if your baby's bald spot is normal:

  • The bald spot is relatively small and circular in shape
  • The surrounding hair is still growing normally
  • Your baby is not experiencing any pain or discomfort in that area

If you notice any redness or irritation around the bald spot or your baby seems to be in pain, it's important to consult with your pediatrician to rule out any underlying health issues.

Step-by-Step Guide to Caring for Your Baby's Hair

While a bald spot on the back of your baby's head is not harmful, it's important to take care of their hair to prevent further hair loss. Here are some steps to follow:

  1. Gently wash your baby's hair with a mild baby shampoo
  2. Avoid pulling or tugging on your baby's hair when brushing or combing
  3. Use a soft-bristled brush to gently massage your baby's scalp, which can promote hair growth
  4. Consider using a baby hat or headband to prevent friction on the bald spot when your baby is laying down or in a car seat

Following these steps can help promote healthy hair growth and prevent further hair loss.

Tips for Preventing Bald Spots

While a bald spot on the back of your baby's head is often unavoidable, there are some tips to help prevent it from occurring:

  • Alternate the direction your baby lays in their crib or bassinet
  • Give your baby plenty of tummy time during the day to relieve pressure on the back of their head
  • Consider using a baby carrier or wrap to carry your baby, which can prevent prolonged pressure on the back of their head

By following these tips, you can help prevent a bald spot from forming in the first place.

FAQs

What causes a bald spot on the back of a baby's head?

A bald spot on the back of a baby's head is typically caused by pressure on the baby's head from laying on their back for extended periods of time.

Is a bald spot on the back of a baby's head normal?

Yes, a bald spot on the back of a baby's head is often normal and not a cause for concern.

Will my baby's hair grow back after a bald spot forms?

Yes, in most cases your baby's hair will grow back after a bald spot forms. However, it may take several months for the hair to fully grow back.

Hair Care for Your Baby

While a bald spot on the back of your baby's head can be concerning, it's important to remember that it's often normal and not harmful to your baby's health. By following the steps outlined in this article and taking care of your baby's hair, you can promote healthy hair growth and prevent further hair loss.
