Introduction

Do you struggle with getting the sides of your hair to lay flat? This is a common problem that many people face, especially those with curly or wavy hair. Fortunately, there are several tips and tricks that you can use to achieve the sleek, smooth look that you desire. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about getting the sides of your hair to lay flat, from how to do it to hair care tips that will help you maintain your style.

How to Get the Sides of Your Hair to Lay Flat

Here are some of the most effective ways to get the sides of your hair to lay flat:

1. Blow Dry Your Hair

One of the easiest ways to get your hair to lay flat is to blow dry it. After washing your hair, use a blow dryer to dry it while brushing it with a flat brush. This will help smooth out any bumps or kinks in your hair and give you a sleek, polished look. To keep your hair from getting too dry, use a heat protectant spray before blow drying.

2. Use Flat Irons

If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, you may need to use a flat iron to get your hair to lay flat. Start by applying a heat protectant spray to your hair, then use a flat iron to straighten your hair in small sections. Be sure to use a high-quality flat iron that won't damage your hair.

3. Apply Hair Gel or Mousse

Another option is to apply hair gel or mousse to your hair while it's wet. This will help hold your hair in place and keep it from getting frizzy. Be sure to use a lightweight formula that won't weigh your hair down or make it feel greasy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get the sides of your hair to lay flat:

1. Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help keep your hair hydrated and prevent it from getting too dry.

2. Apply Heat Protectant Spray

Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair to protect it from heat damage. This is especially important if you plan to blow dry or flat iron your hair.

3. Blow Dry Your Hair

Use a flat brush to blow dry your hair, brushing it from the roots to the tips. This will help smooth out any bumps or kinks in your hair and give you a sleek, polished look.

4. Use Flat Irons

If needed, use a flat iron to straighten your hair in small sections. Be sure to use a high-quality flat iron that won't damage your hair.

5. Apply Hair Gel or Mousse

Apply a lightweight hair gel or mousse to your hair while it's wet to help hold your hair in place and keep it from getting frizzy.

Tips for Keeping Your Hair Flat

Here are some tips for keeping your hair flat:

1. Use a Silk or Satin Pillowcase

A silk or satin pillowcase will help prevent your hair from getting frizzy while you sleep. It will also help prevent breakage and damage to your hair.

2. Avoid Touching Your Hair Too Much

The more you touch your hair, the more likely it is to get frizzy or lose its shape. Try to avoid touching your hair too much, especially when it's humid outside.

3. Use a Humidity-Blocking Hairspray

A humidity-blocking hairspray will help keep your hair from getting frizzy in humid weather. Look for a lightweight formula that won't weigh your hair down.

FAQs

1. Can I get my hair to lay flat without using heat?

Yes, you can get your hair to lay flat without using heat. Try using a hair gel or mousse to hold your hair in place while it air dries.

2. How often should I wash my hair?

It's best to wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent it from getting too dry or oily.

3. Can I use a hair straightener to get my hair to lay flat?

Yes, you can use a hair straightener to get your hair to lay flat. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray and a high-quality flat iron that won't damage your hair.

Hair Care for Flat Hair

Here are some tips for caring for flat hair:

1. Use a Lightweight Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a lightweight shampoo and conditioner that won't weigh your hair down or make it feel greasy.

2. Avoid Over-Styling Your Hair

Try to avoid over-styling your hair, as this can lead to breakage and damage. Stick to simple styles that don't require a lot of heat or product.

3. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims will help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Getting the sides of your hair to lay flat can be a challenge, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve a sleek, polished look. Try out some of the tips and tricks in this article to see what works best for your hair type, and don't forget to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and beautiful.
