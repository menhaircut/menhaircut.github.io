What Kind Of Clothes Were Worn In The 60 S

Written by Robby Aug 31, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're a fan of vintage fashion, you've probably wondered what kind of clothes were worn in the 60's. From mod styles to bohemian looks, the 1960s were a decade of bold fashion statements and experimentation. In this article, we'll explore the key trends of the 60's and how to incorporate them into your wardrobe today.

Key Trends of the 60's

Mod Style

One of the most iconic fashion trends of the 60's was the mod style. This look was characterized by short hemlines, bold patterns, and bright colors. Shift dresses, mini skirts, and go-go boots were popular items in the mod wardrobe. To create a mod-inspired look today, try pairing a colorful shift dress with knee-high boots.

Hippie Style

The hippie style emerged in the latter half of the 60's and was all about free-spirited, bohemian fashion. Long flowing skirts, peasant blouses, and fringe jackets were key items in the hippie wardrobe. This style was also known for its use of natural fabrics like cotton and hemp. To embrace the hippie look today, try pairing a flowy maxi dress with a denim jacket.

Military Style

The military style was another trend that was popular in the 60's. This look was characterized by structured jackets, cargo pants, and combat boots. Olive green and khaki were popular colors in the military wardrobe. To incorporate the military trend into your wardrobe today, try pairing a structured jacket with skinny jeans and combat boots.

How to Wear 60's Fashion Today

If you want to incorporate 60's fashion into your wardrobe today, there are a few key things to keep in mind. First, don't be afraid to mix and match different trends. For example, you could pair a mod-inspired mini skirt with a hippie-style peasant blouse for a unique look. Second, pay attention to the details. Accessories like headbands, statement jewelry, and bold sunglasses can really elevate a 60's-inspired outfit. Finally, have fun with it! The 60's were all about experimentation and self-expression, so don't be afraid to get creative with your fashion choices.

Step-by-Step Guide to Creating a 60's Look

Ready to create your own 60's-inspired outfit? Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Choose your trend

Decide which 60's trend you want to incorporate into your outfit. Do you want to go mod, hippie, or military?

Step 2: Choose your key pieces

Select the key pieces that will define your outfit. For a mod look, this might be a shift dress and knee-high boots. For a hippie look, this might be a flowy maxi dress and a denim jacket. For a military look, this might be a structured jacket and combat boots.

Step 3: Add accessories

Choose accessories that will complement your outfit. This might include a statement necklace, a headband, or a pair of bold sunglasses.

Step 4: Finish with hair and makeup

To complete your 60's-inspired look, don't forget about your hair and makeup. For a mod look, try a sleek bob and bold eyeliner. For a hippie look, try loose waves and natural makeup. For a military look, try a slicked-back ponytail and neutral makeup.

Tips for Wearing 60's Fashion

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when wearing 60's fashion:

Tip 1: Keep it modern

While you want to embrace 60's fashion, you don't want to look like you stepped out of a time machine. Keep your outfit modern by incorporating contemporary pieces and accessories.

Tip 2: Play with color and pattern

60's fashion was all about bold colors and patterns. Don't be afraid to experiment with these in your outfit.

Tip 3: Embrace layering

Layering was a key element of 60's fashion. Try layering a blouse under a shift dress or a denim jacket over a maxi dress for a unique look.

FAQ About 60's Fashion

Q: What hairstyles were popular in the 60's?

A: Popular hairstyles in the 60's included bouffants, beehives, and sleek bobs.

Q: What kind of shoes were popular in the 60's?

A: In addition to go-go boots and combat boots, platform shoes were popular in the 60's.

Q: What fabrics were popular in 60's fashion?

A: Natural fabrics like cotton, hemp, and wool were popular in 60's fashion.

Hair Care for 60's-Inspired Hairstyles

If you're creating a 60's-inspired look, you'll want to make sure your hair looks the part. Here are a few tips for hair care:

Tip 1: Use volumizing products

To create a bouffant or beehive, you'll need plenty of volume. Use volumizing shampoo and conditioner, and consider using a volumizing mousse or spray before styling.

Tip 2: Protect your hair from heat

If you're using hot tools to style your hair, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

Tip 3: Try a dry shampoo

If you're going for a sleek bob or other straight style, try using a dry shampoo to keep your hair looking fresh and clean between washes.

Now that you know what kind of clothes were worn in the 60's, it's time to start incorporating these trends into your wardrobe. Whether you're going for a mod, hippie, or military look, remember to have fun with your fashion choices and experiment with different styles and accessories.
