How Many Inches Do Dreads Grow A Month

Written by Petter Jun 25, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're considering getting dreadlocks, or you already have them, one of the most common questions you might have is, "how many inches do dreads grow a month?" This is a valid concern, as dreadlocks require a lot of patience and commitment. In this article, we'll dive into the details of how fast dreadlocks grow, how to care for them, and tips to help you grow longer, healthier dreads.

Table of Contents

  • How Fast Do Dreadlocks Grow?
  • How to Care for Dreadlocks
  • Tips for Growing Long Dreadlocks

How Fast Do Dreadlocks Grow?

On average, dreadlocks grow about half an inch per month. However, the rate of growth can vary depending on several factors, such as genetics, diet, and how well you take care of your hair. While half an inch may not seem like a lot, it can add up over time, especially if you're consistent with your hair care routine.

It's important to note that dreadlocks grow differently than regular hair. The locking process can cause the hair strands to compact, making the overall length appear shorter. This means that while your hair may be growing, it may not look like it's getting longer because it's being compressed into the dreadlock.

How to Care for Dreadlocks

Caring for dreadlocks requires a different approach than caring for regular hair. Here are a few tips to help you maintain healthy, strong dreads:

1. Keep your scalp clean

Dreadlocks require a clean scalp to thrive. Use a residue-free shampoo to cleanse your scalp, and avoid using heavy oils or products that can clog your pores.

2. Moisturize your hair

Dreadlocks can become dry and brittle over time, so it's important to keep them moisturized. Use a light oil, like coconut or jojoba oil, to keep your hair soft and supple.

3. Avoid over-twisting

While it's important to maintain your dreadlocks, over-twisting can cause damage and breakage. Stick to a maintenance routine that works for you, and avoid twisting your hair too frequently.

Tips for Growing Long Dreadlocks

If you're looking to grow long, healthy dreadlocks, here are a few tips to help you along the way:

1. Be patient

Growing dreadlocks takes time and patience. Don't get discouraged if your hair doesn't seem to be growing as fast as you'd like. Keep up with your hair care routine, and you'll see results over time.

2. Eat a healthy diet

A healthy diet can promote hair growth and overall hair health. Make sure you're getting enough vitamins and minerals, like biotin, zinc, and iron, to support healthy hair growth.

3. Protect your hair

Dreadlocks can be fragile, so it's important to protect them from damage. Avoid using harsh chemicals or tools on your hair, and wear a scarf or hat to protect your hair from the elements.

FAQ

How often should I wash my dreadlocks?

It's recommended to wash your dreadlocks every 1-2 weeks, depending on your hair type and lifestyle. Use a residue-free shampoo to avoid buildup.

Can I comb out my dreadlocks?

Yes, you can comb out your dreadlocks, but it can be a long and tedious process. Make sure you have the time and patience to complete the process before you start.

Hair Care

Overall, growing and maintaining dreadlocks requires a lot of patience and care. By following a consistent hair care routine and being gentle with your hair, you can grow long, healthy dreadlocks that you'll love for years to come.
