How To Know If You Suit Dark Brown Hair

Written by Robby Jul 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you considering dyeing your hair dark brown, but not sure if it's the right shade for you? Dark brown hair can be a bold and dramatic choice, but it's important to make sure it complements your skin tone and features. In this blog post, we'll provide tips and advice on how to know if you suit dark brown hair.

Table of Contents

  • Determining Your Skin Tone
  • Considering Your Eye Color
  • Your Natural Hair Color
  • Matching Your Face Shape
  • Hair Care Tips for Dark Brown Hair
  • FAQs

Determining Your Skin Tone

One of the most important factors in determining if dark brown hair suits you is your skin tone. There are three main categories of skin tone: cool, warm, and neutral. If you have cool undertones, you may look best with a cooler shade of dark brown, such as an ash or espresso brown. If you have warm undertones, a richer, warmer shade like chestnut or chocolate brown may be more flattering. Those with neutral undertones can generally pull off any shade of dark brown.

You can determine your skin tone by looking at the veins on the inside of your wrist. If they appear blue or purple, you likely have cool undertones. If they appear green, you have warm undertones. If you can't tell, you may have neutral undertones.

Considering Your Eye Color

Your eye color can also play a role in determining if dark brown hair suits you. Those with warm-toned eyes, such as hazel or brown, may look best with warmer shades of dark brown. Those with cool-toned eyes, such as blue or green, may look better with cooler shades of dark brown.

Your Natural Hair Color

If you're dyeing your hair from a lighter color, it's important to consider how drastic the change will be. If you have naturally blonde hair, for example, going to a very dark brown may be too extreme of a change. It's often easier to go gradually darker over time, so you can get used to the new shade.

Matching Your Face Shape

Your face shape can also play a role in determining if dark brown hair suits you. Those with oval or heart-shaped faces can generally pull off any hair color, including dark brown. Those with square or round faces may want to opt for a longer, layered style to balance out the sharp angles of their face.

Hair Care Tips for Dark Brown Hair

Once you've determined if dark brown hair suits you, it's important to take proper care of your locks. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent color fading
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can also cause color to fade
  • Use a color-safe hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage and breakage
  • Consider getting regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair

FAQs

What if I have cool undertones but want a warm shade of dark brown?

While it's important to consider your skin tone when choosing a hair color, ultimately you should choose a shade that makes you feel confident and beautiful. If you have cool undertones but love the look of warm brown hair, go for it!

Can I dye my hair dark brown at home?

If you have experience dyeing your hair at home, you can certainly try to dye your hair dark brown yourself. However, if you're new to hair dyeing or want a more dramatic change, it may be best to visit a professional stylist.

How often should I touch up my dark brown hair?

This can vary depending on how quickly your hair grows and how often you wash it. Generally, touch-ups every 4 to 6 weeks are recommended to maintain the color and prevent roots from showing.

In conclusion, dark brown hair can be a beautiful and striking hair color, but it's important to make sure it complements your skin tone, eye color, and face shape. By following these tips and taking proper hair care precautions, you can rock your dark brown locks with confidence!
