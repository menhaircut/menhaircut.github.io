What Determines Hair Type Understanding The Basics Of Hair Type

Written by Luffy Jul 22, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you one of those people who are puzzled about your hair type? Do you feel like you have tried every product and technique out there, but still can't seem to get your hair to behave the way you want it to? Well, don't worry, you are not alone! Understanding your hair type is the first step to achieving healthy, beautiful hair. In this article, we will be discussing what determines hair type and how to determine your own hair type.

What Determines Hair Type?

There are several factors that determine hair type, including genetics, ethnicity, age, and environmental factors. Here are some of the key factors that determine hair type:

Genetics

Your hair type is largely determined by your genetics. This means that if your parents have straight hair, you are more likely to have straight hair as well. The same goes for curly or wavy hair. However, it is important to note that genetics are not the only factor that determines hair type.

Ethnicity

Your ethnicity can also play a role in your hair type. For example, people of African descent tend to have coarser, curlier hair, while people of Asian descent tend to have straighter, thicker hair. People of European descent tend to have a wider range of hair types, from straight to curly.

Age

As we age, our hair can become thinner and more brittle, which can affect its texture and type. Hormonal changes can also affect the texture and type of our hair, particularly during puberty and menopause.

Environmental Factors

The environment can also play a role in determining hair type. For example, exposure to sun, wind, and pollution can damage the hair, causing it to become dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. Chemical treatments, such as hair coloring or perming, can also change the texture and type of the hair.

How to Determine Your Hair Type

Now that you know what determines hair type, it's time to determine your own hair type. Here are some steps to help you determine your hair type:

Step 1: Look at Your Hair

Take a close look at your hair. Is it straight, wavy, curly, or coily? Straight hair is generally smooth and lacks any curl or wave. Wavy hair has a slight curl or wave, while curly hair has a more defined curl pattern. Coily hair is tightly curled or kinky.

Step 2: Check Your Hair Density

Density refers to the amount of hair on your head. To check your hair density, take a small section of your hair and hold it up to the light. If you can see your scalp through the hair, you have low density. If you can see some scalp, you have medium density. If you can't see any scalp, you have high density.

Step 3: Determine Your Hair Texture

Texture refers to the thickness of each individual strand of hair. Fine hair is thin and delicate, while coarse hair is thick and strong. To determine your hair texture, take a single strand of hair and run it between your fingers. If you can barely feel the hair, you have fine hair. If you can feel the hair, you have medium hair. If the hair feels thick and strong, you have coarse hair.

Tips for Caring for Your Hair Type

Once you have determined your hair type, it's important to care for it properly. Here are some tips for caring for each hair type:

Straight Hair

Straight hair tends to be oily at the roots and dry at the ends. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized. Avoid heavy styling products, as they can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair tends to be frizzy and prone to tangles. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and avoid using a brush, as it can break your hair.

Curly Hair

Curly hair tends to be dry and prone to frizz. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated. Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to help define your curls, and avoid using a brush, as it can break your hair.

Coily Hair

Coily hair tends to be dry and prone to breakage. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and avoid using a brush, as it can break your hair. Consider using protective styles, such as braids or twists, to help protect your hair from damage.

FAQ About Hair Type

What if I have more than one hair type?

It is common for people to have more than one hair type. For example, you may have straight hair at the roots and curly hair at the ends. In this case, it is important to care for each section of your hair differently.

Can hair type change over time?

Yes, hair type can change over time due to hormonal changes, environmental factors, and aging. It is important to re-evaluate your hair type periodically to ensure that you are caring for it properly.

Can I change my hair type?

While you can't change your hair type, you can change the way your hair looks and behaves with the right products and styling techniques. Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair.

Conclusion

Understanding your hair type is the first step to achieving healthy, beautiful hair. By understanding what determines hair type and how to determine your own hair type, you can choose the right products and techniques to care for your hair properly. Remember to care for your hair according to its texture, density, and type, and don't be afraid to experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for you.
