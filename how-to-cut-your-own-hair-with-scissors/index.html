How To Cut Your Own Hair With Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 27, 2023 · 4 min read
If you're looking to save some money on haircuts or can't make it to the salon for one reason or another, cutting your own hair with scissors can be a great alternative. While it may seem daunting at first, with a little bit of practice and some helpful tips, you can achieve a fresh new look right from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of how to cut your own hair with scissors.

How to Cut Your Own Hair with Scissors

Cutting your own hair with scissors may seem intimidating, but it can be done with just a few simple steps. First, gather all the necessary tools: a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and clips to section your hair.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Wet hair is harder to cut and can cause unevenness.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Separate your hair into sections using clips. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward. The sections should be about 1-2 inches thick.

Step 3: Cut Your Hair

Take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the ends. Cut slowly and be conservative with the amount you cut off. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back on!

Step 4: Repeat

Continue this process, sectioning your hair and cutting small sections at a time. Remember to cut conservatively and take your time.

Step 5: Clean Up

Once you've finished cutting, comb through your hair and make any necessary adjustments. Clean up any stray hairs and check for any unevenness.

Step-by-Step Guide

To help you through the process, here's a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own hair with scissors.
  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Section your hair using clips.
  3. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight.
  4. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the ends.
  5. Repeat with each section of hair.
  6. Comb through your hair and make any necessary adjustments.
  7. Clean up any stray hairs and check for unevenness.

Helpful Tips

Cutting your own hair can be a challenge, but with these helpful tips, you'll be able to achieve a professional-looking cut in no time.
  • Invest in a good pair of scissors. Sharp scissors are essential for a clean cut.
  • Start small. Cut conservatively and take your time.
  • Use a mirror. Make sure you can see what you're doing from all angles.
  • Ask for help. If you're unsure about cutting a certain section, ask a friend or family member to help.

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair with regular scissors?

While it's possible to cut your own hair with regular scissors, it's not recommended. Regular scissors are often dull and can cause split ends or unevenness.

How often should I cut my own hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! Mistakes can be fixed. If you cut too much off, try blending the hair with scissors or a razor. If you're really unhappy with the result, you can always visit a professional stylist to fix the cut.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain a healthy and polished look. Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair nourished and moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently and opt for air drying instead. Trimming your hair regularly can also help maintain a healthy look and prevent split ends. Cutting your own hair with scissors may seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done from the comfort of your own home. Remember to start small, take your time, and ask for help if needed. With a little bit of practice, you'll be able to achieve a professional-looking cut in no time.
