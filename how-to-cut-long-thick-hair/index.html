How To Cut Long Thick Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Oct 15, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Cutting long thick hair can be intimidating, especially if you've never done it before. But with the right tools and techniques, you can give yourself or someone else a great haircut that looks professional and stylish. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of cutting long thick hair, from preparation to finishing touches. Let's get started!

Preparation

Before you start cutting, it's important to prepare the hair. This means washing and conditioning it thoroughly, and letting it air-dry or blow-dry it until it's completely dry. Wet hair can be deceptive, and it's harder to see the true length and shape of the hair when it's wet. Make sure you also brush or comb the hair to remove any tangles or knots.

Tools You'll Need

To cut long thick hair, you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Spray bottle with water (optional)

It's important to use sharp scissors, as dull ones can damage the hair and make it more difficult to cut. Clippers can be useful for trimming the hair around the ears or the nape of the neck, but they're not essential. A comb will help you section the hair and make sure your cuts are even. Hair ties or clips will keep the hair out of the way while you work. A spray bottle with water can help you dampen the hair if it's too dry.

Sectioning the Hair

Sectioning the hair is an important step that will make your job easier and ensure that your cuts are precise. To section the hair:

  1. Start by dividing the hair down the middle, from the forehead to the nape of the neck. Use a comb to create a straight line.
  2. Divide each half into two sections, from the top of the head to the ears.
  3. Clip each section out of the way, leaving the bottom section loose.
  4. Divide the bottom section into two or three smaller sections, depending on the thickness of the hair. Clip each section out of the way except for the one you're working on.

As you cut each section, unclip the next one and continue until you've cut all the sections.

Cutting the Hair

When cutting long thick hair, it's important to take your time and work slowly. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start by cutting the length. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your other hand to hold the scissors at an angle, so the hair is longer in the front than in the back. Cut the hair in a straight line, using the first cut as a guide for the rest of the hair.
  2. Continue cutting each section to the desired length, making sure to keep the hair even on both sides.
  3. To add layers, take a small section of hair from the bottom layer and hold it between your fingers. Use your other hand to cut into the hair at an angle, creating a V-shape. Continue cutting small sections of hair in this way, gradually working your way up to the top layer of the hair.

Remember to cut less than you think you need to, as you can always go back and cut more later. It's better to err on the side of caution and take your time.

Blending the Layers

Once you've cut the layers, it's important to blend them so they look natural and seamless. Here's how to do it:

  1. Take a small section of hair from the top layer and hold it between your fingers.
  2. Use the scissors to cut into the hair at an angle, so the hair is shorter in the front than in the back.
  3. Continue cutting small sections of hair in this way, working your way down to the bottom layer.
  4. Make sure to check the layers from all angles, including the back and sides, to make sure they're blended properly.

Trimming Bangs

If you want to add bangs to your long thick hair, or trim existing ones, here's how to do it:

  1. Section the hair in the front, from the top of the head to the eyebrows.
  2. Comb the hair straight down and hold it between your fingers.
  3. Use the scissors to cut the hair straight across, starting at the middle and working your way out to the sides.
  4. Make sure to cut less than you think you need to, as bangs tend to shrink up when they dry.

Finishing Touches

Once you've finished cutting the hair, it's time to add some finishing touches. Here are a few ideas:

  • Add some texture by using thinning shears or razor scissors to create choppy layers.
  • Create face-framing layers by cutting shorter pieces around the face.
  • Add some volume by using a round brush to blow-dry the hair.

Remember to take your time and make sure you're happy with the results before you finish.

Tips for Cutting Long Thick Hair

Here are a few tips to help you get the best results when cutting long thick hair:

  • Work in a well-lit area with a large mirror so you can see what you're doing.
  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to take small sections and work your way up.
  • Keep the hair damp with a spray bottle to make it easier to cut.
  • Use your fingers to guide the scissors, rather than relying on the comb.
  • Take breaks if you need to, to avoid getting tired or frustrated.

FAQs

Can I cut long thick hair myself?

Yes, you can cut long thick hair yourself, but it may be easier to have someone else help you. Make sure you have the right tools and take your time to avoid mistakes.

How often should I cut long thick hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it starts to look shaggy or uneven. Generally, it's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks.

What's the best way to care for long thick hair after cutting?

After cutting long thick hair, it's important to keep it healthy and moisturized. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat or styling products. Get regular trims to keep the ends from splitting, and avoid brushing or combing the hair too much, as this can cause breakage.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your long thick hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair, as this can damage the hair and cause split ends.
  • Get regular trims to keep the ends from splitting and to maintain the shape of your haircut.
  • Avoid brushing or combing your hair too much, as this can cause breakage and damage the hair.

By following these tips, you can keep your long thick hair looking beautiful and healthy, even after you've cut it.
