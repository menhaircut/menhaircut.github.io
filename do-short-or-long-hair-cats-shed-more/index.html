Do Short Or Long Hair Cats Shed More

Written by Petter Sep 07, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're a cat owner, then you know shedding is inevitable. However, if you're looking to adopt a cat and are trying to decide if you want a short or long-haired breed, you might be wondering which one sheds more. In this article, we'll be discussing whether short or long hair cats shed more and how to care for their hair.

Why Do Cats Shed?

Cats shed because it's a natural process that allows them to get rid of old or damaged hair and make way for new hair growth. Shedding also helps regulate a cat's body temperature, keeping them cool in the summer and warm in the winter. Additionally, cats shed more during certain times of the year, such as in the spring and fall, due to changes in daylight and temperature.

Do Short Hair Cats Shed More?

Short hair cats do shed, but they tend to shed less than long-haired breeds. Short hair cats have hair that's less than 2 inches long, which means they have less hair to shed. However, this doesn't mean that short hair cats don't require grooming. They still need to be brushed regularly to remove loose hair and prevent matting.

Do Long Hair Cats Shed More?

Long hair cats shed more than short hair breeds. This is because they have more hair, which means more hair to shed. Long hair cats also tend to shed continuously throughout the year, rather than just during certain seasons. This means that long-haired breeds require more frequent grooming to prevent matting and hairballs.

How to Care for a Short Hair Cat

To care for a short hair cat, you'll need to brush them regularly to remove loose hair and prevent matting. Use a soft-bristled brush and brush in the direction of hair growth. You can also use a grooming mitt or a rubber curry comb to remove loose hair. Short hair cats should be brushed at least once a week, but more frequent grooming may be necessary if shedding is excessive.

How to Care for a Long Hair Cat

To care for a long hair cat, you'll need to groom them regularly to prevent matting and hairballs. Use a comb or brush specifically designed for long hair breeds and brush in the direction of hair growth. Long hair cats should be brushed at least once a day, but more frequent grooming may be necessary if shedding is excessive. You may also need to trim their hair to prevent matting and make grooming easier.

Tips to Minimize Shedding

Here are some tips to minimize shedding:

  • Brush your cat regularly
  • Use a high-quality cat food that contains essential fatty acids
  • Keep your cat hydrated
  • Use a humidifier to keep the air moist
  • Wash your cat's bedding regularly

FAQs

Q: How often should I groom my cat?

A: The frequency of grooming depends on your cat's breed and hair length. Short hair cats should be groomed at least once a week, while long hair breeds require daily grooming.

Q: Can I use human shampoo on my cat?

A: No, human shampoo is not safe for cats. Use a cat-specific shampoo that's gentle on their skin and won't dry out their coat.

Conclusion

In conclusion, both short and long hair cats shed, but long hair breeds tend to shed more due to their thicker coat. Regardless of your cat's hair length, regular grooming is necessary to prevent matting and hairballs. By following the tips outlined in this article, you can keep your cat's coat healthy and minimize shedding.
