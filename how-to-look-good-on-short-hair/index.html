How To Look Good On Short Hair In 2023 Tips Tricks And Hair Care

Are you tired of your long locks and ready to chop them off into a chic pixie or bob cut? Short hair is not only trendy but also practical and easy to maintain. However, it can be a bit challenging to style and accessorize short hair to look good and feel confident. In this blog post, we'll share some tips, tricks, and hair care advice on how to look good on short hair in 2023.

How to Choose the Right Short Haircut

Before you chop off your hair, you need to decide on the right short haircut that suits your face shape, hair texture, and personal style. Here are some tips on how to choose the right short haircut:

Consider Your Face Shape

The first step is to determine your face shape, whether it's oval, round, square, heart-shaped, or diamond-shaped. Your face shape will influence the best short haircut that flatters your features and balances your proportions. For example:

  • Oval faces can pull off almost any short haircut, from a textured pixie to a sleek bob.
  • Round faces look best with short haircuts that add height and volume on top, such as a layered bob or a spiky pixie.
  • Square faces can soften their angles with a short haircut that has soft layers and side-swept bangs.
  • Heart-shaped faces can balance their wide forehead and narrow chin with a short haircut that has volume on the sides, such as a layered bob or a tousled pixie.
  • Diamond-shaped faces can accentuate their cheekbones with a short haircut that has layers around the face, such as a choppy pixie or a textured bob.

Consider Your Hair Texture

The second factor to consider is your hair texture, whether it's straight, wavy, curly, or kinky. Your hair texture will affect how your short haircut looks and behaves, so you need to choose a style that works with your natural texture and doesn't require too much styling. For example:

  • Straight hair can look sleek and polished with a blunt bob or a side-parted pixie.
  • Wavy hair can add texture and movement to a short haircut, such as a beachy bob or a shaggy pixie.
  • Curly hair can show off its shape and bounce with a short haircut that has layers and volume, such as a curly bob or a curly pixie.
  • Kinky hair can embrace its natural texture with a short haircut that has defined curls, coils, or twists, such as a tapered cut or a frohawk.

Consider Your Personal Style

The third factor to consider is your personal style, whether it's classic, edgy, romantic, or minimalist. Your personal style will reflect your personality and preferences, so you need to choose a short haircut that makes you feel comfortable and confident. For example:

  • Classic styles include a sleek bob, a French crop, or a side-swept pixie.
  • Edgy styles include a choppy bob, a spiky pixie, or an undercut.
  • Romantic styles include a curly bob, a tousled pixie, or a braided updo.
  • Minimalist styles include a blunt bob, a buzz cut, or a slicked-back pixie.

By considering these three factors, you can narrow down your options and find the right short haircut for you. Don't be afraid to consult with your hairstylist and experiment with different styles until you find the perfect one.

Accessorizing Short Hair

Just because you have short hair doesn't mean you can't accessorize it and add some flair to your look. Here are some ways to accessorize short hair:

Headbands and Scarves

Headbands and scarves are versatile and stylish accessories that can add color, pattern, and texture to your short hair. You can wear a headband or scarf over your forehead, across your crown, or around your neck, depending on the look you want to achieve. You can also experiment with different materials, such as silk, cotton, or velvet, to match your outfit and mood.

Hair Clips and Pins

Hair clips and pins are functional and fashionable accessories that can hold your hair in place and create interesting shapes and designs. You can use hair clips and pins to create a side part, a twisted bang, a braided crown, or a messy bun, depending on your hair length and texture. You can also mix and match different colors and styles of hair clips and pins to express your personality and creativity.

Hats and Beanies

Hats and beanies are practical and trendy accessories that can keep your head warm and add a casual or chic touch to your outfit. You can wear a baseball cap, a fedora, a beret, or a beanie with your short hair, depending on the occasion and your mood. You can also play with different colors and patterns of hats and beanies to complement your hair color and skin tone.

Remember to choose accessories that fit your head size and hair volume and don't overpower your short haircut. You want to enhance your short hair, not hide it.

Styling Short Hair

Styling short hair can be fun and easy if you have the right tools and techniques. Here are some tips on how to style short hair:

Use Heat Protectant

Before you use any heat styling tools, such as a blow dryer, a flat iron, or a curling wand, make sure to apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair. Heat styling can damage your hair cuticles and cause breakage and frizz, so using a heat protectant can minimize the damage and keep your hair healthy and shiny.

Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow drying your short hair can add volume and texture and help your hair hold its shape throughout the day. To blow dry your hair, use a round brush to lift your hair at the roots and direct the airflow from your blow dryer downwards. You can also use a diffuser attachment to enhance your natural curls or waves and reduce frizz.

Curl or Straighten Your Hair

If you want to add some curls or straighten your short hair, use a curling wand or a flat iron that is suitable for your hair length and texture. For curls, wrap small sections of hair around the wand and hold for a few seconds before releasing. For straightening, glide the flat iron from the roots to the ends of your hair, and use a heat protectant if necessary. You can also use a styling cream or wax to define your curls or create a sleek finish.

Tease or Texture Your Hair

If you want to create some volume and texture in your short hair, use a teasing comb or a texturizing spray to lift your roots and create a messy or tousled look. You can tease your hair at the crown or the sides, depending on the style you want to achieve. You can also use a dry shampoo or a sea salt spray to add some grit and hold to your hair.

Accessorize Your Hair

As we mentioned earlier, accessorizing your short hair can add some interest and style to your look. You can use hair clips, headbands, scarves, or hats to complement your hairstyle and outfit. You can also experiment with different hair colors, highlights, or ombre effects to customize your short hair.

Hair Care for Short Hair

Short hair may require less maintenance than long hair, but it still needs proper care and attention to stay healthy and shiny. Here are some hair care tips for short hair:

Wash Your Hair Regularly

Short hair tends to get greasy and limp faster than long hair, so you need to wash it more frequently, depending on your hair type and lifestyle. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair texture and scalp condition, and rinse your hair thoroughly with lukewarm water.
