How To Trim The Sides Of Your Hair With Clippers

Written by Petter Jun 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Trimming your hair at home can be a daunting task, especially when it comes to the sides. But with the right tools and techniques, it's possible to achieve a clean and professional-looking cut. In this article, we'll show you how to trim the sides of your hair with clippers step by step. We'll also provide tips, answer frequently asked questions, and give you some hair care advice to keep your hair looking its best.

Tools You'll Need

To trim the sides of your hair with clippers, you'll need:

  • Clippers
  • Clipper guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Mirror
  • Towel

Make sure your clippers are sharp and the guards are clean before you start. You don't want to risk pulling or tugging your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to trim the sides of your hair with clippers:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. It's best to wash your hair the day before to remove any product buildup.
  2. Attach the appropriate clipper guard to your clippers. If you're not sure which guard to use, start with a longer one and work your way down.
  3. Starting at the bottom, use the clippers to trim upward against the hair growth. Use short, even strokes and keep the clippers level with the head. Work your way up to the temple area.
  4. Switch to a shorter guard and repeat the process, trimming upward from the bottom to the temple area. Be careful not to trim too much off at once.
  5. Blend the sides and top by using a longer guard and trimming the hair around the temple area. This will create a gradient effect and make the transition from the sides to the top smoother.
  6. Use scissors to trim any longer hairs that the clippers missed. Comb the hair straight up and trim the ends with scissors. Be careful not to cut too much off.
  7. Repeat on the other side, making sure to match the length and angle of the first side.
  8. Check the sides in the mirror to make sure they're even. Make any necessary adjustments.
  9. Clean your clippers and guards, and store them in a safe place.

Tips for a Better Cut

Here are some tips to help you achieve a better cut:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down. You can always go shorter, but you can't go back once you've cut too much off.
  • Use a towel around your shoulders to catch any hair clippings.
  • Don't rush. Take your time and make sure you're cutting evenly.
  • If you're not confident in your cutting skills, start with a small section and see how it looks before continuing.
  • Ask a friend or family member for help if you need it.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about trimming the sides of your hair with clippers:

How often should I trim the sides of my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how short you want it. As a general rule, you should trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the same length.

Can I use regular scissors instead of clippers?

You can, but clippers are designed specifically for cutting hair and will give you a more even cut. Scissors are better for trimming longer hairs and creating texture.

How do I clean my clippers?

Remove the guards and use a small brush to remove any hair clippings from the blades. You can also use a damp cloth to wipe down the blades. Make sure your clippers are completely dry before storing them.

Hair Care Tips

Now that you know how to trim the sides of your hair with clippers, here are some tips to keep your hair looking its best:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo to remove any buildup.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it. If you need to use a hair dryer, use a low heat setting.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote hair growth and overall health.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently trim the sides of your hair with clippers at home. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow the steps we've provided. Happy cutting!
