How To Fade Your Own Hair Female A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Fading your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're not familiar with the process. But with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and on-trend fade at home. In this article, we'll show you how to fade your own hair female in a relaxed English language. From understanding the different types of fades to tips on aftercare, we've got you covered.

Understanding the Different Types of Fades

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to understand the different types of fades. The most common types are:

  • Low fade: This type of fade starts at the bottom of your hairline and gradually fades upwards.
  • Mid fade: This type of fade starts at the midpoint of your hairline and gradually fades upwards.
  • High fade: This type of fade starts at the top of your hairline and gradually fades upwards.

Once you've decided on the type of fade you want, you'll need to choose the appropriate guard size for your clippers. The lower the guard size, the shorter the hair will be.

Tools You'll Need

To fade your own hair, you'll need the following tools:

  • Clippers
  • Clipper guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Handheld mirror

Prepping Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to prep it. Start by washing and towel-drying your hair. Comb it out to remove any tangles, and use the handheld mirror to get a good look at the back of your head.

Cutting Your Hair

Start by using the clippers to cut the bottom of your hairline. Use the appropriate guard size for your chosen fade, and work your way upwards. Be sure to go slowly and use a steady hand.

Once you've finished the first pass, switch to a smaller guard size and repeat the process. This time, start a little higher up on your hairline and work your way upwards again.

Blending Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting your hair with the clippers, it's time to blend it. Use the scissors to trim any longer hairs that are sticking out, and use the comb to blend the hair between the different guard sizes.

Finishing Touches

After you've blended your hair, use the clippers to trim the hair around your ears and the back of your neck. Use the handheld mirror to check for any missed spots, and make any final adjustments as needed.

Tips for Aftercare

Once you've finished fading your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a good shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy, and avoid using heat tools too often. You can also use a styling product like pomade or wax to keep your hair looking sleek and styled.

FAQs

Can I fade my own hair if I don't have clippers?

No, you'll need clippers to achieve a fade. You can purchase a pair of clippers online or at your local beauty supply store.

How long does a fade typically last?

A fade typically lasts around two to three weeks, depending on how fast your hair grows.

Can I achieve a fade with curly hair?

Yes, you can achieve a fade with curly hair. However, it may be more difficult to blend the hair between the different guard sizes, so you may want to see a professional stylist for help.

Conclusion

Fading your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and on-trend fade at home. Just remember to take your time, use a steady hand, and follow our tips for aftercare. Happy cutting!
