Is It Haram to Colour Your Hair in Islam? - FAQ and Hair Care Tips

As a Muslim, you may have wondered whether it is haram to colour your hair in Islam. This is a common question among Muslims who are interested in hair care and beauty. In this article, we will explore this topic and provide you with some tips on how to care for your coloured hair.

Table of Content

  • FAQ
  • How To Colour Your Hair in Islam
  • Step By Step Guide
  • Tips for Coloured Hair Care

FAQ: Is It Haram to Colour Your Hair in Islam?

The answer to this question is not straightforward. According to some Islamic scholars, it is haram to change the natural colour of your hair, as it is considered altering the creation of Allah. However, other scholars believe that it is permissible to colour your hair as long as it is done in moderation and does not cause harm to your health.

Ultimately, the decision to colour your hair is up to your personal interpretation of Islamic teachings, and it is always best to consult with a knowledgeable scholar for guidance.

How To Colour Your Hair in Islam

If you decide to colour your hair, it is important to choose a halal hair dye that is made from natural ingredients and does not contain any harmful chemicals. You can find many halal hair dye brands online or at your local Islamic store.

When applying the hair dye, make sure to follow the instructions carefully and avoid getting the dye on your skin or in your eyes. It is also recommended to do a patch test before applying the dye to your entire head to check for any allergic reactions.

Step By Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to colour your hair in Islam:

  1. Choose a halal hair dye that is made from natural ingredients.
  2. Read the instructions carefully and do a patch test.
  3. Apply petroleum jelly around your hairline to prevent the dye from staining your skin.
  4. Mix the hair dye according to the instructions.
  5. Apply the dye to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down.
  6. Leave the dye on for the recommended amount of time.
  7. Rinse your hair thoroughly with water until the water runs clear.
  8. Apply conditioner to your hair and leave it on for a few minutes.
  9. Rinse your hair again and style as desired.

Tips for Coloured Hair Care

Here are some tips for caring for your coloured hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent fading.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as hair straighteners and curling irons, as they can damage your hair and cause the colour to fade.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Stay hydrated and eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth and strength.

In conclusion, whether or not it is haram to colour your hair in Islam is a matter of personal interpretation. However, if you do decide to colour your hair, make sure to use a halal hair dye and take proper care of your coloured hair to keep it healthy and vibrant.
