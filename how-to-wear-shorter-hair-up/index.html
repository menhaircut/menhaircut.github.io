How To Wear Shorter Hair Up

Written by Frank Aug 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Short hair can be a fun and easy way to switch up your look, but sometimes it can be tricky to figure out how to style it. Luckily, there are plenty of ways to wear shorter hair up that are both stylish and practical. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks for wearing shorter hair up, as well as step-by-step instructions for several different hairstyles.

How to Wear Shorter Hair Up

First things first, it's important to have a few key tools on hand when you're styling shorter hair. A good comb or brush, some bobby pins, and a hair tie are essential. From there, you can get creative with different products like texturizing spray, hair wax, or pomade, depending on the look you're going for.

When it comes to actually putting your hair up, there are a few different techniques to keep in mind. One is to use your fingers to twist small sections of hair and then secure them with bobby pins. This can create a messy, textured look that's great for casual occasions.

Another option is to use a hair tie to create a ponytail, either high on the crown of your head or lower at the nape of your neck. You can leave the ends of your hair loose for a more relaxed look, or tuck them under and secure them with bobby pins for a sleeker style.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here are a few specific hairstyles you can try with your shorter hair:

1. Messy Half-Up

  1. Start with dry, textured hair. If your hair is naturally straight, you can use a texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add some grit.
  2. Use your fingers to gather the top half of your hair, from your temples to the crown of your head.
  3. Twist the hair loosely and secure it with bobby pins at the back of your head.
  4. Let the rest of your hair hang loose, or add some extra texture by curling the ends with a curling iron or wand.

2. Low Bun

  1. Tie your hair into a low ponytail at the nape of your neck.
  2. Twist the ponytail into a loose bun and secure it with bobby pins.
  3. Use your fingers to gently pull on the bun and create a slightly messy, undone look.
  4. Finish with hairspray to hold everything in place.

3. Braided Crown

  1. Create two small braids on either side of your head, starting at the front hairline and moving back towards your ears.
  2. Wrap each braid around the back of your head and secure them with bobby pins, crossing them over each other to create a "crown" effect.
  3. Let the rest of your hair hang loose, or tuck it under the braids and secure with bobby pins.

Tips for Wearing Shorter Hair Up

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when styling shorter hair:

  • Experiment with different hair accessories, like headbands or hair clips, to add some extra flair to your look.
  • If you have layers in your hair, try pinning them back individually to create a more interesting shape.
  • Don't be afraid to play around with different textures and finishes, like matte or glossy products, to create a unique look.

FAQ

How can I make my short hair look fuller?

Using a texturizing spray or dry shampoo can help add volume and texture to shorter hair. You can also try curling or waving your hair to create more body.

What are some good hairstyles for short hair?

In addition to the styles outlined above, some other popular options for shorter hair include pixie cuts, bob haircuts, and choppy layers. These styles can be worn both up and down, depending on the occasion.

Hair Care for Short Hair

Finally, it's important to take good care of your short hair to keep it looking healthy and shiny. This includes regular trims to keep split ends at bay, as well as using a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.

You can also try using a hair mask once a week to give your hair an extra boost of hydration. And if you're using heat styling tools like a curling iron or straightener, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

With a little practice and some experimentation, you can create all sorts of fun and stylish looks with your shorter hair. Whether you're going for a casual, messy vibe or a sleek and polished finish, there are plenty of ways to wear shorter hair up that will suit your personal style.
