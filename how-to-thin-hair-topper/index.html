How To Thin Hair Topper A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're wearing a hair topper, you might be wondering how to make it look more natural. One of the most common problems that topper wearers face is that their hair topper looks too thick, which can be a dead giveaway that you're wearing a hairpiece. Fortunately, there's a simple solution: thinning your hair topper. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps of how to thin hair topper so that it looks more natural and blends seamlessly with your own hair.

What is a Hair Topper?

A hair topper (also called a wiglet or a hairpiece) is a type of hair extension that is designed to cover bald spots or add volume to thinning hair. Unlike a full wig, a hair topper is smaller and can be attached to your own hair using clips, tape, or other methods.

Why Thin Your Hair Topper?

While a hair topper can be a great solution for thinning hair, it can also look unnatural if it's too thick. Thinning your hair topper can help it blend in better with your own hair and look more natural.

How to Thin Hair Topper: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Gather Your Materials

Before you begin, you'll need a few tools:

  • Hair shears (scissors designed for cutting hair)
  • A comb
  • A spray bottle filled with water

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair Topper

Before you start thinning your hair topper, make sure it's clean and dry. Wash your hair topper according to the manufacturer's instructions, and then let it air dry or blow dry it on a low setting.

Step 3: Comb Out Your Hair Topper

Use a comb to gently comb out any tangles or knots in your hair topper.

Step 4: Section Your Hair Topper

Use clips to divide your hair topper into sections. Start at the back and work your way forward, creating sections that are about 1 inch wide.

Step 5: Thin Each Section

Starting at the top of each section, use your hair shears to cut small sections of hair at a time. Hold the shears at a slight angle and snip the hair in a downward motion. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as you can't put it back once it's cut.

Step 6: Comb Out Each Section

After you've thinned each section, comb it out gently to remove any loose hairs.

Step 7: Check Your Progress

Once you've thinned all of the sections, check your progress in a mirror. If your hair topper still looks too thick, repeat the process until you're happy with the results.

Tips for Thinning Your Hair Topper

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up.
  • Use thinning shears for a more natural look.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once.
  • Check your progress in a mirror frequently.
  • Be patient and take your time.

FAQ

How often should I thin my hair topper?

This depends on how quickly your hair topper grows and how often you wear it. As a general rule, you should thin your hair topper every 6-8 weeks.

Can I thin my hair topper myself?

Yes, you can thin your hair topper yourself as long as you have the right tools and a steady hand. If you're not comfortable doing it yourself, you can also take it to a professional stylist.

Will thinning my hair topper damage it?

No, thinning your hair topper won't damage it as long as you're careful and use the right tools.

Hair Care for Your Thinned Hair Topper

To keep your thinned hair topper looking its best, be sure to take good care of it. Follow the manufacturer's instructions for washing and styling, and avoid using heat tools (like curling irons or straighteners) on synthetic hair toppers.

Now that you know how to thin hair topper, you can enjoy a more natural and seamless look with your hairpiece. With a little patience and practice, you'll be able to thin your hair topper like a pro.
