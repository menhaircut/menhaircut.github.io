How To Cut Your Own Long Hair In The Back

Written by William Sep 02, 2023 · 3 min read

Do you want to save money and time by cutting your own long hair in the back? It's a challenging task, but with the right tools and techniques, you can do it yourself. This article will guide you through the step-by-step process of cutting your own long hair in the back.

Table of Contents

  2. Preparation
  3. Sectioning Your Hair
  4. Cutting Your Hair

Tools You'll Need

Before you start, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Mirror
  • Spray bottle with water

Preparation

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Comb your hair thoroughly to remove any tangles or knots. If you prefer to cut your hair when it's wet, spray it with water until it's damp, but not dripping.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is an important step to ensure that you cut it evenly. Start by parting your hair down the middle and combing it straight down your back. Then, using hair ties or clips, section your hair into two even parts, dividing it at the nape of your neck.

Creating Subsections

Next, create subsections within each section by parting your hair horizontally from ear to ear. Clip the top section of hair to the top of your head, leaving the bottom section loose.

Cutting Your Hair

Start by trimming the ends of the loose bottom section of hair. Hold the scissors vertically and cut straight across, taking small sections of hair at a time. Repeat this process until you've trimmed all the ends of the bottom section. Then, unclip the top section and repeat the process.

Blending Your Hair

Once you've trimmed the ends of both sections, it's time to blend them together. Comb your hair straight down your back and look for any uneven areas. Use the scissors to blend the two sections together by cutting diagonally into the hair. Be careful not to cut too much, as this can create uneven layers.

Trimming Your Ends

After blending your hair, check the ends to make sure they are even. If there are any uneven areas, trim them with the scissors. Hold the scissors vertically and cut straight across, taking small sections of hair at a time.

Finishing Touches

Once you've trimmed and blended your hair, check it in the mirror to make sure it's even. If you notice any uneven areas, go back and trim them. You can also use clippers to create a tapered look around the edges of your hair.

Tips and Tricks

  • Always use sharp scissors to prevent split ends
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your hair as you cut
  • Trim a little bit at a time to avoid cutting too much
  • Don't rush the process – take your time to ensure an even cut

FAQs

Can I cut my own long hair in the back?

Yes, you can cut your own long hair in the back, but it's important to have the right tools and techniques.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and appearance. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair and prevent damage. Avoid using heat styling tools too often and protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions. With the right care, your hair will look and feel great!
