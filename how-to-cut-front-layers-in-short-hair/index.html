How To Cut Front Layers In Short Hair

Written by Christine Aug 07, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have short hair and want to add some texture and movement to your hairstyle, cutting front layers is a great option. Not only does it give your hair some volume, but it also frames your face and adds a touch of style. In this article, we'll go over the steps on how to cut front layers in short hair, as well as some tips and hair care advice to keep your new 'do looking fresh and healthy.

How to Cut Front Layers in Short Hair

Cutting front layers in short hair can be a bit tricky, especially if you're doing it yourself. However, with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home. Here are the basic steps on how to cut front layers in short hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with your favorite shampoo and conditioner. Once you've rinsed out the conditioner, gently towel-dry your hair until it's damp. You don't want your hair to be too wet or too dry when you start cutting, so aim for something in between.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Using a wide-tooth comb, gently comb through your hair to remove any tangles or knots. Make sure your hair is completely detangled before you start cutting.

Step 3: Create a Center Part

Using the tip of a rat-tail comb, create a center part down the middle of your head. This will help you section off your hair and create even layers on both sides.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Starting at the crown of your head, use the rat-tail comb to section off a 1-2 inch section of hair. Pin the rest of your hair out of the way with hair clips.

Step 5: Cut the First Layer

Hold the section of hair straight up with your fingers, and cut off the desired length using a pair of sharp scissors. Make sure to cut in a downward motion, as this will create a more natural-looking layer.

Step 6: Cut the Second Layer

Take another section of hair from the same side of your head, about an inch below the first layer. Use the first layer as a guide, and cut the second layer to the same length. Continue cutting layers in this way until you reach the front of your hairline.

Step 7: Repeat on the Other Side

Once you've finished one side, repeat the same process on the other side of your head. Make sure to keep your sections even and your cutting technique consistent.

Step 8: Blend the Layers

After you've cut all the layers, use the scissors to blend them together. This will create a more natural-looking finish and prevent any harsh lines or edges.

Step 9: Style Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting your front layers, style your hair as desired. You can blow-dry your hair with a round brush to add volume, or use a straightener to create a sleek and chic look.

Tips for Cutting Front Layers in Short Hair

Here are some additional tips and tricks to keep in mind when cutting front layers in short hair:

  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. This will make the process much easier and give you a cleaner cut.
  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up. It's better to cut too little than too much.
  • Take breaks as needed. Cutting hair can be tiring on your arms and hands, so don't be afraid to take a break and come back to it later.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress. Make sure both sides are even and the layers are blended well.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut front layers in short curly hair?

A: Yes, you can cut front layers in short curly hair. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries, so you may want to cut your hair a bit longer than desired to account for this.

Q: Can I cut my own front layers in short hair?

A: Yes, you can cut your own front layers in short hair. However, it may be easier to have someone else do it for you, especially if you're new to cutting hair.

Q: How often should I cut my front layers in short hair?

A: It depends on your hair growth and how much maintenance you want to do. Generally, you should aim to cut your front layers every 6-8 weeks to keep them looking fresh and healthy.

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair with Front Layers

Now that you've cut your front layers, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are some hair care tips for short hair with front layers:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often, as this can damage your hair and make it look dry and brittle.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair serum to protect your hair from environmental stressors and keep it looking shiny and healthy.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy, and to prevent split ends from forming.

By following these tips and techniques, you can easily cut front layers in short hair and achieve a stylish and trendy look. Whether you're going for a bold and edgy style or a more subtle and classic look, front layers can add some much-needed texture and dimension to your hair. So go ahead and give it a try!
