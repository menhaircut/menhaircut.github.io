How Do I Cut My Son s Hair At Home

Written by Frank Sep 29, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're a parent, you know how expensive trips to the hair salon can be. Cutting your son's hair at home can save you time and money. Not to mention, it's a great way to bond with your child. However, the thought of cutting your child's hair at home can be intimidating. In this blog post, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut your son's hair at home.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Son's Hair at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Son's Hair at Home
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips for Your Son

How to Cut Your Son's Hair at Home

Cutting your son's hair at home can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done successfully. Here are some tips to get you started:

Tools Needed

To cut your son's hair at home, you'll need the following tools:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Clip or hair tie

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have your tools, let's get started:

  1. Start by washing and drying your son's hair. This will make it easier to cut.
  2. Put the haircutting cape on your son to keep the hair off his clothes.
  3. Use the clip or hair tie to separate the top of your son's hair from the bottom. This will make it easier to cut.
  4. Start with the sides. Use the clippers with the guard on to trim the sides to the desired length. You can start with a larger guard and work your way down to a smaller guard for a tapered look. Be sure to use the comb to lift the hair and the clippers to trim it evenly.
  5. Move to the back of the head. Use the clippers with the guard on to trim the hair in the back. Again, use the comb to lift the hair and the clippers to trim it evenly.
  6. Now it's time to blend the sides and back. Use the clippers without the guard to blend the hair between the sides and back. Be sure to use the comb to lift the hair and the clippers to trim it evenly.
  7. Next, move to the top of the hair. Use the scissors to trim the top to the desired length. Be sure to use the comb to lift the hair and the scissors to trim it evenly.
  8. Finally, trim the bangs if necessary. Use the scissors to cut the bangs to the desired length. Be sure to use the comb to lift the hair and the scissors to trim it evenly.
  9. After you're finished, use the spray bottle to wet the hair and comb it out. This will help you see any uneven spots you may have missed.
  10. Once you're satisfied with the cut, wash your son's hair again to remove any loose hair.

Tips for Cutting Your Son's Hair at Home

Here are some tips to help you successfully cut your son's hair at home:

  • Start with a larger guard on the clippers and work your way down to a smaller guard for a tapered look.
  • Use the comb to lift the hair and the clippers or scissors to trim it evenly.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your son's hair, start with a small trim and work your way up to a full haircut.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to be a professional to cut my son's hair at home?

No, you don't need to be a professional to cut your son's hair at home. With the right tools and techniques, anyone can successfully cut their son's hair at home.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim the hair again or take your son to a professional to fix it.

How often should I cut my son's hair?

This will depend on how quickly your son's hair grows. On average, most boys need a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care Tips for Your Son

Now that you've successfully cut your son's hair at home, it's important to take care of his hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash his hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle his hair after washing.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as a hair dryer, on his hair.
  • Trim his hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Cutting your son's hair at home can be a great way to save money and bond with your child. With the right tools and techniques, anyone can successfully cut their son's hair at home. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.
