How To Find The Best Glasses For Your Face Shape

Are you looking for the perfect pair of glasses that will enhance your facial features and make you look stylish? Choosing the right glasses for your face shape can be a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. In this article, we will guide you through the process of finding the best glasses for your face shape.

  • How to Determine Your Face Shape
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing Glasses for Your Face Shape

How to Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in finding the best glasses for your face shape is to determine your face shape. To do this, you need to look at the proportions of your face and compare them to the following face shapes:

  • Round: Your face is as wide as it is long, with soft curves and no angles.
  • Oval: Your face is longer than it is wide, with gently rounded curves and no angles.
  • Square: Your face is as wide as it is long, with strong angles and a prominent jawline.
  • Heart: Your face is wider at the forehead and temples, with a narrow chin.
  • Diamond: Your face is widest at the cheekbones, with a narrow forehead and jawline.

If you're having trouble determining your face shape, you can use a mirror to trace the outline of your face onto a piece of paper. Then, compare the shape to the above descriptions.

Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing Glasses for Your Face Shape

Once you've determined your face shape, follow these steps to choose the best glasses:

Round Face Shape

If you have a round face shape, look for glasses that:

  • Have angular frames to add definition to your face.
  • Are wider than your face to make it appear slimmer.
  • Have a rectangular or square shape to balance out the curves of your face.

Oval Face Shape

If you have an oval face shape, you're in luck! Almost any style of glasses will look good on you. However, consider glasses that:

  • Are wider than the broadest part of your face.
  • Follow the natural curve of your eyebrows.
  • Are not too large or too small for your face.

Square Face Shape

If you have a square face shape, look for glasses that:

  • Are round or oval to soften the angles of your face.
  • Have thin frames to avoid overwhelming your face.
  • Have a low bridge to minimize the appearance of your forehead.

Heart Face Shape

If you have a heart face shape, look for glasses that:

  • Are wider at the bottom than the top to balance out the proportions of your face.
  • Have thin frames to avoid overwhelming your face.
  • Have a low-set temple to draw attention away from your forehead.

Diamond Face Shape

If you have a diamond face shape, look for glasses that:

  • Have a cat-eye shape to balance out the proportions of your face.
  • Have a curved brow line to soften the angles of your face.
  • Have a low-set temple to draw attention away from your forehead.

Tips for Choosing the Best Glasses for Your Face Shape

In addition to the above recommendations, keep these tips in mind when choosing glasses:

  • Consider your skin tone and hair color when choosing frames.
  • Try on multiple pairs of glasses to see which ones look best on you.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for an opinion from a friend or family member.
  • Consider the function of the glasses (e.g. reading glasses, sunglasses) when choosing the style.

FAQs

What if I have a combination of face shapes?

It's common to have a combination of face shapes. In this case, choose glasses that complement the dominant face shape.

Can I wear glasses that are not recommended for my face shape?

Of course! These recommendations are just guidelines. Ultimately, you should choose glasses that make you feel confident and comfortable.

What if I have a unique face shape?

If you have a unique face shape, consider consulting with an optician who can recommend glasses that will suit your face.

How to Care for Your Glasses and Hair

Now that you've found the perfect glasses, it's important to take care of them. Here are some tips:

  • Store your glasses in a case when you're not wearing them to avoid scratches.
  • Clean your glasses regularly with a microfiber cloth and lens cleaner.
  • When putting on or taking off your glasses, hold them by the temples to avoid bending the frames.
  • When styling your hair, be careful not to get product on your glasses.

With these tips, you're sure to find the best glasses for your face shape and look great wearing them!
