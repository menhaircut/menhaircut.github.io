How To Cut Fringe Bangs Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Nov 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your current hairstyle and looking to switch things up? One way to change your look without a drastic cut is by adding fringe bangs. Fringe bangs can give you a fresh and youthful appearance, and the best part is that you can cut them yourself at home. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting fringe bangs step by step.

How to Cut Fringe Bangs Yourself

Before we begin, it's important to gather all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It's also helpful to have a spray bottle filled with water to keep your hair damp and easier to work with.

Step 1: Decide on the Length of Your Bangs

The first step in cutting your own fringe bangs is to decide on the length you want. It's important to remember that the length of your bangs will shrink when they dry, so it's better to start longer than you want them to be.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section off the hair that you want to cut into bangs. Use a comb to create a triangle-shaped section at the front of your head. The base of the triangle should be the width of your forehead, and the point should be at the top of your head.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Hold the hair between your index and middle finger, and slide your fingers down to the length you want your bangs to be. Use your scissors to cut straight across. It's important to keep your scissors parallel to the hair to ensure an even cut.

Step 4: Check for Evenness

After cutting your bangs, check for evenness by combing them straight down. If you notice any uneven spots, you can trim them with your scissors.

Step 5: Style Your Bangs

Once you're happy with the length and evenness of your bangs, it's time to style them. Use a round brush and a blow dryer to blow dry your bangs straight down. If you prefer a side-swept look, blow dry them to the side. You can also use a flat iron to smooth out any kinks or waves.

Tips for Cutting Fringe Bangs Yourself

Cutting your own fringe bangs can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's totally doable. Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect fringe bangs:

  • Always start with longer bangs than you want to account for shrinkage when they dry.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't cut your bangs when they're dry. Wet hair is easier to work with and will give you a more accurate cut.
  • Take your time and cut small sections at a time. It's easier to cut more hair than to fix a mistake.

FAQ

Can I cut my own bangs with regular household scissors?

While you can technically cut your own bangs with regular household scissors, it's not recommended. Household scissors are often dull and can result in a jagged cut.

How often should I trim my bangs?

It's recommended to trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them at the desired length.

Hair Care for Fringe Bangs

To keep your fringe bangs looking fresh and stylish, it's important to take care of them properly. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your bangs frequently to avoid oil buildup.
  • Blow dry your bangs straight down to prevent them from curling up.
  • Use a flat iron to smooth out any kinks or waves.
  • Avoid touching your bangs throughout the day to prevent oil transfer.

Cutting your own fringe bangs can be a fun and exciting way to switch up your hairstyle. With these step-by-step instructions and tips, you'll be able to achieve the perfect fringe bangs in no time!
