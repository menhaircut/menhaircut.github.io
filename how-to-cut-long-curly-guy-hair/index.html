How To Cut Long Curly Guy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Oct 12, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Long curly hair can be a challenge to maintain, especially for guys. But with the right tools and techniques, you can keep your locks looking great. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to cut long curly guy hair. From preparing your hair to finishing the cut, we've got you covered. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Preparing Your Hair
  • Tools You'll Need
  • Cutting Technique
  • Tips for Cutting Long Curly Guy Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care after Cutting

Preparing Your Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to prepare your hair. Start by washing and conditioning your hair thoroughly. This will remove any dirt or product buildup and make your hair more manageable. Once your hair is clean, towel dry it gently. Avoid using a blow dryer, as this can cause your hair to frizz.

Next, detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots. This will prevent any tangles or knots from getting worse as you cut. If you have particularly thick or unruly hair, you may want to use a detangling spray or leave-in conditioner to make combing easier.

Tools You'll Need

To cut long curly guy hair, you'll need a few basic tools:

  • Sharp scissors - Look for a pair of scissors designed for cutting hair. They should be sharp and have a comfortable grip.
  • Clippers - Clippers can be useful for trimming the sides and back of your hair.
  • Comb - A wide-tooth comb will help you detangle your hair and guide your cuts.
  • Sectioning clips - These clips will help you keep your hair organized as you cut.
  • Mirror - You'll need a mirror to see the back of your head.

Make sure you have all of these tools on hand before you start cutting. You don't want to have to stop halfway through to go looking for something!

Cutting Technique

Now it's time to start cutting. Here's the basic technique for cutting long curly guy hair:

  1. Start by sectioning your hair. Use your comb to create a part down the middle of your head. Then, create two more parts from your ears to the top of your head. This will divide your hair into four sections.
  2. Clip the top two sections of your hair out of the way. You'll start by cutting the bottom two sections.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the bottom section. Use your comb to pull it straight out from your head. Then, use your scissors to trim the ends. Start by cutting off a small amount, then continue cutting until you reach your desired length.
  4. Continue cutting small sections of hair from the bottom section. Work your way around your head, making sure to cut evenly on both sides.
  5. Once you've finished the bottom section, unclip the top section and repeat the process. Take small sections of hair, pull them straight out from your head, and trim the ends.
  6. Use your clippers to trim the sides and back of your hair, if desired. Use a guard to avoid cutting your hair too short.
  7. Once you've finished cutting, use your comb to check for any stray hairs. Trim them as needed.

Remember to take your time and be patient. Cutting long curly guy hair can be tricky, but with practice, you'll get better at it.

Tips for Cutting Long Curly Guy Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind as you cut:

  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to take off a little bit at a time than to cut off too much and regret it.
  • Keep your scissors sharp. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it more difficult to cut.
  • Use a light touch. Curly hair can be delicate, so be gentle as you cut.
  • Check your progress frequently. Use your comb and mirror to make sure your hair is looking the way you want it to.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I cut my long curly guy hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it gets damaged. As a general rule, you should aim to get a trim every six to eight weeks. This will help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Should I cut my hair wet or dry?

It's usually best to cut curly hair when it's dry. This will give you a more accurate idea of how your hair will look once it's styled. However, if your hair is particularly unruly, you may find it easier to cut when it's damp.

Can I cut my own hair, or should I go to a salon?

It's up to you! If you're comfortable cutting your own hair and have the right tools, there's no reason why you can't do it yourself. However, if you're not confident in your skills, or if you want a more complex cut, it may be worth going to a salon.

Hair Care after Cutting

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. This will help keep your hair hydrated and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often. Curling irons, straighteners, and blow dryers can damage your hair and make it more difficult to manage.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week. This will help keep it soft and healthy.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much. This can cause frizz and damage.

By following these tips, you can keep your long curly guy hair looking great between cuts.

Conclusion

Now that you know how to cut long curly guy hair, you can take control of your own hair care. Remember to prepare your hair properly, use the right tools, and take your time as you cut. With practice, you'll be able to create the perfect cut for your unique style.
