Tips For Bleaching Asian Hair How To Achieve The Perfect Blonde

If you're an Asian looking to bleach your hair, you may be wondering how to get the perfect blonde without damaging your hair. In this blog post, we'll give you tips on how to bleach Asian hair, step by step instructions, and answer frequently asked questions about hair bleaching. We'll also give you some tips on how to care for your bleached hair to keep it healthy and shiny.

How to Bleach Asian Hair

Bleaching Asian hair can be tricky because Asian hair is typically thicker and more resistant to chemicals than other types of hair. However, with the right products and techniques, you can achieve the perfect blonde without damaging your hair.

Products You'll Need

Before you start bleaching your hair, make sure you have the following products:

  • Bleach powder
  • Developer (20-30 volume)
  • Toner
  • Conditioner
  • Gloves
  • Brush and bowl

Step-by-Step Instructions

Follow these step-by-step instructions to bleach your Asian hair:

Step 1: Do a Strand Test

Before you bleach your entire head, do a strand test to make sure you're not allergic to any of the products and to determine how long you need to leave the bleach on your hair.

Step 2: Protect Your Skin and Clothing

Put on gloves and wear clothing that you don't mind getting bleach on. Apply petroleum jelly around your hairline and ears to protect your skin from the bleach.

Step 3: Mix the Bleach

Mix the bleach powder and developer according to the instructions. Use a plastic bowl and brush to mix the products.

Step 4: Apply the Bleach

Apply the bleach to your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. Make sure you saturate your hair evenly with the bleach.

Step 5: Check Your Hair

Check your hair every 10 minutes to see how light it's getting. If you're not happy with the color, leave the bleach on for a few more minutes.

Step 6: Rinse Your Hair

Rinse your hair with warm water until the water runs clear. Make sure you remove all the bleach from your hair.

Step 7: Apply Toner

Apply toner to your hair to get rid of any brassy tones. Leave the toner on for the recommended time, then rinse it out with warm water.

Step 8: Condition Your Hair

Apply conditioner to your hair to restore moisture and shine. Leave the conditioner on for a few minutes, then rinse it out with cool water.

Tips for Bleaching Asian Hair

Here are some tips to help you bleach your Asian hair:

Tip 1: Use the Right Products

Make sure you use products that are specifically designed for bleaching hair. Don't use household bleach or other chemicals that are not meant for hair.

Tip 2: Follow the Instructions

Read the instructions carefully and follow them exactly. Don't leave the bleach on your hair for too long, or you could damage your hair.

Tip 3: Do a Strand Test

Do a strand test before you bleach your entire head to make sure you're not allergic to any of the products and to determine how long you need to leave the bleach on your hair.

Tip 4: Use Conditioner

Use conditioner after you bleach your hair to restore moisture and shine. You can also use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair healthy.

FAQs about Hair Bleaching

Q: Can I bleach my hair if I have dyed it before?

A: Yes, you can bleach your hair if you have dyed it before. However, the bleach may not lift the color as much as it would on natural hair.

Q: How long should I wait before bleaching my hair again?

A: You should wait at least 2-3 weeks before bleaching your hair again to give your hair a chance to recover.

Q: Can I bleach my hair if I have a perm?

A: It's not recommended to bleach your hair if you have a perm. The chemicals in the bleach can damage your hair and cause it to break.

Hair Care Tips for Bleached Hair

Tip 1: Use a Purple Shampoo

Use a purple shampoo to keep your blonde hair from turning brassy. The purple tones in the shampoo neutralize the yellow tones in your hair.

Tip 2: Use a Deep Conditioner

Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Leave the conditioner on for at least 10 minutes.

Tip 3: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools on your bleached hair as much as possible. Heat can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage.

Tip 4: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to get rid of split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

Following these tips will help you achieve the perfect blonde without damaging your Asian hair. Remember to always use the right products and follow the instructions carefully. With a little bit of patience and care, you can have beautiful, healthy, bleached hair.
