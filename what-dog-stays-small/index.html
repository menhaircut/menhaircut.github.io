What Dog Stays Small A Guide To Small Dog Breeds

Written by Frank Nov 10, 2023 · 4 min read

Welcome to our guide on what dog stays small. If you're looking for a furry companion that won't take up too much space in your home or require excessive exercise, a small dog breed might be the perfect match for you. In this article, we'll highlight some popular small dog breeds, provide tips on how to choose the right one for your lifestyle, and offer some insight on how to care for your new furry friend.

How to Choose the Right Small Dog Breed

Before you start your search for a new furry friend, it's important to consider your lifestyle and living situation. Small dogs may be a good choice for people who:

  • Live in apartments or small homes with limited space
  • Have limited mobility or live in areas with limited outdoor space
  • Prefer a lap dog or a companion to cuddle with
  • Want a dog that is easier to train and groom

When choosing a small dog breed, it's also important to consider the breed's temperament and energy level. Some small dogs, like Chihuahuas, can be high-strung and require a lot of attention while others, like French Bulldogs, are more relaxed and can be content with just a few walks a day.

Popular Small Dog Breeds

Here are some popular small dog breeds to consider:

  • Chihuahua: Known for their small size and big personalities, Chihuahuas are a popular choice for people who want a loyal companion.
  • Pomeranian: These fluffy and friendly dogs are known for their cute looks and outgoing personalities.
  • French Bulldog: A laid-back and affectionate breed, French Bulldogs are great for people who want a dog that doesn't require a lot of exercise.
  • Pug: With their wrinkly faces and playful personalities, Pugs are a favorite among pet owners.
  • Shih Tzu: These friendly and affectionate dogs are known for their long, flowing coats and sweet personalities.

How to Care for Your Small Dog

Once you've chosen the right small dog breed for your lifestyle, it's important to provide proper care to keep your furry friend happy and healthy. Here are some tips:

Feeding

Small dogs require smaller portions of food than larger breeds, so be sure to measure out their meals to avoid overfeeding. Choose high-quality dog food that is appropriate for their size and age.

Exercise

While small dogs don't require as much exercise as larger breeds, they still need regular walks and playtime to stay healthy and happy. Aim for at least 30 minutes of exercise per day.

Grooming

Some small dog breeds require more grooming than others, but all dogs need regular grooming to keep their coats healthy and free of mats and tangles. Brush your dog's coat regularly and trim their nails as needed.

Dental Care

Small dogs are prone to dental problems, so it's important to brush their teeth regularly and provide dental chews or toys to help keep their teeth clean.

FAQ About Small Dogs

What is the smallest dog breed?

The Chihuahua is the smallest dog breed, weighing in at just 2-6 pounds.

Do small dogs live longer than large dogs?

Small dogs typically live longer than large dogs. While the lifespan of a dog depends on many factors, small breeds can live up to 15-20 years.

Are small dogs good with children?

Many small dog breeds are good with children, but it's important to supervise interactions to ensure both the dog and child are safe.

Hair Care for Small Dogs

Small dogs with long coats require regular grooming to keep their fur healthy and free of mats and tangles. Here are some tips:

Brushing

Brush your dog's fur regularly to prevent mats and tangles. Use a soft-bristled brush or comb and work in small sections.

Bathing

Small dogs don't need to be bathed as often as larger breeds, but they still need regular baths to keep their coat clean and healthy. Use a mild dog shampoo and rinse thoroughly.

Trimming

Some small dogs, like Poodles and Shih Tzus, require regular haircuts to keep their coats manageable. Take your dog to a professional groomer or learn how to do it yourself.

Accessories

Consider using accessories like bows, bandanas, and hats to add some style to your small dog's look.

Now that you know what dog stays small, you can start your search for the perfect furry companion. Remember to consider your lifestyle, choose the right breed, and provide proper care to keep your new friend happy and healthy.
