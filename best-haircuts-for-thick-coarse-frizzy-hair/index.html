Best Haircuts For Thick Coarse Frizzy Hair A Guide To Taming Your Mane

Written by Aprilia Jul 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of dealing with unruly, frizzy hair that seems impossible to manage? If you have thick, coarse, and frizzy hair, you know the struggle is real. But don't worry, there are haircuts that can help make your hair more manageable and easier to style. In this article, we'll be discussing the best haircuts for thick coarse frizzy hair, as well as some tips and tricks to keep your hair looking its best.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Thick, coarse, and frizzy hair can be a challenge to manage, but with the right haircut, it can look amazing. The key is to choose a haircut that works with your hair's natural texture and thickness. You want a style that will help tame your frizz and make your hair more manageable, while still looking stylish and trendy.

How To

When it comes to choosing the best haircut for thick coarse frizzy hair, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, you want a style that will help control your frizz, so look for cuts with layers or texture. Second, you want a style that will work with your hair's thickness, so consider a cut that will thin out some of the bulk. Finally, you want a style that will flatter your face shape and features.

Here are some of the best haircuts for thick coarse frizzy hair:

1. Long Layers

Long layers are a great option for thick coarse frizzy hair because they help to thin out some of the bulk while still providing volume and movement. This cut works well with a side-part and can be worn straight or curled.

2. Bob

A bob is a classic haircut that works well with thick coarse frizzy hair. The key is to keep the length above the shoulders and add layers to help control the frizz. This cut works well with a center or side-part and can be worn straight or curled.

3. Pixie

If you're feeling daring, a pixie cut can be a great option for thick coarse frizzy hair. This cut is short and layered, which helps to control frizz and provide volume. It works well with a side-swept bang and can be worn straight or with texture.

Step by Step

Once you've chosen the best haircut for your thick coarse frizzy hair, it's important to know how to style it. Here are some steps to follow:

1. Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are designed for thick coarse frizzy hair. Look for products that contain moisturizing ingredients like argan oil or shea butter.

2. Towel Dry Your Hair

Gently towel dry your hair, being careful not to rub it too hard. Avoid using a blow dryer as this can cause more frizz.

3. Apply Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help tame any frizz and add moisture. Be sure to apply it evenly throughout your hair.

4. Style Your Hair

Depending on the cut you chose, style your hair using a curling iron, straightener, or other styling tool. Use a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from damage.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep your thick coarse frizzy hair looking its best:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to brush your hair
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends

FAQ

Q: Can I still get a layered haircut if I have thick coarse frizzy hair?

A: Yes, layers can actually help to thin out some of the bulk and make your hair more manageable. Just be sure to choose a stylist who is experienced in cutting thick coarse frizzy hair.

Q: How often should I wash my thick coarse frizzy hair?

A: It's best to wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils. Use a dry shampoo in between washes to keep your hair looking fresh.

Hair Care

To keep your thick coarse frizzy hair looking its best, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often
  • Avoid using harsh hair products that contain alcohol
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals

With the right haircut and hair care routine, you can tame your thick coarse frizzy hair and have it looking its best. Experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for you.
