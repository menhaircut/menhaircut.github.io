Hairstyles For Long Faces And Big Noses The Ultimate Guide

If you have a long face and a big nose, finding the right hairstyle can be a challenge. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll give you tips, suggestions, and step-by-step guides to help you find the perfect hairstyle that flatters your unique features. We'll also answer some frequently asked questions and give you some hair care tips. So, let's get started!

How to Determine Your Face Shape

Before we dive into hairstyles, it's important to determine your face shape. This will help you choose a hairstyle that flatters your features. Here are the steps to determine your face shape:

  1. Stand in front of a mirror with your hair pulled back.
  2. Use a lip liner or eyeliner to trace the outline of your face on the mirror.
  3. Step back and look at the shape you drew.
  4. Compare it to the following face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, and diamond.

Once you've determined your face shape, you can move on to choosing a hairstyle that flatters your features.

Hairstyles for Long Faces and Big Noses

Now that you know your face shape, let's talk about hairstyles that flatter long faces and big noses. Here are some suggestions:

Layers

Layers can be a great choice for long faces and big noses. They add volume and dimension to your hair, which can balance out your features. Ask your stylist for long, choppy layers that start around your chin or collarbone.

Bangs

Bangs can also be a great choice for long faces and big noses. They draw attention to your eyes and forehead, which can balance out your features. Ask your stylist for side-swept bangs that start just above your eyebrows.

Bob

A bob can also flatter long faces and big noses. Ask your stylist for a chin-length or shoulder-length bob with layers or bangs. This will add volume and dimension to your hair, which can balance out your features.

Tips for Choosing the Right Hairstyle

Here are some tips to keep in mind when choosing a hairstyle:

Consider Your Face Shape

As we mentioned earlier, your face shape should be the first thing you consider when choosing a hairstyle. Different face shapes look best with different hairstyles, so it's important to choose one that flatters your features.

Consider Your Hair Type

Your hair type can also play a role in which hairstyle you choose. For example, if you have thick, curly hair, you may want to avoid a short, choppy haircut that requires a lot of maintenance.

Consider Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle should also be a factor in your hairstyle. If you're always on the go, you may want a low-maintenance hairstyle that doesn't require a lot of styling. If you have a more relaxed lifestyle, you may have more time to devote to your hair.

Step-by-Step Guides

Here are some step-by-step guides to help you achieve some of the hairstyles we mentioned earlier:

How to Cut Layers

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section your hair into four quadrants.
  3. Starting at the bottom, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers.
  4. Cut the hair at an angle, starting at the middle of the section and working your way down to the ends.
  5. Repeat this process on the other sections of hair.

How to Cut Side-Swept Bangs

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section your hair into four quadrants.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the front of your head.
  4. Cut the hair at an angle, starting at the middle of the section and working your way down to the ends.
  5. Repeat this process on the other side of your head.

How to Cut a Bob

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section your hair into four quadrants.
  3. Starting at the back, cut your hair in a straight line across your head.
  4. Continue cutting your hair in a straight line around your head, working your way to the front.
  5. Once you reach the front, add layers or bangs as desired.

FAQs

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have a long face and big nose?

Avoid hairstyles that are too long, straight, or flat. These can elongate your face and draw attention to your nose. Also, avoid hairstyles that are too short or spiky, as they can make your nose appear larger.

Can I still wear my hair in a ponytail if I have a long face and big nose?

Yes, you can still wear your hair in a ponytail if you have a long face and big nose. Just make sure to add some volume to your hair, either with layers or curls, to balance out your features.

What hair colors should I consider if I have a long face and big nose?

Consider hair colors that add warmth and depth to your hair, such as honey blonde, chestnut brown, or auburn. Avoid hair colors that are too light or too dark, as they can make your nose appear larger.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some tips to keep your hair looking its best:

Use a Heat Protectant

If you use heat styling tools on your hair, such as a curling iron or flat iron, make sure to use a heat protectant spray beforehand. This will help prevent damage and keep your hair healthy.

Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every six to eight weeks.

Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Using a deep conditioning treatment once a week can help keep your hair soft and shiny. Leave the treatment on for at least 10 minutes before rinsing it out.

Now that you have some tips, suggestions, and step-by-step guides, you're ready to find the perfect hairstyle for your long face and big nose. Remember to consider your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle when choosing a hairstyle, and don't be afraid to experiment until you find the perfect look!
