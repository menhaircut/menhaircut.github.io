How To Make Red Tones In Hair Go Away A Complete Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 08, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of seeing red tones in your hair that just won't seem to go away? Whether you're a natural redhead or you recently dyed your hair, getting rid of unwanted red tones can be a challenge. But don't worry, we've got you covered.

Description

Red tones in hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, hair dye, and sun exposure. While some people love the look of red hair, others may find it unflattering or simply want a change. If you fall into the latter category, there are several methods you can use to help reduce or eliminate red tones in your hair.

How to Make Red Tones in Hair Go Away

Method 1: Use a Color-Correcting Shampoo

One of the easiest ways to combat red tones in hair is to use a color-correcting shampoo. These shampoos are specifically formulated to neutralize unwanted tones in hair, such as red or brassy tones. Look for a shampoo that is designed for your hair color and follow the instructions on the packaging.

Method 2: Try a Vinegar Rinse

Vinegar is a natural acid that can help to remove unwanted tones in hair. To make a vinegar rinse, simply mix equal parts water and apple cider vinegar in a bowl. After shampooing your hair, pour the vinegar rinse over your hair and let it sit for a few minutes before rinsing it out with water.

Method 3: Use a Toner

If you have recently dyed your hair and are experiencing unwanted red tones, a toner may be the solution. A toner is a semi-permanent hair color that can be used to neutralize unwanted tones in hair. Look for a toner that is specifically formulated for your hair color and follow the instructions on the packaging.

Step by Step: How to Make Red Tones in Hair Go Away

Here are the steps you can follow to make red tones in hair go away:

  1. Choose a method that works best for you (color-correcting shampoo, vinegar rinse, or toner).
  2. Follow the instructions on the packaging of the product you choose.
  3. Repeat the process as needed until you achieve the desired result.
  4. Maintain your hair by using a color-safe shampoo and conditioner.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you make red tones in hair go away:

  • Avoid overexposure to the sun, as this can cause red tones to become more prominent.
  • Consider getting a professional color correction if your red tones are particularly stubborn.
  • Be patient â it may take several treatments to achieve the desired result.

FAQ

Q: Can I use a color-correcting shampoo on natural red hair?

A: Yes, you can use a color-correcting shampoo on natural red hair to help neutralize any unwanted tones.

Q: How often should I use a vinegar rinse?

A: You can use a vinegar rinse once a week or as needed.

Q: Will a toner damage my hair?

A: No, a toner is a semi-permanent hair color that is gentle on your hair and will not cause damage.

Hair Care for Red Tones

To maintain your hair and prevent red tones from returning, it's important to use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are specifically formulated for your hair type and color. Additionally, you can protect your hair from sun damage by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

By following these tips and methods, you can successfully make red tones in hair go away and achieve the hair color you desire.
