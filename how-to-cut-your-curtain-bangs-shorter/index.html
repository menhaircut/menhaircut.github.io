How To Cut Your Curtain Bangs Shorter A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Aug 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your long curtain bangs getting in the way? Or maybe you just want to switch up your look with a shorter style? Whatever your reasons may be, cutting your own bangs can be intimidating. But don't worry, with some patience and the right tools, you can easily achieve a shorter curtain bang style at home. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps to make the process as smooth as possible.

How to Cut Your Curtain Bangs Shorter

Before you start cutting, you'll want to decide how short you want your bangs to be. It's always better to start with a longer length and gradually cut them shorter, so be conservative with your first cut.

When it comes to cutting your bangs, it's important to take your time and work in small sections. This will help ensure that your cut is even and that you don't accidentally cut off too much.

Tools You'll Need

Here's what you'll need to get started:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Small spray bottle filled with water

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your curtain bangs shorter:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Brush your bangs straight down and clip back the rest of your hair.
  3. Choose a starting point for your bangs. This can be in the middle or off to one side.
  4. Take a small section of hair from the middle of your bangs.
  5. Hold the section of hair between your fingers and cut the ends with your scissors. Remember to start with a longer length and work your way shorter.
  6. Repeat steps 4 and 5, working from the middle towards the outer edges of your bangs.
  7. Check your work in the mirror frequently to make sure your bangs are even.
  8. Once you've reached your desired length, use your scissors to add any additional texture or layers you may want.
  9. Release your clipped hair and style your bangs as desired.

Tips for a Successful Cut

  • Work slowly and carefully, cutting small sections at a time.
  • Keep your scissors at an angle to create a softer, more natural-looking edge.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your bangs, start with a longer length and gradually work your way shorter.
  • Don't forget to check your work in the mirror frequently to make sure your bangs are even.

FAQ

Can I cut my own curtain bangs?

Yes, you can definitely cut your own curtain bangs. Just be sure to take your time and work in small sections to achieve the best results.

How short should I cut my curtain bangs?

This is a personal preference, but it's always better to start with a longer length and gradually work your way shorter. You can always cut more if needed, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.

Can I cut my bangs when they're wet?

It's best to cut your bangs when they're dry, as wet hair can shrink as it dries and you may end up cutting off more than you intended.

Hair Care After the Cut

After cutting your curtain bangs shorter, it's important to keep them looking their best. Here are a few tips:

  • Trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to maintain your desired length.
  • Use a small amount of styling product, such as pomade or wax, to add texture and hold to your bangs.
  • Blow dry your bangs with a round brush to create volume and shape.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can cause your bangs to become oily and flat.

Cutting your own curtain bangs shorter can be intimidating, but with some patience and the right tools, you can achieve a fresh, new look. Remember to take your time, work in small sections, and be conservative with your first cut. With practice, you'll become a pro at cutting your own bangs in no time.
