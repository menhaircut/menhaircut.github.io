How to Start a Barbershop Quartet | Step by Step Guide in 2023

If you're a fan of a capella music, you've probably heard of barbershop quartets. These four-part harmony groups have a unique and timeless sound that's been enjoyed for over a century. If you're interested in starting your own barbershop quartet, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll take you through the steps of how to start a barbershop quartet in 2023, including tips for choosing members, selecting songs, and more.

How to Start a Barbershop Quartet: Step by Step

Step 1: Find Members

The first step in starting a barbershop quartet is finding members. Ideally, you'll want four singers with a range of vocal abilities. You can recruit members from your local community, music schools, or online forums. Look for people who share your passion for barbershop music and have a good sense of pitch and rhythm.

Step 2: Choose Your Songs

Once you have your members, it's time to choose your songs. Barbershop quartets typically sing songs from the early 20th century, such as "Sweet Adeline" and "Shine on Harvest Moon." You can find sheet music and recordings of these songs online or at your local music store. Choose songs that showcase your group's strengths and allow each member to shine.

Step 3: Learn Your Parts

Learning your parts is essential to creating a harmonious sound. Each member of the quartet will have a different part to sing, including lead, tenor, baritone, and bass. You can find sheet music with individual parts online or hire a vocal coach to help you learn your parts. Practice your parts individually before coming together as a group.

Step 4: Rehearse Together

Rehearsing together is where the magic happens. Schedule regular rehearsals to practice your songs and work on your harmonies. Make sure to give each member of the quartet time to shine and provide constructive feedback to help each other improve. Rehearsing regularly will help you prepare for performances and competitions.

Step 5: Perform and Compete

Once you've honed your skills, it's time to perform and compete. Barbershop quartets often perform at local events, such as fairs, festivals, and holiday parties. You can also enter competitions to showcase your skills and compete against other quartets. Look for local or national competitions and prepare your best songs.

Tips for Starting a Barbershop Quartet

Starting a barbershop quartet takes time and dedication, but it's a rewarding experience. Here are some tips to help you get started:

  • Choose members who share your passion for barbershop music and have a good sense of pitch and rhythm
  • Practice regularly and give each member time to shine
  • Record your rehearsals to listen back and identify areas for improvement
  • Perform at local events and competitions to gain experience and exposure
  • Have fun and enjoy the process!

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a barbershop quartet?

A barbershop quartet is a group of four singers who perform a capella music in four-part harmony.

What kind of songs do barbershop quartets sing?

Barbershop quartets typically sing songs from the early 20th century, such as "Sweet Adeline" and "Shine on Harvest Moon."

Do I need to be a professional singer to start a barbershop quartet?

No, you don't need to be a professional singer to start a barbershop quartet. However, you should have a good sense of pitch and rhythm and be willing to practice regularly.

Starting a barbershop quartet is a fun and rewarding experience. With the right members, songs, and practice, you can create a harmonious sound that will delight audiences. Follow these steps and tips to get started on your barbershop quartet journey in 2023!
