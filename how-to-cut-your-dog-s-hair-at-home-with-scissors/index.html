How To Cut Your Dog s Hair At Home With Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Sep 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As a pet owner, it's important to keep your dog's coat clean and well-groomed. While it's always a good idea to take your furry friend to a professional groomer, sometimes it's more convenient to trim your dog's hair at home. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut your dog's hair at home with scissors.

How to Cut Your Dog's Hair at Home with Scissors

Before you get started, there are a few things you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Bristle brush
  • Comb
  • Treats (to keep your dog calm and happy)

Step 1: Brush and Bathe Your Dog

Before you start cutting your dog's hair, make sure to brush and bathe them. This will help remove any tangles or mats and make it easier to cut their hair.

Step 2: Decide on the Length

Decide on the length you want to cut your dog's hair. Keep in mind that cutting too much hair can expose your dog's skin and cause irritation.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start by trimming your dog's hair in small sections. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it with the scissors. Be sure to cut at an angle to avoid creating blunt edges.

Step 4: Use Clippers (Optional)

If your dog has long hair, you may want to use clippers to trim it down before using scissors. This will make it easier to cut the hair evenly.

Step 5: Trim Around the Ears and Eyes

Use scissors to trim the hair around your dog's ears and eyes. Be careful not to poke your dog's eyes with the scissors.

Step 6: Trim the Paws

Trim the hair around your dog's paws with scissors. This will help prevent dirt and debris from getting stuck in their paws.

Step 7: Trim the Tail

Trim the hair on your dog's tail with scissors. Be sure to leave enough hair so your dog can still wag their tail comfortably.

Step 8: Give Your Dog Treats

Give your dog treats throughout the grooming process to keep them calm and happy. This will help make the experience more enjoyable for both you and your furry friend.

Step 9: Clean Up

Once you're done cutting your dog's hair, clean up any loose hair with a vacuum or broom.

Tips for Cutting Your Dog's Hair at Home

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting your dog's hair at home:

  • Start slow and be patient
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid pulling your dog's hair
  • Don't cut too much hair at once
  • Take breaks if your dog gets restless
  • Keep treats handy to reward good behavior

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I Cut My Dog's Hair with Regular Scissors?

While you can use regular scissors to cut your dog's hair, it's best to use scissors specifically designed for pet grooming. These scissors are sharper and have a better angle for cutting your dog's hair.

How Often Should I Cut My Dog's Hair?

The frequency of haircuts will depend on your dog's breed and coat type. Some dogs may need to be trimmed every 6-8 weeks, while others may only need to be trimmed a few times a year. Consult with your vet or a professional groomer for guidance.

Hair Care for Your Dog

Regular grooming is an important part of your dog's overall health and wellbeing. In addition to trimming their hair, make sure to:

  • Bathe your dog regularly
  • Brush their coat daily
  • Check their ears for signs of infection
  • Trim their nails regularly

By following these tips, you can help keep your furry friend healthy and happy for years to come.
