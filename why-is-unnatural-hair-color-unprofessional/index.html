Why Is Unnatural Hair Color Unprofessional

Written by Robby Oct 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Unnatural hair colors have become increasingly popular in recent years. From pastel pinks to vibrant greens, people are experimenting with all sorts of hues. While it may be seen as fashionable and trendy, many employers consider it unprofessional. In this article, we will explore why unnatural hair color is unprofessional and what you can do to maintain a professional appearance.

Description

Unnatural hair color refers to any hair color that is not a natural shade, such as blonde, brown, black, or red. This includes colors such as blue, green, pink, and purple. While some people may view these colors as a form of self-expression, many employers see them as unprofessional.

How to

If you are considering coloring your hair an unnatural shade, it is important to consider the potential consequences. While it may seem like a fun way to express yourself, it could ultimately impact your career. Here are some tips to help you maintain a professional appearance:

1. Know your company's policy

Before coloring your hair, it is important to know your company's policy on appearance. Some companies may have strict guidelines on what is considered professional, while others may be more lenient. If you are unsure, it is always best to err on the side of caution.

2. Choose a subtle shade

If you do decide to color your hair, consider choosing a subtle shade that is closer to a natural color. This will help you maintain a professional appearance while still expressing yourself.

3. Keep it well-maintained

Unnatural hair colors can quickly become faded and unappealing if they are not maintained properly. Be sure to keep your hair well-conditioned and avoid excessive heat styling to prevent damage.

Step by step

Here are some steps you can take to maintain a professional appearance with an unnatural hair color:

Step 1: Research your company's policy on appearance

Before coloring your hair, it is important to know what is considered professional in your workplace. Check your employee handbook or talk to your HR representative to get a clear understanding of the guidelines.

Step 2: Choose a subtle shade

If you do decide to color your hair, consider choosing a shade that is closer to a natural color. This will help you maintain a professional appearance while still expressing yourself.

Step 3: Keep it well-maintained

Unnatural hair colors can quickly become faded and unappealing if they are not maintained properly. Be sure to keep your hair well-conditioned and avoid excessive heat styling to prevent damage.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you maintain a professional appearance with an unnatural hair color:

1. Consider your audience

While unnatural hair colors may be more widely accepted in creative industries, they may be viewed as unprofessional in more conservative fields.

2. Dress professionally

Pairing an unnatural hair color with a professional outfit can help balance out your overall appearance.

3. Be confident

Ultimately, the most important thing is to be confident in your appearance. If you feel good about yourself, it will show in your work.

FAQ

Q: Why do employers consider unnatural hair colors unprofessional?

A: Many employers view unnatural hair colors as distracting or inappropriate for a professional setting.

Q: Can I still have an unnatural hair color and be professional?

A: Yes, it is possible to maintain a professional appearance with an unnatural hair color. However, it is important to choose a subtle shade and keep it well-maintained.

Q: What are some subtle unnatural hair colors?

A: Subtle unnatural hair colors include shades that are closer to natural colors, such as pastel pink or lavender.

Hair Care

Regardless of whether you have a natural or unnatural hair color, it is important to take good care of your hair. This includes regular trims, conditioning treatments, and avoiding excessive heat styling. If you have an unnatural hair color, it is especially important to use products that are specifically designed for colored hair to maintain its vibrancy and prevent fading.

In conclusion, while unnatural hair colors may be trendy and fashionable, they are often viewed as unprofessional in the workplace. If you do decide to color your hair an unnatural shade, be sure to consider your company's policy on appearance, choose a subtle shade, and maintain it well.
