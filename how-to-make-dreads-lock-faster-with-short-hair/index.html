If you have short hair and want to achieve dreads, it may take longer for them to lock compared to longer hair. However, there are ways to speed up the process and get the dreads you desire. In this article, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to make dreads lock faster with short hair, as well as some tips and FAQs to help you along the way.

How to

Before we get into the specific steps on how to make dreads lock faster with short hair, it's important to understand the basics of dreadlocks. Dreadlocks are formed by matting and twisting sections of hair together over a period of time. Short hair may have a harder time locking because there is less hair to work with, but itâs still possible to achieve dreads.

Step-by-step

Here are some steps to help you make dreads lock faster with short hair:

  1. Start with clean hair. Wash your hair thoroughly to remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup that may prevent your hair from locking up.

  2. Section your hair. Divide your hair into small, even sections using a comb or your fingers. The smaller the sections, the faster your hair will lock up.

  3. Backcomb each section. Use a comb to backcomb each section of hair until it begins to mat and tangle. Make sure to backcomb in the same direction each time to create consistency.

  4. Twist and palm roll. After youâve backcombed each section, twist them tightly and palm roll them to help them lock up faster. Keep twisting and palm rolling until you achieve the desired texture.

  5. Use a locking gel. Apply a locking gel to each section of hair to help them lock up faster. Be sure to use a quality product that won't leave residue on your hair.

  6. Sleep with a durag or bonnet. Cover your hair with a durag or bonnet to keep your hair in place and prevent it from unraveling while you sleep.

  7. Be patient. It may take several weeks or even months for your hair to fully lock up, but donât give up. Keep repeating the above steps and be patient.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you make dreads lock faster with short hair:

  • Avoid using conditioner. Conditioner can soften your hair and make it harder to lock up.

  • Avoid using wax. Wax can leave residue on your hair and make it harder to wash out, which can lead to buildup and prevent your hair from locking up.

  • Avoid using a hair dryer. Heat can damage your hair and make it harder to lock up. Let your hair air dry instead.

  • Keep your hair moisturized. Use a moisturizing spray or oil to keep your hair healthy and prevent it from drying out.

  • Get regular maintenance. Visit a professional loctician to have your dreads maintained and tightened as needed.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about making dreads lock faster with short hair:

  • Q: How long does it take for dreads to lock up with short hair?
  • A: It can take several weeks or even months for dreads to fully lock up with short hair.

  • Q: Can I get dreads with relaxed hair?
  • A: Yes, you can get dreads with relaxed hair, but it may take longer for them to lock up.

  • Q: Can I wash my hair while I have dreads?
  • A: Yes, you can wash your hair while you have dreads, but be sure to use a residue-free shampoo and avoid getting your hair too wet.

  • Q: Can I swim with dreads?
  • A: Yes, you can swim with dreads, but be sure to wear a swim cap and rinse your hair thoroughly with clean water afterwards.

Hair Care

Once you have achieved your desired dreadlock texture, itâs important to take care of your hair to prevent damage and maintain the health of your scalp. Here are some tips:

  • Use a residue-free shampoo to wash your hair.

  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh your hair down.

  • Keep your scalp moisturized with natural oils like jojoba or coconut oil.

  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and cause dryness.

  • Get regular maintenance from a professional loctician to keep your dreads looking neat and tidy.

By following these steps and tips, you can make dreads lock faster with short hair and achieve the unique and stylish look you desire. Remember to be patient and take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong.
