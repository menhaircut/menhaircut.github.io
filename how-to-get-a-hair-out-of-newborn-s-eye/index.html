How To Get A Hair Out Of Newborn s Eye A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Aug 09, 2023 · 3 min read

As a new parent, it can be scary to see a hair in your newborn's eye. You want to get it out as quickly and safely as possible. In this guide, we will go over the steps you can take to remove a hair from your baby's eye. We will also provide some tips on how to prevent this from happening in the future.

How to Remove a Hair from Your Newborn's Eye

Here are the steps you can take to remove a hair from your baby's eye:

Step 1: Wash Your Hands

Before touching your baby's eye, make sure to wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water. This will help prevent the spread of any germs or bacteria.

Step 2: Position Your Baby

Position your baby in a way that makes it easy for you to see the hair and access their eye. You can lay your baby on their back or hold them in your arms.

Step 3: Use a Clean Cloth

Gently wipe your baby's eye with a clean, damp cloth. This may help to loosen the hair and make it easier to remove.

Step 4: Use a Cotton Swab

If the hair is still in your baby's eye, you can try using a clean cotton swab. Wet the cotton swab with warm water and gently brush it against the hair to try and remove it. Be careful not to press too hard, as this can cause injury to your baby's eye.

Step 5: Seek Medical Attention

If you are unable to remove the hair from your baby's eye, or if your baby is experiencing any discomfort or redness, seek medical attention. Your pediatrician can help to safely remove the hair and ensure that there are no further complications.

Tips for Preventing Hair in Your Baby's Eye

Here are some tips to help prevent hair from getting into your baby's eye:

  • Keep your baby's hair trimmed and out of their face
  • Use a hair tie or headband to keep your baby's hair back
  • Regularly clean your baby's face and eyes

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

What should I do if my baby gets something else in their eye?

If your baby gets something besides a hair in their eye, do not attempt to remove it yourself. Seek medical attention to ensure that your baby's eye is not injured and the foreign object is safely removed.

Is it safe to use eye drops on my baby?

Do not use any eye drops or medications on your baby's eyes without first consulting with your pediatrician. They can advise you on the best course of treatment for your baby's specific situation.

Hair Care for Your Baby

Regular hair care can help prevent hair from getting into your baby's eyes. Here are some tips for keeping your baby's hair healthy:

  • Use gentle, mild shampoo designed for babies
  • Comb your baby's hair gently with a soft brush
  • Do not use hair products on your baby's hair

By following these steps and taking proper precautions, you can help keep your baby's eyes safe and healthy.
