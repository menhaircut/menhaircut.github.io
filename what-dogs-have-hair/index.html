What Dogs Have Hair A Comprehensive Guide To Understanding Your Canine s Hair

Written by Petter Jul 04, 2023 · 3 min read

If you’re a dog owner, you know that dogs come in all shapes and sizes, with different coat types and textures. Some dogs have hair, while others have fur. In this article, we’ll explore what dogs have hair, how to care for it, and answer some frequently asked questions about canine hair.

Table of Contents

  • What is the Difference Between Hair and Fur in Dogs?
  • What Breeds of Dogs Have Hair?
  • How to Care for Dogs with Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Dog Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Difference Between Hair and Fur in Dogs?

The terms “hair” and “fur” are often used interchangeably when referring to a dog’s coat, but there is a difference between the two. Hair is longer, finer, and grows continuously, while fur is shorter, thicker, and sheds seasonally. Dogs with hair require more maintenance, as their hair can become matted and tangled if not brushed regularly.

What Breeds of Dogs Have Hair?

There are several breeds of dogs that have hair instead of fur. Some of the most common include:

  • Afghan Hound
  • Bichon Frise
  • Maltese
  • Poodle
  • Shih Tzu
  • Yorkshire Terrier

These breeds are popular choices for people with allergies, as they shed less dander than dogs with fur.

How to Care for Dogs with Hair

Dogs with hair require regular grooming to keep their coats healthy and free of tangles. Here are some tips for caring for your dog’s hair:

  1. Brush your dog’s hair daily with a slicker brush to prevent tangles and matting.
  2. Trim your dog’s hair regularly to maintain a healthy length.
  3. Bathe your dog every 4-6 weeks to keep their coat clean and healthy.
  4. Use a high-quality dog shampoo and conditioner to keep your dog’s hair soft and shiny.
  5. Dry your dog’s hair thoroughly after bathing to prevent matting.

Tips for Maintaining Dog Hair

Here are some additional tips for maintaining your dog’s hair:

  • Use a detangling spray to make brushing easier and prevent matting.
  • Feed your dog a high-quality diet to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Provide your dog with plenty of exercise to promote good circulation and healthy skin and coat.
  • Avoid using human hair products on your dog, as they can be too harsh and cause skin irritation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do all dogs have hair?

No, not all dogs have hair. Some breeds have fur, which is shorter and thicker than hair.

What is the best brush for dogs with hair?

A slicker brush is the best type of brush for dogs with hair, as it can easily remove tangles and mats.

How often should I bathe my dog with hair?

You should bathe your dog with hair every 4-6 weeks to keep their coat clean and healthy.

Can I use human shampoo on my dog with hair?

No, you should not use human shampoo on your dog with hair, as it can be too harsh and cause skin irritation.

By following these tips, you can keep your dog’s hair healthy, shiny, and tangle-free. Remember to groom your dog regularly and provide them with a healthy diet and plenty of exercise to promote good overall health.
