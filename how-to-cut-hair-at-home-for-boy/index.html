How To Cut Hair At Home For Boys

Written by Aprilia Nov 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending money on haircuts for your son? Cutting hair at home is a great way to save money and can also be a fun activity for you and your child. In this article, we will provide step by step instructions and tips on how to cut hair at home for boys.

Description

Cutting hair at home for boys can be intimidating, especially if you have no experience. However, with the right tools and techniques, it can be a great way to save money and bond with your child.

How to Cut Hair at Home for Boys: Step by Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step in cutting hair at home for boys is to gather your tools. You will need a pair of hair clippers, scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. If your child has longer hair, you may also want to use hair clips to keep the hair out of the way.

Step 2: Decide on the Haircut

Before you start cutting, you should decide on the type of haircut you want to give your child. Look up pictures online or in magazines for inspiration. Keep in mind your child's hair type and face shape when choosing a haircut.

Step 3: Wash and Dry the Hair

Once you have decided on the haircut, wash and dry your child's hair. Make sure the hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 4: Start with the Clippers

Begin by using the clippers to trim the sides and back of your child's head. Use a guard that is appropriate for the length you want to cut. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using the comb to guide the clippers.

Step 5: Blend the Top

Once you have finished the sides and back, it's time to blend the top. Use scissors to trim the hair on top of your child's head. Start by cutting small sections at a time, and use the comb to blend the hair.

Step 6: Cut the Bangs

If your child has bangs, use scissors to trim them. Start by cutting them longer than you think you need, and then trim them to the desired length.

Step 7: Check for Evenness

After you have finished cutting, check for evenness. Use the comb to check that the hair is even on both sides, and make any necessary adjustments.

Step 8: Clean Up

Once you have finished cutting, it's time to clean up. Use a brush to remove any loose hairs, and then wash your child's hair again to remove any stray hairs.

Tips for Cutting Hair at Home for Boys

- Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair

- Use the comb to help guide the clippers

- Cut small sections at a time when using scissors

- Check for evenness as you go

- Take breaks if your child is getting antsy

FAQ

Can I use regular scissors?

No, it's important to use hair cutting scissors. Regular scissors can damage the hair and cause split ends.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go shorter or take your child to a professional to fix it.

Hair Care

After cutting your child's hair, it's important to take care of it. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat on the hair. Trim the hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy.

Conclusion

Cutting hair at home for boys can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can give your child a great haircut while saving money. Just remember to take your time, check for evenness, and have fun!
