How To Cut Toddler Boy Hair Long A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Aug 16, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of taking your toddler boy to the hair salon or barber shop every month? Cutting your toddler's hair at home can be a daunting task, especially if you're not familiar with the process. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut toddler boy hair long. By following these tips and tricks, you'll be able to save money and time while giving your toddler a stylish and adorable haircut.

Description

Cutting toddler boy hair long can be a challenging task, especially if your child is wiggly or fussy. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can give your little one a stylish and comfortable haircut. In this article, we'll show you how to cut toddler boy hair long in a relaxed and stress-free manner.

How to Cut Toddler Boy Hair Long: Step by Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your toddler's hair, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to dampen the hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Choose the Right Hairstyle

Before you start cutting, decide on the hairstyle you want for your toddler. Long hair can be styled in different ways, such as a side-swept fringe, a shaggy layered cut, or a classic bowl cut. Look for inspiration online or in magazines and choose a style that suits your child's face shape and hair texture.

Step 3: Dampen the Hair

Spray your toddler's hair with water to make it damp. This will make it easier to cut and ensure a more even result. Use a comb to detangle the hair and part it where you want it to be.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Use your scissors to cut the hair in small sections. Start from the back and work your way to the front. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it to the desired length. Be careful not to cut too much at once, as you can always go back and trim more if necessary.

Step 5: Trim the Bangs

If you're cutting long bangs, trim them separately from the rest of the hair. Use your scissors to cut the bangs straight across or at an angle, depending on the style you want. Make sure they're even and not too short.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

Once you've finished cutting, check for evenness. Use your comb to part the hair and check if both sides are the same length. If not, trim the longer side until it matches the shorter one.

Step 7: Style the Hair

After cutting, style your toddler's hair according to the desired look. You can use a blow dryer and a round brush to add volume or a styling gel to create a messy look. Be creative and have fun!

Tips for Cutting Toddler Boy Hair Long

Here are some additional tips to make the haircutting process easier and more enjoyable for both you and your child:

  • Choose a time when your toddler is well-rested and fed to avoid fussiness.
  • Use a seat belt or a booster seat to keep your child still and safe.
  • Play some music or give your child a toy to distract them during the haircut.
  • Don't rush the process and take breaks as needed.
  • Be patient and gentle, and don't get frustrated if your child moves or cries.

FAQs

How often should I cut my toddler's hair?

This depends on the rate of hair growth and the desired length. On average, toddlers' hair grows about half an inch per month. If you want to maintain a long hairstyle, you can trim the hair every 2-3 months to avoid split ends and maintain the shape.

What if I make a mistake while cutting?

Don't worry if you make a mistake or cut too much. Hair grows back, and you can always take your child to a professional to fix the issue. Alternatively, you can try to style the hair differently to hide the mistake.

How can I make the haircutting experience more enjoyable for my child?

Make the process fun and engaging by playing games, singing songs, or watching a favorite show. You can also let your child choose a special treat or activity as a reward for sitting still during the haircut.

Hair Care for Toddler Boys

After cutting your toddler boy's hair long, it's essential to take care of it to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are some hair care tips for toddler boys:

  • Use a mild shampoo and conditioner to avoid scalp irritation.
  • Brush the hair gently with a soft brush or comb to avoid tangles.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as blow dryers or flat irons, as they can damage the hair.
  • Keep the hair trimmed and neat to avoid split ends and breakage.
  • Protect the hair from the sun and wind by using a hat or a scarf.

By following these tips and techniques, you can cut toddler boy hair long like a pro and give your child a stylish and comfortable haircut. Remember to be patient, gentle, and creative, and enjoy the bonding time with your little one.
