How To Cut Your Child s Hair At Home With Scissors

Written by Oliver Nov 08, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of taking your child to the salon every few weeks for a haircut? With a little practice, you can learn to cut your child's hair at home with scissors. Not only does this save you time and money, but it can also be a fun bonding experience for you and your child. In this article, we'll go over How to Cut Your Child's Hair at Home with Scissors, step-by-step instructions, tips, and FAQs to help you get started.

How to

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or ties
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean. Dull scissors can damage hair and make it harder to cut.

Step 2: Prepare Your Child

Choose a time when your child is calm and relaxed. It's best to cut hair when it's dry, so wait until after a bath or shower. Cover your child with a cape or towel to catch any loose hair.

Step 3: Section the Hair

Use the comb to section off the hair. Start at the top of the head and work your way down. Use hair clips or ties to hold the sections in place.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting at the back of the head, hold a small section of hair between your fingers and cut across in a straight line. Be sure to cut small sections at a time, so you don't accidentally cut too much. Continue working your way around the head, cutting each section of hair.

Step 5: Trim the Bangs

If your child has bangs, use the comb to section them off and cut them straight across. Start in the middle and work your way out to the sides. Be careful not to cut them too short.

Step 6: Blend the Hair

After you've cut all the sections of hair, use the comb to blend the hair together. This will help create a more natural look.

Step 7: Check for Evenness

After you've finished cutting, check for evenness. Make any necessary adjustments.

Step 8: Style the Hair

You're done! Style your child's hair as desired.

Tips

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up.
  • Cut in a well-lit area.
  • Use a spray bottle to mist hair if it becomes dry.
  • Take breaks if your child becomes restless.
  • Watch YouTube tutorials for additional guidance.

FAQ

1. What if I make a mistake?

If you accidentally cut too much hair, don't panic! Try to even out the hair as best you can. If necessary, take your child to a professional stylist to fix the mistake.

2. How often should I cut my child's hair?

This depends on your child's hair growth. On average, children should get a haircut every 6-8 weeks.

3. Can I use regular household scissors to cut my child's hair?

No, it's best to use scissors specifically designed for cutting hair. Household scissors can be dull and damage hair.

Hair Care

After cutting your child's hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using hot tools like hair dryers and straighteners. Encourage your child to drink plenty of water and eat a healthy diet to promote strong, healthy hair.

Cutting your child's hair at home with scissors can be a fun and rewarding experience. With a little practice, you'll be able to create professional-looking haircuts in the comfort of your own home.
