How To Cut Layers Into Short Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Oct 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your short curly hair looking flat and lifeless? Adding layers can give your curls the volume and definition they need to look their best. But cutting layers into curly hair can be tricky, especially if you're doing it at home. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut layers into short curly hair like a pro.

Description

Curly hair can be unpredictable, which is why cutting layers can be a great way to enhance your natural texture. Layers can add volume, shape, and movement to your curls, making your hair look healthier and bouncier. However, cutting layers into short curly hair requires a bit of skill and technique to avoid any mishaps.

How to Cut Layers into Short Curly Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a sharp pair of hair cutting scissors, a fine-tooth comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. Here are the steps to follow:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with dry, detangled hair. Comb through your curls to remove any knots or tangles, and make sure your hair is completely dry.
  2. Section your hair. Use the tip of your comb to create a horizontal parting at the back of your head, and clip the top section away. Repeat at the sides of your head, creating two more horizontal partings.
  3. Decide on your layer length. This will depend on your hair type and face shape. For short curly hair, it's best to start with longer layers and work your way up to shorter ones.
  4. Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer. Hold the hair between your fingers, and cut into the ends at a slight angle. This will create a soft, natural-looking layer. Repeat this step on all the sections of your bottom layer.
  5. Release the middle layer of hair. Comb through it to remove any tangles, and repeat step 4 on each section.
  6. Release the top layer of hair. Comb through it to remove any tangles, and repeat step 4 on each section.
  7. Check for balance. Once you've cut all the layers, check to make sure they're even and balanced. If one side is longer than the other, make small adjustments until everything looks symmetrical.
  8. Style your hair. Now that your layers are cut, style your curls as you normally would. You should notice more volume and definition in your hair.

Tips for Cutting Layers into Short Curly Hair

  • Start with longer layers and work your way up to shorter ones. You can always cut more, but you can't add hair back once it's been cut.
  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to make small adjustments as you go to avoid any major mistakes.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep your hair damp. This will make it easier to cut and prevent any flyaways.
  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your hair at home, consider seeing a professional stylist.

FAQs

Can I cut layers into my curly hair myself?

Yes, you can cut layers into your curly hair yourself, but it's important to be careful and take your time. Start with longer layers and make small adjustments as you go to avoid any major mistakes.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It's recommended to cut curly hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy and bouncy. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls when they're wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your hair after washing to define your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair, as this can cause damage and dryness. Instead, air dry your hair or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or scarf.

Cutting layers into short curly hair can be a great way to enhance your natural texture and add volume and definition to your curls. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut at home. Remember to take your time, start with longer layers, and make small adjustments as you go to avoid any mishaps.
