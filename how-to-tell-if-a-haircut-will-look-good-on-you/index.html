How To Tell If A Haircut Will Look Good On You

Written by William Oct 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you thinking of getting a new haircut but you're not sure if it will look good on you? Don't worry, we're here to help! In this article, we'll give you tips and tricks on how to tell if a haircut will suit your face shape, hair texture, and personal style. Follow our guide and you'll be sure to find the perfect haircut for you!

Table of Contents:

  • Determining Your Face Shape
  • Considering Your Hair Texture
  • Matching Your Personal Style
  • Trying on the Haircut
  • Hair Care Tips
  • FAQs

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in determining whether a haircut will look good on you is to determine your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. Each face shape has specific hairstyles that complement it best. For example, if you have a round face shape, you may want to avoid short, blunt cuts that can make your face appear even rounder. Instead, opt for longer, layered hairstyles that create the illusion of length.

Considering Your Hair Texture

Another important factor to consider when choosing a new haircut is your hair texture. Is your hair straight, wavy, curly, or kinky? Different hair textures require different haircuts to look their best. For example, if you have curly hair, you may want to avoid super short cuts that can make your hair look frizzy and unmanageable. Instead, opt for longer, layered cuts that enhance your natural curls.

Matching Your Personal Style

When choosing a new haircut, it's important to consider your personal style. Do you prefer classic, timeless looks or trendy, edgy styles? Do you like to experiment with bold hair colors or do you prefer natural shades? Your personal style should guide your haircut choices. If you're not sure what your personal style is, take a look at your wardrobe and the hairstyles you've worn in the past. This can give you a good idea of what looks best on you.

Trying on the Haircut

Before you commit to a new haircut, it's a good idea to try it on first. There are several ways to do this. You can try on wigs or use virtual haircut try-on apps to see how the haircut will look on you. Another option is to ask your hairstylist to show you a preview of the haircut by securing your hair in the desired style with clips or elastics. This can give you a good idea of how the haircut will look before you make the cut.

Hair Care Tips

Once you've found the perfect haircut, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but not too often. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type. If you have dry hair, use a moisturizing conditioner. If you have oily hair, use a lightweight, volumizing conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers, straighteners, and curling irons too often. Overuse can damage your hair and make it look dull and lifeless.
  • Get regular trims to keep your haircut looking fresh and healthy.

FAQs

Q: How often should I get a haircut?
A: It depends on your hair type and the style you're going for. Generally, it's a good idea to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your haircut looking fresh.

Q: Can I pull off a short haircut if I have a round face shape?
A: Absolutely! There are plenty of short haircuts that look great on round faces, such as pixie cuts and layered bobs. The key is to choose a cut that flatters your face shape and enhances your best features.

Q: How do I know if a haircut is too trendy for me?
A: If you're not sure if a trendy haircut is right for you, start by trying it on virtually or asking your hairstylist to create a preview. If you like the way it looks and feel confident wearing it, go for it! If not, stick with a more classic, timeless haircut that suits your personal style.

By following these tips and tricks, you'll be able to tell if a haircut will look good on you before you make the cut. Remember to consider your face shape, hair texture, and personal style, and always take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Happy hairstyling!
