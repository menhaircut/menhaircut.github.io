What Causes Round Bald Spots On Your Head

Written by Petter Nov 07, 2023 · 4 min read

If you've noticed round bald spots on your head, there could be a variety of reasons why. In this article, we'll explore some of the common causes of round bald spots and what you can do about them.

Alopecia Areata

Alopecia areata is an autoimmune disorder that causes hair loss in round patches. It occurs when the immune system attacks hair follicles, causing them to shrink and stop producing hair. Alopecia areata can affect anyone, but it is more common in people with a family history of the condition or those with other autoimmune disorders.

Ringworm

Ringworm is a fungal infection that can cause round bald spots on the scalp. It is highly contagious and can be spread through direct contact with infected skin or objects. Other symptoms of ringworm include red, scaly patches on the scalp and itching.

Traction Alopecia

Traction alopecia is hair loss caused by pulling or tension on the hair. It is common in people who wear tight hairstyles, such as braids, weaves, or ponytails. Traction alopecia can cause round bald spots where the hair is pulled tightly.

Scarring Alopecia

Scarring alopecia is a type of hair loss that occurs when hair follicles are destroyed and replaced with scar tissue. It can be caused by a variety of conditions, including infections, autoimmune disorders, and burns. Scarring alopecia can cause round bald spots that may be permanent.

Chemotherapy

Chemotherapy is a common cancer treatment that can cause hair loss. The drugs used in chemotherapy target rapidly dividing cells, such as cancer cells and hair follicles. Chemotherapy-induced hair loss can cause round bald spots on the scalp.

How to Treat Round Bald Spots

The treatment for round bald spots depends on the underlying cause of the hair loss. In some cases, the hair may regrow on its own without treatment. However, if the hair loss is caused by a medical condition, medication or other treatment may be necessary.

Step-by-Step Guide to Treating Round Bald Spots

If you're experiencing round bald spots on your head, follow these steps to treat them:

  1. Consult with a dermatologist or hair loss specialist to determine the underlying cause of the hair loss.
  2. If the hair loss is caused by a medical condition, follow the recommended treatment plan.
  3. If the hair loss is caused by traction alopecia, avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair.
  4. If the hair loss is caused by scarring alopecia, treatment may include steroid injections, topical medications, or surgery.
  5. If the hair loss is caused by chemotherapy, hair may regrow on its own after treatment is complete.

Tips for Preventing Round Bald Spots

Here are some tips for preventing round bald spots:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet to promote hair growth.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools that can damage the hair.
  • Manage stress through exercise, meditation, or other relaxation techniques.

FAQs About Round Bald Spots

Q: Can round bald spots be cured?

A: The treatment for round bald spots depends on the underlying cause of the hair loss. In some cases, the hair may regrow on its own without treatment. However, if the hair loss is caused by a medical condition, medication or other treatment may be necessary.

Q: Is traction alopecia permanent?

A: Traction alopecia can cause permanent hair loss if the hair follicles are damaged beyond repair. However, if the condition is caught early and the hair is no longer pulled tightly, the hair may regrow.

Q: How long does it take for hair to regrow after chemotherapy?

A: Hair may start to regrow a few weeks after chemotherapy treatment ends. However, it may take several months for the hair to grow back fully.

Hair Care for Round Bald Spots

If you have round bald spots on your head, it's important to take care of the hair that remains. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools that can damage the hair.
  • Gently massage the scalp to promote blood flow and stimulate hair growth.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid further damage to the hair.
  • Avoid brushing or combing the hair too vigorously.

Now that you know some of the common causes of round bald spots on the head, you can take steps to prevent and treat them. If you're experiencing hair loss, consult with a dermatologist or hair loss specialist to determine the underlying cause and develop a treatment plan.
