What Hair Colour Would Suit My Skin Tone

Written by Petter Oct 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Welcome to our hair colour guide! Choosing the right hair colour can be a daunting task, especially if you're not sure which shades will suit your skin tone. In this article, we'll be discussing what hair colour would suit your skin tone, as well as tips for maintaining your new hair colour.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How To Determine Your Skin Tone
  3. Step-By-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Maintaining Your Hair Colour
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care Tips

Description

When it comes to choosing the right hair colour for your skin tone, the key is to find shades that complement your natural undertones. Your skin tone can be warm, cool, or neutral, and this will determine which hair colours will suit you best.

Warm skin tones tend to have yellow or golden undertones, while cool skin tones tend to have pink or blue undertones. Neutral skin tones are a mix of warm and cool undertones.

How To Determine Your Skin Tone

One of the easiest ways to determine your skin tone is to look at the veins on the inside of your wrist. If your veins appear greenish, you likely have warm undertones. If they appear blue or purple, you likely have cool undertones. If you have trouble telling, you may have neutral undertones.

Another way to determine your skin tone is to look at the jewellery you wear. If you tend to wear gold jewellery, you likely have warm undertones. If you tend to wear silver jewellery, you likely have cool undertones.

Step-By-Step Guide

Now that you know your skin tone, it's time to choose a hair colour that complements it. Here are some general guidelines:

Warm Skin Tones

If you have warm undertones, hair colours that will suit you best are those with golden or honey tones. Think shades of caramel, copper, and chestnut. Avoid cool or ashy tones, as these can wash you out.

Cool Skin Tones

If you have cool undertones, hair colours that will suit you best are those with cool or ashy tones. Think shades of platinum, ash blonde, and cool brown. Avoid warm, golden tones, as these can clash with your skin tone.

Neutral Skin Tones

If you have neutral undertones, you're lucky! You can pull off a wide range of hair colours, from warm to cool. Just make sure to choose a shade that complements your natural undertones.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hair Colour

Once you've chosen your new hair colour, it's important to take steps to maintain it. Here are some tips:

  • Use a colour-safe shampoo and conditioner to help preserve your new hair colour.
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause your colour to fade.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as this can also cause your colour to fade.
  • Consider using a hair mask or treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and vibrant.

FAQs

Can I dye my hair a different colour if it doesn't suit my skin tone?

Of course! Ultimately, the decision of which hair colour to choose is up to you. If you love a certain hair colour but it doesn't necessarily suit your skin tone, that's okay. Just be aware that it may not look as flattering as a shade that complements your natural undertones.

How often should I touch up my roots?

This depends on how fast your hair grows and how noticeable your roots are. Some people may need to touch up their roots every 4-6 weeks, while others can go longer without needing a touch-up.

Can I dye my hair at home, or should I go to a salon?

If you're new to hair dyeing, we recommend going to a salon. A professional stylist can help you choose the right hair colour for your skin tone and provide expert application to ensure the best possible results. If you're experienced with hair dyeing, you can certainly try it at home, but be sure to follow the instructions carefully and do a patch test to avoid any allergic reactions.

Hair Care Tips

Finally, here are some general hair care tips to keep your locks looking their best:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or brush to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as this can cause damage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a balanced diet with plenty of vitamins and nutrients to promote healthy hair growth.

We hope this article has helped you determine what hair colour would suit your skin tone. Remember, the most important thing is to choose a hair colour that makes you feel confident and beautiful!
