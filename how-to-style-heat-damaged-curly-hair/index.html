How To Style Heat Damaged Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 28, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that heat styling can be both a blessing and a curse. While it can give you the sleek, straight look you desire, it can also cause irreparable damage to your curls. If you find yourself struggling to style your heat damaged curly hair, don't worry. This guide will show you how to get the most out of your curls, even if they've been damaged by heat.

Description

Heat damaged hair refers to hair that has been permanently damaged by exposure to heat. This can occur when you use heat styling tools like straighteners, curling irons, and blow dryers on a regular basis. When you apply heat to your hair, it can cause the protein bonds in your hair to break down, resulting in dry, brittle, and damaged hair.

How to Style Heat Damaged Curly Hair

Styling heat damaged curly hair can be challenging, but it's not impossible. Here are some tips to help you get started:

1. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner:

When you have heat damaged curly hair, it's important to use a shampoo and conditioner that will help to moisturize your hair. Look for products that contain natural oils like argan oil or coconut oil, as these will help to nourish and hydrate your hair.

2. Apply a leave-in conditioner:

After you've washed your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help keep your curls hydrated. This will help to prevent further damage and breakage.

3. Avoid heat styling:

If possible, avoid heat styling your hair altogether. Instead, try air-drying your hair or using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to help define your curls without using heat.

4. Use a wide-tooth comb:

When you're styling your heat damaged curly hair, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your curls. Avoid using a brush, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

5. Try a protective style:

If you're struggling to style your heat damaged curly hair, try a protective style like braids, twists, or a bun. This will help to protect your hair from further damage and give your curls a chance to recover.

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Rinse your hair thoroughly and gently squeeze out any excess water.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. This will help to keep your curls hydrated and prevent further damage.

Step 3: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Gently detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots, being careful not to pull or tug on your hair.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Try air-drying your hair or using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to define your curls. You can also try a protective style like braids, twists, or a bun.

Step 5: Finish with a Hair Oil

Finish by applying a hair oil to your curls. This will help to lock in moisture and give your hair a healthy shine.

Tips for Styling Heat Damaged Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you style your heat damaged curly hair:

1. Avoid using hot tools:

If possible, avoid using hot styling tools like straighteners and curling irons. These tools can cause further damage to your hair and make it more difficult to style.

2. Protect your hair from the sun:

The sun's UV rays can also damage your hair, so be sure to protect your hair when you're outside. Wear a hat or use a hair product that contains SPF.

3. Get regular trims:

Regular trims can help to prevent split ends and breakage, which can make it easier to style your hair.

FAQ

Q: Can I still straighten my heat damaged curly hair?

A: While it's generally not recommended to straighten heat damaged hair, you can still do so if you take the proper precautions. Use a heat protectant spray and a lower heat setting to minimize damage.

Q: How long does it take for heat damaged hair to recover?

A: It can take several months for heat damaged hair to fully recover. Be patient and take good care of your hair during this time.

Q: Should I cut off my heat damaged hair?

A: It's not always necessary to cut off heat damaged hair, but it can help to get rid of split ends and prevent further damage.

Hair Care for Heat Damaged Curly Hair

To keep your heat damaged curly hair looking its best, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some tips:

1. Use a moisturizing hair mask:

Once a week, use a moisturizing hair mask to help hydrate your hair and prevent further damage.

2. Avoid harsh chemicals:

Avoid using harsh chemicals like bleach and ammonia on your hair, as these can cause further damage.

3. Eat a healthy diet:

A healthy diet can help to promote healthy hair growth. Be sure to eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, and protein-rich foods.

4. Protect your hair at night:

Use a silk or satin pillowcase to help prevent breakage and damage to your curls while you sleep.

Conclusion

Styling heat damaged curly hair can be challenging, but with the right techniques and products, it's possible to get the most out of your curls. Remember to avoid heat styling as much as possible, use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and try protective styles to help your hair recover. With a little patience and care, you can have healthy, beautiful curls once again.
