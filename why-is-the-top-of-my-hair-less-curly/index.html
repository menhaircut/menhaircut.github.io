Why Is The Top Of My Hair Less Curly

Written by Aprilia Jun 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you noticing that the top of your hair is less curly than the rest of your hair? This can be a frustrating issue, especially for those with naturally curly hair. In this article, we will explore the reasons why this may be happening and provide tips on how to address the issue.

Description

When it comes to curly hair, it is common for the curls to be tighter at the bottom and looser at the top. This is because the weight of the hair pulls down on the curls, making them appear tighter. However, some people may notice that the top of their hair is significantly less curly than the rest of their hair.

How to

If you are experiencing this issue, there are a few things you can do to help encourage more curl at the top of your hair:

1. Use a Diffuser

When blow-drying your hair, use a diffuser attachment to help distribute heat evenly and encourage curls. Focus on the roots and the top of your hair to help lift and add volume to the curls.

2. Try a Curl Enhancing Product

Using a curl enhancing product can help define and enhance your curls. Apply the product to the top of your hair and scrunch your hair upwards to encourage more curl.

3. Use a Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help reduce friction and prevent your curls from getting flattened while you sleep. This can help maintain the curl pattern at the top of your hair.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to address the issue of less curly hair at the top:

Step 1:

Wash and condition your hair as usual.

Step 2:

Towel dry your hair gently, being careful not to rub your hair too vigorously.

Step 3:

Apply a curl-enhancing product to the top of your hair, focusing on the roots.

Step 4:

Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer and focus on the roots and top of your hair to help lift and add volume to the curls.

Step 5:

Sleep on a satin pillowcase to help maintain the curl pattern.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help maintain curly hair:

1. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness and frizz. Try to wash your hair no more than every other day.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When combing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to avoid pulling and damaging your curls.

3. Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning your hair once a week can help keep your curls hydrated and healthy.

FAQ

Q: Can genetics play a role in the curl pattern of my hair?

A: Yes, genetics can play a role in the curl pattern of your hair. Some people may have naturally looser curls at the top of their hair due to genetics.

Q: Can heat styling tools damage my curls?

A: Yes, heat styling tools can damage your curls if used too often or at high temperatures. It is important to use a heat protectant and limit the use of heat styling tools as much as possible.

Q: Can coloring my hair affect my curls?

A: Yes, coloring your hair can affect the texture and curl pattern of your hair. It is important to use a deep conditioner regularly and limit the use of heat styling tools to help maintain healthy curls.

Hair Care

Maintaining curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right products and techniques, you can help encourage more curl at the top of your hair. Remember to deep condition regularly, limit the use of heat styling tools, and use a wide-tooth comb when combing your hair. With a little patience and care, you can achieve healthy, bouncy curls throughout your entire head of hair.
