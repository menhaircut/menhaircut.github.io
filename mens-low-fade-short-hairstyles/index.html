Mens Low Fade Short Hairstyles The Ultimate Guide

Written by William Nov 29, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking for a trendy and versatile hairstyle that suits your personality? Look no further than the mens low fade short hairstyles! This hairstyle has been gaining popularity among men of all ages, thanks to its clean and classic look. In this post, we’ll provide you with a comprehensive guide on how to get the perfect low fade short hairstyle that suits your face shape, hair type, and personality.

Description

The low fade short hairstyle is a classic men’s haircut that features a gradual taper from the sides to the back of the head, leaving the hair on top longer. The low fade is a popular variation of the fade haircut, which is characterized by a gradual transition from short to long hair. The low fade starts just above the ears and gradually fades into the skin, creating a clean and sharp look. This hairstyle is perfect for men who want a low-maintenance yet stylish look that can be easily customized to their liking.

How to

Getting a low fade short hairstyle is fairly simple and can be done at home or at a salon. Here’s how to do it:

Step 1: Choose the right clipper size

The first step in getting a low fade short hairstyle is to choose the right clipper size. You’ll need a clipper with different guard sizes to achieve the desired length. Typically, a size 2 or 3 guard is used for the sides, and a size 5 or 6 guard is used for the top.

Step 2: Start with the sides

Once you’ve chosen the right clipper size, start by trimming the sides. Begin at the temple and work your way down to the ear, using a shorter guard size as you go down. Make sure to blend the hair on the sides with the hair on the back of the head.

Step 3: Fade into the skin

Next, switch to a shorter guard size and start fading into the skin. Begin at the bottom of the sideburn and work your way up, using shorter guard sizes as you go up. Make sure to blend the hair on the sides with the hair on the back of the head.

Step 4: Trim the top

Once you’ve trimmed the sides, it’s time to trim the top. Use a longer guard size and trim the hair on the top of the head, leaving it longer than the sides. You can also use scissors to trim the hair on the top to your desired length.

Step 5: Style as desired

Finally, style your hair as desired using a styling product, such as pomade or wax. You can choose to keep the hair on top slicked back, textured, or messy, depending on your preference.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when getting a low fade short hairstyle:

  • Choose a reputable hairstylist or barber
  • Communicate clearly about the length and style you want
  • Use a good quality clipper and guard sizes
  • Take your time and be patient
  • Experiment with different styling products to find one that works for you

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about the low fade short hairstyle:

What face shape suits a low fade short hairstyle?

A low fade short hairstyle suits most face shapes, but it looks particularly good on oval, square, and diamond-shaped faces.

How often should I get a low fade short hairstyle?

You should get a low fade short hairstyle every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and length.

Can I style a low fade short hairstyle differently for different occasions?

Yes, you can style a low fade short hairstyle differently for different occasions. For a formal event, you can slick back the hair on top, while for a casual event, you can keep it textured or messy.

Hair Care

To keep your low fade short hairstyle looking sharp and stylish, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a good quality shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair
  • Use a hairbrush or comb to style your hair
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape and length
  • Use a hair trimmer to touch up the sides and back between haircuts

With this comprehensive guide, you’re now ready to rock the mens low fade short hairstyle with confidence and style. Remember to choose the right clipper size, take your time, and experiment with different styling products to find one that works for you. Happy styling!
