Does Hair Still Grow With Alopecia

Written by Daniel Jun 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Alopecia is a medical condition that causes hair loss in different parts of the body, including the scalp. It is a common condition affecting millions of people worldwide. The condition can be caused by genetics, environmental factors, and autoimmune disorders. Hair loss can be a traumatic experience for many people, and it can affect their self-esteem, confidence, and mental health.

Does Hair Still Grow with Alopecia?

The question on many people's minds is whether hair still grows with alopecia. The answer is yes, hair can still grow with alopecia. However, the hair growth rate and pattern can vary depending on the type and severity of alopecia.

Alopecia can be divided into several types, including:

  • Androgenetic Alopecia
  • Alopecia Areata
  • Traction Alopecia
  • Scarring Alopecia

Androgenetic Alopecia

Androgenetic alopecia is a genetic condition that affects both men and women. It is commonly referred to as male or female pattern baldness. The condition is caused by the hormone dihydrotestosterone (DHT), which shrinks hair follicles, leading to hair loss. With this type of alopecia, hair still grows, but the growth rate is slow, and the hair is thinner and shorter than before.

Alopecia Areata

Alopecia areata is an autoimmune disorder that causes patchy baldness on the scalp and other parts of the body. With this type of alopecia, hair can still grow back, but the regrowth rate and pattern can be unpredictable. In some cases, hair may grow back fully, while in others, it may not grow back at all.

Traction Alopecia

Traction alopecia is caused by excessive tension or pulling of the hair, leading to hair loss. This type of alopecia is common in people who wear tight hairstyles, such as braids, weaves, and ponytails. With traction alopecia, hair can still grow back, but it may take time, and the growth rate and pattern can be affected.

Scarring Alopecia

Scarring alopecia is a rare type of alopecia that causes permanent hair loss due to scarring of the hair follicles. With this type of alopecia, hair cannot grow back, and the affected area may have a shiny or smooth appearance.

How to Promote Hair Growth with Alopecia

If you have alopecia and are looking to promote hair growth, there are several things you can do:

  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Use hair growth products, such as minoxidil
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can cause tension on the hair
  • Take supplements, such as biotin and iron
  • Reduce stress levels, as stress can worsen hair loss

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair growth and alopecia:

Can hair still grow with alopecia?

Yes, hair can still grow with alopecia, but the growth rate and pattern can vary depending on the type and severity of alopecia.

Can alopecia be cured?

There is no cure for alopecia, but there are treatments available that can help promote hair growth and manage hair loss.

What causes alopecia?

Alopecia can be caused by genetics, environmental factors, autoimmune disorders, and other medical conditions.

Hair Care Tips for Alopecia

If you have alopecia, it is essential to take care of your hair to promote growth and prevent further hair loss. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Avoid harsh chemicals and treatments that can damage your hair
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid hot styling tools that can damage your hair
  • Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and other environmental factors
  • Wear a wig or hairpiece if you feel self-conscious about your hair loss

Conclusion

If you have alopecia, it is important to know that hair can still grow, but the growth rate and pattern can vary depending on the type and severity of alopecia. By taking care of your hair and seeking treatment, you can promote hair growth and manage hair loss.
