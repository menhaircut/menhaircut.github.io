How To Cut My Own Short Hair In The Back A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Nov 18, 2023 路 4 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to cut your own short hair in the back! Whether you're trying to save money on salon appointments, or you simply prefer to do your own hair, cutting your own hair can be a fun and empowering experience. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of cutting your own short hair in the back, from the tools you'll need to the steps you'll take. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, make sure you have the right tools on hand:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or ties
  • Handheld mirror or full-length mirror

Once you have all of your tools, you're ready to move on to the next step!

Prepping Your Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to prep your hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that your hair is clean and free of any product buildup.
  2. Brush or comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  3. Section your hair. Use hair clips or ties to section your hair into manageable sections. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you don't miss any spots.

Once you've prepped your hair, you're ready to move on to the next step!

Cutting Your Hair

Now it's time to start cutting:

  1. Start with the bottom section. Using your comb, lift a small section of hair from the back of your head. Hold the hair between your fingers and use your scissors to trim the ends. Be careful not to cut too much at once.
  2. Work your way up. Continue lifting small sections of hair and trimming the ends until you reach the top of your head. Be sure to check your work in the mirror as you go.
  3. Blend the sides. If you're using clippers, use a comb to blend the sides with the back. If you're not using clippers, use your scissors to trim the sides so that they blend in with the back.
  4. Check for any missed spots. Use your mirror to check the back of your head for any spots that you may have missed. Touch up as needed.

Once you've finished cutting your hair, you're ready to move on to the next step!

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some commonly asked questions about cutting your own short hair in the back:

Q: Can I cut my hair wet or dry?

A: You can cut your hair either wet or dry, but it may be easier to cut when it's wet.

Q: How much should I trim?

A: It's up to you! If you only want a small trim, you can take off just a little bit. If you want a more dramatic change, you can take off more.

Q: Should I use clippers?

A: It's up to you! Clippers can make it easier to blend the sides with the back, but you can also achieve a similar look with just scissors.

Hair Care Tips

Now that you've cut your own hair, it's important to take care of it:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Use a heat protectant when using styling tools.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.

With these tips, you'll be able to keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful!

In Conclusion

Cutting your own short hair in the back can be a fun and empowering experience. With the right tools and a little bit of patience, you'll be able to achieve the perfect haircut in no time. Remember to take care of your hair after you cut it to keep it looking its best!
