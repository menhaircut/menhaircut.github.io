How Often Should I Pick Out My Hair

Written by Aprilia Jun 29, 2023 ¬∑ 4 min read

As someone who enjoys wearing their hair in a natural or Afro-textured style, it can be difficult to determine how often you should pick out your hair. Picking out your hair too often can lead to breakage and damage, while not picking it out enough can cause matting and tangling. In this article, we'll discuss how often you should pick out your hair and provide tips on how to properly care for it.

Table of Contents

How Often Should I Pick Out My Hair?

The frequency at which you should pick out your hair largely depends on your hair type and the style you're wearing. For those with coarser hair textures or tighter curl patterns, picking out your hair once a week is sufficient. If you have looser curls or straighter hair, you may be able to pick it out every other day or as needed.

It's important to note that picking out your hair too often can cause breakage and damage. When you're combing or picking out your hair, you're manipulating it and putting stress on the strands. This can lead to split ends and breakage, which can be difficult to repair.

On the other hand, not picking out your hair enough can cause matting and tangling. When your hair is left in a style for too long without being manipulated, it can become matted and difficult to detangle. This can be especially problematic for those with coarser hair textures.

How to Pick Out Your Hair

Picking out your hair is a process that requires patience and care. Here are some steps to follow:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with clean, detangled hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to clean your hair, and detangle it using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or detangler to your hair. This will help to soften your hair and make it easier to comb or pick out.
  3. Use a wide-tooth comb or a pick to gently comb or pick out your hair. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots. Be gentle and patient, and don't force the comb or pick through any tangles.
  4. If you encounter a knot or tangle, use your fingers or a small comb to gently loosen it before continuing to comb or pick out your hair.
  5. Once you've picked out your hair, style it as desired. You can wear it in a full afro, a twist-out, or any other style that you like.

Tips for Picking Out Your Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when picking out your hair:

  • Be gentle. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause breakage and damage.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or detangler to make the process easier.
  • Don't pick out your hair too often. Once a week is generally sufficient for most hair types.
  • Consider using a pre-poo treatment before washing your hair. This can help to soften your hair and make it easier to detangle.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can cause damage and breakage.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about picking out natural hair:

Q: Can I pick out my hair every day?

A: It's not recommended to pick out your hair every day, as this can cause breakage and damage. Once a week is generally sufficient for most hair types.

Q: What tools do I need to pick out my hair?

A: You'll need a wide-tooth comb or a pick, a leave-in conditioner or detangler, and possibly a small comb for any knots or tangles.

Q: How can I prevent breakage when picking out my hair?

A: Be gentle and patient when picking out your hair, and avoid pulling or tugging on your strands. Use a leave-in conditioner or detangler to make the process easier.

Hair Care for Natural Hair

Picking out your hair is just one aspect of natural hair care. Here are some additional tips for keeping your hair healthy and strong:

  • Moisturize regularly with a leave-in conditioner or oil.
  • Protect your hair at night with a satin or silk scarf or bonnet.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or relaxers on your hair.
  • Trim your ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote healthy hair growth.

Picking out your hair can be a fun and creative way to express yourself, but it's important to do so in a way that promotes healthy hair growth and prevents damage. Follow the tips and guidelines outlined in this article to keep your hair looking and feeling its best.
