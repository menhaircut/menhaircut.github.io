Can You Have Short Hair With A Big Nose

Written by Eveline Aug 13, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you have a big nose, you may have heard that short haircuts are not a good idea. However, this is not true! You can definitely rock a short hairstyle, no matter the size of your nose. In fact, many short haircuts can actually help balance out your features and draw attention to your eyes and cheekbones.

Description

Having a big nose is nothing to be ashamed of, and it certainly shouldn't limit your hairstyle choices. Short haircuts can be incredibly flattering for all face shapes, including those with larger noses. The key is to choose a style that complements your features and makes you feel confident and beautiful.

How to Choose the Right Short Haircut for Your Big Nose

When it comes to choosing a short haircut for a big nose, there are a few things to keep in mind:

Consider Your Face Shape

The right haircut for your big nose will depend largely on your face shape. Oval faces can generally pull off any short haircut, while round faces may benefit from a cut with more volume on top to balance out the nose. Heart-shaped faces can look great with a pixie cut or a bob that hits just below the chin.

Think About Your Hair Texture

Your hair texture can also play a role in choosing the right short haircut. If you have fine hair, a layered bob or a pixie cut can add volume and texture. If you have thick, curly hair, a short undercut or a tapered cut can help keep your hair under control and showcase your features.

Consider Your Personal Style

Ultimately, the most important factor in choosing a short haircut is your personal style. Do you prefer a classic, polished look? Or are you more of a rocker chick? There are plenty of short haircuts that can fit any style, so don't be afraid to experiment and find a look that makes you feel like your best self.

Step by Step: How to Get a Short Haircut

If you're ready to take the plunge and get a short haircut, here's what you can expect:

Step 1: Choose Your Style

Before you head to the salon, have a few styles in mind that you're interested in. You can bring pictures to show your stylist, or simply describe the cut you're looking for. Your stylist can help you choose the best style for your face shape and hair texture.

Step 2: Get a Consultation

Once you arrive at the salon, your stylist will likely give you a consultation to discuss your hair goals and preferences. They may also take a look at your hair texture and face shape to make sure the style you've chosen will work for you.

Step 3: Get the Cut

Once you and your stylist have agreed on a style, it's time to get the cut! Your stylist will likely start by washing and conditioning your hair, then sectioning it off and cutting it in layers. They may also use clippers or scissors to create a tapered or undercut look.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

After your cut is complete, your stylist will likely style your hair using a blow dryer, flat iron, or curling iron. They may also use styling products like mousse, gel, or hairspray to help your hair hold its shape and texture.

Tips for Maintaining Your Short Haircut

Short haircuts can be low-maintenance, but they still require some upkeep to look their best. Here are a few tips for maintaining your new 'do:

Get Regular Trims

To keep your short haircut looking sharp, it's important to get regular trims every 4-6 weeks. This will help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy and shiny.

Use the Right Products

Using the right hair products can make a big difference in how your short haircut looks and feels. Look for products that are designed for your hair type and texture, and avoid products that are too heavy or greasy.

Experiment with Styling

Short haircuts are incredibly versatile, so don't be afraid to experiment with different styling techniques. Try using a flat iron for a sleek, polished look, or use a curling iron to add volume and texture.

FAQs

Q: Will a short haircut make my nose look bigger?

A: Not necessarily! In fact, many short haircuts can actually help balance out your features and draw attention to your eyes and cheekbones. The key is to choose a style that complements your face shape and makes you feel confident and beautiful.

Q: What if I don't like my short haircut?

A: If you're not happy with your short haircut, don't panic! Many hairstyles can be modified or tweaked to better suit your preferences. Talk to your stylist about what you're unhappy with, and they can work with you to make adjustments.

Q: Can I still style my short hair if I have a big nose?

A: Absolutely! Short haircuts are incredibly versatile and can be styled in a variety of ways. Experiment with different techniques and products to find a look that makes you feel confident and beautiful.

Hair Care for Short Haircuts

Short haircuts require some special hair care to keep them looking their best. Here are a few tips:

Wash Your Hair Less

Short haircuts don't require as much washing as longer hair, so try to wash your hair every 2-3 days instead of every day. This will help prevent your hair from drying out and becoming brittle.

Use a Gentle Shampoo

When you do wash your hair, use a gentle shampoo that's designed for your hair type and texture. Avoid shampoos that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates, as these can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Condition Your Hair Regularly

Conditioner is essential for keeping short haircuts looking shiny and healthy. Use a high-quality conditioner every time you wash your hair, and consider using a leave-in conditioner for added moisture and protection.

Avoid Heat Damage

Short haircuts can be styled using heat tools like flat irons and curling irons, but be careful not to overdo it. Use these tools sparingly, and always use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage and breakage.

With the right haircut and hair care routine, you can absolutely rock a short hairstyle with a big nose. Don't let anyone tell you otherwise!
