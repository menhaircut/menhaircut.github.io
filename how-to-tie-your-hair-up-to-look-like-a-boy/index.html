How To Tie Your Hair Up To Look Like A Boy A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 06, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you looking for a new hairstyle that will make you look more masculine? Tying your hair up in a way that mimics a boy's hairstyle can be a great option. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve this look, as well as provide some tips for maintaining it.

How to:

Step 1: Start by brushing your hair back and gathering it at the nape of your neck. Use a hair tie to secure it in a low ponytail.

Step 2: Next, take a second hair tie and place it about an inch above the first one. This will create a small "bubble" of hair between the two ties.

Step 3: Use your fingers to create a small hole in the hair above the second tie. Flip the ponytail up and tuck it down through the hole, creating a twist in the hair.

Step 4: Tighten the second hair tie around the twist, securing it in place.

Step 5: Use bobby pins to secure any loose strands or flyaways.

Tips:

- If you have shorter hair, you may need to use more hair ties to create a similar effect.

- Experiment with the placement of the second hair tie to create different looks.

- Use a small amount of hair gel or pomade to slick back any shorter hairs around your face.

FAQ:

Q: Will tying my hair up like this damage it?

A: As long as you're not pulling your hair too tightly, this hairstyle shouldn't cause any damage. However, if you're experiencing any discomfort or scalp pain, it's best to loosen the hair tie and give your hair a break.

Q: Can I wear this hairstyle to work or school?

A: That depends on your workplace or school's dress code. If you're unsure, it's best to check with your employer or school administration before trying out this hairstyle.

Hair Care:

To keep your hair healthy while wearing this style, it's important to make sure you're taking proper care of it. This includes:

- Washing your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner

- Using a heat protectant spray if you use hot tools on your hair

- Avoiding tight hairstyles that can cause breakage or damage

- Getting regular trims to keep split ends at bay

- Using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to tie your hair up to look like a boy in no time. Whether you're looking for a new style or just want to switch things up, this hairstyle is a great option. Just remember to take care of your hair, so it stays healthy and strong.
