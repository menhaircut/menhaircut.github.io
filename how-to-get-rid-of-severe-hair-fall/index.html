How To Get Rid Of Severe Hair Fall A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Oct 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're experiencing severe hair fall, you're not alone. Hair fall is a common problem that affects millions of people worldwide. While some hair fall is normal, excessive shedding can be a cause for concern. In this guide, we'll explore the causes of severe hair fall and provide you with tips on how to get rid of it.

Table of Contents

  1. Causes of Severe Hair Fall
  2. How to Get Rid of Severe Hair Fall
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips to Prevent Hair Fall
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care Tips

Causes of Severe Hair Fall

Before we dive into how to get rid of severe hair fall, it's important to understand the causes. Some common causes of severe hair fall include:

  • Genetics
  • Hormonal changes
  • Stress
  • Poor nutrition
  • Medical conditions
  • Medications

Identifying the cause of your hair fall is the first step in addressing the problem. If you're unsure about the cause, it's always a good idea to consult with a healthcare professional.

How to Get Rid of Severe Hair Fall

Now that you understand the causes of severe hair fall, let's explore how to get rid of it.

1. Improve Your Diet

One of the best ways to prevent hair fall is to improve your diet. Eating a well-balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help strengthen your hair and prevent breakage. Foods that are good for your hair include:

  • Leafy greens
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Fatty fish
  • Eggs
  • Lean meats
  • Whole grains

Make sure to also drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated.

2. Reduce Stress

Stress can cause hair fall, so it's important to manage your stress levels. Some ways to reduce stress include:

  • Meditation
  • Yoga
  • Exercise
  • Deep breathing
  • Journaling

3. Use Gentle Hair Products

Harsh chemicals in hair products can damage your hair and cause it to fall out. Use gentle, natural hair products that are free of sulfates and parabens to keep your hair healthy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to get rid of severe hair fall:

  1. Identify the cause of your hair fall.
  2. Improve your diet by eating plenty of fruits, vegetables, and lean protein.
  3. Reduce stress by practicing relaxation techniques.
  4. Use gentle, natural hair products.
  5. Consider taking a hair supplement, such as biotin or collagen.
  6. Avoid heat styling tools, such as curling irons and straighteners.
  7. Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  8. Avoid tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails.
  9. Massage your scalp to improve blood flow.
  10. Consult with a healthcare professional if your hair fall persists.

Tips to Prevent Hair Fall

Here are some additional tips to prevent hair fall:

  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair.
  • Avoid using a towel to dry your hair. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet.
  • Avoid using hair elastics with metal clasps.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

FAQs

How much hair fall is normal?

It's normal to lose between 50-100 hairs per day.

Can hair fall be reversed?

In some cases, hair fall can be reversed by addressing the underlying cause. However, it's important to consult with a healthcare professional to determine the cause of your hair fall.

What are some natural remedies for hair fall?

Some natural remedies for hair fall include using coconut oil, onion juice, and aloe vera.

Hair Care Tips

Follow these hair care tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo.
  • Condition your hair after every wash.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair.
  • Protect your hair from chlorine by wearing a swim cap.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

In conclusion, severe hair fall can be a frustrating problem to deal with. However, by following the steps outlined in this guide, you can take control of your hair health and prevent further hair fall. Remember to consult with a healthcare professional if your hair fall persists.
