How To Start Off Dreads With Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 07, 2023 · 3 min read

Starting off dreads with long hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a rewarding experience. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the process of starting off dreads with long hair, from the preparation stage to maintenance tips. By the end of this article, you’ll be well-equipped to start your own dreadlocks journey.

Table of Contents

  • Preparation
  • Sectioning and Backcombing
  • Twisting and Palm Rolling
  • Maintenance Tips
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care

Preparation

Before you start your dreadlocks journey, there are a few things you need to do to prepare your hair. Firstly, make sure your hair is clean and free from any products. You can use a clarifying shampoo to remove any build-up. Secondly, decide on the size of your dreads. If you have long hair, you may want to consider larger dreads. Lastly, gather all the tools you need, including a comb, hair ties, and possibly a dreadlock wax.

Sectioning and Backcombing

The first step in starting off dreads with long hair is to section your hair. Use a comb to create even sections throughout your hair. Once you’ve sectioned your hair, you can start backcombing. Backcombing involves teasing the hair in each section towards the scalp. This creates a tangled, knotted base for your dreads. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Twisting and Palm Rolling

After you’ve backcombed your hair, you can start twisting and palm rolling. Twisting involves twisting each section of hair in one direction until it starts to coil. Palm rolling involves rolling each dread between your palms to smooth out any bumps and encourage the hair to lock together. Repeat this process for each dread.

Maintenance Tips

Maintaining your dreads is just as important as starting them off. Here are a few tips to keep your dreads looking their best:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo
  • Use a crochet hook to pull in loose hairs and tighten up your dreads
  • Avoid using heavy products or conditioners
  • Avoid using heat on your dreads

FAQs

How long does it take to start off dreads with long hair?

The time it takes to start off dreads with long hair varies depending on your hair type and the size of your dreads. On average, it can take anywhere from a few hours to a few days.

Can I start off dreads with long hair on my own?

Yes, you can start off dreads with long hair on your own. However, it may be easier to have someone help you with the backcombing process.

Hair Care

When you have dreadlocks, it’s important to take care of your hair to prevent breakage and maintain healthy locks. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent friction
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your dreads
  • Trim the ends of your dreads regularly to prevent split ends

Starting off dreads with long hair can be a fun and exciting journey. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve the look you want and keep your dreads looking healthy and beautiful.
