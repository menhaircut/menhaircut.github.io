How To Cover Up Thinning Crown Tips And Tricks

Written by Daniel Jul 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Dealing with a thinning crown can be frustrating and can make you feel self-conscious. However, it is a common problem that many people face. If you're looking for ways to cover up your thinning crown, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll provide you with some tips and tricks to help you hide your thinning crown and feel confident again.

Description

Thinning crown is a common problem that can happen as you age or due to other factors like genetics, stress, hormonal changes, or certain medical conditions. It is characterized by hair loss in the area at the top of your head, where the hair tends to thin out and become more visible. This can be a frustrating experience, but there are ways to cover up your thinning crown and regain your confidence.

How to Cover Up Thinning Crown

There are several ways to cover up your thinning crown, depending on the severity of your hair loss and your personal preference. Here are some tips to get started:

1. Change Your Hairstyle

One of the easiest ways to cover up your thinning crown is by changing your hairstyle. Opt for a shorter haircut with layers, which can add volume and texture to your hair, making it look fuller. Avoid slicked-back styles or tight braids that can pull your hair and make your scalp more visible.

2. Use Hair Fibers

Hair fibers are a quick and easy way to cover up your thinning crown. These are tiny fibers that mimic the look of real hair and attach to your existing hair to make it look thicker and fuller. Simply sprinkle the fibers on your thinning areas and brush gently to blend.

3. Try Hairpieces

If you're looking for a more permanent solution, consider wearing a hairpiece like a wig or a hair topper. These can be custom-made to match your hair color, texture, and style, and can cover up your thinning crown completely. Hairpieces can be a bit pricey, but they are worth the investment if you want a natural-looking and long-lasting solution.

4. Use Hair Extensions

Hair extensions can also help cover up your thinning crown by adding length and volume to your hair. Opt for clip-in extensions or weaves that blend seamlessly with your natural hair and avoid extensions that can pull your hair and cause more damage.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to cover up your thinning crown:

Step 1: Shampoo and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. This can help add texture and thickness to your hair, making it easier to style.

Step 2: Blow-dry Your Hair

Use a blow dryer to dry your hair while lifting the roots with a round brush. This can help add volume to your hair and make it look fuller.

Step 3: Apply Hair Fibers

If you're using hair fibers, apply them to your thinning areas by sprinkling them on and gently brushing them to blend. Make sure to use a fiber spray to lock the fibers in place.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired, using a volumizing mousse or hairspray to hold the style in place. Avoid using heavy products that can weigh your hair down and make it look flat.

Tips for Covering Up Thinning Crown

Here are some additional tips to help you cover up your thinning crown:

1. Avoid Overstyling

Avoid overstyling your hair with heat tools like flat irons or curling irons, which can damage your hair and make it look thinner. Instead, let your hair air-dry or use a low-heat setting on your blow dryer.

2. Protect Your Hair

Protect your hair from damage by using a heat protectant spray before using heat tools and wearing a hat or scarf when exposed to the sun or harsh weather.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth. Include foods like leafy greens, nuts, seeds, and fish in your diet.

4. Manage Stress

Manage your stress levels through meditation, yoga, or other relaxation techniques. Stress can cause hair loss, so it's important to take care of your mental and emotional health.

FAQ

1. Can hair fibers damage my hair?

No, hair fibers are safe to use and do not damage your hair. They simply attach to your existing hair to make it look thicker and fuller.

2. How long do hair fibers last?

Hair fibers can last up to 24 hours, depending on the brand and the amount you use. Make sure to use a fiber spray to lock the fibers in place.

3. Can I swim or shower with a hairpiece?

Yes, you can swim or shower with a hairpiece, but make sure to use a waterproof adhesive or tape to keep it in place. Avoid exposing your hairpiece to saltwater or chlorine, which can damage the hair fibers.

Hair Care for Thinning Crown

To prevent further hair loss and promote healthy hair growth, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that is free of sulfates and other harsh chemicals that can strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Massage Your Scalp

Massage your scalp regularly to improve blood circulation and stimulate hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp in circular motions for a few minutes every day.

3. Use a Hair Growth Serum

Use a hair growth serum that contains ingredients like biotin, caffeine, and saw palmetto to promote healthy hair growth and prevent hair loss.

4. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles like ponytails, braids, or buns that can pull your hair and cause damage to your scalp.

By following these tips and tricks, you can cover up your thinning crown and feel confident again. Remember to take care of your hair and scalp to prevent further hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.
