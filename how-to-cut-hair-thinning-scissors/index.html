How To Cut Hair With Thinning Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Nov 03, 2023 · 5 min read

Thinning scissors are a great tool for removing bulk from hair and creating texture. Cutting hair with thinning scissors can be a bit intimidating, but with the right technique and tools, anyone can do it. In this article, we’ll provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut hair with thinning scissors, along with some tips and FAQs.

  1. How to Cut Hair with Thinning Scissors
  2. Step-by-Step Guide

  5. Hair Care after Cutting with Thinning Scissors

How to Cut Hair with Thinning Scissors

Thinning scissors have a serrated edge that removes some of the hair without creating a straight line. This tool is perfect for creating layers and texture in hair without losing too much length. Here are the basics of how to cut hair with thinning scissors:

Step 1: Choose the Right Scissors

The first step is to choose the right thinning scissors. There are different types of thinning scissors available, and each one has a specific purpose. Look for scissors with around 30 teeth for general thinning, and those with fewer teeth for more specific tasks. Ensure that the blades are sharp and in good condition before starting.

Step 2: Wash and Dry the Hair

Before you start cutting, it is essential to wash and dry the hair. This will ensure that the hair is clean and free from any product buildup, which can affect the cutting process. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to prepare the hair for cutting.

Step 3: Section the Hair

Section the hair into small sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don't miss any areas. Start from the bottom and work your way up.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Hold the thinning scissors vertically and cut the hair in a zig-zag pattern. This will create texture and remove some of the bulk. Start at the ends and work your way up, taking small sections at a time. It is important to be cautious and not remove too much hair at once.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you have cut all the sections, use normal scissors to blend the hair. This will ensure that the hair is even and has a natural look. Cut any stray strands and give the hair a final trim.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a detailed step-by-step guide on how to cut hair with thinning scissors:

Step 1: Gather the Tools

Before you start, make sure you have all the tools you need. You'll need a good quality pair of thinning scissors, hair clips, a comb, normal scissors, and a mirror.

Step 2: Wash and Dry the Hair

Wash and condition the hair with a good quality shampoo and conditioner. Dry the hair using a hairdryer or let it air dry naturally.

Step 3: Section the Hair

Section the hair using hair clips. Start from the bottom and work your way up, making sure that all areas are covered.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Hold the thinning scissors vertically and cut the hair in a zig-zag pattern. Take small sections at a time and be cautious not to remove too much hair. Start from the ends and work your way up.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you have cut all the sections, use normal scissors to blend the hair. This will ensure that the hair is even and has a natural look. Cut any stray strands and give the hair a final trim.

Tips for Using Thinning Scissors

Here are some tips for using thinning scissors:

  • Choose the right type of thinning scissors for the task.
  • Hold the scissors vertically for a more natural look.
  • Don't remove too much hair at once.
  • Start from the ends and work your way up.
  • Blend the hair with normal scissors after using thinning scissors.

FAQs on Cutting Hair with Thinning Scissors

Q: Can I use thinning scissors on any hair type?

A: Thinning scissors work best on thick and coarse hair. If you have fine hair, it's best to avoid the use of thinning scissors as it can create a choppy and uneven look.

Q: How often should I use thinning scissors?

A: It depends on the length and thickness of your hair. Thinning scissors can be used every 4-6 weeks to maintain the texture and remove bulk.

Q: Can I use thinning scissors on wet hair?

A: It's best to use thinning scissors on dry hair as it gives more control and precision. Wet hair can be slippery and difficult to work with.

Hair Care after Cutting with Thinning Scissors

After cutting your hair with thinning scissors, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and texture. Here are some tips:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much heat on the hair, as it can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish and strengthen the hair.
  • Trim the hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and texture.

By following these tips, you can enjoy healthy and textured hair that looks great.

Conclusion

Thinning scissors are a great tool for creating texture and removing bulk from hair. By following the steps outlined in this guide, you can cut your hair with thinning scissors like a pro. Remember to choose the right type of scissors, section the hair, and use a zig-zag pattern to create texture. Blend the hair with normal scissors and take care of it with proper hair care. With these tips, you'll have healthy and textured hair that looks great.
