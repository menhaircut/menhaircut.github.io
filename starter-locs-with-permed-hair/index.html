Starter Locs With Permed Hair How To Get Your Dream Locs

Are you a naturalista with permed hair who wants to try out starter locs? That's great news! You can achieve your dream hair even with permed hair, and this article will guide you on how to get started. Here's what we'll cover in this blog post:

Description

Starter locs are a popular style for people looking to transition from loose natural hair to locs. The process involves sectioning your hair and twisting it until it forms a small, coiled dreadlock. However, if you have permed hair, the process can be a bit more complicated. Permed hair is chemically treated, making it fragile and prone to breakage. This means that you need to take extra care when starting your locs to avoid damaging your hair.

How to Get Starter Locs with Permed Hair

The first step in getting starter locs with permed hair is to find a professional stylist who specializes in locs. The stylist should have experience working with permed hair and should be able to guide you on the best approach. They will also help you choose the size and length of your starter locs.

Once you've found a stylist, the next step is to prepare your hair. Permed hair is delicate, so you need to ensure that it's in the best possible condition before starting the locs. This means deep conditioning your hair and trimming off any split ends. You should also avoid using any harsh chemicals or heat styling tools, as these can weaken your hair and make it more prone to breakage.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to get starter locs with permed hair:

  1. Deep condition your hair to ensure that it's in the best possible condition.
  2. Trim off any split ends to prevent them from weakening your hair.
  3. Choose a professional stylist who specializes in locs and has experience working with permed hair.
  4. Consult with the stylist on the size and length of your starter locs.
  5. Section your hair and twist it until it forms a small, coiled dreadlock.
  6. Use a locking gel or wax to help the hair hold its shape.
  7. Repeat the process until all your hair is twisted into starter locs.

Tips for Maintaining Starter Locs with Permed Hair

Maintaining starter locs with permed hair can be challenging, but it's not impossible. Here are a few tips to keep your locs looking their best:

  • Avoid using any harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and strong.
  • Wash your hair regularly to prevent buildup and maintain scalp health.
  • Use a light oil or moisturizer to keep your locs hydrated.
  • Avoid twisting your hair too tightly, as this can cause breakage.

FAQs

Can you get starter locs with permed hair?

Yes, you can get starter locs with permed hair, but you need to take extra care to avoid damaging your hair.

How long does it take to start locs with permed hair?

The time it takes to start locs with permed hair varies depending on the length and thickness of your hair. On average, it takes between 6-12 months for the locs to fully form.

Can you style starter locs with permed hair?

Yes, you can style starter locs with permed hair. However, you need to be gentle and avoid using any harsh chemicals or heat styling tools.

Hair Care for Starter Locs with Permed Hair

Proper hair care is essential when starting locs with permed hair. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and strong.
  • Use a light oil or moisturizer to keep your locs hydrated.
  • Avoid using any harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair.
  • Wash your hair regularly to prevent buildup and maintain scalp health.
  • Avoid twisting your hair too tightly, as this can cause breakage.

With these tips, you can achieve your dream locs even with permed hair. Remember to take extra care of your hair and consult with a professional stylist to ensure that your locs are healthy and strong.
