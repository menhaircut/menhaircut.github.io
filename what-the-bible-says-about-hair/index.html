What The Bible Says About Hair

Written by Oliver Aug 14, 2023 · 4 min read

The Bible has a lot to say about hair, from the length to the style to the color. For many people, their hair is an important part of their identity, and the Bible recognizes this. In this article, we will explore what the Bible says about hair and how we can apply these teachings to our own lives.

Table of Contents

  1. Hair Length
  2. Hair Style
  3. Hair Color
  4. Hair Care
  5. Conclusion

Hair Length

One of the most well-known verses in the Bible about hair is found in 1 Corinthians 11:15, which says, "But if a woman has long hair, it is her glory? For her hair is given to her for a covering." This verse implies that women should have long hair, which was a common cultural practice in biblical times.

However, it is important to note that this verse does not mean that women must always have long hair. In fact, some women may prefer to have shorter hair for practical or personal reasons. The key takeaway from this verse is that hair is an important part of a woman's identity and should be treated with respect and care.

Hair Style

The Bible does not prescribe specific hairstyles, but it does offer some guidance on how we should approach our hair. In 1 Peter 3:3-4, it says, "Your beauty should not come from outward adornment, such as elaborate hairstyles and the wearing of gold jewelry or fine clothes. Rather, it should be that of your inner self, the unfading beauty of a gentle and quiet spirit, which is of great worth in God's sight."

This verse reminds us that our true beauty comes from within, and that we should not place too much emphasis on our outward appearance. While it is fine to experiment with different hairstyles and haircuts, we should always remember that our true worth lies in our character and our relationship with God.

Hair Color

The Bible does not specifically address hair color, but it does offer some principles that we can apply to this topic. In Leviticus 19:27, it says, "Do not cut the hair at the sides of your head or clip off the edges of your beard." This verse may seem unrelated to hair color, but it does suggest that we should not alter our natural appearance too drastically.

Some people choose to dye their hair for cosmetic reasons, and there is nothing inherently wrong with this. However, we should be careful not to become too attached to our appearance or to rely on our looks for our self-worth. As with hairstyles, our true worth lies in our inner selves and our relationship with God.

Hair Care

The Bible also offers some guidance on how we should care for our hair. In Luke 7:44, it says, "Then he turned toward the woman and said to Simon, 'Do you see this woman? I came into your house. You did not give me any water for my feet, but she wet my feet with her tears and wiped them with her hair.'" This verse shows us that hair can be a symbol of humility and service.

When we take care of our hair, we are not just doing it for ourselves, but for others as well. We can use our hair to serve and bless others, whether by donating it to people who have lost their hair due to illness or by simply taking good care of it and keeping it clean and well-groomed.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the Bible has a lot to say about hair, from the length to the style to the color. While it is important to take care of our appearance, we should always remember that our true worth lies in our character and our relationship with God. By following the principles outlined in the Bible, we can use our hair to serve and bless others and to honor God with our whole selves.
