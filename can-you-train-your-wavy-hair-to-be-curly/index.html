Can You Train Your Wavy Hair To Be Curly

Written by Frank Jun 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your wavy hair and want to achieve those bouncy curls? While some people are born with curly hair, others need to put in some effort to achieve the desired results. The good news is that it is possible to train your wavy hair to be curly with the right techniques and hair care routine. In this blog post, we will discuss everything you need to know about training your wavy hair to be curly.

Description

Wavy hair is often described as hair that falls between straight and curly. While wavy hair looks beautiful, some people prefer to have curly hair. However, it is important to understand that not all wavy hair can be trained to be curly. The success of training your wavy hair to be curly depends on the texture and natural curl pattern of your hair.

It is also important to note that the process of training your wavy hair to be curly can take time and requires patience. You will need to follow a consistent hair care routine and use the right styling products and tools to achieve the desired results.

How to Train Your Wavy Hair to Be Curly

Training your wavy hair to be curly requires a combination of techniques, tools, and products. Here are some ways to train your wavy hair to be curly:

  1. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your natural waves and create more defined curls.
  2. Apply a curl-enhancing product such as mousse or gel to your damp hair and scrunch it upwards towards your scalp to encourage curl formation.
  3. Try hair plopping, which involves wrapping your wet hair in a t-shirt or microfiber towel to enhance your curls and reduce frizz.
  4. Use a curling iron or wand to create more defined curls in specific areas of your hair.
  5. Consider getting a perm or using a curl-enhancing treatment to permanently or semi-permanently transform your wavy hair into curls.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're ready to start training your wavy hair to be curly, here is a step-by-step guide to follow:

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  2. Apply a curl-enhancing product such as mousse or gel to your damp hair.
  3. Scrunch your hair upwards towards your scalp to encourage curl formation.
  4. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your natural waves and create more defined curls.
  5. Finish with a hair spray or serum to hold your curls in place and reduce frizz.

Tips for Training Your Wavy Hair to Be Curly

Here are some tips to help you train your wavy hair to be curly:

  • Be patient and consistent with your hair care routine. It may take some time to see results.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often as they can damage your hair and make it harder to train your hair to be curly.
  • Invest in a high-quality diffuser attachment for your hair dryer to enhance your natural waves and create more defined curls.
  • Choose curl-enhancing products that work well with your hair type and natural curl pattern.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and avoid split ends.

FAQs

1. Can all wavy hair be trained to be curly?

No, the success of training your wavy hair to be curly depends on the texture and natural curl pattern of your hair.

2. How long does it take to train wavy hair to be curly?

The process of training your wavy hair to be curly can take time and requires patience. It may take several weeks or months to see results.

Hair Care Routine for Wavy Hair

Here is a hair care routine to follow if you have wavy hair:

  1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  2. Avoid washing your hair every day as it can dry out your hair and make it harder to train your hair to be curly.
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to your hair to keep it moisturized.
  4. Avoid using heat styling tools too often as they can damage your hair and make it harder to train your hair to be curly.
  5. Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and avoid split ends.
  6. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair instead of a brush to avoid disrupting your natural waves.

In conclusion, it is possible to train your wavy hair to be curly with the right techniques, tools, and products. However, it is important to understand that not all wavy hair can be trained to be curly, and the success of the process depends on the texture and natural curl pattern of your hair. Be patient, consistent, and follow a healthy hair care routine to achieve the desired results.
