How To Wear A Maxi Dress That s Too Long

Written by William Jul 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a fan of maxi dresses, you know how comfortable and stylish they can be. But sometimes, you might find yourself with a dress that's just a little too long for you. Don't worry, though - there are plenty of ways to wear a maxi dress that's too long and still look great. In this post, we'll go over some tips and tricks for making the most of your too-long maxi dress.

Description

Maxi dresses are long, flowing dresses that are perfect for a variety of occasions. They come in a variety of styles, from casual to formal, and can be worn year-round. However, if you're shorter than average or just have a shorter torso, you might find that your maxi dress is too long for you. This can be frustrating, but there are ways to make it work.

How to

Step 1: Hem it

The most obvious solution to a maxi dress that's too long is to hem it. If you have basic sewing skills, you can easily hem your dress yourself. Simply measure how much you need to take off and use a sewing machine or needle and thread to hem the dress to the desired length. If you're not comfortable with sewing, you can also take your dress to a tailor and have it professionally hemmed.

Step 2: Wear heels

If you don't want to alter your dress, you can also wear heels to make up for the extra length. A pair of platform sandals or high heels can add a few inches to your height and make your dress look less overwhelming. Just be sure to choose shoes that are comfortable enough to wear for an extended period of time.

Step 3: Belt it

If your dress is too long but fits well otherwise, you can also try belting it. A wide belt worn at the waist can help to define your silhouette and make your dress look more proportionate. You can also experiment with different types of belts, such as a skinny belt or a chain belt, to see what works best for your outfit.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips for wearing a maxi dress that's too long:

  • Choose the right fabric. Flowy, lightweight fabrics like cotton and chiffon are great for maxi dresses, but they can also be more prone to dragging on the ground. Choose a heavier fabric, like jersey or knit, that will drape nicely without getting caught under your feet.
  • Accessorize strategically. A statement necklace or pair of earrings can draw the eye up and away from the length of your dress. You can also try adding a scarf or hat to your outfit to balance out the length.
  • Experiment with layering. If you're still feeling self-conscious about the length of your dress, try layering it with a denim jacket, cardigan, or blazer. This can help to break up the length and give your outfit more dimension.

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about wearing a maxi dress that's too long:

Q: Can I wear a maxi dress if I'm short?

A: Absolutely! Maxi dresses can be flattering on all body types, regardless of height. Just be mindful of the length and make sure your dress doesn't drag on the ground.

Q: Do I have to wear heels with a maxi dress?

A: No, you don't have to wear heels if you don't want to. Flats, sandals, and even sneakers can all work well with a maxi dress, depending on the occasion and your personal style.

Q: What should I do if my maxi dress is too long and too big?

A: If your dress is both too long and too big, you might need to have it altered by a tailor. They can take in the sides and shorten the length to make the dress fit you properly.

Hair Care

When it comes to hair, there are no set rules for wearing a maxi dress that's too long. However, you might want to consider wearing your hair up or in a sleek, pulled-back style to balance out the length of your dress. If you're wearing statement earrings or a necklace, you might want to show them off with an updo or half-up hairstyle. Alternatively, you can keep your hair down and add a headband or hair accessory to complete your look.

With these tips and tricks, you can confidently wear a maxi dress that's too long and still look great. Whether you choose to hem it, wear heels, or try something else entirely, the key is to find what works for you and your personal style.
