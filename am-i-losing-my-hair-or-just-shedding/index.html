Am I Losing My Hair Or Just Shedding

Written by William Aug 20, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you finding more hair in your brush or shower drain than usual? It's normal to shed around 50-100 hairs per day, but if you're seeing more than that, you may be wondering if you're losing your hair. In this article, we'll discuss the difference between hair shedding and hair loss, and what you can do to take care of your hair.

What's the Difference Between Hair Shedding and Hair Loss?

Hair shedding is a natural process where old hair falls out and new hair grows in its place. It's a part of the hair growth cycle that everyone experiences. Hair loss, on the other hand, is when hair falls out and doesn't grow back. This can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, hormonal changes, stress, and certain medical conditions.

How to Tell if You're Experiencing Hair Loss

If you're not sure if you're experiencing hair shedding or hair loss, there are a few things you can look for:

  • Thinning hair
  • Bald spots
  • Hair that falls out easily
  • An itchy or painful scalp
  • Changes in your hairline or part

If you're experiencing any of these symptoms, it's a good idea to see a doctor or dermatologist to get a proper diagnosis.

How to Reduce Hair Shedding

While hair shedding is a natural process, there are a few things you can do to reduce it:

  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair
  • Try not to wear tight hairstyles that pull on your hair
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Manage your stress levels

By taking care of your hair and body, you can reduce hair shedding and keep your hair healthy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it normal to lose hair every day?

Yes, it's normal to shed around 50-100 hairs per day. This is a natural part of the hair growth cycle.

How do I know if I'm experiencing hair loss?

If you're experiencing thinning hair, bald spots, or hair that falls out easily, you may be experiencing hair loss. It's a good idea to see a doctor or dermatologist to get a proper diagnosis.

Can stress cause hair loss?

Yes, stress can cause hair loss. When you're stressed, your body releases hormones that can disrupt the hair growth cycle and cause hair to fall out.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair healthy and reduce shedding, try these tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Don't wash your hair too often
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather

By taking care of your hair and body, you can reduce hair shedding and keep your hair healthy. Remember, shedding is a normal part of the hair growth cycle, so don't worry too much if you see some hair in your brush or shower drain. If you're concerned about hair loss, see a doctor or dermatologist to get a proper diagnosis.
