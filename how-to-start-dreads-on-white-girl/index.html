Description

Dreadlocks, or dreads, are a popular hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are often associated with Rastafarian culture, but can be worn by anyone who wants to embrace a natural, carefree look. Starting dreads on white girl hair may seem daunting, but it is actually quite simple if you follow the right steps. In this guide, we will show you how to start dreads on white girl hair and provide some tips for maintaining them.

How to Start Dreads on White Girl

Before you start, it is important to note that starting dreads on white girl hair may require a longer process than starting them on other hair types. This is because white girl hair tends to be straight and silky, making it harder for the hair to lock naturally. However, with patience and the right technique, anyone can achieve beautiful dreads.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to start dreads on white girl hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product build-up.
  2. Section your hair into small, even parts using a rat tail comb. The size of the sections will depend on how thick you want your dreads to be.
  3. Apply a locking gel or beeswax to each section of hair. This will help the hair to lock and hold its shape.
  4. Twist each section of hair in a clockwise direction until it starts to lock. This may take several weeks or even months, depending on your hair type.
  5. After your hair has locked, you can choose to palm roll your dreads to help them tighten and smooth out any bumps.
  6. Use a crochet hook to pull in any loose hairs and create a more uniform look. Be careful not to pull too hard, as this can damage your hair.
  7. Continue to maintain your dreads by washing them regularly with a residue-free shampoo and avoiding heavy styling products.

Tips for Starting Dreads on White Girl

Here are some tips to help you start dreads on white girl hair:

  • Be patient. It may take several months or even years for your dreads to mature.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products, as they can weigh down your hair and prevent it from locking.
  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo to prevent build-up.
  • Use a satin pillowcase or wrap your hair in a silk scarf at night to prevent frizz and breakage.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreads on white girl hair:

  • Can I start dreads on white girl hair if it is relaxed or chemically treated?

    • Yes, you can start dreads on white girl hair that has been relaxed or chemically treated. However, it may be more difficult for the hair to lock and you may need to use a locking gel or beeswax to help the process.

  • Do I need to cut off my hair to start dreads?

    • No, you do not need to cut off your hair to start dreads. However, it is important to note that the longer your hair is, the longer it will take for your dreads to mature.

  • Can I wash my hair with regular shampoo?

    • No, it is not recommended to wash your dreads with regular shampoo as it can leave residue and prevent your hair from locking. Use a residue-free shampoo specifically designed for dreadlocks.

Hair Care for Dreads

Once you have started your dreads, it is important to take care of them to keep them looking healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips for hair care for dreads:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products or oils, as they can weigh down your hair and prevent it from locking.
  • Use a crochet hook to pull in any loose hairs and maintain a uniform look.
  • Wrap your hair in a silk scarf or use a satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.

Starting dreads on white girl hair may seem like a lengthy process, but with patience and the right technique, anyone can achieve beautiful, natural-looking dreads. Follow our step-by-step guide and tips for maintaining your dreads, and you'll be rocking a carefree, stylish look in no time!
