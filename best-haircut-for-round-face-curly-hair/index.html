Best Haircut For Round Face Curly Hair A Complete Guide

If you have a round face and curly hair, finding the perfect haircut can be a challenge. You want a style that flatters your face shape and enhances your natural curls. Luckily, there are many options available that can help you achieve the look you desire. In this post, we will explore the best haircuts for round face curly hair, as well as tips on hair care and maintenance.

Best Haircuts for Round Face Curly Hair

When it comes to haircuts for round face curly hair, the key is to create volume and height at the crown while keeping the sides of the hair close to the head. Here are some of the best haircuts for this hair type:

1. Long Layers

Long layers can help elongate the face and create movement in the hair. This cut works well for those with thick, curly hair as it can help reduce bulk and weight.

2. Bob

A classic bob is a great option for those with round faces and curly hair. It frames the face and creates a flattering shape. For curly hair, a layered bob can help add volume and movement.

3. Pixie Cut

A pixie cut can work well for those with curly hair and a round face. It creates height at the crown and draws attention to the eyes and cheekbones. However, it requires regular maintenance to keep the shape.

4. Shoulder-Length

Shoulder-length hair is a versatile option for those with curly hair and a round face. Layers can be added to create volume and movement, and the length can be adjusted to suit individual preferences.

5. Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs can help balance out a round face and create a flattering frame. They work well for those with curly hair as they can blend in with the rest of the hair.

How to Get the Perfect Haircut

Getting the perfect haircut for round face curly hair requires communication with your stylist. Here are some tips to ensure you get the cut you desire:

1. Bring Inspiration

Bring pictures of hairstyles you like to your stylist to give them an idea of what you are looking for. This can help facilitate communication and ensure you both have the same vision.

2. Ask for Layers

Layers can help reduce bulk and weight in curly hair, creating volume and movement. Ask your stylist to add layers to your cut.

3. Consider Face Shape

When choosing a haircut, consider your face shape. Round faces look best with cuts that create height at the crown and keep the sides close to the head.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Curly Hair

Curly hair can be challenging to cut, but with the right technique, you can achieve a great result. Here is a step-by-step guide to cutting curly hair:

1. Start with Dry Hair

Curly hair should be cut when dry so you can see the natural shape and movement. This will help avoid any surprises when the hair dries.

2. Section the Hair

Section the hair into four parts: top, sides, and back. Pin each section up, leaving the bottom section loose.

3. Cut the Bottom Section

Cut the bottom section of hair to the desired length. Use a point-cutting technique to create a softer edge.

4. Cut the Top Section

Release the top section of hair and cut to the desired length. Use a point-cutting technique to create texture and movement.

5. Cut the Side Sections

Release the side sections of hair and cut to the desired length. Use a point-cutting technique to blend the sides with the rest of the hair.

Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair

Maintaining curly hair requires a different approach than straight hair. Here are some tips to help you keep your curls looking their best:

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Curly hair is prone to tangles, so use a wide-tooth comb to avoid damage. Start at the bottom and work your way up.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage curly hair, so avoid using hot tools whenever possible. Air-dry your hair or use a diffuser on a low heat setting.

3. Use a Deep Conditioner

Curly hair can be dry, so use a deep conditioner once a week to nourish your locks. Leave the conditioner on for at least 20 minutes before rinsing.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can I Straighten Curly Hair?

Yes, you can straighten curly hair, but it is important to use a heat protectant and avoid excessive heat to prevent damage. Consider using a keratin treatment to make the process easier.

2. How Often Should I Cut Curly Hair?

Curly hair should be cut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends. However, the frequency can vary depending on individual hair type and preferences.

3. How Can I Reduce Frizz in Curly Hair?

To reduce frizz in curly hair, avoid over-shampooing and use a leave-in conditioner. Consider using a microfiber towel or cotton t-shirt to dry your hair, rather than a regular towel.

Hair Care Products for Curly Hair

Using the right hair care products can help enhance your natural curls and keep them looking healthy. Here are some products to consider:

1. Curl-Enhancing Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a curl-enhancing shampoo and conditioner to help define your curls and reduce frizz. Look for products that are sulfate-free and contain natural ingredients.

2. Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help nourish your curls and keep them hydrated. Use it after washing your hair, and apply it evenly throughout your locks.

3. Curl-Defining Cream

A curl-defining cream can help enhance your curls and create definition. Apply it to damp hair, and scrunch your hair to encourage curl formation.

Now that you know the best haircut for round face curly hair, as well as how to care for and maintain your locks, you can rock your curls with confidence. Remember to communicate with your stylist and choose a cut that flatters your face shape and enhances your natural curls.
