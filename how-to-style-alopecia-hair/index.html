How To Style Alopecia Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Jul 14, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're dealing with alopecia, figuring out how to style your hair can be a challenge. Alopecia refers to hair loss, and it can affect people in a variety of ways. Some people experience total hair loss, while others may have patches of hair missing. Regardless of the type of alopecia you have, there are ways to style your hair that can help you look and feel your best. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks for styling alopecia hair.

Description

Alopecia is a condition that affects millions of people worldwide. It can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, autoimmune disorders, and environmental factors. Regardless of the cause, alopecia can be challenging to deal with, especially when it comes to styling your hair.

How to Style Alopecia Hair

1. Embrace Your Natural Style

If you're dealing with alopecia, it's essential to embrace your natural style. This may mean opting for a shorter haircut or embracing your natural texture. Shorter haircuts can be easier to manage and style, and they can also help to conceal any bald patches or thinning areas. If you're not sure what haircut is right for you, talk to your stylist. They can help you find a style that works with your hair type and face shape.

2. Experiment with Headscarves and Hats

If you're not comfortable with showing your bald patches, headscarves and hats are a great way to conceal them. Headscarves can be tied in a variety of ways, and they come in a range of colors and patterns. Hats are also a great option, especially if you're going for a more casual look. Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

3. Try Hair Extensions

If you're looking to add volume or length to your hair, hair extensions can be a great option. There are a variety of types of extensions available, including clip-ins, tape-ins, and sew-ins. Talk to your stylist about which type of extension is right for you.

4. Use Hair Products

Using the right hair products can help to make your hair look fuller and thicker. Look for volumizing shampoos and conditioners, as well as products designed to add texture and hold. Avoid heavy products, as these can weigh down your hair and make it look limp and lifeless.

Step by Step Guide

1. Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Before you start styling your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Blot your hair dry with a towel, and then let it air dry or use a blow dryer on a low heat setting.

2. Apply Products

Once your hair is dry, apply the products that you've chosen. Start with a volumizing mousse or spray to add texture and volume to your hair. Then, apply a styling product, such as a paste or wax, to help hold your style in place.

3. Style Your Hair

Next, it's time to style your hair. If you're going for a more natural look, use your fingers to tousle your hair and create texture. If you're looking for something more structured, use a comb or brush to shape your hair into the desired style.

4. Finish with Hairspray

Finally, finish your style with a light mist of hairspray. This will help to hold your style in place and prevent it from falling flat throughout the day.

Tips for Styling Alopecia Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you style your alopecia hair:

  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and products
  • Invest in a high-quality hairbrush or comb
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner to help nourish your hair and prevent breakage

FAQ

Can I color my hair if I have alopecia?

Yes, you can color your hair if you have alopecia. However, it's important to be gentle with your hair and avoid harsh chemicals that can cause further damage.

What type of haircut is best for alopecia?

Shorter haircuts are often the best option for people with alopecia. This is because they can be easier to manage and style, and they can help to conceal any bald patches or thinning areas.

Can hair extensions damage my hair?

If hair extensions are applied and removed correctly, they should not cause any damage to your hair. However, it's important to work with a stylist who is experienced in applying extensions to ensure that they are installed correctly.

Hair Care for Alopecia

When it comes to caring for your alopecia hair, it's important to be gentle and avoid harsh chemicals and treatments. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid heat styling tools as much as possible. Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help nourish your hair and prevent breakage. And be sure to talk to your doctor or dermatologist about any treatments or medications that may be right for you.
