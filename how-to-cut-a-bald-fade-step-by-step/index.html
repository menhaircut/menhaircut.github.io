How To Cut A Bald Fade Step By Step

Written by William Nov 29, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of constantly going to the barbershop and spending money on haircuts that don't quite meet your expectations? Learning how to cut a bald fade step by step is easier than you might think. Not only can you save money, but you can also achieve the exact style you're looking for. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started.

Description

A bald fade is a popular hairstyle that features a gradual fade from a longer length at the top to a shorter length around the sides and back of the head. This style is also known as a skin fade because the hair is shaved down to the skin. It's a versatile look that can be customized to suit any face shape or hair type. With the right tools and techniques, you can easily master the art of cutting a bald fade.

How to Cut a Bald Fade Step by Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary tools on hand. You'll need a clipper, guards of varying lengths, scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. It's also helpful to have a handheld mirror or a friend to help you check your work.

Step 2: Start with the Top

Begin by cutting the hair on top of the head to the desired length. Use scissors or a clipper with a guard to trim the hair evenly. If you're not sure how long to leave the hair, start with a longer length and gradually go shorter until you achieve the desired look.

Step 3: Taper the Sides and Back

Next, use a clipper with a shorter guard to taper the sides and back of the head. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using a sweeping motion to blend the hair into the longer length on top. Be sure to use a mirror or have a friend check your work to ensure the fade is even on both sides.

Step 4: Create a Hard Part (Optional)

If you want to create a hard part, use a comb and a clipper to shave a straight line into the hair. This can be done on either side of the head or down the middle, depending on your preference.

Step 5: Blend the Fade

Once the sides and back are tapered, it's time to blend the fade. Use a clipper with no guard to carefully trim the hair around the ears and neckline. This will create a smooth transition from the shaved areas to the longer length on top. Be sure to take your time and make small adjustments as needed.

Step 6: Refine the Look

After the fade is complete, use scissors to trim any stray hairs and create a clean, polished look. You can also use a trimmer to shape the hairline and remove any excess hair around the ears.

Tips

  • Start with a longer length and gradually go shorter to avoid cutting off too much hair.
  • Use a handheld mirror or have a friend check your work to ensure the fade is even on both sides.
  • Take your time and make small adjustments as needed.
  • Practice makes perfect – don't be afraid to try different techniques and styles to see what works best for you.

FAQ

What is the difference between a bald fade and a skin fade?

A bald fade and a skin fade are essentially the same thing. Both styles feature a gradual fade from a longer length on top to a shorter length around the sides and back of the head. The term "bald fade" is often used because the hair is shaved down to the skin, creating a smooth, polished look.

Can I cut a bald fade at home?

Yes, with the right tools and techniques, cutting a bald fade at home is possible. It may take some practice to get the hang of it, but once you master the technique, you'll be able to achieve the exact style you're looking for.

How often should I get a bald fade?

The frequency of your haircuts will depend on how quickly your hair grows and how well you maintain your style. Generally, a bald fade will need to be touched up every two to three weeks to keep it looking fresh and polished.

Hair Care

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking healthy and strong. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and flat irons, as they can damage the hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and shiny.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.

By following these tips, you can maintain your bald fade and keep your hair looking its best.
