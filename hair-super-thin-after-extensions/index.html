Why Your Hair Is Super Thin After Extensions And How To Fix It

Written by Frank Sep 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you experiencing hair thinning or hair loss after getting extensions? You are not alone. Many women have reported experiencing hair breakage, shedding, and thinning after getting extensions. In this article, we will explore the reasons why this happens and offer some tips on how to fix it.

Description

Hair extensions are a popular way to add length and volume to your hair. However, they can also cause damage to your natural hair if not installed or maintained properly. Hair extensions can cause hair thinning or hair loss due to the weight of the extensions pulling on your natural hair, improper installation, or improper removal.

How to

If you are experiencing hair thinning or hair loss after getting extensions, there are a few things you can do to fix it:

1. Remove the Extensions

The first step to fixing hair thinning or hair loss after extensions is to remove the extensions. This will give your natural hair a chance to recover and regrow.

2. Give Your Hair a Break

After removing the extensions, it is important to give your hair a break from any styling or chemical treatments. This includes blow-drying, flat-ironing, coloring, or perming your hair.

3. Use Hair Growth Products

You can use hair growth products such as shampoos, conditioners, and serums to help stimulate hair growth and strengthen your natural hair. Look for products that contain biotin, keratin, or collagen.

4. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims will help prevent split ends and breakage, which can lead to hair thinning or hair loss. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Step by Step

Here are the step-by-step instructions on how to fix hair thinning or hair loss after extensions:

Step 1: Remove the Extensions

The first step is to remove the extensions. You can do this yourself or go to a salon to have them removed.

Step 2: Give Your Hair a Break

After removing the extensions, give your hair a break from any styling or chemical treatments. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and let it air dry.

Step 3: Use Hair Growth Products

Use hair growth products such as shampoos, conditioners, and serums to help stimulate hair growth and strengthen your natural hair. Follow the instructions on the product label.

Step 4: Get Regular Trims

Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage. Ask your hairstylist to trim off any damaged ends and shape your hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to prevent hair thinning or hair loss after extensions:

1. Choose High-Quality Extensions

Choose high-quality extensions that are made with 100% human hair. Synthetic hair extensions can cause more damage to your natural hair.

2. Proper Installation and Maintenance

Make sure your extensions are installed and maintained properly. This includes not installing them too tightly, not wearing them for too long, and not using too much heat on them.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles such as ponytails or braids, as these can pull on your natural hair and cause damage.

FAQs

Q: Can hair extensions cause hair thinning or hair loss?

A: Yes, hair extensions can cause hair thinning or hair loss if not installed or maintained properly.

Q: How long should I wait before getting extensions again?

A: It is recommended to wait at least 3-6 months before getting extensions again to give your natural hair a chance to recover.

Q: Can I still dye my hair after getting extensions?

A: It is best to avoid any chemical treatments on your hair after getting extensions to prevent further damage. If you must dye your hair, wait at least 4-6 weeks after getting extensions and use a gentle, ammonia-free dye.

Hair Care

Taking care of your natural hair is important to prevent hair thinning or hair loss after extensions. Here are some tips:

1. Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.

2. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow-drying or flat-ironing.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up.

4. Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

5. Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth, such as biotin, iron, and zinc.

By following these tips and taking care of your natural hair, you can prevent hair thinning or hair loss after extensions and promote healthy hair growth.
