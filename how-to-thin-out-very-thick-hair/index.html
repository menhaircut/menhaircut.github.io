How To Thin Out Very Thick Hair A Step By Step Guide

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it's great to have a full head of hair, it can also be difficult to manage and style. Thinning out your hair can help make it more manageable and give it more movement. In this guide, we'll show you how to thin out very thick hair.

How to Thin Out Very Thick Hair

Thinning out your hair can be done in a few different ways. You can either go to a salon and have a professional do it, or you can do it yourself at home. Here are some methods you can use:

  • Razor cutting
  • Thinning shears
  • Laser hair removal
  • Chemical straightening

Each method has its pros and cons, so you should choose the one that works best for you. We'll go over razor cutting and thinning shears in this guide.

Step-by-Step Guide

Using Razor Cutting

Razor cutting is a technique that uses a razor blade to thin out the hair. Here are the steps:

  1. Wash your hair and let it air dry.
  2. Section your hair into manageable sections.
  3. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers.
  4. Use a razor to make small, quick cuts along the hair shaft.
  5. Repeat on all sections of hair until you achieve the desired thinness.

Be careful not to cut too much hair at once or you may end up with uneven or choppy layers.

Using Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are scissors with teeth that are used to remove bulk from the hair. Here's how to use them:

  1. Wash your hair and let it air dry.
  2. Section your hair into manageable sections.
  3. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers.
  4. Use the thinning shears to cut into the hair, taking care not to cut too much at once.
  5. Repeat on all sections of hair until you achieve the desired thinness.

It's important to note that thinning shears should only be used on the ends of the hair, not near the roots or scalp.

Tips for Thinning Out Thick Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out your hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger ones.
  • Take breaks to rest your hands and avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use a comb to separate the hair and make the cuts more precise.
  • Don't cut too close to the roots or scalp to avoid damaging the hair.
  • Consider going to a professional if you're unsure or don't feel comfortable doing it yourself.

FAQs

Can thinning out my hair damage it?

Thinning out your hair can be damaging if done incorrectly. It's important to use the right tools and techniques to avoid damaging the hair.

How often should I thin out my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it becomes thick again. Generally, it's recommended to thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Can I thin out my hair with regular scissors?

Regular scissors are not designed for thinning out hair and can result in an uneven or choppy cut. It's best to use razor cutting or thinning shears.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Thick hair requires a bit more maintenance than thinner hair. Here are some tips for keeping your thick hair healthy:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much heat on the hair, such as blow dryers and flat irons.
  • Regularly trim the ends of the hair to prevent split ends.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle the hair and avoid pulling or breaking it.
  • Avoid using heavy hair products that can weigh down the hair.

By following these tips and thinning out your hair when needed, you can keep your thick hair looking healthy and manageable.
