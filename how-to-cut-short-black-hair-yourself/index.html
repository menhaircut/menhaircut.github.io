How To Cut Short Black Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

If you're tired of spending money and time at the salon every few weeks, you might be considering cutting your own hair at home. However, if you have short black hair, you might be wondering how to cut it without making a mess. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to cut short black hair yourself, as well as provide tips and answer frequently asked questions.

How to Cut Short Black Hair Yourself

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you've never done it before. However, with the right tools and techniques, it can be a rewarding experience. Here are the steps to cut short black hair yourself.

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather the necessary tools. You'll need:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Clip or hair tie
  • Mirror
  • Towel or cape

Make sure your clippers have a guard that's the right length for your desired haircut. You can also use scissors to trim any stray hairs.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will help you see the natural shape of your hair and prevent any snags or tangles. If you have curly hair, you might want to style it as you normally would to see how it falls.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into four parts: front, back, and two sides. Use a clip or hair tie to keep each section separate.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start with the back section of your hair. Use the clippers with the guard to trim the hair evenly. Work your way up to the crown of your head, using the mirror to check your progress.

For the sides, use the clipper with the guard to trim the hair to the desired length. You can also use scissors to trim any stray hairs.

For the front section, use the comb to lift the hair straight up, and trim the ends with the scissors. Be careful not to cut too much at once.

Step 5: Refine Your Cut

Once you've cut all sections of your hair, use the scissors to refine the cut. Trim any uneven or longer pieces until you're happy with the length and shape.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to cutting short black hair yourself:

  1. Gather your tools.
  2. Wash and dry your hair.
  3. Section your hair into four parts.
  4. Start cutting with the clippers on the back section.
  5. Move to the sides, using the clippers with the guard.
  6. Use the scissors to trim any stray hairs.
  7. For the front section, lift the hair straight up and trim with scissors.
  8. Refine the cut with scissors until you're happy with the length and shape.
  9. Clean up any loose hairs.
  10. Style as desired.

Tips for Cutting Short Black Hair Yourself

Here are some tips to make cutting short black hair yourself easier:

  • Start with a longer guard on the clippers and work your way down to a shorter length.
  • Use the mirror to check your progress and make sure both sides are even.
  • Trim small amounts of hair at a time to avoid cutting too much off.
  • Watch tutorials or ask a friend for help if you're unsure.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I cut my short black hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, you should get a trim every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and get rid of split ends.

What if I make a mistake?

If you accidentally cut too much off, don't panic. You can always go back and trim it again. If you're really unhappy with the result, you can always go to a professional to fix it.

Can I cut my hair when it's wet?

It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be more difficult to cut evenly.

Hair Care After Cutting Short Black Hair Yourself

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain the shape and prevent damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine with a hat or swim cap.

Cutting short black hair yourself can be a fun and rewarding experience, but it's important to take your time and follow these steps carefully. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut without leaving your home.
