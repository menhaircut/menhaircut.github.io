What To Avoid In Conditioner For Curly Hair

Written by Frank Sep 04, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Curly hair can be a challenge to maintain. It requires special care and attention, especially when it comes to choosing the right conditioner. Using the wrong conditioner can lead to frizz, breakage, and dryness. In this article, we will discuss what to avoid in conditioner for curly hair, so you can keep your locks looking healthy and beautiful.

Avoid Silicones

Silicones are commonly used in hair products due to their ability to create a smooth, shiny finish. However, they can be harmful to curly hair. Silicones form a barrier around the hair shaft, which can prevent moisture from penetrating and lead to dryness and breakage. Look for conditioners that are labeled as "silicone-free" or that contain water-soluble silicones, which can be easily washed out.

Avoid Sulfates

Sulfates are harsh detergents that are commonly found in shampoos and conditioners. They can strip the hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Sulfates can also irritate the scalp and cause frizz. Look for conditioners that are sulfate-free or that contain mild sulfates, such as sodium lauryl sulfoacetate or sodium cocoyl isethionate. These are gentler on the hair and scalp.

Avoid Alcohol

Alcohol is commonly used in hair products as a drying agent. However, it can be harmful to curly hair, which tends to be dry and prone to breakage. Look for conditioners that are free of alcohol or that contain fatty alcohols, such as cetyl alcohol or stearyl alcohol. These are moisturizing and can help to improve the overall health of your hair.

Avoid Heavy Oils

While oils can be beneficial for curly hair, some oils can be too heavy and weigh down the hair. Avoid conditioners that contain heavy oils, such as mineral oil or petrolatum. These can lead to build-up and make your hair look greasy. Look for conditioners that contain lighter oils, such as argan oil or jojoba oil.

Avoid Proteins

Proteins are often added to hair products to strengthen the hair. However, too much protein can be harmful to curly hair, which tends to be fragile. Look for conditioners that are protein-free or that contain hydrolyzed proteins, which are broken down into smaller molecules that can be easily absorbed by the hair.

How to Condition Curly Hair

Conditioning curly hair is an important part of your hair care routine. Here are some tips on how to condition your curly hair:

Step 1: Wet Your Hair

Wet your hair thoroughly with warm water. This will help to open up the hair cuticles and allow the conditioner to penetrate more deeply.

Step 2: Apply Conditioner

Apply a generous amount of conditioner to your hair, focusing on the mid-lengths and ends. Use your fingers or a wide-tooth comb to distribute the conditioner evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Leave it in

Leave the conditioner in your hair for at least 5 minutes, or longer if possible. This will give the conditioner time to penetrate the hair shaft and provide maximum moisture.

Step 4: Rinse Your Hair

Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water. This will help to close the hair cuticles and seal in moisture.

Tips for Conditioning Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for conditioning curly hair:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to provide extra moisture to your hair.
  • Don't rub your hair vigorously with a towel after washing. Instead, gently blot your hair with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing to provide extra moisture and prevent frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.

FAQs

What is the best type of conditioner for curly hair?

The best type of conditioner for curly hair is one that is moisturizing and free of silicones, sulfates, alcohol, heavy oils, and proteins. Look for conditioners that contain natural ingredients, such as shea butter, coconut oil, and aloe vera.

How often should I condition my curly hair?

You should condition your curly hair every time you wash it. If you have very dry or damaged hair, you may want to consider conditioning your hair twice a week or using a leave-in conditioner daily.

Can I use regular conditioner on curly hair?

Regular conditioner can be used on curly hair, but it may not provide enough moisture or be formulated for your hair type. It's best to look for a conditioner that is specifically designed for curly hair.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips for curly hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Avoid using hair ties with metal parts, as these can snag and break your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.

By following these tips and avoiding harmful ingredients in your conditioner, you can keep your curly hair healthy, moisturized, and beautiful.
