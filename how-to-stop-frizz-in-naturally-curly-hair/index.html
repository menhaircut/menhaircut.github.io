How to Stop Frizz in Naturally Curly Hair

The first step in preventing frizz is to understand what causes it. Frizz occurs when the cuticle layer of your hair is raised, allowing moisture to enter and causing the hair to swell. This can be caused by a variety of factors, including humidity, dryness, and damage from heat styling or chemical treatments.

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

These products will help to keep your hair hydrated and prevent dryness, which can lead to frizz.

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness and frizz. Try to limit washing your hair to once or twice a week, and use a dry shampoo in between washes if necessary.

3. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can be a great way to add extra moisture to your hair and prevent frizz. Look for a lightweight formula that wonât weigh your curls down, and apply it to damp hair before styling.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands can be damaging to curly hair, causing it to become dry and frizzy. Try to avoid using these tools as much as possible, and let your hair air-dry instead of using a blow dryer.

5. Use a Diffuser Attachment

If you do need to use a blow dryer, use a diffuser attachment to help distribute the air evenly and prevent frizz. Hold the diffuser close to your hair and use a low or medium heat setting.
