How To Get Defined Curls After Shower A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Sep 13, 2023 · 4 min read

Curls are a great way to add texture and volume to your hair, but getting defined curls can be a challenge, especially after a shower. In this article, we’ll show you how to get defined curls after showering. We’ll cover everything from how to prep your hair to how to style it, so you can achieve the perfect curls every time.

Table of Contents

  1. Prep Your Hair
  2. Apply Styling Products
  3. Diffuse Your Hair
  4. Scrunch Your Curls
  5. Let Your Hair Air Dry
  6. Touch Up Your Curls
  7. FAQ
  8. Hair Care Tips

1. Prep Your Hair

The first step to getting defined curls after a shower is to prep your hair. Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help to keep your curls hydrated and prevent frizz.

After washing, gently squeeze the excess water out of your hair with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt. Avoid rubbing your hair with a regular towel, as this can cause frizz.

Next, apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your damp hair. This will help to define your curls and keep them hydrated.


2. Apply Styling Products

Once you’ve prepped your hair, it’s time to apply styling products. Start by applying a small amount of mousse or gel to your hair. This will help to hold your curls in place.

Next, apply a curl-defining cream or serum to your hair. This will help to define your curls and keep them frizz-free.

Make sure to distribute the products evenly throughout your hair, using your fingers or a wide-toothed comb.


3. Diffuse Your Hair

After applying your styling products, it’s time to diffuse your hair. This will help to set your curls and give them definition.

Start by using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer. This will help to distribute the heat evenly and prevent frizz.

Flip your head upside down and use the diffuser to dry your hair. Make sure to scrunch your curls gently as you dry them, to help them keep their shape.


4. Scrunch Your Curls

Once your hair is dry, it’s time to scrunch your curls. This will help to break up any crunchiness from the styling products and give your curls a more natural look.

Start by applying a small amount of oil or serum to your hands. Then, gently scrunch your curls from the ends to the roots.

Don’t be too rough with your hair, as this can cause frizz. Instead, be gentle and use a light touch.


5. Let Your Hair Air Dry

After scrunching your curls, it’s best to let your hair air dry. This will help to set your curls and prevent frizz.

If you’re in a hurry, you can use a diffuser on a low heat setting to dry your hair. But be careful not to over-dry your hair, as this can cause frizz.

Once your hair is dry, you can use a light-hold hairspray to keep your curls in place.


6. Touch Up Your Curls

If you notice any frizz or flyaways after your hair has dried, you can touch up your curls with a curling iron or wand.

Start by sectioning your hair and wrapping small sections around the barrel of the curling iron or wand. Hold each section for a few seconds, then release.

Make sure to use a heat protectant spray before using any hot tools on your hair, to prevent damage.


FAQ

Q: Can I use a regular towel to dry my hair?

A: No, it’s best to avoid using a regular towel on your hair, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze the excess water out of your hair.


Q: Can I use a hair dryer without a diffuser attachment?

A: While it’s possible to use a hair dryer without a diffuser attachment, it’s best to use one if you want defined curls. A diffuser helps to distribute the heat evenly and prevent frizz.


Hair Care Tips

Here are some tips to help you care for your curly hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair too often, as this can cause damage.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, instead of a brush.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your hair before styling, to keep your curls hydrated.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much throughout the day, as this can cause frizz.

By following these tips and using the steps outlined in this article, you can achieve defined curls after showering. Remember to be gentle with your hair, and don’t be afraid to experiment with different styling products and techniques until you find what works best for you.
