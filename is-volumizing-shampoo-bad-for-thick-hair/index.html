Is Volumizing Shampoo Bad For Thick Hair

Written by Robby Oct 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have thick hair, you may be wondering whether or not volumizing shampoo is right for you. After all, you don't need any help in the volume department, right? Well, the truth is that volumizing shampoo can be both good and bad for thick hair. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of using volumizing shampoo on thick hair and provide some tips for getting the most out of your hair care routine.

Table of Contents

Description

Volumizing shampoo is designed to add lift and body to your hair, making it look fuller and thicker. It works by coating your hair with ingredients that bind to the hair shaft and make it appear thicker. While this is great for those with fine or thin hair, it can be problematic for those with thick hair.

Thick hair already has plenty of volume, so adding more can make it look overly voluminous and even frizzy. Additionally, many volumizing shampoos contain drying ingredients that can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it look dull and lifeless.

How to Use Volumizing Shampoo on Thick Hair

If you do decide to use volumizing shampoo on your thick hair, there are some tips you can follow to ensure that you get the best results:

1. Use it sparingly

Only use volumizing shampoo once or twice a week, or as needed. Using it too often can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause damage.

2. Focus on the roots

When applying volumizing shampoo, focus on the roots of your hair, where you want the most lift. Avoid applying it to the ends, which can make your hair look dry and damaged.

3. Follow up with a conditioner

After using volumizing shampoo, be sure to use a conditioner to help restore moisture to your hair. Look for a lightweight conditioner that won't weigh down your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for using volumizing shampoo on thick hair:

Step 1: Wet your hair

Start by wetting your hair thoroughly with warm water.

Step 2: Apply shampoo

Apply a small amount of volumizing shampoo to your scalp and roots. Massage it in gently, using circular motions.

Step 3: Rinse

Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water, making sure to remove all of the shampoo.

Step 4: Condition

Apply conditioner to the ends of your hair, avoiding the roots. Leave it on for a few minutes, then rinse thoroughly.

Tips for Using Volumizing Shampoo on Thick Hair

Here are a few additional tips for getting the most out of your volumizing shampoo:

1. Choose the right shampoo

Look for a volumizing shampoo that's specifically designed for thick hair. These shampoos will typically contain different ingredients than those designed for fine or thin hair.

2. Use a clarifying shampoo

Once a month, use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup from your hair. This will help keep your hair healthy and prevent it from becoming weighed down.

3. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and make it look flat and lifeless. If you do use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray first.

FAQ

Q: Can volumizing shampoo cause hair loss?

A: No, volumizing shampoo does not cause hair loss. However, using it too often can cause damage to your hair, which can lead to breakage and thinning over time.

Q: Can volumizing shampoo be used on color-treated hair?

A: Yes, volumizing shampoo can be used on color-treated hair. However, you should look for a shampoo that's specifically designed for color-treated hair to prevent fading.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

In addition to using volumizing shampoo, there are other things you can do to keep your thick hair healthy and looking its best:

1. Use a wide-toothed comb

When detangling your hair, use a wide-toothed comb to prevent breakage and damage.

2. Don't over-wash your hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause damage. Aim to wash your hair every other day or every few days.

3. Use a leave-in conditioner

Using a leave-in conditioner can help keep your hair moisturized and prevent frizz.

4. Get regular trims

Getting regular trims can help prevent split ends and breakage, which can make your hair look thinner.


In conclusion, volumizing shampoo can be both good and bad for thick hair. While it can add lift and body to your hair, it can also make it look overly voluminous and frizzy. If you do decide to use volumizing shampoo on your thick hair, be sure to follow the tips and guidelines outlined in this article, and take steps to keep your hair healthy and looking its best.
