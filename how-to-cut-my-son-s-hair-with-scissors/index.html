How To Cut My Son s Hair With Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of paying for expensive haircuts for your son? Do you want to learn how to cut his hair yourself? Cutting your son's hair with scissors can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great-looking haircut. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to cut your son's hair with scissors.

How to Cut My Son's Hair with Scissors

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of scissors, a comb, and hair clippers (optional). Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean. It's also a good idea to have a towel or cape to catch any hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Son's Hair

Wash your son's hair and towel dry it. Make sure it's completely dry before you start cutting. Wet hair can be difficult to cut and can lead to uneven results.

Step 3: Decide on the Haircut

Before you start cutting, decide on the haircut you want to give your son. Look online for inspiration or ask your son what he wants. Keep in mind that some haircuts are easier to achieve than others, especially if you're new to cutting hair.

Step 4: Comb and Section the Hair

Comb your son's hair and section it into manageable sections. Use hair clips to keep each section in place. Start with the back of the head and work your way forward.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut with the scissors. Make sure you're cutting at a 45-degree angle to achieve a layered look. Use the comb to lift the hair and trim any stray hairs. Be cautious not to cut too much at once.

Step 6: Blend and Check

Blend any harsh lines with the scissors or clippers. Use the comb to check for any uneven spots and trim them as needed. Don't forget to check the sides and back of the head.

Step 7: Finish and Style

Once you're finished cutting, brush away any hair from your son's head and neck. Style his hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Your Son's Hair with Scissors

Here are some tips to help you achieve a great-looking haircut:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up. It's easier to cut more hair than to fix a mistake.
  • Use the comb to lift the hair and trim it at an angle to achieve a layered look.
  • Be cautious not to cut too much hair at once.
  • Blend any harsh lines with the scissors or clippers.
  • Check for any uneven spots and trim them as needed.
  • Don't forget to check the sides and back of the head.

FAQs

Can I cut my son's hair with regular scissors?

It's best to use scissors specifically designed for cutting hair. Regular scissors can be too blunt or too sharp, leading to uneven results.

How often should I cut my son's hair?

It depends on the length of his hair and how fast it grows. As a general rule, you should cut hair every four to six weeks to maintain the desired length.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my son's hair?

Don't panic! If you make a mistake, try to fix it by blending or trimming the hair. Worst case, you can always go to a professional stylist to fix any mistakes.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your son's hair, make sure to brush away any hair from his head and neck. Wash his hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and towel dry it. Avoid using any hair styling products for at least a day to let the hair settle down.

Cutting your son's hair with scissors can be a rewarding experience, both for you and your son. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. So, grab your scissors and start cutting!
