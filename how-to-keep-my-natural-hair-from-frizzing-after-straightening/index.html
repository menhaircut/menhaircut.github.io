How To Keep My Natural Hair From Frizzing After Straightening

Written by Petter Nov 23, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you have natural hair, you know how difficult it can be to keep it from frizzing after straightening. It can be frustrating to spend hours straightening your hair, only for it to frizz up as soon as you step outside. But don't worry! We've compiled a list of tips and tricks to help keep your natural hair sleek and smooth after straightening.

Table of Contents

  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to

The first step to preventing frizz after straightening is to make sure your hair is properly hydrated. Before straightening, use a deep conditioning treatment to nourish your hair and prevent dryness. Once your hair is straightened, use a lightweight oil or serum to seal in moisture and prevent frizz.

Another key to preventing frizz is to avoid over-styling your hair. Use a lower heat setting on your flat iron and avoid going over the same section of hair multiple times. This will help prevent damage and keep your hair smooth.

Step by Step

1. Start with clean, dry hair. Wash and condition your hair as usual, and make sure it's completely dry before straightening.

2. Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair. This will help protect your hair from heat damage and prevent frizz.

3. Section your hair. Use clips to divide your hair into sections, and work on one section at a time. This will make it easier to straighten your hair and prevent frizz.

4. Use a flat iron with adjustable heat settings. Use a lower heat setting to avoid damaging your hair, and avoid going over the same section of hair multiple times.

5. Once you've straightened your hair, apply a lightweight oil or serum to seal in moisture and prevent frizz.

Tips

- Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

- Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent frizz while you sleep.

- If you're going to be outside in humid weather, consider wearing a hat or scarf to protect your hair.

FAQ

Q: How often should I straighten my natural hair?

A: It's best to avoid straightening your hair too often, as this can damage your hair and lead to breakage. Try to limit straightening to once a week or less.

Q: Can I use a hair mask to prevent frizz?

A: Yes! Using a hair mask once a week can help keep your hair hydrated and prevent frizz.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential to keeping your natural hair healthy and frizz-free. Here are some tips to help you care for your hair:

- Wash and condition your hair regularly.

- Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.

- Avoid using heat styling tools too often.

- Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair.

- Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your natural hair sleek and smooth after straightening. Remember to take care of your hair and avoid over-styling, and you'll have beautiful, frizz-free locks in no time!
