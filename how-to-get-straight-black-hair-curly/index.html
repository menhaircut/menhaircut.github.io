How To Get Straight Black Hair Curly Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Jul 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of having straight black hair and want to switch things up with some curls? Lucky for you, there are a variety of methods you can use to get those luscious locks. In this article, we will discuss how to get straight black hair curly step by step, along with some tips, FAQs, and hair care advice to keep your new curls looking fabulous.

How to Get Straight Black Hair Curly

There are a few different ways to get straight black hair curly, including using heat, chemicals, or natural methods. Here are some of the most popular techniques:

1. Heat Styling

One of the most common ways to get curly hair is by using heat styling tools such as curling irons, wands, or rollers. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to protect your hair from damage.
  2. Divide your hair into small sections and use a curling iron or wand to curl each section.
  3. Alternatively, you can use hot rollers to curl your hair. Simply wrap small sections of hair around the rollers and leave them in for 10-20 minutes.
  4. After you've curled your whole head, use a light hold hairspray to keep your curls in place.

2. Chemical Treatments

If you want a more permanent solution for curly hair, you can try a chemical treatment such as a perm or relaxer. Here's what you need to know:

  1. A perm is a chemical treatment that adds curl to your hair. It works by breaking down the protein bonds in your hair and restructuring them in a new shape.
  2. A relaxer is a chemical treatment that straightens your hair. It works by breaking down the protein bonds in your hair and relaxing them into a straighter shape.
  3. Both treatments can be damaging to your hair, so it's important to consult with a professional stylist before trying them.

3. Natural Methods

If you prefer a more natural approach, there are a variety of ways to get curly hair without using heat or chemicals. Here are some ideas:

  1. Braid your hair while it's damp and let it air dry. When you take out the braids, you'll have natural-looking waves or curls.
  2. Twist small sections of your hair while it's damp and let it air dry. When you untwist the sections, you'll have defined curls.
  3. Use foam rollers to curl your hair while it's damp. Leave the rollers in for a few hours or overnight, then remove them for bouncy curls.

Tips for Getting Straight Black Hair Curly

No matter which method you choose, there are some tips you can follow to ensure your curls turn out great:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair. Avoid using a brush, which can cause breakage.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much while it's drying or after you've curled it, as this can cause frizz.
  • Don't use too much product, as this can weigh down your curls and make them look limp.

FAQs About Getting Straight Black Hair Curly

1. Is it possible to get curly hair without using heat or chemicals?

Yes, there are a variety of natural methods you can use to get curly hair without heat or chemicals. Try braiding, twisting, or using foam rollers to achieve your desired look.

2. How long will my curls last?

The longevity of your curls will depend on the method you use and how well you take care of your hair. Heat-styled curls may last a day or two, while chemical treatments can last several months. Natural curls will usually last until your next wash day.

3. Can I still straighten my hair after getting a perm or relaxer?

Yes, you can still straighten your hair after getting a perm or relaxer. However, it's important to wait at least a week after the treatment to give your hair time to recover.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Once you've achieved your desired curls, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for curly hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define your curls.
  • Avoid using a towel to dry your hair, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a t-shirt or microfiber towel to gently blot your hair.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to help prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to get straight black hair curly, it's time to try it out for yourself. Whether you use heat, chemicals, or natural methods, remember to take care of your hair to keep those curls looking fabulous.
