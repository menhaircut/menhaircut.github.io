How To Pick Bridal Hairstyle

Description

Choosing the perfect bridal hairstyle is an essential part of the wedding planning process. Your hairdo should complement your gown, makeup, and accessories, as well as your personality and personal style. Whether you prefer an updo, a half-up hairstyle, or a down-do, there are numerous options to choose from. In this article, we will provide you with tips and ideas on how to pick the perfect bridal hairstyle for your big day.

How to

The first step in picking a bridal hairstyle is to consider your face shape, hair type, and wedding dress. Your hairdo should enhance your best features and flatter your face shape. For example, if you have a round face, you may want to go for a hairstyle that adds height and volume to the crown, such as a high bun or a half-up hairstyle with a bouffant. If you have a square face, you may want to soften your jawline with loose waves or curls.

Secondly, consider the style and neckline of your wedding dress. If you have a strapless gown, an updo will accentuate your bare shoulders and elongate your neck. If you have a V-neckline, a half-up hairstyle will complement the neckline and create balance. If you have a high neckline or a collar, a low bun or a side-swept hairstyle will showcase your dress without competing with it.

Thirdly, consider your hair type and texture. If you have thin or fine hair, you may want to add volume with extensions, a hairpiece, or a volumizing product. If you have thick or curly hair, you may want to embrace your natural texture with a loose updo or a braided hairstyle. If you have short hair, you can still create an elegant and sophisticated look with the help of accessories, such as a headband, a tiara, or a veil.

Step by Step

Here are the steps to follow when picking a bridal hairstyle:

  1. Consider your face shape, hair type, and wedding dress.
  2. Research bridal hairstyles online or in magazines.
  3. Book a trial appointment with your hairstylist to try out different hairstyles.
  4. Bring pictures of your dress, accessories, and makeup to the appointment.
  5. Communicate your preferences, concerns, and feedback to your hairstylist.
  6. Take pictures of each hairstyle from different angles to compare and contrast.
  7. Ask for advice on how to maintain your hairstyle throughout the day.
  8. Make a final decision based on your personal taste and comfort level.
  9. Schedule a follow-up appointment closer to your wedding day to touch up your hairdo.
  10. Enjoy your wedding day with confidence and grace!

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when picking a bridal hairstyle:

  • Start researching and planning your hairstyle at least six months before your wedding day.
  • Consider the weather, location, and theme of your wedding when picking a hairstyle.
  • Bring your veil, tiara, or headpiece to your trial appointment to see how they look with your hairdo.
  • Choose a hairstyle that you feel comfortable and confident in.
  • Ask your hairstylist for recommendations on hair care, such as shampoo, conditioner, and styling products.
  • Practice your hairstyle at home before your wedding day to get familiar with it.
  • Bring a hair emergency kit with you on the day of your wedding, including bobby pins, hairspray, and a comb.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about picking a bridal hairstyle:

Q: Should I do my own hair or hire a professional?

A: It is recommended to hire a professional hairstylist for your wedding day, as they have the skills, tools, and experience to create a flawless and long-lasting hairstyle. However, if you are confident in your hair skills and want to save money, you can do your own hair with the help of tutorials, practice, and patience.

Q: How much does a bridal hairstyle cost?

A: The cost of a bridal hairstyle depends on various factors, such as the hairstylist's experience, location, and services, the length and complexity of the hairstyle, and the use of accessories and products. On average, a bridal hairstyle can cost between $50 and $300 or more.

Q: Can I change my hairstyle during my wedding day?

A: It is possible to change your hairstyle during your wedding day, but it requires planning, time, and assistance. If you want to switch from an updo to a down-do, for example, you will need to have someone help you take out the pins, brush out the hair, and restyle it. It is recommended to discuss your options and preferences with your hairstylist beforehand and have a backup plan in case of emergencies.

Hair Care

Here are some hair care tips to prepare for your wedding day:

  • Start a healthy hair regimen at least six months before your wedding day, including regular trims, deep conditioning treatments, and a balanced diet.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, too often, as they can damage your hair and cause split ends.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools, and a UV protectant spray before going outside.
  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as it can strip the natural oils and lead to frizz.
  • Avoid using too many hair products, as they can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Get a trim or haircut at least two weeks before your wedding day to remove dead ends and split ends.
  • Drink plenty of water, exercise regularly, and manage your stress levels to promote healthy hair growth and shine.

Picking the perfect bridal hairstyle can be a fun and rewarding experience, as long as you follow these tips and guidelines. Remember to communicate with your hairstylist, experiment with different styles, and choose a hairdo that reflects your personality and style. With the right hairstyle, you can look and feel your best on your wedding day!
