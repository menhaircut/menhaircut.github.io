How To Shave Down A Long Haired Dog

Written by Daniel Aug 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Long haired dogs can be beautiful and majestic, but they also require a lot of grooming to keep their coats healthy and shiny. Sometimes, shaving down a long haired dog can be the best decision for their well-being, especially during the hot summer months. In this article, we will provide step-by-step instructions on how to shave down a long haired dog, as well as tips for hair care and frequently asked questions.

Description

Shaving down a long haired dog means removing most, if not all, of their fur. This process can help prevent matting, overheating, and other health issues related to long hair. However, it is important to note that not all long haired dogs should be shaved down. Some breeds, like Huskies and Malamutes, have double coats that protect them from both hot and cold weather. Shaving down these breeds can actually do more harm than good.

How to Shave Down a Long Haired Dog

Before you start shaving down your long haired dog, it's important to gather the right tools. You will need a pair of clippers with multiple blade sizes, scissors, a comb, and a brush. It's also a good idea to have some treats on hand to keep your dog calm and happy.

Step 1: Brush and Comb Your Dog's Fur

Before you start shaving, make sure your dog's fur is free of mats and tangles. Use a brush and comb to gently remove any knots, starting from the bottom of their fur and working your way up. This will make the shaving process easier and more comfortable for your dog.

Step 2: Trim Your Dog's Long Fur

Using scissors, trim your dog's long fur down to about an inch in length. This will make it easier to use the clippers and prevent them from getting tangled in long hair. Make sure to trim around their paws, ears, and face carefully.

Step 3: Choose the Right Blade Size

Choose a blade size that is appropriate for your dog's fur length and thickness. A shorter blade will give a closer shave, but may be too harsh for sensitive skin. A longer blade will leave more fur, but may not be as effective for thick coats. Start with a longer blade and work your way down if necessary.

Step 4: Shave Your Dog's Fur

Using the clippers, shave your dog's fur in the direction of hair growth. Start from the neck and work your way down to the tail, making sure to shave evenly and avoid cutting too close to the skin. Use scissors to trim around sensitive areas like the face and paws.

Step 5: Finish with Scissors

After using the clippers, go back over your dog's fur with scissors to trim any remaining long hairs or uneven spots. Make sure to be extra careful around sensitive areas like the ears and face.

Tips for Hair Care

After shaving down your long haired dog, it's important to take care of their skin and coat. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Bathe your dog with a gentle shampoo to soothe their skin.
  • Apply a moisturizing conditioner to keep their fur soft and shiny.
  • Use a flea and tick preventative to protect your dog from parasites.
  • Brush your dog's fur regularly to prevent matting and tangling.
  • Provide your dog with a comfortable and cool environment during hot weather.

FAQ

Is it safe to shave down a long haired dog?

It depends on the breed and their individual coat. Some long haired dogs have double coats that protect them from hot and cold weather, and shaving them down can do more harm than good. Consult with your veterinarian or a professional groomer before shaving down your dog.

How often should I shave down my long haired dog?

It's not necessary to shave down a long haired dog regularly, but it can be helpful during hot weather or if their fur is causing health issues. Consult with your veterinarian or a professional groomer for guidance on how often to shave down your dog.

Should I use human clippers on my dog?

No, it's not recommended to use human clippers on your dog. Human clippers are not designed for animal fur and can cause skin irritation or injury. Use clippers specifically designed for dogs instead.

What if I accidentally cut my dog's skin?

If you accidentally cut your dog's skin while shaving, apply pressure to the wound with a clean cloth to stop bleeding. If the wound is deep or does not stop bleeding, take your dog to the veterinarian for treatment.

Hair Care

Shaving down a long haired dog can help prevent matting, overheating, and other health issues related to long hair. By following the steps and tips provided in this article, you can safely and effectively shave down your long haired dog and keep their skin and coat healthy.
