Can You Use Dog Thinning Shears On Human Hair

Written by Petter Jun 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a pet owner, you might be wondering if you can use your dog's thinning shears on your own hair. After all, they're both made of hair, so it might seem like it would work. However, before you attempt to use dog thinning shears on your hair, there are a few things you should know. In this article, we'll explore whether or not it's safe to use dog thinning shears on human hair, and provide some tips for caring for your hair.

Description

Thinning shears are a type of scissors that are designed to remove bulk from hair, making it look thinner and more manageable. They are commonly used by dog groomers to thin out the fur of certain breeds of dogs. However, human hair and dog fur are different in a few ways, so it's important to consider these differences before using dog thinning shears on your hair.

How to Use Dog Thinning Shears on Human Hair

While it is technically possible to use dog thinning shears on human hair, it's not recommended. Dog thinning shears are designed to cut through thicker, coarser fur, while human hair is much finer and softer. This means that using dog thinning shears on human hair can result in uneven cuts, damaged hair, and even injury to the scalp.

Step by Step Guide

If you're still determined to use dog thinning shears on your hair, here are some steps you can follow:

  1. Make sure your dog's thinning shears are clean and sharp.
  2. Wash and dry your hair thoroughly.
  3. Section your hair into small sections and comb through each section to remove tangles.
  4. Hold the thinning shears at a 45-degree angle and cut through the section of hair.
  5. Repeat on all sections of hair.
  6. Style your hair as desired.

While these steps may seem straightforward, it's important to remember that using dog thinning shears on human hair can be risky. You're much better off using a pair of scissors that are specifically designed for human hair.

Tips for Caring for Your Hair

If you're looking to thin out your hair, there are other ways to do it that don't involve using dog thinning shears. Here are a few tips:

  • Visit a hair salon and have a professional stylist thin out your hair.
  • Use a pair of thinning scissors that are specifically designed for human hair.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Be gentle when brushing and styling your hair, and avoid using tight hair ties or clips.
