How To Treat Damaged Hair Due To Straightening

Written by Bardi Aug 08, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Do you love the look of straight hair but hate the damage it can cause? Straightening your hair can take a toll on its health, leaving it dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. But don't worry, there are steps you can take to repair and prevent damage. In this article, we'll cover how to treat damaged hair due to straightening and keep your locks healthy and beautiful.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Treat Damaged Hair Due to Straightening
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips to Prevent Damage
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care Routine

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Trim your hair regularly: If you notice split ends or damage, it's important to get a trim. This will remove the damaged hair and prevent it from spreading. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

2. Use a deep conditioning treatment: Hair that has been straightened is often dry and brittle. To restore moisture, use a deep conditioning treatment once a week. Apply the treatment to damp hair and leave it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing.

3. Avoid heat styling: If possible, avoid using heat styling tools on your hair. This includes blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

4. Use a wide-toothed comb: When combing your hair, use a wide-toothed comb. This will minimize tugging and help prevent breakage. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up.

5. Wash your hair less frequently: Overwashing can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and damaged. Try to wash your hair no more than 2-3 times per week. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner specifically formulated for damaged hair.

Tips to Prevent Damage

- Use a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools.

- Avoid using rubber bands or tight hair ties that can cause breakage.

- Wear a hat or use an umbrella to protect your hair from the sun.

- Use a silk or satin pillowcase to minimize friction and prevent breakage.

- Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote healthy hair growth.

FAQ

Q: Can damaged hair be repaired?

A: Yes, damaged hair can be repaired with the right treatment. This includes deep conditioning treatments, regular trims, and minimizing heat styling.

Q: How long does it take to repair damaged hair?

A: It can take several weeks or months to repair damaged hair. Be patient and consistent with your hair care routine.

Hair Care Routine

Here's a sample hair care routine for damaged hair:

- Wash hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner every 2-3 days.

- Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.

- Apply a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools.

- Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle hair.

- Get a trim every 6-8 weeks to remove split ends.

By following these tips and incorporating a hair care routine, you can treat and prevent damage from straightening your hair. Remember, healthy hair is beautiful hair!
