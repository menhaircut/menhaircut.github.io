What Chemotherapy Does Not Cause Hair Loss A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Chemotherapy is a common treatment for cancer that involves the use of drugs to kill cancer cells. Unfortunately, one of the most well-known side effects of chemotherapy is hair loss. However, there are certain types of chemotherapy that do not cause hair loss. In this article, we will explore what chemotherapy does not cause hair loss, as well as provide tips on how to care for your hair during chemotherapy.

Table of Contents

  • What Chemotherapy Does Not Cause Hair Loss?
  • How Does Chemotherapy Cause Hair Loss?
  • Hair Care Tips During Chemotherapy
  • Frequently Asked Questions

What Chemotherapy Does Not Cause Hair Loss?

There are certain types of chemotherapy drugs that do not cause hair loss. These include:

  • Methotrexate: This drug is commonly used to treat breast cancer, lung cancer, and leukemia, and does not cause hair loss.
  • 5-fluorouracil (5-FU): This drug is used to treat breast cancer, colon cancer, and skin cancer, and does not usually cause hair loss. However, in rare cases, it may cause thinning of the hair.
  • Carboplatin: This drug is used to treat ovarian and lung cancer, and does not typically cause hair loss.

If you are concerned about hair loss during chemotherapy, speak with your doctor about whether these drugs may be an option for you.

How Does Chemotherapy Cause Hair Loss?

Chemotherapy drugs work by targeting rapidly dividing cells, which includes cancer cells. However, hair follicle cells also divide rapidly, making them vulnerable to the effects of chemotherapy. As a result, many chemotherapy drugs can cause hair loss or thinning.

The severity of hair loss during chemotherapy can vary from person to person, and may depend on the type and dose of chemotherapy drugs used. Hair loss may be temporary or permanent, and may occur on the scalp, face, and other parts of the body.

Hair Care Tips During Chemotherapy

If you are undergoing chemotherapy and experiencing hair loss, there are several things you can do to care for your scalp and hair:

  • Gently wash your hair: Use a mild shampoo and gently massage your scalp when washing your hair. Avoid using hot water and harsh chemicals.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling tools can damage hair and make it more prone to breakage. Stick to air drying your hair or using a cool setting on your blow dryer.
  • Protect your scalp: Wear a hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun and cold weather.
  • Consider a wig or hairpiece: Many people choose to wear a wig or hairpiece during chemotherapy to help them feel more confident and comfortable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can hair loss from chemotherapy be prevented?

Unfortunately, hair loss from chemotherapy cannot be prevented. However, there are certain scalp cooling devices that have been shown to reduce hair loss during chemotherapy.

Does hair grow back after chemotherapy?

Yes, hair will typically grow back after chemotherapy. However, it may take several months for hair to start regrowing, and the texture and color of the hair may be different than before.

Can I color or perm my hair during chemotherapy?

It is generally recommended to avoid coloring or perming your hair during chemotherapy, as these treatments can further damage hair that is already weakened by chemotherapy.

In conclusion, while hair loss is a common side effect of chemotherapy, there are certain types of chemotherapy drugs that do not cause hair loss. If you are concerned about hair loss during chemotherapy, speak with your doctor about your options. And remember, even if you do experience hair loss, there are steps you can take to care for your scalp and hair during chemotherapy.
