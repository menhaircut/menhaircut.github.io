Do Long Haired Dogs Shed More A Guide To Understanding Shedding In Long Haired Breeds

Written by Robby Jun 16, 2023 · 4 min read

Long haired dogs are often loved for their beautiful and luxurious coats, but many pet owners wonder if they shed more than their short haired counterparts. In this guide, we’ll explore the question “do long haired dogs shed more?” and provide helpful tips for managing shedding in long haired breeds.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Manage Shedding in Long Haired Dogs
  3. Step by Step Guide to Grooming Long Haired Dogs
  4. Tips for Reducing Shedding in Long Haired Dogs
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care for Long Haired Dogs

Description

Long haired dogs do shed, but the amount of shedding varies depending on the breed and individual dog. Some long haired breeds, such as the Afghan Hound, shed very little due to their unique coat structure. Other breeds, like the Golden Retriever, have a dense undercoat and will shed heavily twice a year during shedding season.

It’s important to understand that shedding is a natural process for dogs and cannot be completely eliminated. However, there are steps you can take to manage shedding in long haired breeds and keep your home clean and fur-free.

How to Manage Shedding in Long Haired Dogs

The key to managing shedding in long haired dogs is regular grooming. Brushing your dog’s coat daily or every other day can help remove loose fur before it has a chance to fall out on your furniture and floors.

It’s also important to bathe your dog regularly to keep their coat and skin healthy. Use a high-quality dog shampoo and conditioner that is formulated for long haired breeds.

During shedding season, you may want to consider using a deshedding tool or undercoat rake to help remove excess fur. These tools are designed to reach deep into your dog’s coat and remove loose undercoat fur that is ready to shed.

Step by Step Guide to Grooming Long Haired Dogs

Here’s a step by step guide to grooming your long haired dog:

  1. Start by brushing your dog’s coat with a slicker brush to remove any tangles or mats.
  2. Next, use a metal comb to comb through your dog’s coat and remove any remaining tangles.
  3. Use a deshedding tool or undercoat rake during shedding season to remove loose undercoat fur.
  4. Bathe your dog with a high-quality dog shampoo and conditioner.
  5. Dry your dog’s coat thoroughly with a towel or blow dryer.
  6. Finish by brushing your dog’s coat one final time to remove any remaining loose fur.

Tips for Reducing Shedding in Long Haired Dogs

Along with regular grooming, there are other steps you can take to reduce shedding in your long haired dog:

  • Feed your dog a high-quality diet that is rich in omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids. These nutrients can help improve the health of your dog’s coat and reduce shedding.
  • Keep your home clean by vacuuming and dusting regularly. Use a lint roller or pet hair remover to remove fur from furniture and clothing.
  • Consider using a dog bed or blanket that can easily be washed and dried to reduce fur buildup in your home.
  • Visit your veterinarian regularly to ensure your dog is healthy and free of any underlying medical conditions that may cause excessive shedding.

FAQs

Do all long haired dogs shed?

Yes, all dogs shed to some extent. However, the amount of shedding can vary depending on the breed and individual dog.

How often should I groom my long haired dog?

It’s recommended to groom your long haired dog daily or every other day to prevent tangles and mats and manage shedding.

What is the best type of brush for long haired dogs?

A slicker brush is a great tool for removing tangles and mats in long haired dog breeds. A metal comb can also be used to smooth out the coat and remove any remaining tangles.

Hair Care for Long Haired Dogs

Along with regular grooming, there are other steps you can take to keep your long haired dog’s coat healthy and shiny:

  • Feed your dog a healthy diet that is rich in protein and vitamins.
  • Ensure your dog is getting enough exercise to promote healthy skin and coat.
  • Visit your veterinarian regularly to address any skin or coat issues and ensure your dog is up to date on vaccinations and preventative care.

By following these tips and regularly grooming your long haired dog, you can help manage shedding and keep your home fur-free.
