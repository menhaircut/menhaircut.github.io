How To Layer Cut Your Own Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 13, 2023 · 4 min read

Layered haircuts are a great way to add texture, volume, and movement to your hair. They are also versatile and can be customized to suit different face shapes and hair types. However, going to a salon every time you need a trim can be expensive and time-consuming. That's why learning how to layer cut your own hair can be a game-changer. In this post, we'll show you how to do just that, with easy-to-follow instructions and helpful tips.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Layer Cut Your Own Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Layer Cutting Your Hair
  4. FAQs

How to Layer Cut Your Own Hair

Before we begin, it's important to gather the right tools. You'll need:

  • A pair of sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • Hair clips or ties
  • A mirror

Once you have these items, you're ready to start layer cutting your hair.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it dry completely. This will make it easier to see your natural hair texture and how it falls. If you have curly or wavy hair, it's best to let it dry naturally. If you have straight hair, you can blow-dry it.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Next, use your comb to divide your hair into sections. Start by parting your hair down the middle and then creating two more sections on each side. Clip or tie each section separately.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Begin by cutting the bottom layer of your hair. Take one of the side sections and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and then cut it straight across. Repeat this step for the other side section. This will create a baseline for your layers.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Now it's time to cut the middle layer. Take the top section of your hair, and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers, and cut it at a slight angle, starting from the baseline you created in step 3. Repeat this step for the other top section.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, it's time to cut the top layer. Take the front section of your hair, and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers, and cut it at a steeper angle, starting from the middle layer. Repeat this step for the other front section.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a quick recap of how to layer cut your own hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Divide your hair into sections.
  3. Cut the bottom layer straight across.
  4. Cut the middle layer at a slight angle.
  5. Cut the top layer at a steeper angle.

Tips for Layer Cutting Your Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect layer cut:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Cut small sections of hair at a time, to avoid mistakes.
  • Always cut your hair when it's dry, not wet.
  • Choose the right length for your layers, based on your face shape and hair type.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. You can always go back and trim it later.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about layer cutting your hair:

Can I layer cut my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can. However, it's best to cut it when it's dry, and to cut it in its natural state. This will help you see how your hair will actually look.

How often should I layer cut my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. On average, you should trim your hair every 6 to 8 weeks.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't worry. You can always fix it by trimming the affected area or by going to a professional stylist.

Can I layer cut my hair if it's short?

Yes, you can. However, it may be more difficult to do it yourself, so you may want to seek professional help.

Hair Care After Layer Cutting

After you've layer cut your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. This includes:

  • Using a shampoo and conditioner that are suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoiding using heating tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Getting regular trims to maintain your layers.
  • Using hair masks or oils to keep your hair hydrated.

Layer cutting your hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. With these tips and instructions, you'll be able to achieve a professional-looking haircut without leaving your home. So go ahead and give it a try!
