Why Is Only The Underside Of My Hair Curly

Written by Frank Jun 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever noticed that only the underside of your hair is curly? It can be frustrating, especially if you're trying to achieve a uniform look. In this article, we'll explore the reasons why this happens and what you can do about it.

Description: Understanding the Science Behind Curly Hair

Before we dive into why the underside of your hair is curly, let's first understand the science behind curly hair. Curly hair is caused by the shape of the hair follicle. People with naturally curly hair have follicles that are oval-shaped, which causes the hair to grow in a corkscrew pattern.

On the other hand, people with straight hair have round hair follicles, which causes the hair to grow straight. The amount of curl in hair is also determined by the number of disulfide bonds between the hair proteins. The more disulfide bonds, the curlier the hair.

How to: Determine Your Hair Type

Knowing your hair type is essential in understanding why only the underside of your hair is curly. There are four main hair types:

  • Type 1: Straight hair
  • Type 2: Wavy hair
  • Type 3: Curly hair
  • Type 4: Coily hair

To determine your hair type, you can use the hair typing system developed by Andre Walker, Oprah Winfrey's hairstylist. This system categorizes hair types based on their texture and curl pattern.

Step by Step: How to Deal with Curly Undersides

Now that you understand the science behind curly hair and have determined your hair type, let's discuss how to deal with curly undersides:

  1. Embrace it. Instead of trying to fight against your hair's natural pattern, embrace it. You can still achieve a uniform look by using products that define your curls.
  2. Get a layered haircut. A layered haircut can help distribute the curl pattern throughout your hair, creating a more uniform look.
  3. Use a diffuser. A diffuser attachment on your hairdryer can help distribute the heat evenly, reducing frizz and defining your curls.
  4. Use a curl-enhancing product. Products like mousse, gel, or curl cream can help define your curls and reduce frizz.

Tips: Maintaining Healthy Curly Hair

Regardless of whether only the underside of your hair is curly or you have a head full of curls, maintaining healthy hair is essential. Here are some tips to keep your curls looking their best:

  • Avoid heat styling. Heat styling tools can damage your hair and cause frizz. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo. Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, causing dryness and frizz.
  • Deep condition regularly. Deep conditioning treatments can help nourish and moisturize your curls.
  • Avoid brushing your hair. Brushing can cause frizz and disrupt your curl pattern. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

FAQ: Common Questions About Curly Hair

Here are some common questions people have about curly hair:

  • Can I straighten my curly hair?
  • Yes, you can straighten your curly hair. However, it's important to use heat protectant products to prevent damage to your hair.

  • How often should I wash my curly hair?
  • It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. Generally, people with curly hair should wash their hair once or twice a week to prevent dryness and breakage.

  • Can I color my curly hair?
  • Yes, you can color your curly hair. However, it's important to use color-safe products and deep condition regularly to prevent damage to your hair.

Hair Care: Love Your Curls

Now that you understand why only the underside of your hair is curly and how to deal with it, it's time to love your curls. Embrace your natural hair pattern and experiment with different products and styles to find what works best for you. Remember to take care of your hair by deep conditioning regularly and avoiding heat styling tools. With a little love and care, your curls will look their best.
