Which Hair Colours Suit Me A Guide For 2023

Written by Petter Oct 22, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering changing up your hair colour but don't know where to start? With so many options available, it can be overwhelming to choose the right shade that will complement your skin tone and facial features. In this guide, we will share some tips on which hair colours suit me and help you achieve your desired look.

Table of Contents

  • How to Determine Your Skin Tone
  • Best Hair Colours for Warm Skin Tones
  • Best Hair Colours for Cool Skin Tones
  • Hair Colour Tips
  • Hair Care Tips
  • FAQ

How to Determine Your Skin Tone

The first step in choosing the right hair colour is to determine your skin tone. Your skin tone falls into two categories: warm and cool. Warm skin tones have a yellow or golden undertone, while cool skin tones have a pink or bluish undertone.

To determine your skin tone, hold a piece of white paper next to your face in natural light. If your skin appears yellowish or golden, you have a warm skin tone. If your skin appears pinkish or bluish, you have a cool skin tone.

Best Hair Colours for Warm Skin Tones

If you have a warm skin tone, consider hair colours that have warm undertones, such as golden blonde, honey blonde, caramel, copper, or auburn. These colours will complement your skin and bring out the warmth in your complexion.

For a dramatic change, you can also consider going for a rich chocolate brown or a deep red. These colours will create a beautiful contrast against your warm skin tone.

Best Hair Colours for Cool Skin Tones

If you have a cool skin tone, opt for hair colours with cool undertones, such as ash blonde, platinum blonde, cool brown, or black. These colours will complement your skin and bring out the coolness in your complexion.

If you want to add some warmth to your look, consider going for a strawberry blonde or a light copper. These colours have warm undertones but are still suitable for cool skin tones.

Hair Colour Tips

When choosing a hair colour, consider your natural hair colour and the maintenance required. If you have dark hair, it may take several sessions to achieve a lighter shade without damaging your hair. On the other hand, if you have light hair, you may need to use toners to prevent brassy tones.

Additionally, consider how the hair colour will complement your wardrobe and makeup. If you wear a lot of warm colours, opt for warm hair colours. If you wear a lot of cool colours, opt for cool hair colours.

Hair Care Tips

After colouring your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain the vibrancy and health of your hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner specifically formulated for coloured hair to prevent fading and damage. Additionally, avoid using heat styling tools too often, and use a heat protectant when styling your hair.

It's also important to get regular trims to remove split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

FAQ

What hair colour suits me if I have a neutral skin tone?

If you have a neutral skin tone, you can opt for both warm and cool hair colours. Consider your personal preference and the maintenance required.

Can I dye my hair at home?

While it's possible to dye your hair at home, we recommend consulting with a professional hairstylist to achieve the best results and prevent damage to your hair.

How often should I touch up my hair colour?

This depends on the rate of your hair growth and the colour of your hair. It's recommended to touch up your hair colour every 4-6 weeks to maintain the vibrancy of the colour.

Choosing the right hair colour can be a daunting task, but with these tips, you can confidently choose a shade that will complement your skin tone and bring out your best features.
