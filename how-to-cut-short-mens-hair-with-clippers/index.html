How To Cut Short Men s Hair With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Oct 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can be daunting, but with the right tools and technique, it can be a breeze. In this guide, we will walk you through how to cut short men's hair with clippers. Whether you're looking to save money on haircuts or just want to try something new, this guide will give you the skills and confidence you need to cut your own hair.

Description

Cutting your own hair can be a great way to save money and time. Plus, you can experiment with different styles and looks without having to worry about the cost of a professional haircut. In this guide, we will focus on cutting short men's hair with clippers. Clippers are a great tool for cutting hair because they are easy to use, efficient, and can create a variety of haircuts. With a little practice and patience, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut at home.

How to Cut Short Men's Hair with Clippers

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step in cutting your own hair is to gather all the necessary tools. For cutting short men's hair with clippers, you will need:

  • A set of clippers
  • Clipper guards in various sizes
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A towel to drape over your shoulders

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to prepare your hair. Start by washing and drying your hair. Then, comb your hair in the direction that it naturally falls. If you have a specific hairstyle in mind, you can comb your hair in that direction instead.

Step 3: Choose the Right Guard Size

Clipper guards come in various sizes, so it's important to choose the right one for your desired haircut. For a short haircut, start with a larger guard size and work your way down to a smaller one if needed.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Begin by attaching the chosen guard to your clippers. Then, starting at the back of your head, begin to cut your hair in an upward motion. Make sure to use the comb to guide the clippers and create an even cut. Work your way around your head, cutting the hair on the sides and top.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

After you have cut the hair with the larger guard, switch to a smaller guard size to blend the hair. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using the comb to blend the hair together. Make sure to use a light touch and go slowly to avoid any mistakes.

Step 6: Clean Up the Edges

Once you have finished cutting and blending your hair, it's time to clean up the edges. Remove the guard from the clippers and use them to trim the hair around your ears and neck. Use the comb to create a straight line and go slowly to avoid any mistakes.

Step 7: Check Your Work

After you have finished cutting and cleaning up the edges, it's important to check your work. Use a mirror to check the back and sides of your head to make sure everything is even and blended.

Tips for Cutting Short Men's Hair with Clippers

  • Start with a larger guard size and work your way down to a smaller one if needed.
  • Use the comb to guide the clippers and create an even cut.
  • Go slowly and use a light touch to avoid mistakes.
  • Use the mirror to check your work and make adjustments if needed.

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair with clippers?

Yes, you can cut your own hair with clippers. With a little practice and patience, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut at home.

What tools do I need to cut my own hair with clippers?

To cut your own hair with clippers, you will need clippers, clipper guards in various sizes, a comb, a mirror, a spray bottle filled with water, and a towel to drape over your shoulders.

What guard size should I use for a short haircut?

For a short haircut, start with a larger guard size and work your way down to a smaller one if needed.

Hair Care

Cutting your own hair can be a great way to save money and time, but it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Make sure to wash and condition your hair regularly and use products that are appropriate for your hair type. Avoid over-styling your hair with heat tools and use a heat protectant spray if necessary. Finally, visit a professional stylist every few months for a trim to keep your hair looking its best.
