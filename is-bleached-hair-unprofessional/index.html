Is Bleached Hair Unprofessional The Truth Behind The Trend

Written by Eveline Jun 22, 2023 · 3 min read

If you’re thinking about bleaching your hair, you might be wondering if it’s considered unprofessional. It’s a common question, and the answer isn’t as simple as a yes or no. In this article, we’ll cover everything you need to know about bleached hair, including whether or not it’s considered unprofessional and how to take care of it.

Description

Bleached hair is a trend that has been around for decades. It involves using chemicals to remove the natural color from your hair and lighten it. The result is a bright, platinum blonde color that can be stunning when done correctly. However, bleaching your hair can also be damaging and requires a lot of maintenance to keep it healthy.

How to Bleach Your Hair

If you’re thinking about bleaching your hair, it’s important to do it correctly. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Choose the Right Bleach

There are many different types of bleach on the market, so it’s important to choose the right one for your hair type. If you have thin or fragile hair, you’ll want to use a gentle bleach. If you have thick or coarse hair, you can use a stronger bleach.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

Before you bleach your hair, you’ll need to prepare it by washing it with a clarifying shampoo. This will remove any product buildup and make sure the bleach can penetrate your hair evenly.

Step 3: Mix the Bleach

Follow the instructions on the bleach kit to mix the bleach and developer. Make sure you wear gloves and mix the bleach in a well-ventilated area.

Step 4: Apply the Bleach

Apply the bleach to your hair using a brush or applicator. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots. Leave the bleach on for the recommended amount of time, then rinse it out thoroughly.

Step 5: Tone Your Hair

After you’ve bleached your hair, you’ll need to tone it to get rid of any yellow or brassy tones. Use a toner in a cool shade of blonde to neutralize the color.

Tips for Maintaining Bleached Hair

Once you’ve bleached your hair, it’s important to take care of it to keep it healthy. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to moisturize your hair
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can further damage your hair
  • Use a purple shampoo to keep your hair from turning yellow
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

FAQs

Q: Is bleached hair unprofessional?

A: It depends on the workplace. In some industries, such as fashion or entertainment, bleached hair might be considered trendy and fashionable. In more traditional industries, such as finance or law, it might be considered unprofessional.

Q: Can I bleach my hair at home?

A: While it’s possible to bleach your hair at home, we recommend going to a professional stylist. Bleaching your hair can be tricky and can result in damage if not done correctly.

Q: Will bleaching my hair damage it?

A: Yes, bleaching your hair can be damaging. It’s important to take care of your hair and use deep conditioning treatments to keep it healthy.

Final Thoughts

So, is bleached hair unprofessional? The answer is that it depends on the workplace and the industry. While bleached hair might be trendy and fashionable in some industries, it might be considered unprofessional in others. If you’re thinking about bleaching your hair, make sure you do it correctly and take care of your hair to keep it healthy.
