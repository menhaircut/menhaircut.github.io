Short Wedge Haircuts For Over 60 A Stylish And Practical Choice

Written by Daniel Sep 27, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you looking for a chic and low-maintenance haircut that suits your age and lifestyle? Short wedge haircuts for over 60 might be the answer. This classic style features a tapered back and sides, with longer layers on top that can be styled in various ways. It's versatile, timeless, and easy to maintain, making it a popular choice among mature women who want to look their best without spending too much time or money on their hair.

Description: What is a Short Wedge Haircut?

A short wedge haircut is a type of bob that has a stacked or graduated shape. It's called "wedge" because the back and sides are cut at an angle, creating a triangular or wedge-like silhouette. The hair is shorter in the back and gradually longer towards the front, with the longest layers framing the face. This haircut can be customized to suit different face shapes, hair textures, and personal preferences. It can be worn straight, curly, wavy, or tousled, depending on the occasion and mood.

How to: Get a Short Wedge Haircut

If you're interested in getting a short wedge haircut, here are some tips to follow:

1. Find a good stylist

Look for a hairstylist who has experience in cutting short hair and understands your needs and preferences. Bring some pictures of short wedge haircuts that you like and discuss them with your stylist. They can advise you on the best length, shape, and texture for your hair type and face shape.

2. Prepare for the cut

Wash your hair and let it air dry or blow-dry it straight. Avoid using any styling products or accessories that can alter the natural texture or shape of your hair. Wear comfortable clothes and bring a few hair ties or clips to keep your hair out of the way during the cut.

3. Sit still and communicate

Relax and trust your stylist to do their job. If you feel uncomfortable or unsure about any step, don't hesitate to ask questions or express your concerns. Communication is key to getting the haircut you want and avoiding any misunderstandings.

4. Style and maintain the cut

Once you have your short wedge haircut, you can style it in different ways using a blow dryer, flat iron, curling wand, or styling products. You can also experiment with different partings, bangs, or accessories to change up your look. To maintain the cut, schedule regular trims every 4-6 weeks to keep the shape and length in check.

Step by Step: How to Style a Short Wedge Haircut

Here's a simple tutorial on how to style a short wedge haircut:

1. Start with clean and dry hair

Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner, then towel dry or blow-dry it until it's completely dry. Use a heat protectant spray or serum to prevent damage from heat styling.

2. Section your hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. Start with the back and work your way to the front, leaving the top layer for last.

3. Use a flat iron or curling wand

Depending on the look you want, use a flat iron to straighten your hair or a curling wand to create loose waves or curls. Work on one section at a time, starting from the roots and moving towards the ends. Hold the iron or wand for a few seconds, then release and move on to the next section.

4. Apply finishing products

Once you've styled all the sections, apply a small amount of finishing products such as hairspray, serum, or wax to add shine, hold, and texture. Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy or stiff.

5. Adjust the shape and volume

Use your fingers or a comb to adjust the shape and volume of your hair. Lift the roots to create more height and body, or smooth down any flyaways or frizz. You can also add a headband, scarf, or barrette to enhance your hairstyle.

Tips: How to Make the Most of Your Short Wedge Haircut

Here are some tips to help you rock your short wedge haircut:

1. Embrace your natural texture

If you have curly or wavy hair, don't try to straighten it all the time. Instead, embrace your natural texture and enhance it with the right products and techniques. Curly short wedge haircuts can look adorable and youthful, adding volume and bounce to your hair.

2. Choose the right color

Consider adding some highlights or lowlights to your short wedge haircut to create depth and dimension. Choose a color that complements your skin tone and eye color, and consult with your stylist to find the best shade and technique for your hair type.

3. Experiment with different styles

Don't be afraid to try different styles and accessories with your short wedge haircut. You can wear it sleek and polished for a formal event, tousled and messy for a casual day, or edgy and textured for a night out. Play with headbands, scarves, hats, or earrings to add some flair to your look.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions About Short Wedge Haircuts for Over 60

1. Is a short wedge haircut suitable for all face shapes?

A short wedge haircut can be customized to suit different face shapes, but it's generally more flattering for oval or heart-shaped faces. If you have a square or round face, you may want to opt for a softer or longer bob that frames your face more gently.

2. Can I wear bangs with a short wedge haircut?

Yes, you can wear bangs with a short wedge haircut, but you need to choose the right type of bangs for your face shape and hair type. Side-swept bangs, blunt bangs, or wispy bangs can all work with a short wedge haircut, depending on your personal style.

3. How often do I need to trim my short wedge haircut?

It's recommended to trim your short wedge haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and length. You can schedule a regular appointment with your stylist or touch up the back and sides yourself with a clipper or scissors.

Hair Care: How to Keep Your Short Wedge Haircut Healthy and Shiny

Here are some tips to help you take care of your short wedge haircut:

1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner

Avoid using harsh or sulfated shampoos and conditioners that can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause dryness or breakage. Look for products that are specifically designed for your hair type and texture, and contain nourishing ingredients such as argan oil, keratin, or vitamins.

2. Protect your hair from heat and sun

Use a heat protectant spray or serum before blow-drying, flat ironing, or curling your hair. Avoid exposing your hair to direct sunlight or high temperatures for prolonged periods, as this can damage the cuticle and cause frizz or split ends. Wear a hat or scarf if you're going to be outdoors for a long time.

3. Moisturize and nourish your hair

Apply a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to restore moisture and vitality to your hair. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to prevent dryness and add shine. Avoid over-styling your hair or using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy or dull.

Short wedge haircuts for over 60 are a great way to look stylish, practical, and confident at any age. Whether you prefer a classic or modern look, this haircut can be customized to suit your needs and personality. Follow these tips and tricks to get the most out of your short wedge haircut and enjoy a low-maintenance and chic hairstyle.
