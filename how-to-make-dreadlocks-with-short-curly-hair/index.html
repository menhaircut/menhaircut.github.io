How To Make Dreadlocks With Short Curly Hair

Written by Oliver Jun 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your short curly hair and want to switch things up with a new hairstyle? Dreadlocks are a great option for those with short curly hair, but many people believe that it is only possible to have them with long hair. However, this is simply not true. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to make dreadlocks with short curly hair.

Description

Dreadlocks are a hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are strands of hair that have been matted together to form long, twisted strands. Dreadlocks can be made with any hair type, and short curly hair is no exception. It may take more time and effort to create dreadlocks with short hair, but it is possible.

How to

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step in making dreadlocks with short curly hair is to wash your hair. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or oils from your hair. Clean hair is necessary to create dreadlocks because it will help the hair lock together more easily.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections using a comb. The sections should be no larger than the width of your finger. Use hair ties to keep the sections separate from each other. This will help you create even and uniform dreadlocks.

Step 3: Apply Dread Wax

Apply a small amount of dread wax to each section of hair. This will help the hair lock together and make the process of creating dreadlocks easier. Be careful not to use too much wax, as this can make your hair greasy and difficult to work with.

Step 4: Backcomb Your Hair

Using a fine-toothed comb, backcomb each section of hair. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends. This will help to create knots in your hair, which will eventually form the dreadlocks. Be gentle when backcombing your hair to avoid damaging it.

Step 5: Twist Your Hair

Twist each section of hair in the same direction to help the knots form into dreadlocks. Keep twisting until the hair starts to lock together. You can use a crochet hook to help the hair lock together if necessary.

Step 6: Maintain Your Dreadlocks

Maintaining your dreadlocks is important to keep them looking neat and tidy. You should wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo and avoid using any products that contain oils or silicone. You can also palm roll your dreadlocks to help them maintain their shape.

Tips

  • Be patient. It may take several months for your dreadlocks to fully form.
  • Avoid using a hairdryer, as this can cause your hair to become brittle and break.
  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to avoid damaging your dreadlocks while you sleep.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. It can be difficult to create dreadlocks on your own, so enlist the help of a friend or professional stylist.

FAQ

Can you make dreadlocks with short curly hair?

Yes, it is possible to make dreadlocks with short curly hair. It may take more time and effort, but it can be done.

Do you need to use wax to create dreadlocks?

No, you don't need to use wax to create dreadlocks. However, it can make the process easier and help the hair lock together more quickly.

How often should you wash your dreadlocks?

You should wash your dreadlocks once a week with a residue-free shampoo. Avoid using any products that contain oils or silicone.

Hair Care

Once you have created your dreadlocks, hair care is important to keep them looking neat and tidy. You should wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo and avoid using any products that contain oils or silicone. You can also palm roll your dreadlocks to help them maintain their shape. Be patient, as it may take several months for your dreadlocks to fully form.

With these tips and steps, you can now make dreadlocks with short curly hair. Remember to be patient, take care of your hair, and enjoy your new hairstyle.
