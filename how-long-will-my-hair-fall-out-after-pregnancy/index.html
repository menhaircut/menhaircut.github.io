How Long Will My Hair Fall Out After Pregnancy Everything You Need To Know

Written by Aprilia Sep 06, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're a new mom, you may be wondering how long will my hair fall out after pregnancy? Don't worry; it's completely normal. Postpartum hair loss is a common occurrence for many women, and it can be frustrating to deal with. In this article, we'll go over everything you need to know about postpartum hair loss, including why it happens, how long it lasts, and what you can do to manage it.

What is Postpartum Hair Loss?

Postpartum hair loss, also known as postpartum alopecia, is a temporary hair loss that can occur after giving birth. During pregnancy, your body produces higher levels of estrogen, which can cause your hair to grow thicker and fuller than usual. However, after giving birth, your hormone levels drop, which can cause your hair to fall out.

How Long Does Postpartum Hair Loss Last?

Postpartum hair loss typically begins around three months after giving birth and can last up to a year. The amount of hair loss varies from person to person, but it's usually not severe enough to cause baldness.

How Can I Manage Postpartum Hair Loss?

While you can't completely stop postpartum hair loss, there are things you can do to manage it. Here are some tips:

1. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid pulling your hair back tightly or using harsh hair treatments. Be gentle when brushing your hair and avoid using heat styling tools.

2. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet can help promote healthy hair growth. Make sure you're getting enough protein, iron, and vitamins A, C, and E.

3. Take Care of Your Scalp

Keep your scalp clean and healthy by washing your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo. You can also use a scalp massage brush to stimulate hair growth.

4. Consider Supplements

Some women find that taking supplements like biotin or prenatal vitamins can help with postpartum hair loss. Talk to your doctor before starting any new supplements.

FAQ

Does Postpartum Hair Loss Happen to Everyone?

No, not everyone experiences postpartum hair loss. It's more common in women who have had multiple pregnancies or who have a family history of hair loss.

Will My Hair Grow Back After Postpartum Hair Loss?

Yes, your hair should grow back once your hormone levels return to normal. However, it may take several months for your hair to fully regrow.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to help manage postpartum hair loss:

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

A wide-tooth comb can help prevent further hair loss by minimizing breakage.

2. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, like braids or ponytails, can pull on your hair and cause further damage.

3. Be Patient

Postpartum hair loss can be frustrating, but it's important to be patient. Remember that it's a temporary condition, and your hair will eventually grow back.

Final Thoughts

Postpartum hair loss can be a challenging experience, but it's important to remember that it's a normal part of the postpartum journey. Take care of your hair and scalp, eat a healthy diet, and be patient. If you're concerned about your hair loss, talk to your doctor. They can help determine if there's an underlying medical condition causing your hair loss and recommend treatment options.
