How To Thin Out Your Bangs At Home

Written by Eveline Sep 21, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're tired of your bangs feeling too thick and heavy, or if you just want to switch up your look, you might be considering thinning them out. While you can always go to a salon to have this done professionally, it's also possible to do it at home. In this post, we'll show you how to thin out your bangs at home in a few simple steps.

How to Thin Out Your Bangs at Home

Before we dive into the step-by-step process, let's talk about what you'll need. For this DIY project, you'll need:

  • A pair of sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. This will help you see how your bangs naturally fall and make it easier to cut them.

Step 2: Comb Your Bangs

Using your comb, gently comb your bangs down so they cover your forehead. Make sure they're evenly distributed and there aren't any clumps or tangles.

Step 3: Determine How Much You Want to Thin Them Out

Decide how much you want to thin out your bangs. If you just want to remove a little bit of bulk, you can thin out the ends. If you want to thin them out more, you can cut into them a bit more.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Hold your scissors vertically and make small, quick snips into your bangs. Start at the ends and work your way up, taking small sections at a time. Be sure to cut at an angle rather than straight across to avoid a blunt cut.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After you've cut a few sections, step back and look in the mirror to see how it's looking. Make any necessary adjustments to the length or the amount of hair you're removing.

Tips for Thinning Out Your Bangs at Home

  • Remember to take it slow and cut small sections at a time. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Consider using thinning shears instead of regular scissors to create a more natural, layered look.
  • If you're not confident in your cutting abilities, consider asking a friend or family member for help.

FAQs

Can I thin out my bangs with regular scissors?

Yes, you can. Just make sure your scissors are sharp and you're cutting at an angle to avoid a blunt cut.

How much should I thin out my bangs?

This depends on your personal preference. If you just want to remove a little bit of bulk, try thinning out the ends. If you want a more drastic change, you can cut into them more.

Hair Care After Thinning Out Your Bangs

After you've thinned out your bangs, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking their best. Avoid using too much heat on them and use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage. You can also use a hair oil or serum to keep them smooth and shiny.

Thinning out your bangs at home can be a great way to switch up your look without spending a lot of money. Just be sure to take it slow and use the right tools to get the best results.
