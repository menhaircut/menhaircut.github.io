Description

Since the outbreak of Covid 19, people have been grappling with different symptoms that come with the virus. Alopecia, or hair loss, has been reported as one of the symptoms that some Covid 19 patients experience. Although not a very common symptom, it is advisable to know more about it and how to deal with it.

How to Identify Covid 19-induced Alopecia

Alopecia can present itself in different ways, depending on the individual. However, there are common signs to look out for if you suspect that you may be suffering from Covid 19-induced alopecia. They include:

  • Increased hair shedding
  • Bald patches
  • Thinning hair

If you notice any of these signs, it is advisable to seek medical attention to confirm whether it is due to Covid 19 or other underlying causes.

Step-by-Step Guide to Prevent Alopecia

While it is not entirely clear how Covid 19 causes alopecia, there are steps you can take to prevent it. Here are some:

  1. Practice good hygiene by washing your hands frequently and avoiding touching your face
  2. Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals
  3. Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water
  4. Manage your stress levels by engaging in activities that help you relax
  5. Get enough sleep to allow your body to rest and rejuvenate

Adopting these steps can help boost your immune system, which is vital in preventing Covid 19 and other underlying conditions that may cause alopecia.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair During Covid 19

While dealing with Covid 19-induced alopecia, it is essential to maintain good hair care practices to prevent further hair loss. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or hairbrush to detangle your hair gently
  • Avoid excessive heat styling

Following these tips can help prevent further hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.

FAQs

Can Covid 19 cause hair loss in everyone?

No. Hair loss is not a common symptom of Covid 19. However, it has been reported in some Covid 19 patients.

Is Covid 19-induced alopecia permanent?

Not necessarily. Hair loss due to Covid 19 may be temporary and may resolve itself once the underlying condition is treated.

Can stress from Covid 19 cause alopecia?

Yes. Stress is a known cause of alopecia, and the stress that comes with Covid 19 may trigger hair loss in some individuals.

Hair Care Routine for Alopecia Sufferers

If you are suffering from Covid 19-induced alopecia, it is essential to take good care of your hair to prevent further hair loss. Here is a hair care routine to follow:

  1. Wash your hair gently using a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  2. Avoid heat styling and harsh chemicals on your hair
  3. Massage your scalp to increase blood flow and promote healthy hair growth
  4. Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals to nourish your hair from the inside out
  5. Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water

Following a good hair care routine can help prevent further hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.

Conclusion

Covid 19-induced alopecia is not a common symptom of the virus. However, if you notice any signs of hair loss, it is essential to seek medical attention to determine the underlying cause. Following good hair care practices can help prevent further hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. Remember to practice good hygiene, eat a healthy diet, manage your stress levels, and get enough sleep to boost your immune system and prevent Covid 19 and other underlying conditions that may cause alopecia.
