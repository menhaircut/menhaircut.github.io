Does Blow Drying Curly Hair Make It Frizzy

Written by Aprilia Nov 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

In this day and age, people with curly hair have a lot of options when it comes to styling their hair. One of the most popular methods of styling is blow drying. However, there is a common concern among people with curly hair that blow drying can make their curls frizzy. In this article, we will explore this concern and answer the question, "Does blow drying curly hair make it frizzy?"

Description

Curly hair is naturally prone to frizz due to its texture and structure. When curly hair is wet, the hair shaft swells and the cuticle layer opens up, making it more susceptible to frizz. Blow drying curly hair can exacerbate this problem if not done properly. However, blow drying can also be beneficial for curly hair if done correctly. The key is to use the right technique and tools.

How to Blow Dry Curly Hair

The first step to blow drying curly hair is to choose the right hair dryer. Look for a hair dryer with a diffuser attachment, which helps to distribute the heat evenly and reduce frizz. When blow drying curly hair, it's important to use a low heat setting to avoid damaging the hair. Start at the roots and work your way down to the tips, using the diffuser attachment to gently scrunch the hair as you go.

Step by Step Guide to Blow Dry Curly Hair

1. Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Sulfates can strip the natural oils from your hair, making it more prone to frizz.

2. Towel dry your hair gently to remove excess water. Avoid rubbing the towel vigorously, as this can cause more frizz.

3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling cream to your hair to help define your curls.

4. Attach the diffuser to your hair dryer and turn it on to a low heat setting.

5. Start at the roots and work your way down to the tips, using the diffuser to gently scrunch the hair as you go.

6. Once your hair is dry, you can use a serum or oil to add shine and reduce frizz.

Tips for Blow Drying Curly Hair

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.

2. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to distribute the heat evenly and reduce frizz.

3. Use a low heat setting to avoid damaging your hair.

4. Avoid touching your hair while it's drying, as this can cause more frizz.

5. Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling cream to help define your curls.

FAQs

Q: Does blow drying curly hair make it frizzy?

A: Blow drying curly hair can make it frizzy if not done properly. However, if you use the right technique and tools, blow drying can actually reduce frizz and enhance your curls.

Q: How often should I blow dry my curly hair?

A: It's best to limit blow drying to once or twice a week, as frequent heat styling can damage your hair.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires a bit of extra care to keep it healthy and frizz-free. Here are some tips for caring for curly hair:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.

2. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, as brushes can cause breakage and frizz.

3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling cream to help define your curls.

4. Avoid touching your hair while it's drying, as this can cause more frizz.

5. Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz.

In conclusion, blow drying curly hair can make it frizzy if not done properly. However, with the right technique and tools, blow drying can actually reduce frizz and enhance your curls. Remember to use a diffuser attachment, a low heat setting, and gently scrunch your hair as you dry it. With a bit of extra care and attention, you can keep your curly hair looking healthy, shiny, and frizz-free.
