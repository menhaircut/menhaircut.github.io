How To Fix A Dress That Was Cut Too Short

Written by Eveline Jul 25, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Have you ever had the perfect dress, only to find out it was cut too short? It can be frustrating and disappointing, but don't worry - there are ways to fix it. In this article, we'll go over how to fix a dress that was cut too short in easy steps. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Fix a Dress That Was Cut Too Short
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips to Keep in Mind
  • Frequently Asked Questions

How to Fix a Dress That Was Cut Too Short

Before we get into the steps, it's important to note that the method you use to fix the dress will depend on the type of dress and the fabric it's made of. Here are a few options:

  • Add fabric to the bottom
  • Add lace to the bottom
  • Add a contrasting fabric to the bottom
  • Make the dress into a top or tunic

Now, let's get into the step-by-step guide.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Measure the Dress

Measure how much length you need to add to the dress. This will help you determine how much fabric or lace you'll need to buy.

Step 2: Choose the Fabric or Lace

Choose a fabric or lace that matches the color and style of the dress. Make sure it's the same weight and texture as the original fabric.

Step 3: Cut the Fabric or Lace

Cut the fabric or lace to the desired length. Make sure to add an extra inch for hemming.

Step 4: Pin the Fabric or Lace

Pin the fabric or lace to the bottom of the dress, right sides together. Make sure to match the seams and edges.

Step 5: Sew the Fabric or Lace

Sew the fabric or lace to the dress using a straight stitch. Use a matching thread color and sew close to the edge.

Step 6: Hem the Dress

Hem the bottom of the dress using a straight stitch or a blind hem stitch. Make sure to fold the fabric or lace under twice to prevent fraying.

Tips to Keep in Mind

  • Always use a matching thread color.
  • Make sure to match the seams and edges when pinning and sewing.
  • Use a serger or zigzag stitch to prevent fraying.
  • Try the dress on after hemming to make sure the length is correct.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I fix a dress that was cut too short without adding fabric or lace?

Yes, you can turn the dress into a top or tunic by cutting off the bottom and hemming the edges.

What if I don't know how to sew?

You can take the dress to a tailor or seamstress to have it fixed.

Can I use a different color fabric or lace?

Yes, you can use a contrasting color for a unique look.

Hair Care While Fixing Your Dress

While fixing your dress, it's important to protect your hair from the fabric and thread. Here are some tips:

  • Wear a hair tie or headband to keep your hair out of the way.
  • Use a lint roller to remove any loose threads or fabric fibers from your hair.
  • Wash your hair after you're done to remove any residue or debris.

And there you have it - a simple guide on how to fix a dress that was cut too short. With a few steps and some patience, you can extend the life of your favorite dress and make it wearable again.
