How To Cut Your Own Hair To Side Swept Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Sep 09, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of your current hairstyle and looking for a change? Have you been considering side swept bangs but don't want to spend money at the salon? Cutting your own hair may seem daunting, but with the right technique and tools, you can achieve the look you desire. In this article, we will guide you on how to cut your own hair to side swept bangs in relaxed English language. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Your Own Hair to Side Swept Bangs
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

How to Cut Your Own Hair to Side Swept Bangs

Side swept bangs are a trendy and versatile hairstyle that can flatter any face shape. Before you start cutting, it's important to gather the right tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips to section off hair
  • Spray bottle with water

Once you have your tools ready, follow these steps to cut your own hair to side swept bangs.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with dry, styled hair. Comb your hair straight down and separate the section you want to cut into a triangle shape. The point of the triangle should be where you want the center of your bangs to be.
  2. Use your comb to hold the hair in place and cut straight across. Be sure to cut only a small amount at a time.
  3. Once you have cut the center section, move on to the sides. Comb down the hair on one side and twist it away from your face. Cut at an angle towards the center. Repeat on the other side.
  4. Check for any uneven pieces and trim as needed. Remember, it's easier to take off more hair later than to try to fix a mistake.
  5. Use your fingers to style your bangs and check if they are even. If you need to make any adjustments, wet your bangs with the spray bottle and cut small amounts at a time.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but these tips can help make the process easier:

  • Start small and cut less than you think you need. You can always cut more later.
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends.
  • Don't cut your bangs straight across. The angle should be slightly longer on the sides.
  • Wet your hair before cutting to make it easier to manage.
  • Take your time and be patient. It's better to go slow and get it right than rush and make a mistake.

FAQs

Here are some common questions people have about cutting their own hair to side swept bangs:

Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your hair if it's curly. However, be aware that curly hair can shrink when it dries, so cut less than you think you need.

How often should I trim my bangs?

It's recommended to trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh.

What do I do if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. Wet your hair and try to fix the mistake. If it's too late, you may need to visit a salon to get it fixed.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After cutting your own hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage.
  • Avoid heat styling tools and let your hair air dry whenever possible.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.

Cutting your own hair to side swept bangs can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve the look you desire without spending money at the salon. Remember to take your time, be patient, and follow our step-by-step guide for the best results!
