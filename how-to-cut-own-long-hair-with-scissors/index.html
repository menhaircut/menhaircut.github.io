How To Cut Own Long Hair With Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to cut your own long hair with scissors, you may be feeling a bit intimidated. After all, you don't want to make any mistakes or end up with a botched haircut. However, with the right tools and techniques, cutting your own hair can be done successfully. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of how to cut your own long hair with scissors, as well as provide some tips and tricks to make the process easier.

Description

Cutting your own hair can be a great way to save money and experiment with different styles. However, it's important to approach the process with caution, especially if you're cutting long hair. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home.

How to Cut Own Long Hair with Scissors

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, it's important to gather all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you have a clean surface to work with.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Next, use your comb to section your hair. Start by parting your hair down the middle, then divide each half into three sections (left, middle, and right). Clip each section out of the way, leaving only the section you want to cut loose.

Step 4: Cut the Ends

Starting with the loose section, comb through your hair to remove any knots or tangles. Then, hold the ends of your hair between your fingers and cut straight across. Be sure to use the hair cutting scissors, as regular scissors can damage your hair.

Step 5: Repeat on Remaining Sections

After you've cut the ends of the loose section, clip it back and move on to the next section. Repeat the same process of combing, holding, and cutting straight across. Continue until you've cut all the sections.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

Once you've finished cutting all the sections, check for evenness. Use your comb to part your hair in different directions and check that the ends are all the same length.

Step 7: Trim Any Uneven Ends

If you notice any uneven ends, trim them using the same technique as before. Comb through the section, hold the ends between your fingers, and cut straight across.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

After you've finished cutting, style your hair as desired. You can use a curling iron, straightener, or other styling tools to create your desired look.

Tips

Here are a few tips to make cutting your own hair with scissors easier:

  • Invest in a pair of high-quality hair cutting scissors. This will ensure a clean cut and prevent damage to your hair.
  • Don't cut too much off at once. It's better to cut a little bit at a time and check for evenness than to cut too much and end up with a lopsided haircut.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so don't be afraid to take a break if your arms or hands start to feel fatigued.

FAQ

Here are some commonly asked questions about cutting your own hair with scissors:

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your own curly hair with scissors. However, it's important to be careful and cut your hair when it's dry, as wet curly hair can shrink and appear shorter than it actually is.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. You can always trim any uneven ends or visit a professional stylist to fix any major mistakes.

How often should I cut my own hair?

The frequency of haircuts depends on your hair type and desired style. However, most people should aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep their hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

After cutting your own hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips for caring for your hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage and breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your haircut.

By following these tips, you can achieve a beautiful, healthy head of hair whether you cut it yourself or visit a professional stylist.
