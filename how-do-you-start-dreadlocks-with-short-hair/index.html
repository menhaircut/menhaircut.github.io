How To Start Dreadlocks With Short Hair

Written by Aprilia Aug 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have short hair and want to start dreadlocks, you might think that it's not possible. However, with the right techniques and a little patience, you can achieve the look you desire. In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about starting dreadlocks with short hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Section Your Hair
  • How to Backcomb
  • How to Twist and Rip
  • How to Palm Roll

  • FAQ

How to Prepare

Before you start dreadlocks, you need to make sure that your hair is healthy and strong enough to withstand the process. This means that you should avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair. You should also avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils. Instead, use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for dreadlocks.

How to Section Your Hair

The first step in starting dreadlocks is to section your hair. This involves dividing your hair into small, equal sections using a comb or your fingers. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and the size of the dreadlocks you want to create.

How to Backcomb

Backcombing is a popular method for starting dreadlocks with short hair. This involves teasing the hair in each section towards the scalp using a fine-toothed comb. This creates tangles in the hair that will eventually form into dreadlocks.

How to Twist and Rip

Another method for starting dreadlocks with short hair is twisting and ripping. This involves twisting the hair in each section and then pulling it apart to create tangles. This method is a bit more gentle on the hair than backcombing, but it can take longer to achieve the desired look.

How to Palm Roll

Once you've created tangles in your hair, you can start palm rolling to encourage the hair to lock together. This involves rolling each dreadlock between your palms in a circular motion. This helps to smooth out any bumps and encourages the hair to knot together.

How to Maintain Your Dreadlocks

Once you've started your dreadlocks, you'll need to maintain them to keep them looking their best. This involves regular washing and conditioning, as well as re-twisting and palm rolling to keep the hair locked together. You should also avoid using any products that contain wax, as this can build up in your hair and make it difficult to maintain your dreadlocks.

Tips for Starting Dreadlocks with Short Hair

Here are some tips to help you start dreadlocks with short hair:

  • Be patient - it can take several months to achieve the look you want
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for dreadlocks
  • Experiment with different methods to find the one that works best for your hair
  • Be prepared to invest time and effort into maintaining your dreadlocks

FAQ

Q: How long does it take to start dreadlocks with short hair?

A: It can take several months to start dreadlocks with short hair, depending on the thickness of your hair and the method you use.

Q: Can I start dreadlocks with relaxed hair?

A: Yes, you can start dreadlocks with relaxed hair. However, you should avoid using any chemicals on your hair while you're starting your dreadlocks, as this can damage your hair.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

To keep your dreadlocks looking their best, you should follow a few basic hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using products that contain wax or other heavy ingredients
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
  • Re-twist and palm roll your dreadlocks regularly to keep them locked together
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair to avoid damaging the dreadlocks

With these tips and techniques, you can start dreadlocks with short hair and enjoy a unique, stylish look. Remember to be patient and take good care of your hair to keep your dreadlocks looking their best.
