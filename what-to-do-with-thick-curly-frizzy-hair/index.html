What To Do With Thick Curly Frizzy Hair Tips Tricks And Hair Care

Written by Luffy Aug 23, 2023 · 5 min read

Thick curly frizzy hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can look stunning and voluminous, it can also be difficult to manage and style. If you're looking for ways to tame your locks and rock your curly hair with confidence, then you've come to the right place.

Description

Thick curly frizzy hair is characterized by tight curls, lots of volume, and a tendency to frizz. This hair type can be difficult to manage because the curls can be unruly and the hair can become dry and damaged if not properly cared for.

However, with the right hair care routine and styling techniques, you can transform your thick curly frizzy hair into a stunning crown of curls that you'll love to show off.

How to Tame Thick Curly Frizzy Hair

If you're struggling with your thick curly frizzy hair, don't worry! There are plenty of ways to tame your locks and get them under control. Here are some tips and tricks that can help:

1. Use the Right Products

The right hair products can make all the difference when it comes to managing thick curly frizzy hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as curl defining creams, leave-in conditioners, and anti-frizz serums.

When choosing products, be sure to read the labels and avoid anything that contains sulfates or alcohol, as these can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.

2. Embrace Your Natural Curls

One of the biggest mistakes people with curly hair make is trying to fight their natural texture. If you have thick curly frizzy hair, embrace your curls and work with them rather than against them.

Instead of straightening your hair or trying to force it to behave in a certain way, experiment with different styling techniques that enhance your curls and make them look their best.

3. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and prone to frizz. Instead, try to wash your hair no more than twice a week, and use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo.

If you need to refresh your curls between washes, try using a dry shampoo or co-washing (using conditioner instead of shampoo).

4. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When combing or detangling your hair, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers instead of a brush. Brushes can disrupt the curl pattern and cause frizz, while wide-tooth combs are gentler and won't pull on your hair.

5. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can be damaging to your hair, especially if it's already prone to dryness and frizz. Try to avoid using hot tools like flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers, and opt for air-drying or diffusing your hair instead.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to taming your thick curly frizzy hair:

Step 1: Shampoo and Condition

Wash your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Be sure to massage the conditioner into your hair and let it sit for a few minutes before rinsing.

Step 2: Apply Products

Apply curl defining cream, leave-in conditioner, and anti-frizz serum to your hair, working in small sections from the ends to the roots.

Step 3: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.

Step 4: Air-Dry or Diffuse

Air-dry your hair or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair on a low heat setting. Be sure to scrunch your hair as it dries to enhance your curls.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, style it as desired. You can leave it down, pull it back into a ponytail or bun, or braid it for a different look.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for managing thick curly frizzy hair:

  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and tangles.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles and accessories to find what works best for you.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQ

Q: How often should I wash my thick curly frizzy hair?

A: It's best to wash your hair no more than twice a week to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.

Q: Can I straighten my thick curly frizzy hair?

A: While you can straighten your hair if you choose to, it's generally better to embrace your natural curls and work with them rather than against them.

Hair Care for Thick Curly Frizzy Hair

In addition to the tips and tricks mentioned above, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some additional hair care tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and heat by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair, as these can cause damage and breakage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and tricks, you can learn to love and embrace your thick curly frizzy hair. With the right hair care routine and styling techniques, you can transform your locks into a stunning crown of curls that you'll be proud to show off.
