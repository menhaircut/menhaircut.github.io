How To Cut Layers Into Your Own Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 18, 2023 · 4 min read
If you're looking to add some dimension and texture to your hair, cutting layers can be a great way to achieve that look. However, going to a salon can be expensive, and not everyone has the time to make an appointment. The good news is that cutting layers into your own hair is actually easier than you might think. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps and give you some tips for achieving the perfect layered look.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Cut Layers into Your Own Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Layers
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Layers

How to Cut Layers into Your Own Hair

Before we get started, it's important to note that cutting your own hair can be risky, especially if you've never done it before. If you're not confident in your ability to cut your hair, it's always better to seek the help of a professional. However, if you're feeling adventurous, here's what you'll need to get started:
  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have everything you need, let's get started:
  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. This will give you a better idea of how your hair naturally falls and will make it easier to see where you want to cut.
  2. Comb your hair straight down and part it down the middle.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the front of your head and comb it straight up. This will be the guide for the rest of your layers.
  4. Hold the section of hair straight up and use your scissors to cut across the ends. Be sure to cut in a straight line.
  5. Release the section of hair and let it fall back into place.
  6. Take another section of hair directly underneath the first section and use the first section as a guide to cut the same length. Continue working your way down the back of your head in this way.
  7. Move to the sides of your head and use the same technique to cut the layers at an angle.
  8. Once you've cut all of your layers, use your comb to blend them together by combing through your hair from top to bottom.
  9. Check your layers in the mirror to make sure they're even. If you notice any unevenness, go back and trim as needed.
  10. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Layers

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting layers into your own hair:
  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections as you gain confidence.
  • Be sure to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can shrink up and make your layers shorter than you intended.
  • If you're not sure how short to cut your layers, it's always better to err on the side of caution and cut less rather than more. You can always go back and trim more if needed.
  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. Using regular scissors or dull scissors can result in uneven and jagged cuts.

FAQs

Can I cut layers into curly hair?

Yes, you can cut layers into curly hair, but it's important to take into account how the curls will fall. Be sure to cut your layers when your hair is dry and in its natural state to get a better idea of how the curls will look.

How often should I cut my layers?

This will depend on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain your layered look. As a general rule, you should plan to trim your layers every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers

After cutting your layers, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips to follow:
  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and make your layers look frizzy.
  • Invest in a good leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair smooth and shiny.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your layers and keep split ends at bay.
Cutting layers into your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. By following these simple steps and tips, you'll be able to achieve a salon-worthy look right from the comfort of your own home. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Happy cutting!
