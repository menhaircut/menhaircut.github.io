Can You Get Rid Of Natural Curly Hair

Written by William Aug 09, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing or a curse depending on who you ask. Some people spend hours trying to achieve the perfect curl, while others wish they could get rid of their natural curls altogether. If you fall into the latter category, you may be wondering if it's possible to get rid of natural curly hair. In this article, we'll explore the answer to this question and provide tips on how to do so.

Description

Before we dive into the specifics of how to get rid of natural curly hair, it's important to understand what causes curly hair in the first place. Curly hair is a result of the shape of the hair follicle. If the hair follicle is more oval-shaped, the hair will be curly. If it's more circular, the hair will be straight.

While there are products and treatments that can temporarily straighten curly hair, there is no way to permanently get rid of natural curls. However, there are some techniques that can help to loosen or relax curls, giving you a more straightened appearance.

How to Get Rid of Natural Curly Hair

If you're looking to get rid of natural curly hair, there are a few techniques you can try. These include:

Chemical Straightening

Chemical straightening, also known as a relaxer, is a process in which a chemical solution is applied to the hair to break down the bonds that give it its curl pattern. This process can be damaging to the hair, so it's important to have it done by a professional.

Blowout

A blowout is a salon treatment in which the hair is washed, blown dry, and styled with a round brush to create a smooth, straight look. This is a temporary solution, as the hair will return to its natural curl pattern once it's washed.

Flat Iron

Using a flat iron is a quick and easy way to straighten curly hair. However, it can be damaging to the hair if not used properly. It's important to use a heat protectant spray and to avoid using the flat iron on wet hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you've decided to try one of the techniques above, here's a step-by-step guide to help you get the best results:

Chemical Straightening

  1. Visit a professional stylist who specializes in chemical straightening.
  2. Discuss your desired results and any concerns you may have.
  3. The stylist will apply the chemical solution to your hair, being careful to avoid the scalp.
  4. The solution will be left on for a specified amount of time, depending on your hair type and desired results.
  5. The solution will be rinsed out thoroughly, and a neutralizing solution will be applied to stop the chemical process.
  6. Your hair will be washed and styled.

Blowout

  1. Visit a salon that offers blowouts.
  2. Choose the style you want, and communicate your desired results to the stylist.
  3. Your hair will be washed and conditioned.
  4. The stylist will use a round brush and blow dryer to create a smooth, straight look.
  5. Once your hair is dry, the stylist may use a flat iron to touch up any areas that need it.

Flat Iron

  1. Wash and condition your hair.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray.
  3. Divide your hair into small sections.
  4. Starting at the roots, clamp the flat iron down on a small section of hair.
  5. Slowly glide the flat iron down the length of the hair, making sure to keep the iron moving to avoid burning the hair.
  6. Repeat on all sections of hair.

Tips for Getting Rid of Natural Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when trying to get rid of natural curly hair:

  • Be gentle with your hair. Chemical straightening and heat styling can be damaging, so be sure to use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip the natural oils from your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle.
  • Invest in a high-quality flat iron or blow dryer to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray whenever you use heat styling tools.
  • Consider getting a keratin treatment, which can help to smooth and straighten curly hair.

FAQs

Is it possible to permanently get rid of natural curly hair?

No, there is no way to permanently get rid of natural curls. However, there are techniques that can help to loosen or relax curls, giving you a more straightened appearance.

Is chemical straightening safe?

Chemical straightening can be damaging to the hair if not done properly. It's important to have it done by a professional and to follow their aftercare instructions.

How often should I wash my hair?

It's best to avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip the natural oils from your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

Hair Care Tips for Straightening Curly Hair

If you've decided to try one of the techniques above for getting rid of natural curly hair, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the natural oils from your hair.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools every day, as this can cause damage to your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  • Avoid using hair elastics that can cause breakage. Instead, use a hair tie made of fabric or a scrunchie.

While you can't permanently get rid of natural curly hair, there are techniques that can help to loosen or relax curls, giving you a more straightened appearance. Whether you choose chemical straightening, a blowout, or a flat iron, be sure to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.
