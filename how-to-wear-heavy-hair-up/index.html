How To Wear Heavy Hair Up A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Oct 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you have long, thick hair that always seems to get in the way? You might be wondering how to wear heavy hair up without it falling down or causing headaches. Fortunately, there are several ways to style your hair that are both practical and stylish. In this article, we'll show you how to wear heavy hair up in a variety of styles, plus give you tips for keeping your hair healthy and looking great.

Table of Contents

  • How to Wear Heavy Hair Up
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Keeping Your Hair Healthy
  • FAQ

How to Wear Heavy Hair Up

Before we dive into the specific styles, let's talk about some general tips for wearing heavy hair up:

  • Start with clean, dry hair. Wet hair is heavier and more difficult to style.
  • Invest in good hair ties and bobby pins. Cheap ones can break or slip out of your hair.
  • If you have particularly heavy or thick hair, consider using a hair donut or sock bun to help create volume and hold.
  • Don't pull your hair too tight. This can cause headaches and damage your hair over time.

Styles for Wearing Heavy Hair Up

Now, let's get into the fun part: the styles! Here are some options for wearing heavy hair up:

The Classic Bun

The classic bun is a go-to for many people with long, heavy hair. Here's how to do it:

  1. Gather your hair into a ponytail at the crown of your head.
  2. Twist the ponytail until it starts to coil around itself.
  3. Wrap the twisted hair around the base of the ponytail, tucking the ends underneath.
  4. Secure the bun with bobby pins.
  5. For a more polished look, use a hairnet or hairspray to tame flyaways.

The French Twist

The French twist is a sophisticated and elegant style that can work well for formal occasions. Here's how to do it:

  1. Gather your hair into a low ponytail at the nape of your neck.
  2. Use your hand or a comb to create a vertical part in the hair just above the hair tie.
  3. Flip the ponytail up and over itself, tucking the ends underneath.
  4. Secure the twist with bobby pins.
  5. For added hold, use a hairnet or hairspray.

The Messy Bun

If you prefer a more casual, undone look, the messy bun might be for you. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start with a low ponytail at the nape of your neck.
  2. Twist the ponytail until it starts to coil around itself.
  3. Wrap the twisted hair around the base of the ponytail, tucking the ends underneath.
  4. Pull out a few pieces of hair around your face and at the nape of your neck to create a messy, bohemian look.
  5. Secure the bun with bobby pins.
  6. Add hairspray or dry shampoo for texture and hold.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know some basic styles, here's a step-by-step guide for wearing heavy hair up:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Decide which style you want to try.
  3. Gather your hair into a ponytail or low bun, depending on the style.
  4. Follow the specific steps for your chosen style.
  5. Secure the style with bobby pins or other hair accessories.
  6. Use hairspray or other products to add hold and texture.

Tips for Keeping Your Hair Healthy

Wearing your hair up can be great for keeping it out of your face and off your neck, but it can also cause damage if you're not careful. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, like high ponytails or tight braids.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can dry out and damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and strong.

FAQ

Q: How can I wear heavy hair up without it causing headaches?

A: Make sure you're not pulling your hair too tight, and use a looser style like a messy bun or low ponytail. You can also try using a hair donut or sock bun to help distribute the weight more evenly.

Q: Can I wear heavy hair up every day?

A: It's generally safe to wear your hair up every day, but make sure you're not pulling it too tight or using styles that could cause damage over time.

Q: What products should I use to keep my hair healthy?

A: Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to nourish your hair. You can also use a heat protectant spray if you use heat styling tools.

Now that you know how to wear heavy hair up, you can try out different styles and find what works for you. Remember to take care of your hair by using gentle styles and nourishing products, and you'll have healthy, beautiful hair for years to come.
