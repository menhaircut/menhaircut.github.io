How To Make My Hair Grow Down Tips And Tricks

Written by William Sep 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of your hair growing outwards instead of down? You're not alone! Many people struggle with getting their hair to grow downwards, but with the right tips and tricks, you can achieve the look you want. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about how to make your hair grow down.

Table of Contents

  • How to Make My Hair Grow Down
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Growing Your Hair Down
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips for Growing Your Hair Down

How to Make My Hair Grow Down

Before we dive into the specifics of how to make your hair grow downwards, it's important to understand why your hair might be growing outwards in the first place. In general, hair grows in the direction that it's pulled. If you're constantly brushing or combing your hair upwards or back, that's the direction it will grow. So, to get your hair to grow downwards, you need to start training it to do so.

Trim Your Hair Regularly

One of the best things you can do to encourage your hair to grow downwards is to get regular trims. This might seem counterintuitive, but trimming your hair regularly will help get rid of split ends and other damage that can cause your hair to stick out in all directions. By keeping your hair healthy and well-maintained, you'll be setting the stage for it to grow downwards more easily.

Use the Right Hair Products

Another important factor in getting your hair to grow downwards is using the right hair products. Look for products that are specifically designed to add weight and volume to your hair, as these will help your hair hang down more easily. Avoid products that are too heavy or greasy, as these can weigh down your hair and make it look limp and lifeless.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're ready to start training your hair to grow downwards, here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

Step 1: Shampoo and Condition Your Hair

Start by shampooing and conditioning your hair as you normally would. Be sure to use products that are designed for your hair type.

Step 2: Towel Dry Your Hair

Gently towel dry your hair to remove excess water. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously, as this can cause damage and breakage.

Step 3: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner or other styling product to your hair. Be sure to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair, using your fingers or a wide-tooth comb.

Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair

Use a blow dryer to dry your hair, using a round brush to pull your hair downwards as you go. Be sure to use a low heat setting to avoid damaging your hair.

Step 5: Finish with a Straightening Iron

Finish off your look by using a straightening iron to smooth out any remaining kinks or waves. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

Tips for Growing Your Hair Down

Here are some additional tips to help you get your hair to grow downwards:

Tip 1: Brush Your Hair Regularly

Brushing your hair regularly can help train it to grow downwards. Use a soft-bristled brush and be gentle, as rough brushing can cause damage and breakage.

Tip 2: Sleep with a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping with a silk pillowcase can help prevent your hair from getting tangled and twisted during the night, which can cause it to stick out in all directions.

Tip 3: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can cause damage to your hair, which can make it more difficult to get it to grow downwards. Try to avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straightening irons as much as possible.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does it take for hair to grow downwards?

A: It can take several weeks or even months to train your hair to grow downwards, depending on how damaged your hair is and how well you stick to your hair care routine.

Q: Can I make my hair grow faster?

A: While there's no surefire way to make your hair grow faster, maintaining a healthy diet and lifestyle can help promote hair growth.

Hair Care Tips for Growing Your Hair Down

Finally, here are some general hair care tips to help you get your hair to grow downwards:

Tip 1: Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a diet that's rich in nutrients like vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth.

Tip 2: Protect Your Hair from the Elements

Exposure to sun, wind, and other elements can cause damage to your hair. Be sure to protect your hair by wearing a hat or using a protective hair spray.

Tip 3: Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, and be gentle when brushing or styling it. This will help prevent damage and breakage, which can make it harder to get your hair to grow downwards.

By following these tips and tricks, you'll be well on your way to achieving the smooth, flowing locks you've always wanted.
