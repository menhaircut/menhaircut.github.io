Causes Of Extreme Hair Loss After Pregnancy

Written by Daniel Jun 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you experiencing extreme hair loss after pregnancy? Don't worry, it's completely normal. In fact, up to 50% of women experience postpartum hair loss. In this article, we will discuss the causes of extreme hair loss after pregnancy and provide you with some tips to help you cope with this temporary hair loss.

Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes during pregnancy can cause your hair to grow thicker and fuller. However, after giving birth, your hormone levels drop rapidly, which can cause your hair to fall out. This is completely normal and usually happens around 3 months after giving birth.

The hormone responsible for hair loss after pregnancy is called dihydrotestosterone (DHT). It is a byproduct of testosterone and can cause hair follicles to shrink, leading to hair loss.

Stress

Stress can also contribute to postpartum hair loss. Giving birth and taking care of a newborn can be stressful and exhausting, which can lead to hair loss.

Additionally, the stress hormone cortisol can also contribute to hair loss. High levels of cortisol can cause hair follicles to shrink, leading to hair loss.

Nutritional Deficiencies

Nutritional deficiencies can also contribute to postpartum hair loss. During pregnancy, your body needs more nutrients to support the growth and development of your baby. However, after giving birth, your body may be depleted of essential nutrients, which can lead to hair loss.

Some common nutritional deficiencies that can contribute to hair loss include iron, zinc, and biotin.

Thyroid Issues

Thyroid issues can also cause postpartum hair loss. The thyroid gland produces hormones that regulate your metabolism. If your thyroid gland is not functioning properly, it can cause hair loss.

Some common thyroid issues that can contribute to hair loss include hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism.

Hairstyling Practices

Hairstyling practices can also contribute to postpartum hair loss. Tight hairstyles, such as braids, weaves, and ponytails, can cause tension on the hair follicles, leading to hair loss.

Additionally, using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, can also contribute to hair loss.

Tips to Cope with Postpartum Hair Loss

While postpartum hair loss is normal, it can still be frustrating to deal with. Here are some tips to help you cope with postpartum hair loss:

  • Be gentle with your hair when washing and styling it
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can cause tension on the hair follicles
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet to ensure you are getting all the essential nutrients your body needs
  • Consider taking a biotin supplement to promote hair growth
  • Try to manage your stress levels through exercise, meditation, or other relaxation techniques

FAQ

When does postpartum hair loss typically occur?

Postpartum hair loss typically occurs around 3 months after giving birth.

How long does postpartum hair loss last?

Postpartum hair loss usually lasts for a few months and should resolve on its own.

Can postpartum hair loss be prevented?

Postpartum hair loss cannot be prevented, but you can take steps to promote healthy hair growth, such as eating a healthy diet and minimizing stress.

Hair Care Tips

In addition to the tips above, here are some additional hair care tips to help you cope with postpartum hair loss:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible
  • Consider using a hair growth serum or oil to promote healthy hair growth
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends
  • Avoid harsh chemical treatments, such as hair dye or relaxers

Remember, postpartum hair loss is completely normal and usually resolves on its own. With a little patience and some TLC, your hair will be back to its pre-pregnancy glory in no time!
