How To Get Volume In Your Hair With A Straightener

Written by Frank Nov 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of flat, lifeless hair? Do you want to add some volume and texture to your locks without spending hours at the salon? Look no further than your trusty straightener. With a few simple techniques, you can transform your hair from limp to luscious in just minutes. Read on for our step-by-step guide to getting volume in your hair with a straightener.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Volume in Your Hair with a Straightener
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Success
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

The first step to getting volume in your hair with a straightener is to start with clean, dry hair. This will allow you to create more texture and hold in your hair. If your hair is naturally oily, use a dry shampoo to absorb any excess oil before styling.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you're getting even coverage with the straightener.

Step 3: Heat Up Your Straightener

Set your straightener to the appropriate temperature for your hair type. If you have fine or damaged hair, use a lower heat setting to prevent further damage. If you have thick or coarse hair, you may need a higher heat setting to achieve the desired results.

Step 4: Start at the Roots

Starting at the roots of your hair, clamp the straightener down and gently pull it through the section of hair, twisting the straightener as you go. This will create a slight bend in your hair and add volume at the roots.

Step 5: Work Your Way Down

Continue working your way down the section of hair, twisting the straightener as you go. This will create loose waves and add volume throughout your hair.

Step 6: Repeat on All Sections

Repeat this process on all sections of your hair, working from the back of your head to the front. Be sure to twist the straightener in different directions to create natural-looking waves.

Step 7: Finish with Hairspray

Once you've finished straightening your hair, finish with a light mist of hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips for Success

  • Use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Experiment with different heat settings to find the one that works best for your hair type.
  • Twist the straightener in different directions to create natural-looking waves.
  • Work in small sections to ensure even coverage.
  • Don't be afraid to use volumizing products, like mousse or root-lifting spray, to enhance your results.

FAQs

Can I use a straightener on wet hair?

No, you should never use a straightener on wet hair. This can cause damage to your hair and potentially burn your scalp. Always start with clean, dry hair.

How often should I straighten my hair?

It's best to limit your use of a straightener to a few times a week to prevent damage to your hair. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray and take breaks between styling sessions to give your hair a chance to recover.

Hair Care Tips

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools on a daily basis to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a swim cap.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote strong, healthy hair.

With these simple tips and techniques, you can achieve voluminous, textured hair with just a straightener. Experiment with different heat settings and products to find the combination that works best for your hair type, and don't forget to take care of your hair with regular conditioning treatments and trims. Happy styling!
