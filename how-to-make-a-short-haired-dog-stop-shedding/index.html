How To Make A Short Haired Dog Stop Shedding

Written by Luffy Sep 22, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you are a dog owner, you know how frustrating it can be to constantly deal with dog hair all over your home. Short haired dogs can shed just as much as long haired dogs, and it can be difficult to keep up with the amount of hair they leave behind. However, there are steps you can take to make a short haired dog stop shedding. In this article, we will provide you with a how-to guide on how to reduce shedding in your short haired dog.

Description

Short haired dogs can shed just as much as long haired dogs, leaving hair all over your furniture, carpets, and clothes. Shedding is a natural process for dogs, but excessive shedding can be a sign of an underlying health issue. Reducing shedding in your short haired dog not only keeps your home clean, but it can also improve your dog's health.

How to

To make a short haired dog stop shedding, you need to take a few steps to maintain their coat and overall health. These include:

1. Brush your dog regularly

Brushing your dog regularly removes loose hair and prevents it from ending up all over your home. Use a brush that is appropriate for your dog's coat type, and brush in the direction of hair growth. Make sure to brush your dog gently to avoid irritating their skin.

2. Feed your dog a healthy diet

A healthy diet can improve your dog's coat and reduce shedding. Make sure your dog is getting enough protein, vitamins, and minerals in their diet. Avoid feeding your dog table scraps or low-quality dog food that can cause skin irritation and excessive shedding.

3. Give your dog supplements

Supplements such as fish oil or omega-3 fatty acids can improve your dog's coat and reduce shedding. Talk to your vet about which supplements are right for your dog.

4. Bathe your dog regularly

Bathing your dog regularly removes loose hair and dirt that can contribute to shedding. Use a dog-specific shampoo that is gentle on your dog's skin, and avoid over-bathing your dog as it can dry out their skin and cause more shedding.

5. Keep your dog hydrated

Adequate hydration is essential for a healthy coat and skin. Make sure your dog always has access to clean water, and add wet food or broth to their diet to increase their fluid intake.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to reduce shedding in your short haired dog:

  • Keep your home clean and vacuum regularly to remove loose hair.
  • Avoid stressors that can cause excessive shedding, such as changes in routine or loud noises.
  • Check your dog for fleas and ticks regularly, as they can cause skin irritation and increase shedding.
  • If your dog is shedding excessively, consult your vet to rule out any underlying health issues.

FAQ

Q: How often should I brush my short haired dog?

A: You should brush your short haired dog at least once a week to remove loose hair and prevent shedding.

Q: Can diet really affect shedding in my dog?

A: Yes, a healthy diet can improve your dog's coat and reduce shedding. Make sure your dog is getting enough protein, vitamins, and minerals in their diet.

Q: Will supplements really make a difference in my dog's shedding?

A: Yes, supplements such as fish oil or omega-3 fatty acids can improve your dog's coat and reduce shedding. Talk to your vet about which supplements are right for your dog.
