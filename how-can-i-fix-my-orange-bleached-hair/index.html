How Can I Fix My Orange Bleached Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Eveline Nov 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you dealing with orange bleached hair? Don't worry, you're not alone. Bleaching hair can be a tricky process, and sometimes it can leave your hair with unwanted orange tones. But fear not, there are ways to fix your orange bleached hair and get the color you want. In this article, we'll discuss how you can fix your orange bleached hair and get the look you desire.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Fix Orange Bleached Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Fixing Orange Bleached Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Fixing Orange Bleached Hair

Description

Orange bleached hair is a common problem for those who bleach their hair at home or go to a salon. The orange tones can be caused by several factors, including the natural pigment in your hair, the level of bleach used, and the amount of time the bleach is left on your hair. These factors can lead to an uneven, brassy, or orange color that can be difficult to fix.

How to Fix Orange Bleached Hair

There are several ways to fix orange bleached hair, including toning, color correction, and deep conditioning. Toning is the most common method to fix orange bleached hair. It involves using a toner to neutralize the orange tones and bring the color closer to your desired shade.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to fix your orange bleached hair:

  1. Choose a toner that suits your hair's needs. Look for a toner with blue, purple, or green tones, as these colors neutralize orange.
  2. Apply the toner to your hair, following the instructions on the packaging. Make sure to evenly distribute the toner throughout your hair.
  3. Leave the toner on your hair for the recommended time, usually between 10-30 minutes.
  4. Rinse the toner out of your hair with cool water.
  5. Deep condition your hair to restore moisture and prevent damage.

Tips for Fixing Orange Bleached Hair

Here are some tips to help you fix your orange bleached hair:

  • Use a toner with blue, purple, or green tones to neutralize orange.
  • Avoid using harsh shampoos and conditioners that can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to restore moisture and prevent damage.
  • Consider going to a professional stylist for color correction if toning doesn't work.
  • Be patient. It may take several toning sessions to achieve your desired color.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing orange bleached hair:

Can I fix my orange bleached hair at home?

Yes, you can fix your orange bleached hair at home with toner, color correction, and deep conditioning. However, if you're unsure about the process, it's best to go to a professional stylist.

How long does it take to fix orange bleached hair?

The time it takes to fix orange bleached hair depends on the severity of the orange tones and the desired color. It may take several toning sessions to achieve your desired color.

Can I prevent my hair from turning orange when bleaching?

You can prevent your hair from turning orange when bleaching by using a high-quality bleach, lifting your hair gradually, and using a toner immediately after bleaching.

Hair Care After Fixing Orange Bleached Hair

After fixing your orange bleached hair, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and color. Here are some tips for hair care after fixing orange bleached hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent color fading.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair to prevent damage.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to maintain moisture.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.

With these tips and tricks, you can fix your orange bleached hair and achieve the color you want. Remember to take care of your hair after fixing it to maintain its health and color. If you're unsure about the process, it's best to go to a professional stylist.
