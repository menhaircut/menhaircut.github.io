How To Fix Bad Synthetic Wig A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Sep 09, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're a wig wearer, you know that synthetic wigs are a great option for their affordability and low-maintenance. However, sometimes things can go wrong and you may find yourself with a bad synthetic wig. Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this post, we'll go over how to fix bad synthetic wigs step by step. From detangling to cutting, we'll cover it all. Let's get started!

Description

Synthetic wigs are made of synthetic fibers and come in a wide range of styles and colors. They are a popular choice for wig wearers because they are affordable and easy to care for. However, sometimes synthetic wigs can become tangled, frizzy or have an unnatural shine. This can make them look less realistic and more like a costume wig. In this post, we'll discuss how to fix these common problems and get your synthetic wig looking great again.

How to Fix Bad Synthetic Wig: Step by Step

Step 1: Detangle

The first step in fixing a bad synthetic wig is to detangle it. Start by gently combing through the wig with a wide-tooth comb. Be careful not to pull too hard on the fibers, as this can cause damage. If the wig is particularly tangled, you can use a detangling spray to help loosen the fibers.

Step 2: Wash

After detangling the wig, it's time to wash it. Fill a sink with cool water and add a small amount of wig shampoo. Gently swish the wig in the water, being careful not to rub the fibers too hard. Rinse the wig thoroughly with cool water to remove all the shampoo.

Step 3: Condition

Once the wig is clean, it's time to condition it. Apply a small amount of wig conditioner to the wig and let it sit for a few minutes. Rinse the wig thoroughly with cool water to remove all the conditioner.

Step 4: Dry

After conditioning the wig, gently blot it with a towel to remove excess water. Do not rub the fibers, as this can cause damage. Place the wig on a wig stand or mannequin head and allow it to air dry. Do not use a hair dryer or other heat source to dry the wig, as this can cause damage.

Step 5: Style

Once the wig is dry, it's time to style it. Use a wig brush or comb to gently style the wig into the desired shape. If the wig has an unnatural shine, you can use a wig powder or dry shampoo to help remove the shine.

Step 6: Cut

If the wig is still not looking great after washing and styling, you may need to cut it. Start by trimming any frizzy or damaged ends. Then, use scissors to shape the wig into the desired style. Be careful not to cut too much, as you can't undo a bad haircut.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to help you fix a bad synthetic wig:

  • Always use cool water when washing and conditioning synthetic wigs.
  • Never use a hair dryer or other heat source to dry synthetic wigs.
  • Use a wig stand or mannequin head to help the wig keep its shape while drying.
  • Be gentle when combing or brushing synthetic wigs, as the fibers can easily become damaged.
  • Trim the wig a little at a time, as you can always cut more if needed.

FAQ

Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on a synthetic wig?

No, regular shampoo and conditioner can damage synthetic wig fibers. It's important to use wig-specific shampoo and conditioner to ensure that the wig stays in good condition.

Can I use a hair dryer to dry a synthetic wig?

No, using a hair dryer or other heat source can damage synthetic wig fibers. It's important to let the wig air dry on a wig stand or mannequin head.

How often should I wash my synthetic wig?

It depends on how often you wear the wig. If you wear it every day, you may need to wash it once a week. If you wear it less frequently, you may be able to wash it less often.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for keeping your synthetic wig looking great. Here are a few tips to help you care for your wig:

  • Store the wig on a wig stand or mannequin head when not in use.
  • Avoid exposing the wig to heat sources such as curling irons or straighteners.
  • Avoid exposing the wig to sunlight for extended periods of time.
  • Use wig-specific shampoo and conditioner to keep the wig clean and soft.
  • Be gentle when combing or brushing the wig, as the fibers can easily become damaged.

By following these tips and steps, you should be able to fix a bad synthetic wig and get it looking great again. Remember to be gentle and take your time, as synthetic wigs can be delicate. With a little care and attention, your synthetic wig can last for many wears and look great every time.
