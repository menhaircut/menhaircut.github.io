How To Cut Short Layers In Long Hair At Home

Written by Bardi Aug 18, 2023 · 4 min read

Cutting short layers in long hair is a great way to add volume and dimension to your locks. It's a simple and easy process that you can do at home with the right tools and techniques. In this post, we'll provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to cut short layers in long hair at home.

Description

Short layers are an ideal hairstyle for those who want to add depth and movement to their hair. It is a versatile hairstyle that can be tailored to suit any hair type, length, and texture. Short layers are perfect for people with thin hair as it can make it look fuller and thicker. It is also an excellent option for those with long hair who want to add volume and shape to their locks.

How to Cut Short Layers in Long Hair at Home

Before you start cutting short layers in your long hair, make sure that your hair is clean, dry, and tangle-free. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. Follow these steps:

Step 1: Part Your Hair

Start by parting your hair in the middle or on one side, depending on your preference. Use a comb to create a straight parting line from the front to the back of your head.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use hair clips to section your hair into four parts: two at the front and two at the back. The front sections should start at your temples and end at the back of your ears. The back sections should start at the crown of your head and end at the nape of your neck.

Step 3: Cut the First Layer

Start with the front section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your index and middle finger and cut it at an angle. The length of the hair you cut will determine the length of the layer. For shorter layers, cut more hair, and for longer layers, cut less hair. Repeat this step on the other front section of your hair.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Move to the back sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the middle of your head, comb it straight, and cut it at an angle. Make sure that you cut the hair shorter than the front layers to create a layered effect. Repeat this step on the other back section of your hair.

Step 5: Cut the Bottom Layer

Finally, take a small section of hair from the bottom of your hair, comb it straight, and cut it at an angle. This layer should be the shortest of all the layers to create a layered effect. Repeat this step on the other bottom section of your hair.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting short layers in long hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends and uneven cuts.
  • Start with small sections of hair and cut less hair first. You can always cut more if you need to.
  • Do not cut your hair when it is wet as it can cause uneven cuts when it dries.
  • Be patient and take your time when cutting your hair. Rushing can lead to mistakes and uneven cuts.

FAQ

Is it possible to cut short layers in curly hair?

Yes, it is possible to cut short layers in curly hair. However, it is best to cut curly hair when it is dry to avoid uneven cuts. Use the same steps as outlined above, but make sure to cut less hair as curly hair tends to shrink when it dries.

How often should I cut my short layers?

It is recommended to cut short layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and volume of your hair. However, the frequency of your haircuts may depend on your hair type, length, and personal preference.

Hair Care

After cutting your short layers, it is essential to take care of your hair to maintain its health and volume. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat tools such as hairdryers, straighteners, and curling irons as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and moisturize your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

Now that you know how to cut short layers in long hair at home, you can achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home. Follow these steps, tips, and hair care advice to maintain healthy, voluminous locks.
