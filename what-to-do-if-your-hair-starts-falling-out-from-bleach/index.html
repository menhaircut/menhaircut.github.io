What To Do If Your Hair Starts Falling Out From Bleach

Is your hair falling out after bleaching? This can be a scary experience, but don't panic. In this article, we will show you what to do if your hair starts falling out from bleach.

Table of Content

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ

Description

Bleaching your hair can cause severe damage to your strands, leading to hair fall out. This can happen due to the harsh chemicals used in the bleaching process, which can strip your hair of its natural oils and moisture. The result is dry, brittle, and damaged hair that can easily break and fall out. If you've experienced hair loss due to bleaching, you need to take immediate action to prevent further damage and promote hair growth.

How to

If you notice hair fall out after bleaching, the first thing you should do is stop bleaching your hair immediately. Continuing to bleach your hair will only make the problem worse and lead to more hair loss. You should also avoid using any other hair products that contain harsh chemicals, such as hair dyes or perm solutions.

Consult with a Professional Hair Stylist

The next step is to schedule a consultation with a professional hair stylist. They can assess the damage to your hair and recommend the best course of action to repair it. This may include trimming the damaged ends, using protein treatments to strengthen your hair, or even cutting your hair short to promote new growth.

Take Care of Your Hair

It's also essential to take care of your hair to prevent further damage and promote hair growth. This includes using gentle shampoos and conditioners that are specifically formulated for damaged hair. You can also use natural hair oils, such as coconut oil or argan oil, to nourish and moisturize your hair.

Step by step

If you're experiencing hair fall out after bleaching, follow these steps to promote hair growth and prevent further damage:

  1. Stop bleaching your hair immediately.
  2. Avoid using any other hair products that contain harsh chemicals.
  3. Schedule a consultation with a professional hair stylist.
  4. Take care of your hair by using gentle shampoos and conditioners.
  5. Use natural hair oils, such as coconut oil or argan oil, to nourish and moisturize your hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to prevent hair fall out after bleaching:

  • Avoid using hot styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, as they can further damage your hair.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet that includes plenty of protein and vitamins to promote hair growth.
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles, such as braids or ponytails, that can pull on your hair and cause breakage.
  • Consider taking hair growth supplements, such as biotin or vitamin E, to promote hair growth.

FAQ

Can hair grow back after falling out from bleach?

Yes, hair can grow back after falling out from bleach. However, it may take some time for your hair to fully recover and grow back to its previous length and thickness.

Can protein treatments help prevent hair fall out from bleach?

Yes, protein treatments can help strengthen your hair and prevent hair fall out from bleach. These treatments can help repair the damage to your hair and promote healthy hair growth.

Is it okay to bleach your hair again after experiencing hair fall out?

No, it's not okay to bleach your hair again after experiencing hair fall out. Continuing to bleach your hair will only make the problem worse and lead to more hair loss. You should wait until your hair has fully recovered before considering bleaching it again.

Hair Care

To prevent hair fall out from bleach, it's essential to take good care of your hair. This includes using gentle hair products and avoiding harsh chemicals, such as bleach or hair dyes. You should also avoid using hot styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, and wear your hair in loose hairstyles that don't pull on your hair. Eating a healthy, balanced diet and taking hair growth supplements can also promote healthy hair growth.

Remember, if you're experiencing hair fall out after bleaching, it's important to take immediate action to prevent further damage and promote hair growth. Follow the steps and tips in this article, and consult with a professional hair stylist for personalized advice.
