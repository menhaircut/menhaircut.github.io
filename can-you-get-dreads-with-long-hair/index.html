Can You Get Dreads With Long Hair

Written by Luffy Sep 17, 2023 · 4 min read

When it comes to getting dreadlocks, many people wonder whether they can achieve this look with long hair. The answer is yes, you can get dreads with long hair. However, the process may take longer and require more maintenance than with shorter hair. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to get dreads with long hair, as well as some helpful tips and frequently asked questions about this hairstyle.

How to Get Dreads with Long Hair

The process of getting dreads with long hair involves several steps, including:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start the process, you need to prepare your hair by washing it thoroughly and letting it air dry. You should also brush your hair to remove any tangles or knots that may interfere with the locking process.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, you need to section your hair into small, equal-sized parts using a comb. The sections should be no larger than the size of a pencil to ensure that the locks form properly.

Step 3: Backcomb Your Hair

Once you have sectioned your hair, you can start backcombing each section. This involves combing your hair towards your scalp in a backwards motion. You should repeat this process until each section is tightly knotted and starts to form a dreadlock.

Step 4: Maintain Your Dreads

After you have successfully created your dreads, you need to maintain them to prevent them from unraveling. This involves palm-rolling your dreads regularly, using a residue-free shampoo, and avoiding excessive use of oils or conditioners that can cause buildup.

Tips for Getting Dreads with Long Hair

Here are some helpful tips for getting dreads with long hair:

  • Be patient – dreadlocks take time to form, especially with long hair.
  • Avoid using wax or other products that can cause buildup and prevent your hair from locking properly.
  • Protect your hair from damage by avoiding heat styling tools, tight hairstyles, and chemical treatments.
  • Consider seeking the help of a professional loctician to ensure that your dreads are formed properly.

FAQ About Getting Dreads with Long Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting dreads with long hair:

Can I Get Dreads with Relaxed Hair?

Yes, you can get dreads with relaxed hair. However, the process may take longer and require more maintenance to prevent breakage and damage to your hair.

How Long Will It Take to Get Dreads with Long Hair?

The time it takes to get dreads with long hair varies depending on your hair type, texture, and the method you use to create your dreads. On average, it can take anywhere from six months to a year for your dreads to fully form.

Do I Need to Cut My Hair to Get Dreads?

No, you don't need to cut your hair to get dreads. However, if your hair is severely damaged or has split ends, it may be beneficial to trim your hair before starting the locking process.

Hair Care for Dreads with Long Hair

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy dreads with long hair. Here are some tips for taking care of your dreads:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or conditioners that can cause buildup and prevent your hair from locking properly.
  • Protect your dreads from damage by wearing a scarf or hat when exposed to sun, wind, or cold weather.
  • Regularly palm-roll your dreads to maintain their shape and prevent them from unraveling.

Overall, getting dreads with long hair is possible with the right techniques and maintenance. By following the steps and tips outlined in this article, you can achieve the dreadlock look you desire and maintain healthy, beautiful hair.
