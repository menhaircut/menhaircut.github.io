What To Do For Bad Hair Breakage A Complete Guide

Written by Frank Jun 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Bad hair breakage can be a frustrating and embarrassing problem, but it doesn't have to be permanent. With the right care and attention, you can help repair your hair and prevent further damage. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about what to do for bad hair breakage.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Repair Bad Hair Breakage
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Preventing Hair Breakage
  • FAQs

Description

Bad hair breakage can be caused by a variety of factors, including over-processing with chemicals, heat damage from styling tools, and lack of moisture. When hair becomes weak and brittle, it's more prone to breakage and split ends. If left untreated, hair breakage can lead to thinning and even baldness.

If you're experiencing bad hair breakage, it's important to take action as soon as possible. With the right care and attention, you can help repair your hair and prevent further damage. Here's what to do:

How to Repair Bad Hair Breakage

The first step in repairing bad hair breakage is to assess the damage. Take a close look at your hair and identify any areas of breakage or thinning. From there, you can develop a plan of action to help repair and strengthen your hair.

Here are some of the best ways to repair bad hair breakage:

1. Get a Haircut

If your hair is severely damaged, you may need to get a haircut to remove the damaged ends. This will help prevent further breakage and give your hair a fresh start.

2. Use Protein Treatments

Protein treatments can help strengthen and repair damaged hair. Look for products that contain hydrolyzed keratin or collagen, which can help rebuild the structure of your hair.

3. Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help restore moisture to your hair, which can prevent breakage. Look for products that contain natural oils, like coconut or avocado oil, to help nourish your hair.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools can damage your hair and lead to breakage. If possible, try to limit your use of heat styling tools or use them on a lower heat setting.

5. Protect Your Hair at Night

Protect your hair while you sleep by using a silk or satin pillowcase, or wrapping your hair in a silk scarf. This can help prevent breakage caused by friction.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to repairing bad hair breakage:

Step 1: Assess the Damage

Take a close look at your hair and identify any areas of breakage or thinning.

Step 2: Get a Haircut

If your hair is severely damaged, consider getting a haircut to remove the damaged ends.

Step 3: Use a Protein Treatment

Apply a protein treatment to your hair once a week to help strengthen and repair it.

Step 4: Deep Condition Regularly

Deep condition your hair once or twice a week to help restore moisture and prevent breakage.

Step 5: Avoid Heat Styling

Limit your use of heat styling tools, or use them on a lower heat setting.

Step 6: Protect Your Hair at Night

Use a silk or satin pillowcase, or wrap your hair in a silk scarf while you sleep to prevent breakage caused by friction.

Tips for Preventing Hair Breakage

Here are some tips for preventing hair breakage:

  • Avoid over-processing your hair with chemicals
  • Limit your use of heat styling tools
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Stay hydrated to keep your hair and scalp healthy

FAQs

Q: Can hair breakage be reversed?

A: Yes, with the right care and attention, hair breakage can be repaired and prevented.

Q: How long does it take for hair breakage to grow back?

A: Hair grows an average of half an inch per month, so it may take several months or more for your hair to fully recover from breakage.

Hair Care Routine for Healthy Hair

Here's a basic hair care routine to help keep your hair healthy and prevent breakage:

  • Shampoo and condition your hair every 2-3 days
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your hair
  • Apply a protein treatment once a week
  • Deep condition your hair once or twice a week
  • Avoid heat styling tools or use them on a lower heat setting
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Stay hydrated to keep your hair and scalp healthy

By following these tips and developing a healthy hair care routine, you can help prevent hair breakage and keep your hair looking and feeling its best.
