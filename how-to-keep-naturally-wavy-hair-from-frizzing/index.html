How To Keep Naturally Wavy Hair From Frizzing Tips And Tricks

Written by Petter Sep 02, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have naturally wavy hair, you know how frustrating it can be to deal with frizz. Frizzy hair can be caused by a variety of factors, such as humidity, dryness, and damage. Fortunately, there are several strategies you can use to keep your wavy hair looking smooth and sleek. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to keep naturally wavy hair from frizzing.

How to Keep Naturally Wavy Hair from Frizzing

The first step to keeping your naturally wavy hair from frizzing is to understand what causes frizz in the first place. Frizz occurs when the hair cuticle is lifted, allowing moisture to enter the hair shaft. This causes the hair to become dry and brittle, resulting in a rough, frizzy texture. Here are some tips on how to keep your wavy hair from frizzing:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfates are harsh detergents that can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and prone to frizz. Instead, opt for a sulfate-free shampoo that will gently cleanse your hair without stripping it of its natural moisture. Look for shampoos that are formulated specifically for wavy or curly hair.

2. Condition regularly

Conditioner is essential for keeping your hair hydrated and reducing frizz. Use a conditioner that is designed for wavy hair and apply it from mid-length to the ends of your hair. Leave the conditioner on for a few minutes before rinsing it out thoroughly.

3. Don't over-wash your hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and lead to frizz. Aim to wash your hair no more than twice a week, and use dry shampoo in between washes to absorb excess oil.

4. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature at a moderate level.

5. Use a diffuser

A diffuser attachment for your hair dryer can help to distribute heat evenly and reduce frizz. Use a low heat setting and gently scrunch your hair with the diffuser to enhance your natural waves.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to keep your naturally wavy hair from frizzing:

Step 1: Shampoo your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo.

Step 2: Apply conditioner from mid-length to the ends of your hair.

Step 3: Rinse out the conditioner thoroughly.

Step 4: Gently squeeze excess water out of your hair with a microfiber towel.

Step 5: Apply a leave-in conditioner or anti-frizz serum to your hair.

Step 6: Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

Step 7: Let your hair air-dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer.

Step 8: Avoid touching your hair too much as it dries to prevent frizz.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for keeping your naturally wavy hair from frizzing:

Tip 1: Sleep on a silk pillowcase

Silk pillowcases can help to reduce friction and prevent frizz while you sleep.

Tip 2: Use a microfiber towel

Microfiber towels are gentler on your hair than regular towels and can help to reduce frizz.

Tip 3: Apply a hair mask once a week

Hair masks can help to nourish and hydrate your hair, reducing frizz and improving overall hair health.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I use a regular shampoo on my wavy hair?

A: It's best to use a sulfate-free shampoo that is designed for wavy or curly hair to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

Q: How often should I wash my wavy hair?

A: Aim to wash your hair no more than twice a week.

Q: Can I use a hair mask on my wavy hair?

A: Yes, using a hair mask once a week can help to nourish and hydrate your hair, reducing frizz and improving overall hair health.

Hair Care for Naturally Wavy Hair

In addition to the tips and tricks mentioned above, here are some general hair care tips for naturally wavy hair:

Tip 1: Trim your hair regularly

Regular trims can help to prevent split ends and breakage, which can lead to frizz.

Tip 2: Use a wide-tooth comb

A wide-tooth comb is gentler on your hair than a brush and can help to detangle your hair without causing damage.

Tip 3: Protect your hair from the sun

UV rays can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz. Wear a hat or use a hair sunscreen to protect your hair from the sun.

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your naturally wavy hair looking smooth, sleek, and frizz-free. Remember to be gentle with your hair and avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools whenever possible. With a little bit of care and attention, you can have beautiful, healthy wavy hair that you'll love to show off.
