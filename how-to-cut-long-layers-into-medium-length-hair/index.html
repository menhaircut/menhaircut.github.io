How To Cut Long Layers Into Medium Length Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Sep 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Long layers into medium length hair can be a great way to add volume, texture, and movement to your hair. Whether you want a new look or just a trim, this guide will show you how to cut long layers into medium length hair in just a few easy steps. In this article, we will cover everything you need to know about how to cut long layers into medium length hair, including tips, FAQs, and hair care.

How to Cut Long Layers into Medium Length Hair

Before we get started, it's important to make sure you have the right tools for the job. You'll need a good pair of shears, a comb, and some hair clips to keep your hair in place while you work.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair, making sure it's completely clean and free of any tangles. Brush your hair thoroughly, and then section it into four equal parts: two at the front and two at the back.

Step 2: Cut the First Layer

Take a small section of hair from the front of your head, and comb it straight out from your scalp. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut it at a slight angle, moving downward towards the ends. This will create a long, subtle layer. Repeat this process on the other side of your head.

Step 3: Cut the Second Layer

Take another small section of hair from the front of your head, just below the first layer. Comb it out and hold it between your fingers, then cut it at a steeper angle than the first layer. This will create a shorter, more defined layer. Repeat this process on the other side of your head.

Step 4: Cut the Back Layers

Now it's time to move on to the back of your head. Take a small section of hair from the bottom of one of the back sections, and comb it straight out from your scalp. Hold the hair between your fingers, and cut it at a slight angle, moving downward towards the ends. Repeat this process on the other side of your head, and then move up to the next section of hair.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all of the layers, it's time to blend them together. Take a comb and comb through your hair from root to tip, making sure all of the layers are evenly distributed. If you notice any uneven areas, go back and trim them as needed.

Tips for Cutting Long Layers into Medium Length Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting long layers into medium length hair:

  • Start small and work your way up. It's always better to cut too little than too much.
  • Use a sharp pair of shears to ensure a clean cut.
  • Work in a well-lit area so you can see what you're doing.
  • Take breaks if you need them. Cutting hair can be tiring, so don't be afraid to step away for a few minutes if you need to.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are a few common questions people have about cutting long layers into medium length hair:

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended that you get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my own hair?

While it's possible to cut your own hair, it's generally recommended that you go to a professional stylist to ensure the best results.

Hair Care

Once you've cut your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or using a protective spray.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips and techniques, you should be able to cut long layers into medium length hair with ease. Remember to take your time, work carefully, and have fun!
