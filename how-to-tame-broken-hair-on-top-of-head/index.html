How To Tame Broken Hair On Top Of Head Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Aug 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're dealing with broken hair on top of your head, you're not alone. It's a common problem that affects many people, and it can be frustrating to deal with. But don't worry, there are ways to tame your broken hair and get it back to looking healthy and beautiful. In this article, we'll explore some tips and tricks to help you tame your broken hair on top of your head.

How to

There are several ways to tame broken hair on top of your head. Here are some tips and tricks you can try:

Trim your hair

One of the best ways to deal with broken hair on top of your head is to trim it. This will help get rid of split ends and make your hair look healthier. It's best to go to a professional hair stylist for a trim, as they will be able to assess the damage and give you the best advice on how much to cut.

Use a nourishing hair mask

Using a nourishing hair mask can help repair your broken hair and make it look healthier. Look for a hair mask that contains ingredients like keratin, argan oil, and shea butter, as these are known to help repair damaged hair. Apply the hair mask once a week and leave it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it off.

Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and make it more prone to breaking. Try to avoid using these tools as much as possible, or at least use them on a low heat setting. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray first to help protect your hair from damage.

Use a wide-toothed comb

When brushing your hair, use a wide-toothed comb instead of a brush. This will help prevent tangles and breakage, as brushes can be harsh on your hair. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, being gentle as you go.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to tame broken hair on top of your head:

Step 1: Trim your hair

Visit a professional hair stylist and get a trim to get rid of split ends and make your hair look healthier.

Step 2: Use a nourishing hair mask

Apply a nourishing hair mask once a week to help repair your broken hair and make it look healthier.

Step 3: Avoid heat styling

Try to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, or at least use them on a low heat setting. Use a heat protectant spray to help protect your hair from damage.

Step 4: Use a wide-toothed comb

When brushing your hair, use a wide-toothed comb instead of a brush. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, being gentle as you go.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you tame broken hair on top of your head:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, as this can cause breakage.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to help prevent tangles and breakage while you sleep.
  • Be gentle when towel-drying your hair, as rough rubbing can cause breakage.
  • Eat a healthy diet and stay hydrated to help promote healthy hair growth.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about taming broken hair on top of your head:

How long will it take to see results?

It can take several weeks to see results, depending on the severity of the damage. Be patient and consistent with your hair care routine.

Can I dye my hair if it's broken?

It's best to wait until your hair is healthier before dyeing it, as dyeing can cause further damage.

How often should I trim my hair?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important for keeping it healthy and preventing breakage. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a hair serum or oil to help nourish your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you can tame your broken hair on top of your head and get it back to looking healthy and beautiful. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and you'll see results in no time.
