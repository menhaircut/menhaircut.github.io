How Can I Make My Dreadlocks Thicker Tips Tricks And Hair Care

Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are a unique and stylish way to express yourself and your culture. However, many people struggle with making their dreadlocks thicker. In this article, we will discuss how you can make your dreadlocks thicker, with tips, tricks, and hair care advice.

How to Make Your Dreadlocks Thicker

Before we dive into the tips and tricks, it is important to understand the basics of how dreadlocks form. Dreadlocks are formed when hair is twisted and knotted into sections. Over time, the hair strands will knot and tangle together, creating the signature dreadlock look.

To make your dreadlocks thicker, you need to encourage the hair strands to knot and tangle together. This can be done through a variety of methods, including backcombing, palm rolling, and interlocking.

Backcombing

Backcombing involves teasing the hair strands towards the root, creating a knotted and tangled base for the dreadlock to form. This method is popular for creating instant dreadlocks, but it can also be used to thicken existing dreadlocks.

Palm Rolling

Palm rolling involves rolling the dreadlocks between your palms, encouraging the hair strands to knot and tangle together. This method is gentle and can be done daily to thicken and tighten your dreadlocks.

Interlocking

Interlocking involves weaving a tool through the dreadlock to create knots and tangles. This method is best for maintaining existing dreadlocks and can be done every 6-8 weeks to thicken and tighten your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you understand the basics of how dreadlocks form and how to thicken them, let's go through a step-by-step guide on how to make your dreadlocks thicker.

  1. Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue.
  2. Blow dry your hair until it is completely dry.
  3. Separate your hair into sections using a rattail comb.
  4. Take one section of hair and backcomb the hair strands towards the root.
  5. Repeat this process on all sections of hair.
  6. Once all sections have been backcombed, use a crochet hook or interlocking tool to weave the hair strands together.
  7. Palm roll each dreadlock between your palms to encourage the hair strands to knot and tangle together.
  8. Repeat this process every 6-8 weeks to maintain and thicken your dreadlocks.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you make your dreadlocks thicker:

  • Avoid using too much product on your hair, as this can weigh down your dreadlocks and prevent them from knotting and tangling together.
  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent your dreadlocks from getting caught or tangled in your sleep.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip the natural oils from your scalp and cause your dreadlocks to become brittle and break.
  • Regularly moisturize your dreadlocks with a natural oil, such as coconut oil or jojoba oil, to keep them healthy and strong.

FAQ

Can I make my dreadlocks thicker without using chemicals or extensions?

Yes, you can make your dreadlocks thicker without using chemicals or extensions. Backcombing, palm rolling, and interlocking are all natural methods for thickening your dreadlocks.

How long will it take for my dreadlocks to become thicker?

The time it takes for your dreadlocks to become thicker will depend on several factors, including the length and thickness of your hair, your hair texture, and the method you use to thicken your dreadlocks. Generally, it can take several months to a year to see significant thickening results.

Hair Care for Thick Dreadlocks

Once you have successfully thickened your dreadlocks, it is important to maintain their health and strength. Here are some hair care tips for thick dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair with a residue-free shampoo every 1-2 weeks to prevent buildup.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your dreadlocks moisturized and soft.
  • Protect your dreadlocks from the sun and heat by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Trim any loose or split ends to prevent breakage and maintain a neat appearance.

By following these tips and tricks, you can make your dreadlocks thicker and maintain their health and strength. Remember to be patient, as the process may take some time, but the results will be worth it in the end.
