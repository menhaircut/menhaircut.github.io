How To Cut Face Framing Pieces For Curly Hair

Written by Robby Nov 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting face framing pieces for curly hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done easily. In this article, we will be discussing how to cut face framing pieces for curly hair in a relaxed language. We will also give you tips on how to care for your curly hair and answer some frequently asked questions.

Description

Curly hair can be beautiful and unique, but it can also be challenging to style and maintain. One way to make curly hair more manageable is by cutting face framing pieces. Face framing pieces can help to frame your face and give your hair a more polished look. However, cutting curly hair can be tricky, and it's essential to know how to do it properly to avoid any mishaps.

How to Cut Face Framing Pieces for Curly Hair

Before you start cutting your curly hair, make sure you have the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. Once you have your tools, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Detangle Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and detangling it with a wide-tooth comb. Curly hair is prone to tangles, and it's essential to remove any knots before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections, starting at the top of your head. Use a comb to create a part down the middle of your head, then divide each side into two sections. Clip each section to keep it out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the First Section

Start with one side of your head and unclip the first section. Wet the hair with your spray bottle, then comb it straight. Use your scissors to cut a small section of hair from the front, just below your cheekbone. Cut at a slight angle, so the hair is longer towards the back.

Step 4: Repeat on the Other Side

Repeat the same process on the other side of your head, making sure to cut the same amount of hair. Keep in mind that curly hair shrinks when it dries, so it's better to cut less hair than more.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut both sides, use your comb to blend the layers together. Comb the hair towards your face, then use your scissors to trim any uneven pieces.

Tips for Cutting Face Framing Pieces for Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when cutting face framing pieces for curly hair:

  • Always cut curly hair when it's dry. Wet hair can be deceiving, and it's better to see the true length of your curls.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to cut small sections and build up to the desired length.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.

FAQ

Can I cut my curly hair myself?

Yes, you can cut your curly hair yourself, but it's essential to have the right tools and techniques. If you're not confident, it's always best to consult a professional.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It's recommended to cut curly hair every 8-12 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Caring for curly hair is essential to keep it healthy and prevent damage. Here are some tips for caring for your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized.
  • Don't brush your hair when it's dry. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle it when it's wet.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.

Conclusion

Cutting face framing pieces for curly hair doesn't have to be complicated. With the right tools, techniques, and care, you can achieve a beautiful and polished look. Remember to take your time and cut small sections of hair at a time. Don't forget to care for your curly hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.
