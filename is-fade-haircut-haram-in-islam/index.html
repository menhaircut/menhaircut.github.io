Description

Fade haircut is a popular hairstyle for men that involves gradually tapering the hair from the sides to the back and leaving the hair on top longer. It is a versatile hairstyle that can be customized according to one's preference. However, some Muslims are wondering if this hairstyle is haram or forbidden in Islam.

There is no clear answer to this question since Islam does not explicitly prohibit any particular hairstyle. However, some Muslim scholars argue that certain hairstyles can be considered haram if they imitate the hairstyles of non-Muslims or if they are associated with a particular group or ideology that goes against Islamic principles.

How to Get a Fade Haircut

Getting a fade haircut is relatively easy and can be done by a professional barber or even at home with the help of a friend or family member. Here are the basic steps in getting a fade haircut:

Step 1: Choose the type of fade

There are different types of fade haircuts, such as low fade, mid fade, high fade, skin fade, and bald fade. Choose the type of fade that you want depending on your preference and hair type.

Step 2: Decide on the length of the hair on top

The length of the hair on top will depend on your preference and the style that you want to achieve. Communicate this clearly with your barber or hairstylist.

Step 3: Trim the sides and back

The barber or hairstylist will use clippers to trim the hair on the sides and back. The length will gradually decrease as the clippers move upward, creating the fade effect.

Step 4: Blend the hair

The barber or hairstylist will blend the hair between the different lengths to create a seamless transition from short to long hair.

Step 5: Style the hair on top

You can style the hair on top according to your preference using a comb, hair gel, or hair wax.

Step-by-Step Guide in Getting a Fade Haircut

Here is a more detailed step-by-step guide in getting a fade haircut:

Step 1: Choose the type of fade

As mentioned earlier, there are different types of fade haircuts. Here are some examples:

  • Low fade - the hair is tapered just above the ears
  • Mid fade - the hair is tapered halfway up the sides
  • High fade - the hair is tapered just below the temples
  • Skin fade - the hair is tapered down to the skin
  • Bald fade - the hair is tapered down to the skin completely

Step 2: Decide on the length of the hair on top

The length of the hair on top will depend on your preference and the style that you want to achieve. You can ask your barber or hairstylist for suggestions. Some popular styles include:

  • Textured crop
  • Slick back
  • Crew cut
  • Quiff
  • Pompadour

Step 3: Trim the sides and back

The barber or hairstylist will start by using clippers with a guard to trim the hair on the sides and back. The guard number will depend on the type of fade that you want. For example, a low fade may use a guard number 1 or 2, while a high fade may use a guard number 3 or 4.

Step 4: Blend the hair

The barber or hairstylist will use scissors or clippers without a guard to blend the hair between the different lengths. This will create a seamless transition from short to long hair.

Step 5: Style the hair on top

You can style the hair on top according to your preference using a comb, hair gel, or hair wax. You can also ask your barber or hairstylist for recommendations.

Tips in Maintaining a Fade Haircut

Here are some tips in maintaining a fade haircut:

  • Visit your barber or hairstylist regularly for touch-ups
  • Wash your hair regularly with shampoo and conditioner
  • Use hair products sparingly to avoid build-up
  • Avoid exposing your hair to heat from styling tools
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protection spray

FAQs about Fade Haircut and Islam

Is fade haircut haram in Islam?

There is no clear answer to this question since Islam does not explicitly prohibit any particular hairstyle. However, some Muslim scholars argue that certain hairstyles can be considered haram if they imitate the hairstyles of non-Muslims or if they are associated with a particular group or ideology that goes against Islamic principles.

Can Muslim men have a fade haircut?

Yes, Muslim men can have a fade haircut as long as it does not violate any Islamic principles or values. It is important to consult with a trusted Muslim scholar or imam for guidance.

What is the Islamic ruling on men's hairstyles?

There is no specific ruling on men's hairstyles in Islam. However, it is generally advised to maintain a neat and tidy appearance and to avoid hairstyles that are associated with non-Muslims or immoral activities.

Hair Care Tips for Men

Aside from getting a fade haircut, here are some hair care tips for men:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair as it can strip away natural oils
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protection spray
  • Avoid using hair products with harsh chemicals
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet to promote hair growth
  • Get enough rest and manage stress to prevent hair loss

In conclusion, a fade haircut is not haram in Islam as long as it does not violate any Islamic principles or values. It is important to maintain a neat and tidy appearance and to consult with a trusted Muslim scholar or imam for guidance. By following proper hair care tips, you can maintain a healthy and stylish hair despite having a fade haircut.
