Can You Layer Naturally Curly Hair

Written by Petter Aug 07, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Layering curly hair is a popular trend that can add volume and texture to your curls. If you have naturally curly hair, you may be wondering if layering is a good option for you. The answer is yes, you can layer naturally curly hair, but there are a few things you should consider before making the decision to do so.

Description

Layering curly hair involves cutting the hair at different lengths to create layers that add volume and texture. This can be a great option for those with thin or fine hair, as it can create the illusion of fullness. However, it's important to note that layering curly hair can also cause frizz and make it harder to manage.

How To Layer Naturally Curly Hair

If you're interested in layering your naturally curly hair, the first step is to find a stylist who is experienced in cutting curly hair. Curly hair requires a different cutting technique than straight hair, so it's important to find someone who knows what they're doing.

Once you've found a stylist, it's important to communicate your wishes clearly. Let them know that you want to add layers to your hair, but you're concerned about frizz and manageability. They should be able to work with you to create a cut that meets your needs.

Step By Step

Here are the steps to layering naturally curly hair:

  1. Find a stylist who is experienced in cutting curly hair.
  2. Communicate your wishes clearly.
  3. Let the stylist work their magic.
  4. Follow a good hair care routine to maintain your curls.

It's important to note that not all curly hair types are the same, so what works for one person may not work for another. It may take some trial and error to find the right cut for your hair type.

Tips

If you're considering layering your naturally curly hair, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Find a stylist who is experienced in cutting curly hair
  • Communicate your needs clearly
  • Be prepared for some trial and error
  • Follow a good hair care routine to maintain your curls

FAQ

Q: Will layering my naturally curly hair cause frizz?

A: It's possible. Layering can create more volume and texture, but it can also make it harder to manage your curls. It's important to follow a good hair care routine to keep frizz under control.

Q: How often should I get my naturally curly hair layered?

A: This depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, you should get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

Maintaining your curly hair after layering is important. Here are a few tips to help you care for your hair:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo
  • Condition your hair regularly
  • Avoid heat styling tools
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet

By following these tips, you can help keep your naturally curly hair healthy, manageable, and looking great.

Conclusion

If you have naturally curly hair and you're considering layering it, be sure to find a stylist who is experienced in cutting curly hair. Communicate your needs clearly, and be prepared for some trial and error. Follow a good hair care routine to keep your curls healthy and looking great.
