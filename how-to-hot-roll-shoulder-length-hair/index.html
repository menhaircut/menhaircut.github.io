How To Hot Roll Shoulder Length Hair A Step By Step Guide

Welcome to our guide on how to hot roll shoulder length hair! If you're looking to add volume and bounce to your shoulder-length locks, hot rollers are an excellent tool to have in your hair care arsenal. In this article, we'll take you through the process of hot rolling shoulder length hair, from preparing your hair to adding finishing touches. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  1. Preparation
  2. Sectioning
  3. Rolling
  4. Cooling
  5. Removing the Rollers

Preparation

Before you start hot rolling your shoulder length hair, it's essential to prepare your hair properly. Here's what you need to do:

Wash and Condition Your Hair

Wash and condition your hair thoroughly before hot rolling it. Use a shampoo and conditioner that are suitable for your hair type. Avoid using heavy products that can weigh your hair down.

Dry Your Hair

Dry your hair completely before hot rolling it. Use a blow dryer or let your hair air dry. If you're using a blow dryer, use a heat protectant spray to prevent heat damage.

Apply a Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair before hot rolling it. A heat protectant will protect your hair from heat damage.

Sectioning

Sectioning your hair is essential for hot rolling it. Here's how to do it:

Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into small sections using clips. Start at the bottom of your hair and work your way up. Use clips to hold each section in place.

Choose the Right Size Rollers

Choose the right size rollers for your hair. For shoulder length hair, use medium-sized rollers. Small rollers will create tight curls, while large rollers will create loose waves.

Rolling

Now it's time to start rolling your hair. Follow these steps:

Start at the Bottom

Start at the bottom of your hair and work your way up. Take a small section of hair and comb it through. Roll the hair around the roller, starting at the ends and rolling up towards the roots.

Secure the Roller

Secure the roller in place by bending the ends together. Repeat this process until you've rolled all the sections of your hair.

Cooling

Once you've rolled your hair, it's time to let it cool. Here's how:

Wait for Your Hair to Cool

Wait for your hair to cool completely before removing the rollers. This will help set the curls.

Removing the Rollers

Now it's time to remove the rollers. Follow these steps:

Unroll the Rollers

Unroll the rollers gently. Start at the bottom of your hair and work your way up. Avoid pulling your hair as you unroll the rollers.

Styling

Now that you've removed the rollers, it's time to style your hair. Here's how:

Finger Comb Your Hair

Finger comb your hair to loosen the curls. Avoid using a brush or comb, as this can make your hair frizzy.

Add Finishing Touches

Add finishing touches to your hair, such as hairspray or serum. This will help keep your hair in place and add shine.

Tips for Hot Rolling Shoulder Length Hair

Here are some tips to help you hot roll your shoulder-length hair:

  • Use medium-sized rollers for shoulder-length hair.
  • Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair before hot rolling it.
  • Divide your hair into small sections for easy rolling.
  • Wait for your hair to cool completely before removing the rollers.
  • Finger comb your hair to avoid making it frizzy.

FAQs

Can I hot roll my shoulder length hair if it's thin?

Yes, you can hot roll your shoulder length hair even if it's thin. Use medium-sized rollers and avoid using heavy products that can weigh your hair down.

How long will the curls last?

The curls will last for several hours, depending on how well you've set them. Use hairspray or serum to help keep the curls in place.

Hair Care for Hot Rolled Hair

Here are some tips to help you take care of your hot rolled hair:

  • Avoid washing your hair for at least 24 hours after hot rolling it.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.

Conclusion

Hot rolling shoulder length hair is an excellent way to add volume and bounce to your locks. By following the steps in this guide, you can achieve beautiful, long-lasting curls that will turn heads. Remember to take care of your hair by using the right products and avoiding heat damage. Happy hot rolling!
