Is It Haram For A Guy To Dye His Hair

Written by Eveline Jul 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you a guy thinking of dyeing your hair but worried about whether it's haram or not? Many Muslim men wonder if it's permissible to dye their hair, especially if they want to cover up gray hair or change their hair color for aesthetic reasons. In this blog post, we'll explore the answer to the question "is it haram for a guy to dye his hair?"

Description

In Islam, changing the creation of Allah is generally discouraged, but dyeing hair is not explicitly mentioned in the Quran or Hadith. However, some scholars believe that it's haram for a man to dye his hair because it's considered imitating women or non-Muslims, which can be seen as a violation of Islamic teachings. On the other hand, some scholars argue that it's permissible to dye hair as long as it's done in moderation and not for the purpose of deceiving others.

How to Dye Your Hair as a Muslim Man

If you decide to dye your hair as a Muslim man, there are a few things you should keep in mind:

Choose a Halal Hair Dye

Make sure to use a halal hair dye that doesn't contain any haram ingredients such as alcohol or animal-derived substances. There are several brands of halal hair dye available in the market, so do your research and choose the one that suits your needs.

Seek Permission

If you're living with your parents or spouse, seek their permission before dyeing your hair. It's important to maintain family harmony and avoid any unnecessary conflicts.

Consider Your Workplace

If you're working in a conservative or religious environment, it's best to avoid bright or unconventional hair colors that may be seen as unprofessional or inappropriate.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to dye your hair as a Muslim man:

  1. Choose the color you want to dye your hair.
  2. Wash your hair thoroughly and dry it with a towel.
  3. Apply petroleum jelly or coconut oil around your hairline to prevent the dye from staining your skin.
  4. Wear gloves to avoid staining your hands with the dye.
  5. Mix the hair dye according to the instructions on the package.
  6. Apply the dye to your hair, starting from the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  7. Leave the dye on for the recommended time (usually 30-45 minutes).
  8. Rinse your hair thoroughly with water until the water runs clear.
  9. Apply the conditioner that comes with the dye to your hair and leave it on for a few minutes.
  10. Rinse your hair again and dry it with a towel.

Tips for Dyeing Your Hair as a Muslim Man

Here are some tips to help you dye your hair as a Muslim man:

  • Choose a natural-looking hair color that complements your skin tone.
  • Do a patch test before dyeing your hair to check for allergies or skin reactions.
  • Avoid dyeing your hair too frequently as it can damage your hair and scalp.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to maintain the color and health of your hair.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to direct sunlight or chlorine water as it can fade the color.

FAQs

Is it haram for a Muslim man to dye his hair?

There is no clear ruling on whether it's haram or halal for a Muslim man to dye his hair. Some scholars believe that it's haram because it's considered imitating women or non-Muslims, while others believe that it's permissible as long as it's done in moderation and not for the purpose of deceiving others.

What is the best halal hair dye for Muslim men?

There are several brands of halal hair dye available in the market, such as Henna Guys, Manic Panic, and Arctic Fox. Choose the one that suits your needs and preferences.

How often should I dye my hair?

Avoid dyeing your hair too frequently as it can damage your hair and scalp. It's best to wait at least 4-6 weeks between dyeing sessions.

Hair Care for Colored Hair

If you've dyed your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its color and health. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the color from your hair.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair as it can fade the color.
  • Apply a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons and blow dryers as they can damage your hair and make the color fade faster.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine water by wearing a hat or using a hair sunscreen.

Overall, dyeing your hair as a Muslim man is a personal choice that depends on your beliefs and preferences. If you decide to dye your hair, make sure to choose a halal hair dye and follow the proper steps and precautions to avoid any harm or conflicts.
