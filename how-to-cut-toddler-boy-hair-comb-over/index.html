How To Cut Toddler Boy Hair Comb Over A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Sep 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have a toddler boy with thick hair, you may be considering a comb over haircut. This classic style is perfect for little ones who want to look stylish and put-together. However, cutting a comb over can be a challenge, especially if you're not experienced with hair cutting. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut a toddler boy hair comb over step-by-step, so you can give your little one the perfect haircut.

Description

A comb over is a popular haircut for toddler boys that involves parting the hair on one side and combing it over to the other side. This creates a sleek, polished look that's great for formal occasions or just everyday wear. To cut a comb over, you'll need a few basic tools, including scissors, clippers, and a comb.

How to Cut Toddler Boy Hair Comb Over: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start cutting, you'll need to gather all the necessary supplies. You'll need scissors, clippers, a comb, a spray bottle filled with water, and a cape or towel to wrap around your child's neck.

Step 2: Choose the Right Length

The length of your child's hair will determine how long the comb over should be. Generally, a comb over should be about 2-3 inches long on the top and shorter on the sides and back. Use your clippers to trim the sides and back first, gradually reducing the length as you move up to the top.

Step 3: Cut the Top

Once you've trimmed the sides and back, it's time to cut the top. Use your scissors to cut the hair in small sections, starting with the front and working your way back. Comb the hair straight up and cut across the top, making sure to keep the length consistent.

Step 4: Create the Part

Once the top is cut to the desired length, it's time to create the part. Use your comb to create a part on one side of your child's head, and comb the hair over to the other side. Use your scissors to trim any stray hairs and make sure the part is clean and even.

Step 5: Final Touches

Once the part is created, it's time for some final touches. Use your clippers to trim any stray hairs around the ears and neck, and use your scissors to make any final adjustments to the length or shape of the cut. Use the spray bottle to wet the hair down and comb it into place.

Tips for Cutting Toddler Boy Hair Comb Over

Cutting a toddler boy hair comb over can be a challenge, but there are a few tips that can make the process easier:

  • Use sharp scissors and clippers to ensure a clean cut.
  • Start with a longer length and work your way down, as it's easier to cut more hair off than to add it back on.
  • Use a cape or towel to protect your child's clothing and keep them comfortable during the haircut.
  • Take breaks if your child becomes fussy or restless, as it's important to keep them calm and relaxed during the process.

FAQs

How often should I cut my toddler's hair?

It's recommended to cut your toddler's hair every 4-6 weeks, depending on how quickly their hair grows.

What if my toddler won't sit still during the haircut?

If your toddler is fussy or restless during the haircut, take breaks as needed and try to keep them calm and relaxed. You may also want to consider distracting them with a favorite toy or snack.

Hair Care Tips for Toddler Boys

Once your toddler boy's hair is cut, it's important to take care of it properly to keep it looking healthy and stylish. Here are a few hair care tips:

  • Wash your toddler's hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a detangling spray or leave-in conditioner to make brushing easier.
  • Brush your toddler's hair gently, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  • Avoid using styling products that contain harsh chemicals or alcohol, as these can damage the hair.

With these tips and techniques, you can give your toddler boy a stylish and professional-looking comb over haircut. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Happy cutting!
