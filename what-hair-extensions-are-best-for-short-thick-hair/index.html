What Hair Extensions Are Best For Short Thick Hair

If you have short and thick hair, you may have limited options when it comes to hairstyling. Fortunately, hair extensions can help you achieve your desired look. But with so many types of hair extensions available, it can be overwhelming to choose the right one. In this article, weâll discuss what hair extensions are best for short thick hair.

Description

Hair extensions are strands of hair that are attached to your natural hair to add length, volume, and thickness. They come in different colors, lengths, and textures to match your natural hair. Hair extensions can be made of human hair or synthetic fibers. Human hair extensions are more expensive but look more natural and can be styled like your natural hair. Synthetic hair extensions are cheaper but have limited styling options and may not look as natural as human hair extensions.

How to Choose the Right Hair Extension Type

The type of hair extension that is best for short thick hair depends on your desired look, budget, and lifestyle. Here are some popular hair extension types:

Clip-In Hair Extensions

Clip-in hair extensions are the easiest and most affordable type of hair extension. They come in small wefts that are attached to your natural hair using clips. Clip-in hair extensions can add volume and length to your hair without damaging your natural hair. They are also easy to remove and can be reused multiple times.

Tape-In Hair Extensions

Tape-in hair extensions are semi-permanent hair extensions that are attached to your natural hair using a special adhesive tape. They are lightweight and comfortable to wear. Tape-in hair extensions can add volume and length to your hair for up to 8 weeks. They require regular maintenance and should be removed by a professional stylist.

Sew-In Hair Extensions

Sew-in hair extensions are a more permanent type of hair extension. They are attached to your natural hair using a braiding technique and a needle and thread. Sew-in hair extensions can add volume and length to your hair for up to 3 months. They require regular maintenance and can be more expensive than clip-in or tape-in hair extensions.

Step by Step Guide on How to Apply Hair Extensions

Hereâs a step by step guide on how to apply clip-in hair extensions:

  1. Brush your natural hair to remove tangles.
  2. Section your hair into layers and clip the top layers away.
  3. Take a weft of clip-in hair extension and place it underneath the layer of hair you just clipped away.
  4. Clip the weft securely to your natural hair.
  5. Repeat steps 3 and 4 until you have achieved your desired look.

Note: Itâs important to match the color and texture of the hair extension to your natural hair to achieve a natural look.

Tips on How to Care for Hair Extensions

Proper care and maintenance can extend the lifespan of your hair extensions. Here are some tips:

  • Brush your hair extensions regularly to prevent tangles.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on synthetic hair extensions.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools on human hair extensions.
  • Wash your hair extensions with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid sleeping with wet hair extensions.

FAQs

Can I color my hair extensions?

Itâs best to consult a professional stylist before coloring your hair extensions. Human hair extensions can be colored but synthetic hair extensions cannot.

Can I swim with hair extensions?

Swimming with hair extensions is not recommended as chlorine and saltwater can damage them. If you must swim, wear a swim cap and wash your hair extensions immediately after.

How long do hair extensions last?

The lifespan of hair extensions depends on the type and quality of the hair extension, how well you care for them, and how often you wear them. Clip-in hair extensions can last up to a year with proper care while sew-in hair extensions can last up to 3 months.

Hair Extensions for Short Thick Hair: Final Thoughts

Proper care and maintenance can extend the lifespan of your hair extensions. Donât be afraid to experiment with different hair extension types until you find the one that works best for you.
