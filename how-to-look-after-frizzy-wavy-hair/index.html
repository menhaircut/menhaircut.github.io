How To Look After Frizzy Wavy Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Petter Jul 22, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have frizzy wavy hair, you know the struggle of trying to keep it under control. Frizzy hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including humidity, heat styling, and genetics. Fortunately, there are steps you can take to manage frizz and keep your waves looking their best. In this guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know about how to look after frizzy wavy hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Look After Frizzy Wavy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Managing Frizz
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Products for Frizzy Wavy Hair

How to Look After Frizzy Wavy Hair

The key to managing frizzy wavy hair is to keep it hydrated and nourished. This means using products that are designed to moisturize and protect your hair, as well as avoiding heat styling as much as possible. Here are some tips for looking after your frizzy wavy hair:

1. Wash Your Hair Less Frequently

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can lead to frizz. Try to wash your hair no more than every other day, or even less frequently if possible.

2. Use a Hydrating Shampoo and Conditioner

When you do wash your hair, use a shampoo and conditioner that are designed to hydrate and nourish. Look for products that contain ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, or shea butter.

3. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help to keep your hair hydrated throughout the day. Apply a small amount to damp hair after washing and leave it in.

4. Use a Microfiber Towel to Dry Your Hair

A regular towel can rough up the cuticles of your hair, causing frizz. Use a microfiber towel instead, which is gentler on your hair.

5. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and cause frizz. Try to let your hair air dry as much as possible, and use heat styling tools only on special occasions.

6. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can also cause damage to your hair, resulting in frizz. Wear a hat or use a hair sunscreen when you’re outside for extended periods of time.

7. Get Regular Trims

Split ends can make frizz worse, so make sure to get regular trims to keep your hair healthy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide for managing frizzy wavy hair:

1. Wash Your Hair with a Hydrating Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a shampoo and conditioner that are designed to hydrate and nourish your hair. Massage the shampoo into your scalp, then rinse thoroughly. Apply the conditioner to the lengths of your hair, avoiding the roots, and leave it in for a few minutes before rinsing.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to damp hair. Use a small amount, and work it through the lengths of your hair with your fingers.

3. Gently Dry Your Hair with a Microfiber Towel

Use a microfiber towel to gently dry your hair, avoiding rubbing or twisting it. Instead, blot your hair dry and let it air dry as much as possible.

4. Style Your Hair

If you want to style your hair, use a styling product that is designed to manage frizz. Apply it to damp hair and style as desired. Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.

5. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Wear a hat or use a hair sunscreen to protect your hair from the damaging effects of the sun.

Tips for Managing Frizz

Here are some additional tips for managing frizz:

1. Use a Silk Pillowcase

A silk pillowcase is gentler on your hair than a cotton one, and can help to prevent frizz while you sleep.

2. Avoid Touching Your Hair Too Much

Touching your hair too much can cause frizz. Try to avoid playing with your hair or running your fingers through it too often.

3. Use a Wide-Toothed Comb

A wide-toothed comb is gentler on your hair than a brush, and can help to prevent breakage and frizz.

FAQs

Q: What causes frizzy wavy hair?

A: Frizzy hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including humidity, heat styling, and genetics.

Q: Can I use hair oil to manage frizz?

A: Yes, hair oil can help to manage frizz. Look for oils that are lightweight and won’t weigh your hair down, such as argan oil or jojoba oil.

Q: Can I wash my hair every day if I have frizzy hair?

A: It’s not recommended to wash your hair every day if you have frizzy hair. Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can lead to frizz.

Hair Care Products for Frizzy Wavy Hair

Here are some hair care products that are designed to manage frizz:

  • Hydrating shampoo and conditioner
  • Leave-in conditioner
  • Styling cream or gel
  • Hair oil

Look for products that are specifically designed for wavy or curly hair, and that contain hydrating ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, or shea butter.

Managing frizzy wavy hair can be a challenge, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep your waves looking their best. Follow these tips and tricks to keep your hair hydrated, nourished, and frizz-free.
