Frizzy Wavy Hair How To Tame Your Mane

Written by Frank Nov 16, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have frizzy wavy hair, you know the struggle of trying to tame your unruly locks. It can be frustrating dealing with flyaways, frizz, and a lack of definition in your waves. But fear not, there are ways to manage your frizzy wavy hair and achieve the beautiful, defined waves you crave. In this article, we'll discuss how to care for and style your frizzy wavy hair, as well as provide tips and answer frequently asked questions.

What Causes Frizzy Wavy Hair?

Frizzy wavy hair is caused by a lack of moisture and/or damage to the hair cuticle. When the hair cuticle is damaged, it allows moisture to enter and exit the hair shaft, which causes the hair to become frizzy. Additionally, wavy hair is prone to frizz because the natural oils produced by the scalp have a harder time traveling down the hair shaft due to the waves.

How to Care for Frizzy Wavy Hair

Shampoo and Conditioner

When it comes to caring for frizzy wavy hair, it's important to use the right shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are designed for curly or wavy hair and are sulfate-free, as sulfates can strip the hair of its natural oils. Additionally, be sure to condition your hair regularly to help keep it moisturized.

Hair Oil

Hair oil is a great way to add moisture back into frizzy wavy hair. Apply a small amount of oil to your hair after showering, focusing on the ends and mid-lengths. This will help to seal in moisture and prevent frizz.

Avoid Heat

Heat styling tools can be damaging to frizzy wavy hair. If you must use heat, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

How to Style Frizzy Wavy Hair

Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help to define your waves and keep frizz at bay. Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner to your hair after showering, focusing on the mid-lengths and ends.

Diffuser

A diffuser attachment for your hair dryer can help to enhance your waves and prevent frizz. Simply attach the diffuser to your hair dryer and use a low heat and low speed setting.

Air Dry

Air drying your hair is the best way to prevent frizz and enhance your natural waves. After showering, gently wring out your hair and let it air dry.

Tips for Managing Frizzy Wavy Hair

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz
  • Don't over-wash your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils
  • Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to dry your hair, as regular towels can cause frizz

FAQ

Can I brush my frizzy wavy hair?

It's best to avoid brushing frizzy wavy hair, as this can cause frizz and disrupt your natural waves. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

How often should I wash my frizzy wavy hair?

It's best to wash your hair every 2-3 days, as over-washing can strip it of its natural oils.

Can I use a hair straightener on my frizzy wavy hair?

While it's possible to use a hair straightener on frizzy wavy hair, it's best to avoid heat styling tools as much as possible. If you must use a hair straightener, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

Conclusion

Frizzy wavy hair can be a challenge to manage, but with the right care and styling techniques, you can achieve beautiful, defined waves. Remember to moisturize your hair, avoid heat styling tools, and embrace your natural waves. With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to tame your frizzy wavy hair and feel confident rocking your natural texture.
