How To Find Out What Is Causing Hair Loss

Written by Petter Jun 27, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you experiencing hair loss and wondering what the cause could be? Hair loss can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, stress, medication, and more. In this article, we’ll go over how to find out what is causing your hair loss and what steps you can take to address it.

Table of Contents

  • How to Find Out What is Causing Hair Loss
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Addressing Hair Loss
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Healthy Hair

How to Find Out What is Causing Hair Loss

The first step in determining the cause of your hair loss is to visit a dermatologist or trichologist. They will be able to examine your scalp and hair to determine if there are any underlying medical conditions that could be causing your hair loss.

It’s also important to review your medical history and any medications you may be taking. Certain medications, such as chemotherapy drugs or blood thinners, can cause hair loss as a side effect.

Other factors that can contribute to hair loss include stress, hormonal changes, and nutritional deficiencies. By working with a healthcare professional, you can identify any potential causes and develop a plan to address them.

Medical Conditions That Can Cause Hair Loss

There are several medical conditions that can cause hair loss, including:

  • Androgenetic alopecia (male or female pattern baldness)
  • Alopecia areata (an autoimmune disorder)
  • Scarring alopecia (caused by scarring on the scalp)
  • Traction alopecia (caused by tight hairstyles)

If you are experiencing hair loss, it’s important to work with a healthcare professional to determine if any of these conditions are the cause.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step by step guide to help you find out what is causing your hair loss:

  1. Visit a dermatologist or trichologist to examine your scalp and hair.
  2. Review your medical history and medications with your healthcare professional.
  3. Undergo any necessary tests or bloodwork to identify potential underlying medical conditions.
  4. Address any nutritional deficiencies or hormonal imbalances.
  5. Consider stress reduction techniques, such as meditation or yoga.
  6. Explore treatment options, such as topical or oral medications, hair transplants, or scalp micropigmentation.
  7. Maintain a healthy diet and hair care routine to promote healthy hair growth.

Tips for Addressing Hair Loss

Here are some tips for addressing hair loss:

  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals, including iron, biotin, and zinc.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair, such as braids or ponytails.
  • Use gentle hair care products and avoid heat styling tools.
  • Consider using a hair growth shampoo or supplement.
  • Discuss treatment options with your healthcare professional.

FAQs

Can stress cause hair loss?

Yes, stress can contribute to hair loss. Telogen effluvium, a condition where hair follicles enter the resting phase prematurely, can be triggered by stress.

Can nutritional deficiencies cause hair loss?

Yes, nutritional deficiencies, particularly iron and biotin deficiencies, can contribute to hair loss.

Is hair loss permanent?

It depends on the cause of the hair loss. Some types of hair loss, such as androgenetic alopecia, are permanent.

Hair Care for Healthy Hair

Maintaining a healthy hair care routine can help promote healthy hair growth and prevent hair loss. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid heat styling tools or use a heat protectant spray.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Consider using hair growth supplements or treatments.

By following these tips and working with a healthcare professional to identify any underlying causes of hair loss, you can promote healthy hair growth and address any hair loss concerns.
