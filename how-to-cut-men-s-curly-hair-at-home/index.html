How To Cut Men s Curly Hair At Home A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of paying a fortune at the barber shop every time you need a haircut? Cutting men's curly hair at home may seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it is possible to achieve a professional-looking cut. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to cut men's curly hair at home, including how to properly prepare and style the hair, as well as some tips and tricks to make the process easier. Let's get started!

  • How to Cut Men's Curly Hair at Home

How to Cut Men's Curly Hair at Home

The first thing you'll need to do is gather the right tools. You should have a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a clipper (optional). You may also want to invest in a mirror and a hair cutting cape to make the process easier and avoid getting hair all over your clothes.

Next, you'll want to prepare the hair. Curly hair should always be cut when it's dry, as wet hair can shrink up to 20% when it dries, making it difficult to get an accurate cut. Comb the hair straight down and trim any split ends or damaged areas.

Once the hair is prepped, it's time to start cutting. The most important thing to remember when cutting curly hair is to cut it in its natural state. Curly hair tends to spring up when it's cut too short, so it's best to leave a little extra length to account for this.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Start by sectioning off the hair. Use clips to separate the top, sides, and back of the hair.

2. Decide on the length you want to cut. Remember, it's best to cut curly hair a little longer than you think you want it.

3. Start cutting the hair using the comb and scissors. Take small sections of hair and cut at an angle, rather than straight across. This will help prevent the hair from looking too blunt.

4. Once you've finished cutting the top, move on to the sides and back. Use the same technique, taking small sections of hair and cutting at an angle.

5. If you want a shorter cut, you can use clippers to trim the sides and back. Use a guard that's one or two sizes longer than the top to create a tapered effect.

Tips and Tricks

- Don't cut too much at once. Take small sections of hair and trim a little bit at a time to avoid making mistakes.

- Use a mirror to check the back of the head. It can be difficult to see what you're doing, so having a mirror can help you get a better view.

- If you're unsure about cutting curly hair, start with a longer length and work your way shorter. This will give you a chance to see how the hair reacts to being cut and make adjustments as needed.

FAQs

Q: How often should I cut my curly hair?

A: It depends on the length and style of your hair, but most men with curly hair should get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep the shape and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I cut my curly hair when it's wet?

A: It's best to avoid cutting curly hair when it's wet, as it can shrink up to 20% when it dries and make it difficult to get an accurate cut.

Hair Care After the Cut

After you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain the shape and prevent damage. Here are a few tips:

- Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair moisturized.

- Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can dry out and damage the hair.

- Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep the hair soft and manageable.

- Get regular trims to keep the shape and prevent split ends.

Cutting men's curly hair at home may seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's possible to achieve a professional-looking cut. Remember to take your time, use small sections of hair, and cut in the hair's natural state. With a little practice, you'll be a pro in no time!
