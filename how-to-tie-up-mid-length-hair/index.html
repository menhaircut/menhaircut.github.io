How To Tie Up Mid Length Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 01, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have mid-length hair, you know just how versatile it can be. But sometimes, you just want to get it out of your face and off your neck. That's where tying up your hair comes in. In this guide, we'll show you how to tie up mid-length hair in a variety of ways, from sleek and chic to casual and carefree.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Tie Up Mid Length Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Tying Up Mid-Length Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care Tips for Mid-Length Hair

How to Tie Up Mid Length Hair

Before we get into the specific styles, there are a few things to keep in mind when tying up mid-length hair:

  • Start with clean, dry hair
  • Use hair ties that won't damage your hair
  • Don't pull your hair too tight, as this can cause breakage
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works for you

Styles for Tying Up Mid Length Hair

Now that you know the basics, here are some popular styles for tying up mid-length hair:

The Messy Bun

The messy bun is a classic for a reason. It's stylish, easy to do, and perfect for those days when you just don't have time to fuss with your hair. Here's how to do it:

  1. Gather your hair into a high ponytail
  2. Twist the ponytail around itself to create a bun
  3. Secure the bun with a hair tie
  4. Pull out a few strands to create a messy, effortless look

The Half-Up Half-Down

The half-up half-down style is perfect for when you want to keep some of your hair down but still want it out of your face. Here's how to do it:

  1. Gather the top half of your hair and secure it with a hair tie
  2. Leave the bottom half of your hair down
  3. For a more polished look, wrap a strand of hair around the hair tie to hide it

The French Twist

The French twist is a chic and sophisticated style that's perfect for a night out. Here's how to do it:

  1. Gather your hair to one side of your head
  2. Twist your hair upwards, tucking it into itself as you go
  3. Secure the twist with bobby pins
  4. For a more voluminous look, tease your hair before twisting it

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to tying up mid-length hair:

  1. Brush your hair to remove any knots or tangles
  2. Gather your hair into a ponytail or section
  3. Secure your hair with a hair tie
  4. Pull out any strands to create a more relaxed look
  5. For a more polished look, use a comb to smooth down any flyaways

Tips for Tying Up Mid-Length Hair

Here are some tips to help you tie up mid-length hair:

  • Use bobby pins to secure any loose strands
  • Experiment with different hair ties to find what works for you
  • Try adding accessories like headbands or clips to dress up your style
  • Don't tie your hair up too tightly, as this can cause headaches and breakage

FAQs

Can I tie up my hair if it's layered?

Yes, you can still tie up layered hair. Just be sure to secure any shorter layers with bobby pins to prevent them from falling out.

Can I tie up my hair if it's thin?

Yes, you can still tie up thin hair. Just be sure to use hair ties that won't damage your hair and avoid pulling your hair too tightly.

How often should I tie up my hair?

It's best to avoid tying up your hair every day, as this can cause breakage and damage. Try to give your hair a break and let it down at least a few times a week.

Hair Care Tips for Mid-Length Hair

Here are some tips to keep your mid-length hair healthy and looking great:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils
  • Use a heat protectant before using hot tools like curling irons or straighteners
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends
  • Avoid over-brushing your hair, as this can cause breakage

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to tie up your mid-length hair in a variety of styles that are both stylish and easy to do. Whether you're running errands or heading out for a night on the town, these styles will have you looking and feeling great.
