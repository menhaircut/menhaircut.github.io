Why Does My Hair Go Curly Underneath

Written by Oliver Oct 21, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you experiencing curly hair underneath, even though you have straight hair on top? This can be a common issue for many people. In this blog post, we will discuss why does my hair go curly underneath and provide tips on how to manage it.

Description

Curly hair underneath can be caused by a variety of factors such as genetics, hair texture, and haircare routine. It is more common in people with wavy or curly hair texture. However, it can happen to anyone, even those with straight hair on top.

How to

If you want to manage your curly hair underneath, here are some tips that you can follow:

1. Moisturize your hair

Dry hair can cause your hair to become frizzy and curly. Make sure to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated.

2. Use a wide-tooth comb

Avoid using a brush that can cause your hair to tangle and become frizzy. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. This will help prevent breakage and split ends.

3. Avoid heat styling tools

Heat styling tools such as straighteners and curling irons can cause damage to your hair. Try to avoid using these tools as much as possible or use them on a low heat setting. You can also use heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.

4. Get regular trims

Regular trims can help prevent split ends and breakage, which can cause your hair to become curly underneath. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair.

Step by Step

Here are the step-by-step instructions on how to manage your curly hair underneath:

Step 1: Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type.

Step 2: Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

Step 3: Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated.

Step 4: Avoid using heat styling tools or use them on a low heat setting.

Step 5: Get regular trims every 6-8 weeks.

Tips

Here are some additional tips that you can follow to manage your curly hair underneath:

1. Use a silk pillowcase

Avoid using cotton pillowcases that can cause friction and tangles in your hair. Instead, use a silk pillowcase that is gentle on your hair.

2. Don't overwash your hair

Washing your hair too frequently can strip your hair of its natural oils, causing it to become dry and frizzy. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

3. Try a deep conditioning treatment

A deep conditioning treatment can help restore moisture to your hair and prevent it from becoming curly underneath. You can use a store-bought deep conditioner or make your own using natural ingredients such as avocado or honey.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about why does my hair go curly underneath:

Q: Can genetics play a role in why my hair goes curly underneath?

A: Yes, genetics can play a role in determining your hair texture and curl pattern.

Q: Is it possible to permanently straighten my hair underneath?

A: Yes, you can permanently straighten your hair using chemical treatments such as a relaxer or keratin treatment.

Q: Can using too many hair products cause my hair to become curly underneath?

A: Yes, using too many hair products can weigh down your hair and cause it to become curly or frizzy.

Hair Care

Managing curly hair underneath can be challenging, but with the right hair care routine and products, it is possible to keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful. Remember to moisturize your hair, use a wide-tooth comb, avoid heat styling tools, get regular trims, and follow the tips mentioned in this blog post. If you have any concerns about your hair, consult a professional hairstylist for advice.
