How To Blow Dry Thick Frizzy Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of dealing with frizzy hair that just won't cooperate? Do you have thick hair that takes forever to dry? Blow drying your hair can be a great solution to these problems, but it's important to do it correctly to avoid causing damage. In this article, we'll walk you through how to blow dry thick frizzy hair step by step, including tips and FAQs to help you achieve sleek, smooth results.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Blow Dry Thick Frizzy Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Blow Drying Thick Frizzy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Thick Frizzy Hair

How to Blow Dry Thick Frizzy Hair

Blow drying can be a great way to tame frizz and achieve a sleek, smooth style. Here's what you'll need:

  • A blow dryer with a diffuser attachment
  • A wide-tooth comb or detangling brush
  • A heat protectant spray
  • A smoothing serum or oil

Before you start blow drying, make sure your hair is clean and towel-dried. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to help prevent damage from the heat of the blow dryer.

Step by Step Guide

Follow these steps to blow dry your thick frizzy hair:

  1. Attach the diffuser to your blow dryer.
  2. Use your wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to gently comb through your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  3. Divide your hair into sections, using clips or hair ties to keep them separated.
  4. Starting with the bottom section, use the diffuser to blow dry your hair on low heat and low speed. Hold the diffuser near your scalp and use circular motions to lift your hair up and away from your head.
  5. Continue blow drying each section of your hair on low heat and low speed, using the diffuser to lift your hair up and away from your head.
  6. When you've finished blow drying, apply a small amount of smoothing serum or oil to your hair to help tame any remaining frizz.

Tips for Blow Drying Thick Frizzy Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the best results when blow drying your thick frizzy hair:

  • Use a microfiber towel or a cotton t-shirt to gently squeeze out excess water before blow drying.
  • Don't use a brush to detangle your hair while blow drying, as this can cause breakage and damage.
  • If you have time, let your hair air dry for a few minutes before blow drying to reduce heat damage.
  • Apply a heat protectant spray liberally to your hair before blow drying.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to help distribute heat evenly and reduce frizz.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about blow drying thick frizzy hair:

  • Can blow drying cause damage to my hair? Yes, blow drying can cause damage if not done correctly. To minimize damage, use a heat protectant spray and a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer.
  • How often should I blow dry my hair? It's best to limit blow drying to once or twice a week to avoid excessive heat damage.
  • What's the best way to prevent frizz while blow drying? Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer and apply a smoothing serum or oil to your hair after blow drying.

Hair Care for Thick Frizzy Hair

Blow drying is just one part of a healthy hair care routine for thick frizzy hair. Here are some additional tips to keep your hair looking its best:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to help hydrate your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons or curling irons too often.
  • Get regular trims to help prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, like bleach or relaxers.
  • Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish your hair.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve sleek, smooth hair even if you have thick frizzy hair. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and always use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage from blow drying.
