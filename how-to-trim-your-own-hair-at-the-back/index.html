How To Trim Your Own Hair At The Back

Written by Frank Aug 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're tired of spending money and time at the salon just to get a simple trim, then learning how to trim your own hair at the back can be a game-changer. It may seem daunting at first, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking result at home. In this article, we'll guide you through the process, step by step, and provide tips and tricks to make it easier.

How to:

Before we begin, make sure you have the following tools ready:

  • Sharp scissors or hair clippers
  • A handheld mirror or a wall-mounted mirror
  • A comb
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A towel or a hair cape

Once you have all the tools, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and drying it completely. This will help you to see the natural shape and texture of your hair, which will make it easier to trim.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair Out Straight

Using a comb, brush your hair out straight and smooth. This will help you to see any split ends or unevenness that needs to be trimmed.

Step 3: Spray Your Hair with Water

Use the spray bottle to mist your hair with water. This will help to make your hair easier to cut and will also help to prevent any flyaways.

Step 4: Cut the Hair at the Back

Using the handheld mirror, stand with your back to the larger mirror and hold the smaller mirror in front of you. This will give you a clear view of the back of your head.

Take a small section of hair at the back and use the comb to smooth it out. Hold the scissors or hair clippers vertically and trim the hair in a straight line. Only cut a small amount of hair at a time, and keep checking your work in the mirror to make sure it's even.

Repeat this process for the rest of your hair at the back, working in small sections.

Tips:

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Use sharp scissors or hair clippers to make clean cuts.
  • Trim your hair when it's dry to make it easier to see any unevenness.
  • Work in a well-lit area to ensure you can see what you're doing clearly.
  • Take your time and work in small sections to avoid making any mistakes.

FAQ:

Here are some frequently asked questions about trimming hair at the back:

Q: How much hair should I cut at once?

A: It's best to cut a small amount of hair at a time, especially if you're new to trimming your own hair. This will help you to avoid making any mistakes and will also give you more control over the process.

Q: Can I use regular scissors to trim my hair?

A: While you can use regular scissors, it's best to use hair cutting scissors or hair clippers. These are designed specifically for cutting hair and will give you a cleaner, more professional-looking result.

Q: How often should I trim my hair?

A: It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends. However, the frequency of trimming may vary depending on your hair type and length.

Hair Care:

After trimming your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions by wearing a hat or using a protective spray.
  • Eat a balanced diet and stay hydrated to promote healthy hair growth.

Learning how to trim your own hair at the back can save you time and money, and with the right tools and techniques, it can be an easy and rewarding process. Follow these steps and tips to achieve a professional-looking result at home.
