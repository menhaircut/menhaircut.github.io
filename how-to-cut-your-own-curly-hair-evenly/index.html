How To Cut Your Own Curly Hair Evenly A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Sep 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of paying a fortune for haircuts? Do you have curly hair that needs regular trimming? Cutting your own hair can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and a little bit of know-how, you can achieve salon-worthy results from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own curly hair evenly, along with tips and tricks to make the process easier.

Table of Contents:

  • Tools You Will Need
  • Preparation
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Trimming Your Hair
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care

Tools You Will Need:

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Sectioning clips or elastics
  • Handheld mirror or stationary mirror

Preparation:

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry. Make sure you do not apply any styling products or oils, as this can make it harder to achieve an even cut. It's also a good idea to wear a shirt that you don't mind getting hair on.

Sectioning Your Hair:

Use your wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and part it in the middle. Then, use your comb to section off your hair into four equal parts: two sections in the front and two sections in the back. Secure each section with clips or elastics to keep them separate.

Cutting Your Hair:

Start with one of the front sections and take a small piece of hair from the bottom. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut off the desired length, making sure to cut straight across. Repeat this process with small sections of hair, gradually working your way up to the top of the section.

Once you have finished one front section, repeat the same process on the other side. Then, move on to the back sections, starting with the bottom and working your way up. It's important to take your time and cut small sections of hair to ensure an even cut.

Trimming Your Hair:

Once you have finished cutting your hair, it's time to trim any uneven pieces. Take your comb and scissors and carefully trim any sections that are longer than the rest. It's important to trim small sections at a time to avoid taking off too much hair.

Tips and Tricks:

  • Invest in a good pair of scissors specifically for cutting hair.
  • Make sure your hair is completely dry before cutting it.
  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Take breaks if you feel fatigued to avoid making mistakes.

FAQs:

  • Can I cut my curly hair wet or dry?
    It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be misleading and can result in an uneven cut.
  • How often should I cut my curly hair?
    It's recommended to trim curly hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.
  • What if I make a mistake?
    If you accidentally cut too much hair, don't panic. You can always trim a little bit more to even it out.

Hair Care:

After you have finished cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your curls hydrated, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Regular trims will also help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Cutting your own curly hair can be a bit nerve-wracking, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut at home. Remember to take your time, start with small sections of hair, and trim slowly to avoid any mistakes. Happy cutting!
