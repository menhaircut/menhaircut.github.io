Should You Layer Wavy Hair

Written by Bardi Jul 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Wavy hair can be a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it gives you a natural, effortless look that many people envy. On the other hand, it can be difficult to manage and style, especially if you have a lot of it. One way to make wavy hair more manageable is to layer it. But is layering right for your hair type? Let's take a closer look.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Layer Wavy Hair
  3. Step-by-Step Guide

Description

Layering wavy hair can add volume and movement to your locks, making it more manageable and easier to style. However, layering is not always the best option for everyone. If you have very fine hair or hair that is prone to frizz, layering may not be the best choice for you. It's important to consult with a stylist who can advise you on the best course of action for your hair type.

How to Layer Wavy Hair

If you've decided that layering is the right choice for your wavy hair, the first step is to find a stylist who is experienced in cutting wavy hair. Wavy hair has a tendency to bounce up when it's cut, so it's important to work with a stylist who knows how to control this. Once you've found a stylist you trust, you can start thinking about the type of layers you want.

Types of Layers

There are many different types of layers, and the type you choose will depend on your hair type and the look you want to achieve. Some common types of layers for wavy hair include:

  • Long layers
  • Short layers
  • Face-framing layers
  • Graduated layers

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're ready to take the plunge and layer your wavy hair, here's a step-by-step guide to get you started:

  1. Wash and dry your hair as you normally would.
  2. Comb your hair straight, and use hair clips to section off the areas you want to layer.
  3. Start with the bottom layer, and cut it to the desired length. Work your way up, layering each section as you go.
  4. Straighten your hair to check for any unevenness, and make any necessary adjustments.
  5. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Layering Wavy Hair

Layering wavy hair can be a tricky process, but these tips can help make it easier:

  • Work with a stylist who is experienced in cutting wavy hair.
  • Be clear about the type of layers you want.
  • Consider the thickness of your hair when deciding on the length of your layers.
  • Use hair clips to section off your hair, and work in small sections.
  • Straighten your hair after cutting to check for any unevenness.

FAQs

Here are some common questions people have about layering wavy hair:

Q: Will layering my hair make it frizzy?

A: If you have hair that is prone to frizz, layering may not be the best choice for you. However, working with a stylist who is experienced in cutting wavy hair can help minimize frizz.

Q: Will layering my hair make it harder to style?

A: Layering can actually make your hair easier to style, as it adds volume and movement to your locks.

Q: Will layering my hair make it look thinner?

A: Layering can make your hair look thinner if you have very fine hair. However, if you have thick hair, layering can actually make it look fuller.

Hair Care for Layered Wavy Hair

Once you've layered your wavy hair, it's important to take proper care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid heat styling your hair too often, as this can damage your locks.
  • Invest in a good quality hair mask to use once a week to keep your hair nourished.
  • Consider using a diffuser when blow drying your hair to avoid frizz.

So, should you layer wavy hair? The answer is, it depends. If you have the right hair type and work with an experienced stylist, layering can be a great way to add volume and movement to your locks. However, it's important to consider your hair type and consult with a professional before making any drastic changes to your hair.
