How Can A Man Cut His Own Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 09, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're a man looking to save some money and take control of your hairstyle, learning how to cut your own hair is a great option. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own hair, along with some tips and tricks for getting the best results.

Tools You'll Need

Before you begin cutting your hair, you'll need to gather some essential tools:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Clip or elastic band
  • Mirror
  • Cape or towel to cover your shoulders
  • Water spray bottle
  • Optional: Haircutting cape or apron, hair thinning shears, hair texturizing scissors

Make sure your clippers are fully charged or plugged in and that your scissors are sharp.

Preparing Your Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to have clean, dry hair. Wash and dry your hair as you normally would, and comb it out to remove any tangles. If you have longer hair, consider trimming any split ends before you begin the actual haircut.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair will make it easier to cut and ensure that you don't miss any spots. Start by parting your hair down the middle, from forehead to nape. Then, section off each side by clipping the hair above your ears. Finally, clip the hair at the back of your head up and out of the way.

Cutting Your Hair

Start with the longest guard on your clippers and work your way down to a shorter guard, if desired. Begin at the bottom of your hairline, and work your way up the back of your head, using the clippers to create an even length. Move to the sides, using the same technique, and then to the top of your head.

When working on the top of your head, use scissors to create texture and blend the hair. Hold a small section of hair between your fingers, and use the scissors to make small, upward cuts into the hair. Be careful not to cut too much at once, as this can lead to unevenness.

Blending Your Hair

Once you've cut the length of your hair, it's time to blend the different sections together. Use the scissors to cut at a slight angle, starting at the shortest length and gradually blending into the longer lengths. Take your time and work in small sections to ensure a smooth blend.

Trimming Your Hairline and Neck

Use clippers or scissors to trim your hairline and neck. Start at the center of your neck and work your way outwards, using the clippers to create a straight line. Use scissors to trim any longer hairs that the clippers missed.

Cleaning Up

Once you're done cutting your hair, it's time to clean up any stray hairs. Use a brush or vacuum to remove any hair from your clothes and the floor. If you used a cape or towel, shake it out outside to remove any hair.

Tips for a Great DIY Haircut

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use a spray bottle to dampen your hair if it's too dry or difficult to cut.
  • Take breaks and step back from the mirror to avoid cutting too much or creating unevenness.
  • Be patient and take your time; rushing can lead to mistakes.
  • If you're unsure of a certain technique, watch tutorial videos or ask for help from a friend or family member.

FAQs

How often should I cut my own hair?

It depends on your hair type and desired style. Generally, men with shorter haircuts should cut their hair every 2-4 weeks to maintain the length and shape. If you have longer hair, you can go up to 6-8 weeks between haircuts.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

No, regular scissors aren't designed for cutting hair and can lead to unevenness or damage. Use hair-cutting scissors, which are sharper and have a different blade angle.

What should I do if I make a mistake?

If you accidentally cut too much or create an uneven spot, don't panic. Try to blend the surrounding hair to create a smoother transition. If necessary, you can always go to a professional stylist to fix any mistakes.

Hair Care After Your DIY Haircut

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it with the right products and techniques. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type, and avoid over-washing or using too much heat on your hair. Regular trims can also help maintain your desired style and prevent split ends.

Learning how to cut your own hair can be a great way to save money and take control of your style. With the right tools, techniques, and patience, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home.
