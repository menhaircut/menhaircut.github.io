How To Use Rollers For Side Swept Bangs

Written by Robby Jun 17, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking for a quick and easy way to style your side swept bangs, rollers are a great option. In this article, we'll go through the steps on how to use rollers for side swept bangs, as well as some tips, FAQ, and hair care advice to keep your bangs looking great.

Table of Contents:

  • How to use rollers for side swept bangs
  • Step by step guide
  • Tips for styling side swept bangs with rollers
  • FAQ
  • Hair care tips for side swept bangs

How to Use Rollers for Side Swept Bangs

Using rollers to style your side swept bangs is simple and straightforward. Here's what you need to do:

Step 1: Prep your hair

Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is naturally curly or wavy, you may want to straighten it using a flat iron before you start. Brush your hair to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Choose your rollers

Choose the right size rollers for your hair. For shorter hair, smaller rollers work better, while longer hair needs larger rollers. If you're not sure which size to use, start with medium-sized rollers.

Step 3: Section your hair

Section your hair into two parts: the top section and the bottom section. Clip the top section of your hair out of the way for now.

Step 4: Roll your bangs

Take a small section of hair from your bangs and place it on the roller. Roll the roller up towards your scalp, making sure to wrap the hair tightly around the roller. Secure the roller in place using a clip or a bobby pin.

Step 5: Repeat on the other side

Repeat the same process on the other side of your head, making sure to roll your bangs in the same direction.

Step 6: Wait for the rollers to cool

Leave the rollers in your hair for at least 20 minutes, or until they've cooled down completely. This will help set the curl and keep your bangs in place.

Step 7: Remove the rollers

Remove the rollers one by one, starting with the one at the bottom. Gently unroll the hair from the roller and let it fall naturally.

Step 8: Style your bangs

Use your fingers to gently separate the curls and create a natural-looking wave. Sweep your bangs to one side and use a bit of hairspray to keep them in place.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step by step guide to help you use rollers for your side swept bangs:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Choose the right size rollers for your hair.
  3. Section your hair into two parts, top and bottom.
  4. Roll your bangs up towards your scalp and secure the rollers in place with clips or bobby pins.
  5. Leave the rollers in your hair for at least 20 minutes to set the curl.
  6. Remove the rollers one by one and style your bangs as desired.

Tips for Styling Side Swept Bangs with Rollers

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when using rollers for your side swept bangs:

  • Use rollers that are the right size for your hair. Too small or too large rollers won't create the desired effect.
  • Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start rolling it. Wet hair won't hold the curl well.
  • Wrap your hair tightly around the roller to create a more defined curl.
  • Leave the rollers in your hair for at least 20 minutes to set the curl.
  • Use a bit of hairspray to keep your bangs in place.

FAQ

Q: Can I use rollers on wet hair?

A: No, it's best to use rollers on dry hair. Wet hair won't hold the curl well and can take longer to dry.

Q: How long should I leave the rollers in my hair?

A: Leave the rollers in your hair for at least 20 minutes, or until they've cooled down completely. This will help set the curl and keep your bangs in place.

Q: Can I use rollers on short hair?

A: Yes, you can use rollers on short hair. Choose smaller rollers to create a more defined curl.

Hair Care Tips for Side Swept Bangs

To keep your side swept bangs looking great, here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools like flat irons or curling irons.
  • Trim your bangs regularly to keep them looking neat and tidy.
  • Use a hair serum or oil to add shine and moisture to your hair.
  • Avoid using too much hairspray or other styling products, as they can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

By following these tips and using rollers to style your side swept bangs, you can achieve a natural-looking wave that's easy to maintain.
