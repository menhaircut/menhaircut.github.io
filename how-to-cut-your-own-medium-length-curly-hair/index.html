How To Cut Your Own Medium Length Curly Hair

Written by Frank Aug 26, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of going to the salon every few months to get your curls trimmed? With the right tools and techniques, you can cut your own medium length curly hair at home. In this article, we’ll cover everything you need to know to give yourself a salon-worthy haircut.

Description

If you have medium length curly hair, you know that finding the right stylist can be a challenge. And even when you do find someone you like, frequent trips to the salon can be time-consuming and expensive. By learning how to cut your own hair, you can save money and have more control over your look. Plus, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut without leaving your home.

How to

Before you start cutting your hair, there are a few things you’ll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Hair clips or elastics
  • Handheld mirror or two mirrors, one in front and one in back
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Once you have these tools, follow these steps:

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Wash and dry your hair as you normally would. It’s important to cut your hair when it’s dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.

Step 2: Section your hair

Using your wide-tooth comb, divide your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Clip or tie each section separately.

Step 3: Cut the back sections

Start with one of the back sections. Unclip or untie it and use your comb to separate a small section of hair. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut off the desired length, using your other hand to guide the scissors. Continue this process, working your way around the back of your head.

Step 4: Cut the front sections

Move on to the front sections, following the same process. For the front sections, you may want to use your handheld mirror or two mirrors to ensure that you’re cutting your hair evenly on both sides.

Step 5: Check for balance

Once you’ve finished cutting, use your mirrors to check that your hair is even on both sides.

Step 6: Make any necessary adjustments

If you notice any uneven spots, make small adjustments until your hair looks balanced.

Step 7: Style as desired

Now that your hair is cut, you can style it as you normally would. If you’re not happy with the results, don’t worry. Hair grows back, and you can always try again in a few weeks.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts. It’s easier to take off more hair than it is to add it back.
  • Don’t cut too much at once. It’s better to make multiple small cuts than one big one.
  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. Dull scissors can cause split ends and frizz.
  • Take your time. Cutting your own hair can be nerve-wracking, but rushing can lead to mistakes.

FAQ

  • Can I cut my hair when it’s wet?

    • We recommend cutting your hair when it’s dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.

  • How often should I cut my hair?

    • This depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, we recommend trimming your hair every 6-8 weeks.

  • What if I make a mistake?

    • Hair grows back, so don’t panic if you make a mistake. You can always try again in a few weeks, or visit a professional stylist to fix any major mistakes.

Hair Care

Cutting your own hair is just one step in maintaining healthy, beautiful curls. Here are some tips for keeping your hair in top shape:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to damp hair to help define your curls.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you can cut your own medium length curly hair with confidence. Remember to take your time, start with small cuts, and invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. Happy cutting!
