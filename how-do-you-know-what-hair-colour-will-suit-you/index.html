How Do You Know What Hair Colour Will Suit You

Written by Aprilia Aug 15, 2023 · 5 min read

Choosing a new hair colour can be exciting, but also a little daunting. With so many options available, it can be difficult to know where to start. In this article, we’ll guide you through the process of choosing a hair colour that will suit you, and help you feel confident and happy with your new look!

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Choosing the right hair colour for you is about more than just picking a shade that you like. Your hair colour should complement your skin tone, eye colour, and natural hair colour. If you choose a colour that clashes with your natural features, you may end up feeling self-conscious or unhappy with your new look.

However, if you choose a hair colour that complements your natural features, you'll look and feel great. You'll also find that your new hair colour helps to bring out the best in your skin tone and eye colour, making you look more radiant and confident!

How To

So, how do you know what hair colour will suit you? The first step is to determine your skin tone.

Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone

There are three main types of skin tone: cool, warm, and neutral. To determine which skin tone you have, look at the veins on the inside of your wrist. If they appear blue or purple, you have a cool skin tone. If they appear green, you have a warm skin tone. If they appear blue-green, you have a neutral skin tone.

Cool skin tone: If you have a cool skin tone, you'll look best in hair colours that have cool undertones, such as ash blonde, cool brown, or burgundy.

Warm skin tone: If you have a warm skin tone, you'll look best in hair colours that have warm undertones, such as golden blonde, warm brown, or copper.

Neutral skin tone: If you have a neutral skin tone, you can wear both warm and cool hair colours.

Step 2: Consider Your Eye Colour

Your eye colour can also help you determine which hair colour will suit you best. Consider the following:

Blue eyes: If you have blue eyes, you'll look best in hair colours that have warm undertones, such as golden blonde or copper.

Green eyes: If you have green eyes, you'll look best in hair colours that have cool undertones, such as ash blonde or cool brown.

Brown eyes: If you have brown eyes, you can wear both warm and cool hair colours.

Step 3: Determine Your Natural Hair Colour

Your natural hair colour can also help you choose a hair colour that will suit you. If you have dark hair, you may want to consider a colour that is only a few shades lighter. If you have light hair, you may want to consider a colour that is a few shades darker.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide to choosing a hair colour that will suit you:

  1. Determine your skin tone
  2. Consider your eye colour
  3. Determine your natural hair colour
  4. Choose a hair colour that complements your natural features
  5. Consider the maintenance required for that hair colour
  6. Consult with a hairstylist if you're unsure
  7. Do a patch test before applying the hair colour to your entire head
  8. Apply the hair colour according to the instructions on the box
  9. Follow up with a hair care routine that is tailored to your new hair colour
  10. Enjoy your new look!

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing a hair colour:

  • Consider your lifestyle and how much time you have to maintain your new hair colour
  • Choose a hair colour that matches your personality and style
  • Take inspiration from celebrities or social media influencers, but remember that their hair colour may not be suitable for you
  • Consider the season and how your hair colour will look with certain outfits
  • Be prepared for some trial and error - it may take a few tries to find the perfect hair colour for you!

FAQ

Can I dye my hair if I have sensitive skin?

If you have sensitive skin, it's important to choose a hair dye that is gentle and non-irritating. Look for hair dye brands that are specifically formulated for sensitive skin, and do a patch test before applying the dye to your entire head.

Can I dye my hair if I have grey hair?

Yes, you can dye your hair if you have grey hair. In fact, many people who have grey hair choose to dye their hair to cover up the grey. Choose a hair dye that is specifically formulated for grey hair, and follow the instructions carefully.

How often should I touch up my hair colour?

The frequency with which you touch up your hair colour depends on the type of hair colour you have and how quickly your hair grows. Generally, permanent hair colour should be touched up every 4-6 weeks, while semi-permanent hair colour can be touched up every 2-3 weeks.

Hair Care

Once you've chosen a hair colour that suits you, it's important to follow a hair care routine that is tailored to your new colour. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that is specifically formulated for coloured hair
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can cause your hair colour to fade
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as this can also cause your hair colour to fade
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and vibrant
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors that can cause damage and fading

By following these tips, you can keep your hair looking great and feeling healthy, long after you've chosen a new hair colour!
