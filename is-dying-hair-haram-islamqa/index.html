Is Dying Hair Haram Islamqa Understanding Hair Dyeing In Islam

Written by William Nov 26, 2023 · 4 min read

As Muslims, we strive to follow the teachings of Islam in every aspect of our lives. This includes our personal appearance, which is why the question of whether dying hair is haram or halal often arises. To provide clarity on this topic, we turn to Islamqa, a reliable source of Islamic knowledge and guidance. In this article, we will explore their views on hair dyeing and provide tips on how to care for dyed hair.

What is Islamqa?

Islamqa is a website that provides Islamic knowledge and guidance based on the Qur'an and Sunnah. It is run by a group of scholars who answer questions related to Islamic beliefs, practices, and lifestyle. The website is in Arabic, but it has an English version for non-Arabic speakers.

Is Dying Hair Haram in Islam?

According to Islamqa, dying hair with black color is haram because it involves changing the creation of Allah. However, using other colors that are not black is permissible as long as it does not cause harm to the person or affect their health. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) allowed the use of henna, which is a natural dye that gives a red or brown color to the hair.

How to Dye Hair Halal Way?

If you want to dye your hair, it is important to choose a halal hair dye that does not contain haram ingredients such as alcohol or animal-derived substances. You can search for halal hair dye brands that are available in your country or ask for recommendations from other Muslims. It is also recommended to consult with a trusted scholar or imam to ensure that the dyeing process is done according to Islamic guidelines.

Step-by-Step Guide to Dyeing Hair Halal Way

Here are the steps to dye your hair in a halal way:

Step 1: Choose a Halal Hair Dye

Choose a hair dye that is halal-certified or does not contain haram ingredients. Read the label carefully and research the brand to make sure it is suitable for Muslims.

Step 2: Do a Patch Test

Before applying the dye to your hair, do a patch test on a small area of your skin to check for any allergic reactions or irritation.

Step 3: Prepare the Dye

Follow the instructions on the hair dye package to prepare the dye. Wear gloves to avoid staining your hands.

Step 4: Apply the Dye to Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections and apply the dye evenly from roots to tips. Use a comb to distribute the dye evenly and avoid clumps or patches.

Step 5: Wait for the Recommended Time

Wait for the recommended time on the hair dye package before rinsing it off. Do not leave the dye on your hair for too long as it can damage your hair or scalp.

Step 6: Rinse Your Hair with Water

Rinse your hair with water until the water runs clear. Do not use shampoo or conditioner immediately after dyeing your hair as it can fade the color.

Tips for Caring for Dyed Hair

Dyed hair requires extra care to maintain its color and health. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid fading the color.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair as it can strip off the natural oils and color.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and avoid pulling or breaking it.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to direct sunlight or chlorine as it can fade the color.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q: Is it haram to dye hair with other colors besides black?

A: No, it is not haram to dye hair with other colors as long as it does not cause harm to the person or affect their health.

Q: Can I dye my hair with henna?

A: Yes, henna is a natural dye that is permissible in Islam. It gives a red or brown color to the hair and has many benefits for the hair and scalp.

Q: How often can I dye my hair?

A: It is recommended to wait at least 4-6 weeks before dyeing your hair again to avoid damaging your hair or scalp.

Conclusion

In conclusion, dying hair with black color is haram in Islam, but using other colors is permissible as long as it does not cause harm or affect health. It is important to choose a halal hair dye and follow the guidelines of Islamqa to ensure that the dyeing process is done according to Islamic rules. Additionally, caring for dyed hair requires extra attention and maintenance to maintain its color and health.
