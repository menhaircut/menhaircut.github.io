How Do I Fix My Medium Length Hair Tips Steps And Hair Care To Help You

Written by Frank Jul 08, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you looking for ways to fix your medium length hair? You're not alone. Many people with mid-length hair struggle to find hairstyles that suit their hair type and face shape. Whether you have straight or curly hair, there are many ways to style your medium length hair without spending hours in front of the mirror. In this article, we'll share tips, steps, and hair care advice to help you fix your medium length hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Fix Your Medium Length Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Fixing Your Medium Length Hair
  • Tips for Fixing Your Medium Length Hair
  • FAQ about Fixing Medium Length Hair
  • Hair Care Tips for Medium Length Hair

How to Fix Your Medium Length Hair

Before we dive into the steps to fix your medium length hair, it's important to know what type of hair you have. There are different types of hair, such as straight, curly, wavy, thick, and thin. Knowing your hair type will help you choose the right products and techniques to fix your hair.

Once you know your hair type, you can follow these general steps to fix your medium length hair:

  1. Start with clean and dry hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  2. Apply a hair serum or oil to protect your hair from heat damage and add shine.
  3. Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools, such as a flat iron or curling wand.
  4. Choose a hairstyle that suits your face shape and hair type. You can try a half-up, half-down hairstyle, a messy bun, a braided hairstyle, or a sleek ponytail.
  5. Use hair accessories, such as hair clips, headbands, or scrunchies, to add some flair to your hairstyle.
  6. Finish with a hairspray or hair oil to hold your hairstyle in place and add shine.

These steps are just a general guide, and you can adjust them based on your hair type and personal preferences.

Step-by-Step Guide to Fixing Your Medium Length Hair

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide to help you fix your medium length hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using hot water, as it can damage your hair and make it frizzy. Rinse your hair thoroughly with lukewarm water and pat it dry with a towel. Avoid rubbing your hair with the towel, as it can cause breakage.

Step 2: Apply a Hair Serum or Oil

Apply a hair serum or oil to protect your hair from heat damage and add shine. Use a pea-sized amount for medium length hair and distribute it evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends. Avoid applying it to your scalp, as it can make your hair look greasy.

Step 3: Use a Heat Protectant Spray

Before using any heat styling tools, such as a flat iron or curling wand, use a heat protectant spray to prevent heat damage. Spray it evenly throughout your hair and comb it through to distribute it evenly.

Step 4: Choose a Hairstyle

Choose a hairstyle that suits your face shape and hair type. For example, if you have curly hair, you can try a messy bun or a braided hairstyle. If you have straight hair, you can try a sleek ponytail or a half-up, half-down hairstyle. Use hair accessories, such as hair clips, headbands, or scrunchies, to add some flair to your hairstyle.

Step 5: Finish with Hairspray or Hair Oil

Finish your hairstyle with a hairspray or hair oil to hold it in place and add shine. Use a light hold hairspray for a natural look or a strong hold hairspray for a more defined look. Apply a small amount of hair oil to the ends of your hair to add shine and prevent frizz.

Tips for Fixing Your Medium Length Hair

Here are some tips to help you fix your medium length hair:

  • Use a boar bristle brush to distribute the natural oils in your hair and add shine.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can cause damage and breakage.
  • Get regular trims to remove split ends and keep your hair healthy.
  • Try a hair mask once a week to moisturize and nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using too many styling products, as it can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

FAQ about Fixing Medium Length Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing medium length hair:

Q: How often should I wash my medium length hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it every other day. If you have dry hair, you can wash it once or twice a week. If you exercise regularly or sweat a lot, you may need to wash your hair more often.

Q: Can I use a hair straightener on my medium length hair?

A: Yes, you can use a hair straightener on your medium length hair, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid using too much heat. Use a lower heat setting and run the straightener through your hair slowly to avoid damaging your hair.

Q: How can I add volume to my medium length hair?

A: You can add volume to your medium length hair by blow-drying it upside down, using a volumizing mousse or spray, or teasing your hair at the roots. Be sure to use a light hold hairspray to hold the volume in place.

Hair Care Tips for Medium Length Hair

Here are some hair care tips to keep your medium length hair healthy and shiny:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot water, as it can damage your hair and make it frizzy.
  • Apply a hair serum or oil to protect your hair from heat damage and add shine.
  • Get regular trims to remove split ends and keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can cause damage and breakage.
  • Treat your hair to a hair mask once a week to moisturize and nourish it.

With these tips, steps, and hair care advice, you can fix your medium length hair and have a healthy, shiny, and stylish hairstyle.
