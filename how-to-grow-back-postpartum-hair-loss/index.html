How To Grow Back Postpartum Hair Loss Tips And Tricks

Written by Aprilia Aug 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Postpartum hair loss is a common phenomenon that affects many women after giving birth. During pregnancy, women often experience thick, luscious hair due to hormonal changes. However, after delivery, the hormone levels return to normal, and the hair may start falling out in large amounts. This can be a distressing experience for new moms, but don't worry! There are steps you can take to promote hair growth and regain your luscious locks. In this article, we will discuss how to grow back postpartum hair loss.

How to Grow Back Postpartum Hair Loss

1. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet is essential for hair growth. Your hair requires specific nutrients like vitamins A, C, D, E, B vitamins, biotin, iron, zinc, and protein to grow. Make sure to include foods like eggs, lean meats, fish, nuts, seeds, and leafy greens in your diet. Avoid processed and fast foods as they can be detrimental to hair growth.

2. Take Supplements

If you can't get all the nutrients you need from your diet, consider taking supplements. Biotin, iron, and vitamin D supplements can help promote hair growth. However, consult your doctor before taking any supplements to avoid any adverse effects.

3. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help stimulate hair growth by increasing blood flow to hair follicles. Use your fingers to massage your scalp gently for a few minutes every day. You can also use essential oils like lavender, rosemary, and peppermint to promote hair growth.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers can damage your hair and slow down hair growth. Avoid using them as much as possible, and if you must use them, use a heat protectant spray.

5. Don't Tie Your Hair Too Tight

Tying your hair too tight can pull on your hair and cause breakage. Avoid tight hairstyles like ponytails, braids, and buns. Opt for looser hairstyles like a messy bun or a loose braid instead.

6. Get Enough Sleep

Sleep is essential for hair growth. Your hair grows during the resting phase, which is when you're asleep. Make sure to get at least seven to eight hours of sleep every night to promote hair growth.

7. Manage Stress

Stress can cause hair loss and slow down hair growth. Practice stress-reducing techniques like meditation, yoga, or deep breathing exercises to manage stress.

8. Be Patient

Regrowing your hair after postpartum hair loss takes time. It may take up to a year or more for your hair to grow back fully. Be patient, and don't get discouraged if you don't see results right away.

FAQ

1. Can postpartum hair loss be prevented?

Postpartum hair loss cannot be prevented, but you can take steps to minimize it.

2. How long does postpartum hair loss last?

Postpartum hair loss usually lasts for six months to a year.

3. Can breastfeeding cause postpartum hair loss?

Yes, breastfeeding can cause postpartum hair loss due to hormonal changes.

Hair Care Tips

In addition to the above tips, taking care of your hair can promote hair growth. Here are some hair care tips:

1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner.

Avoid shampoos and conditioners that contain sulfates and parabens, as they can be harsh on your hair. Opt for gentle, natural products instead.

2. Don't wash your hair too often.

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, causing dryness and breakage. Aim to wash your hair every two to three days.

3. Use a wide-toothed comb.

Avoid using brushes and fine-toothed combs, as they can cause breakage. Use a wide-toothed comb instead, starting from the ends and working your way up to avoid pulling on your hair.

4. Protect your hair from the sun.

UV rays can damage your hair and slow down hair growth. Wear a hat or use a UV protectant spray to protect your hair from the sun.

5. Trim your hair regularly.

Trimming your hair every six to eight weeks can help prevent split ends and breakage, promoting hair growth.

In conclusion, postpartum hair loss can be distressing, but there are steps you can take to promote hair growth. Eating a balanced diet, taking supplements, massaging your scalp, avoiding heat styling, and managing stress can all help promote hair growth. Don't forget to take care of your hair by using gentle products, not washing it too often, using a wide-toothed comb, protecting it from the sun, and trimming it regularly. Be patient, and don't get discouraged if you don't see results right away. With time, your hair will grow back, and you'll have your luscious locks back in no time!
