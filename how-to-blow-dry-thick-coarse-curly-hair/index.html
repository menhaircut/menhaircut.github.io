How To Blow Dry Thick Coarse Curly Hair

Written by Eveline Jun 21, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have thick, coarse, curly hair, you know how difficult it can be to manage. Blow drying your hair can be especially challenging, but with the right technique and tools, you can achieve a beautiful, sleek look. In this article, we’ll show you how to blow dry thick coarse curly hair in a way that’s easy to understand and follow.

Description

Blow drying thick coarse curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a sleek and beautiful look.

How to Blow Dry Thick Coarse Curly Hair: Step by Step

Step 1: Start with clean, damp hair

The first step in blow drying your hair is to start with clean, damp hair. Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to help tame frizz and add moisture.

Step 2: Apply a heat protectant

Before blow drying your hair, it’s important to protect it from heat damage. Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair, focusing on the ends.

Step 3: Section your hair

To make blow drying easier, section your hair into manageable sections. Use clips to keep each section in place.

Step 4: Use a round brush

Use a round brush to help straighten and smooth your hair. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends, using the brush to pull your hair taut as you blow dry.

Step 5: Use a diffuser attachment

If you have very curly hair, use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer. This will help to distribute heat more evenly, preventing frizz and preserving your curls.

Step 6: Finish with a cold blast

Once you’ve finished blow drying your hair, finish with a blast of cold air. This will help to seal the cuticle and add shine.

Tips for Blow Drying Thick Coarse Curly Hair

Blow drying thick coarse curly hair can be challenging, but these tips will help you achieve a beautiful and sleek look:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to help tame frizz and add moisture to your hair.
  • Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair before blow drying to prevent heat damage.
  • Section your hair into manageable sections to make blow drying easier.
  • Use a round brush to help straighten and smooth your hair.
  • If you have very curly hair, use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to prevent frizz and preserve your curls.
  • Finish with a blast of cold air to seal the cuticle and add shine.

FAQ

Q: How often should I blow dry my hair?

A: It’s best to limit blow drying to once or twice a week to prevent heat damage.

Q: What type of brush should I use?

A: Use a round brush to help straighten and smooth your hair.

Q: Can I blow dry my hair without a heat protectant?

A: It’s not recommended. Using a heat protectant spray or serum will help prevent heat damage and keep your hair healthy.

Hair Care for Thick Coarse Curly Hair

When it comes to caring for thick coarse curly hair, here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to help tame frizz and add moisture to your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often.
  • Limit washing your hair to once or twice a week to prevent stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

With these tips, you can keep your hair healthy and beautiful, even if it’s thick, coarse, and curly.
