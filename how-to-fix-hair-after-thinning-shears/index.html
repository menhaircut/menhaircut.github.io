How To Fix Hair After Thinning Shears A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning shears are a popular tool used by hairstylists to create texture and remove bulk from hair. However, if not used properly, they can cause damage to your hair, leaving it looking thin and patchy. If you've fallen victim to thinning shears, don't worry! In this guide, we'll show you how to fix your hair and get it looking healthy and full again.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Fix Hair after Thinning Shears
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Preventing Thinning Shear Damage
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care After Thinning Shears

Description

Thinning shears are scissors that have teeth or notches on one blade. When used on hair, they remove some of the bulk and create texture. While thinning shears can be a useful tool in your hairstyling arsenal, overuse or improper use can cause damage to your hair. Hair that has been cut with thinning shears may look patchy, uneven, and even thin.

How to Fix Hair after Thinning Shears

The good news is that you can fix hair that has been damaged by thinning shears. The best way to do this is by getting a haircut. A professional stylist can help you trim away the damaged parts of your hair and create a style that looks healthy and full.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to fixing hair that has been damaged by thinning shears:

  1. Find a stylist who has experience working with damaged hair. You want someone who can help you create a style that will look natural and healthy.
  2. Explain to the stylist what happened to your hair and what you want to achieve. Be honest about the damage and any concerns you have about your hair.
  3. The stylist will assess your hair and determine the best course of action. This may involve cutting away the damaged parts of your hair and creating a new style.
  4. Be patient. It may take some time for your hair to grow out and look healthy again. In the meantime, take good care of your hair and avoid any further damage.

Tips for Preventing Thinning Shear Damage

The best way to prevent thinning shear damage is to avoid using them altogether. However, if you do use them, here are some tips to minimize damage:

  • Use thinning shears sparingly. Don't go overboard and remove too much hair at once.
  • Don't use thinning shears on wet or damp hair. This can cause the hair to stretch and break.
  • Use sharp shears. Dull shears can cause damage to the hair.
  • Work with a stylist you trust. A good stylist will know how to use thinning shears properly and minimize damage.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing hair after thinning shears:

Can hair damaged by thinning shears be fixed?

Yes, hair that has been damaged by thinning shears can be fixed with the help of a professional stylist.

How long does it take for hair to grow back after thinning shears?

It depends on the extent of the damage. Hair typically grows about half an inch per month, so it may take several months for your hair to grow out and look healthy again.

Can thinning shears cause hair loss?

Thinning shears can cause hair loss if not used properly. Overuse or improper use can damage the hair follicles and lead to hair loss.

Hair Care After Thinning Shears

After getting a haircut to fix your thinning-shear-damaged hair, it's important to take good care of your hair to prevent further damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, which can cause further damage to your hair.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, which can cause breakage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote healthy hair growth.

Now that you know how to fix hair after thinning shears, you can take action to get your hair looking healthy and full again. Remember to be patient and take good care of your hair to prevent further damage.
