In Islam, there are certain rules and traditions that need to be followed, and cutting a baby's hair is no exception. In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about when to cut newborn hair in Islam.

When to Cut Newborn Hair in Islam

In Islam, it is recommended to cut a baby's hair on the seventh day after their birth. This is known as the 'aqiqah and is a tradition that dates back to the time of the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him). The 'aqiqah is a celebration to welcome the baby into the world and involves sacrificing an animal and distributing the meat to family, friends, and the poor.

During the 'aqiqah, it is customary to shave the baby's head and donate the weight of their hair in silver to charity. This is known as 'taqsir' and is a way of showing gratitude to God for the baby's safe arrival. It is also believed to bring blessings to the child and protect them from harm.

However, if the 'aqiqah is not performed on the seventh day, the baby's hair can be cut at any time. There is no specific age or time frame for when the hair has to be cut, but it is recommended to do it before the child reaches puberty.

How to Cut Newborn Hair in Islam

When it comes to cutting a baby's hair in Islam, there are a few guidelines that need to be followed. Firstly, it is recommended to use a sharp object such as a razor or scissors to cut the hair. This is to ensure that the hair is cut cleanly and evenly.

Secondly, it is important to recite the 'adhan' (call to prayer) in the baby's right ear and the 'iqamah' (second call to prayer) in their left ear before cutting their hair. This is believed to protect the child from harm and bring blessings to their life.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Newborn Hair in Islam

Here is a step-by-step guide to cutting a baby's hair in Islam:

  1. Perform the 'adhan' in the baby's right ear and the 'iqamah' in their left ear. This can be done by a family member or a religious leader.
  2. Use a sharp object such as a razor or scissors to cut the hair. Make sure the hair is cut cleanly and evenly.
  3. Weigh the hair and donate the equivalent weight in silver to charity.
  4. You can also perform the 'aqiqah' ceremony at this time if it hasn't been done already.

Tips for Cutting Newborn Hair in Islam

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting a baby's hair in Islam:

  • Make sure to use a sharp object to cut the hair. This will ensure a clean and even cut.
  • Recite the 'adhan' and 'iqamah' before cutting the hair to bring blessings and protection to the child.
  • Weigh the hair and donate the equivalent weight in silver to charity.
  • You can invite family and friends to the 'aqiqah' ceremony to celebrate the baby's arrival and distribute the meat to the poor.

FAQs about Cutting Newborn Hair in Islam

Is it necessary to cut a baby's hair in Islam?

No, it is not necessary to cut a baby's hair in Islam. However, it is recommended to do so on the seventh day after their birth as part of the 'aqiqah' ceremony.

Can a baby's hair be cut before the seventh day?

Yes, a baby's hair can be cut at any time, but it is recommended to do it on the seventh day as part of the 'aqiqah' ceremony.

What is the purpose of cutting a baby's hair in Islam?

The purpose of cutting a baby's hair in Islam is to show gratitude to God for the baby's safe arrival, bring blessings to their life, and protect them from harm.

Hair Care for Newborns in Islam

After cutting a baby's hair in Islam, it is important to take care of their hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash the baby's hair with mild shampoo and water.
  • Use a soft brush or comb to detangle the hair.
  • Avoid using hair products such as gel or hairspray on the baby's hair.
  • Keep the baby's hair covered with a hat or bonnet when going outside to protect it from the sun and wind.

Cutting a baby's hair in Islam is an important tradition that has been practiced for centuries. By following these guidelines and tips, you can ensure that the process is done correctly and the baby's hair is taken care of properly.
