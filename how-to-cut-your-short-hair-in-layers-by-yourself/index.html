How To Cut Your Short Hair In Layers By Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 03, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're looking to switch up your short haircut and add some layers, but don't want to spend money at a salon, you're in luck! With the right tools and techniques, you can easily cut your own hair at home. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut your short hair in layers by yourself, so you can achieve a fresh new look without leaving your house.

  • How to Cut Your Short Hair in Layers
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Cut Your Short Hair in Layers

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's totally doable. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A fine-tooth comb
  • Clips to section your hair

Once you've gathered your tools, it's time to get started!

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your short hair in layers:

  1. Wash and dry your hair. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that your hair is clean and free of any styling products that could weigh it down.
  2. Section your hair. Use clips to divide your hair into four sections: one at the crown of your head, one on each side, and one at the nape of your neck.
  3. Start with the bottom section. Take a small section of hair from the back of your neck and comb it straight down. Hold the ends between your fingers and cut straight across. This will be your guide for the rest of your layers.
  4. Work your way up. Take a small section of hair from the next layer up and hold it between your fingers. Use your first cut as a guide and cut the hair straight across at the same length.
  5. Continue layering. Move up to the next section of hair and repeat the process until you've cut all of your layers.
  6. Check for balance. Once you've finished cutting, comb your hair straight down and check for any uneven spots. Make small adjustments as needed.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair. It's easier to control and less likely that you'll make a mistake.
  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to make small adjustments than to cut too much and have to start over.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to cut.
  • Take your time. Cutting your own hair can be nerve-wracking, but rushing can lead to mistakes.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair:

  • Can I cut my hair wet or dry?
  • Can I cut my hair with regular scissors?
  • How often should I cut my hair?
  • What if I make a mistake?

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain your layers and prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to cut your short hair in layers by yourself, you can experiment with different styles and save money on salon visits. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow the steps carefully for the best results.
