How To Cut Side Bangs By Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Oct 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a way to switch up your hairstyle without making a drastic change, cutting side bangs can be a great option. Not only are they trendy and versatile, but they can also help frame your face and accentuate your features. The best part? You don't need to book an appointment at the salon - you can easily cut side bangs by yourself at home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process step by step.

Description

Side bangs are a type of fringe that are swept to one side, rather than being cut straight across. They can be any length, from short and choppy to long and wispy. Side bangs can complement a variety of face shapes and hair textures, making them a popular choice for many people. Cutting side bangs by yourself can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable.

How to Cut Side Bangs by Yourself

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, you'll need to gather a few supplies. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A fine-toothed comb
  • Clips or hair ties to section off your hair
  • A mirror

Step 2: Decide on the Length and Placement of Your Bangs

Next, you'll want to decide on the length and placement of your side bangs. Use the comb to separate the section of hair that you want to cut into bangs, and use clips or hair ties to keep the rest of your hair out of the way. Hold the hair up to your forehead and use the comb to determine where you want your bangs to start and end. Keep in mind that you can always cut more hair later, but you can't put it back once it's gone!

Step 3: Cut the Bangs

Now it's time to start cutting. Take the scissors and make small, vertical cuts into the hair, rather than cutting straight across. This will help create a more natural, textured look. Start with small snips and gradually work your way up to the desired length. Remember to keep checking your progress in the mirror to make sure everything looks even.

Step 4: Style Your Bangs

Once you've finished cutting, it's time to style your bangs. Use the comb to brush them into place and make any necessary adjustments. You can also add some product, like hairspray or texturizing spray, to help hold the hair in place and add some volume.

Tips for Cutting Side Bangs by Yourself

Cutting your own bangs can be a little nerve-wracking, but these tips can help make the process smoother:

  • Start with small snips and work your way up to the desired length
  • Don't cut too much hair at once - you can always cut more later
  • Use vertical cuts instead of cutting straight across to create a more natural look
  • Keep checking your progress in the mirror to make sure everything looks even
  • When in doubt, go to a professional - they can fix any mistakes you make

FAQ

How do I know if side bangs will look good on me?

Side bangs can complement a variety of face shapes and hair textures, but it's always a good idea to consult with a hairstylist before making a big change. They can help you determine what type of bangs will suit your face shape and overall style.

How often do I need to trim my side bangs?

How often you need to trim your side bangs will depend on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the bangs start to look uneven. As a general rule, you'll probably need to trim them every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking their best.

Hair Care for Side Bangs

Once you've cut your side bangs, it's important to take care of them properly to keep them looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to prevent oil buildup
  • Blow-dry your bangs to help them maintain their shape
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and make your bangs look frizzy
  • Use a small amount of product, like hairspray or texturizing spray, to help hold your bangs in place

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to cut side bangs by yourself like a pro. Give it a try and see how this simple change can transform your look!
