How To Tone Orange Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Sep 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you've recently lightened your hair and ended up with orange tones, don't panic! There are ways to tone down the brassiness and achieve the desired shade. In this guide, we'll show you how to tone orange hair at home using simple ingredients and techniques. Follow these steps to get the perfect hair color without breaking the bank.

How to:

Step 1: Choose the Right Toner

The first step in toning orange hair is to select the right toner. Look for a toner that is designed specifically for orange hair, such as a blue or purple toner. These toners work to neutralize the orange tones and bring out cooler shades. You can find toner at your local beauty supply store or online.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

Before applying the toner, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Shampoo your hair as usual, but avoid using any conditioner or hair products. Towel dry your hair gently and comb it through to remove any tangles.

Step 3: Mix the Toner

Follow the instructions on the toner packaging to mix the toner with a developer. The developer will activate the toner and help it penetrate your hair. Mix the toner in a plastic bowl or bottle, using gloves to protect your hands.

Step 4: Apply the Toner

Apply the toner to your hair using a brush or applicator bottle. Start at the roots and work your way down to the tips, making sure to saturate each strand evenly. Massage the toner into your hair gently with your fingers to ensure full coverage.

Step 5: Wait

Leave the toner on your hair for the recommended amount of time, usually between 10 to 30 minutes. Check the color of your hair periodically to see if the orange tones are fading. If the toner is too strong or too weak, you can adjust the timing for future applications.

Step 6: Rinse and Condition

After the time is up, rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water. Make sure to remove all the toner from your hair, as any residue can affect the final color. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair and leave it on for a few minutes to restore moisture and shine.

Tips:

- Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup before applying the toner.

- Avoid using hot water when rinsing your hair, as it can open up the hair cuticles and cause color fading.

- Wear gloves when handling the toner and developer to avoid skin irritation.

- Do a strand test before applying the toner to your whole head, especially if you have sensitive skin or allergies.

FAQ:

Q: Can I use natural remedies to tone orange hair?

A: Yes, there are several natural ingredients that can help reduce orange tones, such as apple cider vinegar, lemon juice, and chamomile tea. However, these remedies may not be as effective as toner and can cause dryness or damage to your hair.

Q: How often should I tone my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and the level of brassiness you want to eliminate. Generally, you can tone your hair every 4 to 6 weeks, but avoid overdoing it, as it can lead to over-processing and breakage.

Hair Care:

To maintain your newly toned hair, use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that are formulated for color-treated hair. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and fade the color. Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner. With proper care, your toned hair will stay vibrant and healthy-looking for weeks to come.
