How To Cut Curly Hair Into A Short Bob

Description

If you have curly hair, you may be hesitant to cut it into a short bob because of the potential for frizz and unruliness. However, with the right technique and tools, cutting curly hair into a short bob can be a stylish and manageable choice. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of cutting curly hair into a short bob, from how to prepare your hair to the final steps of styling.

How to

The first step in cutting curly hair into a short bob is to gather the necessary tools. You will need sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a spray bottle filled with water. It is also helpful to have a mirror and a steady hand.

Step by Step

Step 1: Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to gently dry your curls.

Step 2: Section your hair into four equal parts by creating a part down the middle of your head and then another part horizontally from ear to ear. Clip each section out of the way.

Step 3: Begin cutting your hair by starting with the back section. Pull a small section of hair straight down and use your scissors to cut at an angle, creating a slight A-line shape. Repeat this step for the other sections, making sure to keep the lengths as even as possible.

Step 4: Move on to the front sections of your hair. Create a guide by pulling a section of hair straight down from the top of your ear. Cut at an angle, following the guide to create a slight A-line shape. Repeat this step on the other side.

Step 5: Use your scissors to create layers throughout your hair, starting at the back and working your way forward. This will help to create volume and shape in your short bob.

Step 6: Once you have finished cutting, style your hair as desired. Use a curl-defining product to enhance your curls and reduce frizz.

Tips

  • Be conservative when cutting your hair. You can always cut more later, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Consider getting your hair cut by a professional stylist who has experience working with curly hair.
  • Invest in a high-quality pair of scissors to ensure a clean and precise cut.
  • When cutting your hair, make sure to cut at an angle rather than straight across. This will help to create a more natural-looking shape.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to gently dry your curls without causing frizz.

FAQ

Q: How short should I cut my curly hair into a bob?

A: The length of your bob will depend on your personal preference and the shape of your face. A good starting point is to cut your hair to just above your shoulders.

Q: Will cutting my curly hair into a bob make it more difficult to manage?

A: Not necessarily. A short bob can actually make curly hair easier to manage because it eliminates some of the weight and bulk that can make curly hair difficult to style.

Hair Care

After cutting your curly hair into a short bob, it is important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and nourished. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair. Instead, embrace your natural curls and let them air dry whenever possible.
