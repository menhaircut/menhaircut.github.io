What To Use On Fine Hair To Hold Curl Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Jul 10, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of your curls falling flat within an hour of styling? Do you have fine hair that just won't hold a curl? Don't worry, you're not alone. Fine hair can be difficult to style, but with the right products and techniques, you can achieve beautiful, bouncy curls that last all day. In this article, we'll go over what to use on fine hair to hold curl, as well as some helpful tips and tricks to keep your curls looking great.

How to Prep Fine Hair for Curling

Before we get into what products to use, let's talk about how to prep your hair for curling. One of the most important things you can do is to start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is damp or wet, it will be much harder to get your curls to stay in place. You should also use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from hot styling tools.

Another helpful tip is to use a volumizing mousse or spray before you start curling. This will give your hair some extra body and hold, making it easier to create and maintain curls. Apply the product to your roots and work it through your hair, focusing on the areas where you want to add volume.

What to Use on Fine Hair to Hold Curl

Now, let's talk about the products that will help you achieve long-lasting curls on fine hair. There are a few different options to consider:

1. Curling Gel

Curling gel is a great choice for fine hair because it provides hold without weighing your hair down. Look for a lightweight formula that won't leave your hair feeling sticky or crunchy. Apply the gel to your hair in sections, working it through from roots to ends. Then, use a curling iron to create your desired style.

2. Texturizing Spray

Texturizing spray is another excellent option for fine hair. This type of product adds grip and texture to your hair, making it easier to create and hold curls. Spray the product onto your hair before using a curling iron, focusing on the areas where you want to add volume and texture.

3. Hairspray

Of course, no discussion of curl-holding products would be complete without mentioning hairspray. This classic styling product is a must-have for anyone who wants their curls to last all day. Look for a flexible hold hairspray that won't make your hair feel stiff or crunchy. Spray the product onto your finished curls to set them in place.

Step-by-Step Guide to Curling Fine Hair

Now that you know what products to use, let's go over the steps to curling fine hair:

Step 1: Prep your hair

Start with clean, dry hair, and use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage. Apply a volumizing mousse or spray to add body and hold to your hair.

Step 2: Section your hair

Divide your hair into sections, using clips or hair ties to keep them separate. This will make it easier to curl your hair evenly.

Step 3: Curl your hair

Starting with one section at a time, use a curling iron to create your desired style. Hold the iron vertically and wrap small sections of hair around the barrel, holding for a few seconds before releasing. Repeat until all sections are curled.

Step 4: Set your curls

Spray your finished curls with a flexible hold hairspray to set them in place. You can also use a texturizing spray to add extra hold and texture.

Tips for Maintaining Curls on Fine Hair

Now that you've created beautiful curls, how can you make them last all day? Here are a few tips:

1. Don't overdo it with products

While it's important to use the right products to create your curls, you don't want to go overboard. Using too much product can weigh your hair down and make it harder to hold a curl. Stick to a lightweight formula and use it sparingly.

2. Avoid touching your hair

Once you've styled your hair, try to avoid touching it too much. Every time you run your fingers through your hair, you're disrupting the curl pattern and making it harder for your curls to stay in place.

3. Sleep on silk

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help prevent frizz and keep your curls looking great. Silk is gentle on your hair, reducing friction and helping to maintain your style.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size curling iron should I use on fine hair?

For fine hair, it's best to use a smaller curling iron, such as a 1-inch barrel. This will help create tighter, longer-lasting curls.

Can I use a diffuser on fine hair?

Yes, a diffuser attachment can be helpful for fine hair. This tool helps to distribute heat more evenly, reducing the risk of damage and making it easier to create curls.

How often should I wash my hair to maintain curls?

It's best to avoid washing your hair every day if you want to maintain your curls. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it harder to hold a curl. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days, using a dry shampoo in between if needed.

Hair Care for Fine Hair

Finally, it's important to take good care of your fine hair to ensure it stays healthy and strong. Here are a few tips:

1. Use a gentle shampoo

Choose a shampoo that's formulated for fine hair, and avoid products that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates. These can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle.

2. Condition regularly

Conditioning is essential for fine hair, as it helps to add moisture and prevent damage. Use a lightweight conditioner and focus on the ends of your hair, where it's most prone to breakage.

3. Protect your hair from heat

Using hot styling tools can be damaging to your hair, so it's important to protect it with a heat protectant spray. You can also try air-drying your hair instead of using a blow dryer, or using a low heat setting if you must use one.

With these tips and tricks, you can achieve beautiful, long-lasting curls on fine hair. Remember to choose the right products, prep your hair properly, and take good care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
