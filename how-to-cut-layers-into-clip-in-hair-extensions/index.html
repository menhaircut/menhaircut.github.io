How To Cut Layers Into Clip In Hair Extensions

Written by Luffy Jul 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Clip in hair extensions are a popular way to add length and volume to your hair without committing to permanent extensions. However, sometimes clip in extensions can look too blunt and unnatural. Adding layers can give your extensions a more natural look and help them blend in with your real hair. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting layers into clip in hair extensions.

Description

Clip in hair extensions are a great way to add length and volume to your hair without damaging it. However, sometimes they can look too blunt and obvious. Adding layers can help them blend in better with your real hair and give them a more natural look. Cutting layers into clip in extensions can be a bit tricky, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done at home.

How to Cut Layers into Clip In Hair Extensions

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, you will need a few tools. These include:

  • Clip in hair extensions
  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • Hair clips

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can cause split ends and damage your extensions.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Extensions

Before cutting your extensions, wash and dry them thoroughly. This will make it easier to see the natural fall of the hair and ensure that you are cutting evenly. Make sure to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and let your extensions air dry or use a low heat setting on your hair dryer.

Step 3: Section Your Extensions

Use your comb to section your extensions into manageable pieces. Clip the top layers out of the way so that you can focus on the bottom layers first. This will help you see where the layers should be cut.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Layers

Starting with the bottom layer, comb out a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Be sure to cut at an angle to create a soft, layered look. Repeat this process on all the bottom layers.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layers

Once you have finished cutting the bottom layers, unclip the top layers and repeat the process. Remember to cut at an angle and blend the layers in with the bottom layers.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you have finished cutting all the layers, use your comb to blend the layers together. This will create a natural, seamless look. You can also use your scissors to trim any uneven pieces or create a more textured look.

Tips

  • Start with small sections and cut a little at a time. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Use a mirror to check the layers from all angles.
  • If you're not confident in cutting your extensions, consider taking them to a professional stylist.

FAQ

Can you cut layers into any type of clip in hair extensions?

Yes, you can cut layers into any type of clip in hair extensions, including synthetic and human hair extensions. However, be sure to use sharp scissors and be careful not to damage the extensions.

How often should I cut my clip in hair extensions?

It depends on how often you wear your extensions and how quickly your hair grows. As a general rule, you should trim your extensions every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain their shape.

Hair Care

After cutting your clip in hair extensions, be sure to take care of them properly to ensure they last as long as possible. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat tools too often. When storing your extensions, keep them in a cool, dry place and avoid tangling them. With proper care, your clip in hair extensions will continue to look great for months to come!
