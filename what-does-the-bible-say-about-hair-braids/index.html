What Does The Bible Say About Hair Braids

Written by Oliver Oct 29, 2023 · 5 min read

Welcome to our blog post about what the bible says about hair braids. In this article, we will explore the biblical references to hair braids and provide you with some tips on how to care for your hair while wearing braids. If you're interested in learning more, keep reading!

Biblical References To Hair Braids

The bible mentions hair braids in a few different places. In 1 Peter 3:3-4, it says "Do not let your adornment be merely outward—arranging the hair, wearing gold, or putting on fine apparel— rather let it be the hidden person of the heart, with the incorruptible beauty of a gentle and quiet spirit, which is very precious in the sight of God."

In this verse, it's important to note that the focus is on the inner beauty of a person rather than their outward appearance. However, it's also not saying that it's wrong to braid your hair or wear jewelry. It's simply saying that these things should not be the only focus.

In addition, in Song of Solomon 7:5, it says "Your head crowns you like Mount Carmel. Your hair is like royal tapestry; the king is held captive by its tresses."

This verse is a compliment to the beauty of the woman's hair, which is described as being like royal tapestry. It's important to note that this verse is not condemning hair braids either.

How To Braid Your Hair

If you're interested in braiding your hair, there are a few different methods you can use. Here are some of the most popular:

French Braids

French braids are a classic braid style that involves braiding your hair close to your scalp. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles.
  2. Take a section of hair at the top of your head and divide it into three equal pieces.
  3. Cross the right piece over the middle piece.
  4. Cross the left piece over the middle piece.
  5. Add a small section of hair to the right piece and cross it over the middle piece.
  6. Add a small section of hair to the left piece and cross it over the middle piece.
  7. Repeat steps 5-6 until you reach the nape of your neck.
  8. Finish the braid by braiding the remaining hair without adding any additional sections.

Dutch Braids

Dutch braids are similar to French braids, but the sections of hair are crossed under rather than over. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles.
  2. Take a section of hair at the top of your head and divide it into three equal pieces.
  3. Cross the right piece under the middle piece.
  4. Cross the left piece under the middle piece.
  5. Add a small section of hair to the right piece and cross it under the middle piece.
  6. Add a small section of hair to the left piece and cross it under the middle piece.
  7. Repeat steps 5-6 until you reach the nape of your neck.
  8. Finish the braid by braiding the remaining hair without adding any additional sections.

Step-By-Step Guide To Braiding Your Hair

If you're new to braiding your hair, here's a step-by-step guide to get you started:

  1. Brush your hair to remove any tangles.
  2. Decide which type of braid you want to do (French or Dutch).
  3. Divide your hair into three equal sections at the top of your head.
  4. Begin braiding your hair by crossing the right section over the middle section (or under, if you're doing a Dutch braid).
  5. Add a small section of hair to the right section and cross it over the middle section (or under, if you're doing a Dutch braid).
  6. Add a small section of hair to the left section and cross it over the middle section (or under, if you're doing a Dutch braid).
  7. Repeat steps 5-6 until you reach the nape of your neck.
  8. Finish the braid by braiding the remaining hair without adding any additional sections.
  9. Tie off the end of the braid with a hair tie.

Tips For Braiding Your Hair

Here are a few tips to help you braid your hair successfully:

  • Brush your hair thoroughly before you start braiding to remove any tangles.
  • Use a hair tie that matches your hair color to avoid it being noticeable in the braid.
  • Practice makes perfect – don't worry if your first few braids aren't perfect!
  • Experiment with different types of braids to find the ones that work best for your hair.
  • Don't braid your hair too tightly, as this can cause damage to your hair and scalp.

FAQ About Hair Braids

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair braids:

Q: Can I braid my hair if it's wet?

A: Yes, but be gentle when braiding wet hair as it is more fragile and prone to breakage.

Q: How long should I leave my braids in?

A: This depends on your hair type and the style of the braid. As a general rule, you should take your braids out after 6-8 weeks to avoid damage to your hair and scalp.

Q: Can I wash my hair with braids in?

A: Yes, but be gentle when washing your hair and avoid using harsh shampoos or conditioners that can damage the hair and loosen the braids.

Hair Care Tips For Braids

Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind if you're wearing braids:

  • Moisturize your scalp regularly to prevent dryness and itching.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your hair and cause buildup.
  • Wrap your hair at night with a silk scarf or bonnet to prevent tangling and breakage.
  • Take breaks between braiding styles to give your hair a chance to rest and recover.

We hope this article has been helpful in answering your questions about what the bible says about hair braids. Remember, the most important thing is to focus on inner beauty rather than outward appearance. Happy braiding!
