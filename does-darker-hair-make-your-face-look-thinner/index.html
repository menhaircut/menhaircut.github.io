Does Darker Hair Make Your Face Look Thinner

Written by Christine Sep 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking for a way to make your face look thinner without going under the knife? One simple solution you might want to consider is changing your hair color. In this article, we'll explore the question, "does darker hair make your face look thinner?" and provide some tips for achieving the desired effect.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

It's a common belief that darker hair can make your face look thinner. The reason for this is that darker shades create contrast between your hair and your face, which can help define your features and make your face appear slimmer. However, there are several factors to consider when deciding whether or not to darken your locks.

For example, skin tone and hair texture can both affect how a new hair color will look on you. Additionally, the cut and style of your hair can also play a role in how slimming your new hue will be.

How to

If you're considering going darker to make your face look thinner, there are a few steps you should take to ensure the best possible outcome.

1. Choose the Right Shade

The first step in achieving a slimmer-looking face with darker hair is to select the right shade. Generally speaking, cool-toned colors like espresso, burgundy, and deep chocolate will have a more slimming effect than warm-toned shades like caramel or honey.

2. Consider Your Skin Tone

When selecting a new hair color, it's important to consider your skin tone. If you have cool-toned skin, a cool-toned hair color will likely look best on you. Conversely, if you have warm-toned skin, a warm-toned shade will likely be more flattering.

3. Opt for a Flattering Cut

Even the most slimming hair color won't do much to change the shape of your face if your cut isn't flattering. Consider a style that frames your face without adding bulk, such as a sleek bob or long layers.

Step by Step

If you're ready to take the plunge and go darker, here's a step-by-step guide to help you achieve the desired effect.

1. Consult with a Professional

Before making any major hair changes, it's always a good idea to consult with a professional stylist. They can help you select the best shade for your skin tone and provide recommendations for a flattering cut.

2. Prep Your Hair

Prior to coloring your hair, it's important to prep it properly. This may involve deep conditioning treatments to ensure your hair is healthy and moisturized.

3. Color Your Hair

Once your hair has been prepped, it's time to color it. Depending on the shade you're going for, this may involve bleaching your hair first. Be sure to follow the instructions on the box carefully, and consider enlisting the help of a friend or family member if you're new to at-home coloring.

4. Maintain Your New Hue

After coloring your hair, be sure to take proper care of it to maintain the color and keep your locks healthy. This may involve using specialized color-safe shampoo and conditioner, as well as regular trims to prevent split ends.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to help you achieve a slimmer-looking face with darker hair.

  • Consider adding highlights to create dimension and further define your features.
  • Avoid haircuts with blunt or heavy bangs, which can add bulk to your face.
  • When styling your hair, consider a sleek, straight look to create the illusion of length.

FAQ

Is it true that darker hair can make your face look thinner?

Yes, it's generally believed that darker hair can create contrast between your hair and your face, which can help define your features and make your face appear slimmer.

What shade of dark hair is most slimming?

Cool-toned shades like espresso, burgundy, and deep chocolate are generally considered the most slimming.

Hair Care

After coloring your hair, it's important to take proper care of it to maintain the color and keep your locks healthy. This may involve using specialized color-safe shampoo and conditioner, as well as regular trims to prevent split ends. It's also a good idea to minimize heat styling and protect your hair from the sun to prevent damage.

In conclusion, while there's no magic solution for achieving a slimmer-looking face, changing your hair color can certainly help. By selecting the right shade, cut, and styling techniques, you can create the illusion of a more defined and slender face.
