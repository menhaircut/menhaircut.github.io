How To Cut Layers In My Own Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 06, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of your short hair looking flat and boring? Adding layers can give your hair more dimension and movement. But going to the salon can be expensive and time-consuming. Why not try cutting your own layers at home? Don't worry, it's easier than you think! In this guide, we'll show you how to cut layers in your own short hair.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Layers in My Own Short Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Layers in Short Hair

How to Cut Layers in My Own Short Hair

Before we begin, make sure you have the right tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Now that you have your tools ready, follow these steps to cut layers in your own short hair:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair. Comb your hair straight down and part it in the middle.

Step 2: Take a section of hair from the crown of your head and clip it up. This will be the top layer.

Step 3: Take a section of hair from the bottom of your head and hold it between your fingers, about an inch above the tips.

Step 4: Cut the hair straight across using your scissors. This will be your guide for the rest of your layers.

Step 5: Take another section of hair from the bottom and hold it up to the guide. Cut the hair at a slightly longer length than the guide.

Step 6: Continue taking sections of hair and cutting them at slightly longer lengths, using the previous layer as a guide.

Step 7: Now, unclip the top layer and repeat the previous steps, cutting the hair at slightly longer lengths than the previous layer.

Step 8: Once you've finished cutting all your layers, check for any uneven spots and trim them if necessary.

Step 9: Style your hair as desired and admire your new layered look!

Tips for Cutting Layers in Short Hair

  • Take your time and be patient. Rushing can lead to uneven layers.
  • Start with small sections of hair and gradually work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head and make sure your layers are even.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own layers, ask a friend to help or consult a professional stylist.

FAQs

Q: How many layers should I cut in my short hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and desired look. Start with a few layers and add more if needed.

Q: Can I cut layers in wet hair?

A: It's best to cut layers in dry hair so you can see the length and shape better.

Q: Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

A: No, you should use sharp hair-cutting scissors to ensure clean cuts.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers

After cutting your layers, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the shape and health of your new style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and health of your layers.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you can confidently cut layers in your own short hair and enjoy a fresh new look without breaking the bank. Happy cutting!
