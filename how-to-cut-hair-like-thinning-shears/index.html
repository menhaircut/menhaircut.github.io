How To Cut Hair Like Thinning Shears A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of the bulky and unnatural look that traditional hair cutting scissors leave behind? Then it might be time to try cutting hair with thinning shears. This technique creates a soft and natural-looking finish that is perfect for clients who want to keep their hair looking voluminous without sacrificing length. Keep reading to learn how to cut hair like thinning shears in just a few easy steps.

Description

Thinning shears are a type of hair cutting tool that has been designed to remove bulk from the hair without significantly changing its length. They work by snipping off only a small amount of hair with each cut, which creates a softer and more natural-looking finish. Thinning shears are an excellent tool for stylists who want to create texture and volume in their clients' hair without making it look choppy or uneven.

How to Cut Hair Like Thinning Shears

Step 1: Prep the Hair

Before you start cutting, you need to make sure that the hair is clean and dry. Comb through it to remove any tangles or knots, and then section it into manageable pieces using clips or elastics.

Step 2: Choose the Right Shears

Thinning shears come in different shapes, sizes, and styles. Choose the one that is best suited for the job at hand. A good rule of thumb is to use a smaller pair of shears for shorter hair and a larger pair for longer hair.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Hold a small section of hair between your fingers and insert the thinning shears about halfway down the hair shaft. Close the shears and then gently pull them through the hair, moving them towards the ends. Repeat this process until you have removed the desired amount of bulk.

Remember to work slowly and carefully. It's better to take off too little hair than too much. You can always go back and thin out more hair later if necessary.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

Once you have finished thinning out the hair, it's time to blend it in. Use regular hair cutting shears to trim any longer pieces that may stick out. This will create a more even and natural-looking finish.

Tips for Cutting Hair Like Thinning Shears

  • Practice on mannequin heads before attempting this technique on a real client.
  • Use the right amount of tension when holding the hair. Too much pressure can cause the hair to stretch and bounce back, which can affect the final result.
  • Work in small sections to ensure that you are removing the right amount of hair.
  • Check your work frequently by letting the hair fall naturally and looking for any uneven patches.

FAQ

What is the difference between regular scissors and thinning shears?

Regular scissors are designed to cut hair cleanly and evenly. Thinning shears, on the other hand, are designed to remove bulk from the hair without significantly changing its length. They have teeth that are set at different angles, which allows them to remove only a small amount of hair with each cut.

Can thinning shears damage hair?

If used incorrectly, thinning shears can cause damage to the hair. It's important to use the right amount of tension when holding the hair and to work slowly and carefully. If you are unsure about how to use thinning shears, it's best to consult with a professional stylist.

How often should you thin out your hair?

This will depend on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, it's best to thin out your hair every six to eight weeks to keep it looking healthy and voluminous.

Hair Care After Cutting Hair Like Thinning Shears

After cutting hair with thinning shears, it's important to take care of your hair properly. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to help keep it healthy and moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage to the hair. Regular trims can also help keep your hair looking fresh and healthy.

Now that you know how to cut hair like thinning shears, you can create beautiful and natural-looking haircuts for your clients. Remember to practice and take your time, and you'll be a pro in no time.
