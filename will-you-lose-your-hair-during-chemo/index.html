Will You Lose Your Hair During Chemo

Written by Christine Nov 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you or a loved one is undergoing chemotherapy, one of the most common concerns is whether or not hair loss will occur. While the answer is not always straightforward, it is important to understand the different factors that can affect hair loss during chemotherapy treatment. In this article, we will discuss the causes of hair loss during chemotherapy, how to prepare for it, and tips for hair care during and after treatment.

Table of Contents

  • Causes of Hair Loss During Chemotherapy
  • How to Prepare for Hair Loss
  • Hair Care During and After Treatment
  • Frequently Asked Questions

Causes of Hair Loss During Chemotherapy

Chemotherapy involves the use of drugs to kill cancer cells in the body. Unfortunately, these drugs can also affect healthy cells, including those in the hair follicles. The severity of hair loss during chemotherapy will depend on the type of drugs used and the dosage administered. Some drugs cause hair loss more frequently than others, and some people may experience complete hair loss while others may only experience thinning.

A common misconception is that all chemotherapy drugs cause hair loss. However, this is not always the case. Some targeted therapies, such as hormone therapy or immunotherapy, may not cause hair loss at all. Additionally, hair loss may not occur until several weeks after treatment has started.

How to Prepare for Hair Loss

While hair loss during chemotherapy is not always preventable, there are steps that can be taken to prepare for it. It is important to discuss the potential for hair loss with your doctor before treatment begins. They can provide more information on what to expect and recommend strategies for coping with hair loss.

Consider cutting your hair short before treatment begins. This can make hair loss less noticeable and easier to manage. Additionally, investing in a wig or hairpiece prior to hair loss can help with self-esteem and confidence during treatment.

Hair Care During and After Treatment

While undergoing chemotherapy, it is important to take extra care of your hair and scalp. Use a gentle shampoo and avoid using hot styling tools, as they can damage fragile hair. Consider using a soft-bristled brush or wide-tooth comb to avoid pulling out hair.

After treatment is over, it may take several months for hair to grow back. Be patient and gentle with new hair growth, avoiding harsh chemicals or treatments. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and consider using a scalp massage to promote blood flow to the hair follicles.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can hair loss during chemotherapy be prevented?

Unfortunately, hair loss during chemotherapy is not always preventable. However, taking steps to prepare for hair loss and taking good care of the scalp and hair during treatment can help minimize the effects.

Will hair grow back after chemotherapy?

In most cases, hair will grow back after chemotherapy has ended. However, it may take several months for hair to return to its pre-treatment state. Additionally, hair texture and color may be different than before treatment.

What can I do to cope with hair loss during chemotherapy?

Investing in a wig or hairpiece prior to hair loss can help with self-esteem and confidence during treatment. Additionally, talking to a therapist or support group can provide emotional support and coping strategies.

While hair loss during chemotherapy can be a difficult experience, understanding the causes and taking steps to prepare for it can help lessen its impact. By taking good care of the scalp and hair during treatment and being patient with new hair growth after treatment, you can help promote healthy hair growth and regain confidence and self-esteem.
