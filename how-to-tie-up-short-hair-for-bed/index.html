How To Tie Up Short Hair For Bed A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Whether you have a short bob or pixie cut, tying up your hair before bed can help keep it in place and prevent tangles in the morning. In this guide, we'll show you how to tie up short hair for bed in just a few easy steps. Read on to learn more.

How to Tie Up Short Hair for Bed

Before we get started, gather your supplies. You'll need a scrunchie or hair tie, a comb or brush, and any hair products you regularly use, such as leave-in conditioner or oil.

Step 1: Brush Your Hair

Using a comb or brush, gently detangle your hair to remove any knots or tangles. This will make it easier to tie up your hair later on.

Step 2: Apply Hair Products

If you use any hair products, such as leave-in conditioner or oil, apply them now. This will help keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage while you sleep.

Step 3: Gather Your Hair

Using your fingers, gather your hair at the back of your head. If you have shorter layers, you may need to gather them separately and secure them with bobby pins.

Step 4: Tie Your Hair

Once you've gathered your hair, use a scrunchie or hair tie to secure it in place. Make sure it's not too tight, as this can cause hair breakage and discomfort while you sleep.

Step 5: Adjust as Needed

If you notice any loose strands or bumps, use your fingers or a brush to smooth them out. You can also adjust the placement of your hair tie to make it more comfortable while you sleep.

Tips for Tying Up Short Hair for Bed

Here are a few additional tips to help you tie up your short hair for bed:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to help prevent tangles and breakage.
  • Avoid using tight hair ties or elastic bands, as these can cause hair breakage and discomfort.
  • If you have bangs or shorter layers, clip them back with bobby pins to prevent them from getting in your face while you sleep.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or oil to help keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.

FAQ About Tying Up Short Hair for Bed

Q: Can I tie up wet hair before bed?

A: It's generally best to let your hair air dry or blow dry it before tying it up for bed. Wet hair is more prone to breakage, so it's important to handle it gently.

Q: How tight should I tie my hair up?

A: Your hair tie should be snug, but not so tight that it causes discomfort or hair breakage. If you notice any pulling or tension, loosen the hair tie or adjust the placement of your hair.

Q: Will tying up my hair for bed cause damage?

A: Tying up your hair for bed can actually help prevent damage by keeping it in place and preventing tangles. However, it's important to use a gentle hair tie and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair.

Hair Care for Short Hair

Tying up your short hair for bed is just one part of a healthy hair care routine. Here are a few additional tips to help keep your short hair looking its best:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, too often.
  • Use hair products, such as leave-in conditioner or oil, to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Get regular haircuts to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and learning how to tie up your short hair for bed, you can help keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful. Sweet dreams!
