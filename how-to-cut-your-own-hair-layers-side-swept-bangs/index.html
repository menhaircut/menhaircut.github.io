How To Cut Your Own Hair Layers And Side Swept Bangs

Written by Frank Aug 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending so much money at the hair salon for a simple haircut? With the right tools and techniques, you can easily cut your own hair layers and side swept bangs at home. Not only will you save money, but you'll also have the satisfaction of doing it yourself. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your own hair layers and side swept bangs in relaxed English language.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Layers

Cutting your own hair layers can seem like a daunting task, but with the right tools and preparation, it can be done easily. Follow these steps to cut your own hair layers:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair layers, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash your hair and let it air dry or blow dry it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use hair clips to section your hair into small sections. Start at the bottom of your head and work your way up.

Step 4: Cut the Layers

Take one section of hair at a time and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle to create layers. Repeat this process for each section of hair until you've cut all the layers.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the layers, use your scissors to blend them together. This will create a seamless transition between the layers.

How to Cut Your Own Side Swept Bangs

Cutting your own side swept bangs can be intimidating, but with a little practice, you can achieve a professional look. Follow these steps to cut your own side swept bangs:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash your hair and let it air dry or blow dry it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use hair clips to section your hair. Separate the hair that you want to cut for your side swept bangs.

Step 3: Cut the Bangs

Take the section of hair for your side swept bangs and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle. Start with small cuts, and gradually cut more if needed.

Step 4: Blend the Bangs

Once you've cut your side swept bangs, use your scissors to blend them into the rest of your hair. This will create a natural look.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair Layers and Side Swept Bangs

Here are some tips to make cutting your own hair layers and side swept bangs easier:

  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. Start with small cuts and work your way up.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress.

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your own hair if it's curly. Just make sure to cut it when it's dry so you can see the natural curl pattern.

How often should I cut my own hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how often you want to cut it. On average, people cut their hair every 6-8 weeks.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't worry if you make a mistake. Hair grows back, and you can always go to a professional to fix it.

Hair Care

After cutting your own hair, it's important to take care of it. Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and use a heat protectant when you do.

With these tips and techniques, you can easily cut your own hair layers and side swept bangs at home. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and be patient. Happy cutting!
