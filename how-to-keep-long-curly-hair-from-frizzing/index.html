How To Keep Long Curly Hair From Frizzing Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Sep 10, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it looks beautiful and voluminous, it can also be prone to frizz and tangles. If you have long curly hair, you know how frustrating it can be to try to tame your mane. Fortunately, there are some simple tips and tricks you can follow to keep your long curly hair from frizzing. In this article, we'll share some of our best advice for keeping your curls looking their best.

How to Keep Long Curly Hair from Frizzing

Frizz is caused by a lack of moisture in the hair, which can lead to the cuticle layer of the hair becoming rough and raised. This allows moisture from the air to enter the hair shaft, causing it to swell and frizz. To combat frizz, you need to keep your hair moisturized and protected from the elements.

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Start by using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for curly hair. Look for products that contain natural oils like argan oil, coconut oil, or shea butter, which will help to hydrate your hair and prevent frizz.

2. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and prone to frizz. Try to wash your hair no more than twice a week, and use a dry shampoo in between washes to absorb excess oil.

3. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help to keep your hair moisturized throughout the day. Apply it to damp hair after washing and comb it through with your fingers or a wide-tooth comb. Be sure to focus on the ends of your hair, which are the most prone to dryness and frizz.

4. Use a Microfiber Towel to Dry Your Hair

Regular towels can be rough on curly hair, causing it to frizz and tangle. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze excess water out of your hair. Avoid rubbing your hair with the towel, as this can cause damage and breakage.

5. Apply a Hair Oil or Serum

After drying your hair, apply a hair oil or serum to the ends of your hair. This will help to keep them moisturized and protected from the elements. Look for products that contain natural oils like argan oil, jojoba oil, or grapeseed oil.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these simple steps to keep your long curly hair from frizzing:

  1. Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Don't overwash your hair.
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner to damp hair.
  4. Gently squeeze excess water out of your hair with a microfiber towel or old t-shirt.
  5. Apply a hair oil or serum to the ends of your hair.
  6. Avoid touching your hair too much throughout the day, as this can cause frizz.

Tips for Managing Long Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for managing your long curly hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair and cause frizz.
  • Sleep on a satin pillowcase to prevent friction and breakage.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends, which can lead to frizz.

FAQs

What causes frizz in curly hair?

Frizz is caused by a lack of moisture in the hair, which can lead to the cuticle layer of the hair becoming rough and raised. This allows moisture from the air to enter the hair shaft, causing it to swell and frizz.

What is the best way to dry curly hair?

The best way to dry curly hair is to gently squeeze excess water out of your hair with a microfiber towel or old t-shirt. Avoid rubbing your hair with the towel, as this can cause damage and breakage.

How often should I wash my curly hair?

You should wash your curly hair no more than twice a week to prevent it from becoming dry and prone to frizz.

Hair Care for Long Curly Hair

Long curly hair requires special care to keep it looking its best. Follow these tips for healthy, beautiful curls:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently.
  • Avoid using hair elastics, which can cause breakage and damage to your hair.
  • Protect your hair from chlorine by wearing a swim cap or applying a leave-in conditioner before swimming.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as it can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your long curly hair looking beautiful and frizz-free. Remember to be gentle with your hair, avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling, and keep it moisturized and protected from the elements. With a little bit of effort, you can achieve the luscious, voluminous curls you've always wanted!
