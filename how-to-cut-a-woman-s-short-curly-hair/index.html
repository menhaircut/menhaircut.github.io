How To Cut A Woman s Short Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a hairstylist or just someone who wants to learn how to cut short curly hair, you're in the right place. Cutting curly hair can be tricky, but with the right technique and tools, you can achieve a beautiful cut that your client will love. In this article, we'll guide you through the steps of cutting a woman's short curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step-by-Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Short curly hair is a popular hairstyle for women because it's low-maintenance and versatile. However, cutting curly hair can be tricky because curls tend to shrink when they dry, making it difficult to achieve the desired length. In this article, we'll guide you through the steps of cutting a woman's short curly hair, so you can give your clients the perfect cut every time.

How to

Before you start cutting a woman's short curly hair, you'll need to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section the hair. You should also have a spray bottle filled with water to keep the hair damp while you work.

Step-by-Step

Here are the steps to follow when cutting a woman's short curly hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

Before you start cutting the hair, you should wash and dry it. This will give you a clean canvas to work with. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair hydrated.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Use hair clips to section the hair into four parts: the front, the back, and the two sides.

Step 3: Cut the Back

Starting with the back section, use your comb to separate a small section of hair. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the ends with your scissors. Work your way up, cutting small sections of hair until you've cut the entire back section.

Step 4: Cut the Sides

Move on to the side sections, using the same technique as you did with the back section.

Step 5: Cut the Front

Finally, move on to the front section. This is the most important part of the haircut because it frames the face. Use your comb to separate a small section of hair in the front, and cut it at an angle to create layers. Work your way up, cutting small sections until you've cut the entire front section.

Step 6: Check for Balance

Once you've cut all the sections, check for balance. Make sure that the hair is even on both sides and that the layers are symmetrical.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting a woman's short curly hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Cut the hair when it's dry so you can see how much it will shrink.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep the hair damp while you work.
  • Cut the hair in small sections to ensure an even cut.
  • Cut the front section at an angle to create layers that frame the face.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting a woman's short curly hair:

Can I cut curly hair when it's wet?

It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry so you can see how much it will shrink.

How often should I cut curly hair?

It's recommended to cut curly hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

How do I prevent frizz in curly hair?

To prevent frizz in curly hair, use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, avoid brushing the hair when it's dry, and use a diffuser when blow-drying the hair.

Hair Care

To keep curly hair healthy and vibrant, it's important to use the right hair care products. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and apply a leave-in conditioner to the hair after washing. Avoid using heat styling tools, and let the hair air-dry whenever possible.
