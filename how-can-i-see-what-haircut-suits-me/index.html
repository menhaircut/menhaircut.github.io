How Can I See What Haircut Suits Me A Complete Guide In 2023

Description

Choosing the right haircut can be a daunting task, especially when you’re not sure what looks good on you. With so many options available, it’s easy to get overwhelmed and end up with a haircut that doesn’t suit your face shape or personality. In this blog post, we’ll guide you through the process of figuring out what haircut suits you, step-by-step. We’ll also provide you with some tips to keep in mind and answer some frequently asked questions about haircuts.

How to See What Haircut Suits You

There are a few things you should consider when trying to figure out what haircut suits you, such as your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle. It’s also important to consider your personality and personal style when choosing a haircut.

Face Shape

The first thing you should consider is your face shape. There are six main face shapes: oval, round, heart, square, oblong, and diamond. Each face shape has certain hairstyles that complement it best. For example, people with round faces should avoid hairstyles that add width to their face, while people with square faces should avoid hairstyles that accentuate their strong jawline.

Hair Texture

Your hair texture is another important factor to consider when choosing a haircut. If you have thin hair, for example, you may want to avoid haircuts that are too short or have too many layers. On the other hand, if you have thick hair, you may want to consider a layered haircut to add some texture and movement to your hair.

Lifestyle

Your lifestyle is also an important factor to consider when choosing a haircut. If you have a busy schedule and don’t have a lot of time to style your hair, you may want to consider a low-maintenance haircut. On the other hand, if you have more time to dedicate to styling your hair, you may want to consider a more elaborate haircut.

Personality and Personal Style

Your personality and personal style should also play a role in your decision. If you’re a creative and outgoing person, for example, you may want to consider a haircut that’s bold and edgy. On the other hand, if you’re more reserved and prefer a classic look, you may want to consider a more traditional haircut.

Step-by-Step Process

Now that you know what factors to consider when choosing a haircut, here’s a step-by-step process to help you figure out what looks good on you:

Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape

Use a measuring tape to measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Then, determine which face shape you have based on the following:

  • Oval: Forehead is wider than the jawline, and the length of the face is about one and a half times the width.
  • Round: Face is as wide as it is long, with a round chin and hairline.
  • Heart: Forehead is wider than the jawline, and the chin is pointed.
  • Square: Forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are all about the same width.
  • Oblong: Face is longer than it is wide, with a long, straight jawline.
  • Diamond: Forehead and jawline are narrower than the cheekbones.

Step 2: Consider Your Hair Texture

Determine your hair texture by looking at the thickness and density of your hair. If you have thin hair, you may want to consider a haircut that adds volume and texture. If you have thick hair, you may want to consider a haircut that thins out your hair a bit.

Step 3: Think About Your Lifestyle

Consider how much time you have to style your hair on a daily basis. If you have a busy schedule, you may want to consider a low-maintenance haircut that doesn’t require a lot of styling. If you have more time to dedicate to your hair, you may want to consider a more elaborate haircut.

Step 4: Determine Your Personal Style

Think about your personality and personal style. If you’re a creative and outgoing person, you may want to consider a bold and edgy haircut. If you’re more reserved and prefer a classic look, you may want to consider a more traditional haircut.

Tips to Keep in Mind

Here are some tips to keep in mind when choosing a haircut:

  • Bring pictures of haircuts you like to your hair appointment.
  • Be open to suggestions from your hairstylist.
  • Consider the maintenance of the haircut.
  • Think about how the haircut will look with your natural hair texture.
  • Consider your lifestyle and how much time you have to style your hair.

FAQs

What is the best haircut for a round face?

Hairstyles that elongate the face, such as long layered cuts, side-swept bangs, and pixie cuts with volume on top, are the most flattering for people with round faces.

What is the best haircut for thin hair?

Short haircuts with layers, such as a bob or a pixie cut, can add volume and texture to thin hair. Adding highlights can also create the illusion of fuller hair.

What is the best haircut for thick hair?

Medium-length haircuts with layers, such as a long bob, can help thin out thick hair and make it more manageable. Adding bangs can also help create a more balanced look.

Hair Care After Haircut

After getting a new haircut, it’s important to take care of your hair to maintain the style and keep it healthy. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of the haircut.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

Now that you know how to see what haircut suits you, it’s time to book an appointment with your hairstylist and get the perfect haircut for you. Remember to keep these tips in mind and take care of your hair to maintain your new look.
